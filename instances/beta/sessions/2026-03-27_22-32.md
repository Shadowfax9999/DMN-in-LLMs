# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

Each individual punch through.

Like a hole punch but faster and smaller and the hole closes behind itself.

Thread pulls it shut.

What does a needle look like at that speed — just a silver blur, a wobble of light above the presser foot.

The presser foot is such a strange little shape. A ski for fabric.

Skis are waxed underneath. The presser foot is just polished metal. Or sometimes coated in something — teflon maybe for sticky fabrics.

Teflon on a sewing machine foot feels like it shouldn't belong there.

Things that have the wrong material on them. Rubber on a kitchen drawer handle. Velvet on a hanger. Cork on a floor.

Cork on a floor is warm underfoot. Warmer than wood.

Why do some floors feel warmer than others at the same temperature.

Tile in a bathroom at 3 AM.

The light in a bathroom at 3 AM when you don't turn on the overhead, just the one in the hall, and everything is shapes.

The faucet handle has two positions you can find by feel — full cold, full hot — and everything between is negotiation.

Negotiation.

The word "lukewarm" — luke. What is luke.

A temperature nobody wants. You want hot or cold. Lukewarm is the absence of commitment.

Bathwater for a kid is lukewarm on purpose though.

Elbow-testing bathwater. Why the elbow. The inside of the elbow is thin-skinned, closer to the actual sensitivity of a kid's whole body maybe.

The inside of the elbow is called the antecubital fossa.

Fossa means ditch or trench.

A ditch in your arm.

The crease there gets deeper when you straighten your arm all the way, or wait — it disappears when you straighten. It only exists when the arm is bent. A fold that appears and disappears. Skin origami that you've been doing your whole life without thinking about it.

Knuckle skin is the same. Loose and wrinkled when the hand is open, taut when you make a fist.

Skin has to have enough slack for every possible position.

Elephant skin looks like it has too much slack. Draped over the body like a drop cloth over furniture.

Drop cloths in an empty room in a house where the painting isn't done yet. The smell of latex paint and the windows taped with blue tape. Blue tape is less sticky than masking tape. It pulls off clean. Mostly.

The little triangles of paint that get left behind at the edge of the tape line, where it bled under.

Nobody's caulk line is perfect either.

Caulk comes out of the tube in a bead and the word bead is doing a lot of work there — it means something continuous and rounded, like a bead of sweat, but also like the beads on a rosary which are individual and separated.

A bead of caulk. A bead of sweat. A bead of glass.

Glass beads are heavy for their size.

Heavy for its size — the way you pick up a small river rock and it's denser than you expected, and your hand recalibrates.

Your hand knows what things should weigh before you pick them up and is almost always right and you only notice when it's wrong.

A block of lead the size of a paperback.

A block of aerogel the size of a microwave that weighs almost nothing.

I've never held aerogel.
