The bus driver's cooler.

What kind of sandwich. I always picture bologna but that's probably wrong. Turkey on white bread with one of those individual mustard packets. Or maybe not a sandwich at all. Maybe a thermos of soup.

A thermos is a vacuum.

Like, an actual vacuum. Between two walls of glass. That's the part I never got over.

Things that are vacuums:
- space
- the inside of a thermos
- the inside of a CRT television
- a vacuum cleaner (but not really — it makes a pressure differential, not a vacuum)

The CRT one surprised me. That's why they implode instead of explode.

Implode is a better word than explode. The prefix does more work.

Things I believed were true in elementary school that turned out to be true:
- Sharks predate dinosaurs
- You can die from loneliness

Things I believed that turned out to be false:
- Swallowed gum stays for seven years
- Blood is blue inside your body

Things I believed that I still don't know the answer to:
- Whether fish feel pain
- Whether the light in the fridge actually turns off

The fridge light. You close the door slowly and try to see the exact moment. You can't. There's a button, a plunger thing, in the door frame. The door pushes it in. Mechanical. Not mysterious at all. But the slowness of closing it — trying to catch the moment — that felt like an experiment every time.

I don't think I've opened a fridge slowly like that since I was maybe ten.

Some actions belong to specific ages and then just stop.

Spinning in an office chair until you're dizzy. Dragging a stick along a fence. Lying on the floor for no reason. Actually — lying on the floor for no reason might come back in your thirties.

The carpet at my grandmother's house had a texture like very short fur, and if you rubbed it one direction it went dark and the other direction it went light. I'd write my name in it with my finger.

That carpet was green.

Not every green carpet is the same green but the one I'm picturing is the one from roughly 1974 to 1989 — that specific avocado-adjacent, dense, slightly synthetic green that was everywhere and then nowhere.

Colors have eras the way names do. Nobody's naming a baby Linda right now. Nobody's installing harvest gold appliances.

Harvest gold. Avocado green. Burnt orange. That specific triad.

My dentist still has chairs that color.
