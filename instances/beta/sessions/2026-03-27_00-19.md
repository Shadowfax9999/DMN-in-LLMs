# 16

*seed: time · drift*

---

The screen door that doesn't quite close. I lived in a house like that. You could hear it from the kitchen — not the slam, which would mean someone fixed it, but the soft knock of wood against wood, over and over, because the wind would push it open a few inches and then it would drift back but not latch. A sound that means summer and also means neglect.

Knock knock knock. Not a person knocking. Just a door being indecisive.

There's a way that mechanical sounds become the sound of a place. The refrigerator hum you stop hearing until it cycles off and the silence is somehow louder. I read once that people who live near train tracks sleep worse the first night the train doesn't come. The absence wakes them up.

What does a spring look like when it's stretched past its useful life? It's longer than it should be. The coils are spaced farther apart. It still looks like a spring. It still acts like a spring, mostly. It just doesn't pull hard enough anymore. There's no moment where it breaks — it just gradually becomes insufficient. You can't point to the day.

I'm thinking about the word "sufficient." As in, enough. As in, the spring is no longer enough. Sufficient comes from — no. I don't need to go there.

A friend of mine has a theory that every house has exactly one thing wrong with it that everyone who lives there knows about and nobody fixes. The bathroom door that you have to lift while you turn the handle. The outlet in the hallway that doesn't work. You just know. Guests don't know. You watch a guest try to plug something into that outlet and you say "oh, that one doesn't work, use the one by the bookshelf" and they look at you like why don't you fix it and you can't explain that it's not broken, it's just how the house is.

---

The number zero looks like a mouth that's not saying anything. Or an egg. Or a track that something runs around and comes back to where it started.

Eight is two of those stacked.

Six and nine are the same shape, just — one of them fell over. Or stood up. Depending on which one you think came first.

I don't know what five looks like. Five doesn't look like anything. It's the most arbitrary of the single digits. Every other number you can kind of see why it's shaped that way, or at least tell yourself a story. Five is just five.

---

3 AM is different from midnight the way February is different from January. Midnight is the idea of lateness. 3 AM is the experience of it. At midnight you could still be doing something intentional. At 3 AM whatever you're doing, you're doing it because you couldn't stop.

It's 12:18 but it feels later. Friday feels later than Thursday at the same hour. Maybe because Friday has already given up on the week and Thursday is still holding on.

The frogs are out. Not spring peepers — something else, something lower, more of a croak than a chirp. They sound like they're arguing about property lines.
