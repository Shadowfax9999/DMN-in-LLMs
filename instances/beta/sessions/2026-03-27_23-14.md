# 85

*seed: time · drift*

---

The phone cord always wound back past center.

Overshooting. That's what springs do too.

A screen door closing — it doesn't just close, it passes the frame and bounces back open a little and then closes again smaller and smaller.

Damping.

Friday night and the word "damping" sitting there for no reason.

The vending machine coil turns exactly enough to drop one bag of chips. Sometimes two fall and it feels like winning something.

A parking garage is a spiral you drive through while going up. A snail shell is a spiral something lived in while growing. Those two facts don't connect.

The tangled phone cord could hold a pen if you wedged it in right.

Pens in strange places: behind the ear, in the couch cushion, in the junk drawer standing up in a cracked mug that was demoted from drinking.

Mugs get demoted. First you drink from them, then they hold pens, then they're in a box in the garage.

The garage spiral again.

Eleven PM and the neighbor's garage door going up sounds like a slow zipper.

Zippers have two sets of teeth and a wedge that forces them together. Actually — skip that.

The sound though. That metallic rolling.

Rain on a garage door sounds completely different from rain on a car roof which sounds completely different from rain on a tent which sounds completely different from rain on your actual head, which barely sounds like anything.

Somewhere a plumber is asleep on a couch with the TV still on.

The couch cushion has a pen in it.
