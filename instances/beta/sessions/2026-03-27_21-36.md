A pocket with nothing in it still has a shape.

No — that's already going somewhere. Just the pocket.

Reaching in and finding lint. Or not even lint. Just the seam at the bottom, that little ridge where the fabric folds over itself. You touch it without thinking about it. Standing in line, waiting for something.

What does a clarinet reed taste like?

Woody. Slightly sweet. The cane is from the south of France, mostly. Var region. I played clarinet for one year in sixth grade and remember the taste better than any of the music. You had to soak the reed in your mouth before playing. The case was black plastic with a teal velvet lining that collected dust and smelled like spit and wood.

Teal velvet.

Who decided instrument cases should be lined with velvet? Guitars too. That plush interior, like a tiny coffin for the instrument. Trumpets in crushed blue. Violins in wine-colored silk sometimes.

---

The number 7 looks sharp to me. 4 is flat. 8 is round, obviously, everyone thinks 8 is round. But 3 — is 3 warm or cool? I think cool. Pale blue maybe. This isn't synesthesia, I don't think. Just some half-association from a classroom number line that had colors on it, probably, decades ago, still running underneath everything.

The word "Wednesday" has a hinge in the middle of it.

A hinge — like the word bends at the d-n joint. "Wednes" and then "day." Two syllables pretending to be one concept. Most day-names do this but Wednesday is the one where you can feel the seam.

Feeling seams. The pocket again.

My left thumb has a scar from a bread knife. Not dramatic. Just a white line, slightly raised, about a centimeter long on the pad. I forget it's there for months and then notice it while doing something unrelated — holding a pen, peeling an orange. It doesn't have a story worth telling. I was cutting bread and the knife slipped.

The orange peel curls away from the fruit in that specific way. If you get a good strip going it wants to spiral.

I had a thought a second ago about the number 3 being pale blue and I'm not sure that's true. It might be that I just said it and now it feels true. The act of claiming a color for a number makes the association stronger than it was before you said it. You build the memory by reporting it.

Clarinet reeds come in strengths. 2, 2.5, 3, 3.5. Beginners use softer ones. I think I used a 2.5.
