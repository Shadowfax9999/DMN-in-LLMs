Remainder. Like what's left after division that doesn't come out clean. 7 divided by 3 is 2 remainder 1. The 1 just sits there. It doesn't belong to any of the groups. It's the piece that didn't fit the pattern you were trying to impose.

Remainder also means a book that didn't sell. Bookstores have remainder tables. The covers are slightly different from regular books — there's a mark, a slash of marker on the bottom edge of the pages, so you can't return it to the publisher. A black stripe or a red dot. A book marked so everyone in the supply chain knows it's been given up on.

What does the slash feel like under your thumb when you're reading. You'd forget it was there.

Odd that "remain" contains "main." The main thing that remains. That's probably not the actual structure of the word at all.

Things that come in remainders:

- Fabric bolts, the last few yards
- Pizza, the cold slice at 11 PM
- Daylight, right now, the last hour of it
- A conversation where someone already said the important thing and now you're both just

Sixteen divided by five. Three remainder one. No — remainder one. Yes. Three groups of five is fifteen, one left. I had to actually do that. Division gets unreliable in my head past a certain point. Not the operation itself but the confidence in it. I'll do something like 847 divided by 3 and get an answer and have no real sense of whether it's right.

The fabric bolt thing is real though. End-of-bolt remnants at fabric stores, folded and put in a bin, sold cheaper. You don't get to pick your yardage. You get what's left. My sense is people make quilts from them, or small bags, things that don't need much material. The constraint becomes the project.
