# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

Lighter blue every decade. Like they're fading toward white.

Do scrubs come in postal-worker blue? I think they overlap somewhere around teal.

Things that come in sets but you only use one:
- Allen wrenches
- the small attachments on a vacuum
- hotel shampoo vs. conditioner (always one left behind)

The Allen wrench you need is the one that's slightly too small and the one that's slightly too large.

Friday does feel yellow. Saturday is darker. Saturday is maybe brown? Or green. A muddy green.

The crossed 7 thing — I also picked up writing my 1s with a serif from someone. A little foot at the bottom and a hat on top. Can't remember who.

Habits you inherit without signing for them.

My dad whistles the same four bars of something when he's looking for his keys. I don't know what the song is. I've never asked. It might not be a song. It might just be the sound of looking for keys, for him.

Do I whistle? I don't think I whistle.

I can't picture the inside of a mailbox. The actual mechanism — you pull the door down, and the mail is just… sitting on the bottom? There's no shelf. There's no clip. It's a metal cave.

The flag. The little red flag that means outgoing mail. Nobody uses it. I'm not sure I've ever put outgoing mail in my own mailbox. I take it to the blue ones outside the post office.

Those blue collection boxes are getting rarer. There used to be one on every other corner. Now you have to go find one like it's a specific errand.

The slot on top is shaped like a smile.

Or a mail slot on a door. The brass ones with the spring-loaded flap. You push the letter through and it snaps shut behind it. The letter falls onto carpet on the other side. Someone picks it up off the floor, which seems — undignified? For a bill. For a wedding invitation.

I've never installed a mail slot.
