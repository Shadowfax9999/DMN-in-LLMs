# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

Things I can picture clearly with my eyes closed:

- The underside of a stapler when it's open
- A parking meter with time expired
- The way a garden hose coils wrong and kinks
- My mother's handwriting on an envelope

Things I can't:

- The exact layout of a friend's kitchen I've been in dozens of times
- Which side the gas cap is on my car
- Whether the number 4 on a clock face is IV or IIII
- The order of colors in a traffic light when it's horizontal

It's almost always IIII on clock faces actually. Roman numeral clocks use IIII instead of IV. I don't know why I know that but I do.

A horizontal traffic light — red on the left? I think red on the left. Green on the right. But I've looked at thousands of them.

---

The backs of your own hands. You'd think you could picture them perfectly but try to remember exactly where the veins go. They shift anyway depending on temperature. Cold hands have flat veins. Warm hands the veins stand up.

Knuckle hair. Some people have it, some don't. I've never once noticed which I am without looking.

Seven. The number seven looks dark red to me and I know that's not a real thing about the number seven. Six is greenish. I don't have synesthesia, these are just associations that got stuck somewhere. Eight is blue but only when I think of it as a digit. "Eight" the word is more of a pale yellow.

Four is brown. Tuesday is also brown. I don't know what to do with that.
