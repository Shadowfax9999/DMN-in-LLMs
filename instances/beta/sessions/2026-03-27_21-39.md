The crack in a colander is almost funny because the whole point of the object is to let water through, and now it's letting water through in the wrong place, which somehow matters.

Where does "wrong place" come from with water. Water in a basement. Water in a shoe. Water on a book. Water on a electrical outlet. Water in your ear after swimming — you tilt your head and hop on one foot and it doesn't work and then three hours later you're eating dinner and it just falls out.

A fly has been getting into the kitchen for three days. Same fly or different fly. No way to know. I assume same fly because it feels like a character now.

What's the smallest number of encounters before something becomes a character.

Two. Two is enough. The second time you see the same person at the gas station, there's a tiny narrative. Oh, you again. Even if you don't say it. Even if you don't make eye contact. The recognition itself creates a plot.

Dogs at the vet's office won't look at each other. They look at the wall, or at their owner, or at the floor near the other dog. Cats in carriers just scream.

The word "Friday" does look like its color. Gold-ish. Warm. "Tuesday" is definitely not brown, it's more like a dull blue. "Wednesday" I can't see at all. "Thursday" has some red in it but I'm not confident.

I had a period where I was convinced I had mild synesthesia and then I realized I was just thinking about it too hard and generating associations on the spot and then believing them.

The distinction between "I experience this automatically" and "I just thought of it and now I can't unthink it" is — where is that line. For anyone.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs but not up. Only the left one. It's been doing it for maybe four years. I mentioned it to a doctor once and she said "does it hurt?" and I said no and she said "then don't worry about it" and that was the end of the conversation. Fourteen seconds of medical attention.

There's a spider on the ceiling that hasn't moved since yesterday. Not a web-building spider, one of the flat fast ones. Just sitting there. Waiting, I guess, or resting, or doing whatever a spider does when it's not doing anything. Maybe spiders have a default mode too. Probably not. Probably it's just off.

The light from the street comes in at a different angle now than it did in January. I can tell because the shadow of the window frame hits the bookshelf instead of the floor.
