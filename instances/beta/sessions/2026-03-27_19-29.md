*seed: time · drift*

It might be for walnuts.

The drawer itself sticks in summer. You have to lift and pull at the same time. Everyone in the house knows this but no one explains it to guests.

A hotel room has no stuck drawers. Everything slides. The Bible is always in the nightstand, second drawer if there are two. I opened one once and there was a Book of Mormon next to a Gideon Bible, like they were roommates.

Hotel ice buckets. The little plastic bag already fitted inside. You take the bucket down the hall in your socks and the ice machine is always around a corner, humming. The hallway carpet has a pattern that hides stains. That's its whole job.

Carpet patterns in casinos are famous for this. Deliberately ugly, deliberately busy. You're not supposed to look down.

Bowling alley carpet.

The shoes. Sprayed inside with something between uses. Size marked on the heel in a font that looks like the 1970s. Half-sizes available but you never ask.

The pencil at the scoring table — before electronic scoring, you had to do it yourself. Those little marks, the slash, the X. My handwriting is worse with a pencil that short. Everyone's is. There's a length below which a pencil stops feeling like a writing instrument and starts feeling like you're gripping a bolt.

Golf pencils. The pencils at the library where you sign up for a computer. IKEA pencils, which are flat so they don't roll off the desk in the showroom. That's a design decision someone made and it's one of the better ones.

Things that are flat so they don't roll: carpenter's pencils, sewing pins with the colored heads, those promotional pens shaped like rectangles that nobody enjoys writing with.

Sewing pins pushed into a tomato-shaped pincushion. The little strawberry dangling off it. I never knew what the strawberry was for — I assumed decoration. Maybe it's for sharpening? That seems unlikely but I also don't have a better guess.
