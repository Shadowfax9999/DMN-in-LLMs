The number 7 looks sharp. 8 looks soft. This isn't about the curves — 3 has curves and looks sharp too. Maybe it's the angle at the top of the 7, that one decisive turn.

A door hinge has two knuckles or three or five. I don't know what determines it. Heavier doors get more, probably. Cabinet hinges only have two.

There's a particular sound a basketball makes on an outdoor court versus a gym floor. The outdoor one has more slap to it. More of the ball's surface hitting at once because the court is rougher.

I was trying to remember what the inside of a walnut shell looks like — not the meat, the shell itself after you've picked everything out. It has chambers. Like a tiny wooden skull with compartments where something used to be. The dividing walls inside are thin and papery but they're still wood. You can't really crush them between your fingers without effort.

Cardboard has a smell when it's warm. Like in the back of a delivery truck in summer. Sweet, almost. Slightly chemical but mostly just vegetal. Paper returning to the idea of wood.

The way you can hear someone else's headphones on a bus but only the high frequencies. Just the tsss-tsss-tsss of the hi-hat and maybe a voice but none of the bass, none of the melody really. You're hearing the skeleton of a song with all the muscle removed.

A rubber band wrapped around a doorknob. I've seen that in a lot of houses and never known exactly why. To keep it from latching? To keep a child from locking themselves in?

The ceiling fan in the room I'm sitting in has a wobble. Not dangerous, just a slight orbital movement at the pull chain. One blade must be slightly heavier or slightly more angled than the others. It's been like that for years and nobody's fixed it because it still works and the wobble is small enough to forget about until you look straight up.

The pull chain itself is brass-colored but it's not brass. Some kind of plated steel, probably, because the plating has worn through where fingers grab it and there's a duller gray underneath.
