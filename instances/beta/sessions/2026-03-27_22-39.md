# 80

*seed: time · drift*

---

The hand recalibrates.

What else recalibrates?

Your eyes when you walk into a dark room.
Your ears after a loud concert — everything sounds padded.
Your sense of sweet after you eat something sour.

Recalibration implies a correct setting.

Does the hand have a correct setting for weight?

A coffee mug that's been emptied feels like it's trying to float.

Balsa wood.
Cork.
Pumice — a rock that floats, which feels like a category error.

Things that float and shouldn't: pumice, ice (if you think about it — the solid form of a liquid floating on its own liquid form is actually unusual), certain ants.

Ants float?

Ants raft. Fire ants link together and become a floating mat. Each ant is not buoyant. The structure is.

That's a different thing than floating.

A raft isn't buoyant the way a cork is buoyant.
A raft is buoyant because of its shape.
A steel ship is buoyant because of its shape.
A steel ball bearing sinks.

Same material, different relationship to water, depending on whether it's hollow.

Hollow.
The word sounds like what it means.
So does "thud."
So does "click."
"Hollow" has that back-of-the-throat openness.

Does "dense" sound dense?
Not really.
"Lump" sounds dense.
"Clod."

A clod of earth.
A clod is also an insult — a stupid person. Heavy, undifferentiated.

When did density become a metaphor for stupidity?
"Thick" does the same thing.
"You're so thick."

And lightness became quickness — "light on your feet."

A welding magnet is shockingly heavy. About the size of a deck of cards and it takes real effort to pick it up off a steel table because of the magnet and the weight combined.
