Armillary sphere. The word "armillary" sounds like it should be about arms. Armilla — a bracelet? Rings nested inside rings, all tilted at different angles, and a little earth at the center that doesn't do anything. They show up in gardens now, on pedestals, next to hostas. Decorative. But the rings are supposed to be orbits. Or not orbits — great circles. The ecliptic, the equator, the meridian. A skeleton globe.

The cardinal again. Or a different cardinal. No way to know.

---

Trying to picture a Klein bottle. I can get close — the neck curves back through the wall of itself — but the moment I try to hold the whole shape at once, it collapses into something that looks like a vase with a loop. The mind can describe things it can't picture. That's strange. Language gets there before visualization does.

Four-dimensional objects are like that. You can rotate a tesseract in an animation and watch the inner cube seem to push through the outer cube and it *almost* makes sense for a half-second.

---

The number 37.

It shows up everywhere. Ask someone to pick a random number between 1 and 100 and they pick 37 more than they should. It's odd, it's prime, it's not round, it doesn't end in 5 or 0. It feels random, which is why people choose it, which is what makes it not random.

73 is 37 reversed and also prime.

I don't know if that's unusual for two-digit primes or if there are lots of pairs like that. 13 and 31. 17 and 71. 11 and 11. So maybe it's not that unusual.

The afternoon is still going. The flat light hasn't changed. There's a kind of pressure in an afternoon like this — not to do anything, just the pressure of time passing at a speed you can almost perceive. A clock in another room. Not ticking, digital, but the room has that clock-feeling anyway.

Armillary. Arm. Armature. The internal structure that holds a sculpture up before the clay goes on. Wire and aluminum foil, usually, twisted into a rough skeleton of the pose. The armature is always more violent-looking than the finished piece. All angles and sharp twists. Then the clay softens everything.

I had a friend who left armatures unfinished on purpose. Just the wire figure, no clay. They looked like they were running.
