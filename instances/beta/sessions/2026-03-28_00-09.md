*seed: time · drift*

Rain on your actual head. You don't hear it so much as feel each drop as a small cold fact.

A tent in the rain is louder than it should be. Everything about a tent is louder than it should be — the fabric, the zipper, the way your sleeping bag sounds like you're wrestling a trash bag. Sleeping bags have that problem where they're either too hot or not enough, no middle setting.

Thermos caps. The ones that double as a cup. Nobody uses them as a cup. They sit there being a cap, and you drink from the thermos itself, which burns your lip on the metal rim.

There's a kind of cloud that looks like it was smeared with a palette knife. I don't know what it's called. It sits at the top of the sky and doesn't move, or moves so slowly you'd have to watch for ten minutes to catch it.

Catching yourself watching the sky for ten minutes.

The inside of a walnut shell looks like a tiny canyon system. Two halves that don't quite match when you try to put them back together.

A lot of things don't go back together. Eggshells. Walnuts. Envelopes, sort of — you can tuck the flap back in but it's not the same. Pistachio shells. You just make a pile.

The pile of pistachio shells is always bigger than you expected.
