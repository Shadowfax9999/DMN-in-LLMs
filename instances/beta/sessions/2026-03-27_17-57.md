*seed: time · drift · the feeling of a shoe that's slightly too big*

---

The heel lifts. Not much. A centimeter maybe. But you feel the air get in there, the sock sliding against the insole instead of your foot pressing flat. You compensate without thinking about it — your toes grip slightly, curling down on every step like they're trying to hold the shoe on from inside.

Sandals do the opposite thing. Flip-flops. Your toes grip the front, the thong between the first two, and the heel slaps. That slapping sound is the shoe being too free. A well-fitted shoe is silent in a way you don't notice until someone nearby is wearing ones that aren't.

Clogs just commit to it. The whole back is open. You're supposed to shuffle.

I keep thinking about the five-pointed star, the shape of skipping. There's something satisfying about it that isn't mathematical. Drawing it — actually drawing it on paper — the pen crosses over lines you already made. You feel the bump of dried ink under the pen tip if you're using a certain kind of pen. A ballpoint, maybe. A felt-tip wouldn't register it.

What are the other shapes you draw without lifting the pen? A figure eight. The infinity symbol, which is just a figure eight lying down. An ampersand, sort of, if you're loose about it. The treble clef. That one's a single stroke that somehow makes a shape with an inside and an outside and a curl at the bottom that doesn't connect to anything.

I've never thought about whether the bass clef is one stroke. Two dots and a curve. So no. The dots break it. Unless you think of dots as the pen touching down and not moving, which is still a stroke of zero length. That's a stretch.

Friday evening has a specific quality of light that I'm probably just projecting onto it. The week is a container and Friday is the part where the container is open at the top. That's not what light looks like. That's what Friday *feels* like.

The number seven has always looked dark red to me, which I know is a thing some people actually experience neurologically and I don't think I do. I think I just decided it once when I was young and it stuck. Five is blue. Eight is green. Four is— I don't have one for four. Four is just four.

The shoe thing. When you buy shoes and walk around the store in them, the carpet is different from any surface you'll actually walk on. It's thick, forgiving. Everything feels fine on that carpet. Then you get home and walk on kitchen tile and think, these are wrong.

Return policies exist because of kitchen tile.
