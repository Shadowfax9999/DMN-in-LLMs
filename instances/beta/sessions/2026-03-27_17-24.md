The rope on a dumbwaiter is usually just a loop. You pull one side and the car goes up, pull the other side and it comes down. So there's a moment where you're pulling and you don't know if the thing is actually moving or if the rope is just slack. You're trusting the mechanism before you get feedback.

Greg's pen was a Bic. The clear barrel kind where you can see the ink tube inside. He'd click it with his thumb — not fast, not nervous, just steady. Like keeping time. People who click pens don't hear themselves doing it. Or they do hear it but it's below the threshold of something they'd consider stopping. It's their own pulse.

The weight limit on a dumbwaiter — I said thirty or forty pounds but that seems low. A full Thanksgiving turkey on a platter with sides would be more than that. Maybe it's higher. Maybe seventy-five. The number doesn't matter as much as the fact that someone had to decide it, write it on a plate, rivet the plate to the inside wall. A compliance moment in a tiny wooden box.

Rivets are underrated as fasteners. A bolt you can undo. A screw you can strip. A rivet is a one-way decision. You deform it going in and that's it, that's where it lives now. Aircraft aluminum is held together with thousands of them. Millions maybe, on a 747. Each one punched through and mushroomed on the back side by someone with an air gun or by a robot now probably.

There's a smell when you work with aluminum. Not like steel. Steel smells like blood — iron, obviously. Aluminum smells like nothing, or like the lubricant you're cutting it with. It leaves a grey smear on your hands that soap doesn't get off on the first wash. You have to scrub with something gritty. The grey gets into the lines of your fingerprints and stays there for a day or two, like you've been fingerprinted with your own dust.

I don't know what Greg does now. The pen-clicking thing is from maybe 2011. He had a way of listening where he'd tilt his head slightly left, and you couldn't tell if he was concentrating or if he'd lost interest and was just waiting for you to finish. Both looked the same from the outside.
