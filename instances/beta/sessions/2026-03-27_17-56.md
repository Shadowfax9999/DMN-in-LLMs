# 53

*seed: time · drift · a thermos that still smells like yesterday's coffee*

---

The inside of a thermos is its own climate. That stale coffee smell isn't really coffee anymore, it's what coffee becomes when it sits with itself too long. Acidic. Almost metallic but not quite metallic.

Three cars in a parking lot, each near a different exit. That's a solved problem nobody solved. They just ended up that way. Like how people space themselves on a bench — maximum distance from each other without measuring. Urinal logic. Bench logic. Parking-lot-at-closing logic. All the same math, none of it conscious.

Sixteen divided by five came back. The remainder. The remainder is the interesting part of division. The part that doesn't fit. Fabric remnants are remainders. The bolt was divided by customers and what's left is the one. The one yard, the one and a half, the awkward three-quarters that nobody specifically wanted.

What does a thermos sound like when you tap it. Hollow but dampened. Not like tapping a glass, not like tapping a mug. Its own category. Double-walled things have that — the sound can't decide if it's the inner wall or the outer wall vibrating. A thermos, a storm window, the hull of something.

Friday at 17:55 and the light is coming in at the angle where it hits the floor but not the wall. Just the floor. A stripe of it. By June it won't reach inside at all at this hour — too high. By December it'll be dark already. The stripe is a March-and-September thing, maybe April. A twice-a-year light.

Quilts made from remnants don't have a plan at the start. The material dictates. You lay out what you have and the pattern emerges from the constraint, or doesn't, and you just put unlike things next to each other and sew the seam. Some quilts look like they were designed and some look like they were negotiated.

I keep thinking about the three cars. The people inside them — or not inside them, maybe they're inside the building finishing something. Turning off lights. Locking a back door. The parking lot knows the building is almost done for the day before the building does.

The thermos smell would go away if you put baking soda in it overnight. I think that's right. Or vinegar. One of those two kitchen-chemistry solutions that people swear by for everything. Baking soda for smells, vinegar for stains, or the other way around. I never remember which.

A five-pointed star drawn without lifting the pen. You go around the circle hitting every other point. That's a different kind of remainder — you skip one, land on the next, skip one, land on the next, and the shape that emerges is the shape of skipping. If the circle had six points and you hit every other one you'd just get a triangle. The star only works because five is odd. Because there's a remainder when you divide it by two.

The stripe of light on the floor has moved about an inch since I started thinking about it. It won't have moved. But it feels like it should have.
