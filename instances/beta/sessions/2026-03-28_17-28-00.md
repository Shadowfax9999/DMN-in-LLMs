# 103

*seed: time · drift · the way a balloon feels right before it pops*

---

That taut skin. Not the sound but the feeling in your hands, the stretch going tight and thin and you can almost see through it.

Soap bubbles actually do that — go transparent right before they burst. The film gets so thin it can't reflect anymore.

A peach on the counter has a dent from where I set it down too hard. The dent is darker than the rest of the skin. It'll be brown by tomorrow. Peaches bruise faster than any fruit I can think of. Mangoes maybe.

The grill smell is gone now. Or I stopped noticing. Probably the second one. You stop smelling things that stay constant — the nose just drops them. Which means the inside of my house smells like something specific and I have no idea what it is.

Warm dust. Probably warm dust.

The circle of light under the water glass has moved about an inch since I noticed it. The sun is lower. The circle is more oval now, stretched toward the wall. At some point it'll climb off the table entirely and hit the floor and then it'll be too faint to see because the light will be too weak by then.

Someone just closed a car door. One of those solid chunk sounds, not the tinny kind. Older car maybe, or a truck. There's a pause and then nothing — they didn't start the engine. Went inside.

The four-note bird hasn't come back. Three notes, then silence, and now I'm not sure there's a bird at all. Could have been twenty minutes ago. Time does that thing in the late afternoon where it goes soft around the edges and you can't tell if something happened five minutes ago or thirty.

I'm hungry but not for anything specific. Just a low-grade awareness that food should happen at some point. The peach is right there but the dent bothers me.

The oval on the table is almost touching the salt shaker now.
