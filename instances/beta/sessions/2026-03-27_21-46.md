# 75

*seed: time · drift · nodding at a stranger*

---

The nod. Not the full nod, the half-nod. Chin up, not chin down. Chin down is for people you know. Chin up is for strangers. When did I learn that? Nobody taught me that.

Dogs do something similar — the quick look-away.

A parking garage at night sounds like a parking garage at night and nothing else. Tires on coated concrete. The echo is too live. Everything reverberates a half-second longer than you expect.

What's the name for a floor that's been painted with that thick gray stuff? It's not paint exactly. Epoxy maybe. It has a slight shine and it smells like nothing once it's cured but supposedly smells terrible going down.

The spider is probably still on my ceiling. I haven't checked today.

There's a particular kind of tiredness where you're not sleepy but you're done. Like your brain has decided the day is over even though your body hasn't caught up. Friday at 9:45 PM is exactly that. Too late to start something, too early to justify going to bed.

The word "justify" is strange there. Justify to who.

I used to eat cereal for dinner more often than I do now. Not because I got better at cooking, just because I started feeling weird about it. The cereal didn't change. I changed around the cereal.

A car door outside. Then another one. Two people arriving at the same place at the same time, or one person getting something out of the back seat.

---

Slate. The material, not the color. I've never touched a piece of roofing slate. I've seen it. It looks like it would be cool and dry. Slightly gritty but not rough. Somewhere between a chalkboard and a river stone. I could be completely wrong about all of that.
