# 100

*seed: time · drift*

---

The squirrel thing — the way they rotate food. Like a lathe. The food is the workpiece and the hands are the chuck.

A bezel that fails on a watch lets moisture in. The dial fogs from the inside. You can still read the time but it looks like the watch is embarrassed about something.

There's a helicopter somewhere. Not close. That particular sound where you can hear the individual blade passes but only if you stop and listen for them. If you don't concentrate it just sounds like a sustained hum.

Fence posts. The tops of wooden fence posts are always a different color than the sides. Darker usually, or greener, because water sits there. The flat horizontal surface ages differently than the vertical one. Same wood, same weather, different life.

I wonder how many different ceiling fans I've looked up at. Dozens. Hundreds maybe. They all wobble slightly at certain speeds and then smooth out at others. There's a speed where every ceiling fan in the world looks like it's about to shake itself apart and then you click it up one notch and it's fine.

The charcoal smell is stronger now. Someone opened a lid.

Grout lines in a bathroom. Another bezel situation — the thing between tiles. Except grout is the opposite of a bezel because it's recessed, it's lower than the surfaces it separates. A bezel sits proud. Grout sits back. Both are borders but one presents itself and the other hides.

A kid on a bike just went past with a playing card in the spokes. Haven't seen that in a long time. The sound is higher-pitched than I remember. Maybe the card was stiffer, or the wheel was smaller, or I'm just wrong about how it sounded.

The helicopter is gone. Didn't notice when it left.

Bathroom caulk is a third thing. Not proud like a bezel, not recessed like grout. It sits exactly flush, or tries to. It's the attempt at a seamless transition between two surfaces that are never quite the same plane. And it yellows. Everything white in a bathroom eventually yellows except the porcelain.

The light right now is doing that thing where shadows are long but not sharp. Soft long shadows, which means the sun is low but there's enough atmospheric haze to diffuse it. The shadow of the fence is draped across the yard like fabric that doesn't quite lie flat because of the grass underneath it.

Someone's wind chimes. Three notes, over and over, in no particular order. They never play the same sequence twice but they also never sound random. There's a word for that kind of constrained chaos but I can't think of it.

The squirrel is gone too.
