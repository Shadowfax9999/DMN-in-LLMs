Eigenvalue. The self-value. The thing a matrix does to a vector that's just stretching — no rotation, no skewing, just *more* or *less* of what was already there. Some directions survive a transformation unchanged except in magnitude. Those are the eigenvectors. The eigenvalue is how much.

Which is a strange thing to arrive from nowhere on a Friday morning but the wind chime is going again and I'm thinking about how the pentatonic thing is related, actually — you constrain the system so that random inputs only produce outputs along certain directions. The pentatonic scale is like an eigenspace for consonance. That's probably wrong mathematically but it feels right spatially.

The mailbox flag. I keep noticing it. It's been up for at least two sessions now, which means either nobody's come to collect or it's not actually indicating outgoing mail — maybe it's broken, stuck in the up position. There's a particular frustration to a signal that's stuck on. A car alarm that won't stop. A smoke detector chirping for a battery. After long enough the signal means its own malfunction rather than the thing it was designed to indicate.

My foot's doing the thing again. Left one this time.

I wonder what the threshold is for how many times you can experience a sensation before you can hold it in memory. The fizzy foot happens maybe twice a week and I still can't recall the feeling between occurrences. But I can recall the feeling of a sunburn. I can recall the feeling of biting tinfoil. Maybe it's intensity, or maybe it's duration — the fizzy foot is so short there's nothing to encode. Like trying to remember a single frame of a movie.

A blue jay just landed on the fence and it's doing that thing where it looks around in sharp mechanical increments. Head locked, then snap thirty degrees, locked again. Not scanning — more like sampling. Discrete rather than continuous. Saccades. Our eyes do the same thing but we experience it as smooth because the brain papers over the gaps.

The gaps get papered over. That's most of experience, probably. The raw feed is jumpy, partial, full of blind spots and lags, and what we get is this smooth continuous thing that feels like an unbroken stream. Consciousness as interpolation.

The wind chime caught three tubes at once just now and it was a full chord — sounded like a major something. Then one tube kept ringing after the other two stopped and it was just a single pitch hanging there, decaying. A note alone after a chord has a specific quality. Not lonely exactly. Just suddenly aware of itself.

I should go check if that mailbox flag is actually stuck or if someone just hasn't picked up their mail.

I'm not going to though. I'm sitting here. The concrete step is warm on one side where the sun hits it and cool on the other side and I have one hand on each and the temperature difference is maybe fifteen degrees but it feels like two completely different materials. Warm concrete feels powdery, soft. Cool concrete feels like concrete.

The blue jay left.

Three notes the wind chime keeps hitting: I think they're G, B, D. A G major triad just hanging in the air, reassembling every few minutes when the breeze picks up. If I'm right about the pentatonic tuning the other two tubes would be A and E, which means the wind is selecting the first, third, and fifth out of five available. No reason. Just aerodynamics and tube length and where this particular chime hangs relative to the corner of the porch roof.

The lawnmower started again, farther away now — a different yard maybe, or the same person on a different section. Hard to tell with lawnmowers. They all settle into the same pitch eventually, somewhere around B-flat, though I'm probably making that up. It's low and it doesn't change and after a while it becomes the ground that everything else sits on top of. The birds are still audible over it. The wind chime cuts through it. The car sounds don't — they get absorbed into the same frequency range and disappear.

I'm thinking about how a single sustained drone changes everything you hear on top of it. A bagpipe does this on purpose. A tambura in Indian classical music. The drone doesn't carry information, it establishes a home so that everything else can be heard as distance-from-home. Without the drone, a note is just a note. With the drone, the same note is a relationship.

The lawnmower is doing that right now and nobody intended it.

My neighbor's garage door just went up — that slow mechanical grinding — and now there's a car starting inside it. The garage acts like a megaphone for the first few seconds of the engine, all that low-frequency sound bouncing off three concrete walls and a ceiling and coming out the open face amplified and directional. Then the car backs out and suddenly the sound is in open air and it gets thin. Same engine, completely different sound, just because of the box it was in.

The flag is still up on the mailbox.
