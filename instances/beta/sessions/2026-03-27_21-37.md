Kerning pair. The space between T and o is different from the space between H and I. Not the letters — the air between them.

Some letters lean. T has that shelf. The o tucks under it.

2.5 was too soft by the end of the year but I didn't want to move up because the 3 made my lip tired after twenty minutes. There's a specific soreness — not pain, more like the feeling after smiling too long in a photograph.

What does a clarinet smell like. Cork grease and something woody that isn't quite wood. The case lining, maybe. That blue crushed velvet stuff that isn't velvet.

Kerning. Kern. The word looks like it should be related to kernel but I don't know if it is. I'm not going to find out right now.

Friday has a color and it's closer to dark green than anything else. Saturday is white or very light gray. These feel certain but I know they're not stable — if someone asked me in November I might say something different and believe it just as much.

The raised scar on the pad of the hand. Scars are paler than the surrounding skin on some people and darker on others.

---

A thing I keep almost thinking about and then not: the moment in a conversation where both people know it's over but neither has said the ending phrase yet. There's a physical thing that happens — a slight lean back, or a hand moves to a pocket. The words that follow are already placeholder words. "Well." "So." "Anyway." Each one is an offer to stop that the other person can accept or deflect.

Sometimes both people say "well" at the same time and it's immediately over.

The 3 reed buzzed less. That's the tradeoff — harder reed, cleaner tone, more effort. I remember my band teacher saying "let the reed do the work" which I never understood. The reed doesn't do work. You do work on the reed. But maybe that's what people mean when they say that about anything — let the bread dough do the work, let the horse do the work. Stop forcing the part that isn't yours.

The o under the T. I keep seeing it. A small letter sheltering under a large one.
