*seed: time · drift*

The egg slides or it doesn't. That's true of a lot of things people pretend are gradual.

A putty knife would be useless in a fight. You could threaten someone with one and they'd just look at you. The handle is usually yellow. Cheap plastic, the kind that gets brittle in a cold garage and cracks along the molding seam.

Garages smell like a specific version of cold. Cold concrete and old gasoline and something like dust but heavier. There's always a coffee can on a shelf with screws in it. Different sizes, different threads, all mixed together. Someone put them there instead of throwing them away and now they're a collection.

Friday night and the neighborhood is doing its Friday night thing. More porch lights on than usual. A car door somewhere. Then another one. People arriving places.

The spatula thing — there's a moment with pancakes where you slide it under and you can feel whether the bottom has set. If it hasn't, the whole thing folds. You committed too early. You have to wait for the bubbles to pop on the surface and stay open. Little craters that don't fill back in.

Craters on the moon are named after people. Some of them are named after people nobody remembers anymore. The crater outlasted the reputation.

A hammock would be a strange place to eat a pancake.

The grid pattern on skin from a rope hammock fades in maybe twenty minutes. It's like the skin was keeping a temporary note and then erased it. Corduroy does something similar to the inside of your forearm if you fall asleep on it.

There's a sound a refrigerator makes when it cycles off. Not silence — the arrival of silence. You didn't notice the hum until it stopped. Then five seconds later you've forgotten it again.

Putty itself is strange stuff. It resists you and then gives in. Not like clay exactly. More reluctant. You push your thumb into it and it holds the thumbprint with too much detail, like it's being passive-aggressive about the whole thing.

I've never seen anyone use actual putty for its actual purpose. Just putty knives doing other jobs.
