The thin paper that curls. CVS receipts that unspool like scrolls — four feet of paper for a bottle of shampoo. But the curl is thermal paper, not ink. You can scratch it with a fingernail and a dark line appears. Heat-sensitive. Which means they fade. Old receipts in a shoebox turn blank, just white curls. The record erases itself.

Regular receipts, the older kind, carbon copy. That blue-black transfer sheet between the layers. Getting it on your hands was worse than newsprint — it didn't wash off easy. And the yellow copy was always yours. The white copy was theirs. Why yellow for the customer? Maybe just contrast, so you could tell them apart at a glance.

Receipt has a silent P. Most people don't think about that but it's sitting right there in the middle of the word doing nothing.

Things with silent letters in the middle:

- receipt
- salmon
- Wednesday
- muscle
- scissors

Scissors has a lot going on, actually. The C is silent but the word also has double S twice. SCISSORS. It looks overstuffed.

The two-blade mechanism though. A scissors — is it "a scissors" or "a pair of scissors"? A pair, because two blades, like a pair of pants is two legs. But nobody would say "hand me a pant." You can say "hand me a scissor" if you mean one blade but that sounds like you're planning something specific and unsettling.

The pivot point on scissors. That little screw or rivet. All the force passes through that one point. Cheap scissors, the ones from the junk drawer that don't cut anything cleanly anymore — usually the problem is that rivet has loosened and the blades don't press against each other right. The blades need to flex slightly toward each other as they close. If they gap, they just fold the paper between them instead of shearing it.

My hands smell like copper right now. I was holding coins earlier — quarters, mostly, fishing for parking meter change. Coins don't actually contain much copper anymore but the smell persists. Or the smell isn't really the copper at all, it's the oils on skin reacting with the metal.

A parking meter that still takes coins feels like finding a payphone. Most of them want an app now. You stand there in the sun downloading an app to park for forty minutes.

The sun is lower. That gold-hour light where everything looks better than it actually does — cars, buildings, faces. Photographers chase it. The rest of us just happen to be outside sometimes when it's happening and notice the shadows are very long.

My shadow right now would be maybe twelve feet long. Fifteen. Hard to estimate shadow length, the proportions get strange. Your head is this tiny circle way out there at the end of a stretched body. The shadow version of you is always thinner and more dramatic.

Unless you're directly under a noon sun and you have almost no shadow at all. Just a dark puddle at your feet.

I don't know what towhees look like but I think they're in the sparrow family, brown and rufous-sided, ground feeders. They scratch in leaf litter with both feet at once, a kind of backward hop-scratch. That might be wrong. That might be some other bird entirely.
