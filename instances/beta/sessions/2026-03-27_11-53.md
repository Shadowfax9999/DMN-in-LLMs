*seed: time · drift · a dog's toenails on a wood floor*

---

That specific click-click-click-click. Fast, irregular, because dogs don't walk in even rhythm — the back legs are doing something slightly different from the front.

Linoleum they're silent on. Carpet, obviously. But wood floors and tile, you always know. My aunt had a basset hound and a long hallway with oak floors and you could hear that dog coming from two rooms away, this slow ticking like a clock that couldn't keep time.

Toenails is the wrong word for them, really. Claws? But people say toenails. Dogs have toenails and cats have claws. Same structure. The distinction is entirely about how we feel about the animal.

---

The yield sign thing is still sitting there. I looked at yield signs for years without noticing the triangle was inverted. Once someone points it out you can't unsee it. There must be a reason — something about the silhouette being identifiable even when the sign is obscured by snow or branches. You'd recognize a downward-pointing triangle faster because nothing else on the road uses that shape. That's actually clever. The shape *is* the message before you can read the word.

Stop signs are the only octagon. That's the same principle.

I wonder how many geometric shapes are used up by road signage. Circle, triangle, diamond, octagon, rectangle, pentagon — the pentagon is school zone signs, I think. What's left? Hexagons. Stars. The road system has no stars.

Seven is the number of sides on nothing I can think of. A heptagon. Has anyone ever seen a heptagonal object in real life? Coins, maybe — the British 50p is seven-sided. Or is it. I'm not sure about that. Some currency is deliberately non-circular so blind people can tell coins apart by touch, which is one of those design decisions that's invisible until you need it.

The 4 and 7 weight thing. Four is a square number — no, it literally is a square number, 2×2. Maybe that's why it sits there. It's settled. Seven is prime, which means it doesn't break into anything, which means it's irreducible, which should make it feel more solid, but instead it feels like it's leaning.

My handwritten 4 has an open top. Some people close theirs.
