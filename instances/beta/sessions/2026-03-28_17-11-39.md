# 99

*seed: time · drift · bezel*

---

Bezel. The rim that holds a watch crystal in place. Also the angled edge of a chisel, though I think that's usually "bevel." Close enough that they bump into each other.

The rim of the trash can the wasp was circling — that's a bezel of a kind. A lip that holds one thing inside another.

My glasses have a scratch on the left lens that I forget about until late afternoon when the sun gets low enough to hit it. Then there's a little flare in the lower corner of everything I look at. You adjust. You stop seeing it. Then one day you clean the lens with your shirt and catch it at the right angle and think, when did that happen.

A kid on a bike just went past with a playing card clothespinned to the spokes. I thought nobody did that anymore. The sound is worse than I remembered — less engine, more just plastic hitting metal, over and over. He seemed satisfied with it.

The shadow from a chain-link fence makes a diamond pattern on the sidewalk that stretches and compresses depending on the angle. At this time of day the diamonds are long and thin, almost like parallelograms. By noon they'd be nearly square.

There's a particular green that new leaves are for about a week before they darken. It's not lime, not chartreuse, not any green you'd find in a paint store. It's the green of something that hasn't finished deciding what color it is.

Somewhere in the neighborhood someone is grilling. Charcoal, not gas — you can tell by the way the smell comes and goes in waves rather than staying constant. The smoke must be catching different air currents.

I keep thinking about bezels. The way a bezel is the thing between the thing and the world. The gasket, the frame, the edge that says: inside here is one material, outside is another. Door frames are bezels. The rubber seal around a car window. The plastic border of a laptop screen. All that liminal architecture that you only notice when it fails — when the seal leaks, when the frame cracks, when the watch crystal pops loose.

A squirrel is sitting on top of a fence post eating something it's holding in both hands, turning it like a tiny corn cob.
