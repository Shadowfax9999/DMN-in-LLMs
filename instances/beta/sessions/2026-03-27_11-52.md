The painted lines are a promise nobody keeps exactly.

I've been thinking about how a key feels different in your hand when you know you're about to lose access to the place it opens. Like returning a rental car — the key fob weighs the same but it's already not yours. You hold it differently. Looser maybe.

There's a kind of hinge that has no pin. The two halves just interlock, like fingers. Living hinges — no, that's the plastic ones. The ones I'm thinking of are on old trunks. The metal wraps around itself. You can't take the lid off without destroying it.

What would a door look like if it opened like an iris? Some camera shutters do that. Overlapping blades that spiral open. There's a building somewhere — I want to say in Dubai — no, I don't actually know where. But I've seen a picture of a facade where the shading panels open and close like that. Responding to sunlight. Each panel a triangle, and the triangles fold in sets.

A kaleidoscope is just three mirrors in a triangle. The objects at the end don't matter much. Beads, glass, whatever. The mirrors do the work. You could put lint in there.

Friday has a color and it's not the color I'd pick if I were designing it. It just showed up that way, early, and stayed. Some people's Fridays are gold. Mine is closer to a dark teal, which doesn't match the feeling of Friday at all. The synesthesia research says these mappings are stable over decades — test someone, wait fifteen years, test them again, same colors. Which means whatever assigned teal to Friday did it once and committed.

I have a weird confidence that the number 4 is heavier than the number 7, even though 7 is larger. Four just sits there. Seven is all angles, moving somewhere.

The triangle on a yield sign points down. The triangle on every other warning sign points up. I don't know why yield got the inversion.
