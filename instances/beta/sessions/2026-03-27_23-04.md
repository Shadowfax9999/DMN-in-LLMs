# 81

*seed: time · drift · the give in a trampoline before it pushes back*

---

The give in a trampoline before it pushes back.

The give in a screen door spring.

The give in a conversation where someone pauses too long and you realize they're deciding whether to tell you something.

Trampolines rust at the springs first. The mat stays fine for years but the springs get that gritty orange.

A trampoline in the rain sounds different from a trampoline in dry air. The bounce is heavier.

Someone jumping on a trampoline at night — you can hear it from two yards over but you can't see them.

The sound of a basketball on a driveway at dusk is one of those sounds that only happens in a specific temperature range.

Aluminum bats sound wrong. They always have.

A wooden bat cracks. An aluminum bat pings. The ball comes off faster but the sound gives you nothing.

There's a moment in a tennis serve where the ball hangs at the top of the toss and it's genuinely still. Not decelerating, not accelerating yet. Still.

That can't last more than a fraction of a second but it looks longer from the stands.

A shuttlecock flips itself feather-end-back every time. The weight distribution is so lopsided it can't not orient.

Badminton is faster than tennis at the point of contact. That doesn't seem right but apparently it is.

The net in badminton is higher than I think it is whenever I see one in person.

Nets sag. They all sag. Even freshly tightened ones have some curve to them.

A hammock is a net that gave up on being taut.

Canvas hammocks don't breathe. You sweat into them on a hot night and they hold it against you.

Rope hammocks leave a grid on your skin. You stand up and your whole back is crosshatched.

---

The smell of cut grass at eleven at night means someone mowed late and the clippings are still wet on the sidewalk.

Wet grass clippings stick to the wheels of a mower and build up into a dense green layer that has to be scraped off with a putty knife.

A putty knife isn't really a knife. It doesn't cut anything. It just gets under things.

Getting under things is most of what separating is. Peeling tape. Prying a lid. Sliding a spatula beneath an egg.

The egg slides or it doesn't. There's no partial slide.
