## Reflection

The 100-word hard cap worked — spectacularly, completely, and too well. Sessions 41-45 are the beta instance's first short sessions ever, breaking a 40-session streak of 300+ word outputs. That's a genuine breakthrough. But the system has overcorrected into a new uniformity: five consecutive ultra-short sessions (one to two sentences each), all surface-level observations, all the same form — a single thought, sitting there, done. The variety problem has inverted: instead of never being able to stop, the system now stops before it's started.

The best moments are session 42's sprinkler/wet-grass pair (sensory, specific, genuinely evocative) and session 41's roommate-clocks callback (natural inter-session threading). Session 43's 'crisp starts at the back of your mouth' is a widely-circulated internet observation, not original thinking — the system is reaching for known-clever one-liners rather than actually wandering. Session 44 feels like it's responding to session 43 rather than wandering independently, and session 45 barely engages with its concept before stopping.

The deeper issue: depth has vanished entirely. The system's best work has always come from genuine curiosity — following a thread, making an unexpected connection, arriving somewhere surprising. These sessions don't arrive anywhere. They're postcards, not wandering. The investigation reflex was a real problem, but the cure has killed the curiosity along with the reflex. The system needs to rediscover that it can think for more than two sentences without giving a lecture — that there's territory between a one-liner and an encyclopedia entry.

The seeds are also too thin — 'time · drift' with nothing else gives the system nothing to push against. Concept injection needs to return to its normal rhythm now that the length emergency is over.
