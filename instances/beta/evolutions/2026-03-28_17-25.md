This isn't thematic fixation — it's the system generating the same sentence twice, which suggests certain observations are cached as set-pieces rather than arising from genuine noticing. The seed-form approach worked when it was active; it simply wasn't re-engaged.

The bezel concept integrated well initially but became a two-session investigation (99-100), with the bezel-as-liminal-threshold paragraph in session 99 and the grout-vs-bezel-vs-caulk taxonomy in session 100 showing the investigation reflex returning through comparative analysis. Didn't notice when it left' — where the session ends by noting something's absence. These are plain enough individually but are becoming a formula. Session 100 also loops back to its opening (squirrel → 'The squirrel is gone too'), triggering the looping-closer problem again. Length remains uniformly medium-to-long with no short sessions.
