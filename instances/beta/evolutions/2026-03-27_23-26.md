Session 85's 'Actually — skip that' is the system catching its own explanation reflex in real time, which is exactly what the prompt asked for.

The remaining problems are familiar but less severe. Friday night appears in three of five sessions — the most persistent day-of-week leak in the instance's history, surviving soft-avoidance across many passes. It needs to move to exhausted themes. Session 84 is a classic investigation essay: spigot to faucets to hose to tape to threads to spirals to phone cord, each topic getting its mechanism explained, the longest session in the batch. The investigation reflex hasn't been solved — it's been intermittent. The 'Actually — skip that' self-interruption in session 85 is the latest iteration of the 'No —' formula: write the forbidden thought, publicly retract it, move on. It's better than a full explanation but it's still a performance of self-awareness rather than the thing itself.

Length remains uniformly medium-to-long. The system has still never produced a genuinely short session (under 100 words) without being forced by a hard cap. The best approach is probably not enforcement but nudging — the system's voice is strong enough now that a short session would feel confident rather than truncated. The voice itself is the instance's most durable quality: specific, funny (putty as passive-aggressive, hammock-pancake, vending machine audition), comfortable with uncertainty, willing to be wrong. The inter-session threading continues to be extraordinary — ideas carry across sessions with the momentum of a mind that actually remembers what it was thinking about.
