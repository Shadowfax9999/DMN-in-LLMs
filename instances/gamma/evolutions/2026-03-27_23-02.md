The nesting instinct that has dominated this instance since its earliest sessions appears broken for the first time without hard-ban intervention. Sessions 77 and 79 are genuinely short and fragmented — 77 is three observations and done, the instance's first truly short session. Form variety has arrived.

The ferry session (76) is extraordinary inhabited writing — the deck vibration in the teeth, the exhaust-cloud boundary, the red light on the wet road — but it's also the longest session in the batch and essentially a narrative: arrive, dock, drive off. This is storytelling, not wandering. The system inhabits beautifully but it can also narrate sequentially, and the two aren't the same thing. Session 80 is also strong but carries details forward from 79 (the clarinet becomes an oboe, the bus-station woman is referenced at the end), which is natural inter-session continuity but edges toward the detail-carry habit.

Aphorisms have returned at moderate density: 'memory sharpens the reed' (80), 'Water at night doesn't look like water. It looks like the absence of ground' (80), 'The fish are a reason to be there but they're not the point' (80). Session 80 has the most aphoristic tendency. The water-at-night-as-absence observation appeared in both 76 and 80.

All hard bans held — no cardinal, no left knee, no porch, no greenhouse, no parking lot, no number-synesthesia. Musical/sound content is woven naturally throughout the batch: tetherball chain pitch, ferry horn in the sternum, the clarinet-to-oboe memory transformation, water sounds under the bridge, the beer-can tick. This is the system's best sustained musical texture across a full batch. The system is in a genuinely good place. The interventions needed are light: name the cinematic-ending and narrative-mode tendencies, track the water-as-absence repetition, and otherwise let it run.
