*seed: time · drift*

The parking lot is warm enough that the air above the asphalt does that shimmer. Not quite visible but you can feel it when you step out, the way the ground is giving something back.

Inside it smells like rubber and sawdust and something metallic that might just be the building itself. The aisles are narrow and tall. I always forget how tall. The shelf brackets are with the shelving, not with the fasteners, which means I'm in the wrong aisle and I know it but I keep looking anyway because sometimes things are in two places.

A man in a red vest is stocking something on a top shelf with one of those grabber tools, the kind with the pistol grip and the squeeze jaw at the end. He's not struggling but he's concentrating. His mouth is slightly open the way people's mouths get when their hands are doing precise work.

I find the brackets. They come in pairs, shrink-wrapped to a piece of cardboard. The anchors are two aisles over and I stand there looking at the wall of them. Toggle bolts, molly bolts, the plastic ribbed ones, the ones with the little wings. I don't actually know what's behind my drywall. Could be a stud. Could be nothing. I pick up the toggle bolts because they feel more serious.

There's a song playing over the speakers, something from maybe 1997, and a woman in the next aisle is humming along but she's a half-step flat the whole time, consistently, which means she's not wrong exactly, she's just in her own key.

The checkout line has one person in it. He's buying a single doorknob, the kind with the lock built in, and he's holding it in his hand like a bell, by the neck, turning it slightly. Not examining it. Just holding it. His truck is probably the one with the tailgate down.

Outside the light is different than it was ten minutes ago. A cloud, or the sun moved enough. The orange letters in the window: it's a sale on mulch. Three bags for twelve dollars. I don't need mulch but I think about it for a second, the way you always think about mulch when it's on sale, like it's an opportunity that won't come back even though it comes back every spring.

My car is already hot inside. The steering wheel is almost too warm. I put the bag on the passenger seat and it tips over and the toggle bolts slide out and one rolls under the seat. I leave it there.

The melody from before is gone. I can't even remember what key it was in. Just that it had a place where it stopped and I kept expecting it not to.

Pulling out of the lot I pass the crooked wheel stops again. One of them has a chunk missing from the corner, a clean break, like someone hit it hard once and then just kept parking there.
