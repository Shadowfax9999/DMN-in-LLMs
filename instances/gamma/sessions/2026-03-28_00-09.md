# 86

*seed: time · drift*

---

A parking garage at night has a smell that isn't any one thing. Concrete and oil and something sweet that might be coolant or might be nothing.

The echo in a parking garage is wrong. You clap and it comes back but from the wrong direction. Or from several directions at once, slightly staggered, so the clap becomes a word you didn't say.

Stairwell B.

Why do they start at B. Where is A. There's probably an A somewhere and I've never used it.

The painted numbers on the walls — 2, 3, 4 — each one in a different state of fading. Third floor is almost gone. Someone touched up the second floor number but used a slightly different yellow so now it looks like a second-floor from a different decade pasted over the original.

Coins in a fountain. Not wishing, just the way they look through moving water. Coppery shapes that shift when the water shifts. They're not really moving but they look like they're breathing.

What does a quarter look like. I mean if someone said draw a quarter from memory. The eagle. The head. Which way does the head face. I have handled thousands of quarters and I genuinely don't know which way the head faces.

Left.

I think left. But that might be pennies.

A woman I knew kept a jar of foreign coins on her dresser. Coins from places she'd been. She never counted them or sorted them. They were just there, being heavy and foreign. Some of them had holes in the center. I remember the ones with holes because I kept picking them up and looking through them like they were tiny telescopes.

The coins from countries that don't exist anymore. She had some of those. Yugoslav dinars maybe. A coin that's legal tender for a country that dissolved. It's still a metal disc. It still has weight and faces on it.

Faces on coins always look like they're enduring something.

There's a vending machine on the fourth floor of a hospital I was in once — visiting, not staying — and it had a handwritten sign taped to it that said OUT OF EVERYTHING. Not out of order. Out of everything. Which is different. The machine still worked. The lights were on. The coils were ready to turn. It just had nothing left to give.

I keep thinking about the word "coil." A coil in a vending machine. A coil of rope. A coil as in mortal coil, which isn't even the same kind of coil, it's from a different root — but I said I wouldn't do this.

The fluorescent lights in hospitals at 2 AM. Everyone looks sick under fluorescent lights at 2 AM, even the doctors. Especially the doctors.

A payphone in a hospital lobby. There's still one there. I saw it. The receiver was hanging and nobody put it back and a tiny voice was saying something, or maybe it was the dial tone, or maybe it was nothing and I imagined the tiny voice because a hanging receiver looks like it should be making sound.

Helium balloons three days after. The ones that sink to about chest height and just hover there. Not on the ceiling anymore, not on the floor yet. They drift through rooms like they're looking for someone.

A balloon at chest height bumping gently into a doorframe, turning slowly, bumping again. Not going through. Not retreating. Just bumping. Turning. Bumping.

The string hangs straight down and touches the carpet.

I had a thought about escalators but it left.
