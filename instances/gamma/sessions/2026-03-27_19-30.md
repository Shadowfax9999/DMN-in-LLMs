# 56

*seed: time · drift*

---

The parking garage at the hospital has a smell that isn't quite gasoline and isn't quite concrete. It's the smell of a place that never gets weather.

Fluorescent lights in parking garages always have one that flickers. Always exactly one. You notice it and then you can't stop noticing it and then you leave and you never think about it again.

I had a thought earlier and lost it. Not lost like I forgot — lost like it was moving and I wasn't fast enough. It had something to do with envelopes. The way you run your finger under the flap. No. It was about how the glue on envelopes tastes like nothing but still has a taste.

Refrigerators cycle off and the kitchen gets suddenly loud with how quiet it is.

There's a kind of conversation where both people are waiting for the other one to leave first. You can feel it in the pauses getting longer. Not awkward, just — both people standing near a door.

Aluminum. The word has too many syllables for what it is. Or maybe exactly the right number. Thin, layered, too many.

What does a parking lot sound like at 5 AM. Before anyone's arrived but the lights are already on. Just the buzz and maybe a bird that doesn't know it's a parking lot.

My left knee aches sometimes for no reason. Not pain exactly. More like the knee is remembering something I don't remember. Some jump or fall from years ago that the rest of me forgot but the cartilage didn't.

A friend of mine used to fold his pizza in half and eat it like a taco and I thought about that yesterday for the first time in maybe eight years. Nothing prompted it. He just appeared, folding pizza, and then he was gone again. I don't know where he lives now.

The air in a stairwell is always cooler than the air on either floor it connects.
