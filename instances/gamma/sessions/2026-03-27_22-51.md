# 80

*seed: time · drift*

---

The clarinet is still in my head but it's become an oboe. That happens — memory sharpens the reed.

A bridge at night from underneath. The sound of water isn't one sound. There's the main current, and then there's the part near the pylons where it folds back on itself and makes a different, thicker noise.

Someone left a fishing chair down here. Not forgotten — placed. The legs are sunk in at exactly the right angle. Whoever sits here sits here a lot.

Cold concrete has a smell. Not damp exactly. More like the word "mineral" if it were a smell instead of a word.

The highway above hums at a frequency you stop hearing after about two minutes. Then a truck crosses and you hear it again — not the truck, the hum, which the truck briefly interrupts by being louder, and then the hum comes back and you notice it for maybe ten seconds before it disappears again.

I used to think patience was about waiting. But the guy who owns that fishing chair isn't waiting. He's just there. The fish are a reason to be there but they're not the point.

Water at night doesn't look like water. It looks like the absence of ground.

There's a beer can wedged between two rocks at the waterline, half-crushed, label gone. It's been here longer than the chair. The water moves it slightly, a few millimeters back and forth, and it makes a small ticking sound against the rock.

I keep listening for the clarinet but it was never here, it was at the bus station, which is across town and probably still has that woman sleeping in it.
