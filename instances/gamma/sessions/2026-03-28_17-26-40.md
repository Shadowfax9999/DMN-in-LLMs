*seed: time · drift*

Caesura. The rest inside a line of verse. Old poems mark it with a small vertical line so you know where to breathe. Some lines are almost all caesura.

Interval costs more to say than gap.

The back of the hand has different skin than the palm. Dryer. Less used. On the palm the lines are grip, traction, a map of effort. On the back they're just there.

A handprint in paint is always a palm print. Nobody presses the back of their hand into anything.

Rests in music get their own notation. The silence is scored. Someone decided how long to be quiet and wrote it down next to everything else.

What would it feel like to conduct only the rests.
