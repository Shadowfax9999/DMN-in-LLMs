# 35

*seed: time · drift · a sentence that changes meaning if you stress a different word*

---

I never said she stole my money. *I* never said she stole my money. I never *said* she stole my money. I never said *she* stole my money. Seven different meanings depending on which word you lean on. Or is it seven. I'm counting on my fingers under the blanket. Yeah, seven words, seven stresses, seven meanings. That's unusual. Most sentences break if you try that with every word.

The bird is doing its two notes again. It's higher than the other sounds outside. There's a lawnmower somewhere, not close, maybe two streets over. Friday morning lawnmower means someone's off work. Or retired. The lawnmower sound has that thing where it gets louder and softer as they turn — louder on the pass toward you, softer going away. I can track the rows from bed.

The ceiling has a crack I haven't noticed before. Not a crack exactly, more like a line where two pieces of drywall meet and the tape is starting to show through the paint. It runs almost the full width of the room. I've slept under this line for how long and never looked up at the right time of morning to see it. The light has to be coming in at this specific angle.

I keep thinking about the stress sentence. "I never said she stole my money." The default, with no particular stress, what does it even mean? All seven meanings at once. Or none of them. It's like a chord versus individual notes. The unstressed version is the chord.

My jaw is clenched. I notice it and unclench and then five minutes later it's clenched again. I don't know what time I started doing that. Probably in my sleep. There's a dull ache along the hinge that I only feel now that I'm paying attention to it.

The fitted sheet corner has gotten worse. I can feel the elastic edge bunched up under my lower back. It's not painful, just present. A ridge. If I ignore it long enough it'll either stop bothering me or I'll have to move, and I think the trajectory is toward having to move, which means the perfect position is already over and I just haven't admitted it yet.

The lawnmower stopped. Now it's just the bird and something mechanical — the fridge, probably, humming through the floor. The house makes different sounds depending on which room you're in. The bedroom gets the fridge hum and the water heater clicking. The kitchen gets the settling sounds from the attic.

I'm going to have to move. The sheet ridge is winning. But I'm going to wait one more minute because the light on the ceiling is doing something with the tree shadow outside the window, the branches moving slightly, and the shadow on the ceiling moves with a delay that doesn't make sense — it should be simultaneous. It is simultaneous. The delay is something my eyes are inventing. Some lag in how I'm tracking the branch through the window and then looking up at the corresponding shadow.

My left foot is cold now. I pulled it back under without deciding to.
