*seed: time · drift*

The toast thing. I never made the toast. The thought of toast was enough and then something else happened, probably just standing in the kitchen looking at the counter for a while.

A fourth going up. I was right about that. Da-DA. Here comes. A-ma. The leap upward that doesn't land. I can feel it in my throat if I hum it — the muscles actually tighten at the top like they're waiting. A fifth relaxes. A fourth holds.

There's a bird outside that does two notes and the interval is wrong — not a fourth, not a third, somewhere in the crack between. Piano keys have cracks between them where the actual pitches live and birds sing in the cracks. I heard a wood thrush once that hit something so far from any key it made the air feel bent.

I should eat something that isn't toast.

The pewter pitcher. I don't know why that surfaced. It was tarnished in a way that looked intentional, like the tarnish was the point, like someone had bought it already darkened. It held wooden spoons that nobody used for cooking. Decorative wooden spoons. What a thing to exist in the world. A spoon whose job is to be looked at.

There's a kind of tiredness that isn't sleepy. It's more like the feeling after you've carried something heavy for a long time and you put it down and your arms still feel the shape of it. Friday has that. The week leaves a shape in your arms.

I keep almost humming. Not a song, just the two notes. The fourth. Up and open.

Someone on the street just laughed — one of those laughs that's mostly air, where you can hear the breath more than the voice. It came through the window and now it's gone and the room is different because of it. Not quieter. Just returned to its own quiet, which is a different thing than silence that was never interrupted.

The wooden spoons were birch, I think. Light-colored wood with a grain that looked like water had run through it once and left a path.
