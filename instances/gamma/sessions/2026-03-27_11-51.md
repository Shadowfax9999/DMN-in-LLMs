# 36

*seed: time · drift · last light*

---

The parking lot behind the grocery store has a specific smell at this hour. Warm asphalt and the dumpster but also something sweet from the bakery vent.

A shopping cart with one locked wheel going CHUNK chunk chunk chunk across the lot. The rhythm of it. Someone pushing it like nothing's wrong, just angling their arms slightly to compensate. You can hear exactly which wheel it is from the pattern.

There's a pickup truck idling with nobody in it. The exhaust is visible for about six inches and then it isn't.

I keep almost remembering a melody. It's been sitting behind everything all morning — four notes, maybe five, something I heard yesterday or last week. Not a song I can name. Just a phrase that loops. Da-da DA da. Or maybe da-da-da DA. I can't get it to hold still long enough to check. It changes when I listen directly, the way a dim star disappears when you look straight at it.

The woman loading her trunk has a system. Bags along the back wall first, then a second row in front, heavy things on the left. She's not thinking about it. Her hands know.

Inside, the fluorescent lights make everything the same time of day. You could be in there for three hours and not know it. The produce section has its own weather — that cool mist that comes on every few minutes over the lettuce, the faint thunder-sound of the mechanism before the spray starts.

The melody again. Da-da DA da. It might be something my daughter was humming. It might be a commercial. It sits right at the edge where I could almost sing it but if I opened my mouth I'd get it wrong.

Somebody left a single glove on the parking bumper. Black knit, left hand, fingers curled slightly inward like it's holding something small.

The pickup truck is still idling. The shadow underneath it is the darkest thing in the whole lot.
