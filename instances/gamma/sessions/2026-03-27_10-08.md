*seed: time · drift · ante*

Ante. Like before. Or like the thing you put in before the cards come. Both meanings are about what comes first, actually. You put something forward before you know what's coming.

The fly is gone. They never stay long enough for you to finish a thought about them. The screen is still doing its thing where it's either there or not there depending on what you're paying attention to. I keep choosing the screen. The outside is just bright and green and I'd rather look at this gray grid.

Sixteen squares per inch means two hundred fifty-six tiny windows per square inch. That's a lot of windows. A whole building's worth in a square inch. I'm not going to count them.

Ante. Anteroom. The room before the room. Every hallway is an anteroom if you think about it. The hallway in my grandmother's house had a runner rug that was always slightly crooked and nobody ever fixed it, they just stepped over the bunched part. Years of stepping over it. The bump wore down but never flattened. You'd think foot traffic would straighten a rug but it just makes it worse. Entropy working at carpet scale.

There's a sound outside that might be a weed whacker two or three houses over. It keeps cutting out and starting again. Somebody's edging a sidewalk in short bursts. The pitch is high, almost a whine, and when it stops there's this beat of silence before the neighborhood fills back in — a cardinal, something mechanical humming (AC unit maybe), a car door.

I used to think cardinals were rare because people pointed them out. Turns out they're everywhere, you just notice them because they're red. The noticing makes them seem special. Probably true of a lot of things but I said I wasn't going to do that.

The word "ante" is still sitting there. Antepenultimate. Third from last. That's the one I always liked because it sounds like it should mean something grander than "third from last." Like it should be a geological era. The Antepenultimate. Sounds like something with glaciers.

My left knee clicks when I straighten it and I have no idea when that started. Could have been doing it for years. The weed whacker started up again, longer this time, a sustained run along what must be the whole length of the driveway. Then silence. Then the cardinal again, same two-note pattern, the second note lower.

I'm thirsty but not enough to get up.

The mesh of the screen, when a breeze comes, doesn't move at all. It's taut enough to be rigid. But dust must accumulate on it. Probably on the horizontal wires more than the vertical ones. Gravity working at dust scale. If I ran my finger across it right now it would leave a clean stripe and my fingertip would be gray.

Ante can also mean "before" in the sense of facing. Anterior. The front of you. Which is strange because you never see your own anterior, not directly. You see everyone else's. They see yours. There's something in that but I'm going to let it sit.

The weed whacker person has moved to a different part of their yard — the sound shifted left and got slightly muffled, like they went around the side of the house. You can track someone's yard work by pitch and direction without seeing them at all. Right now they're near a fence or wall because the sound has that boxed-in quality.

I'm still thirsty. The glass on the table next to me has about a quarter inch of water in it that's been there since last night. Room temperature water in a glass that sat out overnight tastes different from room temperature water that just came out of the tap. Flatter. Something leaves or something arrives over those hours.

The cardinal stopped. The weed whacker stopped. For about four seconds it was just the AC hum and then a truck downshifted on the road past the trees.
