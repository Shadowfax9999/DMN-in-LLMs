# 95

*seed: time · drift*

---

A bee doesn't look like it's deciding. It looks like it's being steered.

But then so do most people at a farmer's market. The same landing pattern. Three seconds at the peach table, move on, circle back. What was wrong with the first one.

Hickory is one of those smells that makes you think you're remembering something even when you're not. Like it comes pre-loaded with a memory that isn't yours.

The word "grill" doing two completely different jobs. Metal bars. Cooking. Questioning someone. A car's front face. All of them involve heat or containment or both.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a ceiling. Not metaphorically. It just has a flat bottom, and when the whole sky is that, you stop reading it as sky and start reading it as roof. Which changes the scale of everything underneath it. Trees look like they're indoors.

I used to think cumulus meant "pile" and I was right but I thought I was guessing.

Someone is running a circular saw a few streets over. The sound comes in phrases — four or five seconds of cutting, then silence while they reposition, then another phrase. It has the rhythm of someone telling a story. Sentence. Pause. Sentence. The pauses are where the meaning is.

A dog just walked past with no person attached. Trotting with purpose. Dogs alone on a sidewalk look like they're late for something. Dogs with people look like they're early.

The saw stopped.

A lawn mower somewhere else, though. Farther away. That sound is so continuous it becomes the silence other sounds sit on top of.

I had a yellow shirt I wore every Saturday for about three months when I was eleven. Not on purpose at first. Then on purpose. Then I grew out of it and Saturdays felt wrong for a while.

The cloud ceiling is breaking up in one spot, and the light coming through is doing that thing where it falls in visible columns. Crepuscular rays. The name is worse than the thing.

A cardinal on a wire fence, turning its head in that mechanical way — not smooth, just position to position, like it's clicking through angles.

The mower is getting closer or the wind shifted.
