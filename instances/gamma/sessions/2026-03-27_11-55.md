# 39

*seed: time · drift · calibration*

---

Calibration. Like when you turn the steering wheel slightly left to go straight because the alignment is off and you've just learned to live with it.

The parking lot is still here but I'm pulling out now. That thing where you check both ways and then check again because you didn't actually see anything the first time. Your eyes moved but nothing registered.

There's a song stuck in my head and it's only the bass line. No melody on top. Just the low part walking along by itself like it's waiting for the rest of the band to show up. Da-dum, da-dum, da-da-dum. I don't know what it's from. It might not be from anything.

Driving past a car wash where the big cloth strips are spinning with nobody inside. Just washing air. The blue and red strips look heavy when they're wet.

My left turn signal makes a slightly different sound than my right one. I've never checked if that's true but I believe it completely.

The road has that one spot where the surface changes — you can hear it in the tires more than feel it. Asphalt to concrete. The pitch shifts up. Then back. Every time I drive over it I notice, and every time I forget exactly where it is until I'm on it again.

A pickup truck ahead of me has a mattress in the bed with one red ratchet strap across it. The mattress is bowing up in the middle from the wind. It looks like it's breathing.

Green light but nobody moves for a second. That collective hesitation. Then everyone goes at once.

The bass line is still there. Da-dum, da-dum. It's comfortable not knowing what it belongs to.

I'm passing the place where there used to be a Blockbuster. It's a urgent care now. The parking lot is the same parking lot. Same curb cuts, same little landscaped island with a tree that's too big for it. The tree was probably planted when it was a Blockbuster. It doesn't know the difference.

My hands are at ten and two. No — one hand is at twelve and the other is in my lap. That's how I actually drive. Ten and two is how I describe driving.

The strap on that mattress is getting farther ahead of me now. I can still see the red of it. Then I can't.
