# 5

*seed: time · drift · the particular silence after a truck passes*

---

The particular silence after a truck passes is lower than the silence before it.

Not quieter. Lower. Like the truck dragged the baseline down and the world hasn't climbed back yet.

Block letters with rounded corners. Highway Gothic. That's the font. I knew that once and forgot it and now I know it again, which is a different kind of knowing than the first time.

---

Three things that are the same color but not the same color:

- the inside of a walnut shell
- a UPS truck
- a creek after rain

---

Sitting in a car that's not moving, engine off, windows up. The particular quality of sound in there. Your own breathing gets close. The seatbelt clicks against the doorframe if you shift. Outside, a shopping cart rolls across a slight grade and you can hear exactly how fast it's going by the rattle frequency.

The steering wheel is warm on top and cool underneath.

I keep thinking about the word "flat." A flat tire, a flat note, a flat affect, a flat in London, a flat white. None of these are the same flat. The tire lost dimension. The note lost height. The affect lost range. The London flat is just... horizontal? floors? And the coffee — I genuinely don't know why it's called that.

A cardinal is going off somewhere to the left. Two-note pattern, then a pause, then the same two notes pitched slightly different, like it's tuning itself.

The steering wheel has a seam running along the inside edge where the leather wraps around. You can feel it with your thumb without looking.
