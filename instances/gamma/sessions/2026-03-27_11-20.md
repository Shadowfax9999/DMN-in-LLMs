The clips would leave marks on the fabric probably. Little tooth-marks from the metal grip. Like binder clips but flat. Binder clips are one of those things where the design is just done, there's nothing to improve, it's been the same object for a hundred years. A stapler is like that too but actually staplers have changed, there are ones now that press the staple flat differently so it doesn't snag. The old way curled the legs inward and the new way bends them flat against the paper. I don't know when that changed.

Flat. A flat note on a piano isn't lower than the note, it IS the note, the flat is part of the name. E-flat isn't a broken E. But it sounds like it should be. Like something went wrong. The word "sharp" sounds like it's trying harder and "flat" sounds like it gave up.

There's a cardinal outside somewhere. Not outside where I am but outside in the sense that cardinals are always outside somewhere in late March doing that sliding whistle. The male sits high up and the female is usually lower, in the bushes. I always look for the female first because she's harder to find. Brown-red instead of red-red. If you're looking at a bush and you see a shape that's the wrong color for a leaf, that's her.

Coffee that's been sitting for forty minutes has a specific temperature. Not hot, not cold. The temperature of the room it's in plus some residual warmth. It tastes different at that temperature — you can taste the acid more. Or maybe you just notice it because the heat isn't masking it. I drink it anyway. The mug has a chip on the rim that my lip finds every time no matter how I rotate it.

Chip. Wood chip, poker chip, chip on a shoulder. The poker chip one is interesting because they're not chipped off anything. They're whole objects called chips. Like how a potato chip is a slice but a poker chip is a disc.

My left knee does a thing when I've been sitting cross-legged where the first step after standing feels like the joint needs to catch up with the bone. Not pain exactly. A lag. Then on the second step it's fine. Every time. Only the left one.

There's a house I pass sometimes where someone has put a Little Free Library in the yard but it's been empty every time I've seen it. The door hangs open. The hinge is one of those small brass ones. I keep meaning to put a book in it but I never have a book with me when I pass it. You'd have to plan it, carry the book specifically for that purpose, and at that point it feels like a project instead of a gesture.

The difference between a gesture and a project is whether you have to remember to do it.

Brass tarnishes green eventually. Copper too. The Statue of Liberty was brown when they put it up. That fact feels like it should be more unsettling than it is — this thing we picture as green was copper-colored for years, and people alive then would find the green version wrong.

I'm thinking about how the number 4 looks stable. Triangular base, a crossbar. The number 5 looks top-heavy. 8 looks like it should topple but it doesn't because it's symmetric. 6 and 9 are the same shape and that bothered me as a kid. I remember writing them and having to think about which direction the tail went. The tail. Like they're animals.

A pen runs out of ink in the middle of a word and you bear down harder as if pressure will fix it, dragging the dry ball across the paper, leaving a groove with no color in it. You can feel the letter you were trying to write in the indent if you run your finger over the back of the page.

The back of the page. Most of the time when you flip a piece of paper over there's nothing there and you don't think about it, but the nothing is a choice, someone printed on one side and left the other blank, and the blank side has a different texture because it didn't go through the printer's heat.

I had a teacher who wrote on both sides of the handout to save paper. The ink from side two would show through and make side one harder to read. She didn't seem to notice or she'd decided it was worth it.

The word "handout" for a piece of paper you give to students. Like you're handing out food. There's something slightly charitable about it that doesn't match the actual experience of receiving a worksheet about the Missouri Compromise.

Missouri has that thing where people who live there disagree about whether it's Missour-ee or Missour-uh. The -uh version sounds more like you've been there a long time.

I'm sitting with my back against the headboard and the pillow is folded in half behind me, which is the wrong use of a pillow but the only way to get the right angle for sitting up in bed. The fold always slowly unfolds and you sink an inch every few minutes until you refold it.

There's a water glass on the nightstand with about an inch of water in it from last night and I can't tell if the tiny bubbles on the inside of the glass were there last night or formed overnight. They cling to the glass in a pattern that isn't random but isn't a grid either. Clustered in some spots, absent in others.

The fitted sheet is off the far corner right now.
