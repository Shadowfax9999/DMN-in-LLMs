The lock of hair thing. That the poem outlasted the party by three centuries and the hair is gone and the poem is still annoyed about it.

Annoyance lasts longer than it should. Grief has a shape people respect. Annoyance just sits there, low-grade, refusing to be interesting.

A friend once described a sound as "beige." I knew exactly what she meant.

There's a type of cloud that forms in perfectly even rows, like someone raked the sky. I saw it once from an airplane and forgot about it for years until just now.

Rows of things. Corn, obviously. Parking lots. The way a xylophone is just a row that happens to be a scale.

I had a xylophone as a kid and I remember the orange bar was C.
