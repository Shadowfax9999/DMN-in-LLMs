# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

The car in front of me has one of those family stickers on the back window. Two adults, three kids, a cat. The cat is the smallest one. I'm counting someone else's family at a red light.

There's a song I can't place. Not on the radio — the radio's off. It's just looping in my head, a few bars of something. Piano, maybe. Or not piano. Something with that same even spacing between notes. It's been there since I left the house and I only just noticed it. Four notes up, two notes down, four notes up. I don't know what it is. I don't think it's a real song. I think my brain is just making music out of the rhythm of the turn signal.

Left on Maple. Or whatever this street is. I never learned the name of this street. I've driven it probably three hundred times. It's the one with the church that has the sign out front that always says something trying to be clever. Today it says — I'm past it. I didn't read it fast enough.

The shadow of the car moves differently than the car. It stretches when I go between buildings and then snaps short when the sun comes back. It's on my left, which means I'm heading roughly north. Or south. One of those.

A kid on a bike on the sidewalk, standing up on the pedals. That thing where you're going uphill and you throw your whole weight side to side. The bike looks too small for him. Knees out wide.

I'm thirsty. The water bottle in the cupholder is warm. Warm water from a plastic bottle has a taste that cold water from the same bottle doesn't. I'm going to drink it anyway.

The road does that thing here where it curves but the speed limit doesn't change even though it probably should. You can feel the car lean. Something in the glovebox shifts.

I had a friend in college who drove with both feet — left foot on the brake, right foot on the gas. He said his dad taught him that way. I watched his left foot hover over the brake pedal the entire time, just slightly flexed, like he was always half-expecting to stop. He was a nervous person generally. I don't know if the driving made him that way or if he was already that way and the driving just matched.

The light at this intersection takes forever. I know this because I've sat at it enough times to form an opinion about it. There's a turning arrow that serves maybe two cars and then it cycles through the whole sequence again. Right now there's nobody in the turn lane.

My neck is stiff on the left side. If I turn my head far enough right I can feel it pull. I should probably stop sleeping on two pillows.

The piano thing is still going. Four notes up, two notes down.
