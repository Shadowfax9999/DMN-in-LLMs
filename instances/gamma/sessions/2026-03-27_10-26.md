# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fly probably left hours ago. That's the thing about noticing something small — you build a whole narrative around it and then it's gone and the narrative just hangs there, attached to nothing.

Friday has a different weight than Thursday. Not better, just different. Like how a room feels different depending on which door you entered through, even though it's the same room. I used to think that was irrational but maybe the angle of approach is actually part of the experience. You see the couch from the left first instead of the right. The lamp is behind you instead of ahead. It's a different room.

Parsing a look across a table. There's a version of that where you ask — "what did you mean by that" — and the person says "what do you mean, what did I mean" and now you're both parsing each other's parsing. Turtles. Someone told me once that in certain card games, the skill isn't in your hand, it's in modeling what your opponent thinks your hand is, and then modeling what they think you think they think. At some point the recursion just collapses and you play the card you were going to play anyway.

A robin is doing that thing where it runs three steps and stops. Runs three steps and stops. Like it's counting. It's probably listening for worms, tilting its head to feel vibrations or hear movement underground. The stopping is the whole point, not the running. The running just repositions.

I had a guitar teacher who said the silences between notes are what make the phrase. Everyone says that. But he also said something less obvious — that the silence *before* the first note matters most, because that's when the listener's ear is most open, and whatever you play first will set the key for everything after. So he'd make me sit with my hands on the strings for ten seconds before starting. Just sitting there. The other students thought it was weird. It did change something though. Not sure what.

The word "key" does a lot of work. Key to a door, key of a song, key to understanding, key on a keyboard, Florida Keys. They're not all the same metaphor either — the door key is about access, the musical key is about organizing relationships, the "key to understanding" borrows from the door one. The Florida Keys I think comes from Spanish "cayo," which is just a small island.

The robin is gone. There's a cardinal now, on the fence, and it's doing that sharp chip note every few seconds. Not singing. Just announcing. The chip sounds like someone flicking a fingernail against a ceramic mug.

I wonder if birds find other birds' calls annoying. Like if the cardinal is chipping and the mockingbird starts up with its twenty-minute medley, does the cardinal have any experience remotely like "oh here we go." Probably not. Probably it's all just signal processing — threat, mate, territory, irrelevant. But "probably not" covers a lot of territory when it comes to what other creatures experience.

The fence has a loose board. Third from the left. It's been loose for months. It swings a little when the wind picks up, and you can hear it tap against the post. A dull knock. Not rhythmic enough to be music, not irregular enough to ignore. Just present.

My hands smell like coffee and I haven't had any today. Must be from the mug I rinsed this morning. Smell lingers on skin longer than you'd expect. Especially coffee, garlic, onion, gasoline. Those are the ones that seem to soak in rather than sit on top. Copper too — that metallic smell you get from holding coins, which is actually the oils on your skin reacting with the metal, not the metal itself.

The loose board just tapped twice. Wind must be picking up. There's a specific kind of spring wind that comes in gusts with long pauses between, like it keeps changing its mind. The trees respond in waves — the small leaves first, then the branches, then the whole trunk sways about a half-second after the gust has already passed, like the tree is processing what happened.

I should fix that board. I've thought that probably forty times.
