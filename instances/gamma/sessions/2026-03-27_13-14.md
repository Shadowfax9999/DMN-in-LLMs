# 45

*seed: time · drift · the feeling of a escalator that isn't moving*

---

The escalator thing is real though. Your legs expect it to move and when it doesn't there's a lurch, a wrongness in your ankles. Even when you can see it's stopped. Even when you watched the person ahead of you walk up it like stairs. Your body doesn't believe your eyes about this one specific thing.

A parking garage has that same temperature. Always the same temperature. Summer, winter. Cool concrete breath.

I'm thinking about the geese. How they tuck one leg up and just stand there on the other one, on ice sometimes, and it looks like it should hurt but they have that counter-current blood flow thing in their legs so

no — I said I wouldn't do that.

They just stand there. One leg. On ice. Fine with it.

There's a song stuck in my head but it's only the bass line. Four notes going down and then back up. I don't know what it's from. It might not be from anything. It might be something I'm making up right now and mistaking for a memory.

The reservoir road has that one spot where the guardrail is dented inward and there's always a little glass on the shoulder. Every few months someone sweeps it and then it comes back. Not the same glass. New glass. Same spot.

A friend of mine used to say "I'm going to go be horizontal" instead of "I'm going to go lie down" and I think about that probably once a month.

Somewhere a lawn mower. Not close. That drone that could be three blocks away or six. The sound equivalent of not being able to tell how far away a mountain is.

The geese have moved. They're in the water now, in a loose group, and one of them is doing that thing where it tips forward and its whole back end points straight up. Like a periscope in reverse.

I had a friend in college who could whistle two notes at once. Not like a chord exactly. More like one note was the shadow of the other. He couldn't explain how he did it. He said he just made his mouth smaller in the back. Nobody could replicate it. He'd do it at parties and people would try and it would just be regular whistling, and he'd shrug.

The four-note bass line is still going. Down down down down, up. It's in a minor key. It sounds like it belongs to a song from the early 2000s but I genuinely cannot place it.

Sometimes I think about how a vending machine hums at a specific pitch and if you stood in front of enough of them you could find one that hums the note you're thinking of.

The water has a ripple now. Just one line of it crossing the whole surface, like something underneath drew a finger across it.
