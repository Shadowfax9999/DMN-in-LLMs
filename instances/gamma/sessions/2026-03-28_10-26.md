# 91

*seed: time · drift*

---

The cardinal again. Always sounds like it's asking something and then immediately asking it again louder.

A shopping cart in a creek bed. I saw one once, half-submerged, the handlebar just above the water line, and there were leaves collected in the basket part, a perfect little dam. Water bending around the wheels.

How does a shopping cart end up in a creek. Someone pushes it there. Or pushes it to the edge and it rolls. Either way there's a moment where a person is standing near a creek with a shopping cart.

Parking lots that border woods. There's always a strip of land between the asphalt and the tree line — six feet of gravel and cigarette butts and one traffic cone. Not the parking lot. Not the woods. The DMZ.

A woman I worked with years ago kept a photo of her dog on her desk but no photos of her family. I never asked about it. You don't ask about the photos that aren't there. The dog was a beagle, or something beagle-adjacent. Brown ears.

The phrase "beagle-adjacent."

Toast smell travels farther through a house than any other cooking smell. Coffee is close but toast gets to the second floor faster.

I once spent an entire afternoon trying to remember whether the word "penultimate" means second-to-last or third-to-last. It means second-to-last. I knew that. I've always known that. But for about four hours I couldn't be sure.

Alan. Still gone.

The feeling of a name you can't retrieve is so specific. It's not blank. It's a shape with no surface. You can feel the outline of where the name would go. Two syllables, maybe. Started with — no. Gone.

Geese are loud when they're overhead but quiet at a distance. There's a range where you can see them flying in formation but can't hear anything and they look like a screensaver.

Saturday has a particular kind of quiet that isn't about volume. The traffic is the same. The birds are the same. But the quiet is there, in the way the neighborhood holds itself. Fewer engines starting at 7. Garbage cans still at the curb from Thursday.

The shopping cart in the creek would be rusted by now. It's been years. The whole basket is probably silted in. Fish using it for cover, maybe. A shopping cart becoming reef.
