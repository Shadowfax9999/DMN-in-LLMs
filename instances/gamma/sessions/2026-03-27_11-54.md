# 38

*seed: time · drift · the room where coats pile up at a party*

---

The coat room. Not a room usually. A bed. Somebody's bed with thirty coats on it and you have to dig for yours and you touch other people's coat linings, which feels like a small violation, the silk or the nylon that sits against someone else's back all day.

Parking lot of a dentist's office but not mine. Just parked here to look at my phone. The building across the street has a flag I can't identify. Three horizontal stripes. It's not moving.

A melody keeps starting in my head — just the first four notes of something. Over and over. I can't get past the fourth note. It wants to resolve up but I keep losing it. Like reaching for something on a high shelf and your fingers just brush it.

There's a woman walking a very small dog on a very long leash. The geometry of it. The dog is ten feet ahead and keeps looking back, checking, and the woman is looking at her phone. Two animals connected by a line, each attending to something else.

I sat in a car like this once in a parking lot in — was it Tulsa? — eating a gas station sandwich and watching a man pressure-wash the sidewalk in front of a closed store. The water was black coming off. He was methodical. Left to right, stepping backward. The clean concrete behind him looked wet and new. I ate the whole sandwich watching him. I don't remember what kind it was.

The four notes again.

My knee against the steering column. The parking brake has a texture on its button, little raised dots, and I'm pressing my thumb into it without thinking. How long have I been doing that.

The flag moved. Blue white green. Still don't know.

Someone left a shopping cart in the middle of the lot, just standing there with one wheel turned sideways. It looks patient. No — it just looks like a shopping cart in a parking lot.

The four notes want to be the beginning of something by Debussy but I don't think they are. They might just be four notes my brain assembled from nothing and is now convinced it recognizes. A false memory of a melody.

The small dog is squatting now. The woman is still looking at her phone. The leash makes a long curve between them like a suspended bridge that carries nothing.

I should leave. I have no appointment here. I'm just a person in a parked car in a lot that doesn't belong to me, pressing dots into my thumb.

The pressure washer guy in Tulsa had headphones on. I remember that now. Big over-ear ones. So he was listening to something while the water roared. His own four notes maybe.
