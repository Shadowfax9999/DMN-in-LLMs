# 1

*seed: time*

---

The number 7 looks sharp. 8 looks soft. This isn't synesthesia, it's just— something about the topology. 7 has that angle. 8 is all curves closing back on themselves.

4 is stable. 4 legs on a chair. But 3 is more stable structurally, triangles being what they are.

---

Sitting in a car that's still running in a driveway. Not yours. Waiting for someone who said "one second" four minutes ago. The radio's on but between songs — just a DJ's voice at a volume where you catch cadence but not words. The steering wheel is warm from the sun on one side and cool on the other.

There's a cat under the car across the street, just the eyes and ears visible beneath the bumper.

The seatbelt warning dings once, gives up.

---

Thresholds. Doorways. That thing where you walk into a room and forget why you came in. There's research on that — doorways as event boundaries, the brain filing one scene and opening a new one. But the experience of it is stranger than the explanation. You're standing in the kitchen holding nothing, and the intention is just *gone*, like it belonged to the room you left.

---

Marjoram. Oregano. How similar they smell until you really don't think they do.
