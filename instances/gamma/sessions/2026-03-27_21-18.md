# 70

*seed: time · drift*

---

The guitar player stopped. Just now, or a minute ago. Hard to tell because the silence filled in so gradually, like water finding level.

Buttons in a cookie tin. My grandmother's was blue. Royal Dansk. Every house had one and nobody ever bought them. They just appeared, already full of buttons, already old.

What I remember about buttons: the ones with fabric still threaded through the shank. Little flags of shirts that don't exist anymore.

The crack is still there. I keep not looking at it and then looking at it.

There's a kind of tired where you're not sleepy, you're just done being the person who was awake all day. The day used its version of you up and hasn't issued a new one yet.

A moth just hit the window. That soft papery thud. Then again. Then nothing for a while. Then again.

I used to think patience meant waiting without minding. Now I think it might just mean waiting.

The fridge compressor kicked on. It hums in something close to B-flat — not the note exactly but the neighborhood of the note. A whole kitchen organized around that one pitch. The ice maker clicks and settles inside it. Two rhythms that have nothing to do with each other, running in the same box.

My feet are cold. Just the toes, actually. Just the left foot's toes.

The moth again. Or a different moth. No way to know. It doesn't matter but I noticed myself wondering, which is its own small thing.

Four chords and then the first one again. I can almost hear it still, the shape of where it went. The not-resolving was the point. You stay in the wanting-to-arrive. Some songs are about the destination and some songs are about the lean.

The ceiling fan is off but it moved just slightly. Some draft I can't feel from here.
