*seed: time · drift*

Lost them and now I'm trying to get them back, which is exactly how you guarantee they won't come back. Like trying to remember a dream by concentrating. The concentration is the thing that pushes it away. You have to look at it sideways.

The fridge just kicked on. That hum. It's always there and then you hear it and then it's always there in a different way. The noticing doesn't add anything to the sound but it adds something to the room.

A fly keeps landing on the same spot on the window. Leaves. Comes back. Same spot. I don't know what it's getting from the glass. Warmth, maybe. Or it's not getting anything, it's just a fly doing fly things and I'm watching it like it has a project.

There was a piano teacher on my street growing up and in the summer with the windows open you could hear the same piece played wrong forty different ways across an afternoon. Each kid found a new way to stumble at a different place. And then one kid would play it through, clean, and it sounded like a different piece entirely. Not better, exactly. Less interesting. The stumbling versions had this quality of reaching. The clean version had already arrived, so there was nowhere for it to go.

I keep thinking about the phrase "nowhere for it to go" and whether that's true about anything. A puddle after a rain. It's just sitting there. But it's evaporating. It's soaking into the ground. It's going somewhere, just not anywhere you'd call a destination.

Destination is a word that assumes you meant to end up there.

My left knee aches in a way that's not quite pain. More like the knee reminding me it's a knee. A joint asserting its joint-ness. It's been doing this since I was maybe thirty. Not every day. Just when the weather does something, or when I've been sitting too long, or for no reason at all. The body keeps its own calendar.

Somewhere a lawn mower. It's far enough away that it's pleasant. There's a distance at which a lawn mower becomes almost musical — just a sustained drone with slight variations as it turns or hits thicker grass. Like a bagpipe with no melody line. Closer than that and it's just noise. Farther and you can't hear it. There's this narrow band where it works.

I had a friend who collected tuning forks. Not as a hobby she talked about. You'd just open a drawer at her house looking for a pen and there'd be six tuning forks rolling around. Different sizes. She'd pick one up and strike it against her knee and hold it to your ear and say "A" or "B-flat" and you were supposed to appreciate something. I never knew what I was supposed to appreciate. The purity, maybe. A note with nothing else in it. No timbre, no texture. Just the frequency and its overtones. It sounded lonely to me. A sound with no context.

She moved to Tucson. Or Tempe. One of the T cities in Arizona.

The lawn mower stopped and now the silence has this raw quality, like skin under a bandage.

I'm hungry but not for anything specific. Just a low-grade wanting that hasn't attached to an object yet. If I open the fridge I'll stand there and nothing will look right and I'll close it and still be hungry. The hunger isn't in my stomach, really. It's more like restlessness that's settled into the area where hunger lives.

A cardinal just landed on the fence. Sat there. Flew off.
