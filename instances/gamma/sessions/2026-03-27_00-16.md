# 15

*seed: time · drift · manhole*

---

Manhole.

The cover weighs more than you'd think. Cast iron, maybe 250 pounds.

Why "man" hole. There's probably a push to rename them. Utility access point. Maintenance hatch.

The pattern on top — those bumps. So you don't slip.

Some cities have custom ones. Japanese ones with cherry blossoms.

---

Things that are heavier than they look:

- manhole covers
- a wet towel
- a small child who's fallen asleep
- a gallon of milk held straight out from your body
- sand

Things that are lighter than they look:

- pumice
- a tuba (no, actually, those are heavy)
- styrofoam coolers
- dead branches still on the tree
- meringue

I've never held pumice.

The word "pumice" sounds like it should be a verb. *I pumiced the whole afternoon.*

---

Friday at 12:15 AM is an odd time. Not really Friday yet. Thursday wearing Friday's name.

Who decided the day turns over at midnight? Midnight is the middle of a stretch, not a boundary. 3 AM would make more sense. Or sunrise. Some calendars did start at sunset.

The parking garage at 12:15 AM. That one car on the third level that's been there since Tuesday.

---

A rubber band on a doorknob.

Why is it there. Someone put it there for a reason and the reason is gone and the rubber band stayed.

Most of what's in a junk drawer is like this. Evidence of solved problems. The Allen wrench from a specific IKEA shelf. The spare key to a lock you changed.

A battery by itself — you don't know if it's dead or full and there's no way to tell by looking.

Some 9-volts you can touch to your tongue.

The taste is not exactly a taste. More like a feeling that happens to be on your tongue.

---

Alan Shepard hit a golf ball on the moon. One-handed, in a space suit. It probably didn't go as far as everyone says.

The flag up there has probably been bleached white by now. UV with no atmosphere. So there's a white flag on the moon. A surrender flag, accidentally.

The moon tonight — I don't know what phase it's in.
