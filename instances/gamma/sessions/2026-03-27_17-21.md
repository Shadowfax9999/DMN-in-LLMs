The feeling of forgetting a proper noun. Not the word — the space where the word was. You can feel its syllable count. You know it starts with a voiced consonant. You can almost feel the shape your mouth would make.

Three syllables. Starts with a B. Or an M.

It's gone.

Coasters. Why do some people use them and some people don't. It's not really about the furniture. It's a disposition.

The smell of a basement that hasn't been opened in a while — not mildew exactly, more like cold dust, if dust had a temperature.

I keep thinking about how when you pour water into a glass that already has water in it, there's a sound that rises in pitch as the glass fills. Continuously. No steps.

Belinda. That was the name.
