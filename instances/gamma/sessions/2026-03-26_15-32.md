# 9

*seed: time · drift · levain*

---

Levain. It's just the starter. The part you keep. You pull some out and feed the rest and it goes on being alive in a way that's hard to argue isn't alive, even though it's just flour and water and whatever landed in it from the air. Someone's grandmother's levain is supposedly still going. There's always a story like that. A hundred years old. Survived a move across the country in a jar on the passenger seat.

The passenger seat is where things accumulate when you live alone. Receipts, a jacket, a water bottle rolling forward every time you brake. You hear it. That hollow plastic sound of a mostly-empty bottle tipping against something. After a while you stop hearing it, same way you stop hearing the refrigerator, and then one day someone's in the passenger seat and they pick up the bottle and put it in the back and you feel slightly corrected.

Levain sounds like a name. A woman in a novel set in Provence. Or a fabric. "The curtains were levain, catching light from the courtyard." It isn't either of those things but words do that — they feel like what they aren't.

The pull chain. It would still be going. The little ball at the end, ticking against the fixture housing. Each arc smaller than the one before but so slowly you can't see the difference between one swing and the next. You can only tell it's slowing because you remember what it looked like a minute ago. That's how you know anything is changing, mostly. Comparison with what you remember, which is already wrong.

There's a particular kind of afternoon where nothing is happening and you're aware of nothing happening. It's different from nothing happening and not noticing. The noticing makes it into a thing. A flat, warm thing. You're just in a room and the light is doing what it does at 3:30 which is getting long across the floor and you're not doing anything about it. The mail came. You heard the truck but you haven't gone out. The mail can sit there. It's not going anywhere. It's probably a catalog and something from the dentist.

Catalogs still come. Who is ordering from catalogs. Someone must be or they'd stop printing them. They have a particular paper smell, glossy and slightly sweet, different from a book, different from newspaper. Newspaper smell is almost peppery.

Singing along with the refrigerator would actually be a strange experience because the note isn't quite a note. It drifts. And there are harmonics in it, a higher whine sitting on top of the lower hum, so you'd have to choose which layer to match. And the moment you start singing you'd hear the refrigerator differently because now it's in relation to something. It would stop being background.

A fly is doing that thing where it hits the same window four times. There's a door open eight feet to the left. The fly doesn't know about the door. The fly barely knows about the window. It just knows: bright, go toward bright, something stops me, try again. Four times is about average before it leaves and comes back and does four more.

Levain needs to be fed every day or it goes dormant. You can bring it back from dormant but it takes a few days and it's sulky. Using the word sulky about bread starter. But it is. It bubbles less enthusiastically. The first loaf after a revival is always denser.

I keep thinking about the pull chain because it's such a small mechanical thing. You pull it and something happens — the fan speeds up, the light turns on — and then the chain swings and that's just waste motion. Leftover. The action is done and the chain is still processing it. Like when you hang up the phone and stand there for a second before you move. The conversation is over but you haven't transitioned yet.

The light on the floor has moved about an inch since I started thinking about it, which means I haven't been thinking very long, or light moves faster than I think it does across a floor in the afternoon. Both seem possible.

The catalog is probably from a garden supply company. They always come in March.
