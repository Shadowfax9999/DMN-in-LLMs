The pool at the community center smelled like a headache. Not chlorine exactly — chlorine plus something sweet, the way the vending machine air mixed with it near the door. The water was too warm. You'd get in expecting relief and instead it was like getting into someone else's bath.

I keep almost remembering a phone number. Seven digits. It starts with 4. It might be a dentist's office from twenty years ago or it might be nothing, just a shape my mind wants to complete.

Cardboard gets soft in a specific way when it's been rained on and then dried. Not wet-soft. After-wet-soft. It tears like cloth.

There's a thing that happens in hotel hallways where you can hear a TV through the wall but you can't tell what show it is and your brain keeps trying to resolve it into words. You walk past and it stays at the same almost-language. Sometimes you catch a laugh track and that's worse because now you know it's a sitcom but you still can't

No — the pool. The tiles around the edge were that blue-white that exists nowhere else. Dental offices have it too, a different version. A color that means institution but tries not to.

Somebody is grilling outside somewhere. I can't see it but the smell has that specific two-part structure — first the charcoal, then the meat, and they arrive separately even though they're coming from the same place.

Alan, I think his name was. He could do a thing where he'd slap the surface of the water with a flat palm and it sounded exactly like a gunshot. We were maybe nine. He did it once during adult swim and they made him sit out for fifteen minutes and he just sat there on the tile with his legs straight out, not upset, just waiting. Patient in a way that was unusual for a kid.

The number might be 482-something.

I don't know what happens to people like Alan. Not in a sad way. Just — there are hundreds of people I spent real time with and I have no mechanism for knowing where they are. Some of them are in the same city. Some of them are dead. The distribution is unknown to me and will stay that way.

The grill smell is gone now. It passed through like a single sentence of a conversation heard from a car.
