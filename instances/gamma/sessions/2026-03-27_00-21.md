The inside of a closed piano.

All those hammers waiting in a row, tilted back at the same angle, like an audience that just sat down.

Felt on the hammers wears thin in the middle octaves first because that's where most music lives.

What does the inside of a stapler look like when it's out of staples. Just a metal trough. A tiny empty bobsled run.

The spring that pushes the staples forward — when you load a new strip it compresses, and then it spends the next two hundred staples slowly relaxing.

Two hundred seems like too many. Fifty? I've never counted.

Nobody has ever counted how many staples are in a strip while loading a stapler.

Someone has. Someone on the internet has absolutely counted and posted about it.

How a straw works. You don't suck liquid up. You reduce pressure at the top and atmospheric pressure pushes the liquid up from below.

This means a straw wouldn't work in a vacuum.

This means astronauts drink from pouches.

Gruntled. Kempt. Reckful.
