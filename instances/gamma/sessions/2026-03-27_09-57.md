# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

Wale. Whale. One letter.

The measuring tape was yellow but some of them are white. The white ones get dirty and you can't read the numbers past about 40 inches.

Pinking shears — why "pinking." The edges they make look like teeth. Small triangular teeth.

A tomato pincushion always has a little strawberry hanging off it on a string.

What is the strawberry for.

---

Friday feels like a color and the color is orange. Not bright orange. More like the orange of a manila folder.

The number 7 is also orange but a different orange.

4 is red. 8 is blue. These aren't decisions.

---

Socks in a drawer that don't match anything. One of them. Just one. You keep it for months thinking the other one will show up.

The inside of a dryer vent.

Lint is always blue-gray no matter what color the clothes were.

---

Hotels where the air conditioner is under the window and it has two settings: loud and off. You sleep with it on loud because the silence is worse. Then at 3 AM it cycles off and the silence wakes you up.

The curtains in those rooms are always heavier than they need to be.

The bedspread is tucked so tight you have to yank it out from the bottom and the whole thing comes untucked at once like peeling something.

There's a lamp between the beds that no one has ever turned on.

---

Trying to remember someone's name and it's right there. Right there. You can feel its shape. Two syllables. Starts with — no. You had it. You had it and then you thought about having it and it left.

The name shows up at 2 PM the next day while you're doing something else.

---

Rubber bands get old. They get flat and they crack when you stretch them and they leave a sort of powder on your fingers.

The mailman used to drop rubber bands on the sidewalk. Red ones. They were everywhere for a while, in every crack in the concrete.

Concrete cracks in straight lines mostly. Not always but mostly.

The tar they fill the cracks with is shinier than the concrete around it and soft in the summer.
