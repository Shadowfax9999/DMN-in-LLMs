# 27

*seed: time · drift · palate*

---

Palate.

The roof of a mouth. Press your tongue up and there it is — ridged in the front, smooth further back. You do this a hundred times a day swallowing and never notice the texture.

Also: palette. Also: pallet.

Three words that sound the same and have nothing to do with each other. Or — no, I said I wouldn't do that.

---

The parking garage is cold for a spring morning. Concrete holds overnight temperature longer than air does.

---

Things I can hear right now, if I were here:

1. The fluorescent tube
2. A car somewhere below, tires on the turning radius
3. The tick of my own engine cooling
4. My breathing
5. A phone vibrating in the cupholder

---

The tick of an engine cooling. Metal contracting. It speeds up at first and then slows down, which seems backward but isn't — the first few minutes after you turn off the car, the temperature is dropping fastest.

I explained that. Fine.

---

Da da da-da.

It's going to bother me. Four notes, descending, the second and third close together. Could be anything. Could be nothing — just a pattern the brain made out of the garage's ambient sound and handed to me like it was a melody.

---

The cupholder in this car has a rubber insert with six little fins that flex inward to grip whatever you put in them. A narrow water bottle, a wide coffee cup, a can. The fins are always slightly sticky. They collect grit in the grooves between them.

---

My left foot is doing the thing where it's braced against the footrest even though the car is in park and I'm not going anywhere. The calf is slightly tensed. Has been for minutes probably.

Let it go.

It doesn't let go. You can't unflex a muscle by thinking about it. You flex the opposite one instead. Pull the toes up. Now the calf releases.

---

Level 3 of a parking garage at 10:50 on a Friday morning is mostly empty. The cars that are here have been here since 8 or 8:30. They're settled. Windshields already dusty with the fine concrete powder that's always falling from parking garage ceilings.

I'm early for something.

---

Da da da-da. Da da da-da.

It repeats. It's just going to live in my head now.

---

The steering wheel leather is warm on the top where the sun comes through the windshield and cool on the bottom where it's in shadow. Half and half. If I put both hands on it at ten and two I'd feel both temperatures at once.

---

The thing I'm early for doesn't matter. Being early is its own specific state. You've done everything you need to do to be where you need to be, and now there's this remainder. Ten minutes, twenty minutes. Too short to start anything. You just sit.

---

A pigeon just landed on the concrete barrier at the edge of the level. It walked three steps and stopped. Walked two more. Stopped. Pigeons never walk in a straight line for more than about four steps.

---

The phone vibrates again. I don't look at it.

---

The ridges on the front of the palate. There's a word for those. Rugae. They help you grip food against the roof of your mouth so the tongue can move it around. Cats have much more prominent ones.

---

Da da da-da.

It might be the first four notes of "Somewhere Over the Rainbow." No — that's an octave leap. This is stepwise. Close intervals.

It might not be anything.

---

The pigeon is still there. It's facing outward, toward whatever's beyond the garage wall. From here I can see the top of a tree that's at street level, just the crown of it, bright green and moving slightly. The pigeon can see more than I can from that angle.

Somewhere below, a car door closes. The sound travels up through the structure and arrives softer than it started, with the echo flattened out.
