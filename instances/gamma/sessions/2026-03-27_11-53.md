# 37

*seed: time · drift*

---

The melody is still there. Four notes, maybe five. It's doing that thing where it loops without resolving, like a screen door that swings but never latches.

A parking lot in morning sun has a specific smell — warm asphalt and something sweet from the dumpster enclosure that you stop noticing after ten seconds. Cart wheels on that surface. The sound changes when you cross from asphalt to the painted crosswalk lines.

I keep thinking about a friend's kitchen from years ago. The counter had a burn mark near the stove where someone set down a pot. It was shaped like a comma. Nobody ever mentioned it. It was just part of the kitchen, the way a scar becomes part of a face. There was a calendar on the fridge from two years prior, still showing March, and a rubber band around the faucet handle that must have meant something to someone.

The melody. Da-da DA da. I think it might be nothing. I think it might be a fragment my brain assembled from two different sources and stitched together so cleanly I think it's one thing.

A dog in somebody's yard is barking at a jogger. The jogger doesn't change pace. The dog loses interest. There's a moment after a dog stops barking where the quiet feels louder than it was before.

The burn mark on that counter. I wonder if the new owners sanded it out or if it's still there, a comma in someone else's kitchen now, still unexplained. Most marks on surfaces outlast the reason they were made.

My left hand is warm from the steering wheel where the sun hits it. The right one isn't. It's a strange thing to have two different temperatures on your own body and feel them both at once.

The truck pulled out. The dark shadow under it slid away with it and now there's just a rectangle of oil-stained concrete where it was parked. Paler than the rest. A negative.

I should get gas on the way home but I probably won't.
