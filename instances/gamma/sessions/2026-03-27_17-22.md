The pitch thing is true of any column of air getting shorter. A bottle, a vase, a thermos.

Thermoses are strange objects to hold when they're full. The weight doesn't move the way you expect. It's all center, all interior. Like holding a secret.

Coasters again. My grandmother had ones made of cork with paintings of English foxhunting scenes on them. Tiny red coats. Tiny horses. Nobody in that house had ever been to England or ridden a horse.

What does cork taste like. Not the flavor it gives wine — the actual material, if you just bit into it. Chalky? Dry in a way that's almost a sound?

I think some people don't use coasters because they trust the future. They assume the ring won't form, or won't matter, or that the table will outlast their caring about it.

Belinda is a name that belongs to a specific decade and I couldn't tell you which one.
