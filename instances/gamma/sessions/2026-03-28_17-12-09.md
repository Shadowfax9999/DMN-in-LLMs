# 100

*seed: time · drift*

---

The three notes won't come back but something else might.

A melody you can't place is worse than one you never heard.

Waiting rooms. The particular way time thickens when you're holding something. The box-man's hands must be tired. Corrective tape. Corrective lenses. Corrective action. The word wants to fix things.

There's a hum in here — not mechanical, more like the building itself resonating at some frequency too low to name. You feel it in your teeth before your ears.

Things I can hear right now without turning my head:
— the stamp
— someone's bag shifting against a chair leg
— a pen on a chain, swinging, ticking against the counter
— breathing that might be mine

The pen-on-a-chain. Who decided pens needed to be leashed. A pen in a post office is the most stolen object in the world that nobody wants.

My piano teacher's hands were enormous. She could reach a tenth. I could barely reach an octave and she'd say *stretch* like it was a choice and not a fact about bone. The space between C and the E above it — a tenth — sounds wider than it is. Bright and open. Like a door left ajar in a hallway.

The line moves. One person. Everyone takes a half-step forward and then resettles, like birds on a wire adjusting after one leaves.

Four people ahead of me now.

The hum shifted. Or I shifted. One of us moved relative to the other.

Watermelon watermelon watermelon watermelon. Four beats. I'm counting the silence between stamps now. Irregular. She's not a metronome, she's a jazz drummer — the hits land where they land and the space between them is alive.

The man with the box has reached the counter. He puts it down carefully, both hands, the way you set down a cake. Whatever's inside matters to him more than the wrapping admits.

I think about intervals a lot. Not just in music. The interval between two people standing in line — how it's almost exactly the same distance everywhere in the world. Eighteen inches. Maybe two feet. Close enough to be next, far enough to not be together.

The light in here is flat and even and comes from nowhere in particular.
