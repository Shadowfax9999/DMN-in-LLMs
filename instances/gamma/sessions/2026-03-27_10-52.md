# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

The underside of a bridge has a color that isn't any color.

Three things I can't stop thinking about today:
1. The way a revolving door commits you
2. Celery salt
3. Whether fish feel acceleration

---

A parking garage stairwell. The handrail is that specific yellow. Not caution-yellow, not school-bus-yellow. Parking-garage-yellow. Cool in the hand even when the air is warm.

Footsteps in stairwells go up faster than they come down.

---

Da da da-da. Still there from yesterday. Stepwise. I've been humming it without the humming, just the shape of it sitting in my jaw. Four notes that don't belong to any song I can name. They might be the beginning of something I heard in a grocery store six months ago. They might be nothing. They have a lean to them — the third note wants the fourth.

---

Celery salt is such a specific commitment. Someone reaches past garlic powder, past paprika, past the reasonable ones, and picks up celery salt. For what. For one recipe. For a Bloody Mary. For potato salad the way someone's aunt made it.

The jar outlasts the intention by years.

---

A revolving door: you push and then you're in the pie-slice of space and you can't back up, you can't stop, you just have to keep going at the speed the door wants. There's a moment in the middle where you're not inside and not outside.

Elevators do this too but slower.

---

I had a piano teacher who would tap the rhythm on the fallboard before I played. Not the melody. Just the rhythm. Tak tak tak-tak. And sometimes the rhythm alone was enough to know the piece. Sometimes it wasn't, and the same rhythm could be anything.

Four notes. Stepwise. A lean.

---

2.

That's how many times I've opened the fridge today without taking anything out.

---

Fish probably do feel acceleration. They have lateral lines. They feel pressure waves from other fish turning. The whole school pivots and it's because each fish feels the water change around it before seeing anything move.

Not thought. Not decision. Just pressure becoming direction.

---

The stairwell handrail is gritty with paint. Seven or eight coats over decades, each one slightly different yellow, and the aggregate is that specific parking-garage-yellow that doesn't exist in nature or on any paint chip. It only exists as accumulation.

I'm standing on the landing between 2 and 3. There's a cigarette butt in the corner that's been there long enough to look architectural.

---

Celery salt. Lateral lines. The note that leans.

---

A car alarm four blocks away. Not the rhythmic one — the one that cycles through five different sounds, each worse than the last, as if escalation were persuasion. Nobody is being persuaded. The car is performing emergency to an empty street.

---

I think the third note is a D.
