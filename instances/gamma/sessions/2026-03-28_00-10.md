# 87

*seed: time · drift · spore*

---

Spore.

A word that sounds like it should be heavier than what it names. Spore. Like door, like floor. But it's a dust-sized thing drifting in billions.

Pollen on a car hood. That yellow-green film that appears in April and nobody sees it arrive. You just walk outside one morning and every dark surface has been claimed. Wiping it with your finger leaves a clean streak and a yellow fingertip.

What were parking lots before cars.

Fields, mostly. Or nothing. Or someone's backyard that got sold. There's a church near where I grew up with a parking lot that's too big for its congregation. Sunday mornings the cars cluster near the entrance and the rest is just open asphalt with painted lines going all the way to the fence. The far lines have never had a car in them. The paint fading for nothing.

A basketball hoop with no net. The sound is different — that hard clang instead of the swish. You can hear the difference from inside a house. Someone's shooting hoops and you know immediately whether the hoop has a net.

Gym class, the climbing rope. The way it smelled. That dry hemp smell. And the knots at intervals if you were lucky, and if you weren't lucky, just the raw rope and your shoes slipping against it and the weird friction burn on your inner forearms.

The word "interval" doing something odd — it contains "vale," a valley, which is what an interval sort of is. A dip between two somethings.

I keep almost thinking about sleep. The thought approaches from the side and I look at it and it backs off. It's after midnight. The body has opinions about this.

A screen door that doesn't latch properly. You close it and it bounces back open an inch. Close it again, it bounces back. You have to hold it and ease it shut, which everyone in the house knows, and guests don't, and you can always hear when a guest has gone outside because the door bounces three times and settles open.

My left knee does a thing sometimes where it clicks on the first step of the day and then never again until the next morning. Every morning, one click. Like it's resetting.

Cashews aren't really nuts. I don't know what they are. They grow on the outside of a fruit, hanging off the bottom like a comma. I saw a picture once and it looked wrong, like the image had been manipulated. A bright red or yellow fruit with this gray kidney-shaped thing dangling beneath it.

The color of a sidewalk after rain at night under a streetlight. That particular dark silver.

Someone is playing music somewhere far away enough that I can only hear the bass. Not the melody, not the words. Just the pulse. It could be any song. It could be three different songs and I wouldn't know the difference. From here it's just rhythm, just someone's subwoofer pushing air through walls and across a distance until it arrives as almost nothing.

The bass keeps going.
