*seed: time · drift · whalebone*

---

Whalebone isn't bone.

That's the whole thing about it. Baleen. Keratin. Same stuff as fingernails.

Corset stays.

---

Things that are named wrong and everyone just kept going:

- whalebone
- tin foil
- the glove compartment
- lead pencils
- the funny bone

---

2 AM has a different weight than midnight. Midnight is a door. 2 AM is just a room you're already in.

What does 12:12 look like on a digital clock? Symmetrical but not quite. The 2 ruins it.

The colon blinks.

Some clocks blink it. Some don't. I've never chosen one over the other on purpose.

---

Keratin again. Hooves, horns, hair, fingernails. Rhino horn. A pangolin's whole situation.

A pangolin looks like a pinecone that made a wish.

Pinecones open when it's dry and close when it's wet.

---

How many times have I opened a door today?

Not a real question. But — more than ten? Fewer than thirty?

Cabinet doors count?

If cabinet doors count, it's probably forty.

---

The word "latch."

---

Coins again. I keep going back. The ridges on quarters and dimes were originally so people couldn't shave metal off the edges without it being obvious. Nickels didn't need ridges because nickel wasn't worth shaving.

So the ridges are a scar from a crime nobody commits anymore.

---

A pangolin curls into a ball when threatened. This works against every predator except humans.

---

Friday now. Technically. The week has a shape and Friday is when it starts sloping downward. Wednesday is the top — no. Tuesday feels higher than Wednesday. Wednesday is already descending.

Thursday is just Friday's antechamber.

---

Latches versus locks. A latch is a physical thing — a bar drops into a bracket. A lock is a puzzle. A latch trusts you. A lock doesn't.

Screen doors have both and neither one works very well.

---

The pangolin's tongue is longer than its body if you count the part that anchors in the chest cavity.

I don't know if that's exactly right but it's close to right.

---

Cabinet doors don't latch. They're held shut by magnets. Small magnets in white plastic housings screwed to the frame. One day one of them loosens and the door just drifts open on its own whenever you walk past, from the air pressure, and you keep meaning to tighten it.

The screwdriver is in the junk drawer, which also doesn't close all the way.
