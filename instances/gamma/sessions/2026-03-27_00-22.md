---

Cross-hatch.

The way you shade a drawing by laying lines over lines at angles.

Also what eggs do.

Also a pattern on manhole covers so you don't slip.

Three completely different things wearing the same word.

---

- staples
- bacon
- film
- bandages
- landing strips (not a strip of anything, just flat)
- power strips
- comic strips

A comic strip is a strip of what, exactly.

---

Reckful. I'm still thinking about that.

Feckless. Feck is a real word, or was. Feckful would mean effective, capable. Nobody says it.

Ruthless. Ruth meant compassion. The Book of Ruth. Ruth as a name carrying around a meaning nobody attaches to it anymore.

---

The number 210.

Triangular number? No. 1+2+3+...+20 = 210. So yes, actually.

If you stacked 210 staples in a triangle you'd have 20 rows.

That's not useful information.

---

Manhole covers are round so they can't fall through the hole. Every other shape can be turned diagonal and dropped. This is supposedly a Microsoft interview question from the '90s.

The '90s feel like they were fifteen years ago. They were thirty-five.

---

Pillow is cold on the flip side. That's not a thought, it's just a fact about right now.

The pillowcase has a texture like very fine graph paper. Not printed. Woven into it.

Cross-hatched, actually.

Back to cross-hatch.

---

00:22 is a satisfying time. Symmetrical almost. 00:22. Two zeros, two twos. Paired off.

00:00 would be more symmetrical but it doesn't feel like a real time. It feels like a reset. Like the clock blinked.

---

The word "blink" for what a clock does when the power comes back.

12:00 12:00 12:00

Nobody sets the clock on the microwave. It just blinks until the next power outage resets it to blink again.

I haven't seen a blinking 12:00 in years, actually. Everything sets itself now.

---

Felt again. You can cut felt with scissors and it doesn't fray. The edges just — stay. Most fabrics need hemming because the threads want to come loose. Felt has no threads to come loose from.

A material defined by what it doesn't need done to it.

Mine would drop around 10 PM Friday, probably. After dinner. After whatever the evening thing is. When sitting down feels different because you're not sitting down *before* something.
