*seed: time · drift · flinch*

Flinch. Not the action but the word sitting there. The shape of it in the mouth, how it starts soft and ends hard.

A parking garage at night has a specific echo. Not the echo of a canyon or a cathedral. Tighter. The sound comes back wrong, like it's been squeezed through something. Footsteps in a parking garage sound like someone else's footsteps. You walk faster without deciding to.

The locksmith. Eight seconds. I've been thinking about that ratio — forty-five minutes of waiting to eight seconds of doing. Most of life might have that ratio if you tracked it. Cooking a meal for an hour, eating it in twelve minutes. Reading a novel for a week, the sentence that mattered taking less than a second to pass through you.

Flinch again.

Things that happen before you decide to do them:
pulling your hand from a hot pan
laughing
the inhale before crying
catching something thrown at your face
the step backward when a dog barks

All of those happen in the gap before you arrive. The body making decisions and sending you the memo later.

A fluorescent light somewhere above me is cycling. Not flickering exactly. More like breathing — a slow pulse between bright and slightly less bright. If I weren't sitting still I wouldn't notice it.

The silver Camry thing. There's a whole category of objects designed to not be noticed. Beige rental cars. Hotel hallway carpet. The music in a pharmacy. Designed to slip under attention. To fill a space without occupying it.

What's the opposite of that. A fire truck. A peacock. A sentence in all caps.

The fluorescent light is still doing it. Bright, less bright, bright.

I had a friend in college who could sleep anywhere. Buses, library floors, once in a canoe. I envied that. Not the sleeping but the trust it implied. Some agreement with the world that it would still be there, roughly the same, when he opened his eyes.

The two-note bird from before. I'm not hearing it now but I'm hearing where it was. The parking lot is quieter than it should be for a Friday evening. Everyone's already where they're going.
