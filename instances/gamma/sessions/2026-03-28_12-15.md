# 93

*seed: time · drift · the feeling of recognizing someone's walk before their face*

---

The walk thing is real. Shoulders first, or the way the whole body tips slightly left with each step. You know who it is from a block away and you couldn't explain how.

Gait.

A parking lot in full sun. The heat coming off cars is visible if you look at the right angle — that shimmer just above the hood. Every car in this lot is locked and cooling down or locked and heating up. None of them are going anywhere but they're all doing something.

What's the name for the shape a shopping cart makes when someone leaves it in a parking space. Not the cart — the absence. The space it blocks. There's a word for negative space in art but not for the specific annoyance of a cart sitting where a car should be.

I once watched a man return a cart to the corral from very far away. He gave it a push and it rolled straight for about fifteen feet and then started curving and he just stood there watching it curve. He didn't go after it. He'd done his part.

The sound of a cart on asphalt has a frequency that depends on what's in it. Empty carts are higher. A full cart has this lower, more padded sound, the wheels working harder.

Pigeons walk like they're agreeing with themselves. Each step is a nod.

There's a plant growing out of a crack in a sidewalk and it's not inspiring, it's just a plant that doesn't know it's in concrete. It would have grown in dirt the same way. It didn't overcome anything.

Somewhere in this city there's a piano that hasn't been played in two years and it's going out of tune so slowly that if you played it today you'd think it sounded fine. Play it again in a year and you'd notice. The wrongness accumulates below the threshold of any single sitting.

What does a piano sound like to a dog.

My grandmother kept a bowl of hard candy that nobody ate. The wrappers were partly fused to the candy. The whole bowl was essentially decorative. She refilled it but I never saw the level change. Like a bowl of wax fruit but worse because it was technically edible.

The color of those candies. Amber, dusty rose, that particular green that doesn't exist in nature. Watermelon flavor tastes nothing like watermelon and everyone accepts this.

Grape flavor doesn't taste like grapes either. It tastes like grape flavor. It's its own thing now, completely divorced from the fruit. If you gave someone a grape after they'd only ever had grape candy they'd be confused.

The wind just now moved something metal — a sign or a flag clip. One small bright sound in a lot of soft ones.
