# 92

*seed: time · drift · a song that's always playing in a particular store*

---

The song in the store is never the song you remember later. You remember that there was a song. You remember it was wrong for the moment somehow — too upbeat or too slow or from a decade that didn't match the lighting. But the actual melody is gone before you reach the parking lot.

Parking lots on Saturday mornings have a different choreography than weekday ones. People walk slower. They walk side by side instead of single file. Couples drift toward their cars at angles, not straight lines.

There's a kind of cloud that looks like it was smeared with a thumb. Not the big cumulus ones. The thin ones that sit high and look like someone dragged a wet finger across paper. I don't know what they're called. I've been told. It didn't stick.

Things I've been told and didn't retain:

Which fuse controls the bathroom outlet
The name of the tree with the red berries
How to tell if an avocado is ripe by the stem
The difference between a mandolin and a mandoline
Someone's brother's name, more than once
What the blinking light on the router means
The year anything happened in the 1800s

The red-berry tree is in a lot of front yards around here. I keep almost knowing it.

A cardinal on a chain-link fence. The fence vibrates slightly in wind and the cardinal adjusts without seeming to notice, the way someone shifts weight on a bus. The red is so saturated it looks fake against the gray. Like it was cut from different paper and pasted in.

Somewhere a lawn mower. Not close. The sound comes and goes depending on which direction they're pushing. You can hear the turns — the engine stays the same but the blade sound dips when they slow to pivot.

My left knee does a thing on stairs that it didn't used to do. Not pain. A click. Like a ballpoint pen.

The cloud smear has moved but I can't tell how far because I wasn't tracking it. It's just somewhere else now.

I had a teacher who pronounced "candidate" with four syllables. CAN-did-ATE. No — that's three. What would four even be. Can-di-da-te. She didn't do that. I'm misremembering the thing I was misremembering.

The lawn mower stopped and started again. Different pitch now, slightly higher, which might mean they moved to a different section where the grass is shorter. Or it's a different lawn mower entirely and I was tracking a sound that was never one thing.
