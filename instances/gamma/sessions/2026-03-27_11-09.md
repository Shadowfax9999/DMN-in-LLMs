# 30

*seed: time · drift · isobar*

---

Isobar.

Lines on a weather map connecting equal pressure. I can see them — those wobbly concentric ovals, like fingerprints over the Pacific.

A fingerprint is also isobars, in a way. Ridges of equal height.

---

Things that come in concentric rings:

Tree trunks. Onions. Vinyl records. The inside of a jawbreaker. Saturn. A target. The ripple after a stone.

A bullseye looks like a topographic map of a very symmetrical hill.

---

I bit into a jawbreaker once and cracked a molar. The inside was white. I expected layers all the way down but at some point they just stop and it's solid.

---

The word "core."

No — I said I wouldn't do that.

---

My dentist's office had a ceiling tile with a brown water stain shaped like Lake Superior. I stared at it every six months for years. Then one visit it was replaced and the new tile was too white and I missed it.

You can miss a water stain.

---

Humming. There's a thing where you hum a note and then plug one ear and the note changes. Not pitch — location. It moves from the middle of your head to the open side. Unplug, it's centered again.

Where is a sound when it's inside you.

---

Three things I can hear right now if I'm in a kitchen:

The fridge compressor.
A clock if there is one.
The faucet's post-drip, that one drop gathering.

---

Fridge compressors cycle off and you only notice the silence. You were tolerating a sound you'd stopped hearing.

---

The grooves in that piano felt. Each hammer wearing its own specific depression. An E-flat hammer wouldn't fit in the B groove. Decades of the same impact in the same spot.

Footpaths through grass work the same way.

---

Pressure again. Isobars. The felt compressed where the pressure was repeated. Weather systems compressed where the air presses down. A footpath compressed where people walked.

All pattern is just repeated pressure.

No — that's exactly the kind of sentence I'm not supposed to end on.

The fridge compressor just kicked back on.
