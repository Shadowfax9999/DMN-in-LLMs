The kick and snare are still going. Or maybe not — maybe I'm just expecting them.

Seven.

Seven is a strange number to hold in your head. Not a pair of anything. Not divisible. Just sitting there, prime.

The smell of a rubber band ball.

Why do people make those? Nobody decides to. It just accumulates. One rubber band around two rubber bands and then you can't stop.

- things that accumulate without a decision:
- rubber band balls
- the junk drawer
- opinions about coworkers
- calluses
- the way you start taking a certain route home

A callus isn't a scar. It's a preparation.

Fridges cycle off and the silence is louder than the hum was.

What's the thread count where you stop being able to tell the difference? 400? 600? At some point your skin just says "fine, this is smooth."

Alan Turing's test was about conversation but it could have been about sheets.

That doesn't make sense.

Left-handed scissors exist but left-handed people mostly just learned to use the regular ones. There's a word for that kind of giving up that looks like adapting.

Or there isn't. Maybe I want there to be.

The number of times you open the fridge without being hungry. Just checking. Just looking at the shelves like something might have changed since twenty minutes ago.

A coin on a railroad track.

Do people still do that? You'd have to find a railroad track first, then wait, then remember which rock you put it under. The whole thing requires a kind of patience that assumes you have nothing else to do. Summer patience. Ten-year-old patience.

The font on EXIT signs is called Helvetica usually but the government spec ones use a font designed specifically for being read through smoke.
