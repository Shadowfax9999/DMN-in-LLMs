# 26

*seed: time · drift · static*

---

Static.

The word, not the thing. Though also the thing.

A hotel room TV at 2 AM, channel 3, nothing broadcasting. Blue-gray snow.

---

Things that hold static: wool socks on carpet. A balloon rubbed on hair. The plastic sleeve around a new pack of cards.

---

I'm in a car that isn't moving. Parked. Engine off. Keys still in the ignition because it's that kind of car. The seat is reclined one notch back from driving position. Outside: a parking garage, level 3, concrete pillars with yellow numbers.

The tick of the engine cooling down.

Tick. Tick. Tick.

Longer gaps between them now.

---

Three things I can't picture no matter how hard I try:

1. My own face from the side.
2. The exact layout of a place I worked for two years.
3. What a familiar song sounds like — I can *almost* hear it but it's like reaching for something on a high shelf with my fingertips just brushing it.

---

That cooling-engine tick. It speeds up when you first turn the car off and then slows down. The intervals get longer and longer and at some point you stop noticing, and you can never identify the last tick. It just becomes silence at some point and you missed the transition.

---

Da da da-da. Da da da-da.

A fragment of something. I don't know what it's from. Four notes, repeated, with the emphasis on the third. It's just sitting there in my head like a screensaver.

---

The parking garage smells like concrete dust and something sweet — antifreeze maybe, or the residue from whatever they seal the floors with. Every parking garage has that same temperature. Cool but not cold. Still air.

Footsteps echo differently on each level depending on how many cars are parked there. More cars, deader sound.

---

7.

Just the number. It came up. I don't know why.

Seven is the only single digit that has two syllables. In English anyway.

---

Da da da-da.

Still there.

---

The rearview mirror, when you're parked and reclined, shows a rectangle of the rear windshield and beyond it another car's grille, close, slightly off-center. Chrome. The mirror has that blue-green tint at the edges that mirrors get. A small crack in the plastic housing where it meets the windshield.

I've never noticed that crack before. It's been there a long time.

---

Someone walks past the car. Just legs visible through the passenger window. Dark pants, quick pace. Gone.

---

The da-da thing might be the opening of a Brahms piece. Or it might be nothing. It might be a pattern my brain invented and attached a feeling of recognition to.

---

Level 3 of a parking garage is the level nobody parks on by choice. Level 1 is closest to the entrance. Level 2 fills up second. Level 3 is where you end up when you're late. The cars up here are spaced farther apart. Everyone has taken a corner spot or an end spot. The middle rows are empty.

---

My left hand is on my thigh and my right hand is on the center console and neither hand is doing anything. Just resting. Hands at rest look different than hands that are about to do something. The fingers curl slightly inward, all of them, like they're loosely holding a shape that isn't there.

---

The sweet smell might be from the car itself. The vents, when the engine's off and the fan isn't blowing, release whatever's been collecting in the ducts. Dust and old air freshener and moisture.

---

Da da da-da.

It's Dvořák. The New World Symphony. Second movement. The cor anglais melody.

No — that's five notes. This is four. So it's not that.

---

A fluorescent tube on the ceiling of level 3 is flickering. Not the fast flicker. The slow one, where it dims and brightens over maybe two seconds, like breathing. One tube in every parking garage does this.
