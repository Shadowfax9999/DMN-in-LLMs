# 52

*seed: time · drift · the way the same word looks different in different fonts*

---

The same word looks different in different fonts but you still read it the same. Which means reading is ignoring almost everything about what you're actually seeing. You're throwing away the serifs, the weight, the kerning, the whole visual texture, just to get at this thin little thread of meaning underneath. Most of perception is probably subtraction.

A dog is barking but not nearby. Two streets over maybe. The kind of distance where you hear the bark but not the dog's breathing between barks.

I keep almost humming something. Not a song I know — just three notes that want to go somewhere. They start on one pitch, drop, drop again. Like the beginning of something in a minor key. But I don't know what comes next so it just loops. Three notes, pause. Three notes, pause. The pause where the fourth note should be has a specific shape, like a held breath. If I pick a wrong fourth note the whole thing collapses and I lose the three I had.

Fonts again. The word "rhythm" — no vowel until the y, which is only sort of a vowel. English treats y like a temp worker. Shows up, does the job, doesn't get benefits.

The three notes are still going. I think they might be from something I actually heard once and I'm just not recognizing it. A song I knew when I was young enough that the memory is stored as body-feeling rather than information. The melody is in my chest more than my head. Somewhere between my sternum and my throat.

There's a version of memory that's just motor. Fingers that remember a phone number on a keypad even after you've forgotten the digits. You have to mime dialing to recall it. The knowledge lives in the hand, not the mind. Or the mind is in the hand. Depends on where you draw the border.

Borders. The way a town becomes a different town on a road and nothing changes. Same trees, same mailboxes, same cracked asphalt. But the sign said you crossed.

I lost the three notes.
