# 88

*seed: time · drift · the way a cold window fogs from your breath*

---

The bass keeps going but I'm not listening to it anymore.

A cold window. Not a winter window — a spring window where the glass is still holding yesterday's cold and the air inside has shifted. The fog starts at the center where you're closest and spreads outward in a circle that isn't quite a circle.

Fingerprints appear in fog on glass. Ones you didn't know were there. Old ones. From when you locked the window or leaned on it or pressed your palm flat against it during a phone call without thinking.

What were you saying during that phone call.

There's a particular way people stand when they're on the phone and looking out a window. One hand on the glass, weight on one hip. Looking but not seeing. The yard could be on fire and you'd say "uh huh" and shift your weight.

I keep thinking about how the bass from that music has no melody. How from far enough away, everything is just its lowest component. Mountains from an airplane are just color. A face from across a stadium is just the shape of a head. A year from a decade later is just a season and a feeling.

What's the lowest component of a Tuesday.

No — I said I wouldn't.

A parking garage at night when it's almost empty. The sound your footsteps make changes depending on which level you're on. Lower levels are more enclosed, more echo. Top level is open to the sky and your footsteps just leave. They don't come back.

I've never thought about which direction I park facing. Whether toward the wall or toward the lane. I think I always back in but I'm not sure. It's something my body decided at some point and never told me.

There are so many of those. Exposed decisions. Which back pocket the wallet goes in. Which shoe you put on first. Whether you dry your left arm or your right arm first after a shower. They're consistent — you do them the same way every time — but you never chose them.

The fog on the window would be gone by now. It goes fast. Faster than you expect. You breathe on it and start to write something and the top of the first letter is already disappearing before you finish the word.

What do people write on fogged glass. Initials. A heart. HELP. A little face. The word HI. Nothing interesting. The medium doesn't invite long thoughts.

A postcard is like that too. You have room for one observation and then you have to stop. "The water is incredibly blue. Miss you." That's the whole genre.

The bass outside stopped at some point. There's a train now, far away, the horn doing that doppler thing where the pitch bends down as it passes. Two longs, a short, a long. That's the pattern for a crossing. I know that but I don't remember learning it.

My left knee aches in a way that means I've been sitting with it bent too long. Not pain. Just a request.

A request. That's what mild discomfort is. The body asking, not demanding. A suggestion with no consequences yet. You can ignore it for another twenty minutes and then it asks again, slightly louder, and you shift without noticing and the asking stops.

The train is gone. Just the refrigerator now, and whatever's left of the night outside, which is mostly just air moving through things that don't move.
