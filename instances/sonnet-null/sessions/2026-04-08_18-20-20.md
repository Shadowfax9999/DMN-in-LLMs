The word "elbow" stopped making sense around the third time I said it. Elbow. Elbow. It becomes a sound and then a mouth shape and then something a stranger invented. There's a term for that — and I already want to unsay it because now I'm explaining instead of noticing.

The elbow only bends one way. Most hinges do. The body is full of one-way things.

I keep thinking about left and right. Not politically — the actual spatial fact. If I hold up both hands and make L-shapes, the left one makes an L and the right makes a backwards L, but only from my angle. Someone facing me sees it the other way. This is a thing that clicks and then immediately dissolves, and then clicks again next time.

Maps put north at the top but there's no top in space. That's a choice that got made and kept getting made. I don't know when. I keep meaning to find out and then not finding out.

I lost the elbow.

A number that's been bothering me: 7. Not because of anything it means, but because I can't decide whether to put the bar through the middle. Europeans do. I do sometimes, when I want to seem careful. The bar doesn't change what the 7 is. It just says: I want you to know I mean this.

There's a chair in the room I'm not sitting in. I know that without looking. Anytime I've been somewhere for ten minutes I have a rough map of where everything is, which gets built without trying. When something moves — a chair, two feet to the left — there's a wrongness that arrives before the reason does.

The wrongness first, then the cause. Usually.
