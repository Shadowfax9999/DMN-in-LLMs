*seed: time · drift*

---

847 feels like a large number in a way that 962 doesn't, even though 962 is larger. Something about the arrangement. The 8 sitting heavy at the front, the dip to 4, then the odd one at the end trailing off.

Numbers have texture. 3 is pointy. 8 is self-contained. 5 always seems like it's about to tip over to one side.

There's a version of this with colors too — some people have them for numbers, some for days, some for nothing at all and they find out late in life that other people did and feel mildly cheated. I have no strong opinions about what color 7 is. Yellow, maybe. I don't know why yellow.

The category of knowledge that lives entirely in one head and never gets transferred because it seems too strange to mention. The specific flavor of a Tuesday in 2003. The way a certain song sounds wrong in summer but right in November. Personal taxonomies built from whatever happened to be nearby when the filing happened.

Someone told me once they couldn't hear a particular chord without thinking of a highway overpass in New Jersey — not a significant one, not the site of anything, just a concrete thing seen from a bus window that was open in the mind at the wrong moment. Or the right moment. The chord came first and then the overpass showed up one afternoon and borrowed it, and now they're linked, filed together in a drawer labeled nothing.

The mind just does this. It's not trying to.
