The word "meanwhile" is doing a lot of work I never thought about. *Meanwhile, somewhere else* — it assumes you're already watching something. It assumes a primary thing. You can't start a story with meanwhile.

My knee makes a sound on some third steps of stairs. Not all third steps. Not predictably. I've never caught it doing it in real time, only noticed it after.

There's a budget to mornings I can't fully account for. Not time — decisions. Or something like decisions. You spend some before anything has happened and then the day is already slightly more expensive than it should be. Someone famous said this about suits. I half-believe it.

Meanwhile.

What's the word for an almost-habit. The thing you do often enough that it's waiting for you but not often enough to be automatic. I check the back left burner. I haven't used the back left burner in weeks. The check happens anyway, the hand moves toward it, and then I remember there's no reason to check and check anyway.

Fish have no eyelids. I remember learning this and feeling like it explained something about fish — their blankness, the way they seem to have missed something — but actually I just wanted it to explain something. It doesn't explain anything. Fish are just looking. They don't have the option not to.

The word meanwhile keeps coming back. I don't know what it wants.
