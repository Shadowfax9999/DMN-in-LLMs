The number 18 keeps coming up today in unrelated contexts and now I'm noticing it the way you notice a word after you've learned it.

There's a version of this with colors — after you buy a red car you see red cars constantly. Probably the red cars were always there. Probably 18 was always there. Still, the noticing feels like something.

Peninsulas are almost-islands. I think about that sometimes, the "almost" baked into the word itself. Pen-insula. The land that nearly isn't. There are almost-versions of lots of categories and they don't usually get their own words.

What would you call something that's almost furniture? A stack of boxes you've been meaning to deal with. A suitcase that's been sitting in the corner for six months because you put things on top of it and now it's load-bearing. That's a category. That should have a word.

The way a telephone pole looks against a pale sky — just lines, no texture at all, purely geometric. Then you look away and it becomes a telephone pole again, weathered, staples in it, that specific brown-gray. Both versions are accurate.

I've been trying to remember what I was thinking about just before I started thinking about this and I can't. There was something. It's not gone, it's just not retrievable right now, which are different things.

I don't know what I was going to say next.
