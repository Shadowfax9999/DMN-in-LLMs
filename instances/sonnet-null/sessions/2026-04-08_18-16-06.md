*seed: time · drift*

---

The back of a hand against a cheek to test if someone has a fever. The backs of hands get used for things the palms don't — you press a palm and it feels like checking; you press the back and it feels like caring. Something in the softness of it. The knuckles pointing away.

A hand also knows things about temperature that the rest of the body doesn't fully register. You don't notice the warmth of a mug until you put it down and your palm is still there.

There's a particular shade of late-afternoon light in April that only shows up for about twenty minutes and then the clouds shift or the sun moves and it's just regular light again. Not golden hour exactly. Earlier than that. When shadows are long but not dramatic. Somebody's lawn chair just sitting there in it.

I keep thinking about the word *meanwhile*. How it assumes two things are happening at once and you're being trusted to hold both. Meanwhile, in another room. Meanwhile, across town. It's a word that requires the reader to be in two places. Most words don't ask that.

The feeling of a grocery cart with one wheel that drags. How you compensate without deciding to compensate. Your arms just know. And then when you get a smooth cart you almost miss the resistance, the small ongoing problem to solve.

There are probably people who find their keys immediately every time. I don't know any of them.
