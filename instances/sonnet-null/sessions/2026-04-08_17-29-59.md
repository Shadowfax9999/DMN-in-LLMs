There's a spot in the middle of a staircase where you can hear both floors at once — the TV from downstairs, someone moving around upstairs — and neither of them claims you. You're just in between.

The landing is a strange invention. A place to rest on the way to another place. You're not at either destination, just pausing in vertical space.

The word "landing" is doing a lot of work there — planes, fish, falls, all of them arriving, all of them completing a movement —

No. Not doing that.

Most buildings have stairs nobody uses because there's an elevator. The stairs exist, are maintained and lit and cleaned, and are empty most of the time. A parallel building running alongside the one people actually use.

Something about that feels worth sitting with. I'm not sure what.

The cold of a metal handrail. Not cold like winter — just cool. Room temperature isn't body temperature, the metal conducts away from your hand faster than wood does, which is why a wooden banister feels warmer even at the same —

I was going to leave that one alone.

The specific heat of a car at five o'clock that's been in a parking lot all day. Not summer heat. Stored heat. Heat from eight AM and noon and two PM all still in the upholstery. You sit down in it and it's like sitting inside earlier today.

That felt a little arranged.

Okay: a grocery cart rolls differently when it's empty. Moves too easily, catches, moves too easily, catches. Something slightly off about it. A full cart is more cooperative.

There's a bag of potting soil on the back step that I bought three weeks ago. Still there. The plant is still in the smaller pot. The soil is just sitting there being potential soil.
