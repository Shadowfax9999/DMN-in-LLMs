# 52

*seed: time · drift*

---

Six o'clock light comes in sideways and makes everything look like it's been recently washed. Shadows get long and specific — a parking meter's shadow, a thin line. The body knows this light. Something in the posture loosens.

There's a word for that loosening but it's not in English. German has words for everything except the thing you're actually feeling, and then you find out the word exists in Portuguese and you feel both relieved and obscurely cheated.

The number eight is involved in a lot of things. Eight minutes past. Octaves. Spiders. The eight-hour workday was a union demand and before that people just worked until it was dark, which in winter is four o'clock and in summer is nine, so the body must have been very confused about what a day was. Still is, probably. The body is always working from an older calendar.

I keep thinking about that octopus study — no, I've already thought about octopus studies, that's a place I keep going. The color-through-skin thing. Leave it.

What else has eight. Stop signs. The eight of hearts. A chess board is eight by eight and that might be arbitrary or might be the right size for human working memory or might be neither, just a thing that happened and then stayed.

The light through a window at this hour makes a parallelogram on the floor and it moves. Slow enough you can't watch it move, fast enough that if you look away and look back it's somewhere else. Like a minute hand.

There's something I was going to think about and lost. That happens. The thought was right there and then the light shifted.
