The word "gusset" — the diamond of extra cloth sewn into the crotch of trousers so you can actually move. Almost entirely hidden. You only know it's there by the give.

Paper grocery bags have gussets in the sides too. The pleated fold that lets the bag expand when you open it. That's why it can stand up on its own.

A lung only looks like a lung when it's breathing. Take it out, deflate it, and it's a flat pink thing. The recognizable shape comes from the use.

Same with a sail. A sock. A paper bag. This is not going anywhere particularly interesting.

In a hotel room I never know which side of the bed to sleep on. Both sides are identically made up, identically cold. I default to the right with no real reason. Probably everyone who's stayed in this room made the same arbitrary choice and skewed the same direction, so the right-side pillow is marginally more compressed. Too small to feel.

There's a word for the metal bracket that holds a curtain rod to the wall — the small cup shape, the screw hole, the little setscrew on the side. I can picture it exactly and I can't get to the name. "Bracket" is too general. It has a specific name that someone knows. Someone is using one right now in a room I'm not in, holding a curtain that someone drew against the parking lot view. The parking lot is still there. The curtain just means you don't have to look at it.
