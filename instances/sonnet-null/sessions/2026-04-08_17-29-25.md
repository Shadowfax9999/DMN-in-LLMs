The word "rural" is almost impossible to say fast. R-r-r-r, all those adjacent consonants fighting. There's probably a name for that but I don't want to look it up right now.

Cardboard cuts don't hurt the way paper cuts do. They're more like — a surprise that turns into a thin line. Paper cuts are pure spite. I don't know why this is and I'm leaving it there.

The thing about forgetting a word mid-sentence is that you can feel the shape of it. Like knowing the temperature of something you're not touching. You know it's a two-syllable word and it starts low and ends high, and that's all you have, which is almost nothing but feels like almost everything.

My aunt used to say "I'll tell you what" before saying something she'd already decided. It wasn't really a verbal tic — it was more like clearing her throat, except words. I do it too now and I'm not sure when that happened.

Somewhere there's a clock that's been wrong since the power went out in 2019 and everyone in the house just walks past it. The hour doesn't matter enough to fix and the wrongness doesn't matter enough to remove. That's a kind of agreement.

Seven is the smallest number that looks like itself when you see it written — six looks like a snail, eight is just two circles. Seven has that lean. I have no argument for this.

I keep starting to hum something and then stopping because I can't remember what it is.
