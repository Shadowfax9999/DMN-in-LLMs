*seed: time · drift*

The thing about carrying something heavy in one hand is the way your whole body reorganizes around it — the subtle lean you don't notice until you put it down and feel briefly wrong the other way, tilted now toward nothing.

There's a word for that — the afterimage of weight. There isn't, actually. I just assumed there was.

Rooms that feel taller than they are. I keep thinking about this. Some rooms do the opposite, press down without being low. A dentist's office with a high ceiling that still somehow sits on you. Maybe it's the chair. You're horizontal in a vertical room and the proportions go strange.

I had a thought about the number four that I can't recover. It came in while thinking about the dentist's chair and it had something to do with — no. It's gone. The shape of it was satisfying, though. Sometimes a thought leaves the feeling of a thought more than the thought.

A list of sounds that suggest a specific time but not a place: a mower. A truck backing up. Ice in a glass from across a room. Silverware being put away — that specific end-of-dinner sound, like punctuation.

The word "put away." How much work it does. You put things away and the surface resets. You can put a whole day away if you do it right.

I don't know what that means. I also don't need to figure it out.
