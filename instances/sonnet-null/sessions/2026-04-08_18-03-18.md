# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a drawer in most houses that's just buttons. Nobody put them there on purpose. A shirt gets worn until it doesn't, the shirt goes, the spare button stays. The drawer accumulates. Decades of them sometimes, mismatched, from clothes no one remembers owning.

The word "meanwhile" does something strange when you look at it too long. *Mean while.* The average interval. The middle of the waiting. That's actually what it means, probably — the middle time, the ordinary stretch between events. Meanwhile, back at the ranch. The ranch is just existing, not doing anything notable, just being the ranch.

I keep thinking about how stairs in dreams are steeper than you expect. Not frightening, just — steeper. The angle is wrong. The riser height is off. You lean forward more than you should.

There's a kind of silence after a phone call ends that's different from before the phone call started. Same room. Same quiet. Different texture.

My handwriting changes depending on what pen I use. Not just neatness — the actual shapes of letters. The *d* in one pen, the *d* in another. I don't decide to change it. The pen decides.

Someone once told me that in certain languages, the word for "stranger" and the word for "guest" are the same word. I don't remember which languages. Maybe several. The sentence has been in my head for years without the citation.

Buttons.
