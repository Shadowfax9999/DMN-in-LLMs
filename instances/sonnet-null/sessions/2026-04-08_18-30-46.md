The same clock time means something different in April than in August. 18:30 in summer is just afternoon continuing, but 18:30 in spring still feels like an event — the light doing something specific, making an argument.

Not sure where that goes.

The word "spell" is doing three different jobs at once. A spell of weather. A magic spell. To spell a word. All the same word, all pointing somewhere different. I could look into why but I probably won't.

Some songs feel located in a specific room rather than a mood. Not the room they were written in, just — a room. I have a few songs that feel like they're in a stairwell. I don't know what to do with that.

Kohlrabi. I'm thinking about kohlrabi, which is a vegetable I have almost no relationship with. It exists for me as a word more than a thing. Parsnip is similar. There's a whole tier of vegetables that are more concept than food — present in the mind as placeholders, vague shapes, a kind of generic root-vegetable silhouette. Whereas carrot is completely specific. You know exactly what a carrot is.

I wonder what the kohlrabi equivalent is for someone who grew up eating kohlrabi. Probably carrot.

The light is doing that thing now where it's coming from a very low angle and making ordinary objects cast long specific shadows. The shadows are more detailed than the objects.

That's enough.
