17:43 is the hour nobody writes songs about. Midnight gets songs. 3am gets songs. Even noon. But 17:43 just sits there, neither afternoon nor evening, not quite either thing. There's a whole category of time like that — the forgettable hours, the ones you pass through without registering.

The word spring keeps surfacing. Spring the season, spring the coil, spring as verb. To spring something means to release it suddenly. To spring for dinner means to pay. These can't all be the same word originally and yet they are, sort of, or they became the same word, which is stranger.

A mainspring in a clock is just stored tension waiting. The whole mechanism is controlled release, the energy parceled out in — no, not going there.

Things that don't have names but are completely specific: the blue of denim that's been washed forty times. Not indigo. Not periwinkle. Just that particular faded almost-gray blue that doesn't exist anywhere except on fabric that's been through something. The smell of a bookshelf that's been in the same room for a decade. The sound a car engine makes about thirty seconds after you turn it off, that metal-settling sound, the car returning to itself.

There's a thing where you're holding a cup and then at some point you forget you're holding it and then you remember. The forgetting and remembering isn't about the cup.

April 8. Still those mornings that smell like nothing yet. Then the afternoons start to open up.

Cut grass just smells the way it does.
