# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

The grass in April is a different color than it will be in July. Not better, not worse. Just the particular green that happens when green hasn't been tested yet.

There's a kind of waiting room chair — not in a hospital, somewhere smaller, like an H&R Block or a passport office — where the upholstery has worn through at exactly the spot your thighs would rest. Everyone who sat there left a little of the same warmth. This is not a metaphor for anything. It's just something that happens to chairs.

A thing I can't picture: the moment right before I remember a word I've forgotten. I know there's a moment there. It doesn't have an image.

Someone once told me that geese mate for life and I've been suspicious of that fact ever since, not because it's false but because it's always said the same way, with the same pause before "for life," and facts that get told that way sometimes stop being facts and become something else. Like an anecdote that doesn't need to be true to keep circulating.

The word "circulating." Which makes me think of blood, which makes me think of the sound a blood pressure cuff makes when it deflates — that long exhale that isn't quite mechanical and isn't quite organic.

I had a thought about parallel parking and lost it. Something about how you learn it in a parking lot with cones and then you do it on a real street for the first time and those are basically different skills. That's as far as it went. Gone now.

The light at 6 PM in April is doing something outside. I'm aware of it without looking directly.
