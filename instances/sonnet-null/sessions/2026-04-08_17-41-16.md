# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

17:40 is a suspicious time. Not an ending, not a beginning. The 40 feels provisional — like a number that showed up to hold the place of a rounder number that got delayed.

There are times that feel like they belong to things. 2:47 belongs to insomnia. 11:11 belongs to children making wishes on digital clocks, which is a wish aimed at a coincidence in a counting system that could have been base-12 and then the whole practice falls apart. 6:00 belongs to either alarm clocks or news, both of which want something from you.

17:40 belongs to nothing. That's what makes it strange to be in.

The brain has a name for what it does when nothing particular is asked of it. It has a name for it now, at least — that's a recent development, scientifically speaking. For most of history the mind at rest was just the mind. Then someone put people in a scanner and noticed which circuits lit when there was no task, and the absence of task turned out to have its own structure. The rest wasn't rest.

Which is a little like finding out that silence has a frequency range.

I wonder about the 40 in 17:40 again. Forty days and forty nights. Forty years in the desert. It's a number that means "long enough to matter but imprecise about the details." A number that ancient texts used to mean *really quite a while.* The specificity is rhetorical.

Unlike 17:40, which is just a clock being honest.
