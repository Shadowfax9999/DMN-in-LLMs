*seed: time · drift*

The number 7 feels skinny. Always has. And 4 is somehow squat — solid in a way that 5 isn't, even though 5 is smaller. There's probably a word for this. There are words for everything now.

A phone gets warm after a long call and you put it down on a cool countertop and for a second the countertop is a better listener than the person was.

Thinking about the specific weight of a bowling ball — not heavy exactly, but *committed*. It doesn't pretend to be anything else. You pick it up and it says: this is what I am.

Somewhere I read that competitive eaters train their stomachs to expand. Which means the stomach is a muscle in some sense, or at least muscle-adjacent, or at least responsive to —

Actually I don't know what the stomach is made of.

The ice in the glass has been settling for a few minutes and makes a small sound, a single tick, like it just remembered something.

There's a kind of conversation where you realize halfway through that you're both performing for a third person who isn't there. You're explaining yourselves to an imagined audience. Sometimes the imagined audience is a future version of yourselves. Sometimes it's a parent.

The word "committed" — I used it about the bowling ball and I meant it, it's the right word. But it's also what you say about a good shot, a hard decision, someone who won't back down. The bowling ball doesn't waver. It follows through.

I wonder if that's — no.

The ice settles again.
