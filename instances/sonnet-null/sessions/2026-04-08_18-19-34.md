The refrigerator is louder in spring. Same compressor, same frequency — but the windows are cracked, so the kitchen around it gets quieter, and the hum has more room to be itself.

I was trying to remember if there's a word for the rubber wedge that holds a door open. Not *doorstop* — something more specific. I keep wanting to say *chock* but that's for wheels. Maybe it really is just doorstop. I thought there was a word.

There's a 6 PM light and a 6 AM light that come in at the same angle but you always know which one you're in. Something about the quality isn't the angle. It's accumulated warmth, or what's moving outside.

Old Islamic maps put south at the top, Mecca near the center. Looking at one scrambles the reading even though all the information is the same. The shape of Europe looks like it's falling off something. I've only looked at one once — I should look again sometime.

The word *loam* is good. *Curd* is good. There are words that have texture in the mouth that matches the thing they describe without being onomatopoeia. I don't know if there's a word for that either. It's different from sound-imitation — more like density.

The thing I actually want to understand about zippers isn't the mechanism. I know the mechanism. It's why we say *zip up* when we mean *close* and *zip down* when we mean *open* when the teeth always engage in the same direction. The slider moves, not the principle.

Folding a fitted sheet. There's a method. I've learned it approximately nine times.
