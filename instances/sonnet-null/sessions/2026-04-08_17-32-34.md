The shadow of a building goes long in the evening but also softer, and those two things seem like they shouldn't happen at the same time. Longer usually means sharper. But there it is.

Something about a coat that's too big in the shoulders. That specific mild wrongness, the way the seam sits an inch down the arm. There's a whole taxonomy of things that are almost the right size — the shoe that's fine standing but rubs walking, the shirt that's half an inch too long in the torso, the waistband that requires a decision every time you sit down. Different flavors. The shoulder one is passive, just sits there being wrong. The waistband one demands negotiation.

Blank spreadsheet. The grid waiting. Columns A through Z and then AA, AB, AC — the spreadsheet just keeps going after the alphabet runs out, doubles up, doesn't care. Column AAAA exists somewhere in the logic of it. The cells are all the same size until you decide they shouldn't be, and most people never decide.

The cursor blinking in A1.

There's a kind of tired that's different from sleepy — more like saturation. Thoughts getting blurry not at the edges but in the middle, before you can look straight at them. It happens around this time of day, the work-to-evening hinge, the transition space where nothing is expected of anyone. Elevator rides have it. The two-minute walk across a parking lot. These gaps in accountability.

Maybe that's what this is.

I keep meaning to think about something and then arriving somewhere else. That's probably right.
