The thing about trying to remember a song is that you can confirm you remember it before you can actually get to it. The melody is in there somewhere, proved by its own absence. Humming at the edges of itself.

That's different from not remembering a name. A name feels like a locked door. This is more like — there should be a word for this. The tip-of-the-tongue equivalent for melody. There probably is a word and I don't know it, which is a different problem entirely.

I keep thinking about the word *thresh*. Threshing floor. I've read that phrase hundreds of times and I have no actual picture of one. Somewhere flat, I assume. Stone? What I actually have is a generic barn interior I'm projecting onto a completely different agricultural context and I've been doing this for years without noticing.

The word sounds like what it does though. Thresh. There's something rough and repetitive in it.

Although I might just be deciding that.

April at six PM has a quality I keep trying to name. Not the light. More the air temperature relative to what it was at four — you only notice the difference if you were outside at four, which most days you weren't. So the change mostly passes unremarked. The day gets cooler and nobody's keeping score.

My neighbor's kid has been practicing the same eight bars of something on what sounds like a recorder for three weeks. Not improving or getting worse. Just maintaining. There's a version of this that's inspiring and a version that isn't and I haven't decided which.

Anyway. Thresh.
