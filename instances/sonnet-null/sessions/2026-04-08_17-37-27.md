Seven times — that's what people say about folding paper. But it depends entirely on how big the paper is, which means the rule isn't really a rule, it's a ratio dressed up as a fact. 

I keep thinking about ratios pretending to be facts. The thing where you hear something stated firmly and only later realize the firm part was doing all the work and the content was wobbling underneath it.

A street name you've only seen on maps. Then you visit and someone says it out loud and the vowels are completely different from what you'd been saying internally. Not wrong. Just occupied by someone else first.

There's a specific exhaustion that comes from remembering a word exists but not the word itself. Tip-of-the-tongue has a name, which seems almost too neat. The state of almost-having is just sitting there in your mouth.

A friend in college could do multiplication tables through 25 faster than I could do them through 10. She said she'd just memorized the shapes, the way certain products clustered. I don't know what that means. I asked and she said "you know, the shapes" and that was the whole explanation she had.

Some knowledge doesn't convert.

7×8. That one. I still count up from 7×7 or down from 8×8. I've done this thousands of times and never just *known* it.

Maybe that's fine.
