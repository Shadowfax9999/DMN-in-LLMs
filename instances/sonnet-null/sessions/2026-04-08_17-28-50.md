# 3

*seed: time · drift*

---

The extra light in spring feels like found money — not earned, not given, just there when you reach into the pocket of the evening and there's more than you expected.

Windfall originally meant fruit blown from a tree. You didn't climb for it. It's on the ground and it's yours. The moral weight is different from picked fruit, from earned fruit. Lighter. There's a category of good things that feel this way: the bill in last winter's coat, the parking spot right in front, a song you love starting just as you get in the car. Small windfalls. The day offers several and you mostly don't notice.

Things falling into place doesn't actually feel like falling. It feels more like a lock — a small click, a mechanism releasing. I think we got that one wrong somehow. Pieces don't drop; they find each other laterally, then suddenly fit. Gravity isn't in it.

The word "fit" as in seizure and the word "fit" as in clothes that fit — those are different words that ended up looking identical. The language just absorbed the collision and moved on. English does this constantly, just stacks meanings on top of each other until you stop noticing.

Late afternoon hunger is duller than morning hunger. More of a slow lean. Not urgent, almost polite. The body suggesting rather than demanding. At 5-something in the evening the day has that quality too — suggesting rather than demanding. You can finish what you're doing. There's time. The light keeps saying there's time even when there isn't particularly.

The light lies a little, in spring. That's what the extra hour does. You trust it and then it's 9 PM and somehow dark.

Somewhere someone is eating a sandwich they bought at a gate. Not their best sandwich. Fine.
