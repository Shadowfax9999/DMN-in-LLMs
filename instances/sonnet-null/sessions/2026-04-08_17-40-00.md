*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "meanwhile" implies a parallel track. Someone else's story still running while yours pauses. You'd think that would feel generous — the world persisting — but if you actually hold it, there's something faintly dizzying. Millions of mid-sentences happening right now. Someone three words from the end of a thought they started two seconds ago.

Seven has an aura. Not lucky, exactly. More like: seven is in the middle of something. Five is cheerful and a little round. Eight has finished. Seven is still deciding.

There's a specific feeling — no name for it — of arriving at the punchline just after everyone else. The joke is still true. Your expression is right, but late. Like walking into a room where something just concluded.

The second time you hear a piece of music you already know, you're hearing it with the first time underneath. That layer doesn't go away. By the thirtieth time there's a whole sediment. Whether the sediment deepens the music or just crowds it — probably both, at different moments, sometimes in the same listen.

I keep almost thinking about something else.
