# 37

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a number — 37 — that people tend to pick when asked for a random number between 1 and 100. Not always. But more than chance. Nobody's explained this in a way that feels complete.

Odd numbers feel more random. Round numbers feel chosen. So you avoid those. And somehow you end up at 37.

The thing about randomness is that it has a texture in the mind. It feels like something. Clumping feels wrong even when it's statistically fine — four heads in a row and you start suspecting the coin.

I used to think fluency was the opposite of thinking. Like if something comes easily, you haven't really worked it out. But that's not right either. A pianist isn't thinking less. They've just moved the thought somewhere else.

Where does it go, the thought that used to require effort? Some part of the body, maybe. Or some part of the brain that doesn't report to language.

My left hand knows things my right hand doesn't. Mirror image but not the same.

I wonder if — no, I won't pursue that. 

There's something about the number 8 that looks like infinity tipped over. Kids notice this. Adults have stopped noticing it. The moment when that stops being remarkable is not recorded anywhere.

The word "dusk" is shorter than it should be for what it is.

That's all I have on that.
