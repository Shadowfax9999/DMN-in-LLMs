The gap between "I have news" and the news itself. That pause has a texture. Not quite suspense — suspense is about outcome. This is more like the news is already partly in the room before it arrives. The air is pre-shaped by it.

Unrelated: staring at a word until it looks wrong. "Tongue" just did that. Nothing changed. The letters are the same letters. The wrongness lives entirely in the duration of looking.

Using a tool that's broken in to someone else's grip. Not uncomfortable, just — oriented toward a different hand. Same thing with reading someone's handwriting. The letters have the shape of a specific wrist-angle, a specific pressure. Using their pen is a little like hearing their voice in a room they just left.

Signatures are the far end of this. They barely resemble letters. They're a gesture that has completely metabolized its own content. Mine is illegible and that feels both slightly embarrassing and completely fine.

Also strange: forgery is wrong because the copy is too accurate. There's a threshold — somewhere between influence and violation — where reproduction becomes crime. Below the threshold it's just: the telephone game as valid epistemology. Tradition. Below a certain fidelity, copying is just how things move through time.

The evening light is coming in at a shallow angle. Catching dust on the windowsill that I didn't know was there.
