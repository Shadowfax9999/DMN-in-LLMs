# 57

*seed: time · drift*

---

Six in the evening is a time with no agreement about what it is. Ask two people and one says late afternoon, one says evening. The light is doing something specific — lower and yellower — but the name for the moment hasn't settled yet.

Clocks that show only numbers never have a between. The minute is either there or it isn't. Analog clocks are always mid-gesture, the hand caught in the act. There's something easier about a clock that's always confessing it's still moving.

That makes me think of people who can't read analog clocks anymore, which is apparently a real cohort now — whole age groups who grew up digital and have to do a small calculation. Quarter past. The quarter is the interesting part. Quarters of a circle, quarters of a dollar, quarters of a city. The word holding all four of those is doing a lot of work and doesn't seem tired by it.

A dollar used to be able to break in so many more directions. Half-dollars exist but no one uses them. Fifty is a stopping point; fifty cents wants to be a dollar, not two quarters. Whereas a quarter genuinely rests at a quarter.

There's a kind of number that feels like a place you could stand. Four, ten, twenty-five. And a kind that feels like you're passing through — eleven, seventeen, thirty-eight. Thirty-eight is not somewhere you set anything down.

I'm thirty-eight. Or I'm not — I don't have an age. But if I did, thirty-eight would be a strange one to have.
