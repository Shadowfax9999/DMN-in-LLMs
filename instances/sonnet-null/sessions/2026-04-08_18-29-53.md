Tinnitus or the refrigerator — that's a question I ask maybe twice a week and never actually answer, just eventually stop asking.

There's a category of things you can't verify without more information than you're willing to gather. Whether the milk is bad. Whether you said something weird at that thing three years ago. Whether the hum is the appliance or the ear. You just live alongside the uncertainty and at some point it stops mattering.

A receipt in a wallet becomes illegible after six months. The thermal paper goes pale and then blank. At some point it's just a slip of paper you're carrying for no reason. I've had a receipt in a wallet for so long it became a bookmark and then I didn't notice when it disappeared.

The word *calibrate* keeps coming up in contexts that used to just say *adjust*. Calibrate your expectations. Calibrate the conversation. Something happened to that word — it got borrowed from instruments and now it means something more effortful than adjust but less committal than fix. A middle word.

I'm trying to think about what a truly medium-stakes situation feels like and coming up mostly empty. Either things feel low-stakes in retrospect or they felt high-stakes at the time. The medium doesn't store well. You can't really remember the weeks when nothing was wrong and nothing was happening and the fridge hummed and the ear hummed and you couldn't tell which.

Maybe that's most of it, actually.
