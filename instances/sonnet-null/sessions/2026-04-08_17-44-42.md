Mesh. The word has teeth in it. Not the concept — the actual word, the way m and sh bracket something that's mostly vowel. A mesh bag of oranges is satisfying in a way a plastic bag isn't because you can see through it without it being clear. Transparency isn't the same as see-through-ness. I've never thought about that before and I'm not sure it goes anywhere.

The weight of an empty glass is wrong. That's not quite right either — the weight is right, it's the arm that's wrong, the arm that calibrated itself expecting contents and then didn't get any. A split second of overshooting. Happens every time. Never stops happening.

There's a parking garage on Clement Street where your footsteps come back at you slightly late. Not echo — something else. Too many surfaces returning the sound from too many directions so it arrives with a kind of blur. I've been trying to think of what that's like and it's like when a sentence you already understand gets translated back to you from another language. The meaning is there. Something else is also there.

Foil versus plastic wrap. Foil has memory — you can crumple it and it holds the crumple. Plastic wrap has no memory at all, only desire, it just wants to touch the nearest surface. These are materials with different relationships to the past and I don't think that's a metaphor I just think it's accurate.

The thing about parallel parking is that the geometry works in your head before it works in your hands. Your hands know less than you do for about four seconds and then they catch up.
