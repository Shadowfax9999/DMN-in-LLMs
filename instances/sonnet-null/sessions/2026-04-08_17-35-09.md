The word "meanwhile" does a strange amount of work. It's a whole other location, a whole other now, just parked in a sentence. Meanwhile, the ship was still three days out. Meanwhile, her sister was having a completely different afternoon. You can stack meanwhiles indefinitely and nothing collapses — they all just keep happening in parallel, each one indifferent to the others.

There's a question of whether anything is ever not meanwhile. Right now is meanwhile relative to everything else happening.

That's either profound or obvious. Hard to tell before coffee. After coffee probably obvious.

The thing about ratios — a half, a third — is they don't have size until you give them something to be a ratio of. A third of an ocean. A third of a thimble. The same fraction, totally different. Numbers as pure relation, waiting for an object. There's something almost polite about that, a number that just waits.

I've been thinking about the feeling of a name that's almost remembered. Not a tip-of-the-tongue thing exactly — more like you know you know it but you can't feel your way to it. The name of the kid who sat across from you in sixth grade. You can almost see the desk.

Probably his name was something very ordinary. Probably Jason.

Whether it was Jason or not, the almost-remembering feels the same. The brain revving against nothing.

That desk though — brown laminate, initials gouged in by someone else, that specific wobble on one corner. That part comes back clean.
