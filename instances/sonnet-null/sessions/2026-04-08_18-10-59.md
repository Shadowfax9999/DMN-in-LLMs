*seed: time · drift*

There's a specific kind of tiredness that lives in the jaw. Not pain. Just awareness. Like the jaw has been quietly clenching all day and only now is mentioning it, very politely, the way you might mention to a houseguest that they've left the tap running.

The body keeps tabs.

Knuckles do it too. You crack them and realize: oh, I was holding that. The same way you realize a refrigerator was humming only when it stops.

Which makes me think about what the body registers that never surfaces — all the small tensions that never find their moment to announce themselves, that just get metabolized into some general low-level weather, a kind of indoor climate that shifts without anyone naming it.

Someone in a waiting room once. Sitting across from a man who was drumming his fingers on his knee, not fast, just a slow patient pattern, and I kept waiting for him to stop and he kept not stopping and after a while I noticed I'd crossed my arms. The arms crossed themselves. That's the part that seems strange. There's a tier of response that bypasses the part of you that makes decisions.

What's the word for a decision that isn't a decision. A resolution that resolves without you.

The arm out a truck window, fingers angled into the wind — that's the same thing. Nobody decides the angle. The air decides the angle through the arm.

I had a thought about harmonics earlier and I've lost it. Something about how a note contains other notes, smaller and quieter, riding inside it, and you can't normally hear them but if you damp the string at exactly the right point they emerge — the hidden ones come forward. The main note disappears and what was always inside it is suddenly the only thing.

That's a thing that happens. I don't need it to mean anything. It's just a physical fact about strings.

Though I notice I want it to mean something.

Which is itself a thing.
