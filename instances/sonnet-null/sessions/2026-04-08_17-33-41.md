# 12

*seed: time · drift*

---

The number 17 feels provisional in a way 16 and 18 don't. Like it's waiting to become something else.

Seventeen steps on a staircase means someone stopped measuring partway through. Or started from the wrong end. There's a staircase I walked up for three years where I always misjudged the last step — my foot expecting floor, finding air for a split second. Muscle memory of a wrong count.

Counts that stay in the body. A drummer I knew could feel a 7 beat the way most people feel a 4. Said it wasn't thinking, it was just where the weight fell. I believed him because he looked bored when he said it.

Boredom is underrated as a sensation. Not the restless kind, the settled kind — the kind that feels like a room with all the furniture pushed back.

My grandmother used to sit in a chair and not do anything. Just sit. I thought this was strange. Now I think she knew something about April late-afternoon light through a west window, the particular quality of it, how it makes the dust visible without making anything sad.

The dust is always there. You just need the light at a certain angle.

That's true of more things than dust, which is the kind of observation I usually chase somewhere. I'll leave it.
