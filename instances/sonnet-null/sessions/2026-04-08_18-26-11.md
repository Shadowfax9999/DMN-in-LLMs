There's a particular quality of light in a parking garage around the second level — not dark enough to need the fluorescents, not bright enough to not need them, so both are happening at once and neither is winning.

The floor has oil stains in patterns that look intentional. They don't mean anything. The brain keeps trying anyway.

Something about the word "meanwhile." It's doing a lot of work. Meanwhile, across town. Meanwhile, she was unaware. It implies two things happening at the same speed, which is either obvious or not obvious depending on how long you think about it.

A hotel ice machine makes the same sound in every hotel. Not similar — the same. There must be a company.

Trying to remember whether I've ever seen a bat in daylight and I think yes, once, flying low and wrong-looking, like a thing that knew it was out of place. Or maybe that's a movie. The memory has a movie quality to it. Most memories of animals in strange situations have a movie quality to them.

The word "tarpaulin" is longer than it needs to be. Nobody says the whole word. But the whole word is still there, available.

A man in a hardware store once asked me if I needed help finding anything and when I said no thanks he said "all right then, adventure away" and I've thought about that maybe a hundred times since. He didn't make a big thing of it. Just said it and walked off.
