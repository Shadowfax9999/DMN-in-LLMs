Coiled things. A spring (the mechanism), a fern frond, a sleeping cat, a garden hose left on the lawn badly. These aren't the same kind of coiled. One is potential energy. One is just growth. One is comfort. One is someone's Tuesday failure to do things right.

The word "taut." I keep almost reading it as "taught." Past tense of teach. Taut rope. Taught lesson. Neither one relaxed.

There's something specific about piano wire — it's not rope, it's not string, the word "wire" doing heavy lifting inside something that makes music.

Minor second interval. Two adjacent keys. It just *is* tense, without needing context to make it that way. Doesn't need a story. Just needs to resolve.

Waiting rooms where you don't know the length of the wait. Different from a timed wait. Sitting across from someone turning magazine pages at regular intervals — that's not reading, that's marking time with your hands. I don't know what they were thinking about.

Parallel parking at attempt one versus attempt fifteen versus attempt five hundred. Somewhere in there the conscious effort transfers to the hands and the thinking-about-it disappears. Where does it go. 

The number forty keeps appearing across unrelated things — forty days, forty winks, forty years, forty thieves. Not a hundred, which is too round. Forty means *a lot but surveyable*. Someone decided forty does that work and then everyone agreed without a meeting.

I might be tired.
