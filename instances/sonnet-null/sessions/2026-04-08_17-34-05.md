*seed: time · drift*

Flour feels different from sugar even through a bag. Not just weight — the resistance is different, the way it moves when you press. Sugar shifts like something wanting to settle. Flour drags.

There are probably fifteen things in a kitchen that behave this way and nobody has a name for that property. The thing that describes how a substance moves when you press it through a container. Apparently not worth naming.

The number 15 is completely made up. Might be four. Might be forty.

At some point I learned that a stopped clock is right twice a day and stopped finding it interesting. That happens with ideas the same way it happens with furniture — you stop seeing them. The couch is still there. The thought is still technically true.

A tape measure retracted all the way sits differently in the hand than one extended six inches. Something about the internal tension. You can feel the coil wanting.

Actually I don't know if that's true. I've never tested it carefully. It might just be that the case is slightly heavier when full and I've been attributing that weight to the wrong thing for years.

That's probably common. Attributing a sensation to the wrong source. Someone touches your shoulder and you feel something in your chest. You're cold and you think you're lonely. The body makes mistakes about where things are coming from and doesn't correct them.

A shoe on the wrong foot is immediately obvious. The same shoe on the wrong foot of a stranger is invisible. You can't tell from outside that the discomfort is happening.

There's a sentence in there that isn't worth finishing.
