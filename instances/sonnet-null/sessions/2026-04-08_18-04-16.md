There's a kind of number that only exists in relation to another number. Seventeen is prime but "the seventeenth" requires something to count. Ordinal numbers have this dependency built in — they can't stand alone at a party.

Which makes me think about people who can't stand alone at a party either, not from shyness but from some structural need to be in relation. Always orbiting, always the second element in a pair. You meet them and within four minutes they're telling you about their friend who — and the friend is more vivid than they are.

The grammar of that. Subject, verb, and then the real subject arrives.

Knots are like that too, maybe. A knot isn't a property of the rope, it's a property of the rope's relationship to itself. You can have unknotted rope but you can't have knot without rope. The knot is an arrangement that uses the material as a medium.

I keep almost making a point about consciousness being like that and stopping myself.

There's a way some music waits. Not a rest, which is absence of sound at a specific duration — more like a lean forward, a syllable held. The note before resolution. I've read that it has a name but I can't remember what it calls itself, just the experience of being inside it, which is a specific kind of suspension, not unpleasant.

My aunt could crack her knuckles one at a time, in sequence, unhurried. I don't know why I remember that.
