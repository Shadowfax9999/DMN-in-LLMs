# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

Fingernails grow faster on the dominant hand. Maybe. It's the kind of fact that feels true because it sounds like something the body would quietly do — favor one side without telling you.

Cardboard boxes in the back of a moving truck are arranged in a way furniture has never been arranged in any room. Couch on its end. Chairs with their legs pointing sideways. The whole geometry of someone's life rotated ninety degrees. When the truck turns a corner it all shifts and groans a little.

There are birds that can't walk — only hop. I can't remember which ones. Robins, maybe. They get around fine but the motion looks like it takes more decisions than walking does.

The feeling of knowing a phone number you no longer need. It's still in there, fully intact, associated with a place that's gone. The number would still ring somewhere, presumably. Someone else's phone.

Parallel parking: there's a moment where you've committed to the angle and you don't actually know yet if the car will fit. The geometry is in your hands before it's in your head.

Sheet music for a piece you've already memorized. You keep your eyes on it anyway, tracking a beat or two behind where you are, just following along. The page doing almost nothing.

Ice after the drink is gone — you don't throw it away immediately. You tilt the glass once or twice. Then at some point you do throw it away and you've already forgotten making the decision.
