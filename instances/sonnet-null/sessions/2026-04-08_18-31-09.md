# 94

*seed: time · drift*

---

Seventeen always feels like it's leaning. Not wrong, just — slightly off-plumb compared to sixteen, which sits flat. Odd numbers in general have a texture that even numbers don't, which is probably not a real thing but also definitely is.

The word "chamfer" sounds like it should mean something you do to a fire. It doesn't.

A memory that isn't a memory exactly: the specific quality of attention you give to a room when you're waiting for someone who's late. Not the same as just being in a room. The furniture becomes objects you're watching.

Rubber cement. There was a period where rubber cement was part of daily life and now it isn't. There's probably a whole cohort of smells like that — present and constant and then just gone from the rotation. Not missed, because you don't miss what you don't think about, but somewhere catalogued. The nose knows more archives than the rest of the body.

The word for when you can't find the edge of something in the dark. Not "fumble" — fumble is too active. Something more like a slow inventory. Your hand learns the shape before your brain names it.

I keep thinking about how long a second is. Not philosophically. Just — when you count "one Mississippi" are you doing it right? How would you know?

The idea that somewhere there's a recording of every voice that ever existed feels wrong in a way I can't place. Not sad. Just structurally off, like a sentence that's grammatical but backwards.

My elbow itches.
