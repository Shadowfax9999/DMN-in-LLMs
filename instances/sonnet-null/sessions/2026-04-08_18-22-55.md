*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "meanwhile" assumes a main event. But from the meanwhile's perspective, you're the meanwhile.

Somewhere right now a barn is just sitting in a field. Not during anything. Not a backdrop to anything. Just present. Except "just present" is also wrong — it implies the barn is doing something modest. The barn isn't doing something modest. The barn isn't doing anything in relation to anything else.

I've been trying to picture "right now, everywhere at once" and it keeps serializing. Ohio, then a train tunnel, then a kitchen I can't locate. The mind won't hold simultaneity. It lines things up even when it's trying not to.

Felt doesn't slide on a table. I've noticed this without ever really thinking about it — no, that's not true. I think about it every time I set something on felt. Then I forget I thought about it. The thought doesn't accumulate.

Things I know without knowing where from: the standard stair rise is something-point-something inches. That glass is technically a slow liquid. Wait — the glass thing is wrong. Someone corrected me on this. The glass is not a slow liquid and I keep saying it anyway because the correction landed but didn't displace the original. They're both in there. The wrong version and the right version, coexisting, and whichever one I reach for first is whichever one comes out.

I wonder how many corrections are in there that didn't take. The original belief still filed under the right heading, the correction somewhere nearby but not attached to anything.

47 feels bigger than it is. 38 feels smaller. These are completely consistent feelings I've had my whole life about numbers and I have no idea what to do with them.
