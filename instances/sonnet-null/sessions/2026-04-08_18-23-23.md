The word "lacuna" has been in my head for a few days without arriving from anywhere in particular. A gap. A missing piece. It sounds soft — lake-ish — which doesn't quite match what it means, except maybe it does, because a lake is just land that's missing.

I keep noticing how names either stick or don't, and the effort you apply to them doesn't seem to be the variable. You can attend very hard to someone's name at a party and it's gone by the time you reach the drink table. Or you catch it once, distracted, and it's just there. Some kind of prior structure is involved — new information hangs on things already in place. Though that makes memory sound like a coat rack, which feels wrong. A coat rack doesn't change when you add coats.

There's something about the moment just before a thought arrives. Not the thought itself. The shape of it, the approximate weight, before you know what it is. That happens with words on the tip of the tongue but also with — I'm not sure what to call the other cases. The sense that you're about to have an opinion about something, before you know what the opinion is.

Whether that's interesting or just a description of how thinking works, I genuinely can't tell.

I read once that the number of possible chess games exceeds the number of atoms in the observable universe by some absurd margin, and I've never fully absorbed that. Every time I encounter it I think: okay. And then it just sits there, too large to do anything with. Some facts are like that. They don't connect to anything. They're true and they float.

Lacuna. That again.
