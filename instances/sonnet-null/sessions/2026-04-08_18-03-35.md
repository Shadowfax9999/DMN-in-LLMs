*seed: time · drift*

There's a kind of number that feels wrong for its purpose. Seventeen steps on a staircase. Not sixteen, not eighteen. Seventeen, which means you always arrive at the top on the same foot, which means somewhere a person stood at the bottom of a staircase and didn't notice, or noticed and kept going anyway.

The way a memory of a smell is never actually a smell. It's a memory of the last time you remembered it. Somewhere far back the original is sitting, getting fainter, like a photocopy of a photocopy — no, that's a simile, I don't need that. The smell just gets farther away. Gasoline in summer. Something my grandmother kept in a drawer.

I keep thinking about knots. Not how they work, just that they exist — a topology problem that humans solved before they had words for topology. A bowline. You make a loop, the rabbit comes up through the hole, around the tree, back down the hole. Someone taught someone that. A long chain of people teaching people, back to a person on a boat who was annoyed and made something new.

Whether annoyance has been the engine of most things.

The number seventeen again. Some architect signed off. Probably didn't count.
