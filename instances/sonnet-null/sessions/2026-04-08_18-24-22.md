There's a point in learning anything where you stop translating and start thinking directly in it. Chords become a spatial shape before they become a sound. Then the sound. Then neither — just a hand moving.

I keep meaning to understand what that shift is. Not the mechanism. Just what it feels like from the inside, whether there's an inside at all or just a before and an after with nothing between them. The transition is invisible. You don't notice acquiring a habit; you notice having one.

There's a game I used to play in waiting rooms and classrooms: pick two people at random and figure out their relationship from posture alone. Not who they are. Just the geometry. Someone slightly turned away but not fully. Someone listening harder than the conversation seems to require. You can get it wrong and still learn something from being wrong — the error shows you what you expected to be there, which is sometimes more interesting than what's actually there.

The filled-in parts of memory are like that. They tell you what you assumed would fit.

Rapport is mutual without being symmetrical. Two people can both be in rapport and doing completely different things to get there. One leads, one mirrors, and the distance closes anyway. Though I might be half-remembering something I read once and building the rest from scratch. That happens more than I notice.

I've been thinking about calibration. How you know something is tuned correctly without a fixed reference. Some instruments tune to themselves — open strings, harmonics — the tuning is internally consistent even if it drifts from concert pitch. The instrument makes sense to itself. 

That's probably not just an instrument thing.
