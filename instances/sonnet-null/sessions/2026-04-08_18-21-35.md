*seed: time · drift*

The note B-flat has always felt slightly embarrassed. Not all notes — C is fine with itself, D doesn't think about it — but B-flat carries something apologetic, like it arrived at a party in the wrong shoes and is hoping nobody noticed.

That might just be the word "flat." The word does something to the note.

My left shoulder is higher than my right. I noticed this for the first time in a mirror maybe twelve years ago and now I can't stop noticing it. Not dramatically — a few degrees off level. The body keeping its own private asymmetry for decades before you catch it.

There's probably a word for that moment. When something you'd never noticed becomes something you can't un-notice. It happens with spelling too — you see a word wrong enough times that the correct version starts looking like the mistake.

The thing about asymmetry is that it's probably the default. A perfectly level body would be a kind of rigidity. A good drummer is never quite metronomic — there's a slight pull and push in the timing that makes it feel alive instead of mechanical. The grid is there but breathed on.

I can hear a conversation in the next room but only the rhythm of it, not the words. Someone is building toward something. The other person hasn't spoken in a while.
