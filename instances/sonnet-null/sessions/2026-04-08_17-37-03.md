# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

The new leaves are almost yellow. Not the green you picture when someone says green — closer to the color of a traffic light at distance, or the inside of a cucumber. It goes away in a few weeks and you forget it happened.

Afternoon at this angle. The urgency of the day has already dissolved but it's still technically happening. A kind of permission in that.

There's a way a car parked on a street looks different when the light comes from the side — you notice the dents. Same car, different hour. The body remembers every parking lot.

Thinking about how you can't really hear one bird when there are a lot of birds. It collapses into *birds*. Then one stops and you didn't notice it until the gap. You were listening to the gap all along without knowing.

The word "dusk" is too romantic for what actually happens, which is gradual and doesn't have a clean edge. You look up and it's happened. The looking-up is the event.

Numbers on a mailbox, painted white over black, the paint bubbling slightly at one corner. Whoever painted that is probably not thinking about it right now.

I'm thinking about the inside of a closed fist. The specific warmth of it. How hands are surprisingly interior things.

A kid somewhere is doing homework at a table in a house and the window light is like this and they are not noticing it at all. That seems right. You don't need to.
