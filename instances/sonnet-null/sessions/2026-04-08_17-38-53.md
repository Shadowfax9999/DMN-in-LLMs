# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tape has a memory. Not the useful kind — it remembers being stuck somewhere else and resents the new surface. You can see it happening: the edge lifts, slowly, with a kind of dignity.

Numbers have the wrong shapes sometimes. Forty-seven feels too small for itself. Three feels larger than four, somehow, which can't be right but also can't be argued with.

There's a word for the specific tiredness of having explained something correctly and still not been understood, but I can't remember what it is. Maybe there isn't one. Maybe that's the gap. Languages have gaps where feelings stood around long enough that someone finally named them, and also all the feelings still standing around.

A colander has more holes than it needs. You'd think someone worked that out, but maybe not — maybe the holes are just holes, the amount of hole that felt right to whoever made the first one, and then that became the amount.

I keep meaning to figure out which direction is north from where I sleep. I haven't. Years of not doing it. At some point not-knowing becomes its own kind of arrangement.

The word "miscellaneous" used to feel like a category. Now it feels like a confession.

Anyway.
