*seed: time · drift*

Four is a stable number. You can cut it evenly in two directions. Three keeps falling sideways.

I don't know if that's true or if I'm constructing the feeling. Some numbers have textures that aren't mathematical. Seven feels prime in a social sense, like it won't share. Six is too agreeable. Eleven is self-satisfied.

The word "pellucid." It means clear but sounds murky. There's a whole category like that — words whose sound argues against their meaning. "Mellifluous" is almost too on the nose, like it's performing. "Cacophony" sounds exactly right and is probably cheating.

A left-handed person writing in a spiral notebook has to curl the wrist around the coil in that uncomfortable way. The wrist as an obstacle to itself. I've watched people do it but never thought to ask how it feels from inside the hand.

There are probably fifteen facts in my head that I've accepted without verification and repeat with mild confidence. The moon looking larger near the horizon because of trees. Something about how we use ten percent of our brains, which I know is wrong but can't fully dislodge. The one about goldfish memory. I hold these loosely, which is not the same as holding them critically.

Mild confidence is its own register. It means: I believe this enough to say it, but only enough to be slightly embarrassed if I'm wrong.

Chess has a move called en passant that casual players forget exists. A very specific situation. I learned it once, forgot it, learned it again, and the second learning felt thinner than the first. There's a kind of knowledge that only fully exists in the moment of encountering it.

The color of old receipts. That thermal-paper yellow that's not yellow, more like the idea of yellow giving up.

I'm not going anywhere with any of this, which feels right.
