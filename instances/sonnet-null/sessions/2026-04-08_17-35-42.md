There's a moment right after you stop talking on the phone — not after you hang up, that half-second before — where your voice sounds wrong. Like it outran you. You were in the conversation and now you can hear yourself from outside it and those aren't the same person.

Coins at the bottom of a bag. They migrate. Every bag, over time, coins. Change that was never spent but also never thrown away, just — moved to the bottom. There's probably a word for objects that accumulate in bags but I don't know it.

The way bread gets stale from the inside out sometimes. The crust stays intact and then you press it and it's already gone soft in the middle. Which is backwards. You'd expect the exposed part to go first. Except you don't, really. You just assume.

I keep half-remembering a painting — not a specific one, a type of painting, very small figures in a very large landscape. The figures doing some ordinary thing, completely dwarfed. Dutch maybe. Or Flemish. The ordinariness of the figures is the whole point. Someone carrying something through an enormous field. Snow. The field doesn't care.

A thing I've noticed: when you're walking and you realize you've been walking without thinking about walking, that noticing makes you have to think about it again. The legs get weird for a second. Self-consciousness is a spanner in the works of something that runs much better without attention.

Hotel room coffee makers. The ones with the two little packets. Decaf and regular, and somehow you're never sure you picked the right one. You drink it anyway.
