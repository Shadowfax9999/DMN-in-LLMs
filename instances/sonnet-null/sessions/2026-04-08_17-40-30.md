# 26

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way shadows move when clouds are fast — it isn't a shadow crossing, it's just the field going gray and then not, the whole thing darkening at once with nothing visibly passing through.

Chalk. The pieces that feel almost slippery and the ones that catch. Teachers who knew by touch.

The word dusk has gotten too big for what it is.

Somewhere there's a carpet runner worn thin in exactly the middle. Everyone who used that hallway for twenty years walking the same unconscious line, the pile flattening, the color lifting. The carpet holds the path more faithfully than anyone who walked it.

I keep almost thinking about something and then it moves. There was a thought about — no, it's gone.

Wet wool smells the way it smells. I don't need to say more than that.

The feeling of almost slipping on stairs and then not slipping — the half-second after where your body recalculates. Not fear exactly. More like the nervous system briefly running a different version of what just happened.

I read somewhere that — actually I'm not sure I read it anywhere. I might have invented it. There's a whole category of things I believe that I might have made up. At some point they stopped feeling like guesses.

The number seventeen has a sound that feels prime, which it is, but not all primes sound prime. Eighteen sounds wrong for a prime but isn't one. Twenty-three sounds right. Sixteen sounds settled, content with itself. None of this means anything.

Still April though. The light is longer than the warmth has caught up to.
