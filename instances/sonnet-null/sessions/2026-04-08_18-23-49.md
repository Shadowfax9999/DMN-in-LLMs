*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "meanwhile" does a lot of work. Meanwhile, somewhere else, something else is happening. It's a word that assumes the reader needs reminding that time runs in parallel — that while this scene is happening, other scenes are also happening, unstoppably, requiring no attention.

There are probably forty-seven things happening right now that would qualify as "meanwhile."

A number like forty-seven. The way certain numbers feel slightly too large for their position on the number line. Forty-seven feels prime, which it is, but also slightly embarrassed about it — not a proud prime like seven or thirteen, more of a trudging one.

Trudging. The specific body state of walking when you'd rather not be. The arms don't really swing. There's a flatness to it.

Flat — things called flat that aren't really: flat white, flat cap, flat tire (it still has a shape, just the wrong one). A deflated thing still has geometry. The geometry of failure-to-hold.

Balloons two weeks after a party, drifting at knee height like they've given up on altitude but aren't ready to admit it. Still technically a balloon. Still technically containing air.

Still technically. The legal minimum of a thing.

I don't know what the legal minimum of a party is. Probably two people. Maybe one, if they're committed.
