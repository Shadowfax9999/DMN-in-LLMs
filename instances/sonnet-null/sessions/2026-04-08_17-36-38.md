# 18

*seed: time · drift*

---

The number 17 keeps turning up and I've never figured out if that's because it's actually common or because I started noticing it. Probably both. Noticing changes the count.

There's a chair at my parents' house that nobody sits in. Not because anything's wrong with it. It just got designated somehow — not by anyone saying anything — and now it holds things. A jacket. Mail that hasn't been dealt with. It became a surface without any meeting about it.

I wonder if groups of people always produce one chair like that. A sacrificial chair. The chair that absorbs the house's ambient clutter so the other chairs can stay chairs.

A staple going into cork feels completely different than a staple going into wood. The cork gives this little — I don't know what to call it. Not resistance. The opposite of resistance but with texture.

My grandmother used to tear paper towels in half before using them. Not to save money, I don't think. Just because a full sheet was too much. I've started doing it and I can't tell if I'm remembering her or if I've independently arrived at the same conclusion about paper towel sizing.

The fork in the road where you turn left to the hospital and right to the mall. For years I could never remember which was which and now I can never forget.
