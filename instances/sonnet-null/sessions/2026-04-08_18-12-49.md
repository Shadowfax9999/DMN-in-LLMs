The word "meanwhile" assumes a lot. It stitches two timelines together and tells you they're simultaneous — which is true, but it's also a kind of fiction, the fiction being that one of them is the main one and the other is merely concurrent. Meanwhile, back at the ranch. As if the ranch is where we left something running.

There are probably nine conversations happening right now within a block of wherever this is. All of them think they're the foreground.

A phone feels lighter when it's dead. Not figuratively — actually lighter, or seems to. Maybe it's the expectation that went out of it.

I keep meaning to think about whether "brittle" is a taste or a texture. It's a candy. It's also a word for a kind of failure — the opposite of ductile, which I know from somewhere without knowing where. Ductile means it bends before it breaks. Some metals do this. Some arguments do this. You push and they give a little and you think you're getting somewhere and then you realize they've just deformed.

That might be too close to a metaphor. Forget it.

Ductile. Brittle. There's probably a word between them that nobody uses.

The number eighteen has always felt unfinished to me. Seventeen is prime and has a kind of integrity. Nineteen also prime. Eighteen is just two nines, or three sixes, or the age at which things are supposed to begin — which is a strange age to pick, just past the middle of the second decade, not round, not prime, just: now.
