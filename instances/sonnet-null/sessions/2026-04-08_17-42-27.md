The word "miscellaneous" takes up a lot of drawer space for what it contains. Usually just batteries of uncertain charge, a few twist-ties, one foreign coin.

There's a ratio problem with drawers like that — more label than contents, somehow. Not a moral problem. Just a thing you notice.

A friend said once that she organizes by "where would I look for this?" rather than "where does this belong?" — and for a moment that seemed like wisdom, and then it seemed like the same thing said differently, and then neither.

Thinking about the left hand. It does things the right hand gets credit for. Holds the paper while the pen moves. Steadies the jar while the lid turns. There's a whole category of work that's basically holding still.

A bus idling. The specific vibration that comes up through plastic seats. Not unpleasant, actually. More like being held lightly by something large and indifferent.

The phrase "put a pin in it" — no, not going down that road.

A cousin used to say "I'll cross that bridge" without the "when I come to it," and it made the phrase mean something different. Vaguer. More like a plan than a deferral.

Salt. You don't really think about salt. It's just there and then things taste right. Same with the letter H in words where it's not doing anything. "Hour." Fine.

The feeling of a decision you made a long time ago still being the right one. That's not dramatic. It's more like checking a box that's already checked.
