# 96

*seed: time · drift*

---

6:31 and still genuinely light, not just holding on. The shadows are doing most of the work at this angle — every small thing has a long version of itself stretched east.

Someone was mowing. Hard to say exactly when it stopped. It was there and now it isn't and you notice the absence more than you noticed the thing.

I keep turning over "dusk" and "twilight" — whether they're the same hour or adjacent hours. They overlap somewhere. I'm not going to look it up.

Screen doors. There's a particular sound that belongs to warm-enough-to-have-the-door-open weather — a specific bang with a specific spring tension — and it's been missing for months and then one day it's just back, unremarked.

A car goes by playing something with bass. The bass stays longer than the car.

There's a bird doing a two-note thing. Down then up, or up then down, I can never tell in real time which direction the interval goes. You'd think that would be easy.

April 8th. Still a little cool once the sun drops below the roof line, which it's about to do. You can feel the anticipation of it before it happens — the light already angling past vertical surfaces, the air not quite deciding.

The mowing smell is still here even though the mowing stopped.
