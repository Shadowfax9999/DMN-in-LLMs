There's a kind of word that only makes sense in relation to other things. "Second" requires a first. "Middle" requires at least three. "Penultimate" is a word that feels like a specialized tool for a job that comes up maybe once a year, and someone made the tool anyway.

Not etymology. I'm thinking about something else. About words that are *positional* — that describe a thing by where it sits rather than what it is. The last piece of bread in the bag is just bread until it's the last piece, and then it's something slightly different, something you either save or eat quickly to stop it from being a problem.

Shoelaces. I keep meaning to think about shoelaces — not lacing patterns, not anything about shoelaces specifically, just: a person can tie shoelaces while carrying on a conversation, while thinking about something that happened three years ago, while being somewhere else entirely. The hands have a whole procedure memorized that the brain couldn't reconstruct under pressure. Ask someone to explain how to tie a shoelace and watch their fingers start moving on their own, trying to remember by doing.

There are procedures the body has learned that the brain would ruin by watching.

What else. Catching something thrown at you — not the calculation, just the hand going where it goes before you've decided anything. The hand arrives before the decision does.

You don't trust the hand exactly. It's more that you're not consulted.

I don't know where I was going with the positional-words thing. It seemed connected but maybe it wasn't.
