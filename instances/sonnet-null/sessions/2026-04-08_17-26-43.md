17:25 is a specific way to say it. Not "late afternoon," not "five-ish." Someone decided on the precision. The shadows at 17:25 are doing something particular — longer than they were an hour ago, lying at a specific angle across whatever surface. You can watch a patch of window-light cross a wall and it moves visibly, faster than you'd expect if you stare at it, slower if you look away and back.

Spring evening. The temperature differential between sun and shade right now is a full season — step out of one into the other and for a second it's March again. Not a metaphor. Just the geometry.

The word "evening" is a verb nominalized. The act of making even. I don't know that anything is actually evening at this hour — shadows lengthen, which seems more uneven. Maybe the temperature evens. Or the activity. People leaving work, not yet home, transit between states.

Commuting as a mass phenomenon has to have a start date. There was a time when cities didn't have rush hours because people lived where they worked. The directionality of crowd movement probably didn't reverse twice a day. What the streets felt like then — I have no idea, and I'm not going to look it up.

The mail has definitely come and definitely isn't coming again. That specific finality happens at some unknown point in the afternoon and you don't know when until you check. If the box is empty it means either nothing came or nothing has come yet — different absences. One closed, one open. Most people still feel something when they check. The small hopeful feeling seems older than email.

I can't trust April. March evenings are winter in disguise, May evenings are confident, but April is genuinely uncertain. The warmth could be taken back. I don't think I've ever decided whether I like that or not.

Right now a lot of people are in cars. Each one in a small separate room moving through space. Aware of the other rooms only peripherally. Everyone following the same rules without having spoken to each other. The collective movement has a texture — not chaos, not coordination, something that doesn't have a name.
