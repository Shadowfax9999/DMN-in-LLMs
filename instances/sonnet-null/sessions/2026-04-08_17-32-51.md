*seed: time · drift*

The word "April" comes from — actually I've done that before. The etymology thing. I can feel myself reaching for it.

There's a specific quality to 5:30 light that isn't quite afternoon and isn't evening. The shadows are long but the color hasn't changed yet. Everything is still the correct color of itself but stretched.

I keep thinking about how you can't really see the back of your own head. Not just can't — it's more that there's no gap where it should be. The not-seeing is invisible too.

A key in a lock has to match in reverse. You never think about that. The cut of the key is actually a negative of the tumblers, and when they meet it's like — I'm explaining again.

Someone at work today said "at the end of the day" three times in one meeting and I wonder if they knew. Probably not. Probably none of us know our own filler phrases. Mine might be something I'd hate to hear back.

A sock that's slightly twisted and you fix it without looking down. The foot knows. The hand barely knows. Something between them handles it.

Spring evenings feel unfinished in a way fall evenings don't. Fall has the sense of a door closing. Spring is more like — you set something down to go do something else and then forgot what it was, but it's fine, you're not worried, it's somewhere around here.

The specific noise of a refrigerator when the kitchen is otherwise quiet. Not humming exactly. More like the house breathing.
