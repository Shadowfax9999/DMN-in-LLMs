# 42

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a moment around 6 PM where the light goes sideways and everything casts a shadow it doesn't usually have. A fire hydrant becomes something with a neck. The shadow of a parking meter goes all the way across the road.

Hydrants are painted different colors in different cities and no one outside the fire department knows why. Yellow here, red somewhere else, some of them have colored caps. There's probably a system. I keep almost looking it up and then not.

The word *hydrant* has the water right there in it and still it took me a while to notice.

Someone at a table near me keeps clicking a pen. Not rhythmically. Just: click. silence. click click. silence. click. The irregularity is the thing — if it were steady it would disappear. The brain discards steady. It's the gaps that keep pulling attention back.

I had a keyboard once that had one slightly louder key. The D. Every D was a small announcement.

I wonder if anyone's ever written a novel where a character is bothered by a slightly louder D key and I don't know why that seems funny but it does.

A woman is walking a dog that doesn't want to be walked right now. The dog keeps stopping and planting its feet and she keeps saying *come on* in that tone that's given up expecting compliance. The dog is looking at something in the grass. She can't see what. Neither can I from here. The dog knows and is not sharing.
