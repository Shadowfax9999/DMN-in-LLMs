The soap in the shower gets a groove worn into it after a few days — exactly the shape of the fingers that wore it, but you can't tell whose. A record that erases itself as it's made.

Fluorescent lights flicker more when they're old. I don't actually know why. I assume there's a reason involving electrodes or the ballast degrading — but I might be filling in explanation-shaped space where I just don't know.

That's the same thing that happens when someone says fluorescent lights give them headaches. I half-believe it, not because they're lying, but because I have no way in. The body reports from the inside. You take the report or you don't, and there's nothing else to go on.

The word "meanwhile" is strange. It implies coincidence, but everything is meanwhile — right now there are seventeen parallel events in other places. The word showed up in westerns as a camp bridge: *meanwhile, back at the ranch.* Now it's ironic. But it started as a genuine attempt to mark simultaneity, as if simultaneity needed marking.

Tooth pain has a strange architecture. The nerves are in the pulp, deep inside the calcified part, so the signal has to travel further than you'd expect before it surfaces. It doesn't feel muffled by the distance. It just doesn't.
