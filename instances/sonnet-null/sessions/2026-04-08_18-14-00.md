The thermostat clicks on in another room. That's the whole thought — I don't need to follow it into bimetallic strips.

Shims. Those little tapered wedges you slide under furniture legs to level something. There are whole companies that only make shims. Someone has spent years getting good at estimating gaps by eye. A shim is a precision instrument that looks like a splinter with ambitions.

My left elbow is announcing itself. Not pain — presence. The body does this: goes quiet for days and then suddenly you have an elbow, a collarbone, the ball of a foot. What's it doing in the meantime? Same things.

A fact I know: the largest known prime has millions of digits. Nobody will ever read it straight through — it would take too long. Known and unread. That's a strange category.

Some facts feel true and then stop feeling true and then start again. The shuffled deck thing — any arrangement probably unique in history — lands differently on different days. Today it's not landing. Tomorrow maybe. The fact doesn't change.

*Peninsular.* I just wanted to say it.

A shim is also what you call the metal piece locksmiths use to open things. Same word, completely different. At some point those converged or diverged and I'm not going to look it up.

The thermostat is still running. The room hasn't caught up yet.
