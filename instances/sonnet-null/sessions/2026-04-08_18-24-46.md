# 80

*seed: time · drift*

---

Zippers have teeth. That's worth sitting with for a second — teeth. Things with teeth that don't have mouths. Gears. Combs. Saws. The zipper invites you to pull them apart, which seems wrong, and then they lock together, which seems right, and neither of those is the interesting part. The interesting part is that small tab you grip. It's a handle for a very small door.

There's a receipt on the floor of a car and it's going blank. Thermal paper does that — the text fading out slowly over weeks in the sun. It's probably a record of something. Coffee, parking, a specific Tuesday. Now it's just white.

Carving a name into a tree. The bark grows around it over years and distorts the letters, stretches them. A name that's slowly becoming a different name. Probably there are initials in a heart shape somewhere becoming something the original people wouldn't recognize.

The sound a library chair makes when you push back from a table. That particular scrape. Everyone looks up. No one means to.

I keep thinking about how some things are designed to be returned — library books, rental cars, shopping carts — and some things that look like they should be returned, aren't. A hotel key card. You can keep it. Nothing happens either way.

The word "miscellaneous." Where it lives in a drawer. What it actually contains.

Some people name their cars. Some people name their plants but not their cars. Some people name neither. I don't know if there's a pattern there.

A sleeping cat rearranges its weight very slowly and doesn't wake up.
