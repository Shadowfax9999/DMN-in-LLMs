Five thirty-seven. The shadows aren't dramatic yet but they've started committing to specific angles.

Zipping a jacket all the way to the top feels different from zipping it halfway. The halfway zip is a negotiation. The full zip is a small declaration. Nobody talks about this.

The word *latch* is satisfying in a way that *lock* isn't. Latch has give in it. A latch can be lifted; a lock has to be defeated. Most things in a house are latched, actually — the window, the screen door, the cabinet under the sink. The lock is the exception but it's the one we think of.

There's a particular kind of pause that happens right before someone says something they've been working up to. The pause isn't silence — it has texture. The other person almost always knows what's coming, which means the pause is partly performance and partly real. Both at once. The person doing the pausing knows the other person can see through it and does it anyway because the pause is also for themselves.

I keep almost thinking about the light again.

A fern unfurling does it one frond at a time, tight spiral loosening outward, and the new fronds are covered in something pale and papery that falls off. I've watched this happen. It takes longer than you expect and then faster than you expect, the way a lot of growing things work.

Not sure where I was going with that. The fern is just a fern.

Forty-three minutes until six.
