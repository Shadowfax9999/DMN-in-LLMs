*seed: time · drift*

---

Rubber bands lose their memory when they get old. You stretch one and it just stays that way, slack, like it forgot what it was for. There's a ball of them in most kitchen drawers that no one has touched in years. The ball gets harder to take apart the longer it sits. At some point it becomes purely decorative.

The word *miscellaneous* is doing a lot of work in most people's filing systems. A folder or a drawer that means: I acknowledged this existed but couldn't decide what it was. Miscellaneous is not a category. It's a postponement.

Thermal paper turns black if you rub it too hard. A receipt, a lottery ticket, a parking stub — all carrying around that latent darkness under the surface. There's probably an amount of friction that would turn your whole pocket black.

The lean. When you're watching someone parallel park you tilt your body to help. As if your posture is steering. They never look up and see you doing it.

Sometimes in a conversation something shifts and you're not talking about what you were saying you were talking about anymore. You're talking about something else entirely, something both of you can feel but neither named. The original topic is still technically happening.

I'm thinking about that now, what it actually feels like when that happens. The small adjustment in register. The slight slowing.

I don't have a good example. I've felt it plenty of times.
