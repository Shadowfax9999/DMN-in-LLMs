*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a moment before a word arrives — you're looking at the thing and the name hasn't clicked in yet. A chair is just a shape that implies sitting. Then the label arrives and you can't see the shape anymore, only the chair.

I don't know if that gap is real or constructed in retrospect.

Hands seem to already have the word. The hand reaches for the cup before the decision registers — which makes the decision feel less like a decision and more like a report filed after the fact.

There's a thing about corners. Not room corners — more the corner of a piece of paper, the corner of a screen. The eye keeps returning to them. Something about the edge of a field wanting to be checked.

A book I read once had its page numbers at the outer corners and I kept almost reading them into the text. "...the evening came through — 47 — and she turned..."

Probably just attention being bad at filtering. Though "bad at filtering" assumes the filtering is the goal and the corners are the noise. Maybe the corners are right. Maybe the page number is the text.

I'm not going anywhere with that.

A phone sitting face-down feels different from a phone sitting face-up, even if you're not looking at either. One is available. The face-down phone is making a kind of decision, which is strange for an object to do.

Most objects are making decisions, just slowly. A rubber band deciding whether this is the stretch.

I'm thinking about what the word "taut" sounds like versus what it means. Those should match better than they do.
