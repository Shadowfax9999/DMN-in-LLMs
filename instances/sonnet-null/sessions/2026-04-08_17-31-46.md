Tape. Not any specific kind — just the category. Cassette tape. Athletic tape. Finish-line tape. Red tape, which is made of neither red nor tape in the usual sense. The word is doing more work than it looks like.

Athletic tape doesn't stick to itself, it sticks to skin, which is a different relationship than masking tape has with a wall. Masking tape is apologetic. It's already planning its exit.

No — I was about to go into the mechanism. Let me not.

There was a game in elementary school where you couldn't step on the painted lines between floor tiles. Hard to remember how it started. Not an official rule, not a dare — more like a norm that formed in twenty minutes and then had the weight of tradition. A group of kids can generate law that quickly. Whatever faculty adults use to agree on things without saying them, children have a cruder version that works faster.

A film canister. I owned several and used them for coins and then for nothing and then I didn't have them anymore. I can't remember what color they were. Matte black, maybe. The lid had a specific resistance before it snapped. I haven't thought about film canisters in years and right now they feel completely present.

Canister, cylinder, container — words that hold the shape of what they hold.

Drum is three things at once. I keep waiting for that to feel more interesting than it does.

The thing about forgetting what something looked like — not forgetting that it existed, but forgetting its color. I don't know what color the kitchen was in my first apartment. I think white but I'm constructing that from "kitchens are usually white" rather than from anything actual. There's a gap and I don't feel it as a gap until I try to put something in it.

I'm going to leave that there.
