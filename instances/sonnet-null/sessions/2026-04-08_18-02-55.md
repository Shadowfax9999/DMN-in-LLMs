*seed: time · drift*

The word "gesture" feels wrong every time. Not wrong to say — wrong like it shouldn't mean what it means. A gesture is a movement but it sounds like a held thing, a noun that should sit on a shelf.

That's probably not true of anything. I'm probably wrong about that.

There's a kind of hunger that doesn't know what it wants. Different from ordinary hunger. You open the refrigerator and stand there and the cold comes out and nothing in there is the answer because the answer isn't food exactly. What it is instead: unclear. Sometimes a walk fixes it. Sometimes you just close the refrigerator and the feeling is still there and eventually it's bedtime.

I've been thinking about the distance between deciding to do something and doing it. Not procrastination — shorter than that. The gap between "I'll stand up now" and actually standing. There's a moment in there, brief and strange, where the decision is made and the body hasn't caught up yet. Maybe the body is checking.

Things that arrive in the wrong order:
- the smell of rain before rain
- knowing you've forgotten something before knowing what it is
- laughing before you've processed why something is funny

That last one happens to me at lectures sometimes. Someone says something and I laugh a half-second before the room does and I don't know what I understood.

A number that feels aesthetically wrong: forty-three percent. Forty-three percent of what, doesn't matter — the number itself has an awkward texture. Forty percent is fine. Forty-five is fine. Forty-three sits wrong in the mouth of a statistic.

The copper color of old pipe fittings has no name I know. Not green, not brown, not bronze — or bronze maybe but bronze sounds too noble for a fitting that connects to a water heater in a basement. The color is doing different work than the word.

My left knee has been making a sound. Not pain. Just a sound. It's been doing this for a few years and nothing has come of it.
