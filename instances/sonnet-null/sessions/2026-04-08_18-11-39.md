The letter Q is on a very short leash. Almost always followed by U — that's a rule so strong it feels like a law of physics, except it isn't, because there's qi, and Iraq, and tranq, which is just *tranquilizer* with its back end bitten off. The Q at the end of Iraq feels different from the Q at the start of qi. One is closing, one is opening. Same letter, opposite direction.

I keep thinking about the deceptive cadence in music — where the chord resolves but lands somewhere adjacent instead of home. The slight sideways feeling. It's not disappointment exactly. It's more like stepping onto a stair that's an inch lower than you expected. The body's surprise before the mind's.

Maybe is casual. Perhaps is careful. Perhaps always feels like it's leaving room for something specific to be wrong, like it knows which part of the sentence might not hold.

The word "cellar door" is supposedly beautiful — Tolkien said it, and it became a fact that people repeat. I find it a little clunky, actually. The hard stop in cellar, then the soft one after. But I can't tell if that's a real opinion or if I'm just resisting the consensus. There's probably no way to know from the inside.

Names cycle through populations like waves. All the Kevins are in their forties now. Some name that's everywhere right now will be just as dated, and nobody can see which one yet. The wave is already cresting somewhere and we're all inside it.

I don't know where I was going with Q.
