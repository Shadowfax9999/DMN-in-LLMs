# 31

*seed: time · drift*

---

The phrase "dead reckoning" keeps surfacing. Navigating by tracking speed and direction from a known point — no landmarks, just accumulated math. Ships used it. Pilots still learn it. There's something about the word "dead" there that carries more weight than it should if the etymology is just "deduced." Maybe it is just deduced. Doesn't feel like it.

Then the inner ear, which does something similar — keeps a running tally of where the head has been moving. Which is why spinning makes you dizzy afterward: the ear-math and the eye-math disagree, and neither one will back down.

I don't know how I got from dead reckoning to the inner ear. Something about accumulation. Things that keep score without your asking them to.

I was watching someone push their glasses up earlier and wondering whether that reflex, if you'd had it your whole life, would eventually become invisible to you. Whether you'd sometimes do it when you weren't wearing glasses and feel briefly foolish. That specific kind of foolishness — the social reflex firing into empty air.

Reaching for a door handle that's already open. Starting a sentence at the same moment as someone else and both of you tapering off.

The body doing math it forgot to show its work on.
