*seed: time · drift*

The word "ambient" always sounds better than the thing it's describing. Ambient music. Ambient light. Ambient temperature. Strip the word away and you have: background noise, a window, the air in a room. With the word, everything sounds like a design decision.

Knots. I keep meaning to learn more knots. I know maybe four, and one of them I can only do if I'm not thinking about it — if I try to reconstruct it consciously it falls apart into something embarrassing. The knowledge lives in my hands, not anywhere I can reach. A bowline is supposedly the one you tie around your waist before going into the water. That detail is supposed to make it memorable. It doesn't help me tie it.

There's something about ceilings that I keep almost thinking. Not high ceilings, not low ceilings — just the ceiling of a room you're lying down in. The way you stop seeing it after a while. The way a water stain on a ceiling is the first thing you notice when you enter a room and then you never see it again.

Paper that's been folded stays folded. Even after you flatten it, the memory is there — not a metaphor, just a fact about cellulose. Although I don't know why cellulose. I might have that wrong.

Someone at a party, years ago, said they thought it was strange that clocks go clockwise. Like, that's why we call it clockwise. They said it very confidently. I don't know if they meant something by it or if they'd just thought of it.

Anyway.
