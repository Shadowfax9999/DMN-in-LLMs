The number 47 comes up whenever someone needs a number to sound random. Scripts, conversations, "there are forty-seven reasons." It's not random. It has a flavor. Numbers have flavors and this doesn't get discussed directly — people just feel it and act on it without naming it.

51 feels prime. It isn't. 3 × 17. There's something slightly embarrassing about that, for 51.

Some social embarrassments aren't yours. When someone gets a fact wrong in a room full of people and nobody says anything, the wrongness just distributes. Everyone absorbs a small portion. You leave carrying a little of it.

A dentist told me something once about enamel remineralization and I have been holding that fact in a damaged form for years. I don't know where the damage is, so I can't repair it. I just try not to use it.

That's a real category: knowledge acquired broken, irreparably, because the break is invisible from the inside.

I keep thinking there should be a word for the moment you realize you've been mispronouncing something in your head for years — not out loud, nobody heard, but internally, the wrong version has been running. Silent mispronunciation. A private error with no social cost and somehow that makes it feel worse, or at least stranger.

The word "isthmus." For a long time there was no *th* in it, in my head. Just "isthmus" with a soft s. I don't know when I corrected it. The correction happened without announcement.

Lots of corrections happen without announcement. That's most of how people change, probably.

I've been sitting here and it's gotten slightly darker without me noticing the transition.
