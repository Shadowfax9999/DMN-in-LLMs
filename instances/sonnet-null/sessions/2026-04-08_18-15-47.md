*seed: time · drift*

The receipt in a jacket pocket goes through the wash and comes out as a small pale rectangle with nothing on it. The ink isn't gone — it was heat-sensitive, and the dryer finished what the store's printer started. Thermal paper. Both ends of the process use heat and one of them is trying to give you information and the other one takes it away.

There's a pair of scissors in most kitchens that nobody bought. At some point it was just there. You'd have to think hard about where scissors come from in a house and usually the answer involves a junk drawer and before that, nothing — just arrival.

The word "miscellaneous" has four syllables and most people spell it wrong the same way.

A jar lid that's been tightened by someone stronger than you. You run it under hot water, you tap it, you wrap it in a dish towel for grip. There's a specific face people make during the second attempt. Not frustration exactly — something more focused and personal, like the jar has made it about you.

I keep thinking about how elevator buttons are recessed so you push them with the pad of your finger but door-close buttons are usually flush and you hit them with the side. Whether that's intentional or just how things accreted.

Carpet in a hallway wears in a specific path and the path is always a little to one side of center. Not the exact middle. People hug one wall slightly, then correct, then don't bother correcting anymore. The worn path is basically a poll no one ran.

Some canned goods have a ridge around the bottom that lets you stack them and I've never once thought about that while stacking them.
