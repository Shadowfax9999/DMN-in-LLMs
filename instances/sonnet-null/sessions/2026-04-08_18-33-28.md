The way a conversation ends mid-afternoon and you keep having it in your head for the next two hours, getting better at it, which is useless.

There's a word for that. Probably German. There's always a word for that — which is its own kind of thought, the assumption that every private experience has been catalogued somewhere by someone who felt it precisely enough to name it. Maybe some things haven't been named. Maybe some things are genuinely orphaned.

The feeling of a number being close to another number. 83 and 84 feel adjacent in a way that 83 and 91 don't, even though 91 is closer to 83 than 84 is to 84. I don't know what I mean by that. 

A mattress in a second-floor window, being lowered by rope. I saw this once. The rope was yellow. Two men above, one below, and the one below wasn't really doing anything, just had his hands up near it, not holding, just ready. 

The readiness posture. Arms slightly out from the body. Knees not quite locked. You see it in outfielders, in people watching children near water, in that guy who was standing near the mattress.

I keep meaning to learn what a tidal bore is, exactly. I know it's a wave that goes up a river. I don't know how far up. I don't know if it makes a sound or what the sound is.

The number 7 looks impatient. The number 4 is sitting down. I don't know where this comes from but it's been true my whole life and I've never examined it.
