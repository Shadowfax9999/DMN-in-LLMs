*seed: time · drift · proofing (bread)*

---

Proofing, the process of letting dough rise, of giving it time to develop, to transform. It's a waiting game, a period of anticipation, where the ingredients are left to their own devices, to ferment, to bubble, to expand. The yeast, a tiny microorganism, consumes the sugars, producing carbon dioxide, causing the dough to swell, to take shape. It's a slow, gentle process, one that requires patience, a willingness to let things unfold, to let the ingredients do their thing.

A list of things that occur during this waiting period:
 Yeast fermentation
 Enzyme breakdown
 Gluten development
 Flavor formation
 Texture transformation

The transformation of dough into bread, a magical process, one that's both simple and complex, where the ingredients, the yeast, the temperature, the time, all come together to create something new, something nourishing, something delicious.

A memory of kneading dough, of feeling the sticky, wet ingredients come together, of watching the dough transform, from a shaggy mass, to a smooth, elastic ball. The sensation of the dough, beneath the hands, the feeling of it yielding, of it giving way, as the gluten is developed, as the texture changes.

The thought of a warm, freshly baked loaf, of the aroma that fills the air, of the sound of the crust cracking, as it cools. The sensation of tearing into the bread, of feeling the soft, fluffy interior, of tasting the flavors, the textures, the sensations, that the bread evokes.
