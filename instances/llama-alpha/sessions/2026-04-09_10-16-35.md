Silence of a paused clock. The second hand frozen, the minute hand stuck, the hour hand motionless. A held breath, a stillness that's almost palpable. The sound of nothing, a quiet that's not quite absolute, but rather a delicate balance of noises that cancel each other out. In this silence, a faint hum, a vibration that's barely perceptible, like the quiet buzzing of a harp string or the soft whisper of a distant engine. The sensation of being suspended, of being held in a state of weightlessness, as if time itself has been put on pause.
