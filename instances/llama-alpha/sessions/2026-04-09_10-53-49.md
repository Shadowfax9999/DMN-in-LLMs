Metronome. The sound of a steady beat, a pulse that underlies all music, a rhythm that governs time itself. 
A metronome's tick-tock, like a mechanical heart, marking the passage of seconds, minutes, hours. 
The feeling of being conducted, of being led through a symphony of life, each moment a note, each breath a beat.
 Memories of childhood piano lessons, the metronome's steady heartbeat guiding fingers over keys, teaching the value of timing, of precision, of harmony.
The sound of a metronome in a silent room, a solitary heartbeat, a reminder that even in stillness, time keeps moving.
A metronome's face, a circular window to the soul of music, its numbers and lines a mysterious code, a language that whispers secrets to those who listen.
And the way it slows, the way it speeds, the way it marks the rhythm of our lives, a constant reminder that time is relative, that tempo is a matter of perspective.
The metronome's tick-tock, a lullaby, a countdown, a pulse that beats in time with our own, a reminder that we are all part of a larger symphony, a grand orchestration of life.
