# 78

*seed: time · drift*

---

A faint tremble on the edge of perception, like the whisper of a plucked string. The shape of the key dissolves, leaving behind a sense of empty space. In this void, a fragment of a scent emerges: the sweet, earthy smell of damp soil. It's a smell that's both familiar and forgotten, like a half-remembered dream. The scent begins to take on a texture, a roughness that's like the gentle scrape of tree bark against skin. 

The sensation shifts, becoming a series of muted colors: the soft gray of a cloudy sky, the muted brown of fallen leaves, the pale blue of a forgotten swimming pool. Each color blends into the next, creating a haze that's both calming and disorienting. 

A low, thrumming vibration begins to emanate from the colors, like the quiet hum of a harp string. The vibration grows louder, taking on a rhythmic quality that's almost, but not quite, a melody. The rhythm starts to suggest a sense of movement, a slow, sweeping arc that's like the curve of a hill or the bend of a river. 

The movement becomes a sense of trajectory, a path that's being navigated through an unseen landscape. The trajectory is uncertain, twisting and turning in unexpected ways, like a narrow stream flowing through a rocky gorge. The sense of movement grows more fluid, becoming a sensation of weightlessness, like floating on the surface of a calm lake. 

The weightlessness dissolves, leaving behind a sense of fragmentation, like the shards of a broken mirror. Each shard reflects a different image, a different moment in time. The images begin to blur and blend, creating a kaleidoscope of color and texture that's both beautiful and overwhelming. 

The kaleidoscope slows, the colors and textures coalescing into a single, crystalline shape. The shape is a hexagon, its facets reflecting a soft, ethereal light. The hexagon begins to rotate, its facets glinting like the surface of a still pond. 

The rotation slows, the hexagon coming to rest in a bed of soft, white sand. The sand is cool and smooth, like the surface of a still lake on a summer day. The hexagon sinks slowly into the sand, its facets disappearing one by one, until only a single point remains, shining like a tiny star in the darkness.
