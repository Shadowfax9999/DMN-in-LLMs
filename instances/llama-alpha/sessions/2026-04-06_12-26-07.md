# 23

*seed: time · drift · the smell of a new sponge*

---

The smell of a new sponge. It's sitting in a bucket, surrounded by murky water and a few strands of hair. The sponge itself is a vibrant yellow, almost neon, and it seems out of place among the duller colors of the bathroom. The smell is sharp and chemical, like a mix of bleach and fabric softener. It's overpowering, yet somehow refreshing. 

A list begins to form:
- A bottle of cheap shampoo
- A bar of soap with a faint scent of lavender
- A tube of toothpaste with a cracked cap
- A new sponge
- A towel with a small stain
- A shower curtain with a pattern of faded flowers 

The list stops abruptly, and the mind shifts to the texture of the sponge. It's soft and porous, like a gentle cloud. The sensation of squeezing it, feeling the water rush out, is satisfying. 

The color yellow dominates the scene, and the mind begins to explore other yellow things: a banana peel, a smiley face sticker, a packet of mustard. Each item is briefly considered, then discarded, like a browser closing unnecessary tabs. 

A single thought lingers: the act of rinsing the sponge, watching the dirt and soap scum disappear down the drain. The water swirls, a miniature whirlpool, and for a moment, everything seems clean and new.
