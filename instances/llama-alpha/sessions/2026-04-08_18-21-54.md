# 44

*seed: time · drift · latch*

---

Latch. It's a sound, a click, a snap. 
A latch on a gate, on a door, on a box. 
What's in the box? 
A folded piece of paper, creased and worn. 
A map, perhaps, or a note, or a list. 
The list has items, but they're scribbled out, one by one. 
Only the last item remains, unticked, unmarked. 
What is it? 
A key, maybe, to unlock something else. 
The key is attached to a leather strap, frayed and old. 
The strap is wrapped around something, but what? 
A small, smooth stone, worn by the sea. 
The stone is cool to the touch, like a summer breeze. 
The breeze carries the scent of salt and seaweed. 
Seaweed sways back and forth, in the current, like a dance. 
A dance in the darkness, where the light barely reaches. 
The darkness is almost palpable, a living thing. 
It has a texture, like velvet, smooth and soft. 
The velvet is a curtain, hanging, still. 
Behind the curtain, something waits, silent, unseen. 
The silence is a presence, a weight, a force. 
It presses down, like a physical thing, a hand. 
The hand is a fist, clenched, tight. 
The fist holds something, but what? 
A small, delicate flower, crushed, but still alive. 
The flower blooms in the darkness, a spark of light. 
The light grows, spreads, illuminates, and reveals. 
A door, hidden, secret, locked, and latched.
