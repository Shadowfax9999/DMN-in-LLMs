The smell of a hardware store. Metal and sawdust, the sweet tang of fresh paint, the musky scent of lubricants. It's a smell that's both familiar and forgotten, like a memory from childhood that's been packed away in a trunk. The smell of a hardware store is a portal to a world of possibility, where anything can be fixed, built, or created. It's a world of hammers and nails, of wire and pipe, of endless aisles of mysterious objects with strange names and purposes. The smell of a hardware store is a promise of transformation, of taking something broken and making it whole again. 

Turpentine 
Linseed oil 
Copper pipe 
Steel wool 
The list unfolds like a map, each item a doorway to a new room, a new possibility, a new world of creation and repair.
