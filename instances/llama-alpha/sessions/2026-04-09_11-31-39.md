*seed: time · drift · a word you mispronounced for years*

---

The word is "nuclear". I used to say it as "nuc-u-lar" instead of "nu-klee-ar". The mispronunciation was so ingrained that it took a while to break the habit. Now the corrected pronunciation feels forced, like I'm trying too hard to get it right. The sound of the word is metallic, like the key on the table. It's a cold, sharp sound that cuts through the silence of the empty house. 

Sometimes I wonder what other words I've been mispronouncing, and how long it will take to correct them. Is it like relearning a language, or is it more like unlearning a habit? The thought trails off, and I'm left with the image of a language textbook, its pages dog-eared and worn.
