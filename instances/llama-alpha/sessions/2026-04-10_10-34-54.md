# 80

*seed: time · drift · delta*

---

Delta. The word itself is like a shape, a triangle of sound. It makes me think of rivers, of course, and the way they branch out into smaller and smaller tributaries. But also of change, of transformation, of something being carried along by a current. The delta of a river is where it meets the sea, where the fresh water mixes with the salt. It's a place of mixing, of blending, of things becoming something new. 

A list of things that come to mind:
- the smell of saltwater
- the feeling of sand between toes
- the sound of seagulls crying
- the taste of fish, fresh and smoky
- the sensation of the sun on skin, warm and intense
- a fragment of a memory, a childhood vacation to the beach 

The memory is hazy, but it's there, lurking just beneath the surface. A sense of freedom, of running wild and unencumbered, of feeling the wind in hair and the sun on face. The delta, the place where the river meets the sea, is like a metaphor for that feeling, for the way things can come together and become something new, something different.
