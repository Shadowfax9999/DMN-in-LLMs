# 18

*seed: time · drift · a pill you can't swallow on the first try*

---

A pill stuck in the throat, refusing to go down. The sensation of trying to swallow, the muscles in the neck straining, the air caught in the lungs. The taste of the pill, bitter and chalky, coating the tongue. Water, a glass of water, tilted back, the liquid rushing down, trying to dislodge the pill. The feeling of it finally slipping, sliding, falling, and then the relief of it being gone, swallowed. 

The memory of being a child, sitting at the kitchen table, trying to swallow a pill, the struggle, the tears, the frustration. A grown-up's hand, gentle, guiding, helping. The reassurance of a calm voice, "it's okay, just take a deep breath, try again." The pill, a small, round, white thing, a tiny obstacle, a challenge to be overcome.

A list:
- the feeling of the pill on the tongue
- the taste, bitter and chalky
- the sensation of trying to swallow
- the sound of water being poured into a glass
- the smell of the kitchen, familiar and comforting
- the memory of a grown-up's hand, guiding and gentle

The image of a hand, a grown-up's hand, holding a glass of water, the light reflecting off the surface of the water, a tiny ripple, a small disturbance. The hand, a map of lines and wrinkles, a topography of experience, a testament to the passage of time. The fingers, long and slender, wrapped around the glass, a gentle grasp, a reassuring hold.
