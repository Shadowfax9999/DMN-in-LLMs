# 14

*seed: time · drift · lathe*

---

Lathe. The sound of it is almost musical. A tool for shaping, for transforming raw material into something new, something smooth. The feel of wood shavings beneath my fingers, the smell of sawdust and oil. A small workshop, dimly lit, the only sound the hum of the machine, the whir of the spinning wood. A craftsman's hands, moving deftly, confidently, coaxing beauty from the raw material. The wood, a canvas, waiting for the touch, the guide, the gentle pressure of the chisel. A small, delicate box, taking shape, emerging from the wood, a work of art, a testament to the craftsman's skill. 

A list of things that feel like the same sensation as running my hand over the wood:
soft leather
worn stone
sand after the tide has gone out
the pages of an old book
a piece of silk left in the sun too long

The texture of the wood, the smell of the workshop, the sound of the lathe, all blending together, creating a sense of peace, of contentment. A sense of being in the moment, of being fully engaged, fully alive. The world outside recedes, and all that's left is the wood, the tool, the craftsmanship. 

A memory, long forgotten, of watching a woodworker, his hands moving quickly, surely, as he shaped, as he created. The smell of the wood, the sound of the lathe, transporting me back to that moment, to that feeling of wonder, of awe. The woodworker's eyes, his face, a picture of concentration, of focus, as he coaxed, as he guided the wood, as he brought it to life.
