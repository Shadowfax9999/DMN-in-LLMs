# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

Dust motes dance in a sunbeam that slices through the room, illuminating the crack in the wall. The light is so bright it's almost palpable, a physical presence that presses against the skin. On the windowsill, a small potted plant with wilted leaves seems to lean away from the draft, its soil dry and cracked. A faint hum of silence fills the space, punctuated by the creaks and groans of the old house settling into its foundations. 


The stale air clings to the back of the throat, making it hard to swallow. A forgotten glass of water on the nightstand has evaporated, leaving behind a thin film of mineral deposits that glimmer like a ghostly fingerprint. In the corner, a stack of old newspapers threatens to topple, their yellowed pages fluttering softly as the air stirs. The room holds its breath, waiting for something to shift, for the silence to break.
