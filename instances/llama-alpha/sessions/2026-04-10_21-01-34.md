Bitten tongue, the taste of metal and salt, a physical reminder to hold back, to keep the words from spilling out. The sensation of the tongue pressed against the roof of the mouth, a attempt to stem the flow of blood, to calm the throbbing. A memory of childhood, of being told to be quiet, to still the tongue, and the physical act of biting it, a manifestation of the desire to obey. 

The image of a metal bridge, suspended high above a river, the sound of the water rushing below, a reminder of the distance between the world above and the world below. The feeling of standing on the bridge, the wind whipping through hair, the sense of being suspended between two states, two worlds.
