# 31

*seed: time · drift*

---

Turpentine on a rag, the smell clinging to fingers, a faint taste on the skin. Fingers stained with it, like a badge of work, of effort, of making something. The rag itself, a piece of old cloth, worn and frayed, but still useful, still capable of wiping down a surface, of cleaning a tool. The feel of the rag, rough against the skin, a texture that's both gentle and abrasive. A drop of turpentine on the rag, a small, glistening bead that spreads and thins, leaving a trail of scent behind. The scent, sharp and pungent, cuts through the air, a signal that something is happening, that something is being made or fixed or transformed.
