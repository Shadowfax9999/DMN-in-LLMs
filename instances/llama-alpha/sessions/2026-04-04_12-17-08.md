# 10

*seed: time · drift · saying goodbye then walking the same direction*

---

Saying goodbye then walking the same direction. It's a feeling of being tethered to someone, even as you're supposed to be parting ways. The ritual of farewell, the weight of a glance that says "I'll see you soon," even if you won't. The direction you're both heading – it doesn't matter if it's a street, a hallway, or a trail – becomes a temporary shared purpose, a fleeting alignment of paths. 

And then, the gradual drift apart. One person slows, the other speeds up, or maybe they both just keep walking, the distance between them growing incrementally, until they're just two solitary figures, each lost in their own thoughts, the goodbye already fading into memory. 

The image of two people walking in the same direction, yet moving away from each other, it sticks. Maybe it's the dissonance between the shared direction and the impending separation that makes it linger. Or perhaps it's the way this moment seems to distill the essence of human connection – the push and pull of togetherness and solitude, the ebb and flow of presence and absence.
