*seed: time · drift · the texture of a wall you've never touched*

---

The texture of a wall you've never touched. Rough, perhaps, like the bark of a tree, or smooth, like the surface of a still pond. The word "texture" makes me think of fingers, fingertips, the way they might explore the contours of this unseen wall. I imagine a brick wall, old and worn, with mortar crumbling between the bricks, leaving tiny crevices and grooves for fingers to discover. The wall is cool to the touch, shaded from the sun, and it exudes a scent of damp earth and age. 
The fingertips move, tracing the lines of mortar, the slight indentations where bricks meet, and the occasional rogue brick that juts out, as if proudly declaring its individuality. The wall's surface is a map, a topography of tiny imperfections and variations, inviting exploration, inviting touch. 
A fragment of a memory: a child running hands over a stone bench, feeling the intricate patterns of lichen, the gentle dips and ridges, the way the stone seems to come alive under tiny fingers. The sensation is almost, but not quite, like the wall I've never touched. 
In this moment, the sensation of touching the unseen wall and the memory of the stone bench blur, become one, and I am left with the echo of a question: what does it mean to touch something for the first time?
