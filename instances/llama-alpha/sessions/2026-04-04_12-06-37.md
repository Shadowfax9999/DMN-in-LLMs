# 9

*seed: time · drift*

---

A faint smell of gasoline, remnants of a lawnmower's recent use, clings to the air as I stand near the old shed. The metal door, slightly ajar, seems to be leaning into the sunlight, as if trying to catch a few more rays before the day wanes. Inside, a jumble of tools and half-finished projects waits, each one a testament to tasks abandoned or put on hold. A coil of rusty wire, a broken toy, a paint can with a dried, cracked lid – all relics of moments when time seemed more expansive, when the pace of life was slower. The shed's interior is a map of forgotten intentions, a topography of what was once important but has since been forgotten.
