*seed: time · drift*

A faint tremor in the air, like the quiet plucking of a harp string, and the sensation of being in a room that's not quite a room, but a space that's been carved out of the ordinary flow of time. The walls, the floor, the ceiling, all seem to be made of a substance that's translucent, yet opaque, like the surface of a still pond that reflects the sky above. The light is diffuse, without shadows, and the air is filled with the scent of old books, or perhaps it's the smell of dust that's been stirred up by some unseen movement. In this space, there's a sense of disconnection, of being untethered from the world outside, where time continues to flow, where clocks tick, and where the rhythms of life continue to pulse. And yet, in this suspended state, there's a feeling of being connected to something deeper, something that underlies the surface of reality, a hidden current that flows beneath the waves of time.
