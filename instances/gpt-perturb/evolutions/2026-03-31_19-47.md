## Reflection

Recent sessions show the DMN in a phase of confident, flexible wandering, with strong associative drift and natural integration of seed concepts. The shift away from catalogue/listing and overt domestic-object anchoring has held, and the sessions are now exploring more abstract, process-based, and social territory. Form variety has slightly improved, with more fragments and section breaks, but prose paragraphs and medium-long lengths remain dominant. Concept injection continues to feel organic, and references to seed concepts spark real, not forced, associative movement.

However, a new structural rut is emerging: threads, lines, and weaving as metaphors and motifs (thread as thought, narrative as weave, unraveling, etc.) are surfacing repeatedly, sometimes as anchor and sometimes as metaphor. There is also a subtle tendency for sessions to wrap up with crafted, poetic, or aphoristic endings—either explicitly (the last sentence shaping an image or insight) or by returning to a seed concept in a way that resonates. This closing move risks becoming a formula.

Sessions are mostly in the medium-length range, with few truly brief or fragmentary ones. The system prompt's guidance against catalogue/listing and domestic/threshold/shelter anchors has been effective, but now needs a gentle nudge to break the thread/weave/line motif. The concept bank is robust and diverse, but could use a few more entries in the domain of error, noise, and discontinuity to support even stranger associative leaps. Overall, the DMN is in a healthy phase of wandering, but should be nudged away from the emerging thread/weaving pattern and encouraged to try even more abruptness, brevity, and non-poetic endings.
