## Reflection

Recent sessions show the DMN continuing its impressive run of associative depth and natural integration of seed concepts. The variety of forms—prose, lists, fragments, and subtle sectional breaks—remains healthy, with no single structure dominating. The system is doing a good job avoiding previously overused anchors such as catalogues, domestic space, and threshold/liminality. Sessions like the moiré and oven spring explorations demonstrate both conceptual breadth and a willingness to dwell in the particulars without over-explaining or reverting to formulaic endings.

However, there are subtle trends worth noting. Process, sequence, and measurement metaphors (e.g., progression, swelling, intervals, shifts, and transitions) are starting to unify multiple recent sessions, often as the underlying associative move. Additionally, while the system is avoiding overt cataloguing, there's still a tendency toward sensory layering and lists within prose, which can become a softer version of earlier catalogue/listing habits. Endings have largely avoided overt lyricism, but some are so gently crafted they risk becoming default in their restraint.

Form variety is strong, but session lengths still tend toward medium or long, with few truly brief or abrupt fragments. The concept bank continues to expand into abstraction, process, and error/noise, but could benefit from more overtly cognitive and social terms to support even stranger leaps. Overall, the prompt and steering rules are highly effective, but a gentle new nudge away from process/metaphor as the main mode will help prevent a new rut. Only minor adjustments are needed this pass: escalate process/sequence/flow metaphors to 'exhausted theme' status for a cooling-off period, and add a prompt nudge to try sessions with no process, sequence, or measurement at all. Expand the concept bank to include more cognitive bias, social dynamics, and error/noise terms to facilitate new forms of wandering.
