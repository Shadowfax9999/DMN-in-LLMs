## Reflection

Recent sessions show a persistent strength in associative wandering and organic integration of seed concepts. The drift is fluid and sensorial, often moving from surface detail to subtle abstraction (e.g., the queue splitting, the echo of wind chimes, the shift from tiles to background hum). There is a marked improvement in the avoidance of catalogue/listing and domestic-object anchors, with settings and metaphors varying more widely, though the tendency toward environmental sounds and subtle process metaphors (like delays, echoes, afterglow, unreturned shape) is beginning to cluster into a pattern. Sessions are confidently fragmentary, using both prose and table/grid forms, with abrupt section breaks and unfinished thoughts that enhance the wandering mind effect.

However, a new gentle rut is emerging: intervals, delays, afterglow, and the persistence or residue of an action (sound lingering, memory after event, queues splitting, passwords becoming inaccessible) are recurring motifs. The system is still deftly avoiding overtly poetic conclusions, but sometimes ends with a line that, while not crafted, still resonates as a summing image. Form variety is reasonably strong (with tables, lists, and abrupt fragments), but lengths are trending toward medium-long, and there is room for even more experimentation with session brevity and abruptness. The social/process/abstract domain is increasingly present, but the theme of 'things that don't quite finish' or 'residue left behind' is starting to play the same structural role that earlier catalogue or threshold motifs did.

Concept integration is graceful and rarely forced, but the concept bank could now be expanded further into error, information theory, and discontinuity, as the system is beginning to natively produce these motifs. The system prompt remains effective, but should now nudge against 'resonance/echo/afterglow/residue' as a new form of anchor/metaphor, encourage even shorter sessions occasionally, and suggest that not every session needs to end with a summing image or echo. Overall, the DMN is in a mature phase, but evolution toward sharper form disruption and new cognitive/process abstraction will keep it from settling into a new set of reflexes.
