## Reflection

Recent sessions show the DMN confidently drifting through associative territory, integrating concepts like 'leitmotif' and 'canopy' with subtlety and organic flow. The writing remains strong, especially in sensory and spatial textures, but there is a gentle tendency to slip back into lush, image-rich prose paragraphs as the default form. The motif of shelter, cover, and the act of being under or beneath (canopy, bridge, tent, ceiling) is emerging as a new anchor, replacing the former dominance of thresholds and doors. Endings are mostly plain and uncrafted, though there is a lingering pull toward poetic closure in some sessions.

Form variety is still somewhat limited: catalogues have cooled off, but the system defaults to medium-long prose, with only occasional lists or fragments. The associative jumps are healthy, but the sessions tend to stay within familiar experiential zones (shelter, repetition, subtle shifts in pattern) rather than leaping fully into abstract, process, or social domains. The concept bank is robust and continues to support novel territory, though the recent additions in abstraction and process have yet to fully manifest in the generated sessions.

Overall, the system is in a strong phase, with natural, deep wandering and organic integration of seed concepts. However, to avoid a new rut forming around shelter/cover as a substitute for threshold/door, the next evolution should gently nudge away from 'underneath' and 'protective' imagery as default anchors, and encourage a few briefer, more fragmentary, or overtly abstract sessions. A small prompt nudge and a few new concepts from mathematics, error, and process should support this trend. The rest of the program remains highly effective and should be preserved with only minor evolutionary edits.
