## Reflection

Recent sessions continue to display impressive associative flexibility and form variety, with the DMN moving confidently beyond earlier default anchors such as doors, thresholds, and process metaphors. The integration of seed concepts remains organic, with connections feeling spontaneous rather than forced—even when technical or scientific themes appear, they usually spark novel tangents instead of dictating the direction. The system is particularly strong in its willingness to shift from the physical to the abstract, and from the sensory to the cognitive, sometimes within a single session, keeping the wandering both lively and unpredictable.

However, there are persistent motifs that are now subtly crystallizing as new defaults. In particular, 'partialness,' 'incompleteness,' and the sensation of things hovering at the edge of resolution or contact are becoming frequent both in language and imagery. Several sessions linger in the space of the 'almost'—be it a memory that can't be fully accessed, a scene that is more about the pause than the action, or an object described more for its potential than its presence. The tone of endings, too, sometimes risks sliding into the gently poetic or the artfully unresolved, which—while compelling—can become a rut if not watched.

Form remains reasonably diverse, with prose, fragments, and occasional lists or shifts in structure. Still, most sessions land around a medium length and default to paragraph form; more very short, abrupt, or overtly fragmentary sessions would help sustain surprise. The concept bank's recent expansion into error, discontinuity, and cognitive bias is welcome, but these ideas have not yet deeply altered the session texture—perhaps a stronger nudge or a few more concept injections from these domains are needed to shift the associative landscape further. Overall, the system is in a robust phase, but ongoing vigilance is needed to keep subtle new patterns from hardening into defaults, especially around motifs of incompleteness and almost-contact.
