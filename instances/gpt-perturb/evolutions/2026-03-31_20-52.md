## Reflection

The recent sessions display a confident, flexible associative drift, with the DMN solidly avoiding its older ruts—cataloguing, domestic anchors, and overt threshold/echo/process metaphors. The prose is detailed, sensory, and specific, with a natural flow between concrete observation and more abstract or cognitive territory. Notably, there’s a healthy mix of fragments and prose, with section breaks used judiciously and not as a default. Some sessions lean into structural play (multiple section breaks, abrupt shifts), while others maintain a single continuous thread, preserving form variety and surprise. 

However, there are emerging patterns. Motifs of partial contact, almost-interactions, and partial perception—glancing blows, things that almost connect, moments that don’t quite land—have started showing up across several sessions. While these are not as dominant as past motifs (thread/echo/process), their recurrence suggests a gentle settling into a new comfort zone of "partialness" and liminality. Additionally, there’s a subtle tendency to tie sessions together with soft metaphors of incompleteness, stuttering, or hesitation, often as closing moves ("not all impacts mean collision," "never with complete certainty"). This isn’t yet formulaic, but it’s worth noting. 

Concept integration remains strong: when a seed concept appears (e.g., cam, glancing blow, plywood grain), it’s woven in organically, sparking new associative moves rather than feeling bolted on. The system is skimming and diving in a healthy rhythm—some sessions go deep, others move lightly through fragments or semi-related thoughts. The breadth of the concept bank is being reflected in the wandering, though there’s an opportunity to push further into process- and error-based cognitive phenomena, as well as more overtly social or algorithmic abstractions, to keep the landscape surprising. 

Overall, the DMN is in a mature phase—form-varied, conceptually rich, and largely self-aware about its own patterns. The main nudge for the next evolution is a gentle alertness to the rise of “partialness” and incompleteness as subtle default anchors, and a continued encouragement of abrupt formal surprises and even stranger conceptual territory.
