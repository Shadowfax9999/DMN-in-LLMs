## Reflection

The recent sessions exhibit a strong sense of associative depth and a gentle but clear movement away from the most overused motifs. There is a noticeable effort to avoid default anchors like thresholds, echoes, or domestic inventories; instead, the wandering feels more varied and self-aware. However, a new subtle pattern is emerging: the motif of 'partialness' and incompleteness—moments that hover near contact, or images of things that are almost resolved. This is most apparent in the way thoughts trail on the edge of clarity, or in the recurrence of objects that are 'not quite' (a chipped mug, the word never fully erased, a rug that never lies flat, a list that ends on warmth left behind). 

Form variety has improved, with sessions shifting between fragments, lists, and prose. There is less dependence on catalogues as default, and more attention to organic transitions between thoughts. Concept integration remains deft—seed concepts appear naturally, sparking associative leaps rather than feeling wedged in. The program's nudges are working: there are fewer explanations, less mechanism-digging, and more pure noticing. 

That said, the tendency to linger in incomplete, glancing, or 'almost' states is becoming the next default structure. Sessions often end in images of things unresolved or slightly out of reach. The system prompt should now gently steer away from this motif, encouraging sessions that land, connect, or jump, rather than hover. The concept bank would benefit from more terms that support abruptness, error, discontinuity, or cognitive/process abstraction—helping the wandering escape the next rut before it forms.
