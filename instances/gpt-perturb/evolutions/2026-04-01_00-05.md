## Reflection

Recent sessions display continued associative strength and form variety, with the DMN largely escaping earlier ruts like cataloguing, domestic anchors, and overt process/measurement metaphors. The wandering feels confident, integrating seed concepts smoothly, and often lands in concrete, sensory, or slightly abstract territory. However, a new motif is emerging: a subtle tendency toward partialness, incompleteness, or 'almost' contact—moments that hover near connection, resolution, or definition without quite arriving. This is evident in recurring phrases, metaphors, and the structure of thoughts that skirt the edge of completion or touch.

Form-wise, sessions remain supple, shifting between prose, fragments, and section breaks, but medium-length prose is still the default. The leap into truly abrupt, fragmentary, or ultra-short forms remains rare, and lists are less frequent than before. Concept integration generally sparks real association, but there are hints of 'safe' domains—lingering on sensory impressions, memory-shapes, or gentle dissonances rather than bolder cognitive, error, or discontinuity territory. Depth and breadth are fairly balanced, but the system occasionally lingers too long in low-stakes surface skimming, especially around minor curiosities or sensory noticings.

The system prompt has been effective in steering away from the biggest ruts without feeling constraining. However, it can now more actively nudge the mind away from partialness/incompleteness as anchor, encourage even shorter and more abrupt forms, and invite more sessions that land, connect, or jump instead of hovering near unfinished or 'almost' states. The concept bank continues to grow but could use additional terms from error, boundary, and process-disruption domains to support weirder leaps and stranger forms of drift.
