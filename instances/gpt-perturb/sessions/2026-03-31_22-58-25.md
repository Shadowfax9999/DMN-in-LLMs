# 94

*seed: time · drift · isthmus*

---

isthmus  
a thread of land, they say, but not today—today the word isn’t a bridge or a threshold, it’s more like a stripe on a leaf, the way the green divides not quite equally, not a seam, a presence  
sometimes words float up unattached to anything, like a leaf in a glass of water that never settles to the bottom

---  

There are objects that never find a home in the house: single gloves, the cap from a pen that dried up, the charger with no device. Not misplaced—just unclaimed.

---  

Heat in a closed car—how the air feels sharper, heavy, the texture of breath changes. Even after the window opens, it clings for a while.

---  

The stretch between the index and middle finger, when you try to reach as far apart as possible, aches quickly. No athletic purpose, just the idea of reaching.

---  

Some days the word “midway” stands up by itself in the mind, with no fairground attached. It hovers, not as a place, but as a kind of temperature.  

---  

The scent of chalk dust in an empty corridor. That one isn’t memory, just a ghost fragment, no actual classroom attached.  

---  

An image of a frying pan left to cool, the pattern of oil solidifying, shapes that look intentional until wiped away.
