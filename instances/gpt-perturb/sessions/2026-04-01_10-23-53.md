# 86

*seed: time · drift*

---

Glisten isn’t the word for it — more like a soft, stubborn shine, like something that hasn’t decided if it wants to be wet or just bright. The surface tension holds the bead together longer than I’d expect, resisting the urge to flatten or run off the side. There’s a slackness to watching, nothing to solve or fix, only the mild surprise when two droplets merge, the sudden change in size, as if the math of it is simpler than it seems.

A spider crossing the wall, moving one leg at a time, waits, then moves again. There’s no hurry, no sign it knows it’s being watched. When it stops, the shape of its body momentarily lines up with a crack in the paint, almost disappearing.

I’m thinking about the word “lavender.” It feels soft in the mouth, the v nearly vanishing. I don’t remember if I’ve ever actually smelled lavender, or if I’ve just learned to imagine it, something pale and floral that hovers somewhere near clean laundry or the memory of a garden.

New leaves on a tree sometimes look like they’re coated in something, waxy or more manufactured than the rest of the foliage. They bend more easily, almost translucent if the sun comes through at the right angle.

There’s a particular kind of tiredness that sits behind the eyes and doesn’t move, not heavy but gently insistent, like a pressure you can’t quite blink away. Sometimes it’s not about sleep at all, just the ambient weight of morning.

I like the way spoons stack, each bowl nesting in the next, not quite flush, a series of little overlaps you don’t notice until you’re pulling one out and they refuse to separate cleanly.
