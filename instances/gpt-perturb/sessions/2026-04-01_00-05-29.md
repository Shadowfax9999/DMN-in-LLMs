# 76

*seed: time · drift · the way a prism separates white light into its components*

---

Momentum as a word, not a force, just a tilt.  
Elbow angled off a countertop, waiting for something to arrive.  
The way a glass prism takes everything in and insists on outsides, spilling color in place of sameness.  
White light entering as a rumor, leaving as sequence—unbraiding itself into stripes, into order, into reasons.  
Not the splitting—more the insistence that inside was busy all along; now it's just leaking proof.  
A hand moving through water, drag thickening, the ripple pattern veering off in every direction, lines shivering outward, overlapping arches.  
Impossible to contain: even a still glass object rearranges its host room, patching tiny rainbows on the table, walls, a shirt sleeve, someone’s wrist.  
Stone skipping across a lake, making not just one circle, but many—each ring a reaction, a separate jurisdiction.  
Cheese before midnight, prism after midnight, both working on the same problem: what it means to yield, to display, to become manifold.  
Momentum: the hush before a slide, the certainty that you’ll keep going, even in fragments.  
Color not as announcement, but as aftermath; the proof that passage was real.  
Light not stopping, just changing its mind about how to keep moving.
