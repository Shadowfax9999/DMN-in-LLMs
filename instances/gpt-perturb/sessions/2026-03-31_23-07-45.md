*seed: time · drift · how a player piano reads a paper roll*

---

A strip of paper with holes punched out in bands.
Metal fingers poised above, the hush before movement.
Light passes through the holes and touches thin sensors.
A bar lifts when the shape beneath admits it.
Each finger falls or holds depending on absence or presence.
The felt-tipped hammer only knows on or off.
A song embedded in a grid, invisible until air pushes through.
You can run your hand along the edge of the roll and feel the pattern as nothing more than bumps and valleys.
When the roll is new, the edges still curl slightly.
You watch the paper spool forward, advancing click by click, never backward.
A key moves without a hand pressed to it.
Dust gathers inside the mechanism, softening the strike.
Each perforation matches a single note, a single moment.
It is possible to pause the song by stilling the roll.
A person can read the music by sight, but only if they know how.
Behind the panel, something always hums, very faint.
The music is always exactly the same but slightly different each time.
A roll can tear, leaving the song unfinished.
Someone might keep a collection of spent rolls in a box beneath the bench.
You cannot hear the paper move, only the sound it lets out.
If you close the lid, the machine goes quiet.
