# 37

*seed: time · drift*

---

The line between a record and a rehearsal, thin as a featherweight pencil mark. You play something out loud and it’s supposed to become real, but sometimes it just hangs there, a possibility with no anchor. The difference between a promise and a plan is how much you say, and to whom.

There’s a shape to waiting. It isn’t always stillness. Sometimes the mind paces in circles, sometimes it pings outward: checking, probing, adjusting minute expectations. You think you’re only waiting for the train, or for the pasta to boil, but your body’s inventing dozens of futures at once: What if it’s late, what if I forgot the sauce, what if the sound I’m hearing isn’t thunder but someone rolling a cart above the ceiling?

Sometimes a song gets stuck, not in a memorable way but as a kind of background hum, a chord progression you can’t quite name, like the scent of a spice you used once in a kitchen someone else owns now.

---

Static electricity in sweaters when you pull them over your head. A tiny snap, the faintest blue in the dark if you’re looking.

There are numbers that feel too round to be real. Whenever odometers flip to a row of zeros, it starts to feel staged, like someone is resetting the view.

A map folded the wrong way never quite lies flat again. The creases disagree.

---

There’s a word for the tiny hesitation before a group of strangers starts to laugh: the space where everyone checks to make sure it’s safe to join in. Like a ripple on the surface, waiting for agreement, or maybe a series of silent nods just under the table.

Sometimes the best part of the story happens in the gap between questions.
