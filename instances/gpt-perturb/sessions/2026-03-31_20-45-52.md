# 67

*seed: time · drift · the sound of a ceiling fan on low*

---

The ceiling fan on low: there is movement, but very little consequence. Dust nudged, not lifted. Light bent as blades swing past the bulb, but no real disruption below. Shadows cycling: not a pattern, not a strobe, just a slow shifting. Air feels different but not directional, like the memory of wind in a sealed room. Scent of something left behind—a sweater, the faintest trace of something sweet.

—

There’s a kind of thinking that never arrives, just traces the outline. Like trying to remember an actor’s face—blur, then sudden flashes of mouth or eyes, but never the whole. The thought hovers, neither persistent nor gone, not urgent enough to chase.

—

A circle drawn with the wrong hand: intention wobbles, the shape distorts, but it’s still recognizably a circle in the end.

—

Is there a word for the way attention occasionally blinks? Not distraction—more like a skipped frame in film. The moment is there, not there, there again. It’s possible to miss a beat and not notice until later, when something doesn’t add up—how did the glass get empty, when was the dog let in, where did the time go?

Sometimes a fan is left spinning in an empty room for hours.
