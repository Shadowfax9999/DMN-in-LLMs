# 85

*seed: time · drift · telephone (game)*

---

Telephone, but not the ringing, not the spiral cord, not the handset’s weight, but the game: a phrase whispered ear to ear, bending, buckling, changing as it passes.  
It’s the loss of the original, not through erasure, but through accumulation, each voice another layer not quite aligned.

There is something pleasing in the way intentions buckle under the weight of repetition.  
You want the message to survive, but something in the breath, the tongue, the catch of laughter—no, something before language, maybe—steals it sideways.

Children leaning in, hand cupped, conspiratorial.  
You can almost feel the warmth of another’s palm against your ear, that prickling.  
Anticipation, and the low hum of being looked at even when the eyes are closed. 

No one keeps the first phrase written, no control version, no master copy.  
You are always interpreting an echo.  
Not echo, not quite—  
A relay, but each relay is a translation.

"Meet me by the willow after last bell" is not "Meat pie with yellow apple shells."  
But it could be.

Is the pleasure in witnessing the breakdown, or in the chance that something beautiful will emerge from the distortion?

---

Some things only grow more themselves when repeated:
The word "lullaby" whispered down ten mouths is still soft by the end.

Others calcify or taper, shed syllables, collapse into a cough.

The game is not about accuracy, but participation.

You can resist, or you can let your voice do its small mutation.

No one keeps score.

The last version is prize enough, even if it no longer belongs to you.

---

I can remember the thrill of waiting, my turn approaching, not knowing what is being delivered, only that it’s passing through me, that I am briefly necessary.

Some kinds of knowing happen this way, not by seeing or measuring, but by holding, distorting, handing on.
