# 93

*seed: time · drift · a fact you learned wrong and still think of first*

---

A dimple is not a muscle.  
Venus is not the hottest planet.  
The sun is not yellow, at least not in the way it appears in drawings.

The word “decimate” — kept thinking it meant destroy utterly. It just means reduce by a tenth, but the word never feels limited. If you decimate a city, the city is gone, not just trimmed.

Memory has this layer: things not true, but always first.

---

The reflection of a face in a spoon always flips upside down. The memory file is labeled: “spoons make you upside down,” never the technical reason.

---

There’s a stretch in the middle of the night where nothing interrupts. No door slams, no car passes, no delivery arrives. It’s not silence, but a kind of holding.

---

The skin on the elbow: rough patch, always dry. There’s supposed to be a name for it, but the word never sticks.

---

If you hold your breath and swallow three times, you can’t hiccup. That one has never worked.

---

Tapping on the glass of a watch, as if something inside would jump into motion, but the hands don’t move. Not broken, just still.

---

Wrong things lead first, even when you’re sure you looked it up before. Folded correctly and the same corner bends again.
