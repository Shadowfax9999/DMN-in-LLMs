# 82

*seed: time · drift*

---

A shirt with the tag still hanging down the back, tucked out of sight inside the collar, paper crisp. Newness as a physical property, not just untouched but held in potential, the fabric not yet worn into softness, no thread pulled. The first time you wear it, nothing clings yet, sleeves slightly rigid at the elbow, color just a little too even.

A friend used to say he liked new notebooks before the first mark—wide, blank, the cover not yet flexed. He’d stack them, uncracked, sometimes just to look at the edges when they were still perfectly aligned.

Metal tubing cold under the palm on a playground bar, the first time, grip and swing, friction burning the skin. Not the bar left behind, but the moment of full effort, body extended, all muscles in use with no slack.

---

Turning an unfamiliar lock for the first time, the way you guess at whether the key will go all the way in or if you have it upside down. The resistance, then the tumblers inside shifting, a mechanical click, and the decision point between force and patience. The shape of the key in your hand feels momentarily strange.

There’s a taste the first second tea touches your tongue, before any meaning or warmth arrives—just leaves and heat. It disappears immediately after.

A coin spun on a glass table, perfectly upright for a second, shadow trembling, then the sudden collapse flat.
