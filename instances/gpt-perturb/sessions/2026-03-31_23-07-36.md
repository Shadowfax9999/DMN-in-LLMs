# 99

*seed: time · drift · the way wet shoes sound on linoleum*

---

Crow feathers: not black, but layered, each over the next, a shimmer that is not quite blue.

There is a kind of fatigue that feels neither good nor bad; it simply settles, rearranging the edges of things.

Between two buildings, a narrow swath of sky, almost too thin for the eye to register.

A thumb idly tracing the spiral on a notebook cover, over and over, until the groove gets warm.

The feeling when someone says your name from another room and you can’t tell if it’s urgent.

---

Numbers on a microwave, always slightly off from the real clock.

A plastic cup with ridges so fine they catch the light in lines.

If you look at a page long enough, the words seem to push at the margins.

There is a taste on the back of the tongue: not sweet, not bitter — a third thing, unnamed.

---

Three times I have seen the same streetlight flicker out as I pass.
