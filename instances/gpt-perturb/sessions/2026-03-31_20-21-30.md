# 56

*seed: time · drift · the way a prism separates white light into its components*

---

Humming buzz in afternoon light. Acrylic block on a windowsill, edges cold and sharp. White light, then color, then thin rungs of nothing between. Not a rainbow, not quite — more like a disagreement in glass, the way an argument splits into smaller, shriller voices. Violet always thinner than red. Green's abruptness, jagged boundary. Layered shadows behind the colors, a ghost of the block itself. The dust’s interference, tiny specks catching away the blue. Moving the prism, angles shifting: yellow slides, weakens, disappears altogether unless the wrist turns exactly so.

White feels like a compromise, a compression, made visible again under pressure. School diagrams, bands painted too thick, the real thing always fainter, color as transparency rather than paint. The triangle’s geometry dictating angles no matter how it tilts, every rotation another slice, another recalculation.

Chalk marks across the page, faint, half-remembered. Locker room with rough glass set high in the wall, early morning, thin blue smear on concrete. The word “fluorescent,” looping somewhere behind it — not a prism, but a tube, light forced through chemicals, all color at once and yet not full. The kind of white that denies shadow.  

The absence of warmth beneath those colors. Skin reflecting a greenish cast, red rendered sallow, blue too clinical. That peculiar fatigue in the eyes after hours of fluorescent wash, as if the body registers something missing, a difference you can’t name.

Hands moving the prism, again, hoping to catch an extra color, but always the same: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, violet, edges blurred, nothing pure. The quiet in the moment before the fragment disperses.
