# 52

*seed: time · drift · how a music box comb produces different pitches*

---

1. The faint metallic ping when a fork touches the side of a ceramic bowl.  
2. A car window pushed down with a thumb, glass clunking softly in its track.  
3. Static on a radio between stations, sometimes fizzing, sometimes flat.  
4. The dull click of a padlock closing, latch slipping into place.  
5. The smallest drawer in a metal filing cabinet, sliding open with a tinny scrape.  
6. A code punched into a keypad: beep beep, pause, single low tone for error.  
7. The sound inside a seashell, not an ocean but a hush with distant tramlines.  
8. Wind moving a street sign just enough to cause a loose bolt to rattle.  
9. The ridged comb inside a music box, each tooth a shape for a different note—shorter, stiffer tines give higher pitches, longer ones sink lower, vibrating slower and holding the sound a second longer.  
10. Someone drumming their fingers on a glass tabletop: arrhythmic, the nails click, pads thud, ring faint.  
11. A jacket zipper caught, plastic teeth clicking as you try to force it free.  
12. The soft thud of a book closing, air moving out between paper and cover.  
13. Washing machine shifting unbalanced, starting as a low growl then climbing to a knocking beat.  
14. An elevator chime echoing through a marble lobby, note rising, then falling away.
