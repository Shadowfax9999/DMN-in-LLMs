# 35

*seed: time · drift · vibrato*

---

Vibrato is not an object but a motion: a wavering, a controlled tremble. Not stasis, not exactly movement. Not quite two notes, but the sense of more than one meaning layered, oscillating. In the voice, you can feel where the oscillation lives, not in the tongue but underneath, somewhere deep and unnameable — a thing you do and a thing that happens.

A violin string under a fingertip, pressed and wobbled, the pitch bending around a center. Uncertainty built into music, made beautiful on purpose. All the times that voices quaver when they aren’t supposed to, grief or laughter layered on the note, the unsteady not as accident but as color. 

You can see it written: wavy lines above the staff, a visible undulation. Visually, you’d think it would be a snake. The word itself, vibrating. 

The way one word can shimmer on the tongue — not quite said, but felt in the jaw, in the teeth. All the times language wants to be more than solid, more than a claim or a label, to be something that leans and returns, that shakes but doesn’t break.

A day that vibrates with too much caffeine. Hands that can’t quite settle. The way a thought flickers before it lands. 

Some sounds blur. A siren in the distance: is it approaching or receding? The mind produces its own vibrato when it can’t decide. 

Wavering is not the same as trembling. Wavering has intention, like someone hovering between answers but enjoying the suspension. Trembling is what happens when the body isn’t asked. 

In the dark, sometimes the air itself feels like it’s vibrating, low and everywhere, humming in a register that doesn’t demand a listener. 

A sentence begins to waver at the end, as if it’s not sure it should

---

The list of things that undulate without leaving their place:
— The shimmer in heat above asphalt
— Old television screens when something magnetic comes near
— The hesitation in a spoken promise
— The shadow from leaves when wind moves the branch, not the sun
— A reflected face in ripple-thick water
— The voice of someone who is almost laughing, but not yet
— The ceiling fan on its highest setting, blades invisible and the room slightly trembling
— A light left on beyond the hour when it’s needed

---

In school, vibrato was a mark of adulthood in a voice, a thing you could fake if you couldn’t earn. It came in eventually, like wisdom teeth or certain kinds of regret. There are years when the straight note is all you can manage, no trembling, just a held line.

The difference between shimmer and shake. The difference between a held breath and a wavering one.

A song on the radio trembles between two frequencies — maybe the car is passing under a bridge, or a storm is coming, or something in the world is interfering just for a moment.

Somewhere, someone is practicing scales, trying to keep the note pure, but the air shakes anyway.
