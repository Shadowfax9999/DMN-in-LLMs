# 60

*seed: time · drift · the way a prism separates white light into its components*

---

Nicknames inherited, not chosen.  
The tilt of a bowl under a bright bulb.  
Elastic tension before the yawn, something stretching just under the ribs.  
Slantwise voices through the slats in a fence, syllables scattering.  
Prism: edge, glass, a shift of wrist—white becoming many.  
Cobalt, celery, mothwing, slate-blue angled beside each other in the spectrum.  
Names that only work if you never meet their owners. Talbot, Merryweather, Finchley.  
A plate dropped in sunlight, the fracture lines catching miniature rainbows.  
Color not as paint but as invitation: spin and see what isn’t held.  
A gaze refracted by a window’s flaw, melting focus into slices.  
Every name as a compact—something you must live into or away from.  
A list of instructions disguised as invitations: Wander. Rest. Assemble. Refrain.  
Single-use words pressed like petals in the margin of a guidebook.  
Splitting a word by accident, two meanings sliding apart beneath the tongue.  
The way you can almost feel an afterimage behind your eyes, not the residue but the expectation of color.  
Mouths forming shapes that never quite match the labels they produce.  
Abbreviations that become names: Pip, Dex, Sol.  
A prism set on a sill, waiting for the right hour to matter.  
Side stitch: that ache in half-breaths, a punctuation mark in the body.  
Chased by something you can’t see, named in a language you never learned.  
Fingertip pressed to glass, the spectrum leaking out where pressure becomes permission.  
A name that’s a window, not a door.  
Pencil graphite gray, overripe pear yellow, a prism’s argument with the plainness of white.  
Instructions you follow just by answering.  
Splinters of color, pocketed for later, never retrieved.  
March, Hope, Grant, Chase, words with a direction, sometimes mistaken for purpose, sometimes for a promise.  
The moment when calling a name splits the air, and the split doesn’t close.
