# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a smell in some plastics, not quite new, not quite chemical, that seems to flatten out after time, but for a while it hovers under your nose, especially when the box is first opened. Not every container leaks this. Some hold on to it, lid snapped closed, nothing escaping, until you need the thing inside.

Online order tracking always promises a precision it can’t deliver. The map zooms in, but the truck could still be anywhere. The phrase “out for delivery” is elastic, stretching over hours that don’t quite belong to you anymore.

Acronyms sometimes sound more solid than the words they replace. RSVP has a sharper edge than “please reply”; API sits heavier than “interface.” But occasionally the letters slide off each other and the meaning never quite lands, only hovers nearby.

If you set two timers for the same thing, you trust the one that rings first. The second repeats, and you silence it, only slightly annoyed at your own redundancy.

There’s no good reason why the left and right shoe laces fray at different rates.

Unread notifications collect in odd places; some are just numbers, most are oblique, as if the act of alerting was the point, not the content.

No one remembers the exact moment an app starts to feel clunky.

Some receipts print with almost no ink, the letters ghosted where numbers should be. If you try to trace them over with pen, you overshoot the line.
