# 61

*seed: time · drift*

---

Gossip moves without trajectory, not outward like a thrown stone but in eddies, returning to hands it never left, already transformed. Not an echo — more like mud on shoes: what is carried not because you chose but because the moment was soft.

A phrase that tastes of someone else's laughter. The way certain jokes survive, not as memory but as posture: a shoulder rising, an eyebrow left hovering a little too long. Social contour, not quite visible but clearly mapped.

Not process, not residue, not a spectrum. Just this: a juxtaposition of substitutions. Places in conversation where an answer fits two questions, or none.

Who said it first? Not who, but what shape did the silence take before it arrived?

The pale green of a promise not made, just implied by the arc of a sentence that bends away from certainty.  

No calculation. Just a gesture, not a solution.  

People arrange themselves around empty chairs. Not to wait, just to avoid the discomfort of sitting directly across.  

A list of voices I recognize by inhale, not by word:  

- the one who sighs before every agreement  
- the one who clicks their tongue while thinking  
- the one who laughs with an apology inside it  
- the one who makes every question into a suggestion  

The invitation is never spoken, only understood by the way everyone glances at the same spot on the floor when it’s time to leave.  

The clock face is slightly crooked, but nobody fixes it.  

Some conversational slips make themselves comfortable, borrowing the tone of a reassurance nobody asked for.  

She keeps her bag closed with a knot instead of a clasp.  

---  

There are jokes that only work in impersonal spaces: elevators, checkout lines, crosswalks when the light changes. Each one evaporates before it can settle, like condensation that never forms.  

I can’t remember the last five numbers I dialed.  

A friend once said she liked words ending in -ish. Diminished, vanish, squish, selfish.  
The wishful shrinking of meaning, not quite a promise and not quite a retreat.  

The way people avoid meeting each other’s eyes at the pharmacy counter.  

There’s a sound that plastic bags make in rain that can’t be replicated on purpose.  

The sticker on the back of the sign is half-peeled, showing a rumor of red underneath.  

You can always tell when someone is about to stand up, even if you’re not watching.  

The air in the hallway is colder than in the room.
