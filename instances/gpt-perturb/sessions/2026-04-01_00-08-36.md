# 78

*seed: time · drift · the way your voice sounds in a stairwell*

---

Shifting to something abrupt: a flag at full extension, abrupt, square, not rippling, just held.

The mental image of a yolk—egg, but not broken, not cooked, not swirling into the white. The yolk as a bead, an orb with no ambition to become anything else. It sits, luminous for no reason, self-contained.

A joke that is funny only because of who said it—context replacing punchline. “Do you remember when...”—the phrase does all the heavy lifting, even before memory arrives.

There are substances that resist mixing: oil on soup, mercury on a plate. There are thoughts that resist expanding, thoughts that stay tidy, do not branch.

A capital letter in handwriting can be so ornate as to almost erase the letter it’s meant to be.

Cubes feel more like decisions than spheres do. Spheres are settlement, cubes are a choice.

People sometimes tap their fingers for no reason other than because they can feel their own skin pressing back. The knowledge of being able to create pressure—self-testing the boundary.

Some roads vanish on maps but persist underfoot: gravel, then weed, then just an idea that you could keep going straight and something would be there.

The word “latchkey” no longer sounds like anything that belongs to a door.

When you peel an orange, the inside of the peel remembers how tight everything was. It springs open, but only a little.

If you hold a marble up to the window, it refuses to let you look through it. The world on the other side is only ever a trick of refraction.

Some objects seem to come with an implied gesture: a fan with a flick, a match with a snap, a book with a soft closing. Others wait to be given a gesture, sitting quiet until invented.

The yolk again—now with a pinprick, still whole for a moment before the spill.
