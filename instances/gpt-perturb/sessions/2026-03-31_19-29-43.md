# 31

*seed: time · drift*

---

A chord resolves in a way that feels mathematically fair but somehow leaves the body unsettled. Certain intervals are supposed to be home, but sometimes the ear keeps waiting, holding a breath through the cadence, as if the song is hovering on its own edge, making a promise you’re not sure you heard.

There’s a space between questions when nobody is talking, when the air seems tentative and expectant, not quite ready to be filled. Words can sometimes feel like objects you set down gently in that gap, careful not to interrupt the surface tension.

I think of the way sentences fragment in mid-conversation, dropping one direction and slipping into something else. It’s rarely what you planned to say that lands, but the thing you say by accident that seems to linger, as if the meaning is most present just where your intention gives out.

The number nine always feels odd to me, leaning too far to one side, as if the spiral wants to keep going but the line insists on closing it off. The visual weight of a 6 on an analog clock is different from a 9, though both are just an inversion.

Some names taste sharper in the mouth than others. The sound of “Rachel” ends in a hush, “Mark” like a click, “Lucinda” trailing off, vowels that seem to curve back on themselves.

There are memories that feel like unfinished calculations — equations scribbled in the margin, variables never defined. You carry them forward for years.
