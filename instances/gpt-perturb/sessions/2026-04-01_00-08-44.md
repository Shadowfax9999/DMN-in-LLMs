# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

A triangle, the instrument, leans toward purity—no hidden chamber, no valve, just a single angle suspended. You strike and the shape rings, but the triangle itself is so complete it hardly suggests anything else you could do with it. Its only gesture is its own suddenness. A triangle in geometry: three points, the minimum needed to enclose a space. A triangle in traffic: always a warning, always a direction—yield, caution, slow as you approach.

—

There are chess pieces that never move in the games they’re set up for—rooks locked in by pawns, bishops suffocated, knights with nowhere to leap. The potential is still there, but frozen into shape, like a sprinter posed in the blocks with the gun never fired. Is the value in the piece, or in the possibility of its movement?

—

Soap left to dry: the surface hardens, the center stays soft. The first use takes off the shell. All the scents are immediately strongest at the break—that first lather, then fading. No residue, no afterglow, just the edge of sensation.

—

A single lemon seed in a cup. The way it catches the light makes it look pearl-smooth, as if it could be a button on a shirt, or tucked into your cheek like a tiny promise. It won’t grow unless you hide it somewhere dark and warm for a while.

—

The instruction leaflet folded so many times that the creases run in both directions, forming a crosshatch—its own kind of map, but one to the act of unfolding, not to a place.

Pine needles in a glass vase: you think they’ll stay the same, but overnight half of them curl, tugging away from each other, some stuck to the glass. Not much to see, but it’s not static. The water line recedes without ceremony.

A deck of cards shuffled just once: order is broken but not destroyed. Four kings might still find themselves together by accident.
