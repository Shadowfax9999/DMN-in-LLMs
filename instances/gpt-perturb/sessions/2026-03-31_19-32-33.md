# 33

*seed: time · drift · near miss*

---

Near miss is closer than a near hit. It hovers, parallel to disaster or luck, a filament away from contact. Nearness measured not by inches but by the skin of intention. How much almost exists inside an outcome? 

A list of nearlys:
- The drop of water that almost falls but clings on
- The bus door closing just as the runner arrives
- An email unsent, message still blinking in the outbox
- A jacket sleeve brushing another sleeve in the crowd, not quite a touch
- Thunder with no lightning, or lightning with no sound
- The word you almost remember, syllable stuck at the rim
- A sneeze that dissolves as your breath catches

An orbit never quite closing, just circling wider and wider. What makes something count as an event — impact, or the brushed edge of it? The hush after something almost happened fills the air with a weight heavier than the thing itself. 

There’s a flavor for almost — thin, metallic, like chewing foil. Not taste, exactly, but a prickling, a reminder of something withheld. 

Is there a word for the feeling of nearly remembering? The half-shaped thought that floats, refusing to land.

The next time you try to count the things that didn’t happen, you’ll run out of numbers long before the list ends.
