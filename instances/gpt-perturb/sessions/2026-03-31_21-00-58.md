# 74

*seed: time · drift · suburb*

---

suburb — maybe just an edge, not a line, not a boundary, but a kind of soft fringe: the part where things thin out, or get greener, or the trees are placed by plan, not by accident. Not quite city, not quite not. A promise of both proximity and escape.

The shape of a suburb could be round or spread like a puddle, always more road than makes sense, and a house that looks right until you see it again and notice the shutters aren’t real.

The sky over the suburb seems wider, but it’s sometimes harder to see it unobstructed, because the roofs and the wires are always in the way.

Nothing burns very brightly in a suburb. Everything is dimmed a few clicks, even the stars.

If you stand outside at night, there are insects humming in a steady, practiced way, as though rehearsed. There are windows that glow in cool blue rectangles — television, always television — and someone, somewhere, running a load of laundry.

Not everyone in a suburb knows the neighbors, but you can learn people by dogs, or by the color of cars, or the way the porch light is left on or off.

Mailboxes cluster at the end of certain streets, and sometimes the mail comes very late, in the dark.

Grass has a smell when just cut, but when left alone, it’s faint and almost sweet.

The feeling that something is about to happen never arrives, but there is a kind of gentle suspense all the same.

Children in bike helmets pass by, one behind the other.

There are more kinds of fences than you’d expect.

Some lawns have little flags in them, marking something below, but you never find out what.

It’s possible to stand perfectly still on a sidewalk and hear only the hum of distant air conditioners.

A porch swing moves slightly in the wind, but there is no one sitting in it.
