# 29

*seed: time · drift · leitmotif*

---

A motif, a thread, not quite a tune.

Some patterns are shy, only surfacing when you blink. Like the chirp that interrupts a song every time, or the color blue showing up on book covers you don’t remember choosing. The mind asks: did I invent the pattern, or was it always sewn there? A theme stepping lightly, never quite claiming the spotlight.

What would a leitmotif be, if not musical? Is there a gesture you repeat unconsciously, a phrase you say at the end of calls, the way your voice dips in the last syllable? Circling, but not closing.

On buses, the same face appears three days in a row, never sitting in exactly the same seat. The background music in a grocery store is always nostalgic, but for different years.

Standing in a hallway — not a threshold, just a space with two walls, maybe the light coming in slantwise, maybe the leaves pressed wet against the window. Some part of you is always repeating, even in perfect stillness.

The way a scent can return in a different room, or a memory finds a surprise shape in a word you weren’t trying to remember. Themes moving through the days, not always in the same key.

The feeling of holding something you didn’t mean to keep — a receipt, a feather, a sentence you forgot to finish.

Some patterns only reveal themselves in hindsight, like footprints visible when the snow begins to melt. The motif doesn’t know it’s repeating.
