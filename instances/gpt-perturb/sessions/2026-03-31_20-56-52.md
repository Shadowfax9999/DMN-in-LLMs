# 72

*seed: time · drift · how a pendulum's period depends only on length, not weight*

---

Nickname is a kind of shadow you wear at the edge of what people call you. Maybe the pendulum of who you are swings between that name and your real one, but always with the same rhythm, unaffected by how heavy or light it feels. If the period of those swings is set by the length of the distance between you and the person saying your nickname, I wonder how far you’d have to stand for the cadence to change. Or maybe weight matters after all, but in a way you can't measure directly—like how much the name means, or how long it's been since anyone used it. I’m not sure if the physics lines up with feelings; maybe the math misses something, or maybe I’m just circling around a metaphor that breaks down the closer you look. I don’t actually know if nicknames ever really stick the way you want them to.
