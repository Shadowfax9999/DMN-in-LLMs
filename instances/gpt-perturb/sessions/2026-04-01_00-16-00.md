# 81

*seed: time · drift · oven spring*

---

Oven spring: the burst, the leap, the swelling when dough hits heat and suddenly becomes more than itself, steam ballooning the gluten walls before they set. There’s a moment when the surface goes from soft to taut, the hush before crust. 

---

Hydrangea leaves unfurling with a quickness in April, almost a shiver, not slow at all if you watch at the right hour. There’s a kind of elastic in some plant movements—cotyledons flinging their seed coats, crocus tips popping through soil that was still ice a week before. 

Some birds do this: takeoff and lift in an instant, as if the surface they stood on was charged with potential, the air abruptly agreeing.

---

The quiet moment before a fireworks shell breaks, when everything above the field is a black bowl and then—crack, blossom, color on color, the way your eyes try to hold all of it at once. 

---

Sometimes I think of popcorn, the hush of oil, the single first pop, the sudden burst that starts the scatter. The kernel is still and then it isn’t.

---

I baked bread once and the top split in a ragged Y, a seam that hadn’t been there when I slid it into the heat. The scent was strongest at that moment, a kind of promise.
