# 57

*seed: time · drift · denim*

---

Denim: not the blue first, but the ridges under fingertips, seams done in yellow thread, the faint give and snap. The way knees fade before anything else, but the inside hem remains indigo so long. Weight of a pocket loaded with coins, pulling the fabric into a new geometry, sagged, creased. Frayed edge at the cuff, single white threads resisting the unravel.

The word “yoke,” in the context of jeans, is the only place it feels ordinary. Topstitching tracing a shallow V. Sometimes the pocket bag is printed with a pattern, secret, barely seen, the inside-out world.

I think of back pockets gone thin — the ghost of a wallet pressed into the fabric, the corner catching on a thumbnail every time you reach. Rivets gone smooth where hands have brushed past for years. Metal buttons shaped with a hollow at the center.

Denim’s particular smell — colder and sharper than cotton jersey, like it carries something from the place it was made. A new pair: stiff, loud, refusing to fold, announcing itself with every movement. The echo after a knee bends and the cloth hesitates before settling.

Zipper teeth that never align unless you pinch just so. 

There’s a memory of waiting in line for a dressing room, the stale warmth of fluorescent light, and the uneasy sense of the mirror’s distance — not a real reflection, but something stretched and slightly off.

Some people roll the cuffs, half to show the selvedge, half to feel the edge. Others let the denim pool at the ankle until it’s worn through, fabric dark above and pale below. 

The smallest coin pocket seems designed for something forgotten. 

Laundry tag printed with more information than anyone could want, creased, unreadable, half-torn by the time you try to check it. 

The word “denim” used as a color, but never quite matching the thing itself.
