# 94

*seed: time · drift*

---

A stamp. The adhesive is already there, slick and chemical, waits for pressure or moisture. There’s a faint design, printed too finely for a cheap envelope. When you press it down the paper ripples, not smooth — an imprint on the other side.

---

Banana bread crust, dense at the corners, the pattern from the ridged pan baked in. Sometimes it tears away in one flat piece when removing from the tin. Warm, it breaks easily; cold, it clings.

---

The word “crust” is blunt, short, but the thing itself can be delicate, fragile at the edges, tougher at the bottom where sugar gathers.

---

There are words that never appear on menus in restaurants but decide everything about the food. Pith, husk, gristle.
