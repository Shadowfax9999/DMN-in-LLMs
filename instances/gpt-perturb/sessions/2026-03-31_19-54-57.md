# 45

*seed: time · drift · wind chime*

---

Lightest metal triggers and glass teeth, always a fragment of a chord, never the whole song. Not melody, but interruption, the kind that doesn’t ask to be noticed.

A gust makes them tangle, and the next stillness is longer, off-balance, an unresolved shape in the air. Phrases that never finish echoing.

Do wind chimes ever fully stop, or do they shift imperceptibly from movement to rest, too slow for hearing to divide them?

The sound is thin, but by the second note, it is already memory.

Sometimes the wrong wind tries to play them, a force too blunt, and they only thud.

---

There’s nothing to see in the middle of a fogged-in morning. Every direction is the same degree of gray, as if the horizon dissolved. Even noise seems smaller, halved and held back.

Somewhere a bus is idling. The anticipation is louder than the engine.

That feeling when you almost remember what you’re supposed to do next, but there’s nothing written down.

---

A queue at the pharmacy splits into two at the register, like water finding channels. Everyone checks the floor tiles for a clue.

Noise-levels shift around a joke not everyone heard. At the moment you commit to the wrong line, the other one starts to move.

The word “queue” looks like it should be pronounced differently. The two silent vowels at the end, a line of letters not doing their expected job.

---

Yesterday’s password can’t be found. The form won’t let you try again for thirty seconds. Counting is slower when you watch the numbers. 

When the screen finally lets you in, you forget why you logged on.

---

Noise-canceling headphones erase more than intended. There’s nothing to notice about silence until you forget the street used to be part of the background.

Muffled footsteps sound larger when there’s no context.

The refrigerator hum becomes the clock.

---

The tiles are a different temperature than the air.
