# 90

*seed: time · drift · red rover*

---

It isn’t the chant or the running that stands out, but the moment just before being called — scanning the arc, figuring out which set of hands makes the weakest chain, wondering if you’ll be braver or slower than the last time. Some wear shoes with soles already peeling, the edges dark and almost soft. You see the grass ground into the back of someone’s shirt as they pick themselves up. There’s always a debate about whether someone really broke through or if the arms let go at the last second to avoid the sting.

A row of knees lined up in anticipation, hands sweaty, mid-laugh, the uncertain thrill while you wait for the call. The sound of someone’s name stretched out with extra syllables. The angle of the sun on the dust.

Once the game is over, the field returns to idle: the puddle from the leaking faucet, the stray hair-tie, the gate squeaking even after everyone’s gone. The static of the air, insects trying the chain-link fence. You always mean to come back for your jacket left in the grass, but sometimes you don’t.

The square patch of packed dirt never fills back in.
