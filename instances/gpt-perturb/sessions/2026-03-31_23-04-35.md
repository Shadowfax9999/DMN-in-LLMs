# 98

*seed: time · drift*

---

A name that doesn’t fit its owner: the dog called Panther, the cat called Steve. Not irony, just dissonance. The mind hesitates for a moment, then accepts the substitution.

---

Ingredients I know exist but would never buy: anchovy paste, rosewater, agar-agar, tamarind concentrate. They always appear in recipes with authority, as if every kitchen keeps a shelf for the improbable.

---

The way some numbers feel smaller or larger than their actual value: twelve feels neat, like stacked boxes; eleven is just barely holding together; thirteen is a dare. Twenty-two looks like a mistake that repeated, but twenty-four is organized.

---

There are windows that won’t open more than an inch—security measure or design flaw—but the view is the same. Nothing changes except possibility.

---

People who say “I’ll let you go” at the end of a phone call. As if permission is required for silence.

---

The peculiar decisiveness of a yes/no checkbox. The little square is waiting for an X, unburdened by nuance. Most things are not that simple, but the box insists on it.

---

There is always one sock that refuses to dry, even if every other piece of laundry is already folded. The sock holds something back, but only by accident.

---

My hands remember the shape of a key I haven’t used in years. The teeth and ridges, the heavy loop, cool against the palm. Not the door it matches—just itself, complete.
