# 47

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way the brightness on a phone screen flickers from one setting to another, not gradually, but in quick notches—slightly too dim, a jump, then slightly too bright. It’s never precise, only moving in increments someone else chose. 

The faint blue underline that appears under a misspelled word while typing, even if it’s a name or a code that only makes sense to you. For a moment, the system proposes an alternative—a word that almost fits, but not quite.  

Scroll bars that nearly disappear unless you touch them, like they’re hiding, or only temporarily permitted.  

A group chat dormant for weeks, then a sudden burst of six messages with no warning. Reading them out of order because some arrived early, some late, and realizing the thread makes less sense reconstructed.  

There’s a specific awkwardness in mistyping your own password, not once, but twice, checking each finger for which one misfired. Sometimes the muscle memory is more faithful to the old password than the new.

The hollow knock of a plastic water bottle, almost drum-like, when it’s mostly empty.  

QR codes that spiral into tiny dots when you zoom in, until they stop looking like a message and become just a pattern.  

If you press a button on a vending machine too lightly, nothing happens; too hard, the whole panel flexes but still nothing dispenses. There’s a pressure that is exactly right but unmarked.

Emails that arrive with the subject line “RE:” but you never saw the original message.  

Someone called out a number in a waiting room and two people stood up at the same time.  

The cursor blinked for an entire minute before the next word appeared.
