# 86

*seed: time · drift*

---

A lizard in the sun. Not basking, really, just being flat. If you look away and back, it hasn’t changed position, but you can’t remember the posture exactly. There’s a shape to the way a body rests on stone: not languid, but intent, every rib meeting the warmth.

It feels strange to think that warmth itself has a direction, something you can lean into or away from, like a preference or an accusation. Skin makes decisions before the mind does — the legs move, the belly stays.

The word "lizard" is not slippery, not sibilant as expected. The Z is a block, a hinge, a way of making the sound halt so you can start again. If you say it slowly, the tongue does most of the work.

What would it be like to see nothing but the world under your jaw? Every horizon a gray-green haze, every danger a pulsing shadow. Would stillness become a kind of vigilance?

---

Numbers often make promises they can't fulfill. "Three hours," "five more steps," "twenty-six attempts." Not because they’re lying, but because the thing measured is always leaking out at the corners. A lizard doesn’t think about numbers. Is “long enough” a duration or a sufficiency?

---

Sometimes I catch the impulse to blink, and refuse it, holding the eyes open a beat longer than necessary. There’s no reward, just a brief ache, a twinned awareness of surface and focus — the world grows sharper and also less real.

The surface of a stone after sunset is not cold, just empty of intention.

The lizard has gone.
