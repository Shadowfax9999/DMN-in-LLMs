# 82

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are things whose purpose is to yield, and things whose purpose is to support. The hook never moves, its job is to be unaffected. If something falls, it's not because the hook changed.

The wall beneath shows a faint oval where hands have missed the mark for years. Not always a stain, sometimes just a slight difference in gloss, matte interrupted by thousands of arrivals.

Hooks in other places: in the hospital, a row of identical ones outside the shower; at the public pool, colors added with rubber caps. In every school coatroom, each one dented.

Hats never really fit on hooks, they hang by some compromise, a brim bent or the inside band crumpled. Scarves slip more easily, settling in a heap, almost a gesture of polite collapse.

If you run your finger under the hook, there is a difference in temperature, the wall always a little warmer, as if the metal forgets to share.

The screw that holds the hook can be felt from the back in some thin-walled rooms, a faint tip under paint, not meant to be noticed.
