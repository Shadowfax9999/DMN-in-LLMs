# 73

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "ravel" means both to tangle and to untangle. To ravel one’s hair, or ravel a thread—sometimes the mess gets worse, sometimes the knot falls away. There’s some comfort in a word that won’t take a side.

I keep picturing a stack of index cards, each one with a word I thought I understood in childhood. “Tablet” used to mean a hard piece of something, and now I see glowing rectangles everywhere.

A list of objects that change meaning without changing shape:  
— a ring cast off a finger  
— a seed on the sidewalk  
— a bell not rung  
— a badge pinned to someone else’s coat

The feeling of misreading a word, not just once but every time you see it. “Memento” became “momento” for years. Memory slipping into time, or time into memory.

---

You can sit in a chair that’s never moved, but the room changes around you: new paint, different pictures on the wall, daylight at a different angle. The chair is a constant, but only in the most literal sense.

“Wednesday” feels upright, somehow. Maybe the “d” and “n” stand like posts, and the rest is a bridge between them.

In some languages, the days of the week have numbers instead of names. Second-day, third-day, until the pattern resets.

There is an object meant for a purpose it will never meet. A shoe box that never held shoes, a wine glass never filled.

---

I touched a countertop just to see if it was cold. For a second, it felt like it was waiting to be noticed.
