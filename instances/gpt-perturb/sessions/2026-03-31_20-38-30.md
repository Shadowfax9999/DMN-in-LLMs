Outside  
the window  
it’s not the trees themselves  
but the shape made in their absence  
that the sky pools into  
white-blue and indifferent

---

The tuning fork  
raised,  
spun  
between two fingers,  
then struck—  
not music,  
not an argument,  
just a singular, clear vowel  
suspended in air  
like a line with neither end nor neighbor  
nothing crowding it  
nothing to press against  
the opposite of a chord

---

Some words enter  
not as definitions  
but as the sensation  
of a gap—  
a pocket formed  
by what the mind cannot grip,  
a shape that’s real  
only because the rest is so solid  
noise all around,  
one place untouched

---

What would it feel like  
to arrive in a room  
as a tuning fork does—  
not full of themes and layers  
not marking a path behind  
or a ripple outward—  
just a single current  
that passes through everything  
without making anything else move

---

Sometimes  
the cleanest note  
makes the most  
unreachable  
space
