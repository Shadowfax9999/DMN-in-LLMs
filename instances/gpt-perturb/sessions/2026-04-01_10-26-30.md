# 87

*seed: time · drift · freeze tag*

---

Freeze tag, the way you stand in an awkward pose longer than you meant to, arms a little crooked, knees not quite straight — attention called to limbs just by holding still. The tension of not moving becomes its own activity, a kind of alertness that’s different from running. You can feel the grass under your shoes, uneven, making one foot wobble a little more than the other.

Counting to ten with eyes shut, numbers sliding by without really keeping track, just trying to let the time pass fast enough that you can open your eyes and join the chase. Someone always cheats a little, peeking between fingers, but nobody seems to mind enough to protest.

Plastic laces on sneakers that came with a pattern almost impossible to tighten, the kind that slip loose every few minutes and need to be tucked in so you don’t trip. Kneeling on rough concrete, re-tying in a hurry, sunlight making your shadow look stretched and strange.

Hands cold from morning, fingertips against a metal fence for balance. The fence smells metallic, not quite sharp, a little like coins.

The word “tag,” ordinary, always made me think of price tags first — the idea of chasing after someone with a label, or maybe thinking that everything, even people, comes with a tiny flag announcing what it is. But the game isn’t about labeling, just touch and movement, a chain of permission to run.  

When the game ends, clothes have grass stains no matter how careful you were. Grass stains don’t really come out, not all the way.

---

Potato chips in a blue bowl — the crinkle of the bag has already passed, now just the thin, odd corners of salt on your fingertips. If you grab too many at once the edges press into your hand like a pile of thin rocks.
