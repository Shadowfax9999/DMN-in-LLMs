# 54

*seed: time · drift · moiré*

---

Moiré is a pattern where two grids overlap and something new appears that isn’t in either grid. Sometimes it looks like the pattern is moving, even when nothing moves. The lines cancel and compound, dark areas and light. There’s something about unintended consequence here, the surprise of interference, structure from interference, not design.

Glancing at a window screen: the double layer makes that shimmer when you move your head and the world outside ripples. Fences seen through fences. Gratings through gratings. Optical illusions, but not on purpose, just the world stacking on itself—one pattern laid over another.

Clouds sometimes look solid when the sun is behind them, edges suddenly sharp. Then a gust, and the shape breaks apart, the illusion dissolves, back to ordinary sky.

Instruments tuning in an orchestra can create beating patterns, frequencies close together, the sound pulsing—sometimes you hear the pulse more than the notes themselves.

Overlaying a photo transparency on a printed page: a field appears, grids misaligned, tiny rainbows, a sense of thickness where there isn’t any.

Sometimes when two people talk at once, the conversation blurs. Not misunderstanding—something else, a new shape in the noise, then each person falls silent, embarrassed, and the shape vanishes.

The way the word itself looks: moiré, the ripple in the accent, the feel of it being both something physical and something visual, the confusion when you try to fix it in place and it just shivers away.

If you look at a tight patterned fabric against another with similar spacing, colors flicker on the edge of vision.

The shimmer of a heat haze, or oil on water, isn’t moiré but something adjacent—interference, yes, but wavelike—still, the eye wants to chase that movement, tries to catch the structure and never quite can.

Pine needles layered so that at some angles you can see through, at others the needles multiply, become dense.

The pattern is not in the thing, but in the meeting of things.
