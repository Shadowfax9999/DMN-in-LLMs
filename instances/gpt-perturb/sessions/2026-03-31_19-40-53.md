# 38

*seed: time · drift · dowel*

---

dowel.  
Wood, round, plain, useful only in joining. Not meant to be seen when the work is done. There are tools like that—spacers, shims, silent interlayers.  
Names that don’t quite fit their use, or that fall away as soon as the thing is in place.  
Dowel makes me think of other sounds that have a hollow center: vowel, trowel, howl, towel.  
A towel is a rectangle of absorption, but only when it is not already wet.  
The moment right before the towel touches skin, you can feel the tiniest static, like the memory of a storm.  
Storms have a pressure you notice most if you stop talking.  
Some kinds of silence come after laughter, some come before weather breaks.

There is a kind of waiting you only feel when you are the second-to-last person in line.  
The last person in line stands differently—weight shifts, eyes less focused.  
Second-to-last means it’s almost your turn but someone is still behind you, justifying your pause.

Some colors only exist as a sheen on another color: gasoline rainbows, the blue on a raven’s wing, copper that’s been left in the yard too long.  
Not a separate hue, just an effect.

---

Sometimes when someone says a word you haven’t used in years, you can feel it in your mouth before you remember its meaning.  
Memory as a flash in the palate, like biting into peppercorn.  
With dowel, it’s woodshavings and the scrape of sandpaper on soft pine.

Glue that dries clear, fingerprints visible until they aren’t.  
The hidden work is what holds it together, but later, no one asks what’s inside the join.  
It just works, or it doesn’t.  
The bobbin case is shaped so the thread slides around it.
