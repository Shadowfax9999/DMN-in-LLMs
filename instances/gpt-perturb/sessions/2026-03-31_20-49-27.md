# 69

*seed: time · drift · alarm in another apartment*

---

A sequence of letters with no referent: KLMN.  
The mind insists one must come next, but there isn’t a rule—nothing after N that feels inevitable.

A shape with too many corners, all the same length. I keep returning to the idea of a regular polygon, but if each angle is one degree wider, eventually the shape unfolds, and the familiar perimeter stops making sense.  
There’s relief in geometry that doesn’t describe anything, just a quantity of edges doing their own thing.

A dog’s tail flicks and pauses, then resumes, then stops entirely. No signal, no meaning, just the rhythm of muscle and air, fur following skin.

Some days the air outside is dense and close, and movement becomes thick—turning your head feels like swimming. This has nothing to do with humidity, just the way the body interprets the day.

---

A word spoken once means welcome; twice, it can mean uncertainty; three times, it becomes a habit rather than a greeting.  
There should be a word for the moment when repetition stops being noticed.

You can stare at wallpaper until the pattern dissolves and the wall loses its intention, becomes just surface.  
There’s comfort in that loss of design—when the eye finally lets go of searching for structure.

When you listen to the clock, the ticking is regular, but time itself is not experienced in even increments. Some hours compress, others stretch so thin you forget you’re inside them.

A kitchen sponge left too long near the sink edge grows stiff, then brittle, and then it stops being a sponge and becomes a piece of debris.

---

A set of instructions read backward never gets you back to where you started.
