# 97

*seed: time · drift · the feeling of pulling tape off a roll*

---

That first moment, the tape’s edge resistant, then yielding, puckering away with a wrinkled certainty, the adhesive giving up its hold molecule by molecule. Fingers pressed into the cold plastic core, the soundless stretch before the sudden rip. The way the tape wants to spring back, curling at the end, catching dust that’s always waiting somewhere.

Not a sound, not a texture, just the drag of intention across a line you can’t quite see until the light lifts an edge. The smallest fold in the strip makes it almost impossible to lay the tape down smooth. Clear tape against cardboard, the subtler war between gloss and matte, one always trying to outshine the other.

Packing a box for no reason, rehearsing closures. The satisfaction of a line pressed flat with the side of a palm, small air bubbles erased in silent waves. Squares and rectangles: the tape always prefers a straight edge but never insists.

---

Vegetable names that seem to belong to other languages: rutabaga, endive, chayote, kohlrabi, salsify, okra. Each one more like a character than a garnish.

---

A tangent: the weird confidence of labeling a button “ESC” and expecting everyone to understand its promise. The button doesn’t escape, nothing leaves, but it suggests something gets left behind.

---

I never trust the expiration date on anything in a glass jar. The glass seems like a guarantee, but the lid keeps its secrets. It’s not the sight of the date, but the faintest twist in your wrist—resistance or ease, the difference between “still safe” and “don’t risk it.”

---

The unexpected cold of a metal doorknob, even in warm weather. Doorways are the most literal promise: you can go, or not. The knob never answers.
