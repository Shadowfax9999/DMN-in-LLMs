# 50

*seed: time · drift · cigarette butt*

---

Cigarette butt. It doesn’t mean anything unless you focus on the shape. Flattened at one end, spongy and misshapen, a tiny wet mass. Sometimes standing upright in pavement cracks, like the flag of some lost city, sometimes mashed against curb edges. A box of matches spilled half-open in someone’s jacket pocket, smell of sulfur and ash.

Parking lot stripes — faded, brackish white against faded tar, wobbly at the ends where the paint didn’t quite hold. The sensation when you try to step exactly on the line, the mind deciding for no reason which foot is the “correct” one for that stripe, the game ending without you noticing.

A person steps out of a car while holding a stack of manila folders pressed flat against their chest. The top folder slides slightly, and the person presses the stack tighter. An elbow pokes the car door, which closes softly. There’s an adjustment — almost a shrug — to keep everything in balance for the short walk in.

The guy in the orange vest has a megaphone clipped to his belt but never uses it. There’s just a sense that he could.

Spray of windshield wiper fluid on glass. Blue fluid trailing down, breaking into forking rivulets, joined by the gray swipe of a blade. A moment when the wipers stop at the apex and pause before coming down. No one is thinking about it except, maybe, the person in the car.

The word “invoice” on a paper folded into thirds. The fold line is never quite exact. Sometimes the edge is jagged, compressed where it was pressed with a thumbnail. Numbers are inside. None of them mean anything except to the few people who need them to mean something.

Someone tosses a coin into their cup holder. It lands with an indistinct clink, not really like any other sound.

There’s a roundabout three blocks away. Some drivers pause uncertainly at the entrance and others sweep through, certain the rules will accommodate whatever they choose to do. The roundabout always looks slightly too small for the cars passing through it.

Across the street, a flag is tangled around its pole, caught halfway up, wind not strong enough to unwind the fabric.
