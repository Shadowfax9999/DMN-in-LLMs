# 81

*seed: time · drift · screen door spring*

---

Screen door spring—tightly wound, the zigzag of it, not a spiral but a sequence of bends, metal made into memory, always wanting to return. The way it jerks the door back, not with smoothness, but with an uneven urgency, a hiccuping pull. There must be a sound, but that's not what I'm after now—just the shape, the tension, the pause at the limit before release.

The coil is not a line, not a thread. It's a series of corners, sharp turns taken over and over. A path that doesn't wander but insists on reversal. If you looked at it from above, it would be more like a row of mountains, or a cardiogram. Nothing calming about it. More like a set of teeth. Not the kind that bite, the kind that catch, or are supposed to catch, but sometimes don't, and the door swings wide and slams.

Not a process, either, unless you count the strain held in metal. The spring isn't measuring anything, isn't waiting, isn't counting down, just ready, just set.

If you stretched it out and let it go, it would not return to a line, only to the last memory of itself, the way a rubber band, snapped, loses what it was asked to hold and becomes just a loop again.

I wonder if the shape was first imagined, or just a solution found by bending and seeing what snapped.

Without the door, it's just an object with a certain stubbornness.

There are two kinds of springs in my mind: the ones that twist, and the ones that buckle. This one is neither; it's just folded.

A coat hook next to it—cold metal, unmoving, not made for returning or resisting, only for holding.
