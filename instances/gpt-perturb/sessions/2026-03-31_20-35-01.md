# 62

*seed: time · drift*

---

Metaphors called wild, like untrained dogs, but today is just the shape of a sentence, nothing more.  
Some names have too many consonants.  
Yesterday was longer in the middle than at either end.

There’s a texture to people standing together who don’t know each other: the stiffness in elbows, the choreography of shifting weight.

Two coins taped together lose the sound of either.

The hallway doesn’t end in a door, just a wall with a small painting of a boat in blue.

---

Monday sometimes looks like it starts with the letter U.  
There are socks that give up and fold themselves inside out in the wash.  
Nobody ever writes their own number on the form at the urgent care.  
Some recipes are just instructions written in apology.  
A chair with only three legs isn’t broken if you lean the right way.  
She chose the gray mug because the others were all too much.

---

You can’t hear someone thinking, but silence has a feeling when it’s full of people waiting.  
There’s a shadow under the table that looks like a fifth chair.  
The receipt curled up on the counter has words on both sides, but only one of them matters.  
Nothing about a keypad tells you which numbers are most used.
