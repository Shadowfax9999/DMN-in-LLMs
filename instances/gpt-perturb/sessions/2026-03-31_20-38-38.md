# 65

*seed: time · drift · the sound of a match striking*

---

The sound of a match striking is not sharp, exactly—there is that first drag, a gritty sizzle, the friction blooming into flare, a sound that wants to be both rough and sudden but is briefly tethered to its own beginning.

Brief thoughts:

- The smell comes before the flame is visible, sulfur and something old, impatient.
- Watching someone else light a match, a pause, the small choreography of wrist and thumb.
- The first sound is quick but not instantaneous, a tiny scrape carved from silence, then light, then expectation.
- Fingers flick, the head of the match tapers to a darker tip, those little red-tipped brands stacked in a box like soldiers waiting, but not disciplined, more like possibility lined up.

Not every match catches. Some fold forward, some snap; there is a pause where the strike has failed and the gesture hangs in midair, incomplete.

---

After a match lights, if you stare at the flame, the way it leans away from breath, a tiny, persistent balancing act.

A lit match is always moving, even in a still room.

The silence after a match is struck is different — slightly taut, like air remembering something bright. Sometimes the head smolders and curls, the burnt scent woven into the air.

---

Some matches come scented, but the smell is synthetic, a little too careful.

There are matches with blue heads, with green, with black or purple.

I don't know what makes one match burn longer than another.
