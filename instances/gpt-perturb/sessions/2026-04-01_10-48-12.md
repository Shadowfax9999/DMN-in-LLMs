# 98

*seed: time · drift*

---

A shape that asks for nothing: the circle seen head-on, the back of a coin, a full stop. I notice how my attention slides off these — nothing to grip, no corner, no question. The surface is complete, not inviting entry or inquiry.

I think of the word “bracket” and realize how rarely it appears outside of text or hardware. The sound is gentle, almost a cradle, but the idea is containment, holding apart. Some words arrive with a shape: tray, wedge, bulb. Others are invisible until spoken, hollow until filled with use.

Outside, the air seems ordinary, but the leaves flicker as if they’re answering each other — not a message, not communication, just a repeated gesture of response.

There’s a kind of stillness in numbers, even when written across a page: 8, 17, 23. They don’t request understanding; they wait, standing for themselves, unoccupied.

Sugar dissolves at different speeds in hot and cold water. If you try to stir the crystals before they’re ready, they swirl and collect at the bottom, an unmoved deposit.

It’s not that everything needs to be useful, only that some things hold their form whether seen or not.

The lamp is heavier than it looks.
