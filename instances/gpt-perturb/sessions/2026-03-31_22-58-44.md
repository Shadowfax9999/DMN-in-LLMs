*seed: time · drift · soundhole*

---

Soundhole. Not the strings, not the wood, but the round opening, which isn’t quite emptiness—there’s the edge, the rim, the lip worn by passing arms and sleeves. To put your hand over it changes the whole sound, but to look inside is to see bracing, maybe some dust, a paper label curling in the dark. Sometimes a sticker. Sometimes the serial numbers are written in pencil and half illegible, the rest hidden in shadow.  

A soundhole on a guitar vs. the F-holes on a cello or violin. F-holes are more like eyebrows, slits instead of a mouth. The sound escapes differently, maybe. The curves look deliberate, maybe even sly—though that’s personifying a shape, and maybe the shape doesn’t mind.

The way the interior smells faintly of glue, wood shavings. Once you realize this, you want to sniff every instrument you pick up, but it’s not something that’s supposed to be done. Still, the inside of a guitar holds a particular scent, different in every instrument, even if from the same tree.

The idea of something existing for what passes through it, not for what it is. A hole as an invitation: light, sound, fingers, breath. 

---

There’s a kind of silence inside a sleeping house that isn’t quite silent—pipes settling, the hum of the refrigerator. Noticing it sometimes feels like being underwater, but not in the sense of weight, more in the way things are muffled and movements happen in a kind of hush, but not empty or lonely, just submerged.

The shape of a potato peeler, how your thumb fits perfectly along the side, even if it wasn’t designed for your particular hand. The metal is always cold at first, and the way it blunts after years, so the peels get thicker until someone finally replaces the blade.

There are verbs that only make sense with particular objects: to decant, to fillet, to lather, to unspool. The words linger, wanting to be attached to something real, but sometimes they just drift by.

---

Sometimes a list is more satisfying than a sentence:

- the edge of a countertop, just below the line of sight  
- wrist bones pressing against a tabletop  
- the taste of a crayon, remembered by accident  
- the seam under a sticker, gathering dust  
- three spoons in a jar by the sink, not matching  
- the ridges on the side of a coin, felt with a fingernail  
- the warmth left behind between folded towels

A session can be short.
