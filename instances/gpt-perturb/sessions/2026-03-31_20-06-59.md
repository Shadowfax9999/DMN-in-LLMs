# 49

*seed: time · drift*

---

In a lineup there is always the one person humming, not loudly enough to be asked to stop, but enough that, after a while, others adjust their posture in little increments, as though to let the humming pass through them with less resistance. The hum doesn’t align with any song I know — it wanders, settling on a half-melody, then drifting off. I wonder if it feels to the hummer like a way to mark the time, or if it’s unconscious, like rubbing a thumb against a coin in a pocket.

Inventory of what people do in lines:
1. Arrange receipts.
2. Shift weight from foot to foot.
3. Read the same poster twice.
4. Open and close a zipper.
5. Count backward, not as a strategy but as something to do with numbers.

The fluorescent light above the counter flickers only in the way it turns the skin of hands a slightly different color, somewhere between gray and yellow.

Sometimes the person at the front is not ready when it’s their turn — rummaging for a wallet, re-reading a slip. The rest of the line doesn’t audibly sigh, but there is a ripple, a recalibration of stance.

A lull happens — nothing for a few seconds, the system resetting.

The employee prints a receipt and the sound is thin and almost metallic.
