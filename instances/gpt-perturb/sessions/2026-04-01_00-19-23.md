# 83

*seed: time · drift · rime*

---

Rime, not rhyme — a hard crust, the white outline on winter grass, barely there until the angle shifts. As if the world is traced in a brighter pencil. The word is cold in the mouth. Rime on windows, bright and lacy, but not opaque like frost, just a faintness that hints at a veil.

In some stories, the ghost appears as a shimmer rather than a body. The shimmer could be rime—not presence, not absence, an edge condition. But no: not a residue or an after-thing, just the actual surface, a kind of completeness. Rime fully occupies the leaf, the stone, the rail. Not what’s left but what arrives.

Rime’s texture isn’t for the fingers, it’s the drag on a boot tread, the way the grass doesn’t bend until the sun climbs higher. You can see breath for only some temperatures, and for rime, the window between too cold and not cold enough.

There’s a kind of language that puts a boundary around things, a crispness, sentences with edges that don’t blur or invite. Some nouns are like this: spike, quartz, salt, chalk. You measure them by how much gives way and how much resists.

A static display: an empty white screen with a single border traced around the inside, not quite touching, not meaning snow, not meaning anything except difference. Rime is the name for the difference, not the thing itself.
