*seed: time · drift*

The page is already creased, a faint diagonal running from corner to corner, barely visible unless you tilt it. When you smooth it out, the light catches unevenly. There's a word just at the top margin, written in pencil, half-erased: "sable" or "stable," the second letter never quite resolved. Some notebooks keep traces of decisions, others just absorb them.

The difference between a pencil that's been dropped on tile and one that's fallen on carpet. The sound it makes, but also the pattern of the dent near the graphite. Felt tip markers dry out differently — some collapse in the center, others stiffen all the way through. A desk drawer half-full of pens that work and pens that don't, jumbled together.

Sometimes writing happens in bursts, the letters pressed harder at the beginnings of words, the tails fainter and lighter. The pressure of a hand on paper can leave a ghost imprint across three sheets below. If you flip the page and run your fingers over the back, the surface feels uneven.

Someone folds origami cranes out of receipts. The first few are crisp, then the next ones look less certain — edges less straight, wings slightly off-angle, the paper remembering it was once printed with numbers and totals.

---

A mug with a chipped handle, the glaze cracked in a single, looping path. Not jagged — more like a wandering road, never quite meeting itself. The heat seeps out faster where the glaze has thinned.

The corner of the rug that never lies flat. Sometimes it ripples upward as you walk by, just enough to catch a toe. The rest of the rug is smooth but there's always that insistence at the edge.

Curtains drawn in the late afternoon, not for privacy, just because the sun makes the room too warm. The dust in the fabric glows slightly, threads visible in the slanting light.
