# 93

*seed: time · drift · rubber band ball*

---

Rubber band ball: not a tool, not a toy, not useful in itself but urging disassembly. The tension between bands is a kind of agreement, each one holding the others in place. As a child, always the urge to unwind — to peel off a single band, see if the whole thing would collapse, but it's harder to start than expected, the first layer is tight, grooved, gripping itself. Sometimes the colors are faded on one side, sun-exposed — the inside ones snap more easily, dry and brittle.

Rubber bands left alone lose their spring, fray into a powdery string. The ball is a way to keep them alive, each new addition tensing the mass. The inner core is unknowable unless you commit to destruction, but removal is never satisfying — loose bands tangle, the sphere is lost.

On a desk, one always wants to roll it but never does, it feels inappropriate, a quiet violence. It absorbs energy, bounces badly.

---

Cake icing spatula: face wide, edge flexible, meant for smoothing but always leaves sign of its passage. Cold icing, stiff, pushes back, the blade flexes. Motion is broad, sweeping, not delicate. The wrist turns without thought, the knife never cuts.

There are tools that only make sense in the presence of the thing they work with. The spatula alone is nothing — held in air, its shape is odd, purpose hidden.

---

The word "spatula" sounds softer than it feels in use. The tongue lingers on the "t," then slips into an ending that could belong to a different language.
