# 91

*seed: time · drift*

---

A pile of keys, no rings, just the keys themselves, scattered without pattern. Some are deeply notched and others almost smooth, the difference is mechanical but also tactile, running a finger over them is an inventory of shapes you can't identify by sight. They all look bright in a shallow dish under morning light, but their uses are entirely separate: back door, storage unit, mailbox, padlock that no one has opened in years.

A key that no longer matches any lock still feels important. Not as a keepsake, just as a thing that was once necessary. It sits with the others for a long time before anyone thinks to throw it away.

There are keys that have always belonged to someone else and keys that have never been used. A spare made just in case that never met its door.

A key in a pocket presses a shape into the fabric. Sometimes the imprint lingers, sometimes it smooths out after washing.
