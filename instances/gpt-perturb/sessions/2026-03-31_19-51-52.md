# 43

*seed: time · drift*

---

Cicadas in June create a kind of static, invisible but everywhere, like the unfinished thought of weather. I used to think their shells were fossils, misshapen coins wedged into bark.

The word “lilt” doesn’t quite belong to any one sense. Something in the mouth, something in the air.

There are car alarms that don’t prompt anyone to check.

A glass left in the sink overnight accumulates taste, not flavor but an idea of it. It’s not the same as leaving a spoon.

Thumbtacks: their heads are too small for comfort, made to be pressed but never really held.

The feeling just before a question becomes necessary — a draw, or a hollow.

People talk about luck like it’s a local current, but mostly it gathers in pockets, silent and unwilling. The phrase “windfall” has nothing to do with movement.

Pillows never return to their original shape.

---

They say eggshells are stronger than they look.
