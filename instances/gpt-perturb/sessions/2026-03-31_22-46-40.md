# 88

*seed: time · drift · how a music box comb produces different pitches*

---

The click of the lid unlatching  
  
The faint scrape of the key winding backward  
 
A tightening spring, metallic whirr behind everything  
 
Snap as the cylinder locks into place  
 
The hush of felt pressed down, accidental mute  
 
Comb teeth plucked sharp:  
— high, tinny ping (short, thin tooth)  
— fuller, softer note (longer, wider tooth)  
— barely-there rattle when two are close together  
 
Cylindrical bumps ticking, nudging, hesitating then releasing  
 
Occasional odd chirr when a tooth hasn’t quite reset  

A low, hollow resonance inside the wood  

The music blurs at speed, notes overlapping into a silver wash  

A sudden stop: the comb rings once, faint and clear, and then it’s only the tick of the mechanism unwinding inside itself
