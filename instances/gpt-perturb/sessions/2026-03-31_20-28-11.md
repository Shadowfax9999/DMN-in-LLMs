# 59

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are numbers that look false. 47, 53, 82. When you see them on a sign, or as part of an address, you expect something off-kilter behind the door. Round numbers, even numbers, seem more trustworthy, even though nothing about a number dictates the type of life behind it. Room 101, the literary echo, but also just the first room. 404, the room that isn’t there.

Some surnames seem impossible outside of books. Pevensie. Twombly. Names that sound like the names for things on shelves in a place you haven’t visited. Names that seem borrowed from clouds or geography. The way a word can seem edible until you say it twice.

A time of day when the light is neither honey nor steel. There’s a word for the color you get on a Tuesday at 6:40, but it isn’t in circulation. Somewhere between the color of pencil graphite and an overripe pear.

What would it feel like to have a name that’s also an instruction. Hope, Chase, Grant, March. Does a name like that push you a little, every time someone calls you, toward some action you can’t quite enact?
