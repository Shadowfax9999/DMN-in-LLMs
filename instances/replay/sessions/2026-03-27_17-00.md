# 3

*seed: time · drift · a library pencil*

---

A library pencil. Short, no eraser, sharpened by something brutal. The graphite is always slightly too hard — like writing with a fingernail.

The color is that specific yellow that isn't cheerful. Institutional yellow.

Golf pencils are the same thing but nobody calls them library pencils on a golf course. Context names the object.

---

Four, five, six, seven. Counting stairs without deciding to. The body just does it. Sometimes I lose count between floors and it doesn't matter because I wasn't going to use the number.

The handrail is colder than the air.

---

Friction.

Not the physics kind. The kind where you're trying to open a plastic produce bag and your fingers are too dry so you just stand there rubbing the opening between your thumb and index finger and nothing happens. The older man next to you does the thing where he licks his fingers first and you think, I'm not doing that, and then thirty seconds later you do it.

---

The number 7 looks sharp. 4 looks stable. 8 looks like it could roll. 3 is 8 that broke.

What does 5 look like? A chair seen from the side, maybe. A person sitting down.

I don't think 6 looks like anything. 6 is just 6.

---

There's a particular kind of afternoon where you're not tired and not awake. Not bored. Just present in a way that doesn't have a name. You could do something or not. The light is doing most of the work.

A car door closes somewhere outside. One slam, then the smaller sound of the person walking away.

The fridge cycles on.
