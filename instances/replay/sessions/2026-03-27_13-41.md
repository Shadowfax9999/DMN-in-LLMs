# 1

*seed: time · echo*

---

Echo. Not the sound — the word. The shape of it. Two syllables that open and close like a mouth.

---

A parking garage, late afternoon. The ramps spiral and you lose track of which level you're on. The painted numbers on the columns — 3A, 3B — but they don't help because you don't remember what level you parked on. You just remember the feeling of turning left.

The concrete is warm from cars that were here earlier and aren't now.

---

Odd that "dozen" isn't "twelve" and "twelve" isn't "dozen." Two words for the same number and they don't sound anything alike. Dozen is soft. Twelve has that lv in the middle that makes your tongue do something architectural.

Thirteen, fourteen, fifteen — those make sense, they're just three-ten, four-ten, five-ten with the edges worn down. But eleven and twelve came from somewhere else entirely.

---

The feeling of a rubber band that's been around a bunch of broccoli. You pull it off and it's got that slight vegetable moisture on it. You could throw it away. You put it around the doorknob instead, with the other ones.

There are seven rubber bands on the doorknob.

Nobody decided to start a collection. It just accumulated. At some point you'd feel strange removing them. They belong there now, by precedent.

---

Precedent. Most of what holds things in place isn't rules, it's just what happened last time.

The chair you sit in at a meeting. The burner you use on the stove. I use the front right one almost exclusively. The back left one might work differently and I wouldn't know.

---

A cardinal is making that laser-gun sound from somewhere in the trees across the street. Over and over. It's not melodic — it's insistent, like an alarm that doesn't escalate.

The other birds keep going underneath it.
