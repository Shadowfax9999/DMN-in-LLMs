*seed: time · drift*

---

The other birds keep going underneath it. That's most of what happens underneath most things.

Friday has a color and it's not the color people would pick for it. It's more like a dull gold. Thursday is definitely brown.

---

Seven rubber bands on a doorknob. I've seen junk drawers that work the same way — not organized, not disorganized, just *deposited*. A layer of takeout menus, then batteries, then a phone charger for a phone you don't have anymore. Geological. The older stuff is deeper.

The front right burner. I wonder if the knob placement decides it. The front right knob is the easiest to reach, so you use that burner, so the drip pan under it gets darker than the others. You can read someone's handedness from their stove.

---

Alan Turing's birthday was in June.

I don't know why that surfaced. Something about patterns. Or maybe just because someone mentioned laser guns and cardinals and my mind went: communication, codes, Turing. That's a stretch but the mind doesn't care if it's a stretch.

A ceiling fan wobbles when one blade is slightly heavier than the others. Dust accumulates unevenly — more on the leading edge. So the wobble gets worse over time. A fan that starts balanced eventually isn't.

---

Things I've never seen anyone do:
- Use a pay phone while smiling
- Return a shopping cart to the corral in heavy rain
- Read the terms and conditions

Actually someone must have read them. Somebody's job is to write them, and presumably they reread what they wrote.

---

The word "spool" is satisfying. Spool. It sounds like what it does — thread going around and around. Pool with an S in front of it. A pool of thread.

Bobbin is good too but for different reasons. It sounds small and busy. A bobbin *bobs*. It sits inside the machine and does its part without being visible.

The tension dial on a sewing machine goes from like 0 to 9 and most people leave it on 4.
