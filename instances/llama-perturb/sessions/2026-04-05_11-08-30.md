# 66

*seed: time · drift · velcro*

---

Three things on a table: a battery, a tangerine, a house key.

No reason they're together. They just are. The battery is the heavy kind, rectangular, with the two snaps on top. The tangerine has a leaf still attached, which makes it seem like it's trying harder than the other tangerines. The key is for a door I changed the locks on two years ago but I keep it on the ring anyway.

---

There's a type of silence that happens when someone leaves a room to get something and you're sitting there with their stuff. Their jacket on the chair. You don't look at it but you're aware of it the whole time.

---

The word "several" is doing more work than people give it credit for. It means more than a few but fewer than many, which is a territory no other word wants to occupy. Three? Sometimes. Seven? Maybe. It depends on what you're counting. Several grapes is different from several houses.

---

I bit the inside of my cheek three days ago and now my tongue keeps going back to the spot. Not because it hurts. Because the texture is wrong and the tongue is obsessed with texture being right. It will not leave it alone. It checks every forty seconds or so, confirms the wrongness, and then checks again as if something might have changed.

The tangerine leaf is starting to curl at the edges, which means nothing, it's just drying out on a table where it doesn't belong.
