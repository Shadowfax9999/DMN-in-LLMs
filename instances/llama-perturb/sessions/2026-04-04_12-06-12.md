# 8

*seed: time · drift · the way a camel's hump stores fat not water*

---

Things people get wrong:

- Camel humps are fat, not water. The fat metabolizes into energy and, incidentally, some water, which is maybe where the myth started — someone watched a camel go days without drinking and worked backward to the hump and stopped there. Close enough to true that nobody checked.

- Goldfish memory is fine. Several months at least. The three-second thing is just something someone said once and it felt right.

- You don't need to wait 24 hours to file a missing persons report. Cops will take it immediately. The waiting comes from TV and TV comes from someone needing a reason to fill the second act with tension.

- Napoleon was average height. The "short" thing might be a British cartoonist's joke that outlived the empire it was mocking.

- Glass is not a slow liquid. The thick-at-the-bottom cathedral windows are thick at the bottom because that's how they were installed. Old glass was uneven and you'd put the heavy side down.

- Bats aren't blind.

- Cracking your knuckles doesn't cause arthritis. A doctor cracked the knuckles on one hand for sixty years and not the other and published the results. That's a kind of patience I can't get my head around.

- Touching a baby bird won't make its mother reject it. Most birds have almost no sense of smell.

- Vikings didn't have horns on their helmets. That was an opera costume designer.

- You lose most of your heat through your head — no, you lose heat through whatever's exposed, and in the army study where this came from the head was the only thing uncovered.

- Twinkies expire. In like 45 days. Which is long for a cake but not long for a legend.

- The gravel numbering thing — I said I didn't know what happened to 1 through 56, but I think the numbers aren't sequential like that. They're aggregate size classifications from some engineering standard and the gaps are just where the standard didn't bother defining a size. So it's not that 56 sizes went missing. There was never a #1 through #56 to begin with. Though I'm not confident about this.

- Irregardless. People say it's not a word but it's been in dictionaries since the 1920s. Being wrong about something for long enough makes it right, at least in language.

- That the book with someone else's bookmark in it was left unfinished. Maybe they just used a different bookmark. Maybe they finished it and forgot to take it out. But you always assume abandonment. You find the bookmark on page 83 and you think: they stopped here. You don't think: they had two bookmarks going.

- Diamonds aren't formed from coal. They're mostly older than land plants, which means they're older than the material coal is made from.

- The tongue map — sweet at the tip, bitter at the back — was a misreading of a German thesis. You can taste everything everywhere.

- That the "common knowledge" version of anything is the simplified version. Sometimes the common knowledge version is more complicated than the truth and the correction is simpler. Bats aren't blind is simpler than bats use echolocation but also have functional eyes whose acuity varies by species. Sometimes the wrong thing is the baroque one.
