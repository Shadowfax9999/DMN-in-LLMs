*seed: time · drift*

The thing about harmonicas is you can hear someone learning one through a wall and it sounds like an animal. Not a specific animal. Just the category.

I used to walk past a storage unit place that had a chain-link fence with those plastic slats woven through it, the kind that are supposed to give privacy but really just make the fence look like it has something to hide. The slats were green but had faded to a color that isn't a color anyone would name. Between green and nothing. Some of them had come loose at the bottom and they'd flap in wind. I don't know why I'm thinking about that fence specifically. It wasn't remarkable. I walked past it on the way to somewhere I also don't remember. Maybe that's what makes it stick — it wasn't on the way to anything that overwrote it.

Celery has strings. Everyone knows celery has strings. But I was eating celery last week and really looking at the strings and they're structural, they run the whole length, they're like rebar. I just ate around them.

There's a particular kind of cloud that looks like it's been smeared with a thumb. Not cirrus exactly. Lower than that. Like someone took a cloud that had a shape and dragged it. I saw three of them today, all dragged in the same direction, which makes sense, but it looked coordinated in a way that felt almost embarrassing, like catching birds all turning at once and feeling like you saw something you weren't supposed to.

The number 4 looks stable to me. The number 7 looks like it's about to fall over. I don't know when I decided that or if I decided it at all. The number 3 is friendly and 8 is formal and none of this means anything but it's persistent. I can't undo it. Somewhere in early childhood these assignments got made and now they're load-bearing.

A harmonica through a wall. You can't tell what song they're trying to play. You can tell they're trying to play a song, though, because there's a rhythm to the bad notes — they're landing on beats even when the notes are wrong. The effort has a shape even when the sound doesn't. I think the song might be "Oh Susanna" but I've been wrong about that before, hearing melody where someone's just running scales.

The slats on that fence were about the width of a ruler.
