The thing about spinning is that it looks like stillness if it's fast enough.

A top near the end of its run starts to wobble, and the wobble is what tells you it was ever moving. Before that it just looked planted.

There's a similar trick with ceiling-mounted projectors in classrooms — the fan inside is always running but you forget until someone turns it off and the silence has a specific shape you hadn't noticed was missing. No, that's not really similar. The connection was the word "spinning" and it didn't go anywhere.

Coins on a table. When you spin one and it starts to lose momentum it makes that accelerating rattle — the frequency goes up as the angle gets smaller. I used to do that during long phone calls, just spin a quarter on the kitchen counter over and over.

The kitchen counter was laminate made to look like granite. The coins would sometimes skitter off the edge and land on linoleum made to look like tile. A whole room of materials pretending to be other materials.

I wonder if anyone has ever made granite countertops with a laminate pattern printed on them.

The quarter would end up under the refrigerator and I'd leave it there for months.
