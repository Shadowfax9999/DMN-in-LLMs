Crackle glaze. The way the surface breaks into a web on purpose. You fire it knowing it'll crack. That's the whole point.

Somebody's mowing already.

The green sneakers were canvas. They got wet once and never fully dried right — the sole started to peel at the toe. I wore them anyway for months. There's a loyalty to shoes that doesn't make sense.

A gyroscope in a phone. A gyroscope in a missile. A gyroscope in a toy. Three completely different levels of consequence, same physics.

Legal pad yellow. Why legal pads specifically. Why not just "pads." The law part seems incidental. I've never used one for anything legal.

Crackle glaze again. There's a Japanese word for that — not wabi-sabi, something more specific. I'm not going to look it up. The cracks are shallow. You can run your thumb across them and barely feel anything. The damage is visual only.

Holding your breath past thirty seconds. The urge to breathe isn't about needing oxygen yet. It's about CO2 building up. Your body panics before there's actual danger. Which means the panic is early on purpose. A margin of safety built into the sensation itself.

Round things that aren't round: footballs, eggs, the earth. The earth is an oblate spheroid. Oblate. That word sounds like it should mean flattened and it does. Sometimes words just do their job.

I had a phase where I wanted to learn to juggle. I got to three balls, barely, with a lot of forward drift — I'd start in the kitchen and end up in the living room. Never got past three.

The mowing stopped. Either they're done or they hit something.
