# 90

*seed: time · drift · grain*

---

The direction wood wants to split is already in it before you touch it.

Sandpaper has numbers and the numbers go the wrong way — higher means finer, smoother, less there. 60-grit feels like a cat's tongue. 2000-grit feels like nothing at all, like rubbing paper on paper, which is basically what you're doing.

A piano key that sticks. Not broken, just slower to come back up than the others. You compensate without deciding to. The left hand knows things the right hand would have to be taught.

Venetian blinds halfway up look like a city has been lifted into the window. Halfway down they just look broken.

The heel of a loaf of bread. Some people fight over it and some people leave it in the bag until the bag goes stale around it. Two slices of bread left and they're both heels and suddenly you don't want a sandwich anymore.

Geese flying in a V but one arm of the V is always longer than the other. I don't know why that bothers me slightly every time.

A pen that works on scrap paper but not on the thing you actually need to sign. You scribble harder on the scrap paper to prove it works and it does and then you go back to the form and it skips again.

There's a particular kind of afternoon where you're not tired but you're not awake either. You're just present in a room. The light is doing what it does. You could get up but there's no specific reason to get up so you don't, and that's not laziness, it's just gravity being slightly more persuasive than anything else.

The ice maker drops ice and it sounds like someone's in the kitchen.
