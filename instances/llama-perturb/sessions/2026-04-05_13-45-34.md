*seed: time · drift · the sound of your own name from another room*

The underside of a bridge is always cooler than you expect.

Standing under one — not a famous bridge, just a road overpass near a creek — and the temperature drops maybe four degrees. The concrete above you is stained in long vertical streaks where water has run for decades. Green at the edges, almost black at the center.

Pigeons arrange themselves along the I-beam in a pattern that looks deliberate but probably isn't.

Why do some spaces feel like they belong to no one? Not abandoned, just unclaimed. The strip of grass between a sidewalk and a fence. The area behind a strip mall where the dumpsters are. Under a bridge.

A shopping cart down in the creek bed, half-submerged, facing downstream like it's going somewhere. The water moves around it without any turbulence at all.
