The plural of "moose" being "moose" has always struck me as a kind of victory.

Elk, though. Elk also does this. Deer. Sheep. Fish, sometimes. There's a pattern — animals we used to hunt in groups. You wouldn't need to distinguish one from many if you were looking at a whole field of them. That might be wrong. I don't care enough to check.

Things that don't have satisfying plurals:
- Cactus
- Octopus
- Syllabus
- Fungus
- The word "apparatus"

All Latin or Greek. All ending in -us. All generating arguments at dinner tables where nobody is qualified.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs but not up. It's been doing this for maybe three years. I keep meaning to ask about it and then the appointment ends and I've forgotten. The click isn't painful. It's more like a notification.

Someone is grilling outside somewhere. You can't localize the smell of charcoal — it seems to come from every direction at once, or from above, like it fell. Burgers, probably. Could be chicken. There's a version of this where I try to explain how volatile compounds disperse in low-wind conditions but I'm just going to sit here and smell it.

A friend's kid once asked me if animals know their own names. I said dogs do. She said no, she meant their *species* name. Like does a cat know it's a cat. I didn't have an answer. I still don't. There's something in there about categories — whether a robin groups itself with other robins or just with everything that moves at robin-speed, robin-height.

The ceiling fan is on the setting where you can't feel it but you can hear it. One of the pull chains is shorter than the other because a link broke off at some point and nobody replaced it.
