The weight thing with library books might just be that you hold them differently. You don't fold a library book open and leave it face-down on the arm of a couch. You keep it more upright. More formal. The way you sit up straighter in a chair that isn't yours.

Actually no. It's that a library book has all its previous readers in it somehow. Not literally. But you open it and there's a crease you didn't make and a faint pencil mark on page 40 that someone mostly erased and the spine bends in a way that suggests someone else's hands. Your own copy is just yours. A library book is a commons.

A commons weighs more than a private holding. I think that's true of most things.
