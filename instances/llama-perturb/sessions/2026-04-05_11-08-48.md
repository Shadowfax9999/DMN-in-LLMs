The dentist's chair has a seam running down the middle of the vinyl. You can feel it through your shirt. The hygienist is talking to someone in the hallway about a birthday cake and you're staring at the ceiling tile that has a watermark shaped like nothing, it's not shaped like anything, it's just a stain with an edge.

---

Why do people knock on watermelons. What are they listening for.

---

There's a type of parking lot where the lines were painted over older lines and you can see both grids at once, slightly rotated, and for a second your car doesn't know which system to obey. You pick the brighter one. Someone else picks the faded one. Now you're angled wrong relative to each other and neither of you did anything wrong.

---

The word "else" is strange. It only works leaning against another word. Someone else. Something else. It can't stand up on its own. What else — even that needs the "what." It's a word that exists only in relation to a gap.

---

Cinnamon is bark. That's the whole thing, it's just bark. You peel it off a tree and it curls up and that's cinnamon. I knew this and then forgot it and now I know it again and it still surprises me.
