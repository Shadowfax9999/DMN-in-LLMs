# 64

*seed: time · drift · why vinyl records are cut as spirals not concentric circles*

---

Tape roll, almost spent. Light now.

You can feel the cardboard through it. The weight told you how much. Now the weight tells you: almost. A thing becoming its container.

Grooves do this too. A record's spiral tightens inward. Not circles. One long diminishing line. The needle rides it like falling. Slowly, toward the label. The song gets closer to cardboard.

Wait — why spiral though. Circles could hold the same music. Each ring, one rotation of sound. But then the needle would jump. Gap between ring three and four. Silence where the groove isn't. The spiral removes the decision. There's no next track to find. Just the continuous falling inward.

That's what almost-empty tape feels like. The cardboard approaching from inside. You're already touching the end. You just haven't run out yet.

A spiral is a circle forgetting. Each pass slightly less than before. My mother wound yarn this way. The ball got lighter as she knit. She'd toss it, test the weight. Same gesture every few rows. Toward the end: practically hollow.

Some things empty from the center. Some things empty from the outside. Tape empties from the outside in. Records empty from the outside in. Yarn empties from the outside in. Is that always how spirals go.

I think spool thread goes the other way.
