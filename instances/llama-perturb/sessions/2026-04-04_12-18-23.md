# 13

*seed: time · drift · bollard*

---

Bollard. Those stumpy little posts outside storefronts. I always want to call them bollocks by accident, which is maybe why the word sticks — it's got that same blunt Anglo-Saxon mouth-feel, the way you have to close your lips twice.

There's a bollard outside the post office near where I grew up that's been hit so many times it leans about fifteen degrees. Nobody fixes it. It just leans there, still doing its job. Still keeping cars out of the wall. There's something about an object that keeps working at an angle.

Folders in a filing cabinet do that too. They lean and lean and eventually everything's at a slant and you can still find what you need.

I was thinking about how "font" used to mean a basin for baptismal water and now it means Helvetica. The container became the content became the style of the content. That's three moves in maybe six hundred years. I wonder what "app" will mean in 2600. Probably something physical again. Things seem to do that — go from concrete to abstract and then back to concrete, like a ball bouncing between floors.

The air smells like cut grass and something floral I can't identify. Not lilac. Something less sweet. Could be Bradford pear, which smells like — well, it's famous for smelling bad, but from a distance it's more complicated than that.

A cardinal just landed on the gutter. Sat there. Left. The whole visit was maybe two seconds. I don't know what it wanted from the gutter. Maybe nothing. Maybe it landed wrong and corrected. Birds must have a version of walking into a room and forgetting why you came in.

The leaning bollard would make a good band name. The Leaning Bollards. They'd play pub rock, obviously. Bass-heavy. The bassist would be the one who named the band and nobody else would know what a bollard was but they'd all pretend they did.

My left knee has that thing where it clicks when I straighten it after sitting too long. Not painful, just present. A mechanical announcement. I wonder if knees click more in cold weather or if that's just something people say.

The cardinal's back, or a different one.
