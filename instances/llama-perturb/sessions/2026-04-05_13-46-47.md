# 84

*seed: time · drift · fermentation in Korean onggi pottery*

---

The earliest clocks weren't trying to measure anything — they were just dripping, one container into another, and someone noticed the dripping was regular enough to trust, which is a strange kind of trust, the trust that a thing will keep doing what it's doing, that a rate will hold, and I think about that when I think about fermentation, the way a jar of cabbage crosses over from raw to preserved and there's no clean line where it happens, the bacteria are working the whole time but at some point you open the lid and it smells like something you'd eat instead of something you'd throw away, and in Korea the onggi pots breathe — the clay is porous enough to let air through in microscopic amounts, so the whole vessel is a membrane, not a wall, and the fermentation inside is managed by the architecture of the container itself, which means someone figured out how to make a pot that participates, and I don't know when they figured it out or whether it was deliberate or just the local clay doing what it did and people noticing the kimchi tasted better in these pots than in those ones, noticing before understanding, which is most of how knowledge works if you're honest about it, the understanding comes years or centuries later and sometimes not at all, you just know which pot and which hillside and which week to start, and the knowledge lives in the practice and never quite makes it into language, my mother could tell when bread dough was ready by how it felt and she could not have written that down in any way that would let me reproduce it, the hand knew something the sentence couldn't carry, and maybe that's fine, maybe not everything needs to be a sentence, but here I am making sentences about it, which is its own kind of problem, like trying to fold water, and the onggi pot doesn't explain itself either, it just sits there with its microscopic pores doing exactly the amount of nothing that turns out to be the right amount of something, and I find that genuinely moving even though a pot is not trying to move anyone, it's clay with holes in it, it was fired at a certain temperature and theiteiteite — I almost typed "it was designed" but I don't think it was designed so much as iterated, generation after generation of potters adjusting without necessarily having a theory, the pot is a theory that never needed to become one, and somewhere outside right now there's a bird I can't identify making a sound I'd recognize if I heard it in a recording but couldn't reproduce with my mouth, and that seems related but I'm not sure how.
