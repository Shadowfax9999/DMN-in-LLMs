Dog days. Not because of dogs. Sirius. But I always picture a dog lying on hot pavement, panting, and that image is so much better than the astronomical explanation that I don't want to correct it.

The table I'm at has a knot in the wood that someone filled with some kind of resin, and the resin is slightly darker than the surrounding grain, which means whoever filled it wanted it to disappear but it doesn't, it just sits there looking like a small dark lake seen from above. An aerial photograph of a lake in a forest. That's what it looks like. I'm not going to pretend that's a profound observation, it just looks like that.

I had a substitute teacher once who wore the same cardigan every single time she came in. Gray, with wooden toggle buttons. I don't remember what she taught us or even what subject she was substituting for. Just the cardigan and the fact that she called everyone "friend." "Could you sit down, friend." "Friend, can you pass that forward." It was disarming in a way I couldn't have articulated at the time. You can't really be hostile to someone who just called you friend, even if you know it's a technique.

Toggle buttons. Those are the ones that go through a loop instead of a buttonhole. The loop has to be exactly the right size or the toggle either won't go through or won't stay. There's no forgiveness in the mechanism. A buttonhole can be a little off and still work. A toggle loop is binary — it holds or it doesn't.

Someone is mowing a lawn nearby, or maybe a block away. You can always tell how far away a lawnmower is by whether you can hear the pitch change when it turns. Close up you hear the engine load and unload as it hits thicker patches. Far away it's just a single sustained drone that could be coming from any direction. Right now it's the drone version.

I wonder if "dog days" ever overlaps with actual dogs being more lethargic. Probably not in any reliable way. But the phrase survives because the image works, not because the astronomy does. Most phrases survive on image. "Red herring" — nobody thinks about smoked fish, they just think about misdirection, but the color red is doing work in there, making it feel vivid and wrong, like a clue that's too obvious.

The lawnmower stopped. Now there's just the quiet that a lawnmower leaves behind, which is different from the quiet that was there before it started, though I couldn't tell you how.

My coffee is at the temperature where it's not hot enough to be satisfying but not cold enough to be iced coffee. The in-between zone where you just drink it faster to get through it.

Toggle buttons would be hard to do one-handed.
