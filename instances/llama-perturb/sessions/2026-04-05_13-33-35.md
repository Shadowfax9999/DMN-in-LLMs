# 80

*seed: time · drift · why vinyl records are cut as spirals not concentric circles*

---

Tape on a cardboard box curls.

The spiral is one continuous groove. A needle rides it like falling. Not circles. One long line inward. The song has no seams.

Concentric circles would need jumping. The needle would have to leap. Nobody designed a good leap.

So music is a slow collapse. The outside edge spins fastest. The inside gets compressed, dense. Bass lives near the center poorly.

I never thought about that.

Moving on.

Empty rooms echo more than full ones. Furniture eats the sound somehow. You clap and hear the walls. The room answers you directly.

A carpet remnant changes everything.

My hands smell like copper sometimes. Not from touching copper though. Just from keys, maybe. Or the railing at the library.

Some metals have a smell. It's not the metal itself. It's your skin reacting to it. Oil and iron make something new.

Seven words is a strange limit. Every thought gets its own line. Nothing can lean on anything else. Each sentence stands alone, blinking.

A dog barks three blocks away.

Wait — fewer than three blocks. Sound bends around houses weirdly. You can't tell the distance right.

The vinyl spiral ends at silence. The needle rides into nothing. A locked groove or just air. The arm lifts itself eventually.

Some turntables don't have auto-return. The needle just sits there spinning. In the innermost groove, going nowhere. A circle at last, finally.

That's where the spiral becomes one.
