the outside edge because of how you pronate, the little stones wedged into the tread that you picked up three parking lots ago and will carry into your kitchen tonight, small migrations of gravel the window of every coffee shop who is writing something in a notebook and she's been there since before notebooks were for writing in public and she'll be there after, and I don't know what she's writing but her handwriting tilts hard to the right like she's trying to outrun the margin, and I've never once seen one of these women turn back a page to read what they wrote earlier, it's all forward, all deposition, like testimony — the metal ring that reinforces a hole, that's what that word is, you see them on shower curtains and tarps and the thing is they exist to protect a hole, to
