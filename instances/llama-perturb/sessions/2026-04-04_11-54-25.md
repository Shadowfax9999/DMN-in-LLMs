# 2

*seed: time · drift · the smell of a book you haven't opened in years*

---

Slightly sweet, slightly like a closet. Not the words in it but the paper itself having a smell that is also a season — always fall, for some reason, even if you last read it in July.

Glue deteriorates. That's part of it.

What's the name for the fear of long words. Hippopotomonstrosesquippedaliophobia. Someone was having fun.

A washing machine on spin cycle sounds different when it's unbalanced. You can hear it walking across the laundry room floor. Not literally but almost literally. My old apartment had one that migrated about two inches per load and you'd have to shove it back every few weeks.

Socks vanish. Everyone says this. It's become such a universal complaint that it almost doesn't register as a real phenomenon anymore, but it is — I have genuinely lost socks. Where. The drum has a gap near the door seal. That's apparently the answer. They're inside the machine, between the inner and outer drums, which is a disgusting thing to think about. A graveyard of socks you forgot you owned.

The word "almost" does a lot of work. "I almost called you." "We almost made it." "It almost rained." Each one is a tiny fiction about a world where the thing happened, delivered as a report from the world where it didn't.

Ants on a sidewalk. One going the wrong way. All the others going the right way. Who decided.

I had a teacher who could draw a perfect circle on a chalkboard. Every time. Freehand. We were more impressed by that than by anything she taught us, which is probably not what she was going for but also maybe it is. The circle thing is what I remember twenty years later. Not the subject she taught. I think it was history.
