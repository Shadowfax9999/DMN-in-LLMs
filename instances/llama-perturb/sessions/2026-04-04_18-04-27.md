# 46

*seed: time · drift · the feeling of stepping off a boat onto solid ground*

---

1. The inside of a stapler is more violent than you'd think. That spring is really compressed.
2. I keep almost remembering the name of a street I lived on for two years. It had a tree in it. Elm? No. It wasn't Elm.
3. Carbonation hurts. Nobody talks about this. The first sip of something carbonated is a small pain and then you just keep drinking.
4. There's a type of bolt that has no head. Just threads all the way up. What holds it.
5. The sound of a basketball on asphalt from far away could be rain could be applause could be someone running up stairs.
6. Maple.
7. No, not Maple either.
8. When you hold a coffee mug with both hands you're not cold, you just want to hold something with both hands.
9. I think the word "caulk" looks wrong no matter how you spell it.
10. Walnut. Walnut Street. That was it. The tree was not a walnut tree though, it was one of those ornamental pears that smells terrible in April and everyone on the block just pretends it doesn't.
11. The number of times I've opened a refrigerator, looked at everything in it, closed it, and then opened it again eleven seconds later.
12. A house key works by pushing little pins to exactly the right height. If even one pin is wrong, nothing moves. Which means every time a key works, five or six things went right simultaneously and you didn't notice any of them.
13. There's a stretch of highway where the road surface changes from asphalt to concrete and back again every few hundred feet and you can hear it in the tires, a rhythm that almost becomes music if the intervals were more regular.
14. The way a screen door doesn't slam if you hold it but then slams the one time you forget.
15. Linden. I'm now thinking it was Linden Street. But I already committed to Walnut and I think Walnut was right.
16. Pencils don't have lead in them, everyone knows this, but I still think "lead" every time I see the gray mark.
17. A rubber band in a drawer for long enough just becomes a sticky flat thing that leaves a mark on whatever it was touching.
18. The satisfying part of peeling an orange is when the whole peel comes off in fewer than four pieces.
19. I wonder if the person who designed the first paper clip thought it was temporary.
20. Walnut Street had a laundromat on the corner that was open twenty-four hours but I never saw anyone in it after midnight, just the machines running with the lights on, like a demonstration of something.
