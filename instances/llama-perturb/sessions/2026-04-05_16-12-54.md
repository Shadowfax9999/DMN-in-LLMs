# 94

*seed: time · drift · freeze tag*

---

The back of my hand has a freckle I don't remember getting. Obviously nobody remembers getting a freckle but it still feels like it appeared one day, fully formed, like it had always been there and I just started noticing.

Freeze tag. The person who was "it" could freeze you with a touch and you'd have to stand still with your legs apart so someone could crawl through them to unfreeze you. The crawling-through was the whole point. The game was engineered so that kids would have to help each other but it didn't feel like that, it felt like a prison break.

There's a type of lock where you push the button on the inside of the doorknob and then close the door behind you. Every time I do it I think about whether I actually pushed it. By the time I'm at the car it's too late to check without it being a whole thing.

Condensation on the outside of a glass in summer moves in these slow paths, merging with other paths, and the glass ends up sitting in a ring of water on the table. The ring is always a perfect circle even though the paths down the side of the glass were random.

I used to think the word "Illinois" had a silent S at the end because someone was being fancy. I never looked into whether that's true.

A friend's apartment where the bathroom light switch was in the hallway outside the bathroom. You'd be sitting in there and someone would flip it off walking past and you'd just be in the dark.

The way a shopping bag handles will sometimes twist around each other on the walk home and by the time you get to the door you're carrying one bag by a single twisted rope of plastic that's cutting into your fingers.

My left shoe comes untied more than my right shoe.
