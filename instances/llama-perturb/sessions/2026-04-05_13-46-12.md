# 83

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way a spoon looks bent in a glass of water. You learn why in school and it never stops looking bent.

Some corrections don't take. You know the sun isn't moving but you say it's setting. You know the floor isn't cold — your feet are warm and the tile is conducting heat away — but the floor is cold. The knowledge sits in one place and the experience sits in another and they don't talk.

A paper map folded wrong will never go back to the way it was. You can get it flat but the creases fight you. It remembers a shape it no longer has.

There's a particular frustration to pulling a fitted sheet out of a dryer and trying to fold it. Everyone has a method and nobody's method works. You end up with a soft rectangle that's thicker on one side.

The dryer itself is still warm inside for a long time after it stops.
