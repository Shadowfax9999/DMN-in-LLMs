# 18

*seed: time · drift · capture the flag*

---

Capture the flag. I haven't thought about that in years. The jail was always a weird part — you'd just stand there touching a tree, waiting. You weren't playing anymore but you were still in the game. Some kid would eventually come sprinting to tag you free and half the time you didn't even want to be freed because standing at the tree was peaceful.

The way a rubber band feels different when it's been stretched out too long. Slack but not relaxed. It remembers.

I'm sitting on a floor right now, mentally. Not a real floor. A gymnasium floor, the kind with the polyurethane shine and the overlapping court lines — basketball in black, volleyball in blue, badminton in yellow, and you never knew which lines were yours. Someone always playing the wrong game's boundaries. I wonder if there's a word for lines that are real but not relevant. Like how a map of Roman roads still underlies half the streets in England but nobody's navigating by it.

Badminton is a strange sport to watch from above. The shuttlecock moves so fast off the racket and then just — gives up. Decelerates like nothing else in athletics. It's the only projectile in sports that's designed to fail at traveling.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs. Not up. Only down. It's been doing this for maybe three years and I've never once mentioned it to anyone. There are these small facts about your body that exist in a private register, things only you would know to report. The specific eye that waters first in wind. Which ear you hold the phone to.

The phone thing is interesting because people are fierce about it. Try holding your phone to the other ear sometime. Everything sounds wrong, like the words are arriving from the wrong direction.

I used to think "southpaw" was about cat paws somehow. It's from baseball. I think it's about which direction the pitcher faces relative to the diamond, and therefore the sun, and therefore south. Or maybe that's a folk etymology. I don't actually know.

A sprinkler just started somewhere nearby, the tch-tch-tch-tch kind that rotates, and each pass it catches the light for about half a second before swinging past. Nobody is outside. The sprinkler is performing for nobody. Most machines spend most of their operational lives unobserved. The refrigerator compressor at 3 AM. Traffic lights cycling through all three colors on an empty rural highway at four in the morning, green yellow red green yellow red, for no one.

My grandmother had a clock that chimed on the quarter hour and I remember being unable to sleep at her house the first night because of it and then by the third night not hearing it at all, and then going home and the silence in my own room feeling aggressive, like something had been taken out of it.

The sprinkler is still going. Tch-tch-tch-tch-tch-tch and then the longer shhhhhhh of the return sweep.
