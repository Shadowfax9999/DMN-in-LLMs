*seed: time · drift · sluice*

Sluice is a word that sounds like what it does, the way the vowel slides through the consonants, and I'm not thinking about that, I'm thinking about the gate itself, the flat board that drops into grooves cut in stone to hold back a millpond, and how the water on one side is calm and the water on the other side is calm and the only violence is in the narrow slot where the board doesn't quite reach the bottom — but actually I'm thinking about something else, which is the sound of a screen door on a spring, not the slam but the three or four seconds before the slam where the pneumatic closer is doing its work and the door is moving at a speed no human would choose, too slow to be closing and too fast to be open, and the person who just walked through is already in the kitchen and the door is still in this intermediate state, belonging to neither side — there's a word for the thirteenth floor that doesn't exist in hotels, or rather there's a number that doesn't have a floor, the elevator buttons go 11, 12, 14, and the building is exactly as tall as it would have been, the concrete doesn't care what you call it, the people on 14 are standing where 13 would be and some of them know this and none of them care because the naming is the thing, not the location — I saw a pay phone last week, still mounted to the wall outside a gas station, the receiver was there but the cord was that steel-braided kind and it had been cut, not torn, cut clean with something sharp, and I couldn't figure out who would bother, what the motivation would be for taking wire cutters to a phone that probably hadn't worked in years, unless the cutting happened years ago too and I was just seeing the result on the same delay that I see everything, which is to say after the reason has left and only the shape remains — the gas station had two prices on the sign, one for cash and one for credit, and the cash price was nine cents cheaper, which means someone did the math on what it costs them to process a card and decided the number was nine cents per gallon and I believe them but I also don't understand how you get to nine, why not eight or ten, what level of spreadsheet precision produces nine — my left thumbnail has a ridge in it that's been growing out for about two months, I can feel it with my right thumb, a horizontal line like a seam in the nail, and I know it means something interrupted the growth at the matrix, some mild stress or illness I've already forgotten, so the nail is a record of something my memory discarded, which means my body is keeping notes my mind threw away, and in another month the ridge will reach the free edge and I'll clip it off and the record will be gone from both places — the gas station also had one of those air pumps where you put in quarters, four quarters for three minutes of air, and the hose was coiled on a hook and the chuck at the end was brass and slightly green with oxidation and I thought about how the brass was once bright and someone chose brass because it doesn't spark, you don't want sparks near a pressurized fitting, so the green is a side effect of safety — I keep almost thinking about rivers but not quite getting there, the thought is downstream of sluice but I keep ending up at gas stations and fingernails and screen doors instead, which is maybe how association actually works, you don't follow the obvious path, you follow the path that's open, and the obvious one is blocked by something you can't see — the ridge in my nail is about a third of the way from the cuticle now, moving at roughly three millimeters a month, which means whatever happened happened in late January or early February, and I genuinely cannot remember anything from that
