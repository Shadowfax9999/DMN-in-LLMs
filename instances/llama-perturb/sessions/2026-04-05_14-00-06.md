# 89

*seed: time · drift · double reed*

---

The back of a spoon is a face if you hold it right but nobody's face in particular.

Oboe players at orchestra rehearsals always look slightly annoyed, even during rests. Bassoon players look like they're solving something. The difference might just be the angle the instrument forces on your neck.

There's a specific kind of parking lot — not a garage, not a strip mall, the kind behind a building that wasn't supposed to have one. Cracked asphalt with dandelions in the seams. A dumpster with the lid propped open by a cardboard box that's too tall. One car parked diagonally across two spaces because there were no lines to begin with.

Dandelion stems are hollow and leak white when you snap them.

I can never remember whether nutmeg is a seed or a bark. I think it's a seed. The inside of it has that marbled pattern, like a cross-section of something geological. Mace is the outer part, the lattice around it, which seems like the kind of fact that can't be right but is.

The word "lattice" does a lot of work. Pie crust. Crystal structure. Garden trellis. The criss-cross pattern on a Waffle House waffle. None of those things are actually related to each other except through the word.

A waffle iron in a drawer is heavier than you expect every single time you reach for it.

There's a particular frustration of trying to fit a lid back on a container when you can't tell which corner matches which corner. Rectangular lids that are almost square. You try it one way, it doesn't snap, you rotate ninety degrees, it doesn't snap, and by the third attempt you're not sure if you've already tried this orientation.

The number of times I've stood in front of an open cabinet just looking at the shelves without reaching for anything.
