# 93

*seed: time · drift*

---

The tag on a mattress that says do not remove — I haven't thought about that in years but it just appeared. Whole generation of kids afraid of mattress police.

Somewhere there's a filing cabinet with my dental X-rays from 2011 in it. Or there isn't. Maybe they shredded them. Maybe the dentist retired and the whole office became a sandwich shop and the files went into a dumpster. My teeth from 2011 are still here but the pictures of them might not be.

A sandal on the highway median. Always one. Never the pair.

I was trying to remember what the inside of my elementary school cafeteria smelled like and I got close — like, the thought of it was in the next room — but it wouldn't come all the way through. There's a version of almost-remembering that's more vivid than remembering. You can feel the shape of the thing you can't reach.

Tongs. You always click tongs twice before you use them. Everyone does this. No one decided to.

The sound of a screen door is not the same as the sound of a screen door in a movie. In a movie it's too clean. It latches. A real one bounces against the frame two or three times, each bounce shorter than the last, and then it doesn't quite close and you hear someone inside say *the door*.

My left shoelace comes untied more than my right one and I have never figured out if that's about the shoe or the foot or the way I tie it.

There's a plant at the office that no one waters on purpose but it's alive. Someone must water it. Or it gets enough from humidity, or from people dumping the last inch of their water bottles into it as they walk past, which I've done maybe three times without thinking about whether anyone else also does that.

The pencil sharpener bolted to the wall in a classroom — the handle you crank — there was always a kid who sharpened their pencil for way too long just to not be at their desk. The teacher knew. The kid knew the teacher knew. But the sharpener was loud enough to make the confrontation not worth it, so both of them just waited.

I can picture the layout of a grocery store I haven't been to in six years better than I can picture my own living room right now.
