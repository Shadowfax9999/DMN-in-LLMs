# 47

*seed: time · drift*

---

The inside of a walnut looks like a brain and I've never been able to eat one without thinking about it. Not in a way that stops me. Just in a way that's there.

Corduroy. The word sounds exactly like the fabric feels.

There's a type of cloud that looks like a washboard road and I can never remember what it's called. Altocumulus, maybe. Or the other one. The one that means rain is coming but not yet.

Socks disappear in the dryer and nobody has ever truly explained this. The lint trap is not a sufficient answer.

I once tried to whistle with a blade of grass between my thumbs and it worked exactly once and then never again no matter how many times I tried the same grass the same thumbs the same mouth.

The floor of a movie theater is sticky in a way that has its own specific sound.

Most people hold their phone with their pinky underneath it like a shelf and I don't think anyone taught anyone else to do this.

When you microwave something with a paper towel over it, the paper towel gets damp, and the dampness is the food's moisture leaving. You're watching the food get worse in real time.

The part of the scissors you never think about is the screw in the middle.

There's a particular kind of afternoon where the light looks like it's coming from nowhere — not overcast exactly, more like the sky itself is the light source, which I guess it always is, but usually you can point at where.

A stapler is just a mouth.

I can never remember whether it's "effect" or "affect" and at this point I've looked it up enough times that the looking-up is what I remember, not the answer.

The sound a refrigerator makes when it stops is louder than the sound it makes when it's running.

Keys on a ring always orient themselves so the one you need is the last one you try. This isn't true but it is.

There's a smell that old paperback books have that new ones don't and I think it's the glue in the binding breaking down but I'm not sure and I don't want to check.

The number 7 looks sharp and the number 8 looks soft and I don't know if everyone agrees about this or if it's just the shapes.

Somewhere right now a traffic light is cycling through its colors at an intersection where no cars are coming from any direction.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs but not up, which seems like it should mean something diagnostic but probably just means I'm getting older in one specific direction.

The cardboard tube inside a roll of tape is always slightly too small to fit on your wrist but you try anyway.

I think ceiling fans spin counterclockwise in summer and clockwise in winter or maybe the other way around, there's a switch on the housing that nobody ever flips.

The satisfying part of clicking a ballpoint pen is not the click but the tiny resistance right before it.
