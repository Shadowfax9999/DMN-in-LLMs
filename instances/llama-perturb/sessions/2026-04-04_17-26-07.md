The thing about a fitted sheet is that it has a right way and a wrong way and you can never tell which is which until you've already committed to a corner. Then you're standing there with elastic bunched in your hands and the diagonal is wrong and you have to rotate the whole thing ninety degrees while it's half on the mattress. Nobody teaches you this. You just do it wrong six hundred times and then you develop a system involving the tag, and the tag is always in a different place depending on the brand.

I used to think cursive was going to matter. They spent a lot of time on it. The capital Q that looks like a 2. The capital Z that looks like nothing in the natural world. Where did those shapes come from. Who sat down and decided that a cursive Z should have that little shelf on it.

A dog sleeping on its side breathes differently than a dog sleeping curled up. Slower, deeper, with sometimes a twitch in the back legs that people say means dreaming but might mean anything. The ribcage rises and falls and you can see the individual ribs if the dog is short-haired and the light is right.

There's a particular kind of cloud that sits flat on the bottom like it was set on a table. All the turbulence is on top, all the architecture, but the bottom is ruled-line straight. You see a whole row of them sometimes and they're all flat at the same altitude like they agreed on it.

The number of things I've started reading and stopped reading. Not because they were bad but because something else appeared. A notification, a sound from another room, a thought about whether I locked the door. The book is still open on the arm of the chair with the pages slowly fanning closed under their own weight. By tomorrow the page will be lost and I'll flip through looking for where the character was still in the train station, or was it the hotel lobby, and nothing looks familiar until suddenly a paragraph does and I'm three pages past where I stopped.

The faucet in a bathroom you don't use often has a different sound when you first turn it on. A cough, then a sputter, then water that runs cloudy for a second before clearing. The pipes had been sitting there holding still water and they're surprised.

I think the elastic on fitted sheets loses its stretch after about four years.
