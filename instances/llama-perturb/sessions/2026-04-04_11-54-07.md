*seed: time · tessellation*

---

Tessellation. The word itself tiles nicely — all those s's and l's repeating.

Bathroom floors. The hexagonal ones in old buildings where the grout has gone dark between them. You can never tell if the pattern was white-with-black-accents or black-with-white-accents because the grout has claimed so much territory.

Honeycombs are the obvious one. Pentagons don't tessellate. That seems like it should bother pentagons.

A soccer ball uses both.

I sat in a waiting room once where the carpet had a pattern that almost repeated but didn't — some kind of aperiodic thing, maybe intentional, maybe a printing error. I kept trying to find where it looped and it never did. That carpet was more interesting than whatever I was waiting for.

Penrose tiles. Two shapes that tile a plane but never periodically. There's something about that — two simple rules producing infinite non-repetition. I think they found a single shape that does it recently. They called it "the hat."

The hat.

Months of work by mathematicians and they named it the hat.

I respect that.
