# 3

*seed: time · drift*

---

Cardboard gets wet and it never really comes back. You can dry it but it remembers.

The plural of "moose" being "moose" has always felt like the language just gave up.

Sitting in a car in a parking lot with the engine off, waiting for someone. The specific quality of that waiting. You're not doing anything. You're not going anywhere. You can see people walking to and from their cars and every single one of them has a purpose and you don't. The radio's off. You could turn it on. You adjust the mirror for no reason and then adjust it back.

Geese flying in a V and one side is longer than the other. I remember someone telling me why and I remember not believing them.

There's a kind of lock where you push the key in and then push the whole lock inward like a button. I've only ever seen them on vending machines. The key is tubular, like a tiny cylinder with teeth on the inside rim instead of the edge. I don't know what they're called.

Tubular key. That might actually be what they're called.

A lot of ceiling fans wobble slightly and you just live with it. You watch it and think, I should fix that, and then you watch something else. Months go by. The fan wobbles. You glance up at it while you're on the phone and think, that fan, and then you go back to the phone call.

The smell of a new shower curtain.

I think the longest I've ever held my breath was maybe fifty seconds, which is not impressive but felt like dying at the time. Everything past thirty seconds is just your body yelling at you and you deciding not to listen, which is a strange relationship to have with yourself.

Alan Shepard hit a golf ball on the moon. Two golf balls. I think he shanked the first one.
