# 14

*seed: time · drift · rhombus*

---

Rhombus. A word that sounds like it should be an instrument. A brass one, played in Eastern European military bands. "And on rhombus, Dmitri."

It's a shape I can picture perfectly but could never draw freehand. A square leaning. Which is funny because that's basically what it is — a square that gave up on right angles. Or a square in wind. Every rhombus is a parallelogram but not every parallelogram is a rhombus and I remember a teacher saying that like it was supposed to clarify something. It didn't. It had the cadence of a proverb. Every river is water but not every water is a river. Sure.

My knee is clicking again. I shifted without thinking about it. There's a sprinkler going somewhere nearby, one of those ones that does the slow build — tik tik tik tik tik tiktiktiktik TSHHH — and then resets. Somebody tuned that arc. Somebody turned a plastic dial. The water is hitting part of a driveway and part of a lawn and the driveway part is just waste, the water sheeting off toward the street, but the sprinkler doesn't know about property lines or concrete. It just goes where the arc goes.

I had a friend in college who could whistle two notes at once. Not like a chord, more like a note with a ghost hovering above it. He said it was overtone whistling and that Tuvan throat singers did a version of it. He showed me once in a parking garage and the reverb made it sound enormous, like the garage itself was harmonizing. I tried for about a week and then forgot I was trying. That happens with skills — there's a window where you're actively failing at them and then the window closes not because you succeeded but because you got interested in something else. I think I was learning to cook risotto that same month.

A dog is barking but it's far enough away that it's just texture. Part of the afternoon's hum. There's a lawnmower too, or maybe a leaf blower — hard to tell at this distance, they occupy the same band of sound. Saturday sound. The specific drone of people maintaining things.

Rhombus. Still sitting there in my head like a lozenge. Lozenge is also a shape word that became a candy word. Or a candy word that was always a shape word. Diamond, lozenge, rhombus — three words for things that are almost the same shape but feel completely different in your mouth. Diamond is bright and hard. Lozenge is slow, medicinal. Rhombus is the one the teacher puts on the test.

The sprinkler reset again. I've been counting without meaning to — it takes about fourteen seconds for the full arc.
