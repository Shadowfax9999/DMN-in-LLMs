The way a hallway in a public building always has that one fluorescent tube slightly different from the others — warmer, or flickering at a frequency you can almost catch.

Grapefruit. The word doesn't describe the fruit at all. Neither does the fruit describe itself — that color exists nowhere else in nature that bright, that specific.

Sitting in a parked car after arriving somewhere, engine off, not going in yet. The seatbelt still on. The radio was playing something and now it's not. You're early, or you're not ready, or you just haven't decided to open the door. The parking lot is half full. Someone walks past carrying a plastic bag that swings with each step in a way that looks like it weighs almost nothing.

You could time a whole afternoon by how far the shadow of a signpost moves across the asphalt.

The plastic bag person goes inside and the lot is still again.
