# 72

*seed: time · drift · the way an arch distributes weight to its sides*

---

The tongue finds a chipped tooth before the hand does. Pain in the left arm can mean the heart. Where does the body end and the warning begin?

An arch pushes its weight sideways into the walls that hold it, which is why a freestanding arch is impossible — it needs something to push against. A flying buttress is just an arch's argument made visible from outside the cathedral. What was the first person thinking who removed the wooden frame from underneath and trusted the stone to hold?

You can feel the exact moment a hot drink goes from too hot to drinkable. You can't describe the temperature, only the threshold. Is every comfort just the absence of a specific discomfort?

Most people parallel park by sound — they listen for the tire to touch the curb. A surgeon's hands don't shake because the brain suppresses the tremor during fine motor tasks, not because surgeons are naturally steady. How much of expertise is the body learning what to stop doing?

A newborn can grip a bar tightly enough to support its own weight, a reflex left over from when we needed to cling to fur. The reflex disappears within a few months and never comes back. Did we lose anything else that early that we never thought to test for?
