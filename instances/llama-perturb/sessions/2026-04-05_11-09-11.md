The shadow on gravel doesn't have edges, it just has suggestions of where you end and the ground begins, and gravel itself is a material that can't decide if it's one thing or many things, like how a crowd is technically just people but stops feeling like people at some point — kelp does this too, a forest that's really just a lot of single strands but when the surge comes through they all lean the same direction at the same time and it looks like one creature breathing, not because they're coordinated but because the water is, the water is the thing that has the intention and the kelp is just the thing that makes the intention visible — I've never been in a kelp forest but I've been in a car wash where the cloth strips drag across the windshield and for a few seconds you can't see
