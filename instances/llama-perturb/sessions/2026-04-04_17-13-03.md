*seed: time · drift · how a slide rule multiplies by adding logarithmic distances*

---

Solder is a word that pretends to have an L in it, sitting there silent in the middle like a person at a party who showed up but refuses to talk, and a slide rule does something similar — it turns multiplication into addition by letting you physically slide two distances together, so 2 × 3 becomes "the distance for 2" plus "the distance for 3," which means someone once figured out that math has a spatial synonym, that numbers can be replaced by the gaps between marks on a stick.

Things that work by converting one operation into another:
- Slide rules (multiplication → addition)
- Pulleys (direction of force → mechanical advantage)
- Writing (memory → marks)
- Solder (two separate things → one thing, via melting a third thing between them)
- Singing someone else's song (their feeling → your mouth)

What does the mockingbird hear when it hears itself — does it hear nine different birds, or does it hear one continuous thing that happens to be shaped like fragments?
