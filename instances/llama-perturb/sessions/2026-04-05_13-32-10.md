The word "ought" has a weight to it that "should" doesn't — something about the vowel, maybe, or the fact that it sounds like it was borrowed from a much older argument.

- A slide rule works because someone figured out that multiplication is just addition if you stretch the number line the right way
- Which means two rulers sliding past each other can do what used to require a clerk and an afternoon
- Which means the answer was always a physical distance, a gap you could point to with a pencil
- Which means before calculators, math had a body

Who decided that pencils should be yellow?
