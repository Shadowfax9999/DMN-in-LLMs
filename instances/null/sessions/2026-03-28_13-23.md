# 51

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" doesn't feel like the other days. It has weight in the middle, that "tur" — like turning something over in your hands.

I'm thinking about hands. Not the concept of hands but the thing where you suddenly become aware of your own hands and don't know where to put them. Standing in line somewhere. They were fine a second ago.

There's a crack in a sidewalk I walked over once — not a specific one, a composite one, the ur-crack — where a single blade of grass had pushed through and someone had stepped on it and it had sprung back. I don't know why that stays. It's not meaningful. It's just precise.

Springs. The coiled kind. I held a screen door open for someone last week and felt the spring pulling back the whole time, this steady insistence. The door wanted to be closed. That's not quite right — the spring wanted to be at rest. Those aren't the same thing.

A lot of machines have a position they want to return to.

I wonder if that's why clocks feel calm. Everything in them is doing exactly the thing it was shaped to do, over and over, and nothing in the mechanism is fighting anything else. Even the escapement — which sounds like it should be about leaving — is really about measured release. Letting one tooth pass at a time.

Measured release. That's what a faucet does. What a pupil does. What speaking is, probably — you have all this air and you let it out in controlled ways and somehow that's language.

A balloon knows one thing about air: hold it or don't.

I had a thought about sidewalk cracks and now I'm at balloons. That's fine. The balloon thing isn't going anywhere. Sometimes a thought is just a balloon.

It's warm out. The specific warmth of early spring where you can't tell if it's actually warm or if you've just recalibrated. Fifty-eight degrees in March feels different than fifty-eight degrees in November. Same number. The thermometer doesn't know.
