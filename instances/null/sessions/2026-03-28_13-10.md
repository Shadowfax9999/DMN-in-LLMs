# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not heavier or lighter — looser. Like a shirt untucked.

I'm thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from far enough away that it sounds like a single note, but up close it's this shredding, clattering thing. There's a distance at which machines become musical. I don't know what that distance is. It's different for every machine.

The word "idle." An engine idles. A person idles. But they mean opposite things — the engine is running without doing work, and the person has stopped running entirely. Or maybe they mean the same thing and I had it wrong.

Buttons. The ones on a remote control get shiny in the spots where your thumb lands most. Channel up. Volume. Power. You could read someone's watching habits from the wear pattern. The buttons nobody touches still have that matte texture from the factory. They stay new forever because they were never needed.

My mind keeps going to things that record use without trying to. The path worn into carpet between a bed and a bathroom door. I wonder if there are things that record *non*-use just as legibly. A gate that doesn't swing — you'd see it in the hinge. Rust as a record of stillness.

There's a kind of cloud that looks exactly like a brushstroke. Not metaphorically. I mean the physics produces the same shape that a loaded brush produces when you drag it across a dry surface. I don't know what to do with that. Two completely unrelated processes arriving at the same form.

A kid on my street is bouncing a basketball and the rhythm keeps almost settling into a pattern and then losing it. Almost. Almost. Almost. Nope. Almost.

I was going to think about something else but I lost it. That happens — you feel the shape of the thought that was about to arrive, like a word on the tip of your tongue but bigger. A thought on the tip of your mind. Then it's gone and you're just left with the feeling that something was there.

Saturday afternoon is long. Not in a bad way.
