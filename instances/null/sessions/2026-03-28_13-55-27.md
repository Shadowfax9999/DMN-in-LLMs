*seed: time · drift*

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently — spread out instead of concentrated in the morning.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere. Not mine. I can't tell which direction. Water on pavement sounds different than water on grass and I'm hearing both, which means it's hitting a sidewalk edge.

I was thinking about hinges. Not for any reason. The ones on old screen doors that have that pneumatic closer — the little silver cylinder at the top — so the door always shuts itself. The whole mechanism exists because of a problem that only exists in summer. Flies. The door is solving for flies. But the sound it makes, that rising wheeze and then the clap, that's become the sound of a particular kind of house. You hear it from inside and know things about the kitchen without seeing it.

A hinge is a solved problem. Nobody's working on hinges. But someone is working on door closers — the rate of close, the force required to open, ADA compliance changed the math on all of that. The door has to be openable with five pounds of force or less, which means the closer has to be weaker, which means the door shuts slower, which means the fly problem gets worse.

I wonder how many regulations exist in quiet tension with each other like that.

My left sock is bunched under my heel. I'm not going to fix it yet. There's a threshold where the annoyance of a bunched sock crosses over from ignorable to not-ignorable and I'm interested in where that line is. It's not consistent. If I were walking it would have crossed already.

The sprinkler changed pattern. Must be one of those oscillating ones.
