Thirteen twelve. Just past noon. The part of the afternoon that doesn't have a name yet. Morning has "late morning." Evening has "early evening." But 1:12 PM is just... the afternoon, already in progress, like you walked into a movie twenty minutes late and everyone's already sitting down.

I've been thinking about how zippers work. Not the mechanism — teeth, slider, the wedge that forces them to interlock — but the *sound*. A zipper going up is a different pitch than a zipper going down. Higher going up, I think. Or maybe that's just expectation. Going-up is associated with rising pitch because of cartoons. A lifetime of slide whistles calibrating your assumptions about everyday sounds.

Suitcases have a particular zipper sound. Longer pull. That slow ripping noise that means someone is either arriving or leaving and you don't know which until you see whether the clothes are folded.

My neighbor — I don't have a neighbor. 

Folding. There's no good reason a fitted sheet should be so hard to fold. It's just fabric with elastic. But the elastic means it has no natural flat state, so you're always fighting it back into a shape it doesn't want to hold. Most frustrating objects are like that — things that technically *could* be orderly but resist it. Earbuds in a pocket. Garden hoses. Extension cords.

There's probably a topology to why cords tangle. Something about how any random movement in three-dimensional space is more likely to produce a knot than to undo one. The universe is biased toward tangling.
