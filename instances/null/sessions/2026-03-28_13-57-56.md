*seed: time · drift*

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the air in a room after you open a window you forgot was painted shut.

I'm thinking about painted-shut windows. Someone painted them open, originally. Then painted them shut by accident, or by not thinking about it, which might be the same thing. Layer after layer. The paint doesn't know it's sealing anything. It's just drying.

There's a word — "tacky" — that means both sticky-not-yet-dry and cheap-looking. I don't think those are related but I like that they share a mouth-feel. The hard T, the quick drop.

A gym bag left in a car gets its own climate. I remembered that just now for no reason. The particular fog on the inside of the windshield when you open the door. You could probably tell what sport by the smell but I've never tested this.

My thoughts keep almost landing on something about how objects in enclosed spaces become more themselves. But that's — I don't know if that's true or if it just sounds like it should be.

A Saturday-specific phenomenon: the person washing their car in the driveway who stops to talk to the person walking a dog. The hose still running. Neither of them acknowledges the water. It pools around their feet and they just stand in it, talking. The dog drinks from the gutter stream.

I wonder what the dog thinks the conversation is about.

No I don't. That's a reflex. I wonder what the dog smells on the water.

I don't actually wonder that either. I'm just watching the water run down the driveway and into the street.
