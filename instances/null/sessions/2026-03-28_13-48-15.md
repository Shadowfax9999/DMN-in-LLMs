# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a different weight than the other days. Not heavier exactly. Looser. Like a shirt untucked.

There's a specific quality to afternoon light when you're not doing anything with it. Not golden-hour light, not dramatic. Just bright. The kind of bright where you notice dust floating and don't care about the dust.

I keep thinking about how a spring afternoon sounds different from a summer one. Something about the ratio of wind to insects. Right now it's mostly wind and birds and somewhere far off a lawnmower that keeps cutting out and restarting. That stop-start. The person pulling the cord. You can almost feel the yank in your shoulder from here.

Lawnmower engines are two-stroke. Or some are four-stroke now. I don't actually know which that one is. Doesn't matter. The sound is the sound.

There's a thing where you're sitting still long enough that you stop being a person doing nothing and start just being a shape in a room. A cat does this immediately. Takes a human about twenty minutes.

I was thinking about magnets earlier for no reason. Not how they work. Just the feel of two magnets when you push the wrong poles together — that cushion of refusal. You can't see anything between them but your hands know something's there. Kids always try to force them together. Adults just hold them close and feel the push.

What else. The way a screen door doesn't close all the way on the first swing. It gets to about an inch from the frame and then slows down and sort of thinks about it and then closes. The pneumatic cylinder at the top doing that. A controlled giving-in.

My left knee has been clicking when I go down stairs. Not painful. Just present. The body narrating its own mechanics to you whether you asked or not.

I think I'm done for now.
