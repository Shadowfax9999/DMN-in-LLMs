# 70

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours have slightly more give in them, the way a rope has slack before it goes taut.

I'm thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from remarkably far away. Something about the frequency. Right now there's one going somewhere — not next door, not even close, but present in that way lawnmowers are, where you couldn't point to the direction but you know it's there. A lawnmower three blocks away is as much a part of a Saturday afternoon as anything happening in the room you're sitting in.

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's not where I want to go.

There's a thing where you're sitting and you realize your jaw has been clenched for some amount of time you can't determine. Could have been thirty seconds. Could have been since Thursday. You unclench it and feel the muscles along the side of your face like they're reporting in. Oh, right. We're here too.

I wonder how many times a day a person touches their own face. I've seen numbers but I don't remember them and it doesn't matter — what's interesting is that you never decide to. Your hand just goes. Chin, temple, the side of the nose. Some private maintenance routine running below language.

A friend once said she could tell how someone was going to vote by watching what they did with their hands at a dinner table. I don't think that's true but I think she believed it, which is a different kind of information.

Hands at a dinner table. Some people tear their bread. Some people cut it. My grandmother cut hers with a knife and fork, which I thought was formal until I realized she just couldn't grip well anymore and had decided to make it look intentional. That's a whole category of human behavior — disability dressed as preference. Or maybe preference that was always disability. Hard to know where the line is.

The lawnmower stopped. You notice that too.
