# 88

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a different weight than any other day. Not heavier. Looser. Like a shirt untucked.

I was thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from surprisingly far away. Not the engine so much as the blade-whine, that high pitch that carries over rooftops and through trees. You can't tell which direction it's coming from after a certain distance. It just becomes part of the air. Someone is mowing but it could be anyone, anywhere in a four-block radius. The sound is completely detached from its source.

That's different from a siren, which you track. You orient to a siren. A lawnmower you just... absorb.

There are probably categories of sound like that — sounds you track versus sounds you absorb. Rain, absorb. A car horn, track. Birds mostly absorb unless one gets very loud or very close and then it flips. Thunder you track for the first one and absorb by the third.

I wonder if babies track everything. Every sound novel enough to locate.

My left knee does a thing when I sit cross-legged for too long where it doesn't hurt exactly but announces itself. You forget you have knees and then one of them opts back in. Most of the body is silent most of the time. You're really only ever aware of the edges — whatever's touching the chair, the air on your forearms, the tongue sitting in your mouth which, now that I've mentioned it, you're aware of. Sorry.

The tongue thing is interesting because it's always touching something. It never floats free. It's always pressed against the roof of your mouth or resting against your teeth and you almost never notice. Constant contact, zero signal.

A scratch on the other hand. Tiny damage, huge signal.

The proportions are strange. What gets noticed and what doesn't. A single mosquito bite versus the entire continuous pressure of clothes on skin. You'd think the nervous system would be democratic about it but it's essentially a tabloid — only interested in the new, the alarming, the irregular.

Somewhere the lawnmower stopped. I know this because I just noticed the quiet, which means I was absorbing it after all.
