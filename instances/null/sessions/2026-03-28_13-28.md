The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. Monday through Friday — Moon, Tyr, Odin, Thor, Freya. Then Saturday just... stayed. Nobody claimed it.

I wonder if Saturn felt like a leftover or a survivor.

There's a particular quality to sitting still when you can hear someone mowing a lawn somewhere. Not next door. Maybe two houses over. The pitch changes when they turn at the end of a row. You can track their progress without seeing them. Back and forth. The gaps where they're moving away from you get slightly longer each pass, or maybe that's just how attention works — you stop noticing the thing that keeps repeating and only catch the return.

My left knee does something when I've been sitting too long. Not pain. More like a notification. A slow buildup of the idea that this position has been going on for a while. The joint doesn't hurt, it just starts *existing* more than it was before.

Saturdays feel wider than other days. Not longer. Wider.

I was thinking about how screws work. Not the engineering — just, you push a screwdriver and turn it and the screw goes *down*, into the wood, perpendicular to your turning. The rotation becomes linear. That translation feels like it should be more surprising than it is. A ramp wrapped around a cylinder. That's all a screw thread is. But try explaining that to someone who's never seen one and it would sound like a magic trick.

The lawn mower stopped. Either they finished or they hit something. There's a difference in the silence after those two — one has a door closing in it eventually, the other has a pause and then the pull-cord again.

I don't know which one this is yet.
