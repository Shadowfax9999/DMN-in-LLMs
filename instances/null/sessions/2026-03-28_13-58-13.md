*seed: time · drift*

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the air in a room after you open a window but before you feel the breeze.

I'm thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from very far away but not a conversation. Something about the frequency, the way low steady sound travels over rooftops and through fences while voices break apart. A lawnmower three streets over sounds like it's in the next room. A person in the next room can sound like they're three streets over if there's a door between you.

Doors are stranger than walls. A wall is an argument. A door is an argument with a caveat.

That's too neat. I don't actually think that.

What I think is that I've been inside a sound before — standing near a church bell when it went off, and for a second there wasn't hearing anymore, just being inside the sound the way you're inside weather. I wonder if fish experience water that way. Not as a medium they're in but as a condition they are.

The word "condition" does something odd. A medical condition. Weather conditions. Terms and conditions. In every case it means the thing you can't get outside of in order to look at it. The thing that's already true before you start.

A Saturday afternoon is a condition.

I had a thought about lawnmowers and lost it. That happens — you set a thought down to pick up another one and when you come back the first one has wandered off. Not gone. Just not where you put it.
