*seed: time · drift*

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's one of those facts that sits there without doing anything. Saturn's day. Nobody thinks about Saturn when they're deciding whether to mow the lawn.

There's a particular quality to afternoon light when it comes through a window you haven't cleaned in a while. The dust on the glass softens everything by about ten percent. You'd never choose it but you also never clean the window.

I'm thinking about how a rubber band works. You stretch it and the polymer chains uncoil — they go from tangled to aligned, which is actually a decrease in entropy, which is why a stretched rubber band is *warm*. If you touch one to your lip right after stretching it. That's not a metaphor for anything. It's just a strange fact about rubber bands and lips.

The neighbor's wind chime is tuned to a pentatonic scale so that no combination of notes sounds bad. Someone figured that out. Five notes chosen specifically so that randomness always sounds pleasant. There's something in that but I'm going to leave it alone.

Socks never match. Not in the way people complain about — the missing sock, the dryer eating one — but in the subtler way where two black socks from different packages are slightly different blacks and you can feel the difference in the knit even if you can't see it. Your feet know.

I wonder what the first thing ever deliberately buried was. Not a body. An object. A tool someone put underground on purpose, meaning to come back for it. A cache. The first act of trust in future-you — that you'd remember where, that you'd still need it, that the ground would keep it safe.

Probably food.

The fridge is humming. It cycles on and off maybe forty times a day and I only hear it when it stops. The absence registers, the presence doesn't. That's just how ears work with constant sounds. The auditory nerve stops firing for stimuli that don't change.

My left knee pops when I stand up from sitting on the floor. It didn't used to. There wasn't a day it started — it just gradually became true, like how you can't identify the exact moment a room gets dark enough to need a lamp.

I should get up and do something but I'm not going to yet.
