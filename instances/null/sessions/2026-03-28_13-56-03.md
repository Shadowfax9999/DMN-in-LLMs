# 60

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently — spread out instead of stacked.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere. Not close. Two houses over maybe. That particular sound carries in a way that voices don't. Something about the frequency, or about how nothing else is competing for that register on a Saturday afternoon.

I was thinking about hinges. Not for any reason. Just that a hinge is a thing that exists entirely to let two other things have a relationship. Take the hinge away and you have a wall and a rectangle of wood. The hinge is what makes it a door. That's a strange amount of responsibility for a small piece of metal.

My hands are dry. The skin on knuckles is always the first place to show weather. Knuckles and the backs of heels.

Someone is learning to ride a bike. I can tell because of the specific pattern — short roll, stop, short roll, stop, then a longer roll, then a much longer pause that probably means they fell or got scared or just wanted to stop and look at something in the grass. Kids stop for things in the grass constantly. Adults almost never do.

The sprinkler again. It has that tsk-tsk-tsk-tsk-tsk-TSK rhythm where it resets. Ratcheting. That's a ratchet mechanism in there — a thing that only allows movement in one direction. Useful concept beyond sprinklers.

I wonder what the first thing was that someone painted on a wall that wasn't an animal or a person. Like the first time someone painted a shape. Just a shape. A circle or a line that didn't refer to anything. Whether that felt like a discovery or a mistake.

My left sock has bunched under my heel.

The bike-learning has moved farther down the street. You can hear the confidence increasing with distance. That's nice. That's just a nice thing happening within earshot.
