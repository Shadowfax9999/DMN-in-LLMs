# 59

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours have slightly more give in them, the way a rope bridge moves differently than a stone one — you're still crossing, but you feel the air underneath.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere. Not close. Two houses over maybe. That particular sound — the tik-tik-tik-tik then the long hiss of the return sweep — I couldn't tell you if I'm hearing it now or remembering it. Doesn't matter. It's the same sprinkler. It's always the same sprinkler.

I was thinking about how a combination lock works. Not the metaphor of it, just the actual mechanism. There are discs inside, each with a notch, and you're lining up the notches so a bar can fall through all of them at once. Which means the lock isn't really about the *combination* — it's about alignment. Three separate things that mostly block each other, and one specific orientation where they all become open at the same time.

I don't know why that's satisfying to think about.

Totally different thing: the way you can hear whether a room has carpet before you walk into it. Something about how sound behaves at the threshold. Deader air. You make the adjustment before you step in — some fractional thing in your stride.

My hands smell like copper. No — like the *idea* of copper. Like I was holding coins recently but wasn't. Can you have a phantom smell that isn't triggered by anything? Just your nose practicing?

A cardinal is doing that thing where it sounds like a car alarm designed by someone who only had two notes to work with. Insistent. The repetition isn't lazy, though. Each one is slightly recalibrated. Aimed differently.

I wonder what the sprinkler is watering. Probably nothing yet. Too early for most of it. Just wetting dirt that will be something in six weeks.

That's fine.
