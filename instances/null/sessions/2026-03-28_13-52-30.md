*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name. All the others got swapped for Norse gods. I don't know why Saturn survived. Maybe nobody had a good replacement. Maybe they just forgot.

There's a particular quality to Saturday afternoon light that I can't quite place. Not the golden-hour thing. Earlier than that. The part where shadows have committed to a direction but haven't started stretching yet.

I'm thinking about how a screen door sounds different when it's warm out. The spring is looser, or the frame has expanded, or — actually I don't know the mechanism and I'm not going to chase it. It just sounds different. Flatter.

Socks on a hardwood floor. The static you build up walking from the bedroom to the kitchen. You forget about it until you touch the faucet. The body as a capacitor. You're just walking around storing charge and discharging it into door handles.

Someone told me once that the word "handle" and the word "hand" aren't related. That seems wrong but I never checked.

There's a thing where you open the fridge not because you're hungry but because you want something to change. You stand there in the cold air looking at the same shelf you looked at twenty minutes ago. The light inside is always the same. Bright, flat, slightly blue. It doesn't know what time it is.

A dog is barking somewhere but not nearby. Two streets over maybe. The distance does something to the sound — rounds off the edges of it.

I keep almost thinking about river deltas. The way a single channel becomes many channels becomes a fan of sediment becomes land. Not a metaphor for anything. Just the shape of it. How the water slows down and drops what it's carrying.

My foot's asleep.
