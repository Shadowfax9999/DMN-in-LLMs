# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours have softer edges.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere. Not visible from here but audible — that tssk-tssk-tssk-tssk-tssk and then the longer hiss of the return sweep. I keep almost counting the intervals. The rhythm isn't quite regular, which is why it holds attention. If it were metronomic it would disappear.

Rubber bands. I was thinking about rubber bands earlier, or not earlier, just now — the way they live in a kitchen drawer for years and then one day they've gone brittle and they snap. What happens to rubber in a drawer. The polymers unlink. Oxygen gets between them. A rubber band is slowly becoming not-a-rubber-band the whole time it sits there, and the snap is just the moment you notice.

My left knee does a thing when I stand up from sitting too long. Not pain. A click. Like a latch releasing.

The sprinkler again. Someone's out there watering a lawn they'll have to mow in four days.

I wonder what the first deliberately flat surface was. Not a rock face — those happen. But the first time someone ground something level on purpose. Probably for grinding something else. A flat surface to make flat surfaces. The recursion goes all the way down. You need a reference plane to make a reference plane. Machinists still deal with this. Three plates, rubbed against each other in rotation, will converge on flat. It's one of those things that works and the proof of why it works is less convincing than just watching it work.

A cardinal just did its laser-gun call. The two-note one that drops.

I had a thought about envelopes that I've lost. Something about the glue strip. It's gone.

The shadow of the house has moved about a foot since I started noticing it. That's the kind of measurement that means nothing — a foot of shadow — but it makes the afternoon feel like it has a speed. Slow. The speed of shadow on grass.
