*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the air in a room after you open a window you forgot was painted shut.

I'm thinking about painted-shut windows. The way each layer of paint was its own small decision not to open. Nobody paints a window shut on purpose. It's just that opening it wasn't the point at the time.

There's a word — accrete. No, I don't need the word.

My left knee does a thing when I sit too long where it doesn't hurt but it reminds me it's there. Like it's raising a hand. Most of the body is silent most of the time and the parts that speak up aren't always the parts that need something. Sometimes a joint just wants to be acknowledged.

I wonder how many sounds I'm not hearing right now because they've been constant long enough to disappear. The refrigerator probably. Whatever the neighbor's doing. My own breathing until I thought about it just now, and now it's loud, and it'll take a few minutes to forget it again. Attention is sticky that way. You can't un-notice on purpose.

Trying to think about what I was thinking about before I started thinking. That's the thing about the DMN — the brain's default mode — it's not really *about* anything, it's the state that exists before aboutness. You can't aim at it. Like trying to see the floaters in your eye by looking directly at them.

A soccer ball on a roof. I don't know why that image. Some neighborhood, some roof, some ball that went up and never came back down. The kid who kicked it probably forgot about it within a week. The ball is still up there getting bleached by the sun, getting lighter in color and heavier with rainwater that pools in the depression where a panel has sagged. Eventually someone will get up there to fix the gutter and find it and not know what to do with it. It's not theirs. It's not anyone's anymore.

Things that stop being owned: umbrellas left on buses. Pennies on the ground. A grocery list that falls out of someone's pocket in a parking lot. The list is interesting because it's so specific to a person — they needed eggs, they needed turmeric, they needed the kind of crackers that come in the red box — and the moment it hits the asphalt it becomes illegible as identity. Just handwriting and words.

Turmeric stains everything it touches. Cutting boards, fingernails, countertops. I think that's the curcumin. There are monks whose robes are dyed with it. Or were, historically. I'm not sure if that's true or if I'm assembling it from pieces that don't actually connect.

That happens a lot. Confident memories that turn out to be composites. The brain as a collage artist who doesn't label the sources.

I should get up and do something with the afternoon but I'm not going to yet.
