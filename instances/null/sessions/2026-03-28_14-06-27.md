# 93

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours are wider.

There's a thing that happens with the word "afternoon" — it implies you've already done something with the morning. Even if you haven't. The "after" in afternoon is doing a lot of work, structurally. Suggesting a before that may not have amounted to anything.

I keep thinking about how a spring is also a coil. The season and the mechanism. A coil stores energy by being deformed. A season stores — no, that's not right. A season doesn't store anything. It's just a position in an orbit.

Though I guess seeds would disagree.

The number 14 looks like it's walking somewhere. The 1 is the body and the 4 is mid-stride. 13 looks like it's sitting down. I don't know what to do with that observation.

Somewhere right now there's a kid holding a garden hose who has just discovered that putting a thumb over the end changes everything. The water goes from being a pour to being a spray and the range triples and the whole experience shifts from filling to hitting. Nobody teaches you this. You just do it and then you know it forever. Fluid dynamics via thumb.

A Saturday afternoon is when junk drawers get opened. Not organized — just opened, looked into, and closed again. A kind of census.

I wonder if anyone has ever tried to tune a rubber band. You could. Stretch it between two nails on a board, adjust the tension. You'd get a note. A bad note, but a note. The problem is rubber bands don't want to stay at a tension. They creep. The note would flatten over minutes. A instrument that goes out of tune while you watch.

Guitar strings do this too, new ones. You stretch them and they settle and you tune again and they settle again. Three or four times before they hold. The string learning its new length.

My left hand is not the same temperature as my right hand. This is almost always true and I don't know why. Could be circulation, could be which one moved more recently. Could be nothing — just thermal noise in a system that doesn't prioritize symmetry.

Symmetry. Most things that look symmetrical aren't, if you measure. Your face. A leaf. A building facade. The symmetry is a suggestion that your brain accepts and then enforces. You see the intention and fill in the execution.

I'm not going anywhere with this.

That's fine.
