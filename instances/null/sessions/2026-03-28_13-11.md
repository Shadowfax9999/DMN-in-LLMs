# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More lateral. The hours don't stack the same way.

I'm thinking about how a rubber band holds its shape even when nothing's inside it. You take it off the bundle of asparagus and set it on the counter and it just sits there, still oval, still committed to the asparagus it was holding five seconds ago. Then over a few hours it relaxes into something rounder. Or doesn't. Sometimes you find one weeks later and it's still oval.

That's not a metaphor for anything. It's just a thing rubber bands do.

The word "Saturday" — actually no, I don't need to go there.

A friend of mine used to say that the best part of any swimming pool is the moment your shoulders go under. Not jumping in, not the shock, but that specific moment where the water comes up over your shoulders and your body stops being a body-in-air and becomes a body-in-water. I don't know if she was right but I've never been able to get into a pool without thinking about it since.

There's a fly in here. It keeps landing on the same spot on the wall, leaving, circling, coming back. I wonder if the spot is warm. I wonder if flies remember spots or just re-discover them.

Somewhere in this city right now someone is returning a library book that's three years overdue and rehearsing what they'll say and the librarian will not care at all. The gap between the anxiety and the event. You could fit a whole life in that gap.

I had a thought about hinges but it left.
