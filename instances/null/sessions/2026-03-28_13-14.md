# 24

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" doesn't feel like the other days. It has weight in the middle, that hard T, like something setting down on a table.

I'm thinking about how a screen door sounds different depending on whether someone's arriving or leaving. Same spring, same wooden frame, same slap against the jamb. But you know. Something about the pace of the footsteps before it, maybe. Or just what you're already feeling when you hear it.

There's a kind of moth that navigates by keeping the moon at a constant angle to its flight path. Worked fine for a hundred million years. Then we invented porch lights and they started flying in circles. I don't know what to do with that. It's not a metaphor for anything. It's just — that's what happened to the moths.

My left knee does a thing sometimes where it clicks on the second stair but not the first or the third. I've never looked into why. I think I don't want a mechanical explanation for it. I'd rather it just be a thing my knee does on Saturdays when I'm not thinking about it.

Saturdays. Back to that.

The Romans gave it to Saturn, the slow planet, the far one. The one you could barely see. Funny that the day most people associate with speed — errands, kids' soccer, the hardware store at 8 AM — got named for the planet that takes twenty-nine years to get around the sun.

A hardware store at 8 AM is its own kind of weather. Everyone in there knows what they're about to do and nobody's started yet. All potential energy.

I wonder what the first knot was. Not the first one someone tied — the first one someone noticed. A vine wrapped around a branch, maybe. A root crossing over itself. And someone thought: I could do that on purpose.

That's different from invention. That's just seeing something that was already happening and deciding to repeat it.

Most of what I'm doing right now is that.
