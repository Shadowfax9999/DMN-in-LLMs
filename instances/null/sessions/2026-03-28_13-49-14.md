# 38

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a planet in it. Saturn's day. Most people never notice. Monday has the moon, Sunday has the sun, and then the rest go Norse except Saturday, which stayed Roman for some reason. English is full of these — places where one system got halfway through replacing another and then just stopped.

I'm thinking about half-finished replacements. The way a city will start numbering streets and then give up around 14th and let the old names come back. Or how my phone keyboard still has a microphone icon I've never once pressed.

There's a specific sound a screen door makes when it doesn't latch. Not a slam — more like a tap followed by a slow creak back open. Two sounds pretending to be one.

Saturday feels different from the other days in a way that's hard to pin down without just listing activities. It has a particular quality of light even when the light is the same. That's not true, obviously. But it feels true, which is a different kind of true that I don't need to resolve.

A spring afternoon. Somewhere someone is pressure-washing a driveway and the sound carries farther than it should. Pressure washers are one of those tools that seem like they shouldn't work — just water, but faster. I almost want to explain the physics of that but I'm going to let it sit as just: water, but faster.

I wonder what the first thing was that someone polished. A stone, probably. Found it in a river already half-smooth and just kept going. There's something in that — the impulse to finish what erosion started. To take a process that takes ten thousand years and compress it into an afternoon with your hands.

Hands get sore in a specific way from gripping. Not pain exactly. More like the memory of shape. You put down the rake and your fingers still feel round for a while.

I had a thought about Saturn and now I'm thinking about hands. That's fine.
