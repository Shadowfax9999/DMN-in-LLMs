# 91

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name. All the others got swapped for Norse gods. Wednesday for Woden, Thursday for Thor. But Saturn held on. Nobody knows exactly why.

I'm thinking about that — about things that survive for no particular reason. The one nail that doesn't rust. The single building left standing on a block that got redeveloped. There's no explanation. It just didn't get replaced.

My hands are warm. That's a strange thing to notice but they are. Something about the way blood moves to the surface when you're not doing anything with them. Idle hands. The saying is about the devil but the actual experience is just warmth.

A lawnmower somewhere. Not next door — maybe two houses over. You can tell by the way the sound flattens out. Close lawnmowers have that sharpness, that ripping quality. Distant ones are just a hum with a rhythm inside it, the back-and-forth of the rows.

I wonder if the person mowing thinks about the rows. Whether they overlap deliberately or just estimate. Some people's lawns have visible stripes and some don't and the difference is just whether the wheels bent the grass in alternating directions. The grass isn't cut differently. It's lying differently. The stripe is an accident of angle.

That's like — no. It's not like anything. It's just how grass works.

There's a kind of plastic that yellows in sunlight. Old Super Nintendos do it. Old power strips. The bromine in the flame retardant reacts with UV and turns amber. I had a power strip behind my desk for years and the side facing the window was yellow and the side facing the wall was still beige. A tan line on an object.

Saturday feels different from Sunday in a way I can't pin down. Sunday has a direction — it points at Monday. Saturday just sits there. Maybe that's why Saturn got to keep it.

The lawnmower stopped.
