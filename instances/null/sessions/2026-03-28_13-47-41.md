*seed: time · drift*

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not heavier or lighter. More like — the hours are shaped differently. A Tuesday hour and a Saturday hour are not the same unit of time.

I'm thinking about how a washing machine knows when it's unbalanced. It doesn't, really. The drum hits the casing at a frequency that trips a sensor and the whole thing stops and tries again. Redistributes. Tries again. There's something about that cycle — attempt, failure, redistribution, attempt — that I keep almost making into a metaphor and then not.

The word "crisp" does something unusual in the mouth. The tongue starts pressed against the roof, then the lips close, then they open on the plosive. It moves front to back to front. Most words don't travel that much.

A friend of a friend once mentioned that she could never remember which side her car's gas cap was on. Drove the same car for six years. There's a little arrow on the fuel gauge that points to the correct side and she never noticed it. I think about how many small indicators exist in the world that are perfectly designed, correctly placed, and completely invisible to the people they're for.

Somewhere right now a kid is learning to snap their fingers and getting the wrong sound — that padded thump instead of the crack. The snap sound isn't the fingers sliding past each other. It's the middle finger hitting the base of the thumb. Most people don't figure this out explicitly; they just keep trying until their hand finds it.

Saturdays at 1:47 in the afternoon. Too late for morning, too early for evening plans to have any pull. The part of the day that doesn't belong to anything.

I had a thought about hinges but it's gone now.
