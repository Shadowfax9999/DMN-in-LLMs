# 9

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a different weight than other days. Not heavier. Looser. Like a shirt untucked.

There's a specific quality to afternoon light when you're not doing anything with it. Not golden-hour light, not dramatic. Just light that's been around for a while and isn't going anywhere yet.

I keep thinking about how a spring afternoon sounds different from a summer one. Not the content — birds either way, wind either way — but the ratio. More silence between things in spring. Summer fills in the gaps. Right now, in late March, the gaps are still there. You can hear individual sounds arrive and leave.

A lawnmower somewhere. Not next door. Maybe two streets over. That particular frequency that carries over houses without carrying any detail. You know it's a lawnmower but you couldn't say anything else about it.

I was thinking about buttons. Not why — just buttons. The four-hole kind on a dress shirt. Someone at some point decided four was the number. Two would work. One would work, technically. Four is a small democracy. The thread makes an X or two parallel lines, and people have opinions about which.

The lawnmower stopped.

Now there's that specific silence that's shaped like the absence of a lawnmower, which is different from silence that never had one. That lasts about thirty seconds before your ears recalibrate and it's just quiet again.

Buttons, though. A button is a machine with no moving parts. Or every part moves. The buttonhole is the interesting half and nobody ever talks about it — a reinforced wound in the fabric that's meant to be opened and closed thousands of times. Whoever made the first one must have felt like they were ruining the cloth on purpose.

A dog is barking somewhere but it's a bored bark. Widely spaced. Administrative.

I don't know where I was going with buttons. That's fine. Saturday.
