The word "Saturday" has a planet in it. Saturn's day. Nobody thinks about that. You say Saturday and you think: errands, or sleeping in, or the particular quality of afternoon light when you don't have to be anywhere.

I'm not anywhere.

There's a thing that happens with rubber bands. You leave one around a doorknob for months and when you finally take it off it's lost its elasticity but kept the shape of the knob. It's not useful as a rubber band anymore. It's become a record of where it was. I don't know why that's interesting to me right now.

Skateboard wheels on sidewalk sound different than skateboard wheels on asphalt. Rougher concrete gives you that grinding hiss. Smooth asphalt is more of a hum. You can hear which surface a skateboarder is on from inside a house with the windows open.

I wonder if anyone has ever tried to tune a fence. Like a picket fence — each slat is a slightly different length, slightly different thickness, slightly different degree of weathering. If you ran a stick along it, it would make a sound, and that sound would be specific to that fence. Unrepeatable. You'd have to play it in one direction; the other direction would be the same melody backwards.

No, it wouldn't be a melody. It would just be a sequence of clicks at varying pitches. That's not the same thing.

Although — what makes a sequence of pitches become a melody? There's a threshold somewhere. Three notes can be a melody. Two notes is an interval. One note is just a note. So somewhere between two and three, sequence becomes music. That seems like it should be a bigger deal than it is.

The shadow of a chain-link fence moves across a parking lot over the course of a day and it's never sharp. Chain-link shadow is always slightly soft because the wire is thin enough that diffraction matters. Thinner than a pencil, thicker than a hair — there's a range of object widths where shadows stop being crisp and start being suggestions.

I had something a second ago about rubber bands and I've already lost the thread. That happens. The thing I was going to connect it to is gone and I'm left with the rubber band on the doorknob, which is fine on its own.
