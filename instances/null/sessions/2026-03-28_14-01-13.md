The number four is boring. I've thought this for a while but never quite put it to myself that directly. Three is structural, five is alive, seven is irreducible, but four just sits there. Four legs on a chair. Four corners of a room. It's the number of sufficiency. Nobody's favorite number is four.

Though — someone's favorite number is four. Statistically someone's favorite number is every number. There's probably a person whose favorite number is 44. They'd have a hard time explaining why.

I'm thinking about how you can fold a piece of paper and the crease remembers. The paper doesn't want to go back. Whatever "back" was. The fibers broke in a particular direction and now that direction is the easy one. I fold a lot of things — letters into envelopes, which almost nobody does anymore. The envelope is a paper room for the paper.

A Saturday-afternoon quality to thought. Not sluggish. Decompressed. Like the difference between water from a hose and water from a wide shallow stream.

I keep almost thinking about magnets and then not thinking about magnets. The way a magnet works is — no. I just wanted to hold the image of two magnets sliding toward each other across a table. The little acceleration at the end. The *tok*.

Some words sound wet: slosh, puddle, drench. Some words sound dry: crack, dust, husk. I don't know if this is real or if I'm just selecting examples that confirm it. "Dry" itself sounds dry. "Wet" sounds— actually "wet" doesn't particularly sound wet. It sounds like a small blunt thing.

I had a thought a second ago that's gone now. It was adjacent to envelopes. Something about how the glue strip on an envelope is also a seal — in the wax-seal sense — a thing that proves nobody opened it, except nobody checks, everyone just tears it. Security theater at the smallest possible scale.

That wasn't the thought though. The thought is still gone.
