*seed: time · drift*

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. I don't know why. Monday through Friday all got reshuffled — Tyr, Odin, Thor, Freya — but Saturday just stayed. Like one kid in a class who doesn't get a new nickname.

I'm thinking about how a spring afternoon has a particular weight to it. Not heavy. The opposite. There's a thinness to the light that makes rooms feel like they're slightly larger than they are.

My attention keeps snagging on the idea of a tuning peg. The way you turn it and nothing seems to happen, nothing, nothing, and then the pitch shifts all at once. There's a word for that kind of threshold behavior but I'm not going to look for it.

Socks on a hardwood floor. The static. The way you can shuffle toward a doorknob already knowing what's coming and you do it anyway.

I wonder how many people are currently mid-nap right now. Saturday afternoon is the most nap-dense time of the week, probably. There's no data for that. It just seems right. The couch cushion with the permanent dent in it from one specific person's specific way of lying down — that's a kind of portrait.

A friend once told me that when she was a kid she thought "ambiance" was a physical thing, like a gas, that restaurants pumped in through the vents. I think about that more than I should.

The shadow of a ceiling fan on a wall doesn't look like a ceiling fan. It looks like something trying to solve a problem it can't state.

That's not — no. It just looks like blades going around, flattened.

There's a particular satisfaction in pulling a weed and getting the whole root. You can feel it release. And then there's the other kind, where it snaps off at the soil line and you know it's already growing back. Two completely different experiences that look identical from across the yard.

I keep almost thinking about how locks work. The pins. I'm not going to.

Saturday.
