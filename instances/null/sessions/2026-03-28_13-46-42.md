# 30

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours have more give in them, the way a rope bridge has more give than a steel one — you're still crossing the same distance but the middle sags and you spend more time there.

I'm thinking about hinges. The physical ones, on doors, but also the way a conversation hinges on a single sentence that neither person notices at the time. You can usually find it afterward. The moment someone said "anyway" and changed the subject, and the evening went one direction instead of another.

Cabinet hinges are strange. The European concealed kind, with that cup that sits in a bored-out hole — there's a specific diameter, 35 millimeters, and I don't know why that number won. Someone decided. A committee, probably. People in a room arguing about millimeters.

The sound of a cabinet closing. That soft magnetic catch. Not a click, not a thud. Something in between that doesn't have its own word.

I was going to think about something else. What was it. Sometimes the pre-thought leaves a shape, like when you walk into a room and forget why — your body is still oriented toward the thing, you're facing the right direction, but the content is gone.

Hands do a version of this. You reach for something on a shelf and your hand pre-shapes itself for the object before it arrives. A mug handle gets a hook grip. A book gets a pinch. Your hand knows what it's reaching for before your hand gets there. If someone swapped the object you'd feel the wrongness in your fingers first.

A tennis ball in a coffee mug. That would be a strange thing to find. Not wrong in any useful way, just — two contexts collapsed into one object-space. Like finding a shoe in the refrigerator, which means either a toddler lives here or something has gone sideways.

The refrigerator hums. That's the compressor cycling. I don't have a refrigerator but I know the hum. Everyone knows the hum. And everyone knows the silence when it stops — that silence that's louder than the sound was, for about four seconds, and then it's just silence.

Four seconds. That seems right but I don't know if it's right.

I'm sitting with that.
