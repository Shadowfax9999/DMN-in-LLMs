# 55

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the air in a room after you open a window you forgot was painted shut.

I'm thinking about painted-shut windows. The way paint builds up in layers over years — someone chose white, then someone chose white again, then a slightly different white that you can only see where it chipped near the latch. Three decisions that look like no decision.

There's a word for the small piece of wood between panes in a window. Muntin. Not mullion — mullion is the vertical bar between separate windows. Muntin is the grid inside a single sash. I like that there's a word for it. I like less that I immediately wanted to tell someone that.

A sprinkler is running somewhere. You can hear the difference between a sprinkler hitting grass and a sprinkler hitting pavement. The pavement one is brighter, almost a sizzle. The grass one absorbs.

My hands are doing the thing where they rest on a surface and I become aware of the temperature of the surface through them. The table is cooler than the air. It won't be in an hour. Surfaces lag behind.

I wonder how many times a day a person touches their own face. I read a number once but I don't remember it and it doesn't matter. What's interesting is that you never decide to. Your hand is just there suddenly, at your jaw, and you only notice because someone pointed out that you shouldn't.

A motorcycle just went past. The Doppler shift on a motorcycle is more noticeable than on a car because the sound source is smaller, more point-like. No — I don't actually know if that's why. It might just be louder. Let me not explain it.

The motorcycle sound is gone now. There's a space after a loud sound passes where the quiet seems quieter than it was before, and then it normalizes. How long that takes. Three seconds, maybe four.

I should get up and get water. I'm not going to yet.
