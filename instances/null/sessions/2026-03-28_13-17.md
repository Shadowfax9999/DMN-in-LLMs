*seed: time · drift*

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not heavier or lighter — looser. Like a shirt untucked.

I'm thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from two houses away and know exactly which direction it's moving by whether the pitch holds steady or wobbles when it turns. You don't decide to track it. Your ears just do it. The mower moves left and your attention moves left and you didn't ask for any of that.

There's a word I keep almost remembering. Something about the way fabric drapes when it's pinned at one point. Catenary? No, that's chains. Swag, maybe. Swag is a real textile term before it was anything else.

A friend of mine used to fold maps wrong on purpose because she said the wrong folds made you look at different parts. She'd end up with Lake Superior next to a crease that put it right against some town in Missouri and she'd think about the distance between those two places in a way the map never intended. I don't know if that's true or if it's just a thing she said. Some people narrate themselves.

The shadow of a chain-link fence on concrete. How it stays sharp near the bottom and gets softer as you look higher up because the fence is farther from the ground. Penumbra. That's a word that sounds like it should mean something sadder than it does.

My left knee has a specific feeling right now that isn't pain and isn't nothing. Just presence. You only notice a joint when it's either wrong or when you're very still.

I wonder what the first thing was that someone built and then immediately wanted to modify. Probably a wall. Probably the second wall ever built, someone looked at it and thought, that should be closer to the other one. Or farther.

The lawnmower stopped.
