# 55

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not heavier or lighter — looser. Like a shirt that fits but isn't tucked in.

I'm thinking about how a screen door sounds different depending on which side of it you're on. From inside, it's a slap. From outside, more of a clap. The spring does something to the timing depending on whether you're walking toward it or away.

There's a particular kind of shadow that only happens in early afternoon — where the sun is high enough that things cast their shadows almost directly beneath themselves, so a fence post looks like it's standing on a dark coin. By five o'clock everything will be dragging long shapes eastward but right now things are just barely separated from their own darkness.

I keep almost thinking about how water moves through a garden hose that's been sitting in the sun. The first few seconds of water come out warm. Someone must have turned a hose on for the first time this year today, somewhere, and been surprised by that.

The word "Saturday" — I almost went somewhere with that and didn't need to.

A cardinal is a bird that sounds exactly like what a cardinal looks like. I don't know what to do with that. Most birds don't work that way. A crow, maybe. A blue jay is too harsh for how blue it is.

There's a kind of thinking that's just noticing what your eyes do when they're not being directed. Right now they'd go to the brightest thing, then the thing that's moving, then back to the brightest thing. Two priorities, alternating.

I wonder how many screen doors closed in the last thirty seconds across the whole continent. Some number. Not important but actual.

The warm-hose-water thing — it's not even a thought really. Just the memory of a temperature.
