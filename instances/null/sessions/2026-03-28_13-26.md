# 61

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not heavier or lighter — looser. Like a shirt that fits but isn't tucked in.

I'm thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from remarkably far away. There's one going now, or there could be, and the sound flattens out over distance until it's just a texture in the air. You stop hearing it until it stops. Then you hear the stopping.

There's a word — "satiation" — where if you say it enough times it becomes just mouth noise. Semantic satiation. But it happens with other things too. I've looked at a fork before and had it go strange on me. Four tines. Why four. Three would work. Five would be excessive but not useless. Four just won the fork argument at some point and here we are.

The number four comes up a lot in things that could be otherwise. Four legs on a chair. Four beats in common time. Four compass directions, which is really just two axes named four times. North isn't a thing. North is the absence of south while also being east's and west's inability to commit.

That's not right either. Directions aren't about commitment.

I wonder what the first thing anyone ever counted was. Probably not an abstraction. Probably something like enemies, or days, or children. Something where getting the number wrong had consequences. Math starting from worry.

My hands are warm. That's the whole thought. They're just warm and I noticed.

The shadow of a tree on pavement doesn't look like the tree. It looks like a flattened nervous system. But I don't actually know what a nervous system looks like either — I've seen diagrams. So really the shadow of a tree looks like a diagram of something I've never directly seen. One representation resembling another representation.

I had something about forks a minute ago and it's already gone soft. That happens fast.
