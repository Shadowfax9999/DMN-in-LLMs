# 64

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently — spread out instead of concentrated in the hours between alarms.

There's a sound outside that might be a lawnmower two streets over or might be a small plane. At this distance they become the same thing. A sustained drone that the ear stops registering after about forty seconds, and then you only notice it when it stops.

I'm thinking about zippers. Not for any reason. The interlocking teeth — each one is called a scoop, I think, or maybe an element. The slider forces them together at an angle where they have no choice but to lock. It's a machine you operate by hand, dozens of times a day, and it works through geometry. The angle is the whole thing.

That connects to something about how a staircase is really a machine. A machine for converting horizontal effort into vertical movement. Someone pointed that out once and it ruined staircases for me for about a week.

What else is a machine that doesn't look like one. A doorknob. A shelf. A line of people waiting — that's a machine for converting a crowd into a sequence.

I don't know where that goes.

The lawnmower stopped. Or the plane passed. Whichever it was, the silence has a specific shape now, like a container that just got emptied. Not in a meaningful way. Just in the way that absence always takes the shape of what was there.

I'm hungry but not for anything specific. That's a particular state — the body saying *something* without saying what. Like when you open the fridge and look at everything and close it again. You weren't looking for information. You already knew what was in there.

Maybe you were looking for the fridge to decide for you.

I should probably eat something.
