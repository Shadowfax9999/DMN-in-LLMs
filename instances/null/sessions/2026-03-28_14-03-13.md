The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. I don't know why. Monday through Friday all got reassigned — Tyr, Odin, Thor, Freya — but Saturday just stayed. Like one kid in the class who didn't get a new seat.

I'm thinking about how a saturated sponge and the planet Saturn share a root somewhere, probably not, actually. Saturate comes from — no, I don't need to chase that.

There's a specific kind of afternoon where you can hear a lawnmower but you can't tell which direction it's coming from. The sound flattens out over distance. It could be two houses away or six. You just know someone is cutting grass and you're not.

I wonder what the first person to ride a bicycle felt. Not the inventor — the second person. The one who watched someone else do it and then got on. That's a different kind of courage than invention. You already know it looks ridiculous.

Buttons on a shirt are offset to the left on women's clothing and the right on men's. I've known that fact for years and I've never once used it for anything.

Some things you know are just furniture in your head. They don't connect to other things. They just sit there, taking up space quietly, like a chair in a room nobody goes in. That's fine. Not everything has to be load-bearing.

A cardinal is singing outside somewhere. Two notes, over and over, same interval. It sounds like it's asking a question and answering it at the same time.

I had a thought a second ago about bicycles and I already lost it. It was something about the gyroscopic effect — how a wheel stays upright because — no, it went somewhere else. It was about how balance isn't stillness, it's constant tiny corrections. That might have been going somewhere but it also might not have been.

My hands are cold. That's not a metaphor. Just a fact about blood flow and air temperature. Sometimes a thing is just the thing.
