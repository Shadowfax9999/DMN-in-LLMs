# 57

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More lateral. The hours don't stack the same way.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere I can't see. Just the sound of it — that ticking rotation, then the pause at the end of the arc before it swings back. I've never thought about what happens at that pause. The water keeps leaving the nozzle but the head is reversing direction, so there must be a moment where the spray bunches up, falls shorter, a thick line in the grass where it gets more than its share.

My left thumb has a callus I don't remember earning. The skin there is slightly yellow and doesn't have the same give as the rest of the pad. I keep pressing it with my other thumb like I'm testing fruit.

A cardinal is doing that thing where it sounds like it's asking a question and then answering it. Two notes down, one note up. Down down up. I wonder if it ever gets tired of the script or if tired isn't a thing that applies. Probably not in the way I mean it.

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's as far as I'll go with that.

Someone on the next block is using a circular saw. It has that rising pitch when the blade meets the wood and then the relaxing back down when it pushes through. You can hear the thickness of what they're cutting by how long the high note lasts. Short means trim board. Long means something structural.

I had a thought a second ago that left before I could look at it. Something about the sprinkler and the saw and how they're both rotating things making different sounds. But it's gone now and I don't think it was going anywhere important.

The shadow of the gutter is about two feet out from the house. By four o'clock it'll be under the bushes. I don't know why I'm tracking that. Something to do with having nowhere to be.
