# 92

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a kind of weight to it that the other days don't. Not meaning-weight. Mouth-weight. Three syllables but you never say all of them.

Sat-ur-day. Sat-er-dee. Sadderdee.

I'm thinking about how a screen door sounds different depending on whether it's the front or back of a house. I don't know if that's true. It feels true. The back door has a looser spring maybe. A more casual slam.

There's a particular quality to afternoon light when it comes through a window that faces south and hits a wooden floor that's been walked on for decades. The finish is gone in the paths between rooms. The bare wood is paler than the finished wood, which seems backward — you'd think wear would darken things. But no. The varnish is what's dark. Under it the wood has been slowly bleaching.

I wonder how long a floor has to go unwalked before you can't tell where the paths were.

A cardinal is doing that thing where it sounds like a car alarm written for one note. Over and over. Neurologists would call that perseveration if a person did it. In a bird it's just Tuesday. Saturday. Whatever.

Buttons. I was thinking about buttons earlier, or maybe I'm inventing the memory of thinking about buttons. The four-hole kind. Why four? Two would hold it. One would hold it if the thread wrapped enough. Four is redundant in a way that feels deeply human — not engineering so much as worry. What if two fail. A small, sewn-on anxiety.

The shadow of a chain-link fence on concrete makes a pattern that looks like it should mean something in mathematics. All those interlocking diamonds stretching and compressing as the sun moves. There's probably a name for that kind of projected geometry but I don't need to look it up.

My foot's asleep. The left one. That fizzing feeling that isn't quite pain and isn't quite pleasure. It's its own category. English doesn't have a word for sensations that are unpleasant but also somehow interesting.

I should move my foot.

I'm not going to yet.
