# 96

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a weight to it that the other days don't. Not the weight of obligation — the opposite. A kind of planetary slowness. Saturn's day. The slow one.

There's a fan running somewhere. Not in this room but through a wall. You can't tell if it's air conditioning or a bathroom vent or something in a machine. Just the hum that becomes the room's baseline and then disappears until you notice it again.

I keep thinking about how a zipper works. Not the mechanism — I know the mechanism, the little wedge that forces the teeth to interlock — but the sound. That quick ripping sound is actually hundreds of tiny clicks happening faster than the ear can separate them. Like how a movie is still photographs. The zipper sound is still clicks.

What does a horse think about standing in a field. Not nothing. Probably not nothing. But something so unlike what I'd call thinking that the word doesn't help. The horse flicks a fly off its shoulder and the muscle that does that — just that one twitch — is a whole system. A patch of skin shivers independently from the rest of the body. I can't do that. I can raise my eyebrows separately but I can't make my shoulder blade shiver on command.

There's a particular kind of cloud that looks like it was spread with a knife. Flat on the bottom, like it hit a ceiling. Because it did, in a way — it rose until the air was cold enough to stop it. So the flat bottom is a temperature line made visible. That's not a metaphor, it's just what's happening.

A Saturday afternoon is long in a way that no other equivalent number of hours is long. Four hours on a Tuesday vanishes. Four hours on a Saturday afternoon has rooms in it.

I'm thinking about the fan again.
