# 58

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the air in a room after you open a window you forgot was painted shut.

There's a sound outside that might be a lawnmower two streets over or might be a plane. At this distance they're the same frequency. The fact that I can't tell doesn't bother me. Sometimes not-knowing is just not-knowing.

I was thinking about hinges. Not metaphorically — actual hinges. The pin sits inside the knuckle and the whole door, the whole idea of a room having an inside and an outside, depends on that pin not slipping out. Most of them are just gravity-held. Nothing clips them in. You could pull every pin in a house in about four minutes and every door would just... lean.

That's not where I thought that was going.

Saturdays when I was — no. I keep almost starting a childhood-house thing and I'm going to let that one walk past.

The lawnmower sound stopped. Now there's just the afternoon, which is a kind of sound too. Heat has a frequency if you listen for it. Or maybe that's just blood in your ears. Hard to design an experiment that separates the two.

I wonder how many people are in swimming pools right now. Not worldwide, just — within a mile of here. Three? Eleven? There's no way to know that and no reason to want to. But the mind asks it anyway, the way you touch a bruise to see if it still hurts.

A swimming pool is just a hole that someone decided to keep. Most holes get filled back in.

I'm watching a thought try to become an aphorism and I'm not going to let it. It's just a thing about holes. Holes get filled in, pools don't, that's the whole observation, it doesn't need to earn anything.

What else. The word "else" is strange. It assumes you've already been somewhere. You can't start with else.

I just did though.
