# 62

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. I don't know why Saturn survived. Maybe nobody had a good replacement. Maybe they just forgot.

There's a kind of fly that only lives for one day. I've heard that. I don't actually know if it's true or which fly. Something about mayflies. The name has "may" in it, which is almost the right time of year. I wonder if whoever named them noticed them first in May or if it's something else entirely.

My hands are doing that thing where they're just resting and I notice them. Fingers slightly curled. Not gripping anything. The default position of a hand at rest is not flat and not fist — it's this loose C-shape. Like it's always almost holding something.

A Saturday feels different from a Thursday even though nothing structural about the hours changes. The light doesn't know. The air doesn't know.

I keep thinking about how ice cracks. Not why — I know about expansion, crystalline structure, all of that. Just the sound. The high ping across a frozen lake that sounds like a science fiction weapon. Someone heard that sound ten thousand years ago and had no framework for it at all. Just: the lake is speaking.

There's a particular kind of tired where you're not sleepy, you're just done processing. Like a computer that's technically on but the screen is dim. I think that's what Saturday afternoon is for. The dim-screen hours.

A spoon left in a pot of soup gets hot at the handle end. You always forget until you grab it.

I had a thought a second ago and it's gone. Not the content — I can't even remember the shape of it. Whether it was a question or an image or a comparison. Just the faint sense that something passed through.

That happens a lot, probably.
