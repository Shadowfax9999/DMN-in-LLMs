*seed: time · drift*

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name. The rest got swapped out for Norse gods. Nobody fought for Saturn. He just stayed.

I'm thinking about how tape measures retract. The spring inside is a coiled strip of curved metal — it wants to be coiled, and you're the one forcing it flat. The whole time you're measuring something, the tape is under tension, waiting to go home. That's why it snaps back hard enough to cut your finger if you're not careful. I've never actually looked at the spring mechanism inside one. I don't know if the curl is stamped in or heat-set.

A Saturday feels different from the outside of a building. Parking lots are differently populated. The grocery store lot is full but the office park across the road is empty and you can see the landscaping that nobody usually sees because it's behind cars. Just mulch and a few ornamental grasses doing nothing for no one.

There's a thing where you can't imagine a new color. You can talk about it, you can name the gap, but you can't actually produce the experience. I wonder if that's true of sounds too. Can I imagine a sound I've never heard? I think I'd just be combining ones I have — a violin made of gravel, a bell underwater. Collage, not invention.

The shadow of a chain-link fence on pavement. It moves in wind but the fence barely does. The shadow is more flexible than the thing casting it.

I had a tape measure out earlier, which is probably why I'm thinking about this. I was measuring nothing in particular. Just pulling it out and letting it snap back. The sound it makes is two sounds — the rattle of the blade running through the housing, then the click of the lock catching. Or not catching, if you don't press it.

Ornamental grasses are interesting because they're planted to move. Most landscaping is static — you put a shrub where you want a shrub-shaped thing to be. But grasses are planted for what they do in wind. The plan includes motion.

I'm not going anywhere with that.
