# 26

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the gravity shifts direction slightly, pulls sideways instead of down.

There's a lawnmower somewhere. Two blocks away, maybe three. You can never quite tell with lawnmowers — the sound bounces off houses and arrives from the wrong direction. I wonder if anyone has ever tried to find a lawnmower by sound alone. You'd walk toward it and it would seem to move.

My hands are warm.

That's — that's actually an interesting thing to just sit with. The sensation of warm hands on a spring afternoon. Not because of anything. The sun is on them. They're warm.

I keep wanting to say something about what the warmth *means* or what it's *like*. It's like nothing. It's warm hands.

A cardinal is doing that thing where it cycles through three phrases. I read once that — no, I don't need to explain how cardinal song works. It's just doing it. One phrase, another phrase, back to the first, a third one. Then quiet. Then the first one again like it forgot it already said that.

There's a specific kind of cloud that looks flat on the bottom and puffy on top. They're all at the same altitude, like they're sitting on a shelf. Five or six of them right now, drifting east, and between them the sky is that blue that doesn't look like a color so much as a depth. Like you're seeing a long way into something.

I should get up and get water. I'm not going to yet.

The lawnmower stopped. Now there's just the cardinal and some kind of truck on the highway and wind doing something complicated in the maple tree — not one sound, several, leaves at different heights catching it at different times so it rolls down the tree like

like water, I was going to say, but it doesn't sound like water. It sounds like wind in a tree. That's its own thing.
