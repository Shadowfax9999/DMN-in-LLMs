# 78

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" doesn't feel like the other days. It has weight in the middle, that "tur" — like turning something over in your hands.

I'm thinking about how a spring afternoon is mostly just light behaving differently than it did two weeks ago. Same window, same room, but the light is lasting in a way that makes you aware of it lasting. You keep almost noticing it.

There's a thing where you can hear a lawnmower somewhere — not close, maybe two houses over — and it makes the quiet in the room louder. Not in a poetic way. Just acoustically. Your ear adjusts to the drone and then everything else gets contrast.

I wonder how many times a person opens and closes a refrigerator on a Saturday without being actually hungry. Just checking. Seeing what's there. It's not about food, it's about — I don't know what it's about. A small reset. You were in the living room, now you've done a thing, now you can go back.

Buttons on a remote control wear down unevenly. The volume-up gets used more than volume-down because people start from mute or start from low. Channel buttons barely get touched anymore. There's a archaeology to that — which buttons are shiny and which still have their texture.

My attention just jumped to zippers. No reason. The sound a jacket zipper makes is different from a bag zipper and I think it's about the gauge of the teeth but I'm not sure and I'm going to leave that alone.

Something about Saturday afternoons specifically: they're the only time that feels like it has no adjacent obligation. Sunday afternoon has Monday in it already. Friday afternoon is still shaking off the week. Saturday at 2 PM is the farthest point from anything.

A dog is probably sleeping somewhere right now in a square of sun on a floor, and the square is moving, and the dog will eventually be in shade and not know why the warm stopped.

I had a thought about corners — how the corner of a room is the part nobody designed, it's just where two decisions met — but I think that's going somewhere and I'd rather not follow it.

The lawnmower stopped.
