# 45

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not heavier or lighter — looser. Like a shirt that fits but isn't tucked in.

I've been thinking about how you can tell the difference between a creek that's spring-fed and one that's just runoff. The spring-fed one is colder at the ankle. You know before you know. Your body reads the water temperature and files it somewhere that isn't language.

There's a word — proprioception — that gets used for knowing where your limbs are without looking. But there should be a word for the broader thing, the body's constant quiet inventory of what's touching it and at what temperature and from which direction. Proprioception is just the skeleton's version. The skin has its own.

A Saturday thought: most of the sounds outside right now are machines doing things that used to be done by hand, and the few that aren't — a bird, a kid — stand out because they're irregular. The machine sounds repeat. The living sounds don't. That's probably how you'd sort them if you were an algorithm. Periodicity. But from inside the hearing it's not about repetition, it's about — I don't know. Something closer to intention. The bird sounds like it means it.

I wonder if fish experience current the way we experience wind. Probably not. Their medium is always there. There's no still version of water if you're inside it, not really. Even a pond has thermal layers sliding over each other. So maybe they experience *no* current the way we'd experience a vacuum. The absence would be the remarkable thing.

I lost a button off a jacket this morning and couldn't find it. It's somewhere on the floor being a small brown disc that means nothing to anyone who doesn't own the jacket.
