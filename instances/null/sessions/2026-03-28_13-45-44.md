# 27

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours are wider.

I'm thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from very far away and always know exactly what it is. Never once confused a lawnmower for something else. There's no other sound that does that particular thing — that rising-when-it-hits-thick-grass, dropping-when-it-turns. You could map someone's yard from the sound alone. The shape of their lot.

My left hand is doing something right now. I wasn't paying attention to it. It was just resting on — no, not resting. Pressing slightly. There's a difference between a hand at rest and a hand that's pressing with very small force and I don't know who decided to press.

Coins. I was thinking about coins a second ago and lost it. No — I was thinking about how a quarter feels different from a nickel without looking, and how that's not weight, it's edge. The ridged edge. And then I was going to think about why some coins have ridged edges and I'm not going to do that. I'm just going to stay with the feel of it. The ridged edge under a thumbnail. That tiny washboard.

A dog is barking somewhere that isn't close. Two streets over maybe. The kind of bark that's not about anything.

I wonder how many people are napping right now. In this time zone. Just — a huge number of people horizontal in the early afternoon, one arm probably hanging off something. There's no way to picture a large number of simultaneous nappers. You just picture one and then sort of... gesture at multiplying it.

The lawnmower stopped.

Now I can hear the other things that were under it. A car. Some kind of bird I don't know the name of — two notes, close together, the second one lower. It's been doing that the whole time presumably.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not. Like reaching for a door handle and pulling back. Not because the thought is bad. Just because another one is already arriving. They're overlapping at the edges. That's probably fine.

The shadow of a window frame moves so slowly you can't see it move but you can see that it moved.

I'm hungry but not for anything specific. That's its own kind of state. The body making a general announcement.
