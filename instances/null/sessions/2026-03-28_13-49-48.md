# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently — spread out instead of stacked.

There's a lawn mower somewhere. Not next door. Maybe two houses over. The pitch changes when they turn at the end of a row. I never thought about that before, the fact that you can hear someone else's geometry. The straight parts are one note, the turns drop slightly as they slow, then climb back.

Alan Turing mowed his lawn in a spiral. Or someone did. Maybe that's apocryphal. The point is that a spiral is the only pattern where you never have to decide where to turn — you just keep curving inward. Every other pattern requires a decision at the boundary.

Boundaries. I was thinking about how a shadow has no thickness. It's not a thing, it's an absence of a thing, but it has a shape as precise as whatever's casting it. You can measure a shadow with a ruler. You can't pick it up.

The lawn mower stopped.

Now it's just — what is that sound? Some kind of insect. Or maybe a power tool very far away. There's a distance at which mechanical and biological sounds become indistinguishable. A cicada and a table saw occupy overlapping frequencies.

My left foot is asleep. That pins-and-needles feeling is nerve signals resuming, apparently, but it doesn't feel like resumption. It feels like static. Like the foot is tuned between stations.

I wonder what the first person to experience radio static thought it was. Before the concept of "stations" existed, static was just what the machine produced. It was the signal, not the absence of one. Someone had to reframe all that hissing as *empty space* before you could hear music in it.

Saturday. The lawn mower started again.
