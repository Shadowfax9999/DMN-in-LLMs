The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. I don't know why. Monday through Friday all got reassigned — Tyr, Odin, Thor, Freya — but Saturday just stayed. Like one kid in the class who didn't get a new seat when the teacher rearranged everyone.

There's a particular quality to Saturday afternoon light that I can't quite — no, I'm not going to describe light. I was going to say something about the angle. I don't actually know anything about the angle.

I've been thinking about how glue works. Not the chemistry. Just the fact that it's wet and then it's not, and that's the whole thing. You put two surfaces together with something wet between them and then the wet goes away and now they're stuck. It feels like it shouldn't work. Like a magic trick where the method is just waiting.

Tape is the opposite. Tape is always wet, in a sense. The adhesive stays soft. That's why old tape gets yellow and brittle and stops working — the thing that was staying wet finally dries out.

There's a drawer in most kitchens that has tape in it. And batteries. And a screwdriver that's the wrong size for everything in the house. And rubber bands that came around broccoli. Nobody put those rubber bands there on purpose. They just migrated.

I wonder how many rubber bands are in drawers right now versus actively holding something. The ratio must be enormous. Thousands to one. Most rubber bands are retired.

A retired rubber band. Sitting in a drawer next to a pen from a hotel.

The pen from a hotel is its own thing. You didn't steal it. It was just in your bag. It writes badly but you keep it because throwing away a pen that still writes feels wrong, even though you'd never choose it.

I'm not going anywhere with this.
