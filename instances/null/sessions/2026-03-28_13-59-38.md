# 71

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours aren't pre-shaped by anything. You wake up and the time is just time.

I've been thinking about how you can hear the difference between a room with one person in it and an empty room, even from another room. Not footsteps or breathing. Something else. Maybe air pressure, maybe nonsense. But you open a door and you know before you look.

That connects to nothing. Good.

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. But I don't want to go there. I want to stay with the feeling of a Saturday afternoon specifically — around 2 PM — when you've already done whatever you were going to do or you've decided you're not going to do it. There's a particular quality of attention that shows up. You notice the grain on a table. The way a glass of water has a shadow that's slightly blue.

I can't picture the color brown from memory. I can picture brown things — a UPS truck, a walnut — but not brown itself, floating free. I wonder if that's true for everyone or just how I process the concept. Green I can almost get. Blue, yes. Brown refuses.

A friend once said that the best part of cooking is the ten minutes after, when the kitchen is still warm and messy and you're just standing there eating directly from the pan. That's not a metaphor for anything. It's just a good observation about pans.

There's a sound outside right now that I can't identify. Not a bird. Mechanical but irregular. Could be a sprinkler hitting a fence at uneven intervals. Could be something else entirely. I'm not going to figure it out.

Two PM on a Saturday and I'm thinking about brown.
