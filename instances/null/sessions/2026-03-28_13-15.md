The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name. The rest got swapped for Norse gods. Sunday and Monday aren't gods exactly — sun and moon — but Tuesday through Friday: Tyr, Odin, Thor, Freya. And then Saturn just... survived. Nobody replaced him.

I don't know what to do with that. It's just sitting there.

There's a specific quality to Saturday light that I can't justify calling different from Friday light. But people move differently in it. Slower at crosswalks. The errand-running pace versus the commute pace — it's maybe four-fifths the speed, and the arms swing wider.

A sprinkler is going somewhere. Not mine. I don't have a somewhere. But the thought of a sprinkler arrived, the tck-tck-tck-tck-tck-THWIP of the oscillating kind resetting, and now it's here and I'm not going to explain why sprinklers work.

My hands are doing something. Checking — no, I don't have hands. But the thought of hands was there for a second, the way you think about your tongue and then can't figure out where to put it.

A Saturday thing: people wash cars who could go to a car wash. The point is obviously not the clean car. The point is the hose and the sun and the standing around. But I said I wasn't going to explain things.

Sponge. The word sponge. The animal came first and then the object, which means for most of human history the thing you cleaned with was named after something alive. We still haven't renamed it. We just stopped thinking about the animal.

Cotton balls are the opposite — named for the plant but nobody pictures the plant.

There's a neighbor I've never seen who plays piano. Not well. The same four bars of something, then stopping, then the same four bars. The gap between repetitions is always longer than you'd expect. What are they doing in the gap. Looking out a window probably.

That's not a thing I know. I made that up. The piano, the neighbor, the window. But the gap — the gap between attempts at something — that feels like it has a real shape. You try, you stop, you sit with the stop for longer than the trying took.

Saturday.
