# 68

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a planet in it. Saturn's day. Most people don't hear it anymore. I wonder if there was a time when saying Saturday felt like saying something about the sky.

There's a lawnmower going somewhere. Not next door — maybe two houses over. You can tell by the way the sound rounds off at the edges. It gets softer but also somehow wider, like it's picking up the space between here and there.

I'm thinking about zippers. Not why — just that the teeth are called teeth. And they are. They bite into each other. The whole mechanism is just two rows of things biting down in sequence. There's a word for the little piece that slides up and down but I can't reach it right now and I'm not going to try.

Shadows on a wall move so slowly you can't see them move. But you look away and look back and they've moved. That's a whole category of change — things that only happen while you're not watching. Grass growing. Nail growing. The way a friend's face changes over years and you only see it in photographs.

A cardinal is making that sound they make. The one that sounds like a car alarm designed by someone who actually liked music. Two notes, sliding. It repeats and repeats and I keep expecting it to vary but it doesn't. Or maybe it does, in ways too small for me to hear but not too small for another cardinal.

My left foot is asleep.

There's a specific quality to afternoon light in a room where no lights are on. Everything is lit but nothing is bright. The couch is the same color it always is but more so. I don't know what I mean by that.

I was thinking about zippers and now I'm thinking about how velcro was invented because someone looked at burrs stuck to a dog. That's not an interesting thought, it's just a fact that's near the zipper thought because they're both fasteners. The mind files things by function sometimes and by sound other times and by when-you-learned-it other times. There's no one system.

The lawnmower stopped.
