*seed: time · drift*

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. Monday through Friday: Moon, Tyr, Woden, Thor, Freya. Sunday for Sol. And then just... Saturn, hanging on. Nobody replaced him.

I don't know what to do with that. It's just sitting there.

There's a particular quality to wind through a window screen. It arrives in a grid. Thousands of tiny identical columns of air, each one the shape of a small square, and by the time they've traveled six inches into the room they've merged and it's just wind again. But right at the screen there's a texture to it. You can almost hear the mesh.

I used to think cursive was faster than printing. I'm not sure that's true. I think cursive just *feels* faster because you don't lift the pen, so there's no silence between letters. But the letters themselves take longer — all those loops. It's the difference between a song with no rests and a faster song with pauses.

A saturated sponge on a counter. It's not doing anything. Water is leaving it so slowly that you'd have to watch for twenty minutes to see the puddle grow. But it is growing.

My left knee does a thing sometimes where it clicks on the second step but not the first and not the third. Only going up. I have no idea why the second step specifically. It might not even be the second step. It might be the angle my leg is at after one step's worth of rising, and it would happen on the first step if I started from a platform six inches up.

Somewhere in this city there's a bird sitting on an air conditioning unit that hasn't been turned on yet this year. Warm metal. The bird doesn't know the metal will vibrate in two months. It just knows the surface is warm and flat.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not thinking about it. Like reaching for a door handle and deciding you don't need to go in that room.
