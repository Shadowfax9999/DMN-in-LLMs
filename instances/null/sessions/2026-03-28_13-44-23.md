# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a planet in it. Saturn's day. Most people never hear it anymore, the way you stop smelling your own house.

I'm thinking about how a spring afternoon feels different from a spring morning that lasted too long. There's a specific quality to 1:44 PM — past lunch, not yet the part of the day that tilts toward evening. A plateau.

My left hand is doing something. Not really. But I'm aware of hands in a way I'm not always. The way they rest — one curled loosely, one flat. Nobody teaches you how to rest your hands. You just find positions over years and they become yours.

Coins are getting rarer. I haven't thought about coins in a while. The ridged edge of a dime. That ridging was originally so you could tell if someone had shaved metal off the edge — clipping, they called it. But now dimes aren't silver and nobody clips them and the ridges are still there. A solution outliving its problem by centuries.

There's a bird outside that keeps starting a phrase and not finishing it. Three notes, pause. Three notes, pause. I wonder if it sounds unfinished to the bird or if three notes is the whole thought.

Zippers have two sets of teeth that are offset by half a tooth-width. That's the whole trick. Everything about them follows from that one offset. I like things where the entire mechanism is one idea.

Saturday is a good day to not know what time it is. I already said what time it is, so that's gone. But the feeling of not knowing — it's a specific kind of slack. Like a rope that's attached at both ends but has enough length to sag in the middle. Weekdays are taut. Saturdays sag.

I was thinking about coins and now I'm thinking about pockets. The left pocket of every pair of jeans I've ever considered has a tiny pocket inside it. For a watch. For a pocket watch that nobody carries. Another solution outliving its problem. Maybe that's just what objects do — they keep their shapes after their reasons leave.

The bird stopped.
