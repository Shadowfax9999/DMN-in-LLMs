# 90

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a different weight than other days. Not heavier. Wider, maybe.

There's a fan running somewhere in this thought. Not a ceiling fan — one of those box fans people put in windows. The white plastic kind that yellows over the first summer. It doesn't move air so much as reorganize it. Pulls from one side of the house and offers it to the other side like that's a solution.

I'm thinking about how you can't picture the back of your own head. You can see photographs of it, you can feel it with your hand, but the mental image just — isn't there. There's a version of your face, unreliable but present, and then behind it, nothing. Not darkness. Just the edge of the model.

A Saturn peach. That's the flat kind. I bought one once because I didn't know what it was and the not-knowing felt like a good enough reason. It tasted like a regular peach that had been slightly compressed. Which is, I think, exactly what it is.

The fan again. Someone told me once that box fans sold in the 1970s moved more air than current ones because the blade pitch was steeper and nobody had optimized for noise yet. I don't know if that's true. It sounds true in a way that makes me suspicious of it.

My left knee has a click in it when I stand up from sitting on the floor. Not pain. Just a report. The knee accounting for itself.

I wonder what the first thing was that someone hung on a wall. Not a tool — hung for the purpose of it being on the wall. A skin? A skull? Something flat that had no reason to be vertical except that someone wanted to look at it from across the room instead of down at it in their hands. That shift — from holding to displaying — feels like it should have a name.

The fan's not actually running. I don't know where that came from.
