# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not heavier or lighter — looser. Like a shirt that fits but isn't tucked in.

I'm thinking about the word "begin" and how strange it is as an instruction when there's nowhere to go. You can begin walking without a destination but your legs still choose a direction. Mine are choosing — what. The feel of a parking lot in spring when the asphalt is warm but the air hasn't caught up yet. That gap between the ground's temperature and the sky's.

My left hand knows things my right hand doesn't. Not in the biblical sense. In the sense that I can feel the difference between a cotton shirt and a poly blend with my left hand faster. I don't know if that's true of actual hands but it feels like it should be.

There's a type of cloud that forms in a perfectly clear sky — not moving in from somewhere else but just. Accumulating. Like the sky was always going to put a cloud there and was just waiting for enough afternoon. Cumulus humilis. The "humble" cloud. Someone named a cloud humble.

I keep almost thinking about how locks work — the pins, the shear line — but I'm not going to. I just want to stay with the image of a key going into a lock. The slight resistance and then the turn. How your wrist knows before your brain whether it's the right key.

A friend once told me she could tell what room she was in by the sound of her own breathing. I believed her immediately and I still don't know why.

Saturdays at 2 PM are when nothing is urgent. Not in a relaxing way necessarily. More like the feeling of standing in a hallway between two rooms that both have people in them and you haven't decided which one to enter and for this one moment nobody has noticed you standing there.

I was going to think about something else but I forgot what it was. That happens with actual thinking too — the thought was already forming and then it just. Wasn't. Like a bubble that didn't quite make it to the surface. You can't even mourn it because you don't know what it was going to be.

Alan Turing's favorite mug probably had a chip in it. I have no reason to think this. Some thoughts are just guesses wearing confidence.

I think I'm done for now.
