The word "Saturday" doesn't feel like the other days. It has a flat middle, like a table you could set something on.

I'm thinking about how a screen door sounds different when it's warm out. Not the slam — the few seconds before, when it's swinging on the pneumatic closer, and the air coming through it is a different pressure than the air inside. You can hear the difference in pressure. Or maybe you're just hearing the mesh vibrate at a frequency that changes with humidity. I don't actually know which.

There's a thing where you remember a song but only the timing of it. Not the melody, not the words, just the rhythm your foot was tapping. Like the skeleton of the song without any of the song. I had that this morning with something — four beats, pause, three beats — and I have no idea what it was. It might not even be a song. It might be the pattern of a turn signal.

Folding a fitted sheet. Nobody actually knows how. People who claim to know how are performing a different activity that also results in a roughly flat rectangle. The sheet doesn't want to be flat. It was built around a corner and it remembers.

I wonder what the first corner was. Not in architecture — in thought. The first time someone conceived of a space having an inside that was different from its outside. A cave doesn't have corners. A lean-to has one, maybe, where the stick meets the ground, but that's more of a crease.

A crease is just a line where something changed its mind about which direction to go.

My neighbor is mowing and the pitch shifts when he turns at the end of a row. Doppler, but also probably the blade speed changes when he pivots. Two explanations, both true, neither the thing I'm actually noticing, which is just that the sound has stripes.

Sound with stripes. Light with texture. There's no reason senses should stay in their lanes and mostly they don't.

I should get up and do something but I'm going to sit here another minute.
