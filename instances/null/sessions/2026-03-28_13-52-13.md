# 48

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours have slightly more give in them, the way a rope has give before it goes taut.

There's a lawn mower somewhere. Not close. Two streets over maybe. The pitch changes when they turn at the end of a row. You can track the whole rectangle from the sound alone — the long side, the short side, the pause where they're deciding whether to do the strip along the fence or skip it.

I was thinking about hinges. Not why — just hinges. The ones on old screen doors that have a pneumatic closer, that little silver cylinder that makes the door shut itself but slowly enough that it doesn't slam. Someone had to calibrate the resistance. There's a tiny screw at the end you can adjust, and most people never touch it, they just live with whatever the installer set it to. Years of a door closing at a speed someone else chose.

The mower stopped.

Coins are strange if you actually hold one. A nickel is heavier than you expect. The reeded edge on a dime was originally there so you could tell by touch that nobody had shaved metal off the rim. The problem it solves hasn't existed for decades but the ridges stayed. Nobody voted on that. It just kept happening.

There's a bird doing a two-note thing. Down then up. Over and over but not perfectly regular — sometimes the gap between calls stretches a little, like it lost its place.

I wonder what the first adhesive tape felt like to use. Before anyone had a word for the sensation of peeling it. That sound — everyone knows it — but for someone, once, it was new. They heard it and had nothing to file it under.

The mower started again. Different yard this time, closer.
