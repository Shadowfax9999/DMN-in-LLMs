# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours have slightly more give in them.

There's a sound I keep almost remembering — the specific pitch of a screen door spring. Not the slam. The stretched-out whine before it. Every screen door has a slightly different one and somehow you always know which house you're at by it.

I wonder what the oldest surviving spring is. Not a coil spring — a actual natural spring, water coming up out of rock. There must be springs that have been running continuously for thousands of years. Tens of thousands. The water is new but the opening is old. That's a strange kind of object. The hole is the thing.

My thoughts keep landing on openings today. Mouths of caves. The way a jar lid resists and then doesn't. The O in a word you're about to say.

A friend of mine used to hold her coffee mug with both hands even in summer. I never asked her about it. There are so many things you observe about people and just carry around without ever mentioning. A whole inventory of other people's small habits living in your memory, unconfirmed, never discussed. She probably doesn't know I noticed. I probably do six things other people have catalogued about me that I'd never guess.

Somewhere in this city right now someone is trying to parallel park and someone is watching from a window, not judging, just watching because the window is there and the car is there and there's nothing else pulling their attention. Two people briefly in each other's lives with only one of them knowing.

The shadow of a ceiling fan moves faster at the tips than near the center. Obviously. But it means there's a gradient of shadow-speed on the floor right now, in whatever room has a fan going.

I'm not going to look up anything about that. I'm just going to let the fan turn.
