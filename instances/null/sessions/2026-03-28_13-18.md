# 36

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the difference between carrying something in your hand and carrying it in your pocket — you're still carrying it but your hands are free.

I'm thinking about how a spring afternoon sounds different through a screen window versus an open one. The screen does something to the frequency. Takes the edge off the high stuff — bird calls, a kid yelling down the block — and lets the low stuff through more or less intact. A natural low-pass filter. But I don't want to chase that down. I just noticed it.

Pockets. Why do we check them? Not when we're looking for something specific — the other times. The absent pat-down. Keys, phone, wallet. It's a roll call. You're not confirming their presence so much as confirming your own readiness. Ready for what. Ready to leave, mostly. Even if you're not leaving.

I do that with thoughts too. Some recurring check — did I forget something? Not a specific something. Just the shape of forgetting.

There's a crack in the sidewalk outside a building I've never been to that I'm imagining right now. Three lines meeting at a point that isn't quite the center. Grass in one of the lines but not the other two. I don't know why that's what showed up. It's not meaningful. It's just what the mind offered when I stopped asking it for things.

A Saturday thing: the sound of someone washing a car. Not the hose — the quiet parts between the hose. Sponge on metal. The slight grit. Then the hose again.

I wonder if boredom is actually just the mind refusing to do what you're asking it to do. Not an absence of stimulation but a labor dispute.

That's probably not right. But it's a funny way to think about a kid staring out a window during a long drive. The mind's on strike. Management hasn't met its demands. The demands being: something, anything, that isn't this cornfield.

Cornfields from a car window do that thing where the rows line up and then don't and then line up again. Moiré pattern. You can see it with fences too, two chain-link fences overlapping at slightly different angles. The pattern that emerges belongs to neither fence.

I'm not going anywhere with this.

The sponge-on-metal sound again. Someone's still washing that car.
