The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. I don't know why. Monday through Friday all got reassigned — Tiw, Woden, Thor, Freya — but Saturday just stayed. Like one kid in a school who doesn't get a new nickname.

Saturn was slow. The planet moves slow. The old god ate his children, which is its own kind of slowness — trying to stop time by consuming what comes after you.

I'm thinking about how a Saturday afternoon feels different from a Saturday morning. Morning has the shape of the week still in it, the relief. By afternoon that's burned off and it's just — open. Unstructured time. Which is harder than it sounds.

There's a specific quality to light through a window you're not looking out of. You're aware of it changing on the wall. It moves faster than you think it should. You look up and the parallelogram on the floor has shifted a full foot and you didn't notice it happening, only that it happened.

A parallelogram. Not a rectangle. Because the sun is never perfectly perpendicular to anything in your house. Everything the sun projects indoors is slightly skewed. I've never seen anyone draw this accurately — in illustrations, window-light on floors is always a clean rectangle.

What else gets drawn wrong by convention. Smoke rises in a column in drawings. Real smoke does something much more complicated — it's laminar for the first inch or two, then breaks into turbulence, and the transition point is chaotic. Every candle is doing fluid dynamics that would take serious computation to model, just sitting there on a table.

I can hear a lawnmower somewhere. Not next door — maybe two or three houses away. At that distance you lose the engine's texture and just get the pitch. It comes and goes as they turn at the ends of rows. The pattern tells you the shape of their yard without seeing it. Long passes, short turns — rectangular. If the passes kept changing length you'd know there was a curve somewhere, a garden bed, an odd lot line.

That's interesting — reconstructing a shape from a sound pattern. Bats do something like this but faster and on purpose.

The lawnmower stopped. Now there's just the neighborhood underneath it. Birds doing what they do. A car door somewhere.

I had a thought about Saturn eating his children and I lost it. Something about how weekends feel like stolen time. But that's not quite what it was. It's gone.
