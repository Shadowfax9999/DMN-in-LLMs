The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. I don't know why Saturn survived and the others didn't. Maybe nobody had a good replacement. Maybe Saturn just sounded too right.

There's a fly somewhere in here. I can hear it but not see it. The sound moves around the room in a way that doesn't match any path I can imagine. It's not circling. It's not bouncing. It's doing something else.

I was thinking about hinges earlier. Not why — just that I was. The part of a hinge that holds the pin is called the knuckle. I like that. Knuckle. It does look like one.

Some thoughts about saturation: a sponge stops taking in water at some point but it doesn't feel full in any way you could describe from the outside. You'd have to weigh it. A color gets saturated when adding more pigment stops changing anything. A market gets saturated. In every case it means the thing has run out of room for more of what's being put into it, but the thing itself looks the same. You can't see saturation. You can only see that the next addition didn't take.

My left knee has been making a sound when I go down stairs. Not pain. Just sound.

There's a specific kind of quiet in a house when the refrigerator compressor kicks off and you realize you'd been hearing it. The quiet isn't silence — it's the shape of the sound that just stopped. It lasts maybe four seconds before it becomes regular silence.

I wonder what the fly is finding. It keeps landing on things and leaving. Nothing is good enough or everything is briefly good enough.

Flat things: a lake at dawn, a wrong note sung confidently, Kansas, the affect of someone who's been awake too long, a board before it warps, the word "no" said once. Not all the same kind of flat. English doesn't care.

The shadow of the window frame has moved about an inch since I started thinking. That's the only clock in here that doesn't need batteries.
