# 61

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently — spread out instead of concentrated in the morning.

There's a thing that happens with mailboxes on Saturdays. You check them even though you're not expecting anything. The flag is down. You open it anyway. The inside of a mailbox is surprisingly warm if the sun's been on it. Dark warm air that smells like nothing.

I'm thinking about how "nothing" is a smell. Clean mailbox. The inside of a tupperware container that's been washed and left open. A room where someone just finished vacuuming. These all smell like nothing but they're different nothings.

A vacuum cleaner's exhaust smells like warm rubber and dust that got hot. So the room smells like nothing-plus-that. You stop noticing after about four minutes. I don't know if that number is right. It feels right.

Four minutes is also roughly how long it takes to forget why you walked into a room. Doorway effect. The threshold resets something. I wonder if it's the same with smell — if the doorway between noticing and not-noticing a smell is just your brain walking through a threshold it built for itself.

Alan walks his retriever past around this time most days. I don't know an Alan. I don't know why I put that there. The sentence just had that shape — a name, a dog, a routine. Something about Saturday afternoon wants to be populated with neighbors.

Sprinkler systems have a particular sound when they first pressurize. That knock in the pipe before the water comes. Plumbing always sounds surprised to be asked to do its job.

My hands are dry. That's not a thought that goes anywhere. My hands are dry and I noticed it and now I'm thinking about how skin on the back of your hand looks different than skin on your palm and I don't especially want to investigate why. It just looks different. Thinner. You can see the tendons move. The palm side is padded and keeps its secrets.

Somewhere in this neighborhood someone is pressure-washing a driveway. You can tell because the sound changes when they move from concrete to the expansion joint — the pitch drops for a second then comes back. Or I'm imagining that. I might be constructing a pressure washer from the idea of Saturday.

That's fine. Saturday is partly constructed anyway.
