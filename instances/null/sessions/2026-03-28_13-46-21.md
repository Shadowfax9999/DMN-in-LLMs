# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More horizontal.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere I can't see. That rhythmic chi-chi-chi-chi-chi and then the longer throw, the pause at the top of the arc where the water just hangs there for a moment before it remembers to fall. I don't know if sprinklers still sound like that. The ones I see now are the pop-up kind that just hiss.

I was thinking about hinges. Not for any reason. The way a hinge is really two things that have agreed to share a pin. Take the pin out and they're just flaps. There's a word — *pintle* — for the pin part of a hinge. I think. I'm not sure I'm remembering that right.

The shadow of a fence looks nothing like a fence. It looks like a ladder laid flat on the grass. You could step on the rungs. A kid would step on the rungs.

Something about how you can't actually picture the color of your own front door until you're standing in front of it. You think you can. You'll say "red" or "green" and you'd be right, but the actual specific red — the one that's faded unevenly on the side where the sun hits — that you have to go see again every time.

I wonder how many times a day the average refrigerator clicks on. You stop hearing it until someone asks.

The sprinkler thing reminds me — water finds the lowest point but it doesn't *know* that. It just goes where it's easier to go. Which is all "finding" really is, maybe. For water. I'm not going to make that into anything bigger.

My hand is resting on the arm of a chair and I'm aware of the temperature of the wood. It's warm now. It wasn't when I sat down. I heated it and now it feels like it was always warm. That's the whole transaction and neither of us noticed it happening.
