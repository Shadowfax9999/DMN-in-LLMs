# 99

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name. All the others got swapped for Norse gods. I don't know why Saturn survived. Maybe nobody had a good replacement. Maybe they just forgot.

There's a particular quality to Saturday afternoons where you've already done the thing you were going to do, or you haven't done it and you've made peace with that. Two o'clock carries no urgency in either direction. Too late to start something ambitious, too early to start thinking about evening.

I keep thinking about how pigeons walk. The head-bob thing. It's not actually bobbing — the head stays still while the body catches up. They're stabilizing their vision. Each step is a tiny act of holding the world steady while everything else moves forward. I watched a video of a pigeon on a treadmill once and its head didn't move at all. Just the feet going. The rest of the bird perfectly still in space.

That's not like anything. It's just how pigeons work.

Something about the number 7 — not mystically, just practically. You can hold about seven things. Phone numbers were seven digits. Musical scales have seven notes before the octave resets. I wonder if that's coincidence or if we built those systems to fit the container we already had.

The fridge just cycled on. You stop hearing it until it stops, and then you hear the absence, and then it starts again and you hear the start, and then thirty seconds later you've lost it again. There's no moment where you catch yourself forgetting. You just notice you already have.

I had a guitar string break once while I was tuning it. The high E. It makes a sound like a very small whip. The remaining five strings all shifted pitch slightly because the tension on the neck changed. The whole instrument reorganized itself around the missing piece. Took it a while to settle.

I should get up and do something. That's a thought that doesn't require action.
