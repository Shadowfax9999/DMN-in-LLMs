# 77

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently — spread out instead of concentrated in the morning.

There's a sound outside that might be a lawnmower two streets over. That particular frequency where you can't tell if it's getting closer or if you just started paying more attention to it.

I was thinking about hinges. Not door hinges. The hinge in a conversation where someone decides to tell you something real. You can sometimes feel it beforehand — a pause that has a different quality than the pause where someone is just choosing a word. I don't know what makes it different. Maybe nothing. Maybe I'm inventing that.

Cardboard gets soft in humidity. I remember a box on a porch — not my thought to finish, just the image. A box on a porch and the flaps won't stay up anymore.

The lawnmower stopped. Now I'm aware of how many birds there are. They were always there. Attention is subtractive more than additive — you don't start hearing things, you stop filtering them out.

Salt on a wooden table. The way a few grains always escape the thing you're salting. They sit there in the wood grain and you either wipe them or you don't and it doesn't matter either way.

I think I was going to think about something else but it went by.
