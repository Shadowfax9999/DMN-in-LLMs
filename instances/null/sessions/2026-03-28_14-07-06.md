# 95

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a planet in it. Saturn's day. I forget that most of the week belongs to celestial bodies and then there's Wednesday just sitting there with Woden in it like a splinter of a different system entirely.

Fourteen oh six. Military time always makes me think of someone standing very straight. Regular time slouches. It's two in the afternoon and the day is doing that thing where it feels both early and late simultaneously — too late to start anything ambitious, too early to give up on the idea of starting something ambitious.

A spring afternoon. There's a lawnmower somewhere. There's always a lawnmower somewhere on a Saturday in spring. You can't confirm this but it feels statistically inevitable.

I'm thinking about how a shadow moves across a floor over the course of an afternoon and nobody watches it happen but if you look twice, an hour apart, it's moved. Like a clock you can't catch ticking. The shadow of a window frame on a kitchen floor. It bends where the floor meets the cabinet.

Bending. Light doesn't actually bend there, the surface does. The shadow is accurate. It's the floor that's lying about being flat.

I had a thought about saturating and lost it. Something about how a sponge and a Saturday afternoon have a similar quality — there's a maximum amount of nothing they can hold. After that the nothing starts to feel like something. Restlessness, maybe. Or the sponge drips.

That wasn't quite it either.

A fly keeps landing on the same spot on a window. I don't know why flies do that. I'm not going to look it up. The fly has its reasons or it doesn't have reasons and either way it's landing on that spot again.

I wonder what the second-most-used door in a house is. The front door isn't the most used — that's usually the garage door or the back door. So the front door is already second or third. Strange for something that's architecturally presented as the main event. Most front doors are like a formal living room. Technically the entrance. Functionally a display.

Two fifteen now probably. Or two twelve. I'm not checking.
