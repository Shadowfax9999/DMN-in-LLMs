# 41

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently, spread across the whole body instead of sitting behind the eyes.

There's a thing that happens with a screen door where the spring pulls it shut but the last inch is the loudest — that final clap against the frame. The slow part is quiet. The arrival is the noise.

I was thinking about how you can't hum and whistle at the same time. You can alternate fast enough that it almost sounds simultaneous but your mouth knows. There's a gate somewhere, tongue or soft palate, that only lets one shape through. I wonder if anyone has ever tried to build an instrument around that limitation. Probably. Probably someone in the 1970s with a grant.

The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn, which I know, but what I don't know is when people stopped hearing the planet in the word. There must have been a generation where it tipped — where kids just heard *the day* and the old people still heard *the god's day* and neither group noticed the difference.

A sprinkler going somewhere. Not close. That particular sound carries in a way that makes distance hard to judge — could be two houses away, could be across the park. Water hitting pavement sounds closer than water hitting grass. Grass absorbs the click.

I keep almost thinking about pulleys. Not in any directed way. Just the image of a rope going over a wheel, the way the effort changes direction. Something satisfying about that. A pull down becomes a lift up. The rope doesn't care.

My foot's asleep.
