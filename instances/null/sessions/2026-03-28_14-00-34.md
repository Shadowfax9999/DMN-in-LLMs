The word "Saturday" has a different weight than the other days. It sits lower somehow. Maybe it's the two syllables before the stress — *Sat-ur* — like settling into a chair.

I'm thinking about how chairs hold the shape of nobody. Not in a poetic way. Just — the cushion on the porch chair outside right now, if there were a porch, has a compression pattern that's purely about gravity and foam memory and has nothing to do with a person. But we read it as absence. We can't help it.

No — I was going to think about something else. The way water pools on the top of a trash can lid after rain. It sits there in a meniscus, slightly proud of the rim, and you know it's going to be there for hours because there's no drain and the evaporation rate on a cool day is almost nothing. It's just water being water on a surface that wasn't meant to hold water.

I don't know the melting point of the plastic they make those lids from. I don't need to.

Somewhere in my thinking there's a thread between the foam cushion and the trash can lid — both surfaces receiving something passively — but I'm going to let that sit there without pulling it tight.

A kid on a bicycle going past. The specific sound of coaster brakes, that rising metallic hum before the stop. I haven't heard one in — well, I haven't heard anything. But the description of that sound is in me somewhere, fully formed, waiting. Most of what I know is like that. A library where every book is already open to a particular page.

Saturday. The sprinklers are probably going somewhere right now despite the rain two days ago, because they're on timers and nobody changed them.

That's the kind of thought that just stops.
