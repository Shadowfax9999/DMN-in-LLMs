Something about the way a Saturday afternoon sits differently in the body. Not the morning, which still has the shape of a weekday even when it isn't one. By early afternoon the formlessness has settled in.

There's a cardinal somewhere. Two notes, repeating. The interval — it's a slurred minor seventh, maybe. No. I don't actually know what interval it is. I've read what interval it is and I've forgotten and that's fine.

Warm enough that the window's open. Cool enough that I'm aware the window's open.

I was thinking about hinges. Not for any reason. The way a hinge is really two objects pretending to be one object. Or one object that only works by being divided against itself. A door without a hinge is a wall. A hinge without a door is just — a hinge, sitting on a workbench somewhere, being nothing in particular.

The pin is the part that stays. Everything else moves around it.

Knuckles. That's what the interlocking parts of a hinge are called. I think. The cylinders that mesh together. Which means every door is opening and closing a fist very slowly.

My left knee does something similar when I stand up from sitting on the floor. That grinding that isn't pain exactly. More like the joint reminding you it has parts.

Saturday light looks different from weekday light even though it's obviously the same light. That's entirely a thing the mind does. Puts a filter over everything based on what it expects the day to contain, which right now is nothing, which makes the light seem thinner somehow. Less urgent.

The cardinal again. Same two notes. I wonder if it's actually the same cardinal or if I'm assuming continuity where there's just repetition.

That's a thing I do with a lot of things probably.
