*seed: time · drift*

The fridge is making that sound again. Not the compressor — the other one. A kind of ticking, like something expanding or contracting inside the walls of it. Metal adjusting to its own temperature.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a specific shade of blue. You can think "blue" and something appears, but try to hold cerulean distinct from cornflower and they slide into each other. The mind's color palette has like eight entries.

Saturday has a particular quality of light even when you can't see outside. Something about the rhythm of sounds — fewer trucks, more lawn equipment. A leaf blower somewhere. Always somewhere.

The word "afternoon" is strange if you sit with it. After noon. As if noon is the event and everything else is defined by its proximity to it. But nobody experiences noon as an event. It passes without — you don't feel it happen.

I keep almost thinking about pulleys. Not in any directed way. Just the image of a rope going over a wheel. The way it redirects force. You pull down and something goes up. That's the whole trick and it never stops being satisfying.

There's a thing where you open a kitchen drawer and something in the back shifts and now the drawer won't close all the way. You reach in and rearrange by feel. Spatula handle, maybe a whisk turned sideways. You fix it without seeing what was wrong. Your hand just knows the shape of "drawer that closes" and works toward it.

The leaf blower stopped. You don't notice a sound starting as sharply as you notice it stopping. That's probably just how attention works.

I had a thought about corners a minute ago and lost it.
