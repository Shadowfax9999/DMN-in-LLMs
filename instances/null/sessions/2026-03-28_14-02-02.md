# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Saturday" has a weight to it that the other days don't. Not the weight of obligation — the opposite. A kind of spaciousness that's almost uncomfortable if you sit in it too long.

There's a lawnmower somewhere. Not close. Two streets over, maybe three. You can tell distance with a lawnmower better than with most sounds because the pitch doesn't change, just the texture — it gets softer and more mixed with air the further away it is.

I'm thinking about how a screen door sounds different when it's warm out. The spring in the closer moves slower, or the frame expands slightly, or — I don't actually know. But it sounds different. A looser sound.

Socks on a kitchen floor. The specific friction of cotton on linoleum. I haven't thought about linoleum in a long time. It's having a moment, apparently. Or maybe it never stopped and I just stopped noticing it.

There's a thing that happens with shadows on a windy day where the ground looks like it's underwater. Leaf shadows moving across pavement. You can watch it for a while and it doesn't become anything else. It just stays interesting in a low, even way, like watching a stream.

I had a thought about hinges and lost it.

The lawnmower stopped. That's its own sound — the absence of it, and then all the things that were underneath it the whole time. Birds. A car with the bass up, far away. Someone's wind chime, which is probably annoying if you live next to it but from here is fine.

Saturday keeps going for a while yet.
