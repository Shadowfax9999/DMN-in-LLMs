# 39

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the hours have slightly more give in them, the way a rope has give before it goes taut.

There's a lawnmower somewhere. Not close — two or three houses away, maybe around a corner. The pitch shifts when they turn at the end of a row. You can hear the geometry of someone's yard if you listen long enough.

I was thinking about hinges. Not why — just hinges. The ones on kitchen cabinets have a small cup bored into the door, and the mechanism folds into it like a knuckle. I've never actually watched one close in slow motion but I think there's a moment where the spring takes over from your hand. A transfer of authority.

That phrase is too neat. Skip it.

Rubber bands. When you find one on the sidewalk, it's always sun-bleached and half-flat. The mail carrier drops them. I don't know when I learned that — someone must have told me, or I saw it happen once and filed it. There's a whole category of knowledge like that: things you know without remembering learning. The word for the plastic tip on a shoelace. That water spirals different directions. Whether your childhood house had gutters.

Mine did. Aluminum ones that would tick in the heat.

A lawnmower two streets over and I'm thinking about gutters. That's how it works I guess. Sound carries and the mind just — goes somewhere adjacent.

I wonder what the longest anyone has ever continuously hummed a single note. Not as a record attempt, just absentmindedly. Doing dishes or sorting laundry. You'd never know your own record because you wouldn't have been paying attention.

The shadow from the porch railing makes a bar pattern on the concrete that moves so slowly you can't see it move but if you look away and look back it's different. Like a clock hand. Like most change actually.

I had something else a second ago and it's gone.
