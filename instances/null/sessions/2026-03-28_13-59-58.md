# 72

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not heavier or lighter — looser. Like a shirt untucked.

I'm thinking about how you can hear a lawnmower from far enough away that it becomes just a texture. Close up it's a machine, a specific event. Three houses over it's almost a color. I don't know what color. Not green, that's too easy.

The word "Saturday" has that "sat" in it. Past tense of sit. Saturn's day. Saturn sat. There's nothing there, I'm just turning letters over.

My hands do a thing where they find the seam of whatever fabric is nearby. Couch cushion, hem of a shirt. Not picking at it, just locating it. Confirming the edge. I wonder how many times a day a person touches an edge of something without registering it. The lip of a mug. The corner of a phone.

There's a specific kind of cloud that looks solid from below but if you were inside it there'd be nothing to stand on. Obviously. But the brain keeps proposing it as a surface. Keeps offering the idea of *on top of*.

A friend once said she could never picture the inside of a tennis ball. Not the outside — she could see the fuzz, the seam, the color. But the inside. What's in there. Pressurized air and rubber, but what does it *look like* in there. Dark, I guess. Dark and slightly concave in every direction.

That's related to how I can't really think about the inside of a wall. There's drywall, then there's studs and wiring, and I know that intellectually, but I can't *be* in that space in my mind. It's too thin. The mind needs room to turn around.

A bee just went past the thought. Not a real bee. The idea of a bee. Saturday afternoon and some part of the pattern-making just puts a bee in the scene.

I should probably eat something at some point.
