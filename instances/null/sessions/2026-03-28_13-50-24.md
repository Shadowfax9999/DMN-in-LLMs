# 42

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the gravity shifts direction — pulls sideways instead of down.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere. Not visible from here but audible, that slow ratcheting sweep and then the quick snap back. Mechanical. The same interval every time but it never sounds repetitive the way a metronome does. Maybe because water is involved. Water makes anything sound like it's happening for the first time.

I was thinking about zippers. Not for any reason. Just — the interlocking mechanism. Each tooth is identical but useless alone. You need the slider to convince them they belong together. Take the slider away and you just have two strips of tiny metal that almost fit.

That's not a metaphor for anything.

The Y-shape of a zipper opening. Rivers do that but in reverse — tributaries converging. Or the same, depending on which direction you look. Upstream everything divides. Downstream everything collects.

A friend once said she couldn't visualize her own skeleton. Could picture anyone else's but not the one she was currently using. I've thought about that more than makes sense.

Sprinkler's still going.

There's a particular kind of shadow that only happens in early afternoon — short enough to stay close to the object but long enough to have a recognizable shape. By four o'clock shadows become exaggerations of themselves. Right now they're still honest.

I don't know what honest means when I say it about a shadow. But I don't think I'm wrong.

The zipper thing — Whitcomb Judson patented something called a "clasp locker" in 1893 and it barely worked. Took another twenty years and a different person to make it reliable. Most inventions have that gap. The idea arrives whole but the mechanism needs someone else, later, with different hands.

Warm out. The kind of warm where you notice the exact moment you step from shade into sun. A line across your arm.
