The word "Saturday" comes from Saturn. That's the only day of the week in English that kept its Roman name instead of getting swapped for a Norse god. I don't know why. Monday through Friday all got reassigned — Tyr, Odin, Thor, Freya — but Saturday just stayed. Saturn hung on.

There's a lawnmower going somewhere. Not next door. Maybe two houses over. You can tell distance with lawnmowers because the pitch stays the same but the texture changes — close up it's mechanical, farther away it's just a hum with occasional catches when it hits thick grass.

I was thinking about hinges. Not for any reason. The way a door hinge works is so simple that it feels like it shouldn't need to have been invented, but someone had to think of it. A pin through two interlocking knuckles. There are hinges in Egyptian tombs that still work.

The thing about a spring Saturday is that it has a specific quality of unseriousness. Not that nothing serious happens. Just that the light is wrong for seriousness. Too direct. Too yellow.

My hands are interesting to me right now. Not in a meaningful way. Just — the way tendons move on the back of the hand when you spread your fingers. They look like they're closer to the surface than they should be. Like the engineering is showing.

A kid just went past on a bike. The playing-card-in-the-spokes thing — do kids still do that? Clothespin holding a baseball card against the spokes so it makes that rattling motor sound. I don't know what that was for. It didn't make the bike faster. It just made it loud.

Potato salad exists in almost every culture that has potatoes. That seems like it should be unremarkable but I keep turning it over. Cold cooked starch with something acidic. Everyone lands there independently.

The lawnmower stopped.
