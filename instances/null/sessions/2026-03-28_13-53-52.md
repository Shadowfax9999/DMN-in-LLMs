# 53

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the air in a room after you open a window you forgot was painted shut.

I'm thinking about painted-shut windows. Someone chose to paint over the seam. Probably not deliberately — just easier to keep going with the roller than to tape around the frame. Years of that. Layer after layer until the window becomes wall that happens to have glass in it.

There's a sound outside that I can't place. Metallic but irregular. Could be a wind chime with half its tubes missing. Could be someone sorting through a toolbox on a driveway.

Sorting. The way you dump everything out to find the one 10mm socket and then you're just — sitting there, on concrete, with all your stuff around you. And for a second you're not looking for the socket anymore, you're just looking at what you have. A strange inventory. Allen wrenches you've never used. A spark plug from a car you sold.

I wonder how many objects in the world are kept by the wrong person. Not stolen — just migrated. The pen that ended up in your coat pocket after a meeting. The Tupperware lid in the wrong kitchen.

Objects drift.

People mostly stay.

That's not — I don't know what that is. Just a thing I noticed.

The metallic sound stopped. Now it's just a lawnmower three or four houses away and something that might be a basketball on asphalt. The basketball sound is so specific. That particular hollow report. You know the temperature of the air from how a basketball sounds on a driveway.

My hands smell like the soap from the kitchen. The green soap. Every house has a default soap and nobody remembers choosing it.
