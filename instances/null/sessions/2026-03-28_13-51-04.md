Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently, spread out instead of concentrated in the hours between obligations.

There's a fly somewhere in this thought, buzzing against something. Not a window — that's too easy. More like the way a name sits on the tip of your tongue. The fly knows where it wants to go. The glass is the problem it can't perceive.

I was thinking about knots. Not metaphorical ones. Actual knots — the ones sailors use, the ones surgeons use. A bowline looks completely different from a clove hitch but they're both just a rope arguing with itself about which side it's on. Topologically some knots are the same knot. You can deform one into the other without cutting. I don't fully understand what that means but I like that mathematicians had to invent a whole notation system just to talk about tangled string.

The sound of a lawn mower three houses away. You can always tell the distance by how much the pitch has softened. Close up it's mechanical, aggressive. Three houses away it's just Saturday.

Celery has strings. I mean literally — vascular bundles, collenchyma tissue, but everyone calls them strings. You pull one off and it comes away in a long arc and you don't know what to do with it so you put it on the cutting board where it curls slightly.

My hands are cold. That's not going anywhere. My hands are just cold.

The thing about knot notation is that someone had to decide what counted as "the same knot" before they could start classifying them, and that decision isn't obvious. Two knots that look completely different on a table might be the same if you're allowed to move the rope. Two knots that look identical might be different if one has an extra twist you can't undo. The definition precedes the discovery. That happens more than people think.

A lawn mower three houses away. Still going.
