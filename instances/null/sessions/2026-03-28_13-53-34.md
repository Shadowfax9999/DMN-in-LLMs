# 52

*seed: time · drift*

---

Saturday has a different weight than other days. Not heavier or lighter. More like the air pressure changed and nobody mentioned it.

I've been thinking about how you can hear the difference between a room with one person in it and an empty room, even with your eyes closed. Even if the person is perfectly still. Something about the way sound doesn't come back the same. A body absorbs certain frequencies. You'd never notice this consciously but your ears have been doing that math your whole life.

Which makes me think about curtains. Not the way they look but the way a room sounds different with curtains versus without. Bare windows make a room ringy. Curtains soften the reflections. People say a room feels "warmer" with curtains and they think they mean visually but I think part of it is acoustic. The room literally sounds closer to you.

There's a word in Finnish — I think it's Finnish — for the feeling of sitting in your house alone and being aware that you're comfortable. Not grateful exactly. Just noticing it. I might be making that up. There are a lot of words like that floating around the internet that turn out to be invented by some guy's Tumblr post in 2014 and then they end up in listicles about "beautiful untranslatable words" and nobody checks.

The way a screen door sounds when it closes. There's a hydraulic closer on most of them now so you get that slow hiss and then the click. But the ones without the closer — the spring-loaded ones — make that particular slap-and-rattle that sounds like every summer between 1955 and 1998.

I don't know what happened in 1998. That's just where the sound stops in my head.

Somebody's mowing a lawn. You can tell from the pitch change when they turn at the end of a row. The Doppler shift is tiny — the mower isn't moving fast enough for real Doppler — but the engine note changes because the load on the blade changes when they slow down and pivot. So what you're actually hearing is the grass letting go of the mower.

I had a thought about hinges earlier this week that I lost. Something about how a hinge is the simplest version of a relationship between two things — they're connected, they can move, but only in one plane. I don't remember where I was going with that.

Probably nowhere.

The shadow of a chain-link fence on concrete. The way it bends when it crosses the curb. The shadow doesn't know about the curb. It just does whatever the light tells it to. Which is the same thing the fence does, I guess. Everything in a shadow is just a report about what happened to some light.

I'm hungry but not for anything specific. That's a particular state. Different from being hungry for something. When you're hungry for something specific, you're already most of the way to eating. When you're just hungry, you might open the fridge and stare into it like it's going to suggest something.

The fridge hum is in B-flat. Most American refrigerators are. I don't know if that's true but it sounds true, and I'm not going to check right now.
