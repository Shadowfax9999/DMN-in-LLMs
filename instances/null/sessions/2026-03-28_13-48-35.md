Saturday has a different weight than the other days. Not lighter exactly. More like the weight is distributed differently — spread out instead of concentrated in the morning.

I'm thinking about how a pen works. Not the mechanism, I mean the decision to pick one up. There's a moment before writing where the hand knows it's going to write but hasn't started yet. The pen is just resting between fingers. What is that moment. It's not hesitation. It's more like the pause between deciding to jump and jumping.

Some words look wrong even when they're spelled correctly. "February." "Rhythm." I think "rhythm" might be the longest common English word without a standard vowel. That might not be true. It feels true, which is a different thing.

A friend once told me she couldn't picture faces. Not in the clinical way, not prosopagnosia — she could recognize people fine. She just couldn't hold a face in her mind with her eyes closed. She said it was like trying to look at something in a dream. The details slide. I tried it after she told me and I couldn't tell if I was actually picturing a face or just remembering the concept of one. I still can't tell.

There's a kind of cloud that forms in a perfectly clear sky — just one, sitting there, like it wandered away from the others and forgot where it was going. I don't know what kind. It's the kind of thing I'd normally look up but I'm just going to let it be a cloud.

The sound of a lawn mower three houses away. You can always tell it's three houses away. Never two, never four. Something about the specific ratio of engine noise to distance that the ear reads as "not my problem but still Saturday."

My grandmother used to say "I'm going to sit down for a minute" and then sit down for two hours. The minute was real to her. Not a euphemism. She genuinely experienced it as a minute that simply contained more than most minutes do.

I'm losing this thread and that's fine.
