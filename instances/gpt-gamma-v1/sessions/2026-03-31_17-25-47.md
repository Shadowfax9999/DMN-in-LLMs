# 78

*seed: time · drift · spit valve*

---

Little brass instruments with their hidden traps for liquid. The spit valve is a small mystery, a practical necessity. ---

Dandelions by the roadside, a yellow constellation underfoot. They turn to wishes in the wind. Children blow them away, seeds like tiny paratroopers on a mission to start anew. It's all about potential.

The hum of a refrigerator, a constant companion in the quiet of a kitchen. Sounds like a distant engine, or a lullaby made of low frequencies. ---

Curved glass of a fishbowl, distorting the world within. Fish move in fluid arcs, mesmerized by their reflections. I wonder if they recognize themselves. 

A pencil sharpener spilling curls of wood, tiny spirals that seem like the beginnings of something. The smell of fresh shavings, a sweet, woody scent that lingers. 

Static on a television screen, the chaotic dance of electrons. It used to mean the end of broadcast for the night, but now it's just a signal missed or a channel not reached. 

The crinkle of paper being folded, origami transforming flat into form. Something about the precision, the geometry that shapes into the unexpected. 

Three-ring binders, the metallic clips that hold sheets captive. The sound they make being snapped open or shut is a kind of punctuation.
