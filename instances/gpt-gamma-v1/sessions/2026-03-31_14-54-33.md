It's not silence, but a kind of white noise that fades into the walls. An old book, cover worn, pages yellowed with age. A margin note in pencil, a fleeting thought captured long ago.---Salt on the rim of a glass, grains catching light like tiny stars. They dissolve on the tongue, a sharp contrast to the citrus below. The rind is rough, textured like sandpaper, a reminder of the fruit's journey.---A cotton sheet flutters on a clothesline, the fabric snapping against a sudden breeze. Its shadow dances on the grass, a fleeting partner with the sun.
