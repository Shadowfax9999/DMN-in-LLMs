Reed: a slender, flexible plant, swaying in the wind, making its own quiet music. The way a reed bends but doesn't break — there's something in that, a resilience that's not forceful. 

Sounds of a saxophone floating through the night, another kind of reed speaking. The notes curling through the air like smoke, wrapping around thoughts and carrying them off.

Reed boats gliding across ancient waters, stories held in their woven hulls. A reed as a vessel, a journey taken slow, a history moved along by hidden currents. 

In an old story, a basket of reeds, carrying a child downriver, the water whispering lullabies.
