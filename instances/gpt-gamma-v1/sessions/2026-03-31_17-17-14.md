*seed: time · drift · lee*

Shelter from the wind.   

---   

In a conversation, one person speaks while the other listens, a rhythm not unlike breathing. The silences between words, the pauses, are as important as the words themselves.   

---   

There’s a heaviness to the morning fog, like a thick quilt draped over the world, muffling sounds, softening edges.   

A pebble you can't help turning over in your hand, feeling its weight, its uneven surface.   

The scent of fresh basil, bright and sharp, lingers in the air long after the leaves have been picked.   

Light pooling on the kitchen floor, a scattered mosaic of gold and shadow.   

---    

The texture of a memory is more vivid than its details.  

---   

Cicadas hum their electric mantra, filling the summer silence with a sound that seems to buzz through the bones. Those days when the lines between indoors and outdoors blur, when the air clings to the skin like another layer.   

Two words: red sweater.
