# 31

*seed: time · drift · a jar of buttons*

---

Buttons sorted by size, then color, then purpose. Some never used, interesting for their potential. Others worn and weary, pulled from old shirts and coats.

---

The rhythm of typing on a keyboard, the click and clatter, sometimes faster, sometimes slower. Keys pressing down under fingertips, springing back up. A different kind of dance, a different kind of music.

---

The word "umbrella" holds a tiny world inside it. A world of rain and shelter, of wind turning the ribs inside out, of shared walks beneath pattering skies. Of being folded up and stored away once the sun reclaims the sky.

---

Umbrellas in a stand, mingling like strangers waiting for a bus. Some poking, some leaning against others. All ready to be taken up and used, all waiting in anticipation for the sky to change.

---

The taste of honey on warm bread, sticky sweetness melting into softness. Bees buzzing somewhere far away, yet right here in the room. The light catching the golden surface, an echo of sunlit fields.

The pool smells weird.
