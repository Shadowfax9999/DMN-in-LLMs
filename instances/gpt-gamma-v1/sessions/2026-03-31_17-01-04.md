Limit — a line drawn, both real and imagined. A boundary on a map, a threshold of patience, a horizon I can't quite reach. 

---

Counting coins from a jar without looking, trusting the weight of each to tell its story. Dimes, nickels, quarters, a penny here and there — a collection of forgotten stops on a journey of small transactions.

---

The shadow of a tree holding the memory of the wind, branches arrested in mid-sway. A snapshot of a motion that never pauses.

---

Why does a single bell feel different than a peal? One solitary ring piercing through, or maybe it's the space it leaves — a sound that hangs and then dissolves, like a bubble forgotten after it pops.

---

What if the sky was just a series of veils, layered and endless? Peeling one back reveals only another, each one a shade of blue that's familiar yet unnameable.

---

Somewhere, a dog barks like a question. Maybe it's more of an exclamation. Or a punctuation mark, full stop. 

---

A list of things without a list: shadows flickering, the scent of distant rain, the buzz of a streetlamp, shoes tapping on uneven pavement, laughter carried by the breeze, the sudden warmth of sunlight breaking through.

The lot around the corner always feels like an unfinished conversation.
