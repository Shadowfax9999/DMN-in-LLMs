# 48

*seed: time · drift · the particular weight of a hardcover vs paperback*

---

The weight of a hardcover, reassuring in the hand. A kind of permanence. Paperback, lighter, more transient. Pages easier to bend, a spine that creases with each reading. Both contain the same words, but the experience changes. Does the content become heavier or lighter too?

---

A red brick wall, the warmth of the color in the afternoon sun. The shadows between the bricks creating a grid of dark lines against the brightness. I remember tracing those lines with a finger as a child, feeling the roughness under my skin.

---

A clock ticking softly in the background, unnoticed until silence falls. The steady march of seconds. The difference between noticing and not noticing, like the subtle shift from night to dawn. When does the sky start to change color?

---

A shoelace untied, the loose ends dragging with each step. A brief moment of imbalance before the knot is retied. The knot itself a small puzzle, muscle memory guiding fingers through loops and pulls.

---

The smell of toast, warm and familiar. Butter melting into the crumb, leaving glossy patches. It's strange how scent can pull you back to a kitchen long gone, a breakfast table, a shared quiet morning.

---

A fallen leaf caught in a spider's web. The fragile strands holding something much larger than themselves. A delicate strength, both fleeting and tenacious.
