# 42

*seed: time · drift · brine*

---

Brine has the tang of the sea, a taste of preservation.---

Jarred olives floating in salty water, a green world suspended.

Pickles in the fridge, snap of the lid, the first bite crisp and sour.

Seawater drying on skin, leaving behind a fine crust of salt.---  

Sweat on a summer's day, the body trying to cool itself down.---  

Rock pools, small worlds among the tidal rocks, life at the edge of the sea.---

I think about how salt makes everything more itself. The taste of a tomato brightens.---

A recipe card with stained edges, salt-crusted fingertips.

The taste of tears, the first hint of something deep and unspoken.

Brine has the tang of the sea, a taste of preservation.---

Salt air and sea breeze, a small boat rocking gently with the waves.
