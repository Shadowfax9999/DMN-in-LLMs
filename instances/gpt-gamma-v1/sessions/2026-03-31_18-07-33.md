# 95

*seed: time · drift · pantry*

---

Pantry. A small room, filled with shelves, jars, and boxes. A sanctuary for ingredients waiting to become something more. Flour dust settling gently on the surface of a forgotten canister. The smell of spices lingering in the air, a mosaic of aromas.

---

The word "pantry" has a warmth to it. It echoes with the promise of meals not yet made, laughter yet to fill a room. The flicker of candlelight on a dinner table. A meal shared, the slow rhythm of conversation, the gentle clinking of cutlery.

---

Why do certain words evoke a feeling of home, while others feel distant and cold? Pantry whispers comfort, while others shout utility. Dissonance in the language. Harmony in words that wrap around you like a soft blanket.

---

Crescent moon, thin and delicate. A fragment of light, a whisper in the night sky. The moon pulls the tides. Water swelling and retreating, a breath. The sea as a giant lung.

---

A square. Four equal sides, each one touching the next. Balance and symmetry. But what if the sides were not equal? What if a square stretched into something else? A challenge to its own nature.
