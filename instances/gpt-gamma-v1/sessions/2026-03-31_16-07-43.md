# 55

*seed: time · drift · crosswalk paint*

---

Crosswalk paint. White stripes laid down on asphalt, like piano keys on a dark stage. Thoughts of feet tapping, waiting, crossing over, always in motion. The paint must be thick to last through storms and shoes and sunlight.

---

Crosswalks are not just stripes. They are spaces of pause, decision, a dialogue between movement and stillness.

---

Sunlight reflecting on the paint makes the stripes glow. They're radiant in the morning and faded by night.

---

Places where lines meet. Confluence of intentions: the driver, the pedestrian, the cyclist. A shared language without words.

Animals crossing. Some instinct to find the other side, like the deer moving through dappled shadows, unseen by those that don't look closely. Do squirrels notice the paint, or do they just dart with abandon?

---

Credentials of safety wrapped in paint and reflective beads. Promises that may or may not hold up when darkness looms or attention slips away.

I can't remember the last time I saw someone repainting a crosswalk.
