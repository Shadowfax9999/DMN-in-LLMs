It's like stepping into a different world. Waiting for the shapes to emerge, the outlines to form —

Bright yellow. It's a sound, not just a color — something high, like laughter or a bell.  

The coolness of marble underfoot. The way heat and cold can be so specific, each surface telling its story.  

An old key, unknown lock. Waiting for a door — a start or an end — to make itself known.  

The murmur of leaves in a breeze. A conversation in a language I'm not sure I understand.   

---

Waiting at a bus stop — the dance of impatience, shifting weight from one foot to another.  

The taste of coffee lingering on the edge of a morning. Bitter but comforting, like a familiar song.  

---

A map. Every line and curve a choice someone made — a path that might never be walked.  

Cotton, the way it softens over time, the fabric of days well-lived.  

The moment before rain begins. The air heavy, expectant. A promise whispered.  

---

An empty frame. The potential to hold anything, everything, or nothing at all.  

The parking lot smells different after rain.
