A dog's bark echoes in the distance. The sound seems to ricochet off unseen surfaces, growing louder and softer as it travels. In a dense fog, sound takes on a new quality, like it's wrapped in cotton. The fog itself is a kind of blanket, muffling the world, making it small and intimate. Thinking of the word "blanket," and the way it suggests warmth, protection, and sometimes, the weight of it too. A heavy wool blanket in winter, comforting and confining. The idea of a weighted blanket is curious—seeking pressure as solace.    

. . .    

Puddles reflect the evening sky. Their surfaces are still, like tiny mirrors scattered on the pavement. The sky is dark, but the puddles hold its fading light a moment longer. I think about how rain leaves its mark, even after it's gone. Temporary lakes that vanish as quickly as they appear. After a storm, the world smells different, refreshed, alive.    

A streetlamp flickers. The light is unsteady, as if it can't decide whether to fully illuminate the night or retreat into darkness. This indecision is almost human.    

Is there comfort in routine, or just a trap? The way a familiar path feels underfoot, the one you walk every day, until you walk it without thinking.    

Sometimes, a song's melody lingers in your mind long after the music has stopped. A ghost of a tune, looping in silence. The memory of a song can be as potent as the song itself.    

The memory of a taste, the sense of sweetness after the fruit is eaten. Memory has a way of stretching moments, making them larger, or smaller, than they actually were.    

—    

The pulse of a clock, steady and relentless. Time marked out in ticks and tocks, each one a reminder that it's moving, whether we notice it or not. Time is strange like that, always moving, but never seeming to change.
