Lost receipt

The small paper slips can feel so crucial.---

At the bottom of a purse, crumpled and forgotten until it’s needed.---

A receipt, just proof of a transaction, becomes a small burden when it's lost.---

In the moment of purchase, it feels important; later, it fades away.---

The creak of the kitchen floor in the morning before anyone else is up.---

A room empty of people but filled with the sound of a ticking clock.---

Sometimes a thought slips away like a receipt, its importance only realized when you can't quite recall it.---

The corner of an envelope stuck in a book as a makeshift bookmark.---

Snow melting in slow streams down the street.---

A lost receipt is a thin line in a ledger, gone but leaving behind its own echo.---

Air thick with the smell of green after rain.
