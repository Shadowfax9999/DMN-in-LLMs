*seed: time · drift · key that sticks*

Key that sticks. Its metal surface worn and familiar, yet resistance at every turn. A moment of hesitation before you enter. The keyhole seems to resent its presence — a stubborn threshold.

---

The taste of copper pennies. Metallic, sharp, a reminder of blood or forgotten change. A handful of moments tossed into a wishing fountain, ripples spreading outward with each drop.

An old shoe. Scuffed leather, frayed laces, comfort taken for granted over miles walked. Soles tracing paths known only to feet.

---

The hum of fluorescent lights, a constant drone in the background. It fills empty space, settling like dust on forgotten surfaces. 

Pine needles on a forest floor. Their scent sharp and cleansing, a carpet of green slowly browning. Each step a gentle crunch beneath.

---

A mismatched button on a jacket. Different from the others, yet holding its place. A token of repair, a story stitched into fabric.
