Tea steeping in a mug, the liquid darkening slowly. 

A lone crow on a telephone wire, watching, always watching. 

The sound of rain against a windowpane, each drop a tiny percussion. 

Laughter echoing from an unseen place, the kind that fills an empty room. 

The texture of old sweaters, those soft pills that speak of wear and comfort.

A traffic light changing at an empty intersection, the silent authority of color.

Wooden spoons in a holder, each with its own history of stews and soups.

The faint melody of a wind chime stirred by a gentle breeze. 

Crickets serenading the dusk, a chorus of hidden voices. 

An old photograph, its corners curling, stories held in sepia tones. 

A kitchen table, scarred with years of meals and conversations. 

---

A single leaf spiraling to the ground, a dance with gravity. 

Frost etching intricate patterns on a window, nature's ephemeral art. 

The distant murmur of a river, a steady lullaby. 

Glimpses of the moon behind drifting clouds, a game of celestial peek-a-boo. 

The pause just before a sneeze, that momentary suspension.
