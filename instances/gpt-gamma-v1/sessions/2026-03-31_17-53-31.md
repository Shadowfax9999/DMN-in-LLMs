# 90

*seed: time · drift · solitaire*

---

Solitaire is a game and a state of being. Solitary.

The deck spread out, face down, waiting for arrangement. Solitary confinement in a house of cards. Sol-air-tare. Does "tare" mean something about weight? 

Building card houses used to feel important. Balancing, testing limits. Wind would destroy, or impatience. Colder air felt heavier, denser.

---

The moon folded into creases of night. Stars as punctuation marks.

A spider's web glints in the early morning dew. Tiny droplets of water sitting in symmetry.

A single shoe left behind, laces undone, resting on the sidewalk. Socks would probably be wet now. It could be a child's shoe, but it doesn't seem small enough.

---

Dandelions holding onto their fluff until a gust scatters it. Wishes whispered on the loss of seeds.

A kettle boiling over on the stove. Steam rising, almost like an angry puff of a dragon. 

---

The word "solitaire" echoes like a bell, reverberating through the empty hall. The sound doesn't stop.
