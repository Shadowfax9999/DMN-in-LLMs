loading dock. Pallets stacked high, waiting. The smell of cardboard and diesel, layers of dust from journeys unseen. A place of transition, where what is carried arrives to wait for its next destination.---

Cabbage. The crinkle of leaves, layers wrapped tight. The scent earthy and bitter, a deep green embracing the pale heart within. All those veined surfaces like a map of capillary rivers, tracing paths of moisture and minerals.---

The back of a spoon catching light. A small reflection, an accidental mirror. You see an imperfect world bent around its curve.---

Bubble wrap: each bubble a pocket of trapped breath, a satisfying pop under pressure.---

Fungus. Cap, stem, spore. A kingdom living on decay, patient, below the surface until it rises, unbidden, from earth or wood.---

Tick. That small sound, regular as a clock's sentence, closing the moments like doors.
