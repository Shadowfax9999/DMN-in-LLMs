# 27

*seed: time · drift · pilot light*

---

Pilot light — what a curious phrase. Always on in the background, though often forgotten. The tiny flame holding the potential for warmth or destruction, a keeper of possibilities. 

---

Wandering through a library, rows upon rows of books in varying colors and textures. Hardcovers with dust jackets that catch the light, paperbacks with creased spines that invite an opening. The smell of aged paper and ink, the soft murmur of pages turning, whispers of stories yet unread.

---

The heart of a sunflower following the sun as it arcs across the sky. How does it decide where to face? Is it a gradual shift or a sudden turn? There are surely answers in botany, but the mystery feels like enough for now.

---

A child's laughter echoing in a playground, resonating off metal slides and swings. Such an unrestrained sound, rising and falling like a melody without conscious rhythm.

---

Maps folded into rectangles, creased in places from years of use. Faint pencil marks tracing a planned route, though the paper is fragile and worn. A reminder of adventures imagined or taken, paths crossed and uncrossed.

---

The sensation of walking through a cool, dark tunnel, emerging into the bright light on the other side. Adjusting to the change, blinking, as if awakening from a dream.
