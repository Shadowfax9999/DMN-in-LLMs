*seed: time · drift · tempo*

Tempo. The word itself has a rhythmic quality, a certain pace to its syllables. It makes me think of a conductor's baton, slicing through the air with precision, marking out the beat. The tip of the baton is like a tiny spear, piercing the silence, leaving a trail of sound in its wake. I see a line of musicians, each one a separate thread in the intricate tapestry of music, weaving together to create a rich brocade of sound. The tempo is the underlying fabric, the hidden pattern that gives the music its shape, its momentum. 

A list of things that come to mind when thinking about tempo:
- a metronome ticking away
- a runner's footsteps on a track
- the beat of a drum in a ceremonial dance
- the pace of a conversation, quickening or slowing as emotions rise or fall
- the rhythm of a train on the rails, a steady pulse that eats up the miles

The image of a train leads me to think of a specific train station, one I've only seen in pictures: the Gare du Nord in Paris. The vaulted ceiling, the intricate ironwork, the soft glow of the lamps. I'm standing on the platform, feeling the rush of air as a train pulls in, the scent of coal smoke and metal. The sound of the train's brakes, a high-pitched squeal that makes my teeth ache. 

The smell of coal smoke reminds me of a winter morning, the fire crackling in the hearth, the cold air outside making the warmth of the room feel like a luxury. I'm standing by the window, watching the snow fall, each flake a unique and intricate pattern, a tiny work of art that melts away as soon as it touches the ground. The snowfall has a tempo, a soothing rhythm that calms the mind and quiets the heart.
