# 42

*seed: time · drift*

---

The scent of blooming flowers lingers, and for a moment, it's the only thing that exists. Then, the sound of ticking grows fainter, and the hum returns, this time closer, louder. A car drives by outside, its engine rumbling, and the sound of tires on pavement hums a low note that vibrates through the air. The bird calls out again, its melody overlapping with the fading rumble of the car, creating a brief, discordant harmony. 

The light on the wall shifts again, and a dust particle floats, suspended, caught in a sunbeam. The particle twirls, its movement random, unpredictable, and then it's still, stuck to the wall, a tiny, insignificant speck. The sweetness of the flowers slowly fades, replaced by the smell of fresh-cut grass, earthy and green. 

A faint memory of a summer afternoon surfaces, the feeling of warm grass beneath bare feet, the sound of children laughing in the distance. The memory is hazy, indistinct, a collection of sensations rather than a clear picture. The hum in the distance grows louder once more, and the memory dissipates, replaced by the sound of the clock, its steady pulse a reminder of time's relentless passage. 

The bird calls out once more, its voice clear, piercing, a solitary sound in the stillness. The world outside is quiet, the only sounds the bird's melody, the ticking clock, and the occasional rumble of a car driving by. The air is filled with the scent of fresh-cut grass, the sweetness of the flowers forgotten, lost in the gentle breeze. 

The dust particle on the wall remains, a tiny, insignificant speck, a reminder of the small, often-overlooked details that make up the world. The hum in the distance grows fainter, and the clock's ticking becomes the dominant sound, a steady, unchanging pulse that underscores the stillness of the moment. The bird's melody fades away, leaving behind a silence that's almost, but not quite, complete.
