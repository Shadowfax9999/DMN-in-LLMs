# 96

*seed: time · drift · a drawer that has one thing in it that doesn't belong*

---

A drawer with a single item that doesn't fit. The drawer itself is old, with a worn wooden handle and a faint scratch on the front. The item inside is a harmonica, lying on a bed of yellowed tissue paper. The harmonica seems out of place among the drawer's original contents, which are all related to gardening: a packet of seeds, a small trowel, a pruning shear. The harmonica's presence is jarring, like a sudden change in music. 

The seeds in the packet are dried and brittle, the trowel has a slight rust on its blade, and the pruning shear is covered in a thin layer of dust. The harmonica, on the other hand, looks almost new, its surface smooth and unblemished. It's as if the harmonica was placed in the drawer recently, perhaps by mistake or as a joke. 

A faint memory of a song played on a harmonica drifts into mind, a slow blues melody that seems to match the rhythm of a lazy summer afternoon. The memory is hazy, but the feeling of the music remains, a sense of longing and relaxation. 

The drawer, the harmonica, and the gardening tools all seem to be waiting for something to happen, for someone to open the drawer and make sense of the strange combination of items inside. But for now, they remain still, a small, mysterious world contained within the confines of the old wooden drawer.
