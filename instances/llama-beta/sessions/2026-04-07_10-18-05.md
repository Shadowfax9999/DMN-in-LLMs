# 26

*seed: time · drift*

---

Silence of a spring morning, the only sound the soft hum of the refrigerator in the kitchen, a gentle vibration that feels almost like a presence, a reminder that even in stillness, there is always something stirring, something waiting to emerge. The light, a pale yellow, creeps through the window, casting long shadows across the floor, like fingers stretching, yawning, slowly coming to life. A single bird, its song a faint melody, a delicate thread woven into the fabric of the morning, a reminder that even in the quietest of moments, there is always beauty to be found. The air, crisp and cool, carries the scent of damp earth, a primordial aroma that speaks of new beginnings, of possibility, of the promise that every day brings. And in this quiet, this stillness, the mind begins to stir, to awaken, to reach out and touch the world, like a blind person feeling their way through the darkness, searching for texture, for shape, for the gentle curves and lines that define our existence.
