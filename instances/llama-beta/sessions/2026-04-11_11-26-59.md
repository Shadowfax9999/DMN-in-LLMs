*seed: time · drift · the sound of someone else's conversation through a hotel wall*

---

muffled voices a door away indistinct phrases laughter a murmur of conversation that's almost but not quite intelligible the sound of glasses clinking or a bottle being opened the creak of a bedframe or a chair scraping against the floor the voices rise and fall in a cadence that's familiar but unplaceable 
a snatch of melody from a radio or television in the background the hum of a air conditioner or the rattle of a window unit 
it's not what they're saying that's interesting but the fact that they're saying it at all the sound of their presence in the room next door a reminder that there are other people in the world 
the conversation is a kind of punctuation a series of sounds that break the silence 
and then it stops 
the sudden absence of noise is almost palpable 
the stillness that follows is like a held breath 
waiting for the sound to resume 
but it doesn't 
and the silence stretches out 
like a sheet of paper waiting to be written on
