# 88

*seed: time · drift · chlorine*

---

Chlorine. The smell of a public pool on a summer afternoon. The taste of it in the air, sharp and acrid. A memory of a swimsuit, faded and worn, hanging from a hook in a locker room. The sound of children laughing and splashing in the background, a cacophony of noise that's both chaotic and joyful. A sensation of sunburned skin, tender and sore, as if the sun's rays had left an imprint on the flesh. 

The smell of chlorine wafts away, replaced by the scent of coconut sunscreen and saltwater. A seagull cries out in the distance, its raucous call piercing the air. The feeling of sand between toes, warm and grainy, as if the beach itself was alive and shifting. A fragment of a song, half-remembered, plays in the mind, the melody elusive and ephemeral. 

A single image: a rusty old lifeguard chair, standing sentinel on a deserted beach, its wooden slats worn smooth by the elements. The chair seems to be waiting, patiently, for something to happen, for someone to climb up and scan the horizon. The sound of waves crashing against the shore, a constant and soothing rhythm, provides a backdrop for the chair's quiet vigil.
