# 37

*seed: time · drift*

---

A grid of squares, but not quite, because the lines are slightly askew, like a woven fabric that's been pulled tight in one direction. The intersections don't quite match up, creating a sense of tension, of something about to slip or shift. Each square is a tiny, distorted reflection of the others, like a funhouse mirror maze. 

If you take one square and rotate it, the whole grid seems to rotate with it, as if it's all connected by invisible threads. But if you try to follow one of those threads, it disappears, leaving just the squares, alone and unconnected, like isolated islands in a sea of emptiness. 

The edges of the grid are rough, like torn paper, and they seem to be unraveling, thread by thread, square by square. But the unraveling doesn't seem to be going anywhere, just circling back on itself, like a snake eating its own tail. 

And then, a single line, a single thread, breaks free, and the whole grid dissolves, leaving only the memory of its shape, the echo of its pattern, the whisper of its existence.
