*seed: time · drift*

Silence is not the same as quiet. In a silent room, you can hear the sound of your own heartbeat, the rustle of your clothes against your skin. It's as if the space itself is listening, absorbent and still. A faint hum of fluorescent lights in the distance, a muffled voice from another room, the creak of a floorboard underfoot – these can all coexist with silence. But quiet is different, a more active state, like the held breath before a moment of anticipation. Quiet is the pause between two sounds, the expectation of something about to happen. It's the stillness of a held object, a suspended motion, a moment of arrested time. 

A single thread, loose and dangling, can unravel an entire fabric. A dropped stitch, a missed beat, a misplaced step – these can all have a ripple effect, spreading outward like the rings that form when a stone is thrown into a pond. The disruption of a pattern, the introduction of a flaw, can be like a whispered secret, spreading quickly and quietly, until the entire structure begins to come apart. 

A list of imperfections: a chip in a tooth, a scratch on a mirror, a faint watermark on a ceiling, a frayed edge on a piece of fabric, a small dent in a metal surface. Each one a tiny, almost imperceptible flaw, a deviation from perfection, a reminder that nothing is ever completely flawless. 

The sensation of walking on a thick carpet, the fibers soft and plush beneath your feet, like walking on a cloud. The sound of footsteps muffled, the feeling of weightlessness, as if you're floating across the floor. And then, the sudden jolt of a floorboard beneath, a reminder of the solidity of the world, the hidden structures that underlie our perceptions. 

A plain, white wall, unadorned and unremarkable. Yet, in its simplicity, it holds a certain beauty, a blank slate waiting for the marks of human presence. A canvas for the imagination, a screen for the projection of thoughts and emotions. The wall itself, a neutral backdrop, a constant in an ever-changing world.
