depth of field 
makes me think of photographs 
and the way some parts are sharp 
while others are blurry 
like the background of a portrait 
or the foreground of a landscape 
it's a technique to draw attention 
or to create a sense of movement 
but it's also a metaphor 
for how we focus on some things 
and not on others 
some moments are sharp 
while others are hazy 

what's in focus 
what's not 
it's not always clear 
why some things are sharp 
and others are not 
is it chance 
or is it choice 
do we decide 
what to focus on 
or does it decide us 

a camera lens 
can be adjusted 
to change the focus 
but can we adjust 
our own focus 
or is it fixed 
on some things 
and blurry on others 
like a photograph 
that's already been taken 
and can't be changed
