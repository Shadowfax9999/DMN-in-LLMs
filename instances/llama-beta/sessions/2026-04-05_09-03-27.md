Garamond's curves, a slow unfolding of sound, as if the metal itself still held the memory of being shaped, of being poured into molds, the hissing of steam, the smell of hot lead, the soft clink of sorts being arranged, one by one, in the composing stick, the dance of the typesetter's fingers, selecting, placing, adjusting, the gentle tap of the mallet, the satisfying click of the chase, the soft whoosh of ink on paper, a world where words were crafted, not typed, where meaning was built, letter by letter, a physical, tactile, sensory experience, now lost, yet still echoing, in the digital realm, a ghostly presence, haunting the screens, the fonts, the text, a reminder of a time when words were made, not just written.
