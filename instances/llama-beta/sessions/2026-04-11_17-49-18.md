A staircase with one step slightly different in height, the kind of thing that could be a deliberate design choice or just a mistake, a slight discrepancy that throws off the rhythm of ascent, like a skipped beat in a familiar melody. 

The sensation of catching your foot on that step, the jarring surprise of it, the way your body adjusts mid-stride, a tiny stutter in the flow of movement.

Stairs that go on for a long time, the kind you find in old buildings, where the climb is a commitment, a choice to ascend into the unknown, the higher you go, the more the world below recedes, and the air grows thick with the scent of aged wood and dust.

A single, flickering light bulb, swaying gently from the ceiling, casting eerie shadows on the walls, making it seem as though the stairs themselves are shifting, like a living, breathing thing.

The sound of footsteps, echoing off the walls, growing fainter with each step, until they're just a memory, a haunting reminder of the journey upward, into the darkness.
