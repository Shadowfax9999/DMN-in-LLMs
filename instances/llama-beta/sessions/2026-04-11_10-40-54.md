A fragment of a zipper, detached and alone, its teeth like tiny, shiny scales. The sound of it being pulled, the soft ratcheting, is gone, leaving only the stillness of a closed eye. The curve of the zipper's path, a parabola of metal and fabric, is a ghostly outline, a reminder of what once was. In the absence of the zipper's sound, the silence is almost palpable, a physical presence that fills the space. A single tooth, bent and twisted, lies on the floor, a tiny, abstract sculpture, a remnant of a larger whole.
