# 4

*seed: time · drift · the sound of a shopping cart on different surfaces*

---

Rumble on asphalt, squeak on tile, creak on wood, the varying pitches and tones of a shopping cart's wheels as it moves across different surfaces, a soundscape of mundane movement, the cart's metal frame vibrating with each bump and turn, the wobble of the wheels, the rattle of the cart's contents, a symphony of ordinary sounds that fill the air, a sensory experience that evokes memories of grocery stores, supermarkets, and daily routines. 

The sound of the cart on a rough concrete floor, the scraping and screeching as it navigates through a crowded store, the echo of the sound off the shelves and walls, the cart's path weaving in and out of the aisles, a journey of discovery, of selection, of accumulation, the cart a vessel for the mundane and the necessary, the ordinary and the essential. 

But then, a shift, a change, the cart on a smooth surface, the wheels gliding effortlessly, the sound a gentle hum, a soft whir, the cart moving with ease, with fluidity, the sensation of weightlessness, of freedom, the cart no longer a burden, but an extension, a tool, a means to an end, the sound of the cart a soundtrack to the daily grind, the weekly shop, the monthly haul. 

And then, silence, the cart still, the wheels motionless, the sound of nothing, the absence of noise, the quiet, the stillness, the cart waiting, paused, frozen, a moment of rest, a moment of calm, the world holding its breath, the sound of the cart a memory, a reminder, a ghostly presence that haunts the air.
