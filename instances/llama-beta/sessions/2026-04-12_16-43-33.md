The court itself, a grid of squares, each one a separate territory to defend and conquer. The ball, a small, rubber projectile, flying through the air, spinning and curving in unpredictable ways. The thrill of victory, the agony of defeat, all played out within the confines of a few, hastily drawn lines. 

A different kind of square, one made of fabric, folded and creased, a piece of cloth that can be wrapped around the body, a makeshift cloak or shroud. The feel of the fabric, smooth and cool, the sound of it rustling, the smell of fresh laundry and clean air. 

The word square, itself, a curious thing, a combination of sounds and shapes that evoke a sense of solidity and structure. A square, a shape, a form, a container, a boundary. Something that holds and encloses, while also defining and separating. 

Four square, a phrase that echoes through the mind, a refrain, a mantra, a call to action. A reminder of the simple joys of childhood, the thrill of competition, the satisfaction of victory. A phrase that conjures up memories of sun-kissed afternoons, laughter and tears, the simple, unadulterated joy of play.
