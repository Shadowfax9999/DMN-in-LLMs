A bruise you don't remember getting. 
The color of it, a deep purple, like the first hint of dawn on a summer morning, when the sky is still a mix of night and day, and the stars are fading, one by one. 
A small, almost imperceptible swelling, a gentle curve where the skin used to be flat, like the soft rise of a hill in a landscape that's otherwise flat and unremarkable. 
The sensation of touching it, a faint tenderness, a whisper of pain, like the quiet hum of a mosquito hovering just out of reach, a reminder that something happened, though what, exactly, is lost to memory. 
No image comes to mind, no scene, no recollection of a fall or a bump, just this small, mysterious mark, a bruise that seems to have appeared from nowhere, like a small, uninvited guest who's arrived without warning, and is now making themselves at home. 
It's on my hip, I think, or is it my thigh, the location uncertain, like the memory itself, hazy, indistinct, a watercolor painting left out in the rain, the colors bleeding, merging, fading away. 
A faint itch, a tickle, a shiver runs down my spine as I touch it again, trying to recall, to remember, but the memory remains elusive, hiding just out of reach, like a word on the tip of my tongue, a word that refuses to be spoken.
