Talus. The sound of it is rough, like the edge of a rock. It's a word that feels like it should be associated with something jagged, something that scrapes against the skin. A mental image forms: a slope of loose, fragmented rock, the kind that shifts beneath your feet as you try to climb. The word talus seems to fit this image perfectly, as if it was made to describe this specific type of landscape. 

A list starts to form:
broken stones
scree
moraines
glacial erratics
avalanche chute

The words are all related to geological formations, but they seem to be drifting apart, like the fragments of rock on a talus slope. Each word sparks a new image, a new association, and the list grows longer, more disjointed. 

The sound of the word talus echoes, a low, rough whisper that seems to come from the earth itself. It's a sound that's both familiar and strange, like the feeling of walking on a rocky slope, unsure of your footing.
