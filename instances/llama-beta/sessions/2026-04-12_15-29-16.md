*seed: time · drift*

A faint scent of turpentine lingers on my fingertips, a remnant of a long-forgotten art project, perhaps, or a whiff of something else entirely, unconnected to any specific memory, yet somehow evocative of a particular mood, a feeling of being suspended between moments, like the pause between breaths. 

No, not turpentine, maybe something sweeter, more floral, like the smell of old books, or worn fabric, a scent that conjures images of attics and trunks, forgotten treasures and dusty relics, the musty aroma of time itself, slowly unfolding, slowly revealing, yet never quite disclosing its secrets.

A series of fragmented images begins to surface, like bubbles rising to the surface of a pond, each one bursting, leaving behind a ripple, a disturbance, a faint impression of something that refuses to cohere, to take shape, to become anything more than a fleeting glimpse, a whispered promise, a hint of a presence, a reminder that something, anything, might be lurking just beneath the surface, waiting to emerge, to reveal itself, to make its presence known.
