*seed: time · drift*

A faint scent of damp earth and moss, the smell of a forest floor after rain. The sound of water seeping, trickling, flowing, a gentle melody that weaves in and out of the silence. A memory, half-remembered, of walking through a forest, the trees towering above, their leaves a vibrant green, their branches a lattice of light and shadow. The feel of leaves crunching beneath feet, the soft give of the earth, the rough bark of a tree trunk. A sense of being small, yet connected, a part of something larger, a thread in a intricate tapestry. 

The color of the trees, a deep, rich green, like the first page of a well-loved book. The smell of old paper, and ink, and leather binding. A library, quiet, and still, the only sound the whisper of pages turning. The feel of a book in hands, the weight of it, the texture of the cover, the smoothness of the pages. A sense of wonder, of discovery, of secrets waiting to be uncovered.

A list, unformed, and unstructured, a jumble of words, and images, and sensations: 
forest floor
old books
leather binding
tree branches
water flowing
moss
damp earth
secrets
discovery
wonder 

The sound of a river, flowing, ever-changing, yet constant. The feel of the sun, warm, on skin, a sense of comfort, of peace. A memory, half-remembered, of a summer day, the sun shining, the world full of possibility. The smell of cut grass, and blooming flowers, a sweet, heady scent. The taste of fresh air, and freedom. 

The color of the sky, a deep, brilliant blue, like the surface of a still lake. The feel of the wind, gentle, and soothing, a soft caress. A sense of being alive, of being present, of being connected to the world around. The sound of birds singing, a chorus of joy, and beauty. 

A single sentence, plain, and simple: The world is full of wonder.
