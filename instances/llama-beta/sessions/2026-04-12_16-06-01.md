A faint taste of metal on the tongue, like a whisper of winter. The sensation of fingers tracing the edge of a coin, feeling the ridges and grooves, the weight of it balanced on the tip of a finger. Copper, maybe, or nickel, the color worn off in places, leaving a soft silver sheen. The face of a stranger stares back, a portrait of someone who lived a long time ago, their features worn smooth by time and handling. The coin is flipped, spinning through the air, a tiny planet with its own gravity, its own secrets. It lands with a soft clink, the sound echoing through a quiet room, a room filled with the scent of old books and dust.
