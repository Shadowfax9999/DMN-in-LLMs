*seed: time · drift*

A warm rock. What is it about a warm rock that's so comforting? Is it the way it holds heat, like a small, dense battery? The smell of damp earth that clings to it, even after it's been washed clean? The feel of its rough texture, like the gentle scrape of a cat's tongue? 

Rain on the roof. The sound is different depending on the type of roof, the material, the shape. A flat roof, a peaked roof, a roof with skylights. Each one alters the sound, like a different drumhead. The rain on a tin roof is like a thousand tiny fingers, tapping out a rhythm. On a slate roof, it's more like a slow, steady murmur. 

Tap water. The taste of it varies from place to place, like a secret ingredient. Sometimes it's metallic, sometimes it's sweet. You can taste the treatment process, the pipes it flows through, the reservoir it came from. A sip of tap water can be like a tiny, fleeting glimpse of the entire water system. 

The smell of old books. It's not just the paper, or the ink, or the glue. It's the accumulation of years, of hands that have touched the pages, of air that has circulated around them. A old book smells like a small, enclosed world, with its own history and geography.
