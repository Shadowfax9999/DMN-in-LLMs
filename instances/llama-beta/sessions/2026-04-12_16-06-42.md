Hum of the fridge in the kitchen, a steady thrum that's always there, even when you're not thinking about it. Like the sound of a far-off engine, constant and reassuring. What if you could see this sound, not as a wave or a line, but as a presence, a thing that takes up space in the room? A haze, maybe, or a shimmer, something that you could walk through or around, but not quite touch. The edges of it would be blurry, indistinct, like the border between wakefulness and sleep. 
It makes me think of the way dust motes dance in a sunbeam, their gentle swirl and eddy like the hum itself, except that the hum doesn't seem to move, it just is. Always there, a constant companion, like the sound of your own heartbeat, or the feel of the air on your skin. 

a list of things that just exist
- the smell of old books
- the feel of a warm rock
- the sound of rain on the roof 
- the taste of tap water

the hum, the thrum, the vibration, it's all just a part of the background, like the creaks and groans of an old house, or the rustle of leaves in the trees outside. You don't really notice it, until you do, and then it's all you can hear.
