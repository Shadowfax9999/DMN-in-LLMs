# 72

*seed: time · drift · the feeling of wet sand compacting under your foot*

---

Wet sand compacting underfoot, the sensation of it giving way, then firming up as the water is squeezed out. The sound of it, a faint squelch. The feeling of the sand shifting, like a slow, cold breath. The image of a beach at low tide, the sand flat and wide, stretching out to the water's edge. A piece of driftwood, worn smooth, lying on the sand. The wood is cool to the touch, and it fits comfortably in the curve of your hand. 

The curve of your hand, the way the fingers wrap around the wood, a sense of containment. The feeling of holding something, of having it fit snugly in your palm. A small, rounded stone, smoothed by the water, fitting in the same way. The stone is heavier than the wood, and it has a more solid presence. 

A list of things that fit in the palm of your hand:
a small stone
a piece of driftwood
a seashell
a coin
a key 

The feeling of turning the key, the satisfying click of the lock disengaging. A door, unlocked, swinging open on its hinges. The sound of the door, a creak, followed by the sound of footsteps, echoing off the walls.
