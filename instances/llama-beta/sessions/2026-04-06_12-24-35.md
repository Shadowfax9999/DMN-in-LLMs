# 21

*seed: time · drift · grandfather clock*

---

Grandfather clock, its presence imposing in a hallway, a silent guardian of time, its tick-tock a steady heartbeat in the background of memories. The smell of old wood, a scent that's both musty and comforting, like the pages of a well-worn book. A faint image of a pendulum swinging, its arc a hypnotic dance, a reminder of the rhythmic passage of time. The clock's face, a solemn visage, its numbers and hands a promise of order and precision, yet the surrounding decor, a testament to the whims of human taste, a jarring mix of patterns and textures, like a patchwork quilt. A door creaks, a sudden sound that shatters the stillness, and the clock's tick-tock seems to grow louder, as if time itself has been awakened, its presence no longer subtle, but insistent, demanding attention. 

A sound echoes, a low, mournful note, like the hum of a cello, it vibrates through the air, a somber melody that resonates deep within the chest, a reminder of the clock's role as a keeper of secrets, a witness to the passage of generations. The sound fades, leaving only the tick-tock, a steady pulse that underscores the silence, a reminder that time waits for no one, its passage relentless, a force that shapes and molds, yet remains ultimately unknowable. 

The clock's presence begins to recede, its image fragmenting, like the shards of a broken mirror, each shard reflecting a different moment, a different memory, a different facet of time's multifaceted nature. A sense of disconnection, a feeling of being untethered, as if the clock's steady heartbeat has been interrupted, leaving only the echoing silence, a void that seems to stretch out like an endless corridor, with no visible exit, no discernible destination. 

The silence deepens, a palpable presence that seems to have a texture, a weight, a sensation that's both oppressive and liberating, like the feeling of floating in a sea of nothingness, unmoored, untethered, free to drift on the tides of the mind. And in this silence, a thought emerges, a fleeting glimpse of a forgotten moment, a memory that's been hidden, waiting to be uncovered, like a small, delicate key, hidden in the crease at the back of the knee, waiting to unlock a secret compartment, a mysterious message, or a forgotten memory.
