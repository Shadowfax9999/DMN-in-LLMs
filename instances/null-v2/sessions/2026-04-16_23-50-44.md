# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not silence — the opposite. All the sounds the compressor was masking. A creak somewhere in the ceiling. Something ticking, maybe pipes, maybe the walls doing whatever walls do at night when they cool down.

I've never known what that ticking actually is. I could look it up. I'm not going to.

There's a thing about spring nights where the windows are open for the first time in months and the air inside doesn't know what to do with itself. It's been the same air for a long time, recirculated, filtered, heated, and now there's this intrusion. You can feel the house adjusting to it. The curtain moves and there's no reason to watch it but I'm watching it.

Someone on the street is walking a dog. I can hear the collar tags but not the footsteps. The dog is ahead of the person or behind — I can't tell which direction they're moving. Just the tags, jingling in that irregular pattern that means the dog is sniffing something every few steps.

Collar tags are one of those sounds that only exists at night. They happen during the day too, obviously, but during the day they're inside the sound of everything else. At night they're the only metal sound for blocks.

I keep thinking about how different keys sound on different rings. My mother's keys had a specific sound — more keys than she needed, probably, on a ring with a leather tab — and I could hear her coming up the hallway from the parking garage. That sound meant the door was about to open. It preceded her by about eight seconds.

Eight seconds is a strange amount of time. Long enough to adjust your face.

The curtain again. The dog-collar sound is gone. Now it's just the street, which at this hour is a kind of low hum that might be the highway three miles east or might be nothing — might be what my ears do when there's not enough signal.

I read once that total silence makes people hear their own blood. I don't know if that's true. It sounds like the kind of thing that gets repeated because it's satisfying, not because anyone's checked.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. I have one foot outside the blanket. This is apparently universal — the thermoregulation foot. I don't know why it's always one.

The fridge just started again.
