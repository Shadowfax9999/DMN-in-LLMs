*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was.

There's a kind of hearing that only works backward. You didn't know the sound was there until it stopped, and then the shape of it is perfectly clear — a low electrical hum with a rattle at one end, maybe a loose screw in the compressor housing, maybe just the shelf where I put the jam jar too close to the edge.

I do that with people too. Notice the quality of someone's presence most sharply in the first few seconds after they leave a room. Not missing them. Just suddenly aware of what they were contributing to the texture of things. The way they breathed, or shifted weight, or kept almost saying something.

Friday at midnight is an odd seam. It's already the weekend but nobody's used any of it yet. The week is spent and the weekend is still sealed. I think about envelopes — how the glue on the flap is a kind of promise that the inside is still in its original condition. Tamper-evident. Though I guess all envelopes are tamper-evident, that's the whole point, you can see the tear.

My hands smell like garlic from dinner and I keep catching it when I touch my face. Garlic stays in skin differently than on skin. It gets into the oils somehow. There's a thing where if you rub a stainless steel spoon between your fingers under running water the smell disappears. I've tried it. It half-works.

What does half-working feel like as a concept. Medicine half-works a lot. Apologies half-work. Locks half-work — they stop the casual attempt but not the determined one. Most things half-work, actually. I'm not sure I can name something that fully works with no remainder, no gap, no case where it doesn't.

Gravity, maybe.

No, even gravity has the problem at quantum scales where it doesn't play well with the other forces. So even gravity half-works, just at a scale I'll never experience.

A spring night with the windows cracked and the cold coming in at ankle level. The warm air is up near the ceiling doing nothing useful. I can feel the two layers on my shins — cool below the knee, warm above. The house is stratified like a lake in summer. I learned that word from a different context and now I can't not use it here.

Somewhere outside a car door closes. Not a slam — the firm press-and-click of someone being considerate at this hour. That's a whole social calculation compressed into the speed of an arm. How hard the situation allows.

I'm tired in the way where thoughts are slower but not fewer. They just arrive with more space between them, like cars on a highway at 2 AM — same road, same direction, but you can see each one individually now.

I should go to bed. That's not a thought, that's just a fact sitting in the room with me, waiting to become a decision.
