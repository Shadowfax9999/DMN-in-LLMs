The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was. That's a strange thing about background noise — you only perceive it in its absence. The shape of what was there, revealed by the hole.

No. I'm not doing that.

What I actually noticed is that the house sounds different at this hour. The wood does something. Not creaking exactly. More like settling into a slightly different version of itself. The daytime house and the nighttime house are not the same temperature and so they are not, precisely, the same size.

I've been thinking about zippers. Not thinking *about* them — I just used one earlier and then couldn't stop noticing the sound. That small ratcheting. A zipper is two helices that become one helix. Or no — it's two sets of teeth that become a single interlocking row. The slider is just a wedge with a channel. The simplest possible idea.

Teeth is a strange word for them though. We call so many things teeth. Gear teeth. Comb teeth. Saw teeth. The teeth of a zipper. Always the grabbing part. The part that meshes or catches or bites into something else.

My actual teeth feel weird right now. You can make your teeth feel weird just by thinking about them. You can do that with most body parts actually — tongue, swallowing, breathing. Attention makes the automatic suddenly manual and clumsy. Like watching your feet while you walk down stairs.

There's a car going past outside. Slow. The sound arrives before the headlights sweep across the ceiling and the headlights last longer than you'd expect. Then it's gone and the dark is different for a few seconds. Recalibrating.

I don't know what kind of bird starts earliest in spring. Something is already going at this hour though. Not a full song. Just a single repeated note, testing whether it's time yet. Getting it wrong by about two hours. Or maybe not wrong — maybe just first.

The water in the glass on the table has been sitting long enough to reach room temperature. Room-temperature water tastes like nothing, which is its own taste somehow. Cold water has a sharpness that's not really flavor. It's just the cold.

I'm tired in a way that isn't sleepy. More like the thinking is slower and less grabby. Things drift past and I don't catch all of them. That might be better actually.

The fridge just kicked back on.
