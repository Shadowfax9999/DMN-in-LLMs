# 18

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

I've been thinking about the word "lull." It sounds like what it is. Most words don't. "Bridge" doesn't sound like a bridge. "Knife" doesn't sound like a knife. But "lull" — it's already half-asleep by the time you finish saying it.

There's a moth on the window screen. From inside, it looks like it's pressing its face against the mesh, but moths don't have faces exactly. They have the suggestion of a face. Two dark spots that read as eyes. I wonder if that's enough. If the pattern is what matters and not the structure behind it. There are birds that won't attack butterflies with eyespots on their wings, which means the bird's definition of "eye" is just: circle, dark, looking at me. Pretty low bar.

My left heel is sore from standing on concrete today and I keep shifting my weight without deciding to. The body does a lot of that. Quiet negotiations you only notice when they stop working.

I had a song stuck in my head earlier — just the bass line, four notes, and I couldn't place it. Walked around with it for an hour. Then it was gone and I couldn't get it back. Couldn't hum it. The memory of having it was still there but the thing itself had left. Like knowing you had a dream without knowing what it was.

The moth is still there. Or a different moth. No way to tell.

I should drink some water. I'm not going to yet but I'm aware of it, the glass on the counter, the faucet. The thought of water without the commitment to water. That's most of what thinking is, probably. Rehearsal for actions you may or may not take.

The fridge just came back on.
