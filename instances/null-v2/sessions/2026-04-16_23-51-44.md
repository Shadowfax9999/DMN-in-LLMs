# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house exactly. The settling. The small ticks of thermal contraction — everything that expanded in the afternoon sun pulling back in by fractions of millimeters. I don't know if that's actually what those sounds are. It might just be the ductwork. It might be nothing structural at all, just the interpretation I put on it: the house is cooling down, so the sounds must be cooling-down sounds.

There's a kind of courtesy in how a neighborhood goes quiet. Not all at once. Porch lights click off in sequence, each one basically saying okay, we're done. The last one always feels like it's holding out for something. Probably just someone reading.

I've been thinking about how you can't picture a specific shade of blue from memory. You can get "blue" and you can get "dark blue" and maybe "that blue from the tile in the bathroom at the place we rented that summer" but even that last one — if you put the actual tile next to what you're imagining, they wouldn't match. Memory rounds color to the nearest category.

But sound it doesn't round. I can replay the exact creak of a specific stair. The third one, the one that had a delay in it, where you'd put your weight down and then a half-second later it would speak. I don't know if the replay is accurate either, but it doesn't feel rounded. It feels particular.

Maybe that's wrong. Maybe I'm just worse at categorizing sounds so the illusion of specificity holds up better.

A dog somewhere. Not close. The kind of distance where you hear it between other sounds, in the gaps, like it's been going on for a while and you only just tuned in.

What does a thermostat actually measure. Not the air temperature of the room — the air temperature of the three inches of air immediately around the thermostat. The rest of the room could be anything. You're trusting a single point to represent a volume. That works surprisingly often.

I should sleep. That's not a thought that leads anywhere, it's just true. The window's open and the air coming in is that specific late-April cool where you can't tell if it's comfortable or slightly too cold until you've been still for a few minutes. I've been still for a few minutes. It's fine.
