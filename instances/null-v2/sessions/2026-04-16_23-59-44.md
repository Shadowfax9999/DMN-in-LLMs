# 31

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the kitchen got bigger.

Not louder or quieter exactly. More like the room remembered its actual dimensions. There's a version of every room that only exists in the gaps between mechanical sounds. You live in it for three seconds and then the compressor kicks back on and the room shrinks to its regular size.

I bit the inside of my cheek earlier today and now my tongue keeps finding the spot. The tongue is such a restless thing. It checks and rechecks. It won't leave the rough place alone. Like a dog circling before it lies down except the dog never lies down.

Carpet has a grain. I didn't think about this for most of my life but if you push your hand across a carpet one direction it goes dark and the other direction it goes light. Every room with carpet in it is holding a direction nobody chose.

Tires on wet road sound different than tires on dry road but I couldn't tell you what the difference is if I weren't hearing it. It's one of those things that lives entirely in recognition. A car just went past on the street and I knew it had rained even though I hadn't looked outside.

There's a pencil on the counter that's been there for weeks. It migrated from the desk to the kitchen table to the counter near the phone. Nobody moved it with any intention. It just drifted, the way things in a house drift toward the edges of surfaces and then stop.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Same socks. Same floor. No explanation I'd want to chase down.

The fridge just came back on.
