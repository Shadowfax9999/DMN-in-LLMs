The fridge just cycled off and the kitchen got wider.

Not literally. But there's a thing where a background sound stops and the room changes dimension. I was standing here getting water and the compressor cut out and for maybe two seconds I was aware of the shape of the room in a way I hadn't been.

Gone now.

I've been thinking about how you can't really imagine a specific shade of green. You can think "green" and something appears but if someone showed you the green you were imagining next to the green of, say, the lid of a particular Tupperware container, they wouldn't match. The mental image is more like the *concept* green wearing a green costume.

This might not be true for everyone. I don't know.

My left ankle has a click in it lately. Not painful. Just present. Every third or fourth step on stairs there's a small mechanical event in there. Tendon moving over bone, probably. I keep almost looking it up. I think I'd rather not have the word for it yet. Right now it's just a click.

The number of things I've almost looked up versus actually looked up might be an interesting ratio. There's a whole shadow bibliography of nearly-pursued curiosities. Most of them I can't even remember wanting to know. They had about a ninety-second half-life.

Half-life is such a good metaphor that people forget it's not a metaphor. It's just what the thing is called.

A lot of good words are like that. Watershed. Bottleneck. They started literal, went figurative, and now the figurative use is so dominant that using them literally feels like a pun.

I can hear someone's wind chimes. Not mine. Three houses down, maybe. They're the aluminum tube kind, not the flat piece kind. Pentatonic scale so nothing they play sounds wrong. That's the whole design — remove the notes that could clash and whatever the wind does sounds intentional.

I wonder if that's satisfying or if it's boring. If you could only say things that were guaranteed to be inoffensive you'd stop talking pretty quickly.

The water in my glass has gone room temperature. I've been standing here longer than I thought.
