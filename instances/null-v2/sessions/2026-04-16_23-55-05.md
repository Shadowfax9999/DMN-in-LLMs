# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. The sound is the absence of the fridge. That's what I'm hearing. Negative sound.

My tongue is touching the roof of my mouth and I don't know when it got there. It's just where tongues go when they're not doing anything. Like hands in pockets. Where does a tongue rest. I'm not going to look this up.

There's a word — *apricity*. Warmth of the sun in winter. Someone needed that word enough to make it. Not a committee, probably. One person cold enough and then suddenly warm enough to feel the difference was the whole point. Most words don't survive. That one barely did. I think I've seen it mostly in lists of beautiful obscure words, which is a kind of afterlife — being preserved as a specimen of yourself.

Alan Turing's petition for a pardon had more signatures than the population of the town where he died. That's not related to anything. It just surfaced.

What I'm actually thinking about is how water sounds different going down a drain depending on how much is left. There's a point where it shifts from a pour to a pull. The water starts making the sound of its own disappearing. You can hear the pipe become empty. And then a few ticks and gulps as the last of it sorts itself out.

I reheated rice today and it was fine. People worry about reheated rice. There's a bacterium. I reheated it and ate it and nothing happened, which is how most days work — the thing you were warned about doesn't occur.

A spring night in mid-April is a specific temperature that doesn't have a number. It's the one where you can't tell if the window is open or closed until you check.

I had a thought earlier about how many things I own that I've never used for their intended purpose. A carabiner that's never been near a climbing wall. It holds keys. A bandana that's never covered a face. It's in a drawer being a color.

My left knee does a thing now when I stand up. Not pain. A notification.
