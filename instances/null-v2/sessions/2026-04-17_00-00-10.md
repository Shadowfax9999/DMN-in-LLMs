# 32

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the kitchen got bigger. That's what silence does to a room — gives it back its dimensions.

I was thinking about how you can't hum and breathe in at the same time. Or — you can, actually, some people can circular breathe, didgeridoo players, but for most of us there's a gate. Sound out, air in. You have to pick.

That feels related to something about reading and writing. You can't do both at the same moment. The eye that's composing and the eye that's receiving — same organ, different directions. I knew someone who said she couldn't read fiction while she was drafting a novel. Not wouldn't. Couldn't. The door only opens one way at a time.

Doors that only open one way. Turnstiles. The bar across your hip, that ratcheting sound. I haven't been through a turnstile in maybe two years. The subway ones in New York used to have that full-height cage version in some stations, the kind that made you feel briefly processed, like a coin in a sorting machine.

Coin sorting. The way pennies sound different from quarters falling into the tray. Pitch as a function of mass and diameter. No — I said I wasn't going to do that. They just sound different. The penny is thin and bright and the quarter has a duller knock to it.

My grandmother had a jar of foreign coins that were worthless. Lira, drachma, old francs. She kept them on a shelf with a broken clock and a geode cracked in half. The geode bothered me as a kid. The outside looked like a dirt clod and the inside was purple crystal and I couldn't get those two things to be the same object.

I still can't, actually.

The wind just moved something on the porch. A plastic pot, maybe, scraping an inch across concrete. Spring wind is different from fall wind — warmer obviously but also less directional. Fall wind has an argument. Spring wind is just restless.

I'm restless. It's almost midnight and I'm not tired and I'm not doing anything and that's fine. The neighbor's light is on across the way, the blue of a screen in a dark room. Somebody else not sleeping. We're not doing it together but we're doing it at the same time, which is its own kind of thing.
