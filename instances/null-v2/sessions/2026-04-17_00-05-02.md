# 44

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house got wider.

That's not a metaphor. There's a hum you stop hearing and when it stops you hear the stopping. The kitchen has more air in it now. The tap is dripping but only every eight or nine seconds, irregular enough that you can't stop waiting for the next one.

I was thinking about how you can't hum and whistle at the same time. I tried it once as a kid and couldn't figure out where the failure was — mouth, throat, both. You can hum and tap your teeth together. You can whistle and blink. But hum-and-whistle breaks something.

Friday already. The weeks have a texture to them that I can never describe while I'm inside one. Monday has a specific gravity. Friday at midnight doesn't feel like Friday yet — it feels like Thursday that stayed too long.

There's a moth on the window screen. From inside, with the lamp on, I can see its underside, which is paler than you'd expect. Most of the color is on the top of the wings. The underside is for the branch, the bark, the staying-alive part. The top is for the other moths.

Or maybe not for anyone. Maybe the top is just what happened.

I keep almost thinking about rope. Not in any useful way. Just the twist of it — how two things that aren't strong get strong by being wrapped around each other in opposition. Thread isn't strong. But thread arguing with thread is rope.

The drip again. I lost count of the seconds that time.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't, which means I'm sitting with one foot tucked under me and the other on the tile. I didn't decide to sit this way. The body just arranges itself and then you notice the arrangement.

Rope, though. There's something about how it works that feels like it should apply to other things but I'm not going to chase that down. It's rope. It twists. It holds.

The fridge is starting again.
