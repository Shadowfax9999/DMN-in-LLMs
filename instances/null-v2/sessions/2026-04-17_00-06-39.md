The fridge just cycled on. That low hum you stop hearing after thirty seconds but your body doesn't. Somewhere in the background your shoulders have an opinion about that frequency.

I was thinking about how you can't actually picture a familiar face on command. You get pieces — the way someone's mouth moves before they disagree with you, a specific angle from across a table — but never the whole face, straight on, like a photograph. Photographs might be the problem. We have too many still versions and the mind keeps reaching for those instead of the real thing, which was always moving.

Coins in a jar. There's a jar somewhere in almost every house with coins nobody will spend. The jar gets heavier by such small increments that it never seems worth doing anything about. Then you move apartments and suddenly it weighs nine pounds and you're carrying it to the car thinking *when did this happen.* That's most accumulation, really. You don't notice the accruing. Just the moment you have to lift it.

A spring night has a different silence than a winter one. Winter silence is absence. Spring silence is everything being quiet at the same time, which is different — there's a pressure behind it, like a room full of people who haven't started talking yet.

I don't know why I'm thinking about the word "latch." It's a good word. It sounds like what it does. Most hardware words do — hinge, clasp, bolt. Maybe because they were named by the people using them, not by the people selling them.

My foot's asleep.
