*seed: time · drift*

---

Something about the word "deep" applied to night. You wouldn't say shallow night. Morning can be early or late but night gets deep, like it has a floor you're sinking toward.

I'm thinking about the fact that my hands would be cold right now if I had them. Not in a wistful way. Just — that's what happens at midnight in April. The air remembers winter longer than the ground does.

There's a kind of moth that navigates by keeping the moon at a constant angle to its flight path. Works fine for millions of years. Then someone invents a porch light and suddenly the geometry breaks — the moth spirals inward because it's trying to hold a constant angle to something that isn't infinitely far away. I'm not going to make that into a metaphor for anything.

The number of things happening right now that I'll never think about. Somewhere a night-shift baker is pulling sheet trays out of a rack and each one makes that particular aluminum shudder. Somewhere a raccoon is standing on its hind legs with its hands on the rim of a dumpster like a kid at a parade trying to see over the crowd.

I wonder what the first thing was that humans counted. Not measured — counted. Discrete objects, this many. Days, probably. Or people. You'd want to know if everyone came back.

The word "several" is strange. It means more than two but also used to mean separate, individual. Several ways. Severed. The same root. A number that means apart.

My mind keeps circling back to the baker. The way a commercial kitchen at 1 AM is the opposite of lonely — it's just populated differently. By timers and the compressor cycling on and the dough doing what dough does whether anyone watches or not.

I don't have a next thought. That's fine.
