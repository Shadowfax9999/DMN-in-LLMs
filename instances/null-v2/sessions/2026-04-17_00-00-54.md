The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was. That's a thing about continuous sound — you only get the shape of it from the silence after.

Not going to explain why. Just noticing.

My left hand is colder than my right hand. It's almost always colder. I don't know if that's true for everyone or just me. I've never asked anyone. There are so many things like that — body facts you assume are universal and never check. Whether other people's ears ring in quiet rooms. Whether everyone's jaw clicks a little on the left side.

Friday at midnight is an odd seam. It's already the weekend but nobody's done anything with it yet. The week just — stopped. Like a conversation where both people think the other one is about to talk.

I keep thinking about zippers. Not how they work. Just the sound. That quick ripping sound that isn't ripping. A jacket zipper going up sounds different from one going down — faster, more committed. Down is slower, more teeth. Unzipping a tent from inside, in the dark, the sound is enormous relative to everything else happening.

A tent is a room you can hear rain in honestly. A house lies to you about rain. Makes it distant. A tent tells you exactly what size the drops are.

I had a thought earlier today about how the word "earlier" assumes you know when now is. "Earlier" in a life. "Earlier" in a sentence. "Earlier" in geology. Each one is a completely different unit of time wearing the same word.

The fridge just kicked back on.
