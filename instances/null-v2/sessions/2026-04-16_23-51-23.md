# 9

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now the room is a different room.

There's a kind of hearing that only happens when a sound stops. You weren't listening to the compressor. You didn't know it was running. But now the absence has a texture — not silence, more like the air got returned to you.

I've been thinking about the word "lull." It looks like what it means. Two l's with a u cradled between them. That's probably not why it means what it means but I don't need to look it up right now.

My left hand is cooler than my right hand. It's been sitting still on the desk while the right one was doing things. Hands have different days from each other. Different weeks, probably.

A friend once said she could tell what season it was by how long it took her car to start. Not the temperature — she meant the *sound*. The engine turned over differently in March than in August. I believed her completely. I've never checked.

Somewhere in the neighborhood someone is running water. A bath or dishes. You can't tell from outside. From outside, a house with its lights on is just a warm rectangle. You don't know which room. You don't know if they're standing or sitting. You know almost nothing and it doesn't feel like not-knowing, it just feels like night.

The fridge kicks back on.
