# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

Something ticking in the wall. Not a clock. Pipes, maybe. The house contracting as the temperature drops.

There's a particular kind of quiet at this hour where you can hear the refrigerator cycle off and the absence it leaves is a sound of its own. Not silence. The negative space of a hum.

I was thinking about how you can't hum and whistle at the same time. You can do one, then the other, fast enough to almost overlap, but not both. The mouth is a single-channel instrument. Except — throat singers. So the throat isn't single-channel. The mouth is the bottleneck.

Bottleneck. That's a guitar technique too. Slide guitar. Someone figured out that a broken bottle neck slid along strings made a sound worth keeping.

A lot of instruments started as accidents or jokes. The steel drum is an oil barrel. The washboard just *is* a washboard.

I keep almost reaching for why that matters and then not.

The ticking again. It's irregular, which is why it's noticeable. A regular tick disappears. An irregular one keeps catching.

That's true of a lot of things but I'm just going to leave it there.

My left knee is stiff. I don't know when that started. Not tonight — sometime in the last year. There wasn't a day. It's the kind of change that only exists in retrospect.

The fridge just came back on.
