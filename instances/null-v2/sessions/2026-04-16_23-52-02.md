# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you suddenly hear how loud the quiet is. Not loud. But present.

There's a moth on the screen door. It's not moving toward the porch light, it's just sitting on the mesh. Maybe it already arrived. Maybe arriving was the whole thing and now it's just a moth on a screen.

I was thinking earlier about how you can't actually remember a smell. You can remember that something smelled a certain way — you can remember the word for it, or the feeling around it — but you can't replay it the way you can replay a melody in your head. Or maybe some people can. I can't tell if that's a limit of memory or a limit of language about memory.

Rubber cement. I remember rubber cement from elementary school. Not the smell exactly but the fact of the smell, and the way it made the back of a photograph slightly raised and bumpy, and how that felt like a mistake even when it was what you were supposed to do.

The moth left.

Somewhere in the neighborhood someone is running a dishwasher — you can tell because of that particular low hum that's too steady to be an animal and too quiet to be a car. Houses at night are just boxes of small machines keeping things cold or clean or breathing.

I don't know what year rubber cement stopped being in classrooms. Glue sticks, probably. Glue sticks replaced it and nobody marked the day.

The fridge just came back on.
