# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the kitchen got larger.

Not louder. Larger. The corners come back when the hum stops. I never notice the hum starting but I always notice it stopping. There's probably a name for that asymmetry. I don't need to know it right now.

My left sock has a twist in it, near the toe seam. I've been aware of it for about forty minutes without fixing it. That's interesting — the threshold where mild discomfort becomes part of the background. If someone asked me "are you comfortable" I'd say yes. But the sock is right there.

Tape. I was trying to find tape earlier and opened three drawers. Every house has a tape drawer but it's never the same drawer twice. The tape migrates. It ends up near batteries and rubber bands and a single birthday candle, still bent from the last cake. I closed all three drawers without finding it and then forgot what I needed it for.

The word "April" doesn't sound like a month. It sounds like a person. Most months sound like what they are — January is heavy and sealed, November is brown — but April just sounds like someone's name. I think it's the two syllables. June does this too.

A dog somewhere. Not close. The kind of bark that's not about anything — just checking in with the dark.

I used to think pencils were made by drilling a hole through the wood and threading the graphite in. I believed this for an embarrassingly long time. The actual method — two grooved halves glued together — is less elegant but more obvious once you see one split open on a sidewalk. Most of the things I assumed were difficult turn out to be two simple halves glued together.

That last thought wanted to become something bigger and I'm going to let it not.

The twist in the sock is gone. I didn't fix it. It just resolved on its own at some point while I was thinking about pencils.
