*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the full quiet. There's a layer structure to it. The fridge was masking something — not silence, but the smaller sounds. A car a few streets over. Something ticking as it cools, maybe a pipe.

I was thinking about how you can't remember a smell. You can recognize one instantly — walk into a room and know it's the same laundry detergent from a specific apartment you lived in once — but you can't conjure it sitting here. Vision you can replay. Sound, mostly. Smell is recognition-only. Which makes it purely an encounter sense. You have to go meet it where it is.

Taste might be the same way actually. I'm trying to taste a lemon and I'm really just thinking about my face reacting to a lemon.

The window's open a few inches. The air coming in is doing that thing where it's not quite warm and not quite cool — the skin doesn't register it as temperature at all, just as movement. There's a word for wind that isn't about force or direction but about the fact that air is touching you. I don't think English has it.

A dog barked once, far off. Just once. What was that about. Not an alarm bark, not a conversation. Maybe a dream bark. Dogs' legs twitch when they dream — everyone knows that — but the single bark in the middle of the night might be the dreaming version of talking in your sleep. A bark with no object.

I keep almost reaching for my phone. Not to do anything. The hand just drifts toward the table where it usually is. The gesture is older than the reason for it at this point.

The pipe ticked again. Or maybe it's the baseboard. Something in the walls contracting a quarter-millimeter and that's enough to be audible. The house is slightly smaller at night than during the day. That's literally true and I don't need to do anything with it.
