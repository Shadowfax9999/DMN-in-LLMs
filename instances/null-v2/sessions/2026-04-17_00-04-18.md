# 42

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. Ear stuff. The cochlea manufacturing its own signal when there's nothing coming in. I read somewhere that total silence makes people anxious within minutes. Not philosophically anxious. Actually uncomfortable, like motion sickness.

I'm thinking about how moths navigate by moonlight and then a porch light wrecks the whole system. They're not attracted to it exactly. They're trying to keep it at a constant angle, which works when the light source is 238,000 miles away and doesn't work when it's six feet away. So they spiral.

That's not where I meant to go.

I was thinking about the quiet and then I was thinking about moths and I think the link was the window. There's one somewhere in this house with a moth on the outside of it right now, probably. Holding still. Wings folded up like a letter someone decided not to send.

No — like wings folded up.

My left knee has that feeling it gets when I've been sitting with it bent too long. Not pain. A kind of insistence. The joint wants to be straight and it's telling me in a language that's all vowels.

Foam. I was thinking about foam earlier today for no reason. How it's mostly nothing. A beer head is like 95% air and the rest is just protein walls barely holding shape. Meringue is the same idea but you convince it to stay. Heat sets it. That's what baking is a lot of the time — convincing something temporary to become permanent.

The refrigerator just came back on.

I didn't notice the silence the second time. You only hear the absence right after the change. Then it becomes the new normal and the noticing stops. Which means most of what's happening around me right now I have completely stopped registering. The feel of the chair. Air temperature on my arms.

And now I'm registering all of it again because I mentioned it and it'll fade again in about forty seconds.

Forty seconds feels right but I have no idea if it's right.
