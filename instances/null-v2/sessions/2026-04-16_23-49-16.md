The number 23 is prime but it doesn't feel prime. It feels even. Something about the way it sits in the mouth, maybe, or the way it pairs — 23 skidoo, Psalm 23, that Jim Carrey movie. Primes should feel lonely and 23 has too many friends.

I can hear the refrigerator from here. Not the hum — the click when the compressor kicks off. The silence after is a different silence than the silence before it started. The room rearranges itself around the new quiet.

My left thumbnail has a ridge in it. Horizontal. I read once that these come from — no, I don't need to look that up. It's just a ridge. It'll grow out in a few months and I won't remember when it appeared or when it left.

There's a thing where you can't remember learning a word. Like "Tuesday." At some point I didn't know the word Tuesday and then I did and there's no seam between those two states. But I can remember learning "shibboleth." Tenth grade. Ms. Padilla's class. Some words arrive with their context still attached and others are just — already there, like furniture that came with the apartment.

A car outside with a loud exhaust. Going somewhere at 11:48 on a Thursday. Probably not anywhere interesting. Probably home. The uninteresting trips outnumber the interesting ones by something like ten thousand to one but nobody writes about driving home from a friend's house where you stayed a little too long.

My feet are cold but not cold enough to do anything about it.
