# 45

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house got suddenly larger.

That's the thing about continuous sound — you don't hear it until it stops, and then what you hear isn't silence, it's the shape of the room. The walls push back a little. The ceiling lifts.

I was thinking about how Friday doesn't feel like the other days. Monday has weight, Wednesday has that hinge quality, but Friday at midnight is already dissolving into weekend, which isn't really a place. It's more like the negative space between two weeks. People say they live for the weekend but what they mean is they live for the *approach* of the weekend. Saturday morning at 11 is already leaking.

A friend of mine used to collect hotel soaps. Not use them. Collect them. Lined up on a shelf in her bathroom. Tiny rectangles and ovals, each one wrapped in paper with a typeface chosen by someone who had a meeting about it. She said she liked that they were promises nobody kept. I never asked her what she meant and now I can't.

The word "promise" comes from — actually I don't know where it comes from and I'm going to leave that alone.

My left knee has been doing a thing where it clicks on the second step going downstairs but not the first and not the third. Only the second. Only going down. The body has these specificities that resist interpretation. It's not a metaphor. It's a knee.

Somewhere in this neighborhood there's a pool pump running. Or maybe a transformer on a pole. One of those sounds that could be three blocks away or thirty feet, because it has no edges.

I keep almost thinking about how water finds the lowest point in a landscape but I'm not going to follow that into anything. It just surfaced and now it's going back down.

Friday. Still Friday. Barely.
