# 6

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the kitchen got loud with quiet.

There's a moth on the window screen. Not moving. I keep almost checking if it's actually a leaf but from here the legs are visible so it's settled. Moths rest with their wings flat. Butterflies fold theirs up. I don't know why I know that. Someone told me once or I read it on a cereal box or something.

My left hand smells like garlic from dinner. Three hours later and it's still there, fainter, almost sweet now. Garlic changes as it oxidizes. That's — no, I don't need to know the chemistry. It just smells different than it did at seven o'clock.

I've been thinking about how you can hear a car turn onto your street before you can see the headlights. The sound bends around the house before the light does. For maybe two full seconds you know a car is coming and you don't know how you know and then the light swings across the ceiling and you think oh, right.

What does a street sound like from above. Like if you were on a roof. I've never been on a roof at night. Daytime once, helping someone's dad with gutters, and the neighborhood looked like a model of itself. All the backyards visible at once. Someone had a trampoline with no net. Someone had a above-ground pool half-covered with a blue tarp. It felt like I was seeing something private even though it was all just open sky.

The moth is gone. I didn't see it leave.

Warm night. The window's open about four inches and the air coming in is heavier than the air inside. You can feel the weight of it. Not humidity exactly. Just density. Spring air at night has a thickness to it that winter air doesn't. Winter air is thin and sharp and spring air is — I want to say soft but that's not right either. It has more stuff in it. Pollen and moisture and whatever the trees are doing.

My phone screen is face-down on the counter and the notification light is blinking green through the case. I'm not going to check it. It's almost midnight and whatever it is can just keep blinking.

I should go to bed. I'm not going to yet but I should.
