# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. The sound is the absence of the fridge. A shape left behind.

My left knee has that thing where it's not exactly uncomfortable but I'm aware of it. Like it's filing a report. *Noting: knee exists. Carries weight. Has opinions about the stairs from earlier.*

I was thinking about how you can't hum and plug your nose at the same time. I tried it once in a car and the person next to me asked what I was doing and I didn't have a good answer. The air has to go somewhere. That's not interesting, that's just tubes.

Thread from somewhere: the word "April" might come from *aperire*, to open. Or it might not. There's a whole category of etymologies that are appealing enough that nobody checks too hard.

A dog is barking a few streets over. Not urgent barking. Conversational. Checking in with the dark.

I keep almost thinking about folding laundry. Not thinking about it — the thought is in the next room, like something I walked past and will walk past again. Warm cotton. Static on a sock.

The number 4 feels square and the number 7 feels sharp and I have no idea where that came from or when it started. Probably birth. Probably birth is too strong. Probably early enough that asking "when" doesn't help.

My mouth tastes like toothpaste still. Mint is a strange choice for the last flavor of the day. Aggressive. You'd think we'd have landed on something quieter.

The fridge just came back on.
