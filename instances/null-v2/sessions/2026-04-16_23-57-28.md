# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

The number twelve has always felt round to me even though it isn't. Dozens. Eggs come that way. Months. Jurors. Apostles, if you're counting. There's something about twelve that closes a set.

Six doesn't do it. Six is half of something.

I was thinking about how you can hear a refrigerator compressor cycle off but you can't hear it cycle on. You only notice the absence. Then five minutes later you realize it's been running again and you don't know when that started. There's no seam. The sound just reappears in the room like it was always there and you were the one who left.

Tape. Specifically, the sound of someone pulling tape off a roll in another room. You know exactly what it is. You could not describe why you know. It doesn't sound like anything else but it also doesn't sound like what you'd predict if someone said "adhesive separating from a roll." It's its own thing. A lot of sounds are their own thing.

My left knee does something when I stand up from sitting on the floor. Not pain. A click that has a particular depth to it, like it's from the center of the joint rather than the surface. I don't know what makes that sound. Probably I could find out. I don't especially want to.

There's a word — I'm not going to look it up — for the way plants grow toward light. I know the word and I'm not going to reach for it right now. It's just sitting back there. That's a strange sensation, knowing you know something and choosing to leave it where it is. Like a book on a shelf you can see the spine of.

Anyway it's almost midnight and the specific quality of almost-midnight in spring is that you can't tell if the air is warm or cool. It's just the same temperature as your skin. You walk through it and there's no information.
