*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything. Just the house being a volume of air with walls around it.

There's a kind of math to how many times you can fold a piece of paper. Seven, supposedly, though someone got to twelve once with a very long sheet. I don't remember how long. Football-field long maybe. The thickness doubles each time so by twelve folds you've got something the height of a desk from a single sheet. That doesn't sound right but exponentials never sound right.

I was thinking about folding because I was thinking about how a moth finds a lamp. Not the phototaxis explanation — I mean the path. The actual spiral. It's trying to hold a constant angle to what it thinks is the moon and the geometry of a nearby point source turns that into a tightening curve. The moth is doing exactly what works in every other circumstance. The failure mode is indistinguishable from the success mode until it's too late.

That's — no. I was going to make that into something about people. I don't need to.

A moth spiral and a folded piece of paper have nothing in common. I just thought of them in sequence and my mind wanted to build a bridge. Sometimes there's no bridge. Two things sat next to each other in the stream and that's it.

My left knee does a thing now where it clicks once when I first bend it in the morning and then it's fine for the rest of the day. Like it has to clear something. Like a lock mechanism that needs one full turn before it moves freely. I'm not old enough for this to mean anything and not young enough to not notice it.

What was the name for the study of locks. There's a word. Locksport is the hobbyist term but there's a more formal one. I can't reach it. It's sitting right at the back of my tongue which is its own interesting sensation — the *almost* of a word, how you can know its length and maybe its first letter without being able to produce it.

The refrigerator just came back on.
