*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you suddenly hear how loud the quiet is. There's a sprinkler somewhere — not close, maybe two houses over. You can't tell direction with sprinklers at night.

I was thinking about how you can fold a map wrong and it will never go back. Not in the sense that it's ruined, just that the creases have opinions now. They want to go a different way than you want them to go. Paper remembers.

That's not — I don't need to make that into anything.

My left foot is asleep. The specific numbness of a foot that's been tucked under you on a couch. There's a moment where you decide whether to move it and deal with the pins and needles or just leave it. I'm leaving it.

The sprinkler stopped. Or I stopped hearing it.

I keep almost thinking about envelopes. Not a specific envelope. Just the object. The way you run your tongue along one and it tastes like nothing you'd ever choose to taste. Somewhere there was a person whose job was to formulate that glue and they had to make it not-toxic but also not-good, because if it tasted good people would — actually I don't know what would happen. People would just lick envelopes more, I guess. That's not a crisis.

There's a word I can't reach. It's for the little perforated strip you tear off the side of continuous-feed printer paper. The tractor-feed edges. I used to fold those into accordion shapes. I don't think the word I'm looking for exists. I think I'm remembering a word that was never there.

My foot is fully asleep now. Committed to it.

The fridge is back on.
