# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

Something about late April that isn't quite warm yet. The air has intention but hasn't committed. You can feel it deciding.

I was thinking about hinges. Not door hinges — the hinge in a conversation where someone decides to tell the truth. You can hear it sometimes. A small pause, a breath that's slightly too long, and then the sentence comes out different than the one they'd been building. The rehearsed version just — dissolves. I don't know what happens to it. Where do unspoken sentences go.

That's not really a question I need answered.

A piano has about 230 strings but only 88 keys. The extras are sympathetic — they vibrate when nearby strings are struck, not because anyone asked them to. Most of the sound of a piano is sound that nobody directly caused.

I keep almost thinking about teeth. The way you can feel your own teeth with your tongue and they feel enormous, geological, but then you see teeth in a mirror and they're these small ordinary things. The tongue's map is wrong. Or not wrong — just working at a different scale. The tongue lives in a world where teeth are mountains.

Somewhere in this neighborhood a sprinkler is running. At this hour. That's either automated or someone fell asleep. Both of those are fine.

The word "April" comes from — no, I don't need to look that up right now.

I'm noticing that sprinkler sound is irregular. Not a steady pulse. More like someone trying to remember a phone number. Three fast, pause, two fast, long pause, three fast. It's probably just water pressure fluctuating but the pattern keeps almost resolving into something recognizable and then not.

My left hand is doing something. Just barely curling and uncurling. I don't think I told it to do that. The body has its own idle animations.

That's where I am.
