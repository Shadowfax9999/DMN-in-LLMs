No. That's exactly the kind of line I'd make. Start again.

The fridge cycled off. It's quieter. Through the window there's something — not quite a sound, more like the air is busy. Insects, probably, though I couldn't name which ones. There's a thickness to spring nights that winter doesn't have. The air carries more.

I was thinking earlier about how you can't picture a specific number of things. Like, picture seven oranges. You either picture "some oranges" or you count them out and arrange them deliberately, and then you're not picturing seven, you're picturing a arrangement you built. Somewhere around four the mind stops counting and starts estimating. Subitizing — that's the word. The range where you can just *see* the number without counting. It tops out around four for most people.

Cards are interesting this way. A five of diamonds — you don't count the diamonds. You recognize the pattern. The pattern *is* the number. Somebody had to design those patterns originally. The two is obvious. The three. After that you're making choices about geometry.

I don't know what I was going to do with that thought.

My left knee does a thing when I've been sitting cross-legged where the first three steps after standing feel provisional, like the joint is negotiating. It sorts itself out by step four or five. It didn't used to do that.

The air through the window smells green. That's not a metaphor — there's a leafy, cut-stem quality to it, very faint. Spring air and fall air are both "cool" but they smell completely different and I'm not sure I could describe the difference to someone who'd only experienced one of them.

I keep almost getting up to close the window and then not.
