# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The number twelve has always seemed too convenient. Months, hours, apostles, eggs. Like someone picked it early and everything else just agreed.

Though dozens make sense if you're dividing things. Halves, thirds, quarters, sixths. Ten only gives you halves and fifths. Fifths are useless in a kitchen. Nobody cuts a pie into fifths unless they're trying to start a fight.

I keep almost remembering a card game. Not the rules — the feeling of holding the fan of cards close to my chest in a way that suggested I had a plan. I think most card-playing is that. Performed deliberation. You already know you're going to play the seven. But you look at your hand like you're weighing something.

The word *bluff* is good. It's also a cliff face. A bluff is a cliff that doesn't follow through on its threat — it just stops, flat, and there's a river below it doing something else entirely.

There's a kind of moth that has no mouth. It emerges, mates, and starves. I don't have a thought about that. It just surfaces sometimes.

Plastic chairs in a stack make a sound when you pull one free that's specific and unrepeatable. A thick, airy pop. Suction and flex. Every school cafeteria has that sound in its bones.

I wonder if there's a name for the moment when you're carrying two grocery bags and you realize you're going to have to set one down to open the door and you just — stand there. Not solving it yet. Just standing in the problem for a second.

Probably not.

The buds on the maple outside are at that stage where they're not leaves yet but they're not buds either. Swollen. The whole tree looks like it's holding its breath. That's a cliché but it's also just what it looks like. Sometimes the cliché got there first because it was right.

My left knee pops when I stand up from sitting on the floor. It has for years. It doesn't hurt. It's just a fact my knee announces to the room.

I think I was thinking about twelve because of the clock.
