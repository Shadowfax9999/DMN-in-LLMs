# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled on. That low hum you stop hearing after a minute but your body doesn't.

I was thinking about how you can't whistle and hum at the same time. Or — you can, actually, but it becomes a different thing. A chord from one person. I tried it once in a parking garage and the reverb made it sound like a pipe organ being stepped on.

Parking garages always smell like the same parking garage. Concrete dust and something sweet underneath, maybe the sealant they use on the floors. Every level has the same layout but you always forget which one your car is on. There's a moment where you're just standing between two identical concrete pillars pressing the key fob and listening for the chirp and you could be anywhere. Any city. Any year after about 1995.

My left knee does a thing now where it clicks on the first bend of the day. Not painful. Just informational. Like a status report from a machine I didn't know I was operating.

Sheets dry differently on a line versus in a dryer. Line-dried sheets are stiffer and smell like whatever was in the air that day. My grandmother's sheets smelled like cut grass and faintly like the metal of the clothesline itself. Dryer sheets smell like a chemical's idea of a meadow. I don't know which I prefer. That's not true — I prefer the dryer. The nostalgia is for the memory, not the experience.

A dog is barking somewhere far enough away that I can't tell the direction. Just the fact of a dog, somewhere, with an opinion about something.

I should drink more water. I never think about drinking water until it's past midnight and then it seems urgent, like the day used a resource I forgot to replenish. The glass from the tap has tiny bubbles on the inside for the first thirty seconds. They form in lines along invisible scratches in the glass.

I don't know what I was going to think about next.
