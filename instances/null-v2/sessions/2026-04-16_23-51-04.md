# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. But something in the wall. Some pipe or wire doing its thing at a frequency that only exists when the fridge isn't running.

I've never understood how I decide which leg to cross. Right over left right now. I didn't choose it. There's no preference I can name but there's clearly a preference because it's always this one first and the other one feels like wearing a shirt inside out.

Shirts inside out. My grandmother used to say that meant someone was thinking about you. Or was that backwards? Someone was talking about you. No — itchy palm means money, ears burning means talking, shirt inside out means... I don't actually know what she said it meant. I might be inventing the whole thing. The memory of her saying it is clear but the content is gone, which is a strange kind of remembering.

There's a moth on the window screen. From inside it's just a dark shape. From outside it would be all detail — the dust-fur on its body, the compound eyes. I'm seeing the silhouette version. The moth is seeing whatever moths see, which is the porch light I left on, which I should turn off, which I'm not going to.

Eleven fifty. In ten minutes it won't be Thursday anymore. There's no sensation that goes with that. Midnight on New Year's has one — forced, but real. Regular midnights just happen. The date turns over like an odometer in an empty car.

I'm thirsty but not enough to get up.
