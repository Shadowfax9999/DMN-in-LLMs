# 38

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house got loud with quiet.

There's a kind of counting you do without numbers. How many times the cat has shifted positions since I last looked. Whether the candle has lost a third or a half of itself. The milk is closer to done than not done. None of these are math. They're something else. Inventory by feel.

I was thinking about how a song you haven't heard in years can come back whole — not the melody first, not the words, but the *weight* of it, the way it sat in your chest — and then the melody fills in around that shape like water finding a channel. The feeling is the architecture. The notes are just what lives there.

But that's not quite right either. Sometimes what comes back is just a fragment. A vowel held too long. A drum fill before a chorus. And the rest doesn't come. You're left with this shard that doesn't attach to anything and you hum it to no one in a dark kitchen and it means nothing and it means everything you were at seventeen.

My left knee does a thing when I've been sitting too long where the first three steps are a negotiation. Not pain. A stiffness that's also a question. *Are we doing this? Now? Okay.* By step four it's forgotten itself.

Funny that joints forget. Funny that we say "forget" about a joint.

The word "latch" is satisfying. The thing it describes is satisfying. The small heavy click of a gate latch dropping into its cradle. There's a gate somewhere — someone's side yard — where the latch is a piece of bent rebar that drops into a pipe welded to the post. It works perfectly. It will outlast the fence. No one will ever admire it.

I don't know what I was going to think next.

The candle's definitely past half now.
