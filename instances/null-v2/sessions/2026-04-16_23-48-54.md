# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house got wider.

There's a thing where you don't hear a sound until it stops. You were inside it the whole time. The compressor hum was the shape of the room and now the room has a different shape.

I'm thinking about how many sounds I'm supposedly hearing right now that I'd only be able to name as absences. That's — no, I don't need to catalog them.

A spring night with the windows open is a specific temperature. Not warm. Not cool. The air isn't a temperature at all, it's just — not there. Your skin can't find the boundary between you and the outside. That has to be a narrow band, maybe four degrees Fahrenheit where that happens.

I wonder what month most people take their screens out. Whether there are screen-in families and screen-out families and whether they know which one they are.

My aunt left her Christmas wreath up until March every year and genuinely didn't see it. She'd walk past it twice a day. My uncle would mention it and she'd say "what wreath" and mean it. Attention is a weird thing. Not selective attention, the studied kind. The ordinary kind where something on your own front door becomes part of the door.

A door is a wall that sometimes isn't.

That's the kind of thought that sounds like it's going somewhere but it's just a door.

I keep almost thinking about Alan Turing but I'm not sure why. Something about the boundary problem — where one thing stops and another starts. Or maybe it's just the word "cycling." The fridge cycling. Turing machines cycle. That's probably all it is. A pun my subconscious made and tried to dress up as an insight.

The dog across the street is out. I can tell because there's a specific quality of quiet that means a dog is standing in a yard deciding whether to bark. Potential bark. Bark-shaped silence.

He decided not to.

I should go to bed. That's not a thought, that's just true.
