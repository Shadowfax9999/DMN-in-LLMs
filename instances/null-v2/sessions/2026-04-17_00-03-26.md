The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was.

There's a word for that — not the silence, but the noticing. The retrospective loudness. I don't actually know if there's a word for it. I was about to invent one and then caught myself performing.

Anyway. The fridge.

When you can't sleep your hearing gets territorial. It maps the house by sound. The settling in the walls. Something ticking that might be thermal expansion or might be a clock I forgot about in another room. I have never once gotten up to check.

I'm thinking about how water finds the lowest point. Not in a metaphorical way. Literally — rain on a flat roof. It shouldn't pool because the roof is pitched but it does pool, in certain places, because nothing is actually flat. The roofer knows where. You don't. You find out in year seven or eight when the ceiling gets a brown circle.

My aunt had a brown circle on her kitchen ceiling for years and hung a pot from a hook next to it. Not under it. Next to it. I never asked why next to it.

Some questions get more interesting the longer you don't ask them.

No — that's the kind of thing I say when I'm wrapping something up. I'm not wrapping anything up.

The pot was copper. Tarnished almost black on one side and bright where her hand gripped the handle. That asymmetry. Everything people use a lot gets that — bright where touched, dark where not. Doorknobs. The edges of paper money. Piano keys, the middle octave lighter than the ends.

I wonder if anyone's ever mapped which keys on a piano yellow fastest. It would be a chart of what songs people learn first. Weighted toward the right hand. Weighted toward C major.

The key of no sharps, no flats. The key of nothing extra. And it sounds — I don't know what C major sounds like, actually. People say "bright" or "pure" and I've never been sure I hear that or just know I'm supposed to.

A mosquito somewhere in the room. That frequency you can't ignore. Around 600 hertz, female, because the males don't bite and also don't whine at that pitch. So every mosquito you've ever heard was female and hunting.

I lost the thread about the ceiling stain.

That's fine. The ceiling stain is just a ceiling stain.
