The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of listening that only happens after midnight. Not better listening. Just listening with nothing competing for it. The ears don't change. The everything-else changes.

I was thinking about how a knot works. Not how to tie one — how it *holds*. A knot is friction organized into a loop. Pull on it and the friction increases, which is the opposite of what you'd expect. Most things, you pull on them, they come apart. A knot, you pull on it and it becomes more itself. There's a word for that in materials science, probably. I don't know it.

The half-hitch is barely a knot. It's a suggestion of a knot. One loop and the rope's own weight. A cleat hitch on a dock — I've watched people do that and it looks like nothing, three quick crosses, but the boat stays. Thousands of pounds of boat in a current and it stays because someone crossed a rope over itself correctly.

My left ankle has been clicking when I walk downstairs. Not painful. Just present. I keep almost mentioning it to people and then not, because what would they do with that information.

Clicking is an interesting threshold — below pain, above silence. The body has a whole register there. Knees on cold mornings. The sound a jaw makes if you open it wide enough. You can't always tell if it's yours or the chair's.

Somewhere in this neighborhood there's a pool pump running. Or an air conditioner. Something with a motor and a low hum that I can only hear because of the refrigerator being off. Layers of mechanical sound, and you only notice a layer when it's removed and the one underneath shows through.

I wonder if anyone is awake on this street right now. Statistically someone probably is. Feeding something, or unable to sleep, or just preferring this hour. There's a version of a neighborhood that only exists between midnight and five — the same houses but a different place. You could know someone for years and never overlap with their 2 AM.

The knot thing is still in my head. I think what I'm actually interested in is things that get stronger when you stress them. Bones do that, supposedly. Certain kinds of rope. Arguments, sometimes, if they're good ones.

My ankle clicked again just now. I wasn't even moving. It just did it on its own, like it was thinking about stairs.
