# 26

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "April" has always sounded wet to me. Not rain-wet. More like the inside of a mouth.

There's a kind of fly that only lives for one day. I've known that since I was maybe ten. I don't know what I expected to do with the information. I've just been carrying it around.

Carrying things around. I have a pocketknife I haven't opened in two years. It moves from the junk drawer to my jacket pocket and back. It's not sentimental. I don't think it's sentimental. It might be sentimental.

The springs inside a retractable pen are surprisingly long for what they do. You take one apart and the spring is like a third of the whole pen and you think, all that coil for a single click.

Click. The sound a dog's nails make on a kitchen floor at night when everyone's in bed. You hear the whole route. Living room, hallway, pause, kitchen, the water bowl, pause, back.

I was trying to remember earlier whether the Great Lakes are in alphabetical order on a map, west to east. Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, Ontario. They're not. They're almost not. They're close enough that someone in a bar could argue it either way.

Bar napkin math. There's a pleasure in doing arithmetic with a pen that has nothing to do with needing the answer.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs. Not up. Just down. I don't know when it started, which means it started gradually, which means there was a first time and I missed it.

A lot of things are like that.

The fridge just cycled on in some room I'm imagining. That low hum you stop hearing after eleven seconds. I made that number up but it feels close.

Close enough. Most things are close enough.

I'm tired in the way where thoughts get shorter and you don't mind.
