The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was. That's the thing about refrigerators — you only notice them in the negative. The absence arrives and you feel it as a presence.

No. That's exactly the kind of sentence I don't need to make.

The fridge stopped. The room got wider.

I'm thinking about how I know it's spring without checking. Not temperature exactly. Something about the air at night having weight to it. Winter air is thin. This air leans on you a little.

There's a word — *petrichor* — no, everyone knows that one. There's a less famous thing where plants at night do something different with their gas exchange. I don't remember what. I just remember that someone told me once that you can smell the difference between a summer night and a winter night even if they're the same temperature, and it's not imagination, it's chemistry. I don't need to know which chemistry.

Friday at midnight is an odd hour. Not late enough to be late. The people who went out are still out. The people who stayed in have been in for a while. It's the hour where you open a browser tab and then forget why.

I keep almost thinking about music but not actually thinking about it. Like there's a song at the edge of something. Not a specific song. The idea of a song. The space where one would go.

My left knee does a thing when I've been sitting too long where it doesn't hurt but it announces itself. Just: hello, I'm a knee, I'm at an angle, I have been at this angle. It's not pain. It's more like the knee version of the fridge cycling off. Sudden awareness of a thing that was there the whole time.

I wonder how many times a day that happens — something that was background becomes foreground for a few seconds and then sinks back. Dozens probably. Hundreds. The whole day is just a long sequence of things surfacing and submerging and you only remember the ones that surfaced while you were already paying attention to something nearby.

A car went past outside. Not fast. The sound had that late-night quality where you can hear the tires separately from the engine. During the day it's all one thing.

I'm not going to make that into a metaphor.

It's just tires on asphalt, slower than daytime, and then gone.
