# 16

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything. The house being a volume of air with a particular shape.

There's a thing where you can tell the size of a room with your eyes closed. Blind echolocation, but everyone does it a little. You feel the walls with your ears without knowing you're feeling them. A bathroom sounds different from a hallway and you know it before you know you know it.

I was thinking about how tape measures retract. That spring coiled inside — it's always under tension. The tape measure is always trying to close. You pull it open and the whole time it's waiting to go back. Every measurement you take is a negotiation with a spring that disagrees.

Unrelated: the word "penultimate" gets misused so often it might just mean what people think it means now. That's how it works. That's how "literally" started meaning "not literally." There's no court of appeals.

My left knee does a thing when I stand up from sitting on the floor. Not pain. A click, dry, like a latch. It's been doing it for maybe two years. I don't remember when it started, which means it started slowly, which means there was a first time and I didn't notice it. Strange that a body can change and the person in it just absorbs the change without marking it.

The air through the window smells like wet bark. Something blooming underneath that but I can't name it. I'm not going to try to name it.

The tape measure thing — I think what I was actually thinking about is objects that are always in one state and you temporarily force them into another. Clothespins. Binder clips. Spring-loaded door hinges. Their resting state is closed. You open them and they're just waiting. The default is shut.

Other objects are the opposite. A door propped open with a rubber wedge. A book held open face-down on an armrest. Their default is closed too but you've overridden it with something dumb and physical. A wedge. Gravity. Your thumb.

I can hear a car on a wet road. The pitch drops as it passes. I always expect it to and it always does and it's satisfying every time, the way a familiar chord progression is satisfying. You know what's coming and the knowing is the pleasure.

My knee doesn't click when I walk. Only when I stand up from low. Only that one transition. The rest of the day it's just a knee.

It's late. The fridge will cycle back on soon.
