*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops.

There's a kind of hearing that only works backward. You become aware of a sound at the moment it's absent. Which means some part of you was listening the whole time and not telling you about it.

What else is that true of.

Gravity, probably. You'd notice if it stopped. The pressure of the chair seat against the backs of your thighs — there it is now, I've ruined it, now I'm aware of it. It'll fade again in about forty seconds. There's probably a name for that interval. The time it takes for a sensation to become furniture again.

Friday at midnight is nobody's time. Thursday people have gone to bed. Friday people haven't started yet. It belongs to whoever's just sitting here.

I keep almost thinking about ice. Not ice as a concept — a specific piece of ice. The cube I dropped on the kitchen floor three days ago and kicked under the stove instead of picking up. It's gone now. Evaporated or absorbed into dust or whatever happens to ice under stoves. But for a while there was a tiny puddle I was responsible for and chose not to deal with, and that's the kind of thing that floats up at midnight on a Friday when no one's asking you to think about anything.

The word "midnight" is so literal. Mid. Night. Right in the middle of it. Noon is the same — from *nona hora*, ninth hour, which was 3 PM, and somehow it migrated six hours backward over a few centuries. Time words drift.

A dog is barking somewhere but it's far enough away that it sounds like it could be anything. Could be a gate. Could be a branch.

My left sock is slightly twisted. The seam is under my second toe instead of across the tips. I'm not going to fix it.

That's where I am.
