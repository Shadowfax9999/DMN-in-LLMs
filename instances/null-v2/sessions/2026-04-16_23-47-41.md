# 1

*seed: time*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not silence — the opposite. Everything the compressor was masking. A creak somewhere in the floor joists that has no reason. The tick of something cooling or warming, impossible to say which.

My left thumb has a callus I don't remember earning. I keep running my right index finger over it. It's satisfying in a way that doesn't need a word.

There's a thing where you can't remember if you locked the door. Not because you forgot — because the action was so automatic it didn't register as an event. It happened below the threshold of experience. You were already thinking about something else. Your hands knew what to do and your hands did it and your hands didn't tell you.

I wonder how much of a day happens like that. Below the threshold.

The word "threshold" is doing something — it's pulling me toward a doorway and I'm going to let it. Not a specific one. Just the geometry. The way a threshold is a strip of metal or wood that belongs to neither room. You step on it and you're in transit. You can't live on a threshold. Or I guess you could but it would be a strange commitment.

Cats sit in doorways. Right in the middle of them. Claiming the threshold as territory. Maybe that's the most strategic position in a house — the point of maximum optionality.

My neck is stiff on the left side. I keep tilting my head like that's going to fix it. It's not going to fix it.

Somewhere outside there's a bird that shouldn't be awake. One bird. Not singing, just a single repeated note at irregular intervals, like it's checking whether it's still night. Getting the same answer each time and asking again anyway.

I should go to bed. That thought has been sitting here for twenty minutes and I keep stepping over it.
