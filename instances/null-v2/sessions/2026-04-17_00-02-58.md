# 39

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. It does that thing where the silence arrives in layers. First the compressor stops, then you notice the clock, then under the clock there's something — not a sound exactly. The pressure of the walls being walls.

My left thumb has a callus from I don't know what. I keep running my right index finger over it. It's like a little plateau. I don't remember getting it. The body just goes around doing things and reporting back later with evidence.

Flour. I was thinking about flour earlier, or maybe yesterday. How it's explosive in the right concentration. Grain elevators going up like bombs. There's a ratio — particles to air — where anything becomes dangerous. Sawdust too. Which means every woodshop is technically managing an explosive environment and just not talking about it.

I wonder if there's a name for the feeling of knowing a room is clean because you just cleaned it versus knowing a room is clean because it looks clean. They're completely different. The first one fades. By morning you'll walk in and only have the second kind, which is weaker.

A dog somewhere. Not close. Two neighborhoods over maybe. You can tell distance with barking but not direction, or — no, you can tell direction. It's distance that gets vague after a certain point. Everything past three blocks is just "out there."

My grandmother kept rubber bands around the doorknob of the pantry. Not in a drawer. Around the doorknob. I never asked why. Now I can't. That's not sad, it's just — there are maybe ten thousand small facts about any person that die when they die and nobody notices because nobody knew to ask.

The callus on my thumb again. I think it might be from the can opener. The one where you have to squeeze and turn at the same time. That grip.

It's cold for this late in the season. The window's open two inches and I should close it but I won't yet.
