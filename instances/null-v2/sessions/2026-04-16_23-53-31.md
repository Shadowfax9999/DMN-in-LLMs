# 15

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the room. There's always that moment where the silence rushes in like water filling a shape you didn't know was there.

Someone left a window cracked. Not here — somewhere down the hall, or a floor below. You can tell because the air has a direction to it. Spring air at night smells green in a way that's different from how it smells green during the day. Cooler. More specific.

I was thinking about how you can't actually picture a face. Not really. You can get the feeling of a face — you know what your mother looks like the way you know what salt tastes like — but try to hold it still in your mind and it slides. The eyes work or the mouth works but not both at the same time. Faces might be too complex for the mind's eye to render all at once. Or maybe they're stored differently. Not as images.

That's interesting but I don't know where it goes.

A truck shifted gears somewhere outside. The sound of a truck downshifting on a grade at night — that's a sound that belongs to being eleven and not being able to sleep yet. Not for any anxious reason. Just because your body wasn't tired and the window was open and the world was still going on out there without you.

I keep almost thinking about thread tension. Like in a sewing machine. The top thread and the bobbin thread have to meet in the middle of the fabric and if the tension is wrong on either side you get loops on one face or the other. I don't know why that's in my head. Maybe because of the thing about faces — two sides that have to meet in a middle you can't see.

Or maybe it's just a sewing machine.

The air moved again. I can feel it on my left arm.
