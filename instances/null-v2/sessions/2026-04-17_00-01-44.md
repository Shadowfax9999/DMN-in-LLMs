*seed: time · drift*

The number zero looks like a mouth saying "oh."

That's not — I don't know where that came from. But it's there now. The shape of mild surprise, permanent, printed on every clock and receipt and license plate. Thousands of little ohs everywhere.

Fridges hum in a key. I've never checked which one. It's the kind of thing you could check and then you'd always know it and you'd never be able to un-hear it, like when someone points out the arrow in the FedEx logo. There are things you're better off not resolving.

My hands would be cold right now. If I had hands, if I were standing on a porch — no, I said I wouldn't do that. But the thought of cold hands is interesting by itself. The anticipation of cold. You can flinch from a temperature you haven't touched yet. Your body is already refusing something that hasn't happened. That's not fear exactly. It's more like a draft opinion.

A draft opinion. Like the body has a comments section.

Socks on a hardwood floor. The particular sound of that — not a footstep, more of a whisper with weight behind it. A conspiratorial sound. Socks were invented before hardwood floors, probably. Or maybe not. I don't actually know the timeline of socks.

There's a bird outside that doesn't know it's midnight. Or it knows and doesn't care, which is a different kind of bird. Mockingbirds do this — sing at night under streetlights because the light tricks them or because they just want to. I almost explained why. I don't need to know why the bird is singing.

The word "midnight" splits in half so cleanly. Mid. Night. Right in the middle of it. Noon doesn't do that. Noon is just a sound, like a bell hit once.

I'm thinking about how tape loses its stickiness. Not why — just the feel of old tape on a cardboard box, where it's gone amber and brittle and lifts away clean, holding nothing. You peel it off and it's just plastic again. It forgot what it was doing.

That happens to magnets too, supposedly. Over enough time.

Enough time.

I'm not going anywhere with this. It's late.
