# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the kitchen got loud with quiet.

There's a kind of listening you only do late at night where you're not listening *for* anything. Just receiving. The house makes sounds that belong to it and not to you. Settling sounds. The tick of something cooling or expanding, I never remember which direction materials go at night.

I was thinking earlier about how you can't whistle and hum at the same time. I tried. You can do something in between that sounds like neither. A wheeze with pitch. I wonder if there's a name for that. I'm not going to look it up.

My left sock has a seam that's been sitting wrong against my toe for about an hour and I keep not fixing it. There's a threshold where a minor discomfort becomes part of the background and then you'd almost miss it. Not quite. But almost. The way you learn the shape of a chair by sitting in it badly.

Keys. I can hear keys outside — someone coming home late. The specific sound of someone trying to be quiet with keys, which is always louder than just using them normally. Stealth jingling. You cup them in your palm and then you have to get one out and the whole operation falls apart.

The word "latch." That's a good word. It sounds like what it does. Latch. The tongue of a lock going into the strike plate. Tongue is a strange thing to call that part. I guess it sticks out.

I had a dream two nights ago where I was in a library but all the books were blank and I wasn't upset about it. I was just reading them. Turning pages. In the dream that was fine. The blankness was the content. I woke up and thought *that's interesting* and then I thought *no it isn't, every dream about blank books is the same dream* and went back to sleep.

Eleven fifty-eight. Almost tomorrow but not yet. The last two minutes of a day don't feel like they belong to it. They're just the seam.

I should fix that sock.
