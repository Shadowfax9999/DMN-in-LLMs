# 3

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ears reaching for something. I read once that in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood and it becomes unbearable within minutes but I don't know if that's true or if it's one of those facts that sounds too good and just keeps getting repeated.

Friday barely exists yet. It's still Thursday's house. The dishes in the sink are Thursday's dishes. At some point — maybe when I next sleep, or maybe when the light changes — it'll become Friday, but right now the date is just a number somebody agreed on.

Socks on a hardwood floor at night feel different than socks on hardwood during the day. That's not a temperature thing. Or maybe it is a temperature thing. The floor is probably cooler. But it feels more like a category thing — daytime floor is just floor, nighttime floor is a surface you're aware of being on top of.

There's a cobweb in the corner of the window I keep meaning to deal with. No spider in it. Hasn't been for weeks probably. It just collects dust now. A net catching nothing it was designed to catch.

I wonder how long a spiderweb lasts if nothing touches it. If you sealed a room. Months? The silk is protein, so eventually something would break it down, but in a dry room with no UV — I actually don't know. Could be a long time.

The neighbor's porch light is on. It's always on. I've never seen it off. It might be on a sensor that never trips because the threshold is set wrong, or it might just be on. Some people leave porch lights on when they travel and then when they come back they forget it's on because they stop seeing it from outside.

My left knee does a thing when I stand up from sitting on the floor. Not pain. A click. It's been doing it for a year maybe. Longer. It doesn't mean anything or it means something I'm not going to find out about tonight.

The fridge just kicked back on.
