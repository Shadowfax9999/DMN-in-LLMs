# 32

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ears reaching for something. I read once that in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood. I don't know if that's true or if it's one of those facts that sounds too good so everyone repeats it.

Friday at midnight is a strange seam. It's already the weekend but nothing's changed yet. The garbage goes out tomorrow. No — today. It's already today.

I've been thinking about how water finds the lowest point. Not in a metaphorical way, just — the gutter outside has a place where leaves collect and the water backs up and makes a little pool on the sidewalk. It's been there for weeks. The leaves are black now, composting in place. Someone would have to reach in and pull them out and it would feel like pulling a wet cloth out of a drain. Nobody wants to do that so the puddle stays.

That's not interesting. But I keep noticing it.

The other thing is that my left hand is cold and my right hand isn't. I'm not doing anything different with them. Circulation is asymmetric, I guess. The heart is on the left side, you'd think the left hand would be warmer, but probably it doesn't work like that. Probably it's about which hand is moving more. The mouse hand. Okay.

Somewhere out there a train. You can't tell how far. Sound at night — it could be two miles, it could be eight. The pitch drops as it passes something but I can't hear it pass, just the horn and then the horn lower, and then the horn gone, and then the refrigerator kicks back on and that's the end of that.
