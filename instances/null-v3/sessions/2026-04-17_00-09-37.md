# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen got louder. That's the wrong way to say it. The kitchen got more present. There's a drip somewhere — not a faucet, something slower, maybe condensation on a pipe under the sink. It has no rhythm to it. You wait for the next one and it doesn't come when you expect.

I was thinking about how moths navigate. Not the porch-light thing, the other thing — how they flew before there were porch lights. Transverse orientation to the moon. Keep the moon at a constant angle and you travel in a straight line. It works because the moon is so far away that the angle barely changes as you move. Then someone invents a candle and the whole system breaks. The moth keeps correcting to hold the angle constant against a light source twenty inches away and spirals inward.

That's not tragic. I don't want to make it tragic. It's just — the system worked fine for a hundred million years and then the input changed.

There's a word in Finnish, *kuumuus*, that means heat. I don't know why that surfaced. Sometimes a word just has a good shape. Two u's in a row. English doesn't do that much. *Vacuum.* I guess *vacuum* does it.

The drip again. Longer pause that time.

I can't picture the inside of a walnut. I can picture the outside, the shell halves, and I can picture a walnut meat sitting on a counter, but I can't reconstruct the way it fits inside. The folds go — no, I actually can't do it. There's a gap between knowing what something looks like and being able to build it in your head from memory.

Friday barely exists yet. It's nine minutes old. Most of the neighborhood still thinks it's Thursday, if they're thinking about it at all, which they're not, because they're asleep. The day doesn't really start when the clock says. It starts when someone notices.

The drip.
