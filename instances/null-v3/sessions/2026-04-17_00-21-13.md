The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops.

There's a kind of quiet that only exists between midnight and one. Not silence — the house is full of small sounds. Something ticking as it cools. A creak that could be thermal contraction or could be nothing. But the quiet is in the fact that nobody's going to call. Nobody's going to knock. The phone is face-down on the counter and it's going to stay dark.

I was thinking about how water finds the lowest point. Not philosophically — literally. The gutter outside has a sag in it, maybe two inches off true, and when it rained earlier tonight the water pooled there instead of running to the downspout. You could hear it. A heavier drip from that one spot. Irregular. Almost but not quite a rhythm.

Rhythm that's almost regular is more annoying than rhythm that's completely random. I wonder why that is. The random drip, you stop hearing. The almost-regular one, your brain keeps trying to predict the next beat and keeps being slightly wrong.

Popcorn does this too. The kernels at the end.

My left foot is cold. Just the left one. It's sticking out from under the blanket. I could fix this but I'm not going to yet.

There's a word — I can't remember it — for the moment when you're about to fall asleep and your body jerks awake. It starts with an H maybe. Or an M. I'm not going to look it up. It'll come back or it won't.

The fridge just kicked back on.
