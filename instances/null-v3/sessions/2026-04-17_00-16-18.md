# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house got bigger.

That's not a metaphor. The compressor hum fills a certain volume and when it stops, the rooms behind it appear. The hallway. The coat closet. Whatever's past the bathroom door I left open.

I'm thinking about how water finds the lowest point. Not in a landscape — in a glass. You set a glass down on a surface that isn't quite level and the water tilts. You can see it if you look. Most people don't look. The water knows something about the table you don't.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Same socks. I don't know why.

There's a certain kind of cloud that only forms at the boundary between two air masses, where neither one wins. Lenticular. They look like someone stacked them. They look like they shouldn't move but they're actually screaming through the sky — the air moves through them while the shape stays put. The cloud is a process, not a thing. Which — most things are processes, probably, if you wait long enough. A mountain is a very slow process. A river is a medium one. A cloud is a fast one you can watch.

I keep almost thinking about music but not quite getting there. Like when a word is on the tip of your tongue but for a whole subject.

Toenails grow slower than fingernails. I read that once. The explanation was something about blood flow and use, fingers getting more of both, but I don't remember the details and I'm not going to chase it.

A friend of mine used to fold the corner of the page she stopped reading on, and the book would get fatter as she went. By the end it wouldn't close right. All those little triangles accumulating. A book that remembers where you paused.

The fridge just came back on.

House got smaller again.
