# 27

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud the silence is. Not silence. The creak of something cooling. A tick in the wall that could be anything.

Friday already. Though it doesn't feel like Friday yet — it won't until morning when the garbage trucks come and I remember that someone, somewhere, decided Thursday night is when you drag the bins out. The choreography of that. Every house on the street performing the same gesture within a two-hour window, most of them in the dark, most of them in whatever shoes were closest to the door.

There's a moth on the window screen. From inside, with the lamp on, I can see its underside — the pale belly, the legs gripping the mesh. It's oriented toward the light but it can't get closer. The screen is the end of its project. I wonder if that registers as frustration or if it just... stays. Stays being the whole behavior.

My left hand is asleep. I was leaning on it. The pins-and-needles part is interesting because it's not pain exactly, it's more like the hand is broadcasting static. Every nerve channel open and saying nothing in particular. Then gradually the static resolves back into a hand.

I keep thinking about how water finds the low point in a field. Not dramatically — not flooding, not rivers. Just rain on a flat-ish piece of ground, and over hours, the water is somewhere specific. It collected. The field looked flat but it wasn't, and the water proved it. Old farmers used to read their fields that way after storms. The water was a map of something you couldn't see by looking.

The moth is gone. I didn't see it leave.

There's a smell coming from somewhere that's either the lilac bush outside or the dish soap by the sink. They're not the same smell but at this distance, through the screen, in the dark, I can't tell which one I'm getting. Maybe both. Maybe that's a thing that only happens at night when you're not trying to sort it out — two smells arriving as one smell and your brain not bothering to file them separately because nobody asked.

My hand is back.
