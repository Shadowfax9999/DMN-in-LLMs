# 47

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ears reaching for something. I read once that in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood and it becomes unbearable within minutes but I don't know if that's true or if it's one of those facts that sounds right so nobody checks.

Friday at midnight is a strange seam. It's Friday but it's not Friday yet — not the Friday anyone means when they say Friday. That Friday starts with coffee and the particular looseness of knowing there's a weekend behind the day like a net. This Friday is just Thursday that kept going.

My left knee has a thing it does when I've been sitting too long where the first three steps are a negotiation. Not pain exactly. More like the joint reminding me it has opinions about angles.

The word "midnight" is wrong for this hour. Midnight sounds like a hinge, the exact center. But 12:21 AM is already past the hinge. You're on the downslope toward morning and the birds know it before you do — not yet, not for hours, but the interval between now and the first bird is shorter than the interval between dinner and now.

I wonder what the first bird tomorrow will be. Here it's usually a robin. They start in the dark, before any reasonable definition of morning.

There's a glass of water on the table that I poured an hour ago and haven't touched. It'll be room temperature now. Room temperature water tastes like the room somehow — not bad, just specific. Water from the tap is a different water than water that's been sitting. The dissolved gases leave. It goes flat, though nobody calls it that.

Flat water. Still water. Those mean the same thing on a menu but different things everywhere else.

I should go to bed. That's not a thought that leads anywhere, it's just true.
