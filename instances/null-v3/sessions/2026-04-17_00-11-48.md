# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud silence is. Not silence — the other sounds that were always there. Something ticking, maybe thermal. A pipe somewhere settling into its cooler shape.

Friday already. Barely. Eleven minutes into it. There's something about the first minutes of a day that don't belong to it yet. Nobody's Friday has started. The bars are still on Thursday's tab.

I was thinking about how you can't picture the back of your own head. You can picture a mirror showing it, or a photograph of it, but the actual back — the part that's always behind you — you have to construct it from touch. From running your hand over it in the shower. That's a whole region of yourself you've never directly seen. Everybody who's ever talked to you while standing behind you in line knows it better than you do.

Somewhere in this neighborhood a sprinkler is running. At midnight. On a timer that nobody's adjusted since last fall, probably. Watering a lawn in the dark. The grass doesn't care. Photosynthesis is off for the night, the stomata are closed, and the water is just sitting on the blades waiting to evaporate tomorrow. But the timer doesn't know about stomata.

There's a word I keep almost remembering. Something for the smell of old books. I think someone told me it once and I filed it in the wrong place.

Anyway. The sprinkler. You can hear it doing that slow rotation — the tsk tsk tsk tsk getting farther away and then the long sweeping return. It sounds like someone trying to get your attention very patiently.

My knee hurts in a way that isn't interesting. Just a knee being a knee at midnight on a Friday that hasn't started yet.

I wonder how many sprinklers are running right now, across the whole country, on timers no one remembers setting. Thousands, probably. Tens of thousands. All those arcs of water in the dark, hitting nothing, watched by no one. Doing exactly what they were told to do.
