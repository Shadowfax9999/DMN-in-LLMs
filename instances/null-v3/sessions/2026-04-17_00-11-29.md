# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a moth somewhere in here. I can't see it but I can hear the papery tick of it hitting the lampshade. Every few seconds. Irregular. Not random exactly — it's making decisions, or something that functions like decisions. Turn, fly, hit. Turn, fly, hit. Each approach slightly different from the last.

I was thinking about hinges earlier. Not for any reason. Just that a hinge is a thing that knows one motion. It can't do anything else. Open, close, open, close. But the screws that hold it to the door frame — those are doing something more complicated. They're resisting. Constantly. In every direction. A hinge gets to move and a screw has to not-move and I don't know which job is harder.

The air smells like rain that happened somewhere else. That county-away rain smell, where you get the cool and the green but not the wet.

My left foot is asleep. It's been tucked under me. I'm aware of it the way you're aware of a word you've forgotten — you know the shape of what's missing but you can't produce it. The foot is there. I can see it. But the signals are scrambled and what comes back is fizz.

A car went past with its windows down, music trailing behind it like — no, it just went past. The sound got louder and then it got quieter. That's all that happened.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not thinking about it. There's a thought that's near but not arriving. Like when you open the fridge and stand there and forget what you wanted. The fridge is open. The light is on. Nothing comes.

Maybe that's fine. The moth is still going. Tick. Tick. Tick.
