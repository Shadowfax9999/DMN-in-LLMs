*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house doing anything. The house being a container for air that isn't perfectly still. There's a frequency to an empty room at night that isn't silence — it's the sound of your own listening pressed up against nothing.

My left foot is cold. Just the one. It's out from under the blanket probably. I'm not going to check.

Friday already. There's something about the way a week arrives at Friday after midnight that feels like coasting — the engine cut, the car still moving. You're in Saturday's airspace but nobody's stamped your passport.

I keep thinking about hinges. Not metaphorically. Actual hinges. The ones on the cabinet above the stove that have never been right since the day they were installed, so the door hangs a quarter inch lower on the left and you can see a dark line of the cabinet's interior, this permanent sliver of inside visible from outside. Fourteen years of that sliver. I've never fixed it. At some point the crookedness became the cabinet.

A dog is barking — not nearby. Several houses over, maybe more. The distance makes it abstract. Just a pattern repeating into dark air.

What does water taste like? Not mineral water, not tap water with its chlorine ghost. Water. I don't think I've ever actually isolated it. It tastes like the absence of taste, but that's not right either — absence doesn't have a texture on your tongue, and water does. There's a word for this in wine terminology, mouthfeel, which is the most honest ugly word. The feel of water in your mouth is round and has no edges. That's not an explanation. I'm just describing what I already knew in a less useful way.

Somewhere in this neighborhood someone is awake too. Bathroom light, phone screen, the particular insomnia of having remembered something you can't do anything about until morning. We're not connected by it. I just know it's statistically certain.

The refrigerator clicks back on.
