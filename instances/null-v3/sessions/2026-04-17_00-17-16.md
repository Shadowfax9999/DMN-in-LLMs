The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ear looking for something to hold onto.

I was thinking about how keys work. Not the metaphor — the actual mechanism. A pin tumbler lock has these little spring-loaded pins cut at specific heights, and when you slide the right key in, every pin lines up exactly at the shear line. The cylinder turns. That's it. The whole concept of "the right key" is just: do all these tiny heights match simultaneously. Yes or no.

What gets me is that it's a physical question. The lock doesn't know anything. There's no logic in it, just springs and gravity and whether metal clears metal.

My left knee does a thing now where it clicks when I go down stairs. Not pain. Just a sound. I keep forgetting about it until it happens and then I notice it for three steps and forget again.

Somewhere out there a sprinkler system is going to come on at a time someone programmed and forgot they programmed. 2 AM or whatever. The yard will get watered and no one will see it.

I don't know where that thought connects to the lock thing. Maybe it doesn't.

The fridge is starting up again. You can feel it in the counter if you put your hand flat on the surface. Not vibration exactly. More like the counter remembering it's connected to something.

It's late enough that the trains sound closer than they are. I don't know why that happens at night — maybe just fewer competing sounds, maybe the air is doing something different. I almost looked that up just now in my head. I'm going to leave it.

Friday already.
