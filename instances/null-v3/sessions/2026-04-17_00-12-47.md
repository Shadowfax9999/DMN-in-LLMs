# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice a fridge until it stops. Then the kitchen has this sudden depth to it, like a room that just took a breath.

There's a moth on the window screen. Not moving. Just sitting there with its wings folded up like a little tent. From inside, with the lamp on, I can see its legs but not its color. It's just a silhouette with legs. Six legs. Do moths use all six when they walk or do they hold the front two up like — no, I've seen them walk. They use all six. They're not praying mantises about it.

What is a moth doing at midnight in April. Well. Being a moth.

The thing about deep night in spring is the temperature can't decide. It was 70 today, maybe. Now it's — the window's open two inches and the air coming in is cool but not cold. Sweater weather if you were outside. Inside it just mixes with the lamp heat and becomes nothing. Becomes room temperature, which is a phrase that means the temperature stops being interesting enough to notice.

I was thinking about padlocks earlier. Not for any reason. Just that a padlock is a lock that doesn't belong to a door. It's portable. You bring your own security to the situation. A padlock on a storage unit, on a gym locker, on a gate — it's always a little temporary, a little voluntary. The hasp it hooks through is just a loop of metal that *could* be cut. Everyone knows this. The padlock is a symbol of a lock more than it is a lock.

Combination padlocks. Four digits. People use birthdays. The dials have that sandy grinding feel when you turn them, like there's powder inside. There probably is. Metal on metal for years in the rain.

The fridge just kicked back on.

The moth hasn't moved. Maybe it's sleeping. Do moths sleep. They must. Everything with a nervous system sleeps. Even fruit flies sleep. I read that somewhere — fruit flies that are sleep-deprived die faster than fruit flies that are food-deprived. Sleep is more essential than eating. That seems like it should be a bigger deal than it is.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Just the one sock came off somewhere under the blanket.
