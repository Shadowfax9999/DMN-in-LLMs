# 41

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house exactly. The gaps between things in the house.

There's a pitch that lives in fluorescent tubes even when they're off. Not a sound. A readiness to sound. I think filaments hold tension the way a sleeping cat holds tension — no, that's not right either. The filament doesn't know anything. It's just metal that happens to be thin.

Thin. There's a word that does more work than it should. Thin ice, thin excuse, thin smile. Thin patience. All of them meaning *almost not there*. Getting close to the boundary of a thing where it stops being the thing.

How thin can gold get. I know this. A single ounce can be beaten into something like a hundred square feet of leaf. You can see light through it. Green light, weirdly — gold transmits green. Which means gold isn't gold all the way through. At some depth it gives up and lets the opposite through.

I don't know what to do with that.

A friend once said she could tell what season it was by the sound of tires on the road outside her apartment. Winter tires on dry spring pavement have a particular hum. Then one week the hum changes and you know people have switched back. She said it was like the neighborhood collectively deciding something without discussing it.

Decisions that happen without anyone deciding. Foot traffic wearing a path. The way everyone in a pool slowly migrates to the deep end on a hot day. The way a party moves to the kitchen.

Parties always move to the kitchen. Even when the kitchen is small. Especially when the kitchen is small. Something about being near the sink, the counter, having a surface to put a drink on, having a reason to be standing where you're standing. The living room asks you to perform — sit, arrange yourself, face someone. The kitchen lets you just be next to people.

I'm thinking about the word *next*. Next in line. Next of kin. What happens next. It always means adjacent but also always means *not yet the thing itself*. Standing beside something that hasn't started.

The fridge just kicked back on.
