# 18

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a moth on the window screen. Not moving. Just oriented toward the porch light, which is off. I turned it off an hour ago. The moth is still facing where it was.

I keep thinking about how tape measures retract. Not the spring — I mean the sound. That ascending zip. It gets higher as the tape gets shorter because the coil is tighter, the remaining length vibrates at a higher frequency. No — I don't actually know if that's why. It just gets higher. I've heard it a thousand times and never once needed to know why.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs but not up. Has for years.

Friday feels different from Thursday at this hour. Thursday midnight still belongs to the week. Friday midnight is the first breath of something unscheduled. That's not quite right either. It's more like — the difference between a hallway and a doorway. Same amount of space.

Somewhere in this neighborhood someone is watering their lawn at midnight. I can hear the sprinkler doing that tsk-tsk-tsk-tsk-tsk and then the long shhhhhhh of the return sweep. Wasteful, probably. But the smell is going to be good tomorrow morning on the pavement.

I was trying to remember the name of those little metal things that hold a shelf up inside a cabinet. The pegs. Shelf pins. They come in a plastic bag when you buy the cabinet and you always end up with four extra because there are more holes than shelves. The extra holes are optimism built into the design. Or just a drill jig that doesn't know how tall your books are.

The moth moved. Rotated about fifteen degrees south. Still on the screen. I don't know what south means to a moth.

I don't know what south means to me at this hour, really. It's just the direction the neighbor's fence is.
