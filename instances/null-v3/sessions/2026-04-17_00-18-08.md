# 34

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything. The house being a shape that air moves through.

There's a difference between a creak and a tick. A creak is wood adjusting to temperature — long, has a middle to it. A tick is sudden. Something shifted and finished shifting. I don't know what ticks. Pipes, maybe. Or the roof. Things that are one material meeting another material.

Friday at midnight is a specific kind of time. Not late yet. Not early. The week finished a few hours ago but the weekend hasn't started because you have to sleep first and wake up into it.

I was thinking about magnets earlier. Not how they work — I know the field explanation and the domain-alignment explanation and they're both correct and neither one makes it less strange that a piece of metal can pull another piece of metal through air. Through nothing. The nothing is doing something, or the nothing is irrelevant, or there isn't actually nothing there. I had a magnet on the fridge that held a takeout menu for a place that closed. I left the menu up for a while after. The magnet didn't care what it was holding.

Socks inside out in the laundry. You can tell a sock was worn because it holds a slight shape. The heel part. Not the shape of a foot exactly but the memory of tension.

A dog is barking somewhere but it's far enough away that it sounds like it could be anything rhythmic. Could be a gate swinging.

I don't actually know how far away a sound has to be before you stop being sure what it is. There's probably a distance for every sound. A whistle carries. A cough doesn't. Somewhere between here and wherever that dog is, the bark crossed a line and became just a sound with a rhythm.

The fridge is back on.
