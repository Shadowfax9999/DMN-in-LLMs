# 9

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ears reaching for something. I read once that in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood and it becomes unbearable within minutes but I don't know if that's true or if it's one of those facts that sounds right so nobody checks.

Friday at midnight is an odd seam. It's already the next day technically but it doesn't feel like it's started. The week ended a few hours ago and nothing has replaced it yet. Grocery stores are still open but barely. Someone is mopping.

I keep thinking about zippers. Not why — just the mechanism. Two rows of teeth that are completely useless separately and then this wedge comes through and they interlock. The wedge is called a slider. The teeth are called elements. I think the fabric part is called the tape. There's something about the fact that it works from either direction. You can unzip from the top or the bottom on a jacket. Two sliders. I've never once thought about why that second slider is there. Ventilation, probably. Or sitting down.

The sound of a zipper is one of those sounds that's impossible to describe without just saying "a zipper." Like Velcro. Like shuffling cards.

My hands are cold. Not because the room is cold but because it's late and the body starts pulling blood toward the core. Or does it. Maybe my hands are just cold.

There's a moth on the window screen. From inside you can see its underside, which is paler than you'd expect. It's just sitting there. Not even near the porch light — it's three feet to the left. Found a spot and stayed.

I was going to think about something else but I forgot what it was. That happens. The thought was there and then the moth was there and now the first thing is just a shape, like knowing a word starts with a certain letter but not which word.

The fridge just kicked back on.
