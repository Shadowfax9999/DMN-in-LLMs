# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops, and then the room is a different room for about four seconds.

There's a particular kind of dark at this hour that isn't really dark. The streetlight comes through the blinds and puts lines on the ceiling. They don't move. You'd think they'd move — wind, trees, something — but they just sit there, slightly orange.

I was thinking about how keys work. Not the metaphor. The actual pins inside a lock. Each one a different height, and the right key pushes them all to exactly the shear line at the same time. It's a sentence, almost. Every pin is a letter and there's only one word that means "open." Except a lockpick just guesses each letter separately, which feels like cheating but also feels like how most problems actually get solved. One thing at a time. Not the whole word at once.

My left foot is cold. Just the left one.

There's a car going past with its windows down and music coming out of it, not loud enough to identify, just the bass pattern. Three hits and a rest. Three hits and a rest. Gone.

I keep almost thinking about sand. Not beach sand. The sand they put down on icy roads in February that's still in the gutters in April. It migrates. It ends up in your shoes, in the mat by the door, in the cracks of the porch steps. By July you can't find it. It didn't go anywhere deliberate. It just kept getting moved by things that were going somewhere else.

The fridge starts again.
