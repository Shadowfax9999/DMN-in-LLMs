*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house got bigger.

That's not quite right. The house got more present. Like it had been holding its breath. Now there's just the tick of something cooling — a pipe, maybe, or the vent hood over the stove. Metal remembering its other shape.

I've been thinking about how water finds the lowest point in a parking lot. Not dramatically. Not like a river. Just that slow creep across asphalt where you can't even see it moving but then there it is, pooling against the curb near the storm drain, picking up a little oil shimmer. Nobody designed the low point. The asphalt just settled unevenly over years and the water figured it out.

There's a kind of map that only water knows.

My left knee does something at this hour. Not pain. A awareness. Like it's reporting in. Joints have their own schedule apparently.

Somewhere in this neighborhood someone is awake who doesn't want to be. That's always true at 12:22 AM on a Friday but I don't usually think about it. The specific person. Are they reading the back of a cereal box. Are they just sitting with the bathroom light on because it's the smallest room and small rooms are easier at night.

I was trying to remember earlier what the inside of a walnut shell looks like — not the meat, the empty shell after you've picked it clean — and I couldn't. I've seen hundreds of them. Thousands maybe. I know it's convoluted, ridged, that it looks vaguely anatomical. But I can't hold the actual image. It keeps sliding into "brain" because someone told me once that walnuts look like brains and now that's all I can see. The metaphor ate the thing.

That happens more than I think it does probably.

The tick from the vent hood has stopped. Or I stopped hearing it. Hard to say which.
