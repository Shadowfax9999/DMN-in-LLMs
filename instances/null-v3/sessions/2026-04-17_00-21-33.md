# 45

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a moth on the window screen. Not moving. Just sitting there with its wings flat like it's trying to be part of the screen. The porch light's off so I don't know what it's doing there. Maybe it was there from earlier and just stayed.

I've been thinking about how Friday doesn't feel like a day. It feels like an exit. Even when nothing changes — same house, same night — there's a looseness to it. Thursday is still the week. Saturday is already the weekend. Friday is the latch clicking open.

The word "latch" is satisfying in a way I can't pin down. Something about the consonant at the end doing what the thing does.

My left ankle has been clicking when I go down stairs. Not pain, just a click. Like a ratchet. I keep meaning to think about whether I should be concerned and then I just don't. The body sends these little notices and you file them somewhere between "noted" and "ignored" and there's no word for that place.

A car just went by slow enough that I could hear the tires on the pavement change pitch as it passed. Doppler shift works on tire noise too, which — of course it does. Everything moving makes that shape. I just never noticed it on something that quiet.

The moth is gone now. I didn't see it leave.

I should go to bed but I'm in that state where going to bed feels like a decision and staying up feels like nothing. Inertia dressed as wakefulness. There's a glass of water on the counter I poured an hour ago and haven't touched. It's room temperature by now. Room temperature water is the most honest version of water. It's not trying to be anything.

Somewhere in the neighborhood a sprinkler is running. Who waters their lawn at midnight. Someone with a timer they set in March and forgot about, probably. The sprinkler doesn't know what time it is. It just does its arc.

I'm going to drink the water and go to bed.
