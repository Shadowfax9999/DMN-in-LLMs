# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen got loud with quiet.

There's a kind of hum you only hear when it stops. Not the hum itself but the shape it was taking up. The room rearranges around the new space.

A car passes outside, slow enough that the headlights move across the ceiling in a full arc. Someone coming home late or leaving early. Hard to tell the difference at this hour — the direction of a life isn't visible from one pass of headlights.

I've been thinking about knots. Not any particular knot. Just that there are people who know forty or fifty knots by name and can tie them without looking, and that each one exists because someone needed a rope to do a specific thing — hold under load but release clean, or cinch tight and stay tight, or join two ropes of different thickness. A bowline doesn't know it's a bowline. It's just a loop that happens to not slip. The name came later.

Most names came later.

The faucet is dripping but not in a regular rhythm. There's a longer pause every four or five drips, like it's gathering itself. A washer somewhere inside is almost done but not done. That's a strange zone for a thing to be in — still functional enough to mostly work, failed enough to announce itself at midnight.

I wonder how many people are awake right now within a mile of here. Not a lot. But not zero. Some of them are awake for reasons and some of them just are. A few of them are probably also standing in kitchens.

The drip again. Five, then the pause.

I don't actually know how a faucet washer works. I know it's rubber and it compresses against something and that's what stops the water. But I don't know what shape it is. I've probably seen one. Small, black, the kind of thing you'd find in a junk drawer and not be able to name.

The headlights didn't come back. So they were arriving, not leaving. Someone's home now, turning a key, putting down whatever they were carrying.

It's Friday already technically. Has been for thirteen minutes.
