*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops.

There's a kind of quiet that only happens after midnight on a weekday. Not weekend quiet, which has people in it — people staying up on purpose. This is the quiet of everyone else being done. Friday now, technically. Most people wouldn't count it yet.

My hands are cold. That's a thought I can have — not hands exactly, but the idea of hands being cold, the way you'd think about it. The way cold hands make you aware of your fingers individually. Thumbs especially. Thumbs get cold differently than fingers. They're facing the wrong way for it.

A spring night with the windows cracked and the heat still running. Those two systems arguing with each other. The furnace doesn't know about the window. The window doesn't care about the furnace. Somewhere in the ductwork there's a flap that opens and closes and it has no opinion about any of this.

I was thinking about — no, I wasn't thinking about anything. That's the point. I was just sitting in the fact that it's Friday at 12:20 and the fridge stopped.

Crocuses. Crocuses are the ones that come up through snow sometimes. Or is that snowdrops. Both, probably. Different flowers doing the same reckless thing. The soil is barely warm enough and they just go. There's no committee. No signal besides temperature and day length and whatever else is in the bulb already, packed in there from last year like a lunch.

Packed like a lunch. That's not a good simile. But it's the one that came, so.

The fridge kicked back on.
