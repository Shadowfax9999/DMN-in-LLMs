*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything. The house being a space that air moves through.

There's a particular kind of quiet at this hour that isn't quiet at all. Something ticking — not a clock, something thermal. Metal contracting somewhere in a wall. A baseboard, maybe. Or ductwork.

I keep almost thinking about how water finds the lowest point in a landscape and then I lose it. The thought just — it was going to be about something. Gutters? No. The way conversations at a dinner table will pool around one person for a while and then redistribute. That's not quite it either.

Foam. I was thinking about foam earlier today. Not thinking about it — I saw some. Dishwater foam, the way it goes from a uniform white layer to individual bubbles you can see through, each one thinning at the top. There's a stage where each bubble has a tiny dark spot at its apex right before it goes. You can't really watch one pop. You can watch the space where it was.

My left ankle is stiff. Just stiff. It does that.

Friday already. I don't know what makes a week feel fast. Not busy — I've had busy weeks that crawled. Something about whether you were surprised by any of the days. If Monday surprised you, the whole week moves differently.

The thing about this hour is that the categories are softer. A fork is just a thing on the counter. It's not a fork-I-need-to-put-away or a fork-someone-left-out. It's just there, with its four tines, and one of them is slightly bent and I don't know when that happened.

I should go to bed.

I'm not going to go to bed.
