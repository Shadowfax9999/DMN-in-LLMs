# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything. The house being a volume of air with walls around it.

There's a specific quality to silence after a compressor stops. It isn't the absence of sound, it's the presence of the space the sound was masking. The room gets bigger for a second.

I'm thinking about how Friday at midnight isn't really Friday. It's the seam. Thursday people are asleep and Friday people haven't started yet. The date says one thing but the feel says neither.

A moth outside the window. It keeps tapping the glass in the same spot, which means it's not learning but it also means it has commitment. I don't know what to do with that observation.

Water in pipes somewhere. Not in this room. The sound travels through the framing and arrives without direction — could be above, could be left. Wooden structures do that. They smear the source. You just know: water, moving, somewhere in the building.

I was trying to remember earlier today whether the word "latch" sounds like what it does. It almost does. The *tch* at the end has that finality. But "hinge" doesn't sound like what it does at all. Hinge sounds quick and small and a hinge is neither of those things.

The moth stopped.

I keep almost thinking about something and then the thought dissolves before I can look at it directly. Like a floater in your eye — moves when you move. That's been happening all week, a thought that's nearby but won't come forward. Could be nothing. Could be something I'm not ready to think yet. Could be the same thing.

Cold feet on a wood floor. That's the actual sensation right now. The floor is giving heat away faster than skin replaces it. I could put on socks but I'm not going to.
