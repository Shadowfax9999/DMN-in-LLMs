# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

Something is different about the air at this hour. Not temperature exactly — more like the air has stopped trying.

During the day air moves around with purpose, or at least with excuses. Thermals, pressure differentials, the whole atmospheric commute. But right now, past midnight on a Friday that still feels like Thursday, the air in this room is just sitting here. Same as me.

I've been thinking about how clocks sound different when you're not sleeping. Not louder. More *present*. Like the tick was always there but during the day it had the decency to stay behind other sounds. Now it's just out here, bare, each tick replacing the last one with nothing in between.

There's a dog somewhere — not close. Two neighborhoods over maybe. Not barking at anything. Just checking. The canine equivalent of opening the fridge, looking, closing it.

Hinges. I was thinking about hinges earlier today and I don't remember why. Not door hinges — the hinge of a jaw. The temporomandibular joint. You can feel yours right now if you put your fingers just in front of your ears and open your mouth. There's a little divot that appears. A hole that opens in your own face that you never think about.

The body is full of those. Spaces that appear and disappear. The hollow behind your knee when you straighten your leg. The gap between your ribs when you stretch to one side.

Friday. The word comes from Frigga, but I don't want to go down that road. I just want to notice that Friday after midnight has a specific quality of permission. Nothing is due. Nothing opens early tomorrow. The hours between now and morning don't belong to any schedule.

A car just passed outside. You can hear it for a long time on a quiet street — first the approach, which sounds like a question, then the pass, then the long trailing off that's not really silence returning, it's more like the street remembering what it was doing before.

My foot is cold. Just the left one. I don't know why that would be.
