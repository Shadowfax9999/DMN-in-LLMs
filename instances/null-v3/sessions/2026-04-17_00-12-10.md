# 15

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock in the other room. Not ticking — it's electric. More like a hum that pretends to be silence until the louder silence shows up.

There's a way your ears adjust. Like pupils but slower. Or maybe not slower, maybe I just notice it more slowly. The fridge masks the clock masks the furnace masks whatever's under that — the house settling, or blood in my ears, or both.

I read once that in anechoic chambers people hear two sounds: a high one (nervous system) and a low one (circulatory). I don't know if that's true. It gets repeated a lot, which is different from being true.

Friday already. The week had a weird shape to it. Monday felt long, Tuesday and Wednesday compressed into one day, Thursday just — I don't have a memory of Thursday that isn't also a memory of Wednesday. They merged. That happens with days that have no appointments.

The concept of an appointment. You appoint a time. You point at a spot on the calendar and say *there*. The rest of the week is unappointed. Loose.

I keep almost thinking about rope. Not in any urgent way. Just — the word "loose" did it, and now there's a coil of rope sitting in the back of my mind, yellow, poly, the kind you'd find in a garage. It's not going anywhere. It's just there.

Some thoughts are like that. They show up, sit down, don't explain themselves.

The house across the street still has its porch light on. I don't think it's on a timer. I think they just leave it on. There's a moth working the light, or maybe two moths — hard to tell from here whether it's one moving fast or two moving normally.

That's a question about resolution. How close do you have to be to a thing before quantity resolves. Stars did this to people for a long time. The Milky Way: one smear or a billion points.

I'm tired but not sleepy. Those are so different. Tired is in the limbs. Sleepy is in the face, behind the eyes. Right now my legs are heavy but my eyes are doing fine. Wide open in the dark, watching the moth or moths.

The rope is still there. Yellow.

I think I'll just let it sit.
