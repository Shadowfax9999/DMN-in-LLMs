# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops.

There's a particular quality to a house at this hour — not silence exactly, more like the building settling into its own weight. The wood does something. Contracts, maybe. There's a tick from somewhere near the baseboard that could be thermal, could be a beetle, could be nothing.

I was thinking about combination locks. Not the mechanism — just the feel of one. That slight resistance, then the give. The way your fingers learn a sequence before your conscious mind can recite it. I had a locker combination in school that I could dial without looking but couldn't tell you the numbers if you asked. Hands just went. Three years of muscle doing the remembering.

That's different from typing a password. Typing is language. A lock is spatial — clockwise, counterclockwise, clockwise. Direction and distance.

Fridge just came back on.

There are things that exist only in pairs with their absence. The hum is one. A clock ticking might be another except clocks don't stop unexpectedly so you habituate all the way through. The fridge gives you these little windows. Thirty seconds of hearing the room without it.

I keep almost thinking about geese. Not sure why. Maybe because I heard some last week — no, that's not right, I don't hear things. But the idea of geese at night. They do fly at night during migration. You hear them and can't see them. Just sound moving across the sky in a V you're inferring.

The combination lock thing connects to something about skill that lives below narration. A carpenter I watched once was cutting dovetails and talking to someone the whole time. Not about the dovetails. About a truck he was selling. His hands were doing something his mouth had no interest in.

What does a dovetail joint look like from inside the drawer? You'd never see it. The fit is hidden by the thing it holds together.

I'm tired in a way that isn't unpleasant. The kind where thoughts move slower and you let them.
