*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. Ears reaching for something. I read once that in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood. I don't know if that's true or if it's one of those facts that sounds too good so everyone repeats it.

Friday at midnight is a strange seam. It's already the weekend but nothing has started yet. The week is just — over. You're in the remainder.

I'm thinking about zippers. Not why, just — the sound of a jacket zipper in a quiet room. That teeth-meshing. Someone figured out that interlocking metal scoops could replace buttons and it must have seemed insane. The patent was 1917 or something close to that. No. I don't actually know. I think I'm confusing it with something else. It doesn't matter.

What I'm actually thinking about is how a zipper works from the middle. If you start one from the middle it doesn't. It needs the slider to marry the teeth in sequence. You can't skip ahead. That's not a metaphor for anything, it's just how the mechanism works.

The fridge kicked back on.

There's a dog somewhere — not close. Two blocks maybe. Just one bark, then nothing. Testing. Or not testing. Dogs don't test. That's a human reading. The dog just barked.

My hands are cold. April and my hands are cold at midnight. The furnace is off for the season already, probably. There's always a week in mid-April where you regret turning it off but you don't turn it back on because that would be admitting something.

I keep almost thinking about caulk. The word. How it sits in the mouth. The silent L that you can feel even though you don't say it. Caulk, walk, talk. The L is doing something in there even when it's not pronounced. It's shaping the vowel from behind.

I should go to bed.
