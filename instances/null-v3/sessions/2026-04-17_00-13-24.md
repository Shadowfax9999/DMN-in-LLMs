# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a frequency the body tracks without knowing it. Not the hum exactly but the pressure of the hum. And then it stops and your shoulders do something — not relax, more like recalibrate. Like they were braced against a thing that wasn't heavy enough to notice bracing against.

Friday at midnight is an odd seam. It's already the weekend but nobody's done anything with it yet. The week just — stopped being the week. No ceremony. Thursday was there and then it wasn't.

I'm thinking about how water finds the lowest point in a parking lot. Not in a physics way. Just — I've watched it. The thin sheet that moves almost too slowly to see moving, until you look away and look back and it's six feet further along. It doesn't search. It just goes where going is easiest, which happens to be down, which happens to be toward the drain, which happens to be near the cart return.

My hands are dry. That's not a thought that goes anywhere.

A friend once said she could tell what season it was by the sound of her front door closing. Something about the wood swelling. I never checked whether that was true. I liked it better as something she said.

There's a kind of thought that's just inventory. Right now: the dark window, the glass of water I poured an hour ago and haven't touched, the power strip light that's always on. Green. Small enough to ignore, bright enough to find if you need it.

I wonder if anyone studies what people look at first when they enter a room they've been in a thousand times. Whether it's always the same spot. Whether the spot means anything or is just where the eye lands because of the geometry — door angle, light source, whatever's nearest to eye level.

The water in the glass has gone room temperature by now. There's no way to see that from here but I know it anyway.
