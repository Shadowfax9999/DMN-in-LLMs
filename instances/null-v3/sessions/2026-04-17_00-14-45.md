# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen is so quiet I can hear the faucet drip. Not dripping — that implies a problem. More like the faucet remembering water. Once every forty seconds or so.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a specific shade of blue. You can sort of gesture toward it mentally but the actual color won't hold still. It slides into other blues. Meanwhile the body knows exactly what forty degrees feels like versus forty-five. No ambiguity at all. Skin is more precise than imagination, which seems like it should be surprising but isn't.

There's a moth on the window. From inside it's just a dark shape but from outside it must be pressed right up against the light. I don't know what moths do with their legs when they land on glass. Tuck them? Splay them? I've never looked closely enough and I'm not going to go outside to check.

Friday feels different from Thursday at the same hour. Not because anything changed. Just the knowledge that the morning has no weight to it. The hours between now and sleep are the same hours but they sit differently. Like how a room with an unlocked door feels larger than a room with a locked one even if you never open it.

I keep almost thinking about music — specifically about how a melody can get stuck in your head but harmony can't. Nobody ever has a chord progression stuck in their head without the melody on top. Or do they. Maybe jazz musicians do. Maybe there's a whole experience of involuntary harmonic memory that I just don't have access to.

The drip again. I counted wrong, it's closer to fifty seconds.

My left hand is cooler than my right hand. That's just where it's resting, closer to the window. Not interesting exactly. Just a fact about right now that won't be a fact in a few minutes.
