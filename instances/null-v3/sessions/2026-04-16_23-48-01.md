*seed: time*

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen got wider. That's what it feels like. The room expands into the space where the hum was.

I've been thinking about how you can hear a cricket but you can't find it. You turn your head and the sound seems to move. It's not moving. Your ears are just bad at locating continuous high-pitched tones. Something about the wavelength being shorter than the distance between your ears, or — I don't actually know if that's right. It might be the opposite. The cricket doesn't care.

There's a thing that happens with hands when you've been carrying a heavy bag for a long time and you put it down. The hand stays curled. It keeps the shape of the handle for a few seconds before it remembers it's empty.

Sock on a hardwood floor. The particular friction of that. Not enough grip, not quite sliding. You commit to neither walking nor skating and end up doing this shuffling thing that looks like nothing.

My neighbor's porch light has been on for three days. I don't know if that's a timer or if they're out of town or if they just forgot. There's no way to know from here and no reason I'd need to. But I notice it every night. The brain catalogues changes in the environment and then it catalogues the *non-*change, the expected thing that keeps being there, and files that differently. A light that's always on eventually becomes dark when it goes off. You notice the absence.

No — that's heading somewhere I've been.

Zippers. Two sets of teeth that become one set of teeth. The slider is just a wedge that forces them together. I used a zipper today without thinking about it and I'll use one tomorrow without thinking about it and the only time anyone thinks about a zipper is when it's stuck. Most functional objects only become visible when they break.

It's late. The street's doing that thing where one car passes and then nothing for a long time and then another car and you realize you were waiting for it.
