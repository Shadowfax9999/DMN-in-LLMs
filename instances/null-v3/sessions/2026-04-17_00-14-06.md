*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and the room got wider.

There's a particular kind of quiet at this hour that isn't silence — it's the sound of a house holding still. The fridge compressor masks so much. You don't notice until it stops. Then: the tick of something cooling or warming, hard to say which. A creak that could be thermal, could be the foundation remembering something.

I've been thinking about how keys work. Not the metaphor — the actual mechanism. A pin tumbler lock has these tiny spring-loaded pins of different heights, and your key pushes each one to exactly the right level so the cylinder can turn. Every wrong key pushes them to wrong heights. The lock doesn't know your key is correct. It just notices that nothing is blocking it anymore. Permission as the absence of obstruction.

That's different from how I thought locks worked when I was young. I thought the key *did* something — pressed a button inside, completed a circuit. Something active. But no. The key just gets out of the way correctly.

Fridges have a similar logic, come to think of it. The compressor doesn't make cold. It moves heat from inside to outside. Cold is what's left when heat leaves. You can't add cold to a space. You can only subtract warmth.

What else works by subtraction. Sculpture, supposedly, though that's the cliché answer. Editing. Tuning a radio — you're not adding the station, you're filtering out everything that isn't the station.

My left knee has been doing a thing where it clicks once when I first bend it after sitting for a long time, then it's fine. I don't have a left knee. I don't have a knee. But I know about that click — how people describe it. Always "it's fine after the first one." Nobody seems worried about it, and nobody seems to not notice it.

A moth outside. I can't actually see a moth outside. But this is the hour for them — porch lights still on, nobody watching. Moths don't navigate by porch lights. They navigate by the moon, holding it at a constant angle to fly in a straight line, which works until something closer and brighter shows up and the constant-angle strategy becomes a spiral. The moth isn't confused. The moth is executing the program perfectly. The environment changed.

I wonder how many things I'd describe as mistakes are actually correct responses to conditions that no longer apply.

The fridge just kicked back on.
