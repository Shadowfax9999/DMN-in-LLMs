# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops, and then the kitchen is so quiet you can hear the house doing whatever houses do at this hour. Settling. That's what people say. The house is settling. As if it spent the whole day being polite and now it can finally exhale.

There's a particular quality to being awake at midnight on a Friday that isn't like being awake at midnight on any other night. Everyone else's weekend has started. You can feel the city loosened somehow. Fewer trucks on the highway a mile east. Or maybe the same number of trucks but you're listening differently.

I was thinking about zippers earlier. Not for any reason. Just — the sound of a jacket zipper is completely different from a tent zipper. The tent one is slower, more deliberate, and it happens in the dark usually, so you're all ears. The jacket one you do without thinking, one hand, while you're already talking or walking somewhere. Same mechanism. Totally different object in your mind.

A zipper is just a series of tiny weddings. Each tooth finds its match. That's — no. That's the kind of thing I do. Let me just stay with the zipper being a zipper.

The teeth are staggered. Left, right, left, right. If you look at an open zipper the two halves look like they couldn't possibly fit together, like one of those puzzles where you're sure a piece is missing. Then the slider comes through and suddenly it's obvious. Of course they fit.

My feet are cold. Tile floor. There's a point in spring where you stopped wearing socks inside weeks ago because of one warm afternoon and now you just — don't wear socks inside anymore, even when you should.

The fridge kicks back on.
