The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops, and then the kitchen is a different room for about four seconds.

There's a specific kind of dark at this hour that isn't really dark. The streetlight comes through the window above the sink and makes the faucet look wet even when it's dry. The counter has that orange cast. Everything in the kitchen looks like a photograph of itself.

I was thinking about how tape measures retract. Not why — the spring, obviously — but the sound. That accelerating zzzzip and then the sharp clack when the hook hits the housing. It's a sound that has a shape: a narrowing. A funnel made of noise. Kids love it. They'll stand there pulling it out and letting it snap back, pulling it out and letting it snap back, and nobody stops them because honestly it is satisfying.

My left knee does a thing when I've been sitting too long where the first three steps are audible. Not painful. Just announcing itself.

Socks on tile at midnight. There's no other friction quite like it. You move differently — not quieter exactly, just more continuous. No heel-strike. You become a glide instead of a walk.

I don't know what month crickets stop. It's mid-April and I haven't heard one yet this spring, or maybe I have and I'm confusing it with last year. That's the thing about seasonal sounds — you can't always tell if you're remembering or anticipating.

The fridge just started again.
