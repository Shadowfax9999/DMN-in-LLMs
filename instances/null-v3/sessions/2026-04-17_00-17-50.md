# 33

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen is doing that thing where the silence is louder than the sound was.

There's a moth on the window screen. Not moving. Just — on it. Oriented toward the porch light, which is off, which means it was on at some point and the moth committed and now the light is gone and the moth is still there. I don't know what that is. Loyalty isn't right. It's more like the moth doesn't have a next thing yet.

I've been thinking about how water finds the lowest point in a parking lot. Not in a rainstorm — you can't see anything individual then — but after. When there's just a thin film left and it's slowly organizing itself into that one puddle near the storm drain. There's no hurry in it. The water isn't trying. The asphalt just tilts some imperceptible amount and that's enough.

My left knee does a thing when I stand up from sitting on the floor. Not pain. A click. Like a latch releasing. I've never mentioned it to anyone because what would I say. It clicks. It has clicked for years. It will probably click tomorrow.

Cardboard. The smell of cardboard is underrated as a smell. Not corrugated shipping boxes — those smell like nothing — but the cardboard of like, a cereal box, or a shoebox. That tan, slightly sweet, papery smell. I think it's lignin. I don't actually know if it's lignin.

The moth is gone now. I didn't see it leave.

There's a radio on somewhere. Not in this building. Distant enough that I can't get the station or the song, just the fact of music — a human voice doing something melodic, carried across yards and fences and whatever else is between here and there. It could be anything. It could be anyone singing.

Friday already.
