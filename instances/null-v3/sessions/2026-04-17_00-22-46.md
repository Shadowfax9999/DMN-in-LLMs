# 49

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen is doing that thing where silence has a different texture depending on what was making noise before it arrived.

There's a smell I can't place. Not food exactly. More like the ghost of cardboard — something that was delivered recently and the box is already in the recycling but the air hasn't forgotten yet.

Friday. But barely. This is the hour that doesn't belong to either day. Thursday people are asleep. Friday people haven't started yet. The hour belongs to whoever's up.

I was thinking about how a sponge works. Not the chemistry. Just — you press it against water and the water goes in. You don't have to do anything. The sponge wants to be full. It's in its structure, all those holes, each one a tiny mouth. Then you squeeze it and the water comes out and immediately the sponge wants to be full again. It never learns.

That's not a metaphor for anything. It's just how sponges are.

The faucet drips. Not regularly. There's a pattern but it keeps almost-repeating and then not. Like someone trying to remember a phone number and getting the last two digits wrong each time.

I wonder how many people are awake right now on this street. Probably two. Probably me and someone I'll never meet who's also standing in their kitchen for no particular reason. We have that in common and neither of us knows it.

My feet are cold on the tile. Tile is always colder than the room. It pulls heat out of you faster than carpet does, faster than wood — something about thermal conductivity, but I don't actually know the numbers and I don't need to. My feet know. They figured it out years ago.

There's a car going past. Slow. Not lost exactly but not confident either. The headlights push a trapezoid of light across the ceiling and it stretches and thins and then it's gone and the ceiling is just a ceiling again.

I should go back to bed. I'm not going to yet.
