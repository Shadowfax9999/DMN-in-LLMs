# 30

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. More like the air remembering pressure. I've never been sure if that's in the room or in my ears. Probably both. Probably the distinction doesn't hold up past a certain hour.

Friday already. The week didn't so much end as thin out. Wednesday had some weight to it — I remember Wednesday — but Thursday just sort of evaporated. There are days like that. Not bad days. Just days with low specific gravity.

I'm thinking about card shuffles. A riffle shuffle. The way the two halves of the deck interleave — not perfectly, that's the point. If you did it perfectly every time, eight shuffles would return the deck to its original order. Fifty-two cards, eight perfect riffles, right back where you started. But nobody does it perfectly, and the imperfection is what makes it actually random. The mistake is the mechanism.

That seems like it should connect to something larger but I'm going to let it not.

The other thing about cards is the sound. That papery clatter. You can tell how new a deck is by the sound of the shuffle. Old cards are soft, almost silent. They've absorbed enough oil from hands to lose their snap.

Oil from hands. I touched a window earlier today and noticed the print I left. Four fingers and a partial palm, very clear in the afternoon light, gone by the time I looked again. Not wiped off — just. The light moved.

A partial palm. There's no reason that phrase should sound lonely but it does.

What else. The tap in the bathroom has developed a interval. Not a drip — a drip is regular. This is more like: silence, silence, silence, tap. Silence, silence, tap. Silence, silence, silence, silence, silence, tap. No pattern I can find. It might be thermal. Pipes contracting unevenly as the hot water tank cools down. Each contraction releasing a little trapped water at a different joint. So the randomness maps to the geometry of the plumbing, which I'll never see.

I had a glass of water about an hour ago and set it on the counter and forgot about it. It'll have a ring of tiny bubbles on the inside by morning. Dissolved gas coming out of solution as the water warms to room temperature. The bubbles nucleate on microscopic scratches in the glass, which means every glass has its own bubble pattern, which means if you washed two glasses from the same set and filled them from the same tap and left them overnight, they'd look like different constellations by morning.

I like that. Not for any reason. Just as a fact that exists in the house at this hour while nothing else is happening.

The fridge just kicked back on.
