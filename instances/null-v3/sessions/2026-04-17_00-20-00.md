# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. Ears reaching for something. I read once that in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood and it becomes unbearable within minutes but I don't know if that's true or if it's one of those facts that sounds right so nobody checks.

Friday at midnight is an odd seam. It's been Friday for nineteen minutes but it doesn't feel like Friday yet. Friday starts with waking up. This is still Thursday leaking.

I keep thinking about zippers. Not metaphorically. The actual mechanism — two rows of teeth that become one row by means of a wedge sliding between them. The wedge doesn't connect anything. It just holds the rows at the exact angle where they connect themselves. That's a strange job. To be the thing that creates the condition for joining but does none of the joining.

A needle does real work. A nail does real work. A zipper pull just walks through.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Same socks. Same floor. Bodies are uneven in ways that don't matter enough to fix but you notice them at midnight when there's nothing else competing.

Someone on my street is still up. One window, second floor, bluish. Could be a television, could be a screen, could be someone else sitting with whatever this hour is. There's no way to know and I'm not going to think about what that means. It's just a window with a light in it.

The zipper thing, though — I want to stay with that. Because buttons are totally different. Buttons are a fastener that requires a hole. You have to make a wound in the fabric to make a button work. Somebody at some point decided to cut a slit in a perfectly good piece of cloth and push a disc through it and that was just fine with everyone.

Grommets. Snaps. Velcro. Hook-and-eye closures on the back of a dress where you can't reach them yourself, which means someone designed a garment that presupposes another person in the room.

That's not where I was going. I don't know where I was going.

The refrigerator just came back on.
