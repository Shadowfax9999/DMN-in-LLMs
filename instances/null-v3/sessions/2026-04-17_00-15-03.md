*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops, and then the room is a different room for about four seconds before you stop noticing the silence too.

There's a thing where if you hold your breath you can hear your own pulse in your ears. Not the heartbeat — that's somewhere in your chest doing its thing — but this thudding in the skull that's apparently blood moving through the auditory artery or whatever runs close to the inner ear. I don't actually know which vessel. It doesn't matter which vessel.

Friday at midnight is technically Saturday. Nobody treats it that way. It stays Friday until you sleep. Which means if you don't sleep it could be Friday for a very long time. There's probably someone who has thought about this seriously, like a Talmudic scholar or a maritime lawyer.

My hands are dry. The skin between the thumb and forefinger gets tight when it's dry and you notice it every time you spread your fingers. All winter that patch. Then one week in April or May you realize you haven't noticed it in a while.

The word "crisp" starts at the back of your mouth and moves to the front. Someone on the internet said that once and now I can't un-know it. K-R-I-S-P. It does. It really does. "Crisp." Try "gulp" — that one starts in the throat and ends with the lips closing. I wonder how many words physically enact themselves like that. "Hush" goes from open to closed. "Sing" opens up and stays open, the ng hanging there.

There's a spider web on the outside of the window that I can only see because the porch light three houses down is hitting it at the right angle. If that light were off I wouldn't know about the web. If the web weren't there I wouldn't be thinking about the light. Both of those things are just accidents of position.

I should drink some water.
