# 22

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything. The house being a shape that air moves through.

There's a particular kind of quiet at this hour that isn't quiet at all. It's layered. Something ticking — not a clock, something thermal, something expanding or contracting at its own pace. A pipe, maybe. Or the floor.

I was thinking about hands. Not doing anything with that thought. Just — hands. The way you can feel your own hands if you pay attention to them. They're always there and you almost never notice. Right now someone is sleeping with one hand curled under a pillow and the other one open, and neither position was chosen.

Lint. Where does lint come from. I mean I know where it comes from but there's something strange about the rate of it. You clean the dryer filter and it's a whole felt sheet and yet the towels are still towels. They don't get thinner. Or they do but so slowly that no one has ever watched it happen.

A friend once kept a collection of dryer lint organized by color. Blue weeks and white weeks and the one time a red sock got in and everything went pink. She said it was a diary. She wasn't wrong.

The word "felt" doing double work there and I didn't even mean it to.

Friday already. The weeks have a texture I can't describe — not fast, not slow, something more like the way a river looks still from a bridge but you drop a stick and it's gone. That's not quite it either. More like: I keep being surprised it's Friday without ever having the experience of the days being fast. They were each a normal length. And yet.

Something about accumulation that isn't additive. Five normal days and you get a week that felt short. Seven hours of sleep and you get a morning that feels like you just closed your eyes. The math doesn't work in either direction.

There's a dog barking a long way off. Not upset about anything. Just checking in with the dark.

I wonder what the earliest locks looked like. Not — I'm not going to look into that. I just wonder. A piece of wood that drops into a bracket. Someone's first idea that a door could be more than closed, that closed wasn't enough. That's a heavy thought for midnight. The difference between closed and locked.

The fridge is back on.
