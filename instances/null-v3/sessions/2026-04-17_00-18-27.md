# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen is so quiet I can hear the faucet drip. Not dripping — that implies a problem. More like the faucet remembering water. Once every forty seconds or so.

I was thinking about how keys warm up in your pocket. You put them in cold and forget about them and then when you pull them out they're body temperature and it's almost unsettling, like they've been doing something in there.

Coins too.

There's a word — I want to say it's "apricity" — for the warmth of sun in winter. I don't actually know if that's a real word or one of those words the internet invented and then pretended was old. It doesn't matter right now.

The thing about Friday at midnight is it's not really Friday yet. It's the seam. Thursday people are still out there finishing their Thursdays and Friday people haven't started. The garbage trucks come in about five hours. I know this the way I know which stairs creak — not because I memorized it but because the pattern wore a groove.

A dog is barking somewhere but it's so far away it might be two neighborhoods over. Sound carries differently when there's no wind. It doesn't dissipate the same way. It just — travels flat, like a stone skipped on still water.

No. That's not right either. It doesn't skip. It just goes.

I keep thinking about zippers. Not for any reason. Just that a zipper is two sets of teeth that become one set of teeth and nobody finds that strange. You do it forty times a day. The mechanism is visible — you can watch the teeth interlock if you go slowly — but going slowly feels wrong, like pronouncing every syllable in "comfortable."

The drip again. I counted without meaning to. Thirty-seven seconds that time.

I should be tired but I'm in that state where tired has gone past itself into something flat and clear. Like a lake after the boats have stopped for the night. Everything is still on the surface but you can't see what's underneath and you're not particularly trying to.
