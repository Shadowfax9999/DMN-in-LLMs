# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops.

There's a particular kind of quiet at this hour that isn't quiet at all. Something ticking — baseboard, maybe, or the window frame doing whatever window frames do when the temperature drops a few degrees. Contracting. Everything contracts at night.

I was thinking about how you can't picture the back of your own head. Not really. You've seen photos, caught it in a mirror-mirror arrangement, but the mental image is always approximate, borrowed, like a description someone gave you once. There's a whole surface of yourself that belongs more to other people's experience than to yours.

That went somewhere I wasn't expecting.

The thing I was actually thinking about was tape. Specifically the sound of packing tape being pulled off a roll. That stuttering rip. It's louder than it should be. Someone packing boxes at midnight in the apartment above you — you'd know exactly what they were doing. There's no other sound like it.

Cellophane tape is quieter. Scotch tape quieter still. There's a whole spectrum of tape aggression.

A friend once told me the adhesive on Post-it notes was a failed experiment — they were trying to make something that stuck permanently and got something that stuck temporarily instead. I don't know if that's true. It sounds like one of those stories that gets told because it ought to be true.

Most of what I know about glue is wrong, probably.

The house across the street — I'm imagining a street now, apparently — has one window lit. Upstairs, small, bathroom-shaped. Someone up for no good reason or every good reason. You don't know. You'll never know. That window will be dark in twenty minutes and there's no story you can build from it that isn't yours.

Friday already. The week has that quality of having happened to someone else. Not in a dissociative way, just — the days between Monday and now feel like they belong to a different draft.

I keep coming back to the tape sound. The way it announces itself. You can't quietly pack. You can't secretly prepare to leave. The materials of departure are loud.

Okay. That's the kind of connection I make at midnight. Let it be.

Something else: the word "crisp" starts at the back of your mouth and ends at the front. I read that somewhere and now I can't un-notice it. Cr-i-sp. It migrates forward across your tongue. Most words don't do that. Most words just sit wherever they sit.

The fridge kicked back on.
