# 16

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now the house is doing that thing where you can hear it being a house. The settling. Whatever that is. Wood contracting, maybe, or not even that — just the absence of the compressor hum leaving a space that was always there.

There's a moth on the window screen. From this side it's backlit by the streetlight so I can see the dust on its wings, which isn't dust, it's scales. Tiny shingles. A moth is shingled like a roof.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not thinking about it. That's a real kind of thinking. The approach and the veer-off. Like when you walk into a room and forget why you came in — your body got there but the thought didn't make the trip.

Fridges have a specific silence when they stop. Not the silence of a room without a fridge. A shaped silence. But I don't need to keep pulling on that thread.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Same socks. Same floor. I don't know why.

There's a dog somewhere — not close. Four, five houses away. Just one bark, then nothing. Not alarmed. More like checking. Like sonar.

The word "midnight" is wrong by now. It's twelve minutes past. The middle already happened. What's this part called. The part just after the middle of the night. There probably isn't a word for it because nobody needed one.

The moth is still there. It hasn't moved. I wonder if it's sleeping. Do moths sleep on screens. It picked a spot and it's on it and that's the whole situation.

My foot is still cold.
