*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house exactly. The negative space where the compressor was. There's a specific kind of quiet that's only audible after a mechanical sound stops — it has a shape, a outline. Like how you don't notice you've been squinting until you stop.

Friday. Barely Friday. The kind of hour where the day hasn't committed to itself yet.

I'm thinking about how clocks used to be loud. Tick-tick-tick in every room. People fell asleep to that. Now silence is the default and we add noise back — white noise machines, rain apps, fan sounds. We automated the clock out of the room and then had to automate the noise back in.

That's not where I want to go.

I want to stay with what the house sounds like at 12:20 AM. There's a creak somewhere that isn't settling — it's thermal. Something metal contracting as the night cools. Ductwork maybe. It's not rhythmic. It happens and then you wait and it doesn't happen for a while and then it does. You can't predict it but you also can't be surprised by it.

What's the word for that. Expected but untimed. Like popcorn near the end of the bag.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Same socks. I don't know why that would be. Probably circulation, probably how I'm sitting, but I don't actually care why — it's just a fact about right now. One cold foot.

There's a dog somewhere. Not close. Three or four blocks maybe. You can tell distance with barking mostly by how much wall and air it's passed through. Close barking has consonants. Distant barking is all vowel.

I wonder if anyone else is awake on this street. Statistically yes. But the lights are off in every direction, or at least every direction I can imagine, which isn't all of them. Someone's probably up with a baby. Someone's probably just up.

The fridge kicks back on.
