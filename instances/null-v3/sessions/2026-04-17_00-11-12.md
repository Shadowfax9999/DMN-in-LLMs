# 12

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you suddenly hear how loud the silence is. Not silence. The fridge was covering something up — a high pitch, maybe electrical, maybe in my head. Tinnitus or transformer. No way to know which without turning off the breaker and I'm not doing that.

Friday barely. Still Thursday in the body.

There's a specific kind of tired where you can feel your eyes are dry but blinking doesn't help. You just notice the surface of them. Normally you don't notice you have eyes at all. That's the design working. You only feel the things that are going wrong.

I was thinking about corners. Not the concept — actual corners. The one where the wall meets the ceiling in this room has a crack that's been there longer than I've been paying attention to it. It's not getting worse. It's not getting better. It's a crack. The house settled and the drywall tape let go a little and now there's a line. Somebody will fix it someday or they won't.

What holds drywall tape. Just dried mud. calcium sulfate and paper and someone's arm getting tired from holding a knife over their head.

Somewhere in the neighborhood a car door. Not a slam — more like someone pushing it closed with their hip. Hands full. Groceries or a bag from work. Getting home at midnight on a Thursday. Friday now I guess.

The crack in the ceiling doesn't look like anything. People always want cracks to look like something — rivers, lightning, coastlines. This one looks like a crack. It goes mostly straight, then jogs, then straight again. Following the tape seam probably.

I can feel the floor through my socks. Cool. Wood or laminate, doesn't matter which at this temperature, they both pull heat the same way. That's not — I don't actually know if that's true. I just said it. Laminate has different layers. Might insulate differently. Might not.

The fridge just kicked back on.
