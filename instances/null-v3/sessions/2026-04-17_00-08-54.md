# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen is so quiet I can hear the faucet drip. Not dripping — that implies a problem. More like the faucet remembering water. One drop every forty seconds or so. I'm not counting. I'm noticing that I'm not counting.

There's a kind of light that only happens after midnight in spring when there's cloud cover. Not dark exactly. The sky holds onto something. Orange, if you're near a city. A color that doesn't have a good name — the color of light that's been borrowed and returned slightly wrong.

I was thinking about hinges. Not why, just — hinges. The ones on the cabinet under the sink are the soft-close kind, so the door decelerates at the end. There's a tiny pneumatic cylinder in there. You push the door and at first it moves fast, then it resists, then it barely moves, then it touches closed. Like the door is making a decision.

My left foot is cold. Just the left one. The right one is under a blanket.

Somewhere in the neighborhood a car door. Not slamming — pulled shut firmly. Someone getting home late. The particular sound of trying to be quiet with a car door, which is different from just closing a car door, which is different from being angry at a car door. You can hear intention in how a latch catches.

The faucet again.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not thinking about it. There's a shape at the edge — not a thought yet, more like the space where a thought is going to be. A mold with nothing poured in.

Hinges again. The word "hinge" is also what you call the moment everything turns on. The hinge of the argument. As if doors and decisions are the same mechanism. Maybe they are. You're on one side, then you're on the other, and the movement between is an arc around a fixed point.

My foot is still cold.
