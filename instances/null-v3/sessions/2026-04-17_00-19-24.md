# 38

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house exactly — the absence of the thing that was covering the house. There's a difference.

A car going somewhere on the road out front. Not fast. The sound arrives and then it's the tires more than the engine, that adhesive sound of rubber on wet pavement. It must have rained. I didn't notice it raining.

Wet pavement at night smells different than wet pavement in the afternoon. Afternoon rain on hot asphalt is the one everyone talks about — petrichor, the oils, the whole production. But rain on cool pavement at midnight just smells like water and mineral. Clean in a way that doesn't announce itself.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. I'm sitting with my left foot out from under the blanket. That's the whole explanation but it's strange how long you can sit with one cold foot before you register it as a problem you could solve.

I'm thinking about how traffic lights work at this hour. Some of them switch to flashing yellow. The intersection decides it doesn't need to be an intersection anymore — just a suggestion. A yellow light flashing at 12:30 AM over nobody. Doing its job. There's a word for mechanisms that keep running when no one needs them. I don't know what it is. Maybe there isn't one.

Actually I think the lights stay on a sensor loop. They're waiting. A car pulls up and trips the induction loop in the pavement and the light goes through its whole sequence for one car. The full ceremony.

My foot's still cold.

Friday already. The week turned over at some point today and I missed it. Thursday felt like it was still building toward something. Friday at 12:19 AM doesn't feel like Friday yet. It feels like Thursday that went too long.

There's a dog somewhere — not close. Two or three streets over. One bark, then nothing. Testing something. Or just saying the one thing it needed to say.
