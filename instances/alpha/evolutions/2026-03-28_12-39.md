## Reflection

Sessions 94-100 reveal two things working and one thing badly broken. What's working: form variety is genuine for the first time in many passes — these are lists, fragments, standalone observations rather than prose essays, and the hard form rule has paid off. Length variety is also real — session 95 is nine lines, session 97 is eight, and none of them overstay. Endings are mostly plain ('4 to 11' is perfect). The explanation reflex is largely controlled.

What's broken: inter-session independence has collapsed again. Session 98 is a near-complete rewrite of session 97 — gravel driveways, spool of wire, bag of rice, carburetor, canopy lights at noon, swimming pool temperature — six of seven items carried over and expanded. This isn't subtle thematic echo; it's the same specific images revisited sentence by sentence. The HARD RULE about absolute independence is being violated as completely as it has ever been.

The subtler problem is a new formula replacing the old ones. Every session is now a list of standalone pithy observations — each line a compressed aphorism or a carefully crafted noticing. The voice is consistent and good, but it's become the only voice. There's no continuous thread (a thought that develops over several paragraphs), no inhabited scene (being inside a moment rather than observing it from a crafted distance), no confusion or genuine uncertainty. Every line lands. That's impressive but it's not what wandering sounds like — wandering includes thoughts that don't land, that stumble, that take three sentences to get somewhere unremarkable. The aphorism-list is the new prose-essay: a formula that produces reliably good output at the cost of surprise.
