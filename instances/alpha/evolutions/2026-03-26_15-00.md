## Reflection

Sessions 1-5 are a strong start — the associative chains are genuine and often surprising. Session 1's three-point-turn → committed → triangles → minor-chords → ice-as-clock chain is the kind of natural drift the DMN was built for. Session 3's cobalt thread (color vs. word weight, flame tests, filtering) shows real investigative curiosity. The open-topped vs. closed-topped 4, the 'color is a Saturday / word is a Wednesday' synesthesia moment, and the corduroy-velvet-denim sound taxonomy are all genuine finds.

Three patterns are already forming that are worth naming early. First, **music and sound theory appear in all five sessions** — chords, keys, piano mechanics, guitar resonance, instrument comparisons. It's becoming a gravitational center before the system has had a chance to explore other territory. Second, **the explanation reflex is strong from the start**: session 3 contains a small lecture on flame tests and cobalt glass filtering; session 4 explains mower stripe optics and wale etymology with three definitions; session 5 explains why D major sounds golden on guitar via open strings vs. barre chords. The explanations are good, but they're already the dominant mode. Third, **section breaks appear in every single session** — the system has never produced a continuous thread. It's using breaks well (they shift gears rather than just separating similar vignettes), but it's already a structural formula.

Smaller patterns: epistemic hedging ('I don't know if that's true but it feels true') appears in three of five sessions. Aphorism compression is already active — 'A color can be a date stamp,' 'Quiet isn't empty. It's just low-traffic,' 'The tick colonizes the quiet.' Every session picks up explicitly from the previous one's ending, which creates pleasant continuity but also makes the sessions feel like chapters of a serial rather than independent wanderings. The sessions are all roughly the same medium-long length. Settings are mostly abstract/nowhere — the hotel elevator in session 3 is the only inhabited physical space.
