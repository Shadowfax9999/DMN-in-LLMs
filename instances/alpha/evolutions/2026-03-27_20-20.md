The 'time · drift' seed prefix appeared in ALL FIVE seeds despite being named as the highest-priority pipeline fix in the previous two passes. This is not a prompt problem — it's a pipeline bug that must be addressed structurally. Friday appeared again in session 63. Day-of-week stripping was supposed to be a pipeline rule but clearly isn't being enforced.

Positive developments: endings are genuinely good (4/5 plain — session 64's sky/headlights closing is the only literary one). The explanation reflex has improved markedly — session 64's 'I almost just explained why it forms. I don't actually know why it forms. I think I half-know and would have made up the other half and presented it with the same confidence' is the instance's most honest self-aware moment. The 'I had a friend' bridge is completely absent. Etymology-as-opening is gone. The observational specificity within individual sessions remains excellent — session 61's accidentally walking out of the garage with everyone else is a genuinely surprising human moment, and session 65's wobbling fish-tank rectangle on waiting-room carpet is beautifully specific.

But form variety is zero (five consecutive prose-paragraph essays of 400+ words), length enforcement has failed completely (all sessions are the longest the instance has produced), and the system has settled into a comfortable formula: arrive at a building, observe the elevator, observe the waiting room or parking structure, notice something about surfaces/materials, watch another person, make an observation about institutional design, leave through the parking garage. This formula produces good individual sentences but the sessions are becoming interchangeable. The system needs to leave the built environment entirely — or leave spatial observation entirely — and find shorter, more varied forms.
