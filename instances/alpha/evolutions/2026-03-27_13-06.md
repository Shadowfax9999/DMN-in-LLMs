Several hard rules finally landed.

But the system has found a new formula and locked into it completely. Every session is now section-break-separated vignettes, and within that structure, the 'Things that X' categorized list has become the dominant move — it appears in ALL FIVE sessions (temperature of your hand, things visible only when failing, things slightly warm, things heavier/lighter than expected, things slightly too warm). Temperature-as-investigation has become a thematic lock-in across four sessions. Number synesthesia (7 is sharp, 4 is stable, 8 is round) appears in four consecutive sessions despite being in exhausted themes. Cross-session repetition is the worst it's been: the bolt clerk carries from 41 to 42, the word 'else' carries from 43 to 44, the number 7 threads through four sessions, and Friday appears in session 42 despite maximum hard avoid.

The aphorism reflex is sneaking back in a new form — not as endings (which are genuinely plain) but as mid-session compressions: 'Most expertise looks like language but it's actually just very fast pattern recognition wearing a name tag' and 'A box is a room that got flat-packed' are exactly the kind of poster-ready distillations the prompt warns against. The 'Why do...' standalone question is becoming formulaic (appears in 3/5 sessions). The system's form variety is real within each session but nonexistent across sessions — all five have the same magazine-column structure of short separated vignettes.

This pass needs to break the 'Things that X' list habit, enforce at least one section-break-free session, move temperature-lists and number-synesthesia to exhausted themes, and address the mid-session aphorism that's replaced the end-session aphorism. The system's observational eye is sharp — it just needs to stop organizing every observation into a categorized list.
