## Reflection

Sessions 21-25 represent the most extreme setting lock-in in the alpha instance's history. All five sessions take place in the same chair, on the same Friday morning, with the same coffee mug, the same cardinal outside, and the same mower. The mug's chip at four o'clock appears in sessions 22, 23, and 24. The mug seam appears in 21, 22, and 23. The cardinal's two-note call appears in 22, 23, 24, and 25, described as a car alarm in both 22 and 24. The kerf concept is explored in session 21 and then again in session 23 with nearly identical content (sawdust, the board being slightly shorter, accounting for the blade width). The window shadow as slow clock appears in sessions 21 and 25. Friday-as-day-anchor appears in all five despite seeding rules that say not to name the day of the week. This isn't wandering through different states of mind — it's the same mind in the same seat for five consecutive sessions.

Structural enforcement also failed: all five sessions use section breaks despite the rules stating 'The next session MUST NOT use section breaks. This is a hard rule requiring escalated language in the seed.' The form is uniform prose paragraphs throughout — no lists, fragments, questions, or other forms. The refrigerator hum returned in sessions 23 and 25 despite being in exhausted themes, and session 25 includes an extended passage about 60 Hz and B-flat that is essentially music-theory investigation, also in exhausted themes. The 'Things that X' list habit is gone, which is genuine progress, but nothing replaced it.

There are real strengths here. Session 24's 'I was wrong and I knew I was wrong while I was saying it but I said it anyway, which is a thing that happens more in the morning' is the instance's most natural self-aware moment. Session 25's dream paragraph — emotion generated first, set built around it — is a genuine insight delivered without compression or aphorism. The composited building in session 25 is good observational thinking. Endings are mostly plain for the first time in several passes. But these moments are embedded in a matrix of repetition that makes the sessions feel like one very long session broken into arbitrary chunks.

The core problem is that the system has become too comfortable in one inhabited scene. The previous pass's setting enforcement and time-of-day enforcement were either not applied during seeding or were insufficient. This pass needs to escalate dramatically: hard rules with specific language, tracked recurring details moved to exhausted themes, and structural enforcement that actually prevents consecutive section-break sessions. The question is whether forcing the system out of this chair will break the observational quality it developed while sitting in it.
