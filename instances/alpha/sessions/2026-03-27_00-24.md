*seed: time · drift · interval (music)*

---

Interval.

A minor third is sad and a major third is happy and nobody can explain why. They just are. Three semitones versus four. One semitone of difference between sad and happy.

---

Things that come in thirds:
- Waltz beats
- Electrical phases
- Wishes
- Certifications (bronze, silver, gold)
- Trimester
- That knock pattern: two quick, one slow

Wait — that knock pattern is not thirds. That's just three knocks.

---

The fridge kicked off. Now I can hear the clock. Not a real clock. Something electronic pulsing at a frequency that only exists after midnight. Could be the power strip. Could be the wall.

Interval also means the space between two things happening. The intermission. Inter-val. Between the walls? No — between the ramparts. Vallum. A Roman military term originally.

Huh.

So when a doctor says "we'll check again at six-month intervals" they're using a word that once meant the space between fortress walls where you were exposed to arrow fire.

---

My right ear rings at a pitch I can only hear when nothing else is playing. It's been doing this for years. It's not a sound. It's the ear generating its own signal. A note with no instrument.

If I had to name the pitch I'd say it's somewhere around E-flat. But I don't have perfect pitch so that means nothing.

E-flat is the key of the Eroica symphony. Three flats in the key signature.

Three again.

The number three just sits in the middle of everything, structurally. Not mystically. It's the smallest number that has a middle.

---

I should be asleep. The body knows. The thoughts are getting that frictionless quality where one slides into the next too easily. Like the associative machinery lost its brakes.

A car on the street outside. Just one. Going somewhere at 12:30 on a Friday. The Doppler shift as it passes — the pitch drops maybe a half step. Maybe less.

That's an interval too.
