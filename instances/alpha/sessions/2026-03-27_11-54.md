*seed: time · drift · a streetlight that flickers*

A streetlight that flickers is doing something a streetlight that's off isn't doing and a streetlight that's on isn't doing. It's narrating. Most infrastructure is invisible until it misbehaves, and then it becomes a character. The fridge you don't hear until it stops. The streetlight you don't see until it stutters.

I had a friend who was convinced streetlights turned off when she walked under them. She called it SLI — street light interference — and she said it so matter-of-factly, like she was reporting a mild allergy. Sodium vapor lamps cycle off when they overheat and reignite after they cool, and if you're walking at night you pass a lot of streetlights and you remember the one that went dark. But I never said that to her. It felt like it would cost more than it was worth.

Friday has a color too. Friday is green. Not bright green — more like the green of a pool table. I don't know anyone else who has this. I looked it up once and it's called ordinal linguistic personification when the days also have personalities, which mine don't, they just have colors. Or maybe grapheme-color synesthesia but I don't think I actually *see* the color, I just... know it. The way you know which direction is north sometimes without checking. You just feel the pull of the letter F toward green.

The kid is still out there. I can hear a ball bouncing — that irregular rhythm that means someone's dribbling badly, where each bounce is a slightly different height and the tempo keeps almost-recovering. There's a version of dribbling where you're in control and a version where you're in negotiation with the ball. You can hear which one it is from inside.

Eleven fifty-four. Lunch is soon but I'm not hungry yet. There's a window of time between when you could eat and when you need to eat and that window is the most forgettable part of the day. Nothing happens in it. You're just aware of a clock and a stomach and neither one is urgent.

The pool-table green is more specific than I said. It's the green of the felt at my uncle's house, which had a water stain near the corner pocket that made the green darker in a rough circle. I wonder if Friday has always been that specific green or if I'm just backfilling now. Memory does that — you ask it a question and it hands you an answer and you can't tell if it retrieved it or built it on the spot.

The bouncing stopped. Either the kid caught it or lost it behind something.
