"Yonder" fell off English gradually — it did specific work, the visible middle distance, over there and seeable. The hill yonder. Then it started sounding affected and then it was just gone, with no particular word taking its place.

What's the opposite of "here"?

wedding dress
tools sealed in a guitar case
a single shoe
a live lobster
daily medication

These turn up in unclaimed airline luggage. The lobster is the one I think about most. Someone had a plan that required a lobster at altitude.

There's a store in Alabama that sells unclaimed baggage. The inventory is arbitrary in the way that only accumulation can make it. A wedding dress in there once had a specific destination.

Old farm buildings creak in the afternoon. Not wind.
