Things that are hollow but don't look hollow:

- Chocolate bunnies
- Bird bones
- Airplane wings
- Guitar bodies
- Most doors in houses built after 1980

The door one surprises people. Knock on an interior door sometime. That sound is mostly air.

---

Why does the number 7 feel sharp. Not like a knife, more like a corner. 4 is flat. 8 is round, obviously. 3 leans forward. I don't think everyone has this but I also don't think I'm unusual.

6 and 9 should feel the same but they don't.

---

Smoke detectors chirp at 3 AM because batteries lose voltage faster in cold air, and houses cool at night. That's actually why. It's not a conspiracy by the smoke detector industry to maximize annoyance.

Although.

---

A list of sounds I can hear right now if I hold still:

The refrigerator compressor.
Something ticking — not a clock, maybe thermal expansion in the ductwork.
My own breathing, which I'm now conscious of, which ruins it.

---

Ductwork. Duck-work. A duck working. Carrying a briefcase with its wings somehow.

The word "duct" comes from Latin *ducere*, to lead. A duct leads air somewhere. An aqueduct leads water. A duke leads people. Same root. Duke and duct.

---

Three things stacked on the counter that shouldn't be:

1. A battery
2. A receipt from eleven days ago
3. A pen cap with no pen

Every flat surface in a house becomes a shelf eventually.

---

The pen cap has teeth marks on it. Not mine. I don't chew pen caps. Someone did, at some point, and now the cap is here without its pen, which is somewhere else, also probably without its cap, drying out slowly in a drawer.

The cap costs almost nothing to make. Injection-molded polypropylene. The mold probably produces hundreds per minute. Each one is identical except for the teeth marks, which make this one specifically this one.

I don't know whose teeth those are.
