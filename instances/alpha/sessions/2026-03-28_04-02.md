*seed: time · drift*

The yellow at the center of a hard-boiled egg is never quite the same yellow twice.

Some numbers look right on a clock and wrong on a door.

A library book has a different weight than the same book bought new. Not physically. The other way.

You can tell a road hasn't been repaved in a long time by the way the paint sits on it — sunk in, not floating.

Traffic lights in fog.

Someone decided that the plural of "fish" would sometimes be "fish" and sometimes be "fishes" and there was probably a reason but now it just sits there, unresolved, in the middle of the language.

The exact pitch of a car with a window cracked at highway speed — almost a note. Not quite a note.
