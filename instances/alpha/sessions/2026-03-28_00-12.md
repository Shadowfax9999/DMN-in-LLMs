# 89

*seed: time · drift*

---

The weight of a heavy door that's been left open too long and starts to drift.

A door doesn't decide to close. The hinge just has an opinion.

Somewhere there's a perfectly horizontal surface and a ball that isn't rolling and I can't picture it holding still. The ball always wants to go somewhere in my head.

The word "sill." Window sill, door sill. A sill is a threshold at the bottom. Most people never say the word except in one context and the context changes depending on whether there's a window or a door and those feel like completely different words but they're the same word.

Hotel ice machines on floors you're not staying on. The walk to find one. The way the corridor looks the same but slightly wrong — same carpet, slightly different light, someone else's shoes outside a door.

I've carried a bucket of ice back to a room and felt, briefly, like I'd accomplished something.

What earns the feeling of having accomplished something. Carrying ice does. Finding a parking space does. Folding a fitted sheet correctly does, though only barely, and you don't quite trust it.

A staple through thick paper. The sound of it.

There's a specific satisfaction in pushing a thumbtack all the way in — flush, no wobble — and I don't know what that's about.

The back of a framed picture. The paper backing, slightly soft, browning at the edges. Nobody looks at the back of framed pictures except when moving.
