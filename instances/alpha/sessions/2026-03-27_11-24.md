# 35

*seed: time · drift · tic-tac-toe*

---

Tic-tac-toe. Nine squares. The center is always the right first move but nobody wants to be the person who always takes the center.

The parking lot has that bright, overexposed look — everything slightly washed out. White cars look silver. Silver cars look white.

A shopping cart is sitting in the middle of an empty space, turned slightly, like it rolled there and stopped on its own. One wheel pointing a different direction than the other three.

---

Fulcrum again. The word is just sitting there. Ful-crum. It sounds heavier than it should for something that's essentially a pivot point. A fulcrum is the thing that doesn't move. Everything else moves around it.

What's the opposite of a fulcrum. Not a lever — a lever needs one. Something that moves without any fixed point at all. A bird? A thrown ball?

No. A thrown ball has the hand. A bird has the air.

Maybe there's no such thing.

---

Three sparrows on the curb near the cart return. They're doing that thing where they hop instead of walk. Why hop. Two legs, same as me, but they don't alternate. Both feet leave the ground at the same time.

The automatic doors open for nobody. Then close. Then open again. Something in the sensor's range — a balloon tied to the bench, maybe, moving in the wind.

A man comes out carrying a single bag, holding it away from his body slightly. Something heavy at the bottom pulling the plastic into a point.

The doors close behind him and stay closed this time.
