# 18

*seed: time · drift · the exact temperature where you can't tell if you're cold*

---

Thermoneutral. That's the word for it, maybe. Or maybe there isn't one.

Fingers on a steering wheel that's neither warm nor cool. That's the temperature.

---

Things that have two names and neither one is right:

- The bit of wall between two windows
- The meal you eat at 4 PM
- Whatever you call the relationship with someone you see every day at the bus stop for three years

---

A faucet dripping in another room. You can't tell which room. You just know it's not this one.

The alphabet has letters nobody likes. J gets overlooked. U is just a cup. But Q — Q needs help. Q can't go anywhere alone. There's something about that. No there isn't. It's just a letter.

Pliers. Scissors. Tweezers. Tongs. All of them are two things pretending to be one thing. Or one thing that only works by being two. Chopsticks don't even have a hinge and they still pull it off.

---

I keep almost thinking about gravity but not actually thinking about it.

The sound of a refrigerator is the sound of not-sleeping. You only hear it when everything else stops. It cycles off and the silence is louder than the hum was. Then it kicks back on and you've already stopped noticing.

Friday at midnight is either very late Thursday or very early Friday depending on whether you've slept. I haven't slept. So it's Thursday still, in the body.

The body keeps its own calendar.

My left knee does this thing where it pops on the first stair but not the second. Every morning. Just the first one.

Alan Shepard hit a golf ball on the moon. I think about this more than is reasonable. Not the fact of it but the arc. How long it stayed up. How it came down slower than wrong.

The pigeon from before — pigeons can find their way home from hundreds of miles away and nobody fully understands the mechanism. Magnetite in the beak, maybe. Or something with infrasound. The bird has a compass in its face and it still walks like that.
