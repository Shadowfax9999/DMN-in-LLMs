# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

Things that last fifteen years without being fixed:

- the toaster slot
- a screen door that doesn't latch unless you lift and push
- a drawer that only opens if you pull it at an angle
- the way someone pronounces a word wrong and you just let it

The word "workaround." Work. Around. Going around the work instead of doing it. Except workarounds are work. Sometimes more work than the fix.

---

Striped dark on one side. That's how sunburns happen when you fall asleep on a lawn chair. One arm. One side of the face.

The phrase "fell asleep" — you don't lower yourself into sleep. You drop. Or you don't. You lie there.

3 AM is the hour when lying there becomes a different activity than trying to sleep. At some point you cross over from "I'm falling asleep" to "I'm awake now" and the moment you name it, it's true.

---

Brown.

Not a color anyone picks first. Not a color of sports cars or neon signs. Brown is the color of things that used to be alive. Leaves. Wood. Leather. Dirt, which is partly dead things. Coffee beans after roasting. They start out green.

Green to brown. That's the direction of time for most organic matter.

Except spring, which goes the other way.

It's spring now. Deep night spring. The particular quiet where you can tell things are growing but you can't point to evidence yet. The ground is doing something.

---

T.

Dental consonant. Tongue hits the ridge behind the teeth. Try saying it slowly. There's a tiny sealed chamber of air pressure and then a release. Every T is a small explosion.

P is the lips doing the same thing.

K is further back, soft palate.

B, D, G — the voiced versions. Same positions, but the throat hums.

I don't know why I find this satisfying. Sorting sounds by where they happen in the mouth.

The mouth has geography.
