*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a particular quality to a Saturday at 5 PM in late March — still light but going orange at the edges, and you feel like you should be doing something but you don't know what, and that feeling isn't bad exactly, just slightly unresolved, like a question that stopped being urgent before it got answered.

A refrigerator stops running and you notice it stopped. That's the whole thing. You were in a room not thinking about the refrigerator at all, and its stopping is an event.

I keep thinking about thresholds you can't locate. The exact moment water gets too cold to stay in. You're in and then you're not — but you didn't feel the line. The decision was made somewhere below the level of deciding. The body just said *now* and you got out.

A name I cannot place. Not a person — a brand, maybe. Something on the side of a truck I saw several times as a kid, probably a regional thing, probably gone now. Whatever it was I associated it with a particular stretch of road, wet pavement, the back seat. It's possible the name itself was unremarkable and the whole thing is just the road.

The satellite dish that used to be in the neighbor's yard. Enormous, white, aimed at something specific. Someone knew the exact angle. Someone adjusted it by hand, or maybe they didn't and it just sat there slowly drifting off target over years until the signal degraded so gradually nobody noticed.

I've been in rooms where the ceiling was too low by maybe three inches. Not enough to crouch, but enough that the room held its breath a little.
