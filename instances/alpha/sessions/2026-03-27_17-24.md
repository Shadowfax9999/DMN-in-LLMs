Umbra is the dark part. The part where no light reaches at all. Penumbra is the almost-dark, the partial shadow, which is a better word honestly. Pen-umbra. Almost-shadow. There's a thing where the better prefix makes the secondary concept more interesting than the primary one. Penultimate is more fun to say than ultimate. The pen- does something, it loosens the word up, gives it room to breathe. Ultimate is so final it's boring. Penultimate has somewhere still to go.

I don't actually know if pen- in penumbra and pen- in penultimate are the same pen-. They might not be. One might be Latin for almost and the other for... I don't know. They feel like the same prefix but feelings about prefixes are unreliable.

The email is still sitting there. I can see the notification number on the icon from across the room. The 1 in the red circle. There's a particular kind of not-checking where you're aware of not-checking the entire time, which is different from actually not noticing. I'm performing ignoring it, to myself, for myself, which is strange. Who's the performance for. The pot on the windowsill has a saucer under it that's glazed a slightly different brown than the pot, like they came from different sets but someone decided they were close enough. They're not close enough. One is more orange. But it doesn't matter because function doesn't care about matching.

Friday at 5:23 the light is coming in almost horizontal. Late March. The shadow of the window frame makes a cross on the floor that isn't centered on anything, it just falls where it falls, partly on the rug and partly on the hardwood, and the part on the rug is softer-edged because the rug has texture that breaks the line up. The part on the hardwood is sharp. Same shadow, two versions of itself depending on what it lands on.

I had a coworker named Dan who would reply-all to things within forty-five seconds at any hour. You'd send something at 11 PM thinking you'd deal with responses in the morning and Dan would just be there, immediately, with a fully formed thought. It made everyone else feel slow but really it just meant Dan was always on email. That's not speed, that's just presence. He moved to Portland I think. Or Bend. Somewhere in Oregon where you'd expect someone named Dan to end up.

The cross on the floor is going to drift left over the next hour and get longer and eventually it'll climb the far wall and then it'll be gone. If I sat here and watched it the whole time I wouldn't be able to see it move at any given moment but I'd know it moved. Like a clock hand. You can't catch it in the act. There's probably a word for motion that's real but imperceptible in any single instant and I probably knew it once from a philosophy class and it's gone now.

The saucer has a chip on the rim that's been there longer than I've had the plant.
