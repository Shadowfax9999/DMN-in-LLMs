"Incumbent" has two meanings that don't seem to come from each other: the person currently in office, and pressing heavily upon. Both feel right at once. The senator is also the stone. The word just holds both.

Sleeping bag stuff sacks. The cylindrical weight of them. Rolling it correctly always takes longer than expected, and the surprise each time is genuine.

"Mauve." Neither pink nor purple. Lavender commits. Mauve hedges. There's no specific object I can picture when I say it — just a quality, something approximate and embarrassed about itself.

Whether objects in constant use are different from objects used only occasionally. A spatula versus a candy thermometer. One gets worn smooth. The other just waits.

The candy thermometer is probably in a drawer somewhere, behind everything else.
