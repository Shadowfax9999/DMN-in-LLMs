# 32

*seed: time · drift*

---

The parking lot of a grocery store at 11 in the morning is mostly empty. Not weekend-empty, where you can tell people are sleeping in. Friday-empty, where everyone's at work and the lot feels like it belongs to retirees and the overnight staff finishing up.

A shopping cart in the corral has one of those reusable bags still in it. Green. Someone forgot it.

---

Things that are slightly too warm today:

- the steering wheel
- the inside of my shoe
- the air coming out of the vent before I switched it to cool

---

I keep almost remembering the name of a song. It has a piano part that goes down in steps. Not stairs-down, more like settling. Each note a little lower and a little softer. I can hear it but I can't get to the title. It's in a room I can see through the window but the door is on the other side.

No — I'm not going to do that.

I just can't remember the song.

---

Two women standing by the pharmacy drive-through, not in cars, just standing on the sidewalk talking. One of them is holding a prescription bag but hasn't opened it. The other one keeps shifting her weight from one foot to the other. They're not in a hurry. They're doing that thing where the conversation is almost over but neither person makes the move to end it, so it keeps restarting. Little nods that look like goodbyes but then someone says "oh and—"

The bag is white with that blue stripe pharmacies use. I wonder if there's one company that makes all those bags.
