Things that are always slightly warm:

- The back of a TV
- Charger bricks
- The spot on the floor where the fridge meets the wall
- Other people's chairs

The number 7 thing is still bugging me. Heptagons. You almost never see one. There's no heptagonal tile pattern in any bathroom I've ever been in. Octagons get paired with squares and suddenly they're everywhere. Sevens just sit there being prime.

Five sounds on a car alarm is too many. Two would be enough. One would be enough. The point is volume, not variety.

---

Fridges cycle on and you don't hear it. Fridges cycle off and you hear the silence. The absence is louder.

No — I just heard myself heading somewhere with that. Skip it.

The pen cap is still on the counter. Pen caps migrate. They end up in junk drawers, between couch cushions, in the bottom of bags that don't even belong to you.

---

Trying to picture a friend's face with my eyes closed and getting the wrong nose. I know the face. I'd recognize it instantly. But I can't *build* it. Recognition and construction are completely different operations.

Same with voices. I can hear my mother's voice in my head but if I try to actually describe it — pitch, texture, speed — it dissolves. The memory is confident and vague at the same time.

A dog barking somewhere. Not close. Four or five houses away, maybe. The kind of bark that's not about anything. Just periodic, like checking.

The fridge just cycled on.
