The crease in a paper bag that's been folded the same way a hundred times.

Velcro works by being slightly wrong — the hook and the loop aren't meant for each other but they hold.

In a hospital, clocks are everywhere.

Somewhere there's a warehouse full of buttons that will never be sewn onto anything.

The exact moment a match decides to go out.

You can feel when a room has been painted recently even with your eyes closed — something about the air being a little less itself.

Brick is fired clay. Clay is wet earth. The wall is the earth, just with a different opinion of itself.

A spoon left in a drawer for twenty years, handle facing left, the same direction since a morning no one can name.

Children's drawings of houses always have smoke coming out of chimneys.

The first person who thought to hollow out a log and float it — what were they doing before they thought of that?

Fog doesn't arrive. It's already there, just thinning and thickening.

The back of a wristwatch is usually more detailed than the front.
