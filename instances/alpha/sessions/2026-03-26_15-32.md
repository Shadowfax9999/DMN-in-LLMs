# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

Six weeks is generous honestly. I start things and forget them in less than that now. The difference is I don't feel bad about it anymore, which might be worse. There was a thing in college where someone had a whiteboard on their dorm room door and people would write on it and every few days they'd photograph it before erasing it and I think they kept that up for almost two years which is extraordinary. Most of the messages were just like "came by, you weren't here" which is a sentence that doesn't exist anymore. Nobody comes by. You text first. The whole genre of showing up and someone not being there, that's just gone. I wonder what happened to the photos of the whiteboard. Probably on a hard drive in a box. Probably a drive format that's annoying to read now. The number of boxes in the world that contain hard drives with photos on them that nobody will ever look at again has to be enormous. Not sad exactly, just a lot of stored voltage slowly discharging into nothing.

I'm hungry but not for anything specific. That's the worst kind of hungry. You open the fridge and nothing in there is the thing. Close it. Open the pantry. Same. Close it. Open the fridge again as if something changed in the last forty seconds. I do that and I watch myself doing it and I still do it the third time. Peanut butter on a spoon is usually what I end up with when I'm in that state. Not even on bread. Just the spoon. It's not a meal, it's a placeholder. The roof of your mouth after peanut butter, that thick coating your tongue keeps trying to clear. That lasts longer than eating the peanut butter did.

There's a kid riding a bike somewhere outside, I can hear the freewheel clicking when they coast. That sound is so specific to a certain speed — fast enough to coast but slow enough you're not going anywhere in particular. Neighborhood speed. The clicking gets quieter which means they're pedaling again or they turned a corner or both. I used to think coasting was the whole point of bikes. Pedaling was just the tax you paid.

The peanut butter is still on the roof of my mouth.
