# 101

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a specific quality to a Saturday afternoon that's different from a Saturday morning — something shifts around two or three, the light gets a little flatter, and the day stops feeling like it has potential and starts feeling like what it already is. You can be in the middle of doing something and feel it happen. The sandwich you made is sitting there and you're not sure you want it anymore.

Folding chairs at an outdoor event. The specific problem of folding chairs, which is that they're always either too wobbly or require more force to fold than seems right. A folding chair that works perfectly is almost not a folding chair anymore. Something about objects that have a job to do — the job is part of the object, and if the object is too good at the job, the job-ness disappears and you just have a thing.

A can opener that works on the first try. You're almost disappointed.

The sound of a public pool through a fence when you're not at the pool. Voices and water but you can't make out anything specific. It has a particular quality at a distance. Closer it's just noise. Farther it might not reach. There's a zone where it's exactly right without being anything.

Sprinklers in the early evening, the oscillating kind that you can hear ticking as they sweep back. The whole neighborhood used to come on at once when the timers were all set wrong. That particular overlap of water sounds. I'm not sure why the ticking is satisfying — something about being able to predict where the water is going to be.

A street that dead-ends into a field. The road just stops and there's a field. Nobody made a sign about it.
