*seed: time · drift · the space between the last step and the landing*

---

The space between the last step and the landing is longer than the others. Your body knows this before you do. Your foot reaches and the floor isn't there yet and then it is.

---

Things that have a different name than what you call them:

- The aglet (I call it "the plastic thing")
- The ferrule (I call it "the metal part at the bottom")
- The muntins (I call them "the little bars in the window")
- The desire line (I call it "the dirt path people actually use")
- The philtrum (I call it nothing, I never refer to it)

The philtrum. Who needs to refer to their philtrum.

---

Can you picture a seven-sided shape without counting the sides? I can't. I can picture a hexagon immediately. An octagon. But seven just looks like a circle that's trying.

Five is easy. Six is easy. Eight is a stop sign. Seven is nothing. Nine is also nothing. They don't tile, maybe that's why. You never see them in floors or walls so you never build the shape in your head.

---

A car alarm going off at 12:30 AM and nobody reacting to it. Not even the owner. It cycles through its five sounds — the wail, the yelp, the chirp, the oscillating one, the one that sounds like a European siren — and then it stops, and then it starts again.

Who chose those five sounds. Someone sat in a meeting and picked them.
