---

One pill sounds different from many pills.

Many pills: a maraca. One pill: a knock from inside.

Two pills you can't tell from three.

---

Things that rattle:

A doorknob that's loose but not broken. Seeds in a dried pod. The plastic cover over a fluorescent light in a classroom. A shopping cart with one wheel. Ice in a cup when you're just holding it, not drinking. A pen cap that doesn't quite fit anymore.

A toolbox carried at a jog.

---

I'm trying to picture a doorknob from the inside of the door. The mechanism. There's a square bar that goes through — the spindle, I think. And then a spring that pushes the latch back out after you've turned it. But I can't picture where the spring sits. I've taken a doorknob apart before. I remember the rosette, the little plate that sits against the door. I remember the feeling of the whole assembly sliding out and being surprised how short the spindle was. But the spring — I don't know where it goes.

Some things you can take apart and put back together and still not know how they work.

Prattle is a little percussive.
