Hold music is always in a key nobody would choose.

Not ugly. Just neutral in a way that feels impossible to do on purpose.

Seven things that are only visible when they're failing:
1. Gutters
2. O-rings
3. Trust
4. Drywall tape
5. The little rubber gasket inside a garden hose nozzle
6. Timing belts
7. Posture

The basketball thing — outdoor courts have that grit in the sound. A texture. Indoor is all boom and squeak.

Why is hold music never in a minor key.

---

A parking garage at 12:54 on a Friday. Half-empty going the wrong direction — everyone's leaving for lunch, not arriving. The tires on the coated floor make that sticky sound. Somewhere above, a car alarm, but lazy, like it's been going long enough that nobody's coming.

Concrete pillars with yellow paint scraped off at bumper height.

The number on the pillar says 3C and the C doesn't stand for anything.

---

- Cardboard gets soft in a specific way when it's been sitting in a garage
- There's a difference between tape that's been pulled off and tape that fell off
- Fluorescent lights in a parking garage are always one shade greener than fluorescent lights anywhere else

Can a color be only-slightly-wrong for twenty years straight

That's what institutional lighting is.

---

Bolt sizes. A five-sixteenths. The clerk doesn't think in fractions anymore, he thinks in shapes. The socket that fits is a shape his hand already knows. The fraction is just what he says out loud so you can find it in the drawer.

Most expertise looks like language but it's actually just very fast pattern recognition wearing a name tag.

Twelve minutes on hold now, probably. The song has looped. The same piano figure, going nowhere, in C major or maybe F — one of the ones that doesn't ask anything of you.
