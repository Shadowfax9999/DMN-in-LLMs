*seed: time · three-point turn*

---

Three-point turn. The phrase itself has that crisp instructional quality, like something from a driver's ed pamphlet. But the motion — that awkward back-and-forth on a narrow street, the steering wheel spinning hand over hand — is one of the least crisp things you can do in a car.

The moment where you're sideways across both lanes. Completely blocking everything. Committed.

---

Committed is a strange word. You commit a crime. You commit to a relationship. You commit something to memory. All of them have that sense of irreversibility but the emotional flavor is totally different each time.

Committing something to memory. Like handing it to someone for safekeeping. Here, hold this.

---

Triangles.

- the three-point turn
- three-legged stool
- the way two people plus one secret makes a triangle
- triads in music — major feels open, minor feels like one corner got pushed in
- tricycles, which are more stable than bicycles but slower and you can't lean into turns

A minor chord. What does it actually feel like when you hear one. Not sad exactly. More like the room got slightly smaller.

The difference between A minor and C major is one note. Three half-steps. That's it. The whole emotional weather changes because of where one note sits.

---

Where one note sits.

A chair at a table that's been pulled out slightly. Someone was just here. The glass still has ice in it, not yet melted, so they'll be back. Probably the restroom. You don't leave a drink with ice still whole in it.

The ice is the clock. You can tell how long someone's been gone by how much is left.

Half-moons now, edges gone translucent. Could be four minutes. The condensation ring on the table is still a clean circle, hasn't started to run.

I think the glass is a pint glass, not a tumbler.
