Provisional parking. The yellow paint half-worn. Somebody decided on that word — provisional — and it has a bureaucratic softness to it, a permission that could be revoked. Mauve is also provisional, come to think of it, keeps agreeing to be slightly different things in different rooms. A bruise is provisional, the body working something out. There's a whole category of things that are on their way to being something else and you catch them in the middle: the half-frozen puddle, the permission slip not yet signed, the house before people move into it, that specific smell. How a vacant house smells like itself with no interference. Cardboard. Old window. Some memory of heat. The combination lock clicking before you're ready — the readiness question is interesting, whether you can be ahead of yourself or always slightly behind, the sense of having arrived somewhere before you realized you were going. A clutch foot on a hill, the second-by-second negotiation between the engine and gravity, some part of the body that knows what to do before the thinking part catches up. Whether that part is the body or something inside the body running faster. The cast iron pan heavy in a specific direction — cantilevered, that's the word, but I'm not going to chase it. The trust required to hang something on a hook you can't see. Not faith exactly. More like accumulated evidence. The drawer full of batteries is also a kind of trust, the belief that one of them still has enough. The lighter that might work. The specific flick and the brief hope. Gas stations at night look the same everywhere, the fluorescent light the same color, the pump making the same sounds, you could be in any state. There are people whose job it is to make sure all the pumps sound the same. Or maybe it's just physics, the same mechanism everywhere making the same noise the way rain on a roof sounds like rain on a roof wherever the roof is.

The receipt from the bottom of a jacket pocket, dated months ago. I can't read the items.
