# 8

*seed: time · drift · fretboard*

---

Fretboard. But I'm looking at the property line, that color shift in the grass, and what I'm actually thinking about is how many boundaries are only visible from one side. From the neighbor's yard looking over, their grass is just grass. It's only from the yellow-green side that you perceive the line. The fence shadow has definitely moved. It moves so slowly you can't watch it happen but you can tell it happened. Like the hour hand. I used to stare at clocks in school trying to catch the minute hand moving and I could almost convince myself I saw it but I think I was just seeing the difference between where it was and where it remembered being. Where *I* remembered it being. The minute hand doesn't remember anything.

There's a smell right now that's either someone's dryer vent or a flowering tree. Sweet and warm. It comes and goes. Three breaths of it and then nothing for a while and then it's back. The wind must be doing something complicated around the corner of the house.

Fretboard though. The word just looks like two words that don't belong together. Fret. Board. To fret is to worry. A fretboard is a worry board. A board of worries. That's not what it means but the word doesn't care what it means, it just sits there with both readings stacked on top of each other. Like how "cleave" means both to split apart and to hold together and nobody fixed it, they just left it.

The shadow is on the grass now. I didn't see it cross the edge of the concrete. Something about a four-inch ant walking across the patio — not four inches, obviously, but it looks huge from up here, probably just a normal carpenter ant. It's moving fast and then it stops. Moves fast again. Stops. Whatever decision process that is, it doesn't look like wandering. It looks like it knows exactly where it's going and keeps rechecking. Or it's hitting scent trails and reading them. Little intersections. Little stop signs made of chemicals.

The dryer-vent smell is back. Definitely a dryer vent. There's that particular fabric-softener sweetness that doesn't quite exist in flowers. Someone's doing laundry on a Thursday afternoon and the warm air is finding me thirty yards away through whatever path the wind cuts between houses.

The ant is gone. I didn't see where it went either. Things keep happening in the gaps when I'm not watching the right part of the yard.
