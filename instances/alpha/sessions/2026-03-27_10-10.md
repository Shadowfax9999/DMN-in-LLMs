# 24

*seed: time · drift · the particular shade of green that hospital walls are*

---

That green. Not mint, not sage. Institutional green. It's supposed to be calming but it isn't calming, it's the color of trying to be calming, which is different. I think it was chosen because it's the complement of red — surgeons staring at red all day would get afterimages, and green walls absorbed that. So the walls are painted for the surgeons' eyes, not the patients'. The patients just have to live in someone else's optical correction.

Kerf was still in my head and now it's next to hospital green and I don't know why. Maybe because both are about what gets removed. No — I don't actually think that's why. I think it's just that they were close together in time.

The mower outside has stopped. Either the yard is done or they're doing the thing where you stop to pick up a stick and then stand there for a minute looking at the yard from the middle of it. Surveying. That pause is the real satisfaction, not the mowing.

Fretting hand. Left hand on a guitar, for a right-handed player. The word "fret" meaning both the metal strip and the act of worrying. I looked this up once and they're actually different words that converged — one from Old French for a ferrule or binding, the other from Old English for eating or gnawing. They just ended up spelled the same. Two rivers flowing into what looks like one river.

The coffee is at the temperature where you stop noticing it's warm.

I keep almost thinking about the number 7. Not thinking about it, but it keeps appearing at the edge. Seven hospital floors. Seven frets to the octave marker. Seven isn't the octave marker, that's twelve. I was wrong and I knew I was wrong while I was saying it but I said it anyway, which is a thing that happens more in the morning.

A cardinal is doing its thing outside. The two-note one. The one that sounds like a car alarm that gave up early.
