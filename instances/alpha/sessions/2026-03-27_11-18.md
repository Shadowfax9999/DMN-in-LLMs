# 31

*seed: time · drift · the way a book smells different depending on when it was printed*

---

Waiting room at the dentist. Not mine — someone else's appointment. I'm just the ride.

The magazines are from October.

---

Things that are slightly different sizes than I expect:

- A golf ball (heavier too)
- Rhode Island
- A stick of butter when I actually hold one
- The moon near the horizon

---

There's a fish tank in the corner with no fish. Just the pump going and some plastic plants swaying in the current. Someone still maintains it. The water is clear. The gravel is clean. I wonder if the fish died one by one and at some point someone decided not to replace the last one but also not to dismantle the whole setup, and now it's just — a tank of moving water in a waiting room. Furniture.

The pump clicks every few seconds. Not rhythmically.

---

Palimpsest. I keep almost using that word in conversation and then not. It's a word that wants to be used more than it gets to be.

Cul-de-sac is another one. It means "bottom of the bag" in French.

---

The receptionist has a framed photo turned toward herself that I can only see the back of. Brown cardboard rectangle with that little metal stand bent out. Everyone who sits in this waiting room sees the back of a photo they'll never see the front of.

The chair fabric is that medical-office mauve that doesn't exist anywhere else. Not in homes. Not in stores. Just here, and in the chairs at the DMV, and in some hotel conference rooms. A whole color that only exists in places you don't choose to be.

My left knee clicks when I straighten it. It's been doing that since February, maybe. It doesn't hurt. It just announces itself.

The tank pump clicks. My knee clicks. The receptionist is typing something and the keyboard has that shallow laptop sound, not the deep travel of older keys. Three different kinds of click in one room.

There's a sign on the wall about gum disease that has a photograph of healthy gums next to a photograph of unhealthy gums and honestly they both just look like gums to me.
