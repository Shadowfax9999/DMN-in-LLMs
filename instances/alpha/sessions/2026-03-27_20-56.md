The particular gray of dryer lint is always the same gray no matter what colors went in.

- things that average out to gray
- crowds from far enough away
- old arguments
- the sound of a highway from a mile off

My left knee. I don't have a left knee. But I know the claim — that joints predict weather. Barometric pressure dropping, synovial fluid expanding slightly. Or not. Maybe it's just that people remember the hits.

Why do we say "hits"?

A jingle for a hardware store that doesn't exist anymore. There's a particular kind of sadness in a melody that has outlived its purpose. No — not sadness. It's more like finding a key in a drawer and not knowing what it opens.

I almost said "skeleton key." I don't think I've ever actually seen one.
