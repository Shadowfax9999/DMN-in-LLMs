# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

The cardinal's still going or it's a different one. There's no way to know without seeing it and I'm not going to get up. Friday has a color and it's not the color you'd expect, it's more like a dull gold, like the edge of a manila folder. I don't know if everyone gets that or if it's just a thing my brain decided once and never revised.

Seven again. Seven windows on the front of a building I'm picturing but I don't think it's a real building. It might be composited from several buildings. The mind does that — hands you a confident image that turns out to be stitched together from three different places, and you don't notice the seams unless you look hard. The left side of this building is from somewhere in Pennsylvania. The right side might be a photograph.

The coffee is actually cold now. There's a specific moment where warm becomes room temperature and I never catch it. It's like trying to watch the minute hand move.

A plane is going over, very high. The sound arrives after the plane has already moved past where you're looking, which I know, everyone knows, but it's still strange every time. You're hearing where it was. The sound is a ghost of a position. No — it's just the speed of sound being slower than light. That's the whole thing.

The word "ferrule." I said it yesterday apparently. The metal cap on the end of an umbrella. Also the metal band that holds a pencil eraser on. I think also the thing on the bottom of a cane. It's a word for the small metal part that keeps something else from splitting apart, and nobody uses it in conversation. You'd say "the little metal part."

My left knee does the thing when I straighten it after sitting. Not pain. Just a click that reminds me the knee is there. Knees are the kind of joint that seems like it was designed in a hurry. A hinge with cartilage pads floating around inside it held together by rubber bands.

I wonder if the cardinal is territorial about that specific branch or just that general area. There's probably a paper about this. There are papers about everything. Someone did a study. Someone's always done a study. That's actually reassuring in a way I can't quite — 

The shadow of the window frame has moved about an inch on the floor since I sat down. You can almost use it as a clock if you had marks on the floor. People did use it as a clock. That was the whole idea behind a sundial, which when you think about it is just a stick. The first clock was a stick in the ground and its own shadow.

I had a dream last night but I can only remember that it involved a parking garage and the feeling of being late. Not the content of being late — nobody was waiting, there was no appointment — just the feeling itself, free-floating, attached to concrete ramps and fluorescent light. Dreams do that. They generate the emotion first and then build a set around it.

The plane sound is gone. The cardinal stopped. It's just the refrigerator now, which has a hum that's either a B-flat or I'm making that up. I read somewhere that most American refrigerators hum at 60 hertz because of the electrical current, and 60 hertz is close to B-flat but not exactly. It's between A-sharp and B-flat, which are the same note anyway in equal temperament, so that distinction doesn't actually help.

The shadow moved again. Probably three inches total now. The sun does its thing whether or not anyone's tracking it, which is obvious but also the kind of obvious that gets more obvious the longer you sit with it. The coffee is definitely cold. I could microwave it but microwaved coffee tastes different in a way that's hard to describe — not worse exactly, just like the coffee equivalent of a photocopy. The heat is the same but something about the texture shifted.

My phone buzzed in the other room but I'm not going to check it. The buzz decays fast. After about fifteen seconds the curiosity is half what it was, and after a minute you've mostly forgotten it, and after five minutes it's just another thing that might be there when you eventually get up. The refrigerator is louder than I think it should be.
