The door-holding thing. When the distance is wrong and you're both committed. You can see them speed up slightly and you can feel yourself smiling in a way that means nothing, just holding the smile open like you're holding the door open, and the whole thing takes four seconds but you're both working.

I used to think revolving doors were invented to solve wind pressure in tall buildings. That might be true. But they also solve the door-holding problem completely. Nobody owes anyone anything in a revolving door. You're just in your little wedge.

Wedge is a good word for a pie slice shape. It doesn't sound like what it is. It sounds heavier, denser. A wedge of cheese makes sense. A wedge of lemon barely makes sense. A wedge of sky makes no sense.

Geese fly in a V and everyone knows the aerodynamic reason but I wonder if anyone has ever mapped what happens when the lead goose drops back. Like, does the formation wobble for a second? Is there a moment of confusion? Or is it seamless, the way a school of fish turns. I think geese are probably worse at it than fish.

Fish don't blink.

That sentence just sitting there feels true in a way I can't do anything with.

I had a geometry teacher who would say "clearly" before things that were not clear. "Clearly the perpendicular bisector intersects at—" and I would think, no. Not clearly. But I never said anything because the word "clearly" has a silencing effect. If it's clear to everyone else. If I'm the only one who.

The number of times I've understood something only after pretending I already understood it. Like the understanding needed the shape of confidence to pour itself into.

There's a type of cookie that every office break room has that nobody specifically bought. It's just there. In a clear plastic tub. The cookies are round and have one chip visible on the surface. They taste like the idea of a cookie rather than a cookie. I think they might generate spontaneously.

My left foot is asleep right now and it's that stage where it's not pins and needles yet, just absence. Like the foot is a rumor.

Somewhere right now a traffic light is cycling through its colors for no one. Green, yellow, red, green. A completely empty intersection at 2 AM in some town in Kansas, and the light doesn't know. It's not waiting. It's just the next color, then the next color.
