A pickup is a magnet wrapped in wire. Thousands of turns of wire so thin you can break it by looking at it wrong. The string vibrates above it and the magnetic field wobbles and that wobble becomes current and the current becomes sound in a speaker somewhere across the room. I've never wound one but I've seen the machines that do it, the wire feeding off a spool so fast it blurs, and the counter ticking up — seven thousand, eight thousand turns — and if it snags or breaks you start over. The difference between a bright pickup and a warm one is just more or fewer turns of the same wire around the same magnet. That and the magnet itself, alnico versus ceramic, and people will argue about this for hours, for years, for the entire duration of internet forums. Someone is always arguing about pickups right now, at 10:53 on a Friday morning, when they should be doing something else. I should be doing something else. I'm standing in the kitchen holding a glass of water and not drinking it. The light is coming through the window over the sink at the angle where you can see every smudge on the glass. Fingerprints, lip marks, a faint cloudiness near the bottom that means the dishwasher didn't finish its job. The water inside the glass is fine. The glass around it is what's suspect. I drink it anyway. The water is cold enough that I can feel it go down, that line of cold tracking through my chest, which is a strange thing to be able to feel, the inside of your own esophagus, but only when the temperature is different enough from the rest of you. You're mostly opaque to yourself. You can feel your surfaces — skin, the inside of your mouth, the bottoms of your feet — but the middle of you is just a vague hum. Somewhere in there a liver is doing something. Kidneys. The pancreas, which I could not point to on my own body with any confidence. Left side? Behind the stomach? It's in there. A pickup converts one kind of energy to another and the pancreas converts one kind of molecule to another and I don't know where either of them sits exactly, the pickup somewhere under the strings between the neck and the bridge, the pancreas somewhere under the ribs between the spine and the front of me. Both of them doing critical work in locations I'd describe as "around there somewhere." The glass is empty now and I put it in the sink on top of yesterday's glass which I also didn't put in the dishwasher. Two glasses in a sink is fine. Three starts to look like something. Four is a statement about how your week is going.
