# 93

*seed: time · drift*

---

Stairs counted in the dark are always one more or one fewer than you expected.

The underside of a bridge is a different country from the top of it.

Scaffolding goes up and then it comes down and the building doesn't remember.

Someone's grandmother still has a telephone number memorized that no longer connects to anything.

There are fonts nobody has named yet because the sign painter died.

You can hear when a gym is empty.

The inside of a walnut shell looks like a map of a place that isn't flat.

Paper absorbs a pen differently depending on which direction you're pushing it, and you learn this without deciding to.

A bus schedule is someone's guess.

The smell of a wool coat coming in from rain is almost the same as the smell of rain coming in from outside, but not.

A concrete floor in a warehouse — the scale of it makes the ceiling feel apologetic.
