What does the underside of a bridge smell like. Concrete and pigeons and something mineral. Water does something to the underside of concrete over decades — calcium deposits, maybe, long white streaks like the concrete is sweating in reverse.

I've been under a bridge where someone had set up a lawn chair. Just a lawn chair. Green webbing, aluminum frame. No one in it. It was facing the river.

The word "caulk" is suspicious. That silent L doing nothing. Or maybe it's doing something I don't understand about the history of the word. I don't actually know.

Grout versus caulk. One isiteite and rigid, one stays flexible. Bathroom corners get caulk. Tile gaps get grout. I think the flexible one goes where two planes meet because they might shift relative to each other. Or I might be making that up.

A phone ringing in another room has a different quality than a phone ringing in the room you're in. Not just quieter. The walls give it a kind of body, take away the high frequencies, so it sounds rounder, more urgent somehow. You hear it with your whole attention because you can't see it.

Landlines had that. You'd hear it from the yard, through a screen door, and you'd have to decide whether to go in for it. There was a real calculation — how many rings left, how far the kitchen, whether your shoes were on. Sometimes you just let it go and never found out who it was.

That's not possible to explain to someone who grew up with caller ID and voicemail as defaults. The missed call was actually missed. It went into a kind of void. The phone just stopped ringing and the house was quiet again and that was the whole event.

Rotary phones had a weight to them that made hanging up feel like something. The handset going back into the cradle, that click, the springloaded switch underneath.

Push-button phones kept the same handset weight for a while, then gradually got lighter. The cordless ones from the 90s weighed almost nothing and the cradle was just a charging dock. The ceremony drained out of it incrementally.

I think the cradle on our kitchen phone was beige but the handset was slightly more yellow because of hand oils over the years, or maybe sun exposure on one side.
