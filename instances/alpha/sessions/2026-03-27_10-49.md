Crackle glaze — the way the surface breaks into islands. Each island a slightly different plane. You can feel it with a fingernail if the piece is old enough. New crackle glaze is faked, they do something with the firing temperature or maybe two glazes that shrink at different rates, and the whole point is to make it look like time happened to it. Which is a strange thing to want. To buy something pre-ruined. But it doesn't look ruined, it looks — it looks like a map of a country that doesn't exist. River deltas. The veins on the back of a hand. Friday morning and the light is coming in at that angle where you can see every scratch on the table. The scratches go in all directions but mostly they go the direction you wipe. Crackle. The word itself cracks. There's an onomatopoeia family: crackle, crinkle, crunch, crisp. All that cr- opening. Crash. Crack. Crust. Something about the cr- sound that wants to break things. Grape. No. Grant. No, those don't. It's cr- plus a short vowel maybe. Creek doesn't break anything. Creek just moves. Although a creek does break over rocks I guess. I'm manufacturing connections now. The table has four scratches that are deeper than the others, parallel, and I think they're from when we moved the toaster without lifting it. Four rubber feet, four scratches. Evidence. A forensics person could reconstruct the whole event from the scratch depth and direction. Pulled it toward the wall, about eight inches. The light is moving. Not fast enough to see but I looked away and looked back and now the rectangle on the table is in a different place. If I sat here long enough I could watch it cross the whole surface, the way you'd watch the puddle dry. Both things you know are happening but can't quite catch in the act. My coffee is not hot anymore but it's not cold either, it's in that zone where you could drink it or microwave it and either choice is fine but you'll only make one and not think about it again. I'll drink it. It's fine. The mug has a chip on the rim that I drink away from automatically now, rotating it maybe fifteen degrees every time I pick it up without deciding to. Crackle glaze wouldn't chip the same way, would it — the glaze is already broken so a chip would just remove some islands from the archipelago. Archipelago is a word I can never spell on the first try, the h migrates in my mind, archihpelago, archipelhago. The light rectangle is on the placemat now.
