# 33

*seed: time · drift · fulcrum*

---

Fulcrum. The L in the middle of it does something. Ful-crum. It sounds heavier than the thing it describes, which is just a point. A pivot. The thing that doesn't move.

Seesaws. The bolt through the middle is always rusty.

---

Things that are fulcrums but nobody calls them that:

- the knuckle of a door handle
- the hip, when you're carrying a toddler
- that spot on the edge of a table where you balance a long board to see which end is heavier

---

Standing in line at the post office. There are four people ahead of me and one window open. The guy at the counter has a box he didn't tape well enough and the clerk is explaining this to him patiently. He's patting his pockets for something. Tape, maybe. Or a pen.

The woman in front of me is holding a padded envelope against her chest like a schoolbook. She's wearing one of those lanyards with a work badge still clipped to it. The photo on the badge is too far away to see but I can tell it's a face turned slightly left.

The floor has that terrazzo with the brass divider strips. Some of the brass strips have pulled up slightly at the edges. You can feel them through thin shoes.

There's a pen on a chain and the chain is exactly too short to write comfortably. You have to hunch over the counter and hold the form down with your other hand. The pen works, though. Blue ink, a little gummy.

---

Fulcrum comes from Latin. I think. Fulcire — to support? That sounds right but I'm not sure.

The guy found a pen but not tape. The clerk has tape. There's a brief negotiation about whether the tape is free. It is.

---

Seven. The number seven looks like it's leaning. Like it could fall over. Eight is stable. Nine is seven with better posture. Six is nine doing a handstand. I've thought this before, probably. Four is the only number that looks like furniture.

The line moved. Three people ahead now. The woman with the envelope hasn't shifted her grip once.
