The number 7 looks sharp. 8 looks soft. This isn't synesthesia, it's just — 7 has that angle. 8 is all curves. 3 is soft too but in a different way, more like a mouth than a body. 4 could go either way depending on whether you write it open or closed.

Rubber bands. The way they go brittle in a drawer. You reach for one and it snaps immediately, not from force but from time. How long does that take. A year? Two years? There's some drawer right now with a rubber band that's exactly at the threshold.

I keep almost sneezing. That pre-sneeze state where your face is just suspended. It's one of the only things where you can feel a reflex loading.

The word "caulk." The L is silent but it changes the word anyway. Without it you'd read it differently. Silent letters aren't doing nothing, they're changing the mouth even when the mouth doesn't use them.

Alan someone. Alan — I'm thinking of a specific Alan and I can't get to the last name. He wore glasses. This happens with names more than anything else, the first name arrives and then there's just a door. Sometimes the last name comes twenty minutes later while you're doing something else. Sometimes it doesn't come at all and you have to look it up and then you think "of course."

A puddle on concrete dries from the edges in.

I wonder if the Alan thing is going to resolve. It feels close. Wire-frame glasses, taught something. Might have been an Alan, might have been an Allen. That distinction matters for searching but not for remembering.

Friday feels different from Thursday in a way that's not just about the weekend. Friday has a color. Thursday doesn't really. Monday has a weight. These are probably just associations built up over decades of school schedules but they feel intrinsic, like the days actually have those properties.

The puddle thing — I've watched that happen but I've never watched the whole process start to finish. You'd have to sit there for an hour probably. The middle is always the last part to go and it gets smaller in a shape that isn't quite the shape of the original puddle.
