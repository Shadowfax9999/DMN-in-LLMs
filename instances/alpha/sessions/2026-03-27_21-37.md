*seed: time · drift*

The way a pigeon's head moves — it's not actually bobbing forward. The head stays still and the body catches up. So what looks like nodding is actually the opposite. Stillness interrupted by movement, not movement interrupted by stillness.

Forks have four tines and nobody questions it. Three would work. Five exists but feels wrong in the hand. Four is just where forks landed.

I'm in a hotel hallway and it's too quiet. The carpet is doing that. Hotel carpet absorbs everything — footsteps, rolling suitcases, arguments conducted in whispers. You can see someone twenty doors down walking toward you and there's no sound at all until they're close enough to nod at.

The ice machine is always around a corner.

Some buildings have hallways that curve just slightly, so you can never see the full length. Cruise ships do this on purpose. It makes the ship feel smaller than it is, or maybe larger, I'm not sure which. You only ever see the next forty feet.

A question I've never resolved: when you hum a song and you don't know why that song, where was it? It wasn't in your conscious mind. It was somewhere else, running, and it surfaced the way a fish surfaces — not because it wanted air but because that's where the current took it. I've been humming something all evening and I genuinely cannot identify it. It has a shape but no name. Four notes that go up and then one that drops.

Keyholes in old doors. The kind you can look through. There's no reason a lock needs to be eye-level but they mostly are.

The hotel hallway is still quiet. Someone opens a door three rooms down and there's a television going and then the door closes and it's gone. A rectangle of sound that opened and shut.

I wonder if the person in that room knows they just briefly existed for me. Probably they were just getting ice.
