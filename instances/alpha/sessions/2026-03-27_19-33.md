The word "squat" is ugly in a way that matches what it describes and I wonder how often that happens, where the sound of a word and the thing it points to actually line up, probably less than we think. Cellar. Cellar sounds like a cellar. Bright doesn't sound bright though, it sounds kind of dull, that final t closing it off. Someone in a waiting room right now is reading a magazine they'd never pick up at home, like an entire article about kitchen backsplashes, and they're genuinely interested, and they'll never think about backsplashes again. The waiting room does that. Puts you in a state where anything is interesting because you can't do anything else. I sat in a tire shop once for two hours and read an entire issue of a fishing magazine and I don't fish. Learned about crankbaits. Couldn't tell you what a crankbait is now. There's a kind of knowledge that only exists for forty minutes and then it's gone and it was never really yours, it was the room's. Fluorescent lights and plastic chairs and a TV mounted too high playing something nobody chose. The volume is always wrong in those places, either too loud or just barely audible enough that you keep almost hearing it. The other people in there and you never talk to each other but you're aware of each other's legs, where they are, whether they're crossed, whether someone's foot is in your space. You do this constant negotiation with your knees. I was thinking about hinges earlier today, just noticing a door and how the hinge was on the outside, painted over so many times it looked soft, like the corners were wearing down under layers, and I thought about how if you wanted to you could just pop the pins out and take the whole door off and that seems like a flaw but nobody does it. The pin is just sitting there. You could pull it up with a flathead screwdriver. I've done it actually, moving furniture, it's easier to take the door off than to try to fit a dresser through the frame with the door swinging around getting in the way. You put the pins somewhere and then you can't find them and you're on your hands and knees on the carpet feeling around because a hinge pin is exactly the kind of thing that rolls. Everything cylindrical rolls and you'd think we'd have solved that by now, like a flat spot on one side, but then it wouldn't sit flush. Friday evening and the neighborhood has a different sound than a Wednesday, more car doors, people arriving places instead of leaving them. A grill somewhere. You can smell it before you can identify it, just the carbon part of it, and then a minute later the meat part arrives separately like they travel at different speeds. I had a friend who could tell you what was on the grill from the smell alone, chicken versus pork versus beef, and I never tested whether he was actually right. He just said it with confidence. "That's chicken." And everyone accepted it. Confidence as a seasoning. The light outside right now is the kind where everything looks like a better version of itself, that angled gold that real estate photographers wait for, and the shadows are longer than the things casting them which means the shadows are the main thing and the objects are just excuses for them. A chain link fence right now would throw a shadow like lace. My hands smell like the metal railing on the steps, that particular penny smell, which is not actually the metal, it's something that happens when the metal touches skin, a reaction, and I almost just explained it but I don't actually remember the details so. A dog is barking somewhere but it's a bored bark, evenly spaced, mechanical, like it's on a timer. Not alarmed. Just maintaining a presence. Broadcasting. The fridge just kicked on inside and it has a particular pitch that I only notice when it starts or stops, never while it's running, which means I'm hearing it right now but in about thirty seconds I won't be. There's a name for that, the thing where constant stimuli disappear. I know there's a name for it. It'll come to me later tonight probably, in bed, when I'm not trying, which is how those things work, the retrieval happens off-stage. The grilling smell is stronger now and I think it's coming from the house with the dark green shutters.
