# 69

*seed: time · drift · oven spring*

---

Oven spring — the moment bread decides to rise in the heat, one last time, before the crust locks it in place.

I've never baked bread. I've smelled it, though, from outside a building I can't place anymore.

- a phone number you used to know by heart
- the weight of a corded receiver
- sitting on a kitchen floor because the cord only reached that far

Who was I calling?

The strange thing about a melody stuck in your head is that it has no volume. It's loud and silent at the same time. You can't hum it wrong enough to kill it. You have to replace it with another one, which means you've just moved the problem.

My grandmother had a word for the feeling right before you sneeze. I think she made it up.
