*seed: time · drift*

---

Pint glasses are heavier than they look when they're empty.

The way a chord changes the room — I keep coming back to that. Not the theory of it. The felt size of it. E minor is bigger than A minor somehow. More open. Like the ceiling went up. But they're structurally the same interval pattern just starting on a different note. The piano doesn't care. The guitar doesn't care. Something in the ear cares.

Rooms with high ceilings sound different when you clap. That delay.

---

I was trying to remember the name of the street where the pharmacy had that weird step — not a full step, maybe three inches, just enough that you'd trip if you weren't expecting it. They'd painted the edge yellow. The yellow was wearing off in the middle where feet hit. A path through the paint.

The pharmacy smelled like rubber and something floral. Not flowers. Floral like a cleaning product pretending to be flowers.

---

Counting things without meaning to:

- Syllables in someone's sentence
- Steps between the car and the door
- How many times the turn signal clicks before the light changes
- Bricks in a row, but only for a few feet, then you lose interest
- The rings on a coffee cup stain, though those aren't really countable

Why does the mind count. Not for any reason. Just because things come in sequences and the brain can't help tapping along.

Fourteen. That's the number of steps in a standard residential staircase. I don't know if that's true but it feels true. It's the kind of number you'd believe.

Seven is always the number people guess when you say "pick a number between one and ten." Not always. But often enough that magicians use it.

Five is the number of things you can hold in your mind at once. Or seven. It changed at some point.

The font on a digital clock makes the number 4 look either open-topped or closed-topped and I have a strong preference for open-topped but I couldn't tell you why.
