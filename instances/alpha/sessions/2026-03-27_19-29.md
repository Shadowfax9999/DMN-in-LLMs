# 56

*seed: time · drift · baffle*

---

Baffle. A baffle is a thing you put inside a thing to redirect something else. Inside a muffler, inside a speaker cabinet. The word itself sounds like someone trying to talk with their mouth full. Baffled. I am baffled. The thing that redirects and the state of being redirected — same word. That's unusual, that an object and the condition it produces share a name.

There's a helicopter somewhere northeast of here, or what I think is northeast. You can never tell with helicopters because the sound arrives from a direction that isn't quite where the helicopter is. It's already moved by the time the sound gets to you. So you look where you hear it and there's nothing and then you find it a little further along. Like how the place a fish actually is in the water isn't quite where you see it. Refraction. But with the helicopter it's time, not light.

Bafflement is underrated as a state. Not confusion — confusion is anxious, it wants resolution. Bafflement just sits there with its mouth open. A baffled person has stopped trying. There's something restful about it.

I had a friend in college who pronounced "banal" to rhyme with "canal" and another who said it like "buh-NAHL" and they got into an argument about it at dinner once that lasted longer than it should have. I think they were actually arguing about something else. People usually are.

The helicopter is gone or I stopped hearing it. Both of those feel different but I can't tell which one happened. There's a point where a sound fades below your threshold and you don't notice the exact moment, you just realize it's been quiet for a while. The absence was already underway before you detected it.

Friday evenings have a specific quality of permission. Like the air itself loosens. Saturday morning is different — Saturday morning already has the faint forward lean of the weekend having started, the sense that you should be doing something with it. Friday evening is pure decompression. Nothing has to happen yet.

The pollen on my car this morning was so thick it looked deliberate, like someone had dusted it. Bright yellow-green. I drew a line through it with my finger on the hood and the paint underneath looked almost black by contrast.

I wonder what color pollen looks like to bees.

My left knee has been making a sound when I go down stairs. Not pain, just a sound. A dry click. It started maybe two months ago. I keep meaning to pay attention to whether it's every step or just certain angles but then I'm at the bottom of the stairs and I've forgotten to track it.

The word "baffle" has already left. It came, it did whatever it was going to do, and now I'm thinking about knee cartilage. A baffle redirects flow. The helicopter is three miles away by now, probably. The pollen is still on the car.
