# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

Things that are heavier than you expect:

- A small glass paperweight
- Batteries
- A single tile
- Wet denim
- A bowl of change
- An old telephone handset

Things that are lighter than you expect:

- A kayak
- Styrofoam cooler full of nothing
- A mounted deer head
- Most hats, when you pick them up

---

The word "crisp" starts at the back of your mouth and moves to the front.

---

Sitting in a parked car in a parking garage. Engine off. The tick tick tick of something cooling. The concrete smell. Parking garages always feel like they're the same temperature, but they're not. They just look like a temperature — that gray, that fluorescent humming.

The lines on the floor are so confident. Someone painted them knowing exactly where every car would go, for years.

A shopping cart against a pillar. One wheel turned sideways.

I locked the car already but I'm still sitting here. Not because I'm thinking about anything. Just because I haven't moved yet. There's a version of rest that's just: the last action is over and the next one hasn't started. You're in the gap.

A pigeon is walking along a ledge on the level above. You can hear the feet more than you'd think. That scratchy, tapping sound. Pigeons walk like they're always late for something but refuse to run.

---

Do scissors work because of the lever action or because of the shearing? Both, probably. Two blades passing each other very close. The thing being cut has nowhere to go.

Left-handed scissors exist but nobody ever has them.

The handles are usually different sizes — one round, one oval. I never thought about which finger goes where until just now. The thumb gets the small one. Always the thumb.
