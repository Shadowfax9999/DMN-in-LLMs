# 54

*seed: time · drift · wind chime*

---

Wind chime — but which note. You never choose which note a wind chime plays. You hang it and the wind decides.

Five aluminum tubes. Or four. Never counted the ones on the porch across the street growing up.

Tubular bells in an orchestra are tuned. Wind chimes aren't, usually. Or they are, but to a pentatonic scale so nothing clashes. So the wind can't make a mistake.

That's a strange design decision. Building something so that randomness always sounds good.

Dice that only have good numbers on them.

---

The word "pentatonic" — penta, five, tonic, tone. Five tones. But which five. There are different pentatonic scales. The one on the black keys of a piano is one. You can play only the black keys and it sounds like something.

I wonder if anyone has ever made a wind chime tuned to a whole-tone scale. That would sound like Debussy falling down stairs.

---

Falling down stairs has a rhythm. It's not regular but it's not random either. The body keeps almost catching itself.

A slinky going down stairs has a more regular rhythm.

Things I can hear right now: nothing specific. I'm inventing this. There's no porch. There's no wind chime.

---

Aluminum oxidizes but the oxide protects it. Steel rusts and the rust eats deeper. Two metals, two relationships with air.

Copper turns green. Bronze turns green. Brass turns green eventually. All the copper alloys go green given enough time. The Statue of Liberty was brown when they put it up.

I don't know what color wind chime tubes start as before they get that dull matte look. Probably shiny.

The tubes are open at both ends, I think. Or closed at the top where the string goes through. I'm not sure. Whether a tube is open or closed at one end changes what note it plays — open tubes and closed tubes resonate at different harmonics. An organ pipe that's stopped at one end plays an octave lower than an open pipe of the same length.

So a four-inch tube that's capped plays the same note as an eight-inch tube that's open.

Or is it the other way around. The capped one plays lower. I think. The air column bounces back instead of escaping, so it behaves like it's twice as long.

I'm not sure that's right either.
