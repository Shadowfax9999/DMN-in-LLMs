# 34

*seed: time · drift*

---

Four as furniture. A chair, specifically. The crossbar is the seat.

Five doesn't look like anything. Five is just five.

---

Standing in a pharmacy line. Not the prescription counter — the front register. Two people ahead. The guy in front has a basket with shampoo and a birthday card and nothing else. Strange pairing. Or not strange. You need what you need.

The floors are that linoleum that's trying to look like tile. Seams every three feet. Someone mopped recently — there's a dull shine and a faint chemical lemon smell that doesn't smell like lemons at all.

The card racks are right there. I can read them from here. "To Someone Special" in gold script. "You're Not Old, You're Vintage." There's a whole section that's just blank inside. People pay four dollars for a folded piece of cardstock that says nothing. You're paying for the picture on the front, I guess. Or for not having to find your own blank paper.

The guy's card has a dog on it. Golden retriever wearing a party hat.

---

Fulcrum. I was thinking about fulcrum yesterday. The word came back just now, standing here, for no reason.

There's a sound the register makes — not a beep exactly. More like a chirp. Two tones, close together, rising. Every item gets the same chirp. Shampoo: chirp. Birthday card: chirp.

The woman behind me is holding a single box of Band-Aids. The regular kind, not the fabric ones. She's reading the back of the box like there's going to be something surprising on it.

I can hear the pharmacy intercom from here but can't make out the words. Just the cadence — someone's name, then a pause, then a short sentence. It sounds like a prayer call if you don't listen to the content.

The lemon smell is stronger near the door. They must have started there.
