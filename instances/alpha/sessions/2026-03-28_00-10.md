*seed: time · drift · counterweight*

Counterweight going down while the cab goes up, somewhere in the shaft wall, never visible.

Two things agreeing to move in opposite directions so neither one has to work as hard.

A hammock hung between two trees — neither tree chose this.

When you carry something heavy on one shoulder for a long time, the other shoulder rises slightly. You don't notice until you put the bag down and one shoulder is suddenly higher than the other for a few seconds, finding its way back.

There's a kind of person who always looks like they're leaning slightly away from a conversation they're in. Not unfriendly. Counterweighted by something else, somewhere else.

In old clocks the weights are visible — hanging chains, slowly descending all day. You wind them back up before bed. The clock only runs because something is always falling.

The word "ballast." Ships carrying rocks so they don't tip. Trains. Hot air balloons dropping sandbags to climb. The logic of heaviness used to become lighter.

I don't know why my mind keeps going to held breath. The moment before something resolves. The counterweight just sitting there doing its quiet half of the work.

A suitcase with one wheel that sticks. You develop a slight lean to the right to compensate and you carry this lean for three days through airports.
