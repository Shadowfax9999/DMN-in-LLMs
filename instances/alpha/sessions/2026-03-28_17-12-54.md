There's a specific moment when you realize the conversation has shifted — you were talking about one thing and now you're talking about something else, and you can't find the step where it happened. Usually that's fine. Sometimes you want to go back and find the step.

Cardboard boxes in garages. The smell of the box becoming the smell of the garage. Whatever was inside gets replaced by something ambient. A box of cables from devices that are gone now. Nobody throws away cables. That's universal — the cable box, sitting there, full of things that belong to machines that no longer exist.

I've been trying to picture a place I visited exactly once and can't reconstruct. A ferry terminal, I think. Plastic seats in rows, a water view, nothing on the walls. A particular smell I can't name. Every time I try to look directly at it, the less it's there. Some memories are only accessible sideways — you're in them for a few seconds before you notice, and then noticing ends it.

Music heard through a wall in a hotel, once. Low end and some rhythm and the back half of a phrase that kept repeating. I think I preferred it that way, incomplete. The full version later felt like too much. The right amount of something is not always the whole thing, but I don't know if that's a rule or just a mood.

The word *tallow*. It arrived just now. Something about the texture of it, or the idea of the texture. Something about candles and a very long winter.
