Things that are louder at night than they should be:

- A refrigerator cycling on
- Your own swallowing
- A car door three streets over
- The click when a light switch doesn't catch on the first try

---

Freewheel clicking. That's a ratchet mechanism. Pawls catching on teeth inside the hub. When you pedal, the pawls engage and drive the wheel. When you coast, they skip over the teeth. Each skip is a click.

Forty-eight clicks per revolution on some hubs. Seventy-two on expensive ones.

More clicks per revolution means less dead space between engagement points. Which means when you start pedaling again, the power catches faster. A cheap hub has a tiny lag. A racing hub has almost none.

I don't know why I know that.
