UTC doesn't stand for what it should. It's a compromise between the English order (Coordinated Universal Time) and the French order (Temps Universel Coordonné) so they picked letters that match neither language. A negotiated acronym. Deliberately wrong in two directions at once.

There's a clock in my dentist's office that runs about four minutes fast and I always wonder if it's intentional. Four minutes of buffer built into every appointment. Or nobody's fixed it. Both explanations feel equally likely and I'll never ask.

Clocks in exam rooms face the student. Clocks in waiting rooms face the person waiting. Clocks in kitchens are above the door you came in through so you have to turn around to check them.

I've been thinking about how Friday night feels different from Tuesday night even when you're doing the same thing. Sitting in the same chair reading the same book. The air has a different quality. Some of that is just knowing other people are out, that the city is louder, that the week has a shape and you're at the trailing edge of it. But some of it is less explainable than that. Friday dark is a different dark.

The word "weekend" only dates to 1879. Before that there was just Sunday, and before that there was just the Sabbath, and before that the days didn't cluster into sevens everywhere — the Romans had an eight-day market cycle, the nundinae, and the off-day was the ninth day because they counted inclusively.

Counting inclusively. The fence-post problem. If you build a fence that's 100 feet long with posts every 10 feet you need 11 posts, not 10. Programmers hit this constantly. Off-by-one errors. The most common bug in software is arguably a disagreement about whether you're counting the posts or the gaps.

A fence I walked past this week had one slat replaced with a newer, lighter piece of wood. It'll darken to match eventually. But for now it looks like a tooth.
