*seed: time · drift · obsidian*

---

Obsidian is heavier than you expect.

Things I held once and remember the weight of:
- a cast-iron doorstop shaped like a terrier
- someone's sleeping head
- a green glass fishing float found on a beach in Oregon
- a phone with bad news on the screen

The fishing float was hollow but felt solid. I think because I was afraid of dropping it.

Fear adds weight. That's not a metaphor — your hand actually grips harder, the muscles register more effort, and effort is how the body estimates mass. So the phone really was heavier.

My grandmother kept a bowl of marbles on a shelf. Not decorative marbles. Old ones, clay, from when she was a kid. They were ugly. She never explained them and I never asked.

I keep almost asking questions about tuning forks.
