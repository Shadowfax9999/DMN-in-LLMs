# 85

*seed: time · drift*

---

Floor two of a parking structure is the loneliest floor. Not the roof, which at least has sky. Not the ground floor, which has the entrance and the smell of outside. Floor two is the one where you park when you're late, and the ceiling is low, and the yellow paint on the concrete pillars is flaking, and there's always one car with a ticket on the windshield that makes you wonder how long it's been there and whether the owner is coming back or has just decided, somewhere between that day and now, to let it go.

A ticket on a windshield in the rain. The ink running. At some point it's just a wet rectangle.

Scaffolding on a building for so long you stop seeing the building and start thinking the scaffolding is the building. Whether there's a word for that. Whether there needs to be.

The coin in the jacket pocket — you can almost feel which coin it is before you look. The weight is different. Quarters have a particular gravity.

Handwriting you can't read later. Not because it's messy but because the thought behind it is gone and the letters don't point anywhere anymore. Like a path in the woods after the season changes.

A moth in a house, navigating by something that isn't there.

Forgetting to reply so long it would be strange to reply — and then doing it anyway, six months later, and the person writes back in four minutes.

There's a font on a cornerstone that belongs to no one anymore. Not the architect. Not the decade. Just the stone.
