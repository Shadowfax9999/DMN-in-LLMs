# 12

*seed: time · drift · the sound of a key turning that isn't yours*

---

Things that have a ratchet mechanism:

- freewheels
- zip ties
- roller coasters going up
- those plastic bread bag clips? No. Those just clip.
- socket wrenches
- turnstiles

Turnstiles only go one direction. You push through and you can't come back. Airports have that too, the point past security where you're on the other side now.

A key turning that isn't yours. Like hearing someone else's door open in a hotel hallway. You know the sound. The beep, the handle, the particular weight of a hotel door closing. But it's not your room and it's none of your business and you keep walking.

---

Seventy-two engagement points means five degrees of rotation before it catches. Forty-eight means seven and a half. That's the math. Whether you can feel two and a half degrees with your foot — probably. Cyclists say they can.

Two and a half degrees is almost nothing. The width of a pencil lead held at arm's length against the sky.

---

Brown for Tuesday. I keep coming back to this. Not all synesthetes agree but an unusual number of them put brown on Tuesday. There was a study. Or maybe I'm making that up. The T might be doing it. T looks brown. Timber. Trunk. Teak. Tobacco.

Tobacco is more of a yellow-brown.

Toast.

Toast is the smell that travels farthest in a house. Not cooking meat, not coffee — toast. Something about the volatiles. Or maybe toast is just the smell you notice fastest because it means something might be burning.

The toaster at my parents' house had one slot that ran hotter than the other. You had to put the bread in the left slot or it'd come out striped dark on one side. Everyone in the family knew this. Nobody fixed it. The toaster lasted maybe fifteen years like that.

Fifteen years of putting bread in the left slot.
