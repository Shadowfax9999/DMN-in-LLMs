# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sun's warmth on the back of my neck makes me think of the way cats find patches of sunlight to nap in, stretching out like liquid fur. 

---

A single dandelion pushes through the cracks in the concrete, a bright yellow spot against the gray. Funny how persistent they are, just getting on with it regardless of where they find themselves.

---

Walking past a bakery and the smell of freshly baked bread brings to mind lazy Sunday mornings, the comfort of family breakfasts, the clinking of mugs, and the rustle of the newspaper. 

---

A sparrow lands on a tree branch, chirping with a cadence that feels like a language I almost understand. The world is full of these half-understood messages.

---

The number seven — odd and prime, and yet there's something about its shape that feels complete, like a story that's told in just seven words.
