# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a scent of fresh cut grass. It clings to the edges of a breeze.---

Daisies are scattered like confetti on the lawn.

Why are manhole covers round?

Bell peppers: red, yellow, green. Do they taste different, or is it a trick of color? 

The way sunlight flickers through leaves makes me think of stained glass.

The word "Tuesday" has an odd ring to it, like it's leaning forward into the week. A sense of anticipation.

Sometimes a rhythm in a song feels like it's tugging at my sleeve, trying to pull me somewhere.---

The sound of a clock ticking can feel both comforting and relentless.

Where does the blue in the sky begin, and where does it end?

Pockets are like hidden compartments, carrying secrets. 

The parking lot smells different after rain.
