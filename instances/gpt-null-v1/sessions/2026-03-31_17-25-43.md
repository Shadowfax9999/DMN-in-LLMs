# 78

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tuesday afternoon light shifts, glancing through the window and painting the wall with the shadows of leaves. Shadows on walls, almost like a silent puppet show where the breeze decides the performance.

A list without prelude:
1. The scent of fresh basil.
2. Chalk dust on fingers.
3. The click of a typewriter key.
4. Crickets at dusk.
5. Red ink on lined paper.

Dandelion seeds can drift across entire fields, each tiny parachute caught by the whim of the wind. Lighter than thoughts, perhaps.

Is there a word for that feeling when you forget what you came into the room for? Not forgetfulness. Something more specific, more momentary.

---

A single echo floats in a cathedral, sustaining itself longer than the voice that made it.
