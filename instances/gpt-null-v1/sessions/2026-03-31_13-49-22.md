# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "afternoon" sometimes feels heavy, like it should be two separate things.

---

A red balloon. The weight of it drifting up, pulling slightly on the string tied to a small child's wrist. A moment when gravity feels less certain.

---

Does sunlight have a sound? Not in the sense that we can hear with our ears, but in the way it touches leaves and creates a silent symphony of shadows.

---

Numbers repeat. 1, 2, 3. The comfort of sequence, a rhythm in counting, like footsteps. 

---

There's a texture to forgetting. It's rough around the edges, like the fuzz of an old sweater.

---

The wayward fluff of a dandelion seed, floating through the air. Dreamlike.

---

Silent applause. The idea of it intrigues me. Can you feel the intention without the sound? 

The parking lot smells different after rain.
