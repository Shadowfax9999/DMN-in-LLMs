# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

Grass blades bend under a soft weight. Sun patches play on the ground, forming shapes that don't last. A breeze tips a leaf just enough for it to lose its balance and fall. The shadows of branches stretch long and thin across the pavement, waving ever so slightly in the wind's dance.

---

What does the inside of a balloon feel like before it's filled? Empty, flat, without tension. It waits quietly for the breath or pump that will give it form.

---

The word "caterpillar" sounds like a gentle crawl, something soft and undulating. 

---

Two words: "Starfish arms." That's what they call the branches on a cactus, reaching out in every direction, looking for their share of sunlight. But there is a cactus somewhere that stands alone, its arms tucked close, hugging itself.

---

I'm sitting in a chair that's seen better days. The cushion is worn, fabric pilled, but familiar. It holds the shape of countless hours spent reading or thinking. 

There are crumbs lodged in the seams.
