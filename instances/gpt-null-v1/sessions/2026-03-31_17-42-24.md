# 85

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sky is a dull blue, the kind that hints at the sun's descent but holds onto the day a little longer. The air smells like grass and something faintly sweet, maybe from flowers or the trees starting to wake up. A single bird's call — sharp and quick. Fallen petals on the concrete, their color fading. Shadows growing longer, stretching like they're trying to touch the horizon.

---

Does a day have a scent? Morning might smell like coffee or dew. Afternoon like warm asphalt. Evening carries a hint of smoke sometimes, maybe from a distant grill or a fireplace rekindled. Night is cooler, maybe earthy.

---

There's a pebble lodged in the street grate. Small, but stopping the water from flowing freely. I wonder how long it's been there, how many times it's been kicked around before settling into this momentary home. The water gathers, hesitates.

---

A wristwatch ticks quietly. A sound you don't always notice unless the room is silent and you're still. There's something comforting in its steadiness. The second hand ticks on, each movement a tiny permission to continue.
