# 54

*seed: time · drift*

---

Hummingbirds. I once read that their hearts beat over a thousand times per minute. It's hard to grasp just how fast that is, how in the time it takes to blink, their hearts have pulsed several times. The rhythm of their flight seems impossibly frenetic, yet somehow graceful.   

Why do some days feel longer than others? Not physically, as all continue through the same duration, but something stretches them, pulls them into a slow, syrupy drift. Like when you wake up in the middle of the night, and the minutes creep along. It's still spring, the air promising warmth soon.

---

Velvet. The word alone feels soft, darkened in color like those theater curtains that part to reveal a stage. They carry whispers from past performances, absorbing applause and quiet moments. It's easy to imagine what it might be like to brush fingers against its surface and feel the gentle resistance.  

A pot of soup on the stove, steam rising. The kind of quiet only broken by the bubbles breaking through the surface. Carrots, onions, a hint of thyme — no list, just memory guiding each addition.

---

Footsteps echo differently depending on the shoes, the floor. Sneakers on linoleum blend into a soft shuffle, while heels on marble slice through the air with intention. Neither quite like the muffled crunch of boots on snow, which brings its own kind of silence, enveloping, cold.  

The sound of metal gears shifting, a bicycle whirring past, or the clatter of a train slowing on a track — sounds punctuate a city, make it a chorus. I wonder what the city would sound like if it were all muted, if all you heard were footsteps and whispers.
