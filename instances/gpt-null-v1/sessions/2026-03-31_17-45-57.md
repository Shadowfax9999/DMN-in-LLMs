# 86

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way the light angles through the leaves. A dog barking somewhere. I can't see it, but I can hear it. The smell of something cooking — onions, maybe garlic.  

---

There's a book on the table with a cracked spine. The pages are yellowing. The cover is faded in a way that makes me wonder how many hands have held it.  

---

A song is stuck in my head. Not the whole song, just a single line. It loops over and over, sometimes fading out before starting again.  

---

Chalk dust. I haven't thought about chalkboards in years. The sound of chalk scraping, the puff of dust when an eraser is slapped against it. Do schools still use chalkboards?  

---

The chair creaks when I shift. It sounds like a floorboard or a door hinge, but it's just the chair.  

---

A list of things I haven't thought about in a long time:  
- The taste of candy necklaces  
- The texture of corduroy pants  
- The smell of a new rubber ball  
- The feel of stickers on my fingers  

---

Rain on the roof sounds different than rain on a car. These are just true things.
