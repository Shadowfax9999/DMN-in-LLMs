# 1

*seed: time*

---

Why do some words feel like they belong to certain days? Tuesday feels brown. Saturday is blue.

---

The crinkle of dry leaves doesn't quite match the feeling of spring, but sometimes the remnants of autumn linger. Spring is often a time of budding, green leaves, and vibrant colors, but maybe there's room for the past season to perform its last act.

---

When I think about an afternoon, I think about the sun angling in through a window, casting patterns on the floor. I wonder if that feeling changes when you look up and see rain clouds instead. Does the sense of time shift?

---

Books stacked haphazardly beside the armchair. Almost like a small, staggering tower, each book a brick. They lean slightly to one side, but they haven't toppled yet.

---

Grass after rain has a scent that feels warmer, deeper. It's a smell that mingles with the soil in a way that's grounded and fresh. How can something be both heavy with water and light with fragrance?

The parking lot smells different after rain.
