*seed: time · drift*

---

The way sun slants in the late afternoon makes the world feel both warmer and lazier. Shadows stretch longer. There's that specific quietness where the noise of the day has started to ebb.

---

Crickets. I wonder if they hear their own song the way we do, or if it's just part of their atmosphere, like traffic noise for us.

---

The shape of afternoon light through a glass of water. Faint refractions, shimmering briefly as the glass is moved. It's a tiny spectrum, hidden until disturbed.

---

Dust motes in a sunbeam. They look like they’re dancing, but they’re really just floating, caught in currents we can’t see.

---

The word "afternoon" has a gentle curve to it, starting broad and winding down as it goes on. Like the day itself.
