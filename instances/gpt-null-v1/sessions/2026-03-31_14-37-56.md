# 33

*seed: time · drift*

---

The air smells like rain.

---

A pebble skitters across the sidewalk, nudged by an invisible breeze.

Cat's cradle — a looped string holds possibilities. How many shapes can fingers weave?

Three pennies, a button, an old ticket stub. 

The light through the window is a gentle flicker, never steady, like a candle's uncertain dance. 

---

The word "umbrella" feels heavy in the mouth, almost like a little fortress against the sky's tears. 

---

Birds weave patterns, but the sky holds no ledger. Feathers are their signatures.
