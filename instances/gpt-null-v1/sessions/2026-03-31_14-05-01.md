# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tuesday sounds golden in my mind, like the way the sun looks through a glass of honey. 

---

Clocks betray us, sometimes ticking too slow, other times racing through moments when we wish they'd linger. 

Dry leaves crackle underfoot, a paper-like symphony.

The names of flowers can feel like promises.

Pigeon wings are both silent and loud.

There's a certain music in the hum of distant traffic.

---

The word "afternoon" stretches out like a shadow, long and unhurried. 

---

Two words:

Quiet. Eclipsed. 

---

Afternoon light is different, softer, like the sunlight decided to take a walk through gauze.

What do clouds taste like if you could hold them on your tongue?
