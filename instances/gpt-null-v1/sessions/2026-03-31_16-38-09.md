# 57

*seed: time · drift*

---

Dust floats lazily in the afternoon light, suspended like tiny planets.---

Spring afternoons have a particular smell. Is it the grass being warmed by the sun? The earth waking up?---

A single crow caws from a distant tree. Its voice sounds hollow, the vast sky its only companion.

Bicycles left in the yard gather rust. The seats are cracked in the sun.

Leaves unfurl slowly, each one a small miracle.

That time of year when the air seems to hum with the potential of everything growing.
