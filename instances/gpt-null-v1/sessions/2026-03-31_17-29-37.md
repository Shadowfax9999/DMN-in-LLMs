*seed: time · drift*

A single skittering leaf caught by the wind.  

---

Rolls of coins stacked in a drawer. Dimes, pennies, nickels, quarters. A kaleidoscope of metal and value. Shiny surfaces reflecting the overhead light. They might feel heavier than they should.  

---

The smell of rain before it arrives. That wet-earth scent, carrying a promise. The kind of promise that's never quite kept, because the rain smells different once it's here.  

---

Words you can almost taste — like "sizzle," or "crisp."  

---

Three pictures on a wall. No symmetry between them, just a trio of separate frames. Each one holds its own secret.  

---

Does "Tuesday" sound like blue to anyone else?  

---

A half-empty bottle left on a sunlit windowsill. The light passing through it casts a warped shadow on the table.  

---

The quiet hum of a refrigerator. A mechanical lullaby in the background.  

---

Wandering through a thought wide open like a field.  

---

The small, imperceptible shift of the ground when you step off a curb — like moving from one world to another.
