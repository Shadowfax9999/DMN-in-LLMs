# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

A spiderweb glistens in the corner of a windowpane. The intricate patterns, almost invisible, capture the light in a way that seems deliberate, yet happenstance.

Grass stains on jeans that never quite wash out. They carry with them the memory of play, of abandoned caution, of the earth itself clinging to fabric.

---

The word "gossamer" feels light in the mouth, like a whisper. It conjures wings of moths, the thinnest of veils.

Old photographs sometimes have a yellow hue. It's not so much the color itself but the sensation of time layering on paper. The past is not black and white; it's tinged with sepia.

---

Droplets on a windshield just before the wipers sweep them away. There's a moment of scattering, a brief dance of water.

Do elevators ever dream of the sky?
