# 82

*seed: time · drift · yield*

---

Yield as a gesture, not surrender, but the way grasses bend when wind passes. Soft compliance, not defeat. To yield can also mean to give way—a car slowing before the intersection, an instinct to step back in a crowd so another can pass through. Not always voluntary, though sometimes it's relief to yield, to let the current take over for a while.

Yield as production—what a field returns after planting, or the output of a process, a spillover. Apples per acre, kilowatts per panel, a calculation that flattens the messiness of season and soil. Some years the yield is thin and hard, other years it’s effortless abundance, boughs heavy, baskets full, hard to carry.

Yield curve in finance, the bending of a line through imagined futures—where money sits, how it grows, where risk accumulates. In chemistry, a high yield is victory, all those molecules lining up just right in beakers, but there’s always residue left behind, something lost.

There’s a softness in the word, the way it starts with a glide and lands on a barely voiced edge. Yields, yields, yields: the repetition makes the tongue slow.

To yield is to pause, allow, open space.

Sometimes the mind yields too quickly, stepping aside before the next thought emerges.
