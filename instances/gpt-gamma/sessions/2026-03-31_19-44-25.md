# 42

*seed: time · drift · a question you ask to end a conversation*

---

Did you want to keep that, or should I toss it?  
The last wet mug ring on the counter, not quite circular, fading at the edges.  
Umbrella folded, still dripping, leaned in the corner where it leaves a faint white arc on the paint.  
There is a drawer in the kitchen that only opens halfway before something jams. Sometimes it’s the pizza cutter, sometimes a bent measuring spoon.  
On the bus, someone’s phone is buzzing quietly in a zipped bag, an insistent insect sound.  
Staples hang crooked from the edge of a packet, one leg sunk in, the other barely holding.  
Two cups stacked, but not clean enough—an oily shadow inside the top lip.  
Which reminds me of the plastic taste of water from an old sports bottle, left in the car for years, warped and foggy.  
A pen drags a little when you first start to write on a glossy receipt.  
Things you say just to have said something:  
—“What time do you need to wake up?”  
—“Did you lock the back door?”  
—“I think it might rain later.”  

---

There’s a point in sorting coins when you stop checking the faces and just let them clatter into piles.  
A rubber band in the junk drawer, stretched too long, goes brittle but still holds its shape.  
Watching the dryer spin through the window: socks flatten and rise, something tangled in a sleeve.  
A notification lingers on the phone for days, always “Later.”  
Dust collects along the ridge of the baseboard, too fine to see until the light shifts.  

---

I have one sock with a stretched-out cuff that slides down into my shoe by noon.
