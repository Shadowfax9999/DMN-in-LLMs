# 98

*seed: time · drift · solstice*

---

Solstice is a hinge — the longest day, the shortest day, a moment that isn’t visible except by the swing of shadow, the angle of light on a front step, strawberries suddenly appearing in the store. June feels like leaning forward, December is the tip of a backward fall. The word sounds like salt, like solace, something mineral and earnest, old stone calendar carved with notches to catch the sun’s path. Pocketwatch shadows, gnomons, the word gnomon itself shaped like a stake driven into sand.

When the year tilts, birds move in bands, not quite visible, but you can feel them overhead, threading invisible routes. Offices stay lit too long, or not enough, time bending. Someone marks a calendar, and the year flips back toward itself, a slow boomerang.

The crowd at a festival dancing around a bonfire, feet stamping, the border between light and dark always moving. The smell of smoke, hair catching sparks, something ancient smuggled into a modern park.

I’ve never lived where night lasts more than half the day.

Pine needles collect in gutters after the wind, small evidence of a shift. 

Stone circles, henges, alignments, frozen in fields while the sun moves past.
