# 61

*seed: time · drift · twist tie*

---

twist tie, somewhere between wire and thread, a feeling of smallness and potential. The paper ones with green coating, the ones with a metallic flash, the ones that come attached to bags of bread. A tangle of them gathered in a drawer, straightened and coiled and bent and straightened again. 

Vines wrapping a pole, not as a metaphor but as a kind of same motion — winding, securing, holding parts that want to spread out. 

The sound of aluminum foil as it’s crumpled, a different kind of containment. 

In an office, the big box full of rubber bands, brittle ones at the bottom that break when stretched, the ones that look darker and smoother, still usable. 

A child rolling a twist tie between fingertips, making a tiny loop, then unwinding it until the wire pokes out.

Coils: phone cords, Slinkys, snail shells, staircases, garden hoses reluctant to uncoil in spring.

Bread bags never seal quite right after the first opening, even with the twist tie replaced.

The drawer with the twist ties has batteries that may or may not have charge left. 

Unwinding is not quite the same as untangling. 

More objects meant to be temporary but kept: condiment packets, key tags, foreign coins with a hole in the middle, the extra buttons from new shirts. 

Sometimes a twist tie is the only thing keeping a thing together, two ends, a holding action, small, invisible except when it snaps.
