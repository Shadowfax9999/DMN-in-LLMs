# 97

*seed: time · drift · a price tag left on a gift*

---

1. A price tag left on a gift, accidental or deliberate. Sometimes it's a sticker, sometimes the faint square of residue, sometimes a slip of paper hidden inside a box. The gesture of removing it is ritual: the slow scrape, the thumb pressed and rolled, the sticky edge, the faint smell of adhesive, the possibility of tearing the paper underneath.

2. The tag is an index of value, now exposed: what does it do to the gift, to the giver, to the one who receives? Does it anchor the meaning or erode it? Value is named, when gifts usually try to float free of price.

3. The moment of noticing. Should you point it out? Quietly peel it later? Pretend it was never there? The small choreography: eyes flick, hand hesitates, slight flush.

4. Is there relief, sometimes, in knowing what something costs?

5. The memory of childhood, opening boxes in sunlight, the slickness of birthday paper, the urge to peel every sticker from every surface.

6. Sometimes the tag is left on purpose, a declaration ("See how much I spent!") or a mistake, or a thoughtless handoff. 

7. The residue: the glue that doesn't come off, the rectangle of roughness, a mark that remembers what was supposed to be hidden.

8. Is the act of removing a price tag an act of care, or of erasure?

9. What does a price tag know?

10. Sometimes you keep the tag, folded inside a book or a pocket, its numbers no longer relevant. Some tags are tiny, some are extravagant, some are coded with shapes, colors, barcodes, black slashes.

11. How many price tags are in landfill, not even attached to objects anymore, but loose among apple cores and receipts? 

12. The sound of a tag being torn off—a brief, papery sigh.

13. The difference between a tag and a label: tags dangle, labels stick.

14. Returning a gift: the presence or absence of a tag can decide what’s possible.

15. Can a feeling be tagged with a price? Not metaphorically, but as a thought: anxiety, $7.95; anticipation, marked down; joy, out of stock.

16. Does the gift notice the tag as it changes hands? 

17. Some things come pre-labeled "priceless," or "not for sale," but even these have barcodes, somewhere.

18. Is the price a fact, or a moment?

19. The tag is a small rectangle of time, caught between the shelf and the ceremony.

20. If you leave all the tags on, does the world become a store again?
