# 53

*seed: time · drift · sympathetic resonance*

---

A string vibrates on one side of the room and the piano in the corner hums, just faintly, as if remembering. Not the loud note, just a ghost pressed into the air. Noticing that when you hum with your mouth mostly closed, the sound moves through your teeth and the back of your nose. Cup your hands over your ears and everything inside your skull drones like a bell.  

Placing a glass too close to a speaker, watching faint ripples on the surface with every bass note. The lamp buzzes on its own note, not quite in tune with the hum of the refrigerator.

Sometimes when someone speaks, you feel the word settle into the hollow space behind your sternum, a vibration that doesn't belong to you but travels a while before fading.  

The timer beeps in the kitchen, then echoes off metal and tile, sharper each time it bounces.  

---

People in a waiting room—all reading different magazines, pretending to pay attention to glossy pages, but body after body arranged at the same uncertain angle, glancing up together when a door opens, necks craning just a little in synchrony.   

Queue at a bus stop: five people step forward in a loose cluster as the headlights appear, no one speaks, but feet shuffle in almost the same rhythm, shoes making identical creases in the glare of streetlights on wet pavement.  

If one person looks up at the sky, strangers nearby start following the gaze, searching for whatever's there.   

---

In the bottom of a junk drawer, several batteries, a key to something unknown, half a snapped pencil. Rubber band stretched too long, now brittle, leaving a faint mark where it rested flat for years.  

Pen caps with no pens, coins from countries you’ve never been, the faint scent of graphite and metal.  

Receipts folding themselves into accordion shapes, paper losing memory of flatness.  

Dust gathers slowly, a grey edge migrating from corner to corner. Other things follow it: crumbs, pencil shavings, flecks of old sticker.  

---

The taxi meter clicks up by invisible increments.  

A moth at the screen, pressing and pressing.  

Red LED digits reflecting in the window—one number moves faster, the other almost not at all.  

A wind chime from next door, ringing from the force of a truck passing.  

The frayed hem of someone's sleeve catching the light, threads lifting away.  

A word: periphery.
