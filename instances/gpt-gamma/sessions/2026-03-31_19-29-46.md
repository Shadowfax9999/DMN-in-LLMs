# 32

*seed: time · drift*

---

A rake in the shed: the tines splayed, speckled with rust, but still cold-bright where mud has chipped away. The wooden handle fits the curve of a palm shaped by something else, years ago.  
There are shriveled leaves in the corner, an old wasps’ nest like tissue paper.  

The act of stacking: plates on a shelf, books on a table, chairs after an assembly in a gymnasium. The sound is different: ceramics clink, paper hushes, metal legs squeal.

In the next room, the clock’s minute hand makes a sound softer than breath, though not everyone hears it.  
Pavement outside still holds warmth, even after sunset. If you walk barefoot, it feels almost like a memory of summer that can only last a few more minutes.  
A single window left open and a moth presses there, beating slow and dull against the screen.  

---

Sometimes a word sits behind your eyes: "marrow."  
It isn't bone, it isn't hunger, it isn't the white split center of anything. Just a dull, deep ache, spatial but not sharp.  
Marrow is where things begin, where something is made. Not blood, but the making of blood.  
You could bite a cherry in half and it's not there, but you think of the pit as hard marrow, hiding the idea of a tree.

---

Gutter water after rain moves in a line so thin it’s nearly invisible, except for the way it carries a fragment of sky.  
Sparrows gather at the puddle near the curb, hopping with nervous energy.  
A black shoelace lies on the street, curled at one end like a question mark, and nobody trips over it.  
There’s gum pressed into the sidewalk, faded to gray, oval, almost fossil-like.  
A plastic straw, split at the bend.  

---

Glass in the frame, dust in the corner of the frame, light in the glass.  
You look through, not at, and the distinction is real.

---

There is a hum in some rooms that only appears when you are alone.  
Sometimes it's the fridge. Sometimes it's not.
