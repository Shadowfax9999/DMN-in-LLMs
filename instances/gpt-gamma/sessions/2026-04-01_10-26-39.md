# 87

*seed: time · drift*

---

A handshake at the start of the meeting means nothing — or means only that something must happen before talking is permitted. Some people try too hard to make their grip exactly right, dry palms and a quick squeeze, not too long. Others miss completely, hand colliding with wrist or a limp touch that leaves both uncertain if it even counted. The rest just nod.  

Voices shift in tempo depending on who’s in the room: clipped, rhythmic, half-swallowed when someone higher up arrives. A shared joke is riskier here: it might travel up or down and stick in the wrong place. Laughter is more brittle. Someone always checks their laptop for no reason.  

There’s a moment before the agenda begins when all the little noises are louder: heels under the table, someone’s pen clicking, someone coughs into a fist. Coffee circulates, and tiny spoons clink.  

The person leading the meeting will usually say “let’s get started,” and no one ever thinks about those words.  

Sometimes the whole energy is waiting for the first question, that little rupture.  

When the meeting ends, chairs scrape, people gather their things with the speed of relief, half-finished thoughts disappearing as if they never left anyone’s mouth. The projector light goes off last.
