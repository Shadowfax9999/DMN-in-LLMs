# 80

*seed: time · drift · the sound of ice cracking in a glass*

---

Ice cracking in a glass — vertical, sudden, it radiates out. The tiny echoes inside the cup, the feeling of a brief, sharp split and then a slow fizzing underneath. Not a shattering — a release. It draws attention for a second, makes voices pause, then resumes.  

The word "shard" — hard and bright. But "splinter" feels finer, more hidden, something you might miss until you brush it.  

A single splinter in the pad of a finger, invisible except for the twinge when pressure is just right. Tweezers, needle, time. Sometimes it works up to the surface in days.  

Surface tension — water holding above the rim before spilling. The way you can tilt a glass and watch the meniscus slide, all the molecules sticking together one instant too long.  

There's a point where enough ice cubes make the liquid rise higher than you'd think, then you sip and the cubes clink, shifting, rearranging their small world.  

Condensation gathers, beads, traces small tracks down the outside, leaving rings that soak into coasters or disappear into wood.  

The word "coaster" feels engineered, circular, as if it must travel, but sits still.  

Some tables are already marked — old circles, impossible to erase.  

The glass leaves a mark even after it's gone.  

Salt gathers on the rim if it's a particular kind of drink. Sometimes you taste the salt before the drink itself.  

A single drop runs down your knuckle, cold, outpacing the rest.  

The napkin sticks, then peels away with a sound that's quiet but decisive.  

Someone else sips from the same straw and the taste changes, not by flavor but by expectation.  

There’s a moment before swallowing when the cold passes all the way down.  

The cubes melt, the drink weakens, someone asks if you want another.  

The word "another" is heavier late at night.  

Bar glasses are often thicker at the base. The weight feels deliberate in your hand.  

The ice is almost gone. The last sip is mostly water, a memory of sweetness diluted, a faint echo of the first taste.  

The glass is empty.  

The coaster is damp.  

The check arrives.
