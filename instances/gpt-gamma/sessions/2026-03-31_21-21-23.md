# 84

*seed: time · drift · crumb (bread)*

---

Crumb. The smallest evidence of something being bitten, broken, left behind. In pockets, in beds, at the bottoms of dark bags. Crumbs lodge in the corners of mouth and memory. Not dust but kin to it, heavier, shaped by a knife or a jaw. There are crumbs too fine for fingers to grasp, obliterated by cloth, gathered in seams. A single crumb on the lip of a cup looks deliberate, as if it could mark a story. Whole worlds in the trail of a single slice, each crumb a possibility for ants, for birds, for small hungers.

Mouthfeel: bread crumb versus cake crumb, the difference only obvious when wet. Some people pick crumbs from others without asking, a gesture of care or annoyance or both. The tablecloth shakes out last night’s conversation onto the grass. Not all crumbs are food.

Wedding cakes tend to hide their crumbs, layered with icing, bound up, as if ceremony cannot tolerate the reminder of something crumbling. Children are watched as they eat, adults unobserved, but the same evidence is left behind.

There is a special brush at some restaurants for sweeping the table between courses. The brush moves with quick, practiced arcs, making space for something new. Crumbs are currency and confession, the record of absence and touch.

Mouse droppings mistaken for poppy seeds, or the other way around. The perimeter of a keyboard: crumb museum, dust archive, intake of quiet habits. Some crumbs grow soft, others petrify, waiting for an absent-minded tongue to find them hours later.

The word itself, crumb, round and blunt with a sudden stop, not quite the end of something, more a piece that almost remembers its shape.
