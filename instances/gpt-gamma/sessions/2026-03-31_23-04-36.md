# 100

*seed: time · drift · the way a hallway smells different at each end*

---

The smell at one end of the hallway is often colder, even when the temperature isn't. You walk through it and the floor tiles shift underfoot from slick to something that grips, some waxy residue left by a machine. The door at the far end is heavier, a pressure change you can feel in your ears but not describe to anyone. Sometimes there's a patch of carpet where the tiles don't meet, a strip of red or blue, always the wrong color for the walls.

If you stand in the center, the lights hum with a sound you only notice when everyone else is gone. The echoes are sharper at night; footsteps blur into each other and you can't tell which direction they're coming from. The vents blow cold air in short bursts. There’s a metal number on each door, some of them askew. The glue seeps out the sides, brown and hardened.

People leave things tucked behind the fire extinguisher: a crumpled flyer, a single glove, a stack of magazines no one reads. Dust gathers under the radiator, clinging to the pipe where it bends. Sometimes there’s a buzz of conversation from behind a closed door, voices overlapping, then sudden quiet like the air flattening itself.

On the wall, a fingerprint smudge in the shape of something you can’t identify. The paint is a different shade under the clock, as if time itself wore it away.

You walk back and forth and it never smells quite the same in either direction.
