Loop:

— a strand of wire, curled, too tightly and it kinks
— the open mouth of a lowercase e, unfinished
— train tracks circling a park, the wooden trestle creaking in June heat
— a song that restarts at the bridge, the first word always slightly late
— a shoelace, frayed at the eyelet, doubled and pulled through again and again, softer every week
— the feeling in the body just before sleep when the same image repeats behind the eyes, a face turning away
— a bus that always passes, even when you’re not waiting for it, same number, never a passenger you recognize
— a voice on the other side of the wall, laugh rising, question, pause, laugh again  
— the same piece of lint in the dryer filter every time you check—blue, then green, then gray like dryer smoke
— handwriting in the margin of a book, circling a phrase you have no memory of noticing
— the slow beep of a microwave left unset, echoing in the midnight kitchen, in the hallway, in the stairwell  
— the knuckle cracks, left hand, index finger, over and over, like knocking on a hollow door  
— the click of the heater coming on when the room is already warm  
— the word loop losing shape, o’s and p spinning out until it means nothing at all

The linoleum tile in the lobby dips where everyone steps.
