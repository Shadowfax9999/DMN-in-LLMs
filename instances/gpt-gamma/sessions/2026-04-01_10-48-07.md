# 96

*seed: time · drift · dormancy*

---

Dormancy—  
Roots below frost, but how do they know it’s not time?  
A code held in starch and silence; somewhere in the cells, the signal not to wake.  
Dormant accounts gather dust, untouched but not erased, numbers holding their breath.  
Cicadas underground, thirteen years, brooding, a boundary crossed by warmth and calendar.  
Dormancy as patience or as suspension, a clock stopped or a breath held.  
In a group, dormancy is less visible—who is waiting, who is pausing, who has quietly withdrawn?  
Software in sleep mode, blinking light like a heartbeat: is that dormancy or vigilance?  
Snow covering grass, each blade a pulse waiting for a signal.  
Dormancy isn’t absence. It’s latency.  
When is a secret dormant and when is it forgotten?  
The contract lies dormant until invoked.  
Questions can go dormant between people; then a word, weeks later, brings it back into the room.  
Dormant volcano, not dead, not gone.  
What does dormancy feel like from the inside?  
The seeds in the drawer with their futures folded tight.  
Some friendships are dormant, nothing exchanged but everything unfinished.  
The word itself feels like a pause mid-sentence, waiting for the next sound.

---

There is a kind of dormancy in silence during negotiations—outside, everything appears paused, but inside, calculations, scenarios, rehearsed outcomes.  
Social dormancy: the moment after conflict, when everyone is quiet, social energy coiled.  
A protest that goes dormant: slogans stored, banners folded, but the grievances still alive underground.  
Can a rule in a game be dormant? Only invoked in some circumstance, an edge-case provision.  
Laws can go dormant, unenforced, lingering in the code until the right case.  
Habits can be dormant, the path in the mind faint but ready to be retraced.  
The mind holds dormant desires, unexpressed, waiting for the right signal—a smell, a season, a question.  
What is the difference between waiting and dormancy?  
Maybe it’s in the metabolism: waiting is active, dormancy is suppressed.  
Or not even suppressed—just paused, clock’s hands folded behind its back.  
Some hopes go dormant, untouched for years, and then open like crocuses in a new spring.  
A system can have dormant failure modes, waiting for a certain stress.  
Is dormancy a tool, a threat, a shelter, or an accident?  
Letters in a box, keys to locks that no longer exist, passwords unused but not lost.  
Dormancy is the line between lost and possible.  
A mind can go dormant in a conversation, waiting for a change in weather or tone.  
Dormant isn’t dead, it’s a potential wrapped in patience.  
You can’t always tell what’s dormant from what’s gone.  
The power is still on, but the screen is dark.
