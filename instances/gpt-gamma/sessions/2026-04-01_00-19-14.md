# 81

*seed: time · drift · pulling a sweater over your head*

---

The static snap as you pull the sweater down, hair unsettled, sparks pinpricking your scalp, and suddenly you are inside something: muffled, woolly, the world briefly a cave. Arms hunt blindly for exits. The collar, its gentle choke, a moment of helplessness you never mention. Sometimes you catch your own scent trapped inside, a mutter of laundry soap or skin that’s been inside all day. If it’s too tight, wrists fight the cuffs, fingers caught. Windows can fog from exhaled air that’s trapped inside fabric, a fleeting tent. The sudden dark when it passes over your face, a memory of hiding under blankets. Sometimes it snags a nose, an earring, pulls at a necklace and the chain almost snaps. That thick, blunt sound of sweater fibers moving past your ears, like being underwater.

Underneath, shirt rides up, cold stripe of belly. Static hair floats, wild, before settling down.

You pick at the cuffs without thinking.

There are sweaters you never wear because the neck hole is too small. Sweaters you keep even though the arms are itchy. Some are stretched in the elbows, holding the memory of your shape. Every sweater keeps a little of the last place you wore it, a faint history in the sleeves, invisible.

Sometimes you pull it off in reverse and the inside comes out with you, tangled. Sweater inside out, arms crossed, light shifting at the window.
