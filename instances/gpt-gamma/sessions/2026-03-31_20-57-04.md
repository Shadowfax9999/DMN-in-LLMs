# 75

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tablecloths never quite clean, the faint ring where a vase once stood. Chairs stacked in the corner, legs tangled together, dust scurrying backwards on the linoleum when the air moves. The elevator doors close with a soft slap, no stops between floors. Pocketful of change, lint clinging to a single nickel. A directory sign one floor too low, arrow pointing nowhere, half-peeling from its post. 

The sharpness of air after a copier’s finished its cycle. Corridors that hum at low frequencies, barely audible unless you press your forehead to the glass. Markers drying out in their capped sleep, ink pooling in the fibers. Stale orange in a bowl—dimpled, heavy, unfurling the smell of rind when squeezed.

A clock facing the wrong way, visible only in the mirror above the sink. Someone left a file folder on the chair across from reception; the label is smudged, and no one is coming back for it. The ceiling tiles are not level. Light falls unevenly, grim arcs where bulbs have flickered out, pools of dimness between sharp squares. 

Sometimes you can hear someone typing when the room is empty.
