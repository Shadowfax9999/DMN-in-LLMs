# 55

*seed: time · drift · fathom*

---

Fathom: measurement, depth, touch—reaching with both arms, as if to hold what is otherwise out of reach.  
Fathom is also a grasp, a gesture, a span, a human-scale unit written into the body.  
How far down is the bottom? How much can you reach before the water darkens.  
A fathom of rope, coiled on a dock, always a little wet, soft with salt.

---

Salt lines drying on skin, leaving a map of vanished water.  
Not quite sweat, not quite sea.  
Sometimes after swimming, the world blinks brighter for a few minutes, the surface of things outlined, a thin film of vision.  
Head underwater, hearing blood and pressure, then the sudden open air again.

---

Depth is not only ocean — it’s a drop in pitch, a lowering of voice, a heaviness in the evening.  
When people say "I can't fathom it," they're talking about a mind’s inability to reach, or an idea that keeps moving further away as you try to measure.

---

Moths at glass: sometimes two, sometimes more, all pressing, leaving dust, the thrum and tap.  
Insects drawn to light they can't enter, fading faster than they expect.  
If you look up close, moth wings are fur, not scales.  
Fur that powders onto fingertips if you dare to touch.

---

Trade winds, trade routes, trade-offs—everything circulating, leaving signs behind, only sometimes crossing paths.  
How many people, in one night, pass through the same intersection, each thinking they’re alone in the pause.

---

The red digits in the taxi never stop counting.  
Everything moves, even when you’re waiting for the light.  
The antenna on the roof leans a little to the left.
