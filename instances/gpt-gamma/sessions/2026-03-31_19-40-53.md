# 40

*seed: time · drift · a receipt that faded to blank*

---

Receipt without numbers, paper absent memory, but the weight of it is still in your pocket. Thermal paper, the kind that turns black if you press it with a fingernail. Sometimes the printing is so faint it’s like the record never existed — items, totals, lost. That prickle of wondering whether you’ll need to prove the purchase, but even if you had the slip, it says nothing now.

A couch cushion split at the seam. Orange foam peeking through. The inside is always brighter than the outside. Dust gathers in the crease.

Remembering how receipt rolls in cash registers are changed — the feel of the new spool, tight and hopeful, compared to the slack, curling tail of the old. The edge fed carefully under the black plastic guide, sometimes tearing jagged, the first inch wasted and crimped.

Pockets lined with lint and a folded bit of paper you can’t throw away. Not useful, just a fragment. Someone else’s handwriting, not yours. You keep it anyway.

---

Standing in line at the bank, everyone holding something: envelope, phone, keys, check already signed. The hush is not quiet, it's pressurized. Carpet that smells like shoes, windowless light.

Molded plastic pen chained to the counter, the ball stops working halfway through a signature. Strange how the chain is never quite long enough.

The glass between you and the teller: reflection layered with what’s inside. You see your face, overlaid with the greenish tint of the glass, and her hands sorting bills.

---

Listening for a sound the fridge makes when it cycles, the click and gentle hum, except now it seems silent. Waiting for the sound so you can know things are normal.

There are receipts you keep because you think you might return something, but you never do.
