# 77

*seed: time · drift · the specific rattle of a spray paint can*

---

Sh-sh-sh-sh-shake. The inside marble clicking, a short orbit, shifting the weight—heavy to light, dense to hollow, depending on the angle. The can is cold and always heavier than you expect, even after the hiss has started. Night projects: lines curling around corners, wet on brick, a silver scrawl that looks flat until the light hits. Is there a word for the feeling of knowing you're making a mark that won't be seen until morning? Some colors only show up under sodium streetlamps; neon orange turns ghostly, green refuses to glow. Hands ache from holding the nozzle halfway, balancing pressure to avoid the dribble, trying not to arch too sharply. Breathe in, taste the edge of chemical, sweet-sharp, metallic. Painted over in layers, a fossil record of tags and failed announcements. Sometimes one letter is all that remains visible: dark blue looping into nothing, a G or a P or the start of a name.

The rattle is a warning or a promise. Above the painted wall, windows don’t open. Curtains pulled. The click of a skateboard wheel over broken pavement. Chalk dust left on the curb, another kind of message. 

It’s easier to write on glass than on concrete, but the line never feels permanent. Everything written at this hour feels less true by daylight. 

Empty cans lined up under the bridge, no two exactly the same, caps scattered, a spectrum of almosts.

The metal marble stays inside, even when the can is empty.
