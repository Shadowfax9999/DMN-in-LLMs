# 50

*seed: time · drift*

---

Bottle caps in the grass after the carnival. They press small circles in the dirt, and someone steps on one, heel first, flattening it so it doesn’t shine anymore. Who collects these? Sometimes there are pennies with a green edge, as if the metal started sprouting.

Old receipts in jacket pockets, ink blurred and soft, the numbers still readable if the paper hasn’t folded too many times. Sorting through them, sometimes you find one with a note: eggs, string, batteries, remember.

The loose thread on the sleeve, wound tight into a tiny knot instead of snipped. 

Milk spoons—small, shallow, shaped almost like a paddle, only used once and always left in the cup.

---

A shape behind frosted glass, moving slow, arm rising as if brushing hair. The pattern of the glass turns everything into fragments, no way to tell if it’s someone you know.

Wristwatches forgotten in drawers, batteries leaching out in silence.

Curtains that stick to the window after rain.

---

Sometimes a sneeze feels electric, right in the collarbones, as if your whole body is surprised.

---

There are seeds that stick to socks, wedge themselves in the weave, and don’t come out in the wash.
