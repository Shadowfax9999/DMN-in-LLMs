# 39

*seed: time · drift · spokeshave*

---

spokeshave—a tool for shaping, drawing away thin curls of wood, smaller than a plane, less bossy, the word itself poking up, nothing it points to in the room right now, but somewhere there is a workbench, red handle glossy from grip, blade tucked under the metal lip.

Sometimes the mind throws out a word like a life preserver. You’re not in water, but there it is, spinning.

Wood shavings coil up, spiraled and light, barely anything, you can crush them flat in a fist. The sound of a blade drawing across grain, a dry sound, not quite a scrape.

—

“Spoke” in “spokeshave” has nothing to do with speech, but the sound stutters against itself: spokeshave, shaped, shaven, shavings. Language curls off too, thin and spiraled, left behind after the shaping work.

Sometimes a word means nothing in the moment. Just sits above the mind, casting a little shadow.

—

Plastic garden chairs, always a little soft in the middle. The legs splay out over time. You can press your thumb into the seat and it pops back slowly, not entirely convinced.

Garden hose threading onto a spigot. Rubber gasket missing. Water leaks from the joint, running down the concrete and leaving a tiny trough of algae.

A dog barking three houses away, brief, exactly once.

The spokeshave is not for the spokes of a wheel, not anymore.

—

There’s a badge inside the lining of some jackets, a square of patterned thread, never noticed except when a finger snags it. The stitching makes a faint raised grid.
