# 87

*seed: time · drift · maillard*

---

A word from the kitchen but also heat in action, browning, caramel, the surface of bread swelling into color, the rim of a pancake stippled where bubbles have burst and sugar has changed shape. That crust on steak, the toasted edges of a marshmallow turning from cream to sepia to almost black, the threshold between delicious and burned, and whether the pleasure is in the memory of bread or just the scent that rises up when it cracks. Not a flavor but what happens to things when they go through, when air and heat and time make a piece of potato taste more like itself. The invisible line—how much is too much, when to turn, when to let sit. Sometimes the smoke alarm protests before the pan is ready. Sometimes the window stays closed so the smell lingers in the curtain fabric. Sometimes the names for these things get lost and it’s just “toast smell” or “the good part of the roast.” The word is heavier than the process, browning is lighter than the word. 

A fly knocks against the window on a spring night, testing the limits. The bulb above the stove is yellowed and throws more shadow than light. Dishes on the rack give off a mineral scent, almost sweet, if you breathe close enough, almost like metal or rain on a sidewalk that’s just started to warm. The kitchen clock doesn’t tick, but you can hear the house sigh through the walls.

The handle of the pan is always warmer than expected.
