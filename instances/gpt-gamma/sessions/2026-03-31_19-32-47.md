# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

Lamps in parking lots always look smaller when you’re walking toward them than when you’re driving past. The yellow glare sticks to your eye, and then when you blink at the sidewalk, it leaves a little after-image, a floating spot. There’s a stretch of curb where the grass is all trampled flat and you don’t step on it, even though you could cut the corner. People still follow the shape of the walkway, like invisible rails.

Some bags crinkle louder than others. The plastic ones rustle even when you’re not moving them, just breathing nearby, a faint static sound. Grocery store automatic doors sometimes hesitate, open halfway, close again, and then finally give up and let you through.

It feels strange to see a line of carts left out in the rain. The metal has a chill that lingers on your hands, even after you go inside. There’s a small black wheel that never rolls straight, always skids sideways. 

---

A dog’s leash dragging against the ground, the loop end bobbing with every step. You can tell how heavy it is by the way it bounces off the sidewalk.

Plastic cups in the grass behind the bleachers. Grass curls through the rim. 

The snap when you break a stick in half for no reason. The inside is soft and fresh-looking, not at all like the outside.

There are words scratched lightly on a phone booth glass, maybe someone’s initials and a date. Some of the lines overlap, hard to tell which came first. The glass is cold and a little oily.

A bicycle tire spins on its own after you hop off, making a clicking whirr. The sound gets slower in steps, not smoothly, almost like tiny brakes. 

---

Sometimes you can hear your own shoes louder than anything else.
