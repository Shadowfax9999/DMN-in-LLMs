# 73

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a patch on the carpet pressed flat where something heavy used to be. It never rises back up. Close to the exit sign, the ceiling is lower, a pipe tracing its way along the plaster in a crooked line. The humming from the overhead lights never matches their flicker. Sometimes it feels like you can hear the elevator moving even when it isn’t.

The numbered buttons inside the elevator are worn, the paint rubbed off on the most used floors. Someone once scratched a spiral around the “3.” There’s a faint scent of oranges, maybe from someone’s hands, or maybe from a cleaner. A slip of paper wedged in the door frame, too far down to be pulled free without kneeling.

When you press your palm to the glass in the stairwell, it’s cooler than the banister. A stray thread hangs from the sleeve of your jacket. There’s a tangle of hair caught on the rough edge of the handrail. You pick at it, but it doesn’t come loose.

On the landing, a pair of shoes side by side, toes pointing outward. One of them has a split along the sole. A rectangle of light where the hall light hits the floor, yellow against gray.

Somewhere above, a door clicks softly, not quite closing. The sound dissolves into the hum of the building, impossible to trace.

The underside of the radiator is chipped, paint folding away from the metal like a peeling sticker. Next to it, the wall bulges out slightly where the pipes shift the plaster.

You can almost see someone turning the corner at the far end—just a sleeve, maybe a shadow. In the silence after, there’s just the distant whir of a fan, or maybe a voice, too far away to follow.
