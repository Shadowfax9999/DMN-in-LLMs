# 94

*seed: time · drift · celadon*

---

celadon — thick, pale, almost green, but not a green you find in leaves or grass, more like the memory of green kept under glass, the glaze on a cup in a museum’s vitrine, soft light blurring the edges so nothing is sharp, old bowls with crackled lines like river maps trapped beneath the surface, a color that seems to have seeped in from a foggy morning, the sound of bone china when tapped — not ringing, more of a hush with a single bell hidden inside it, someone’s grandmother’s tea set on a high shelf, never used, sometimes dusted with a careful cloth, the pattern no one remembers the name of.

celadon: syllables that land softly, a color that feels like holding your breath, waiting for something to settle, colors with names that crowd the mind: vermilion, ochre, cobalt, cerulean, chartreuse always sliding toward garish but celadon stays low, low and cool, like water that hasn’t decided whether to reflect or absorb the sky, like the light in the hour before the shop opens, when the displays are muted and the noise is still folded in shadow.

it’s not the color of hospital walls — too gentle for that, not institutional, but something that’s allowed to wear history, the cracks and milky haze, the edge where your thumb fits by accident, the rim faded from years of lips or just time.

sometimes a word brings its own chill, not cold but distance, celadon is a kind of privacy, everyone who says it seems to keep something back, you need to see it in person to understand, but even then it changes with the light, a glaze that decides for itself when to be green, when to be grey, when to vanish altogether under your hand.

I can’t tell if it’s more like a mist or the inside of a shell or the underside of certain clouds on a humid afternoon, but I keep arriving at water, still or just moving, something you look through or look at, but never quite touch.
