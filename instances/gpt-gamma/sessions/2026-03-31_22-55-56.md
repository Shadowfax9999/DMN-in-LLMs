# 95

*seed: time · drift · the particular bounce of a flat tire*

---

That thudding, soft but insistent, a rhythm out of phase with the street’s own pattern — you feel it more than you hear it, each revolution a little less hopeful, everything shifting leftward, a drag that rewrites the path even as you’re steering straight. The difference between air that’s captive and air that’s gone. The rim only ever meant to be a suggestion, now suddenly in contact with the world, exposed. All the while the rest of the car oblivious, the mirrors clear, the radio steady, only the steering wheel telling on itself, heavy in the hands.

Sometimes a system reveals itself only in failure — the way you notice the hum of the fridge when it stops, the weight of a coat when you take it off, the presence of birds when they’re missing. Pothole, crosswalk, a slant in the asphalt, none of it matters until the edge touches bare metal.

Bounce is the wrong word, really. It’s not lively. It’s fatigue, repetition, the opposite of spring. But even that droop is a kind of motion. It keeps going because it has to, because weight insists, returning always to the lowest point.

Softer in the rain, harsher on gravel, almost musical on an empty road at night. Some things conduct sound differently when they’re deflated.

Next thought: the pause before a group laughs, when you feel everyone inhale at once, the moment after a punchline but before the room picks up the signal to respond. Air fills in strange ways. Someone coughs. The laugh comes late, never quite synchronized, a little like the wrong rhythm of a wheel losing its shape.

The word "tire" looks wrong when you stare at it long enough. The e gets lost. Tyre, tier, tie, tear. Flattened, stretched, meant to bear weight and collapse at the same time.

I don’t know what happens to all the lost air.
