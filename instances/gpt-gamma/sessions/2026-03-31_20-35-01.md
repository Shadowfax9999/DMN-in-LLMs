# 64

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sometimes a cough, dry and sudden, punctures the artificial quiet. The magazines on the rack all have torn corners, old dates, pages missing. Patterned carpet, low-pile, the kind that leaves diagonal impressions from shoe soles and chair legs. On the TV above the door, silent captions scroll behind flickering news anchors. Someone shifts, checks the clock on the wall, then looks at their own wrist as if the time might be different. The security mirror in the corner makes everyone’s heads into strange, stretched ovals. There’s always a water cooler, but the cone cups are gone, or they’re hidden, or you don’t want to be the first to stand.

The vents blow warm and cold at intervals, hard to predict, just enough to lift a paper napkin or ruffle a sleeve. The window glass is thicker than it looks. Someone squeezes a packet of hand sanitizer until it clicks twice, but nothing comes out. The child with the untied shoe starts to spin very slowly in a circle without letting go of the adult’s arm. The overhead lights hum, not loud but constant, like a note held so long you stop hearing it.

Paperwork goes into the slot facing down. Names are called, sometimes by first name only, sometimes last name and initial. The door next to the intake desk is always closing just as you’re almost close enough to see what’s beyond it. The keys on the receptionist’s lanyard catch the light every time she stands up.
