# 33

*seed: time · drift · on-ramp*

---

On-ramp, the word, looping upward, not a road but a possibility. Merging, yielding, accelerating, the stretch of pavement narrower than it looks from a distance, and then the sudden vibration where the grooves start, marking lanes that were invisible until you crossed them. The painted arrows always faded. The little green sign with the number.

Some on-ramps are so long you forget you’re merging at all, and others pitch you straight out into traffic with no room to think. There’s a strip of grass between the road and the ramp, sometimes full of trash—crumpled receipts, a single black glove, oil-darkened dirt.

The way cars pause, blinkers ticking, then leap, the decision not entirely yours. Radio static as you pass under a low overpass, the sound stuttering for a second.

A metal guardrail, dented. The taste of pennies if you’re nervous. Gas smell, rubber smell, hot morning asphalt, just before you reach speed.

---

Shadows on the dashboard look different when you’re moving east in the evening. A pencil rolls just enough to be noticed at red lights.

Tea cools faster in a travel mug when the lid is off, but nobody does that while driving.

The seatbelt edge is always stiff at first, rough against your neck, until the car warms up and you settle in and stop noticing. 

---

The light inside the glove compartment is yellow. The manual is always thicker than you expect.
