# 88

*seed: time · drift · detour*

---

Detour, the sense of drift, of a forced sideways, a route marked by orange barrels or by instinct, the way a mind can be nudged out of the familiar path and set to wandering an unfamiliar neighborhood, side streets with names you don’t recognize, houses with different porches, lawns at slightly different angles, someone’s lilacs blooming weeks ahead or behind.

The feeling of arriving, not lost but not quite oriented, pausing at the corner to look for the street sign, green against the dusk, letters half-worn. The small thrill of not knowing—will this way bring me out where I think? Does the road curve back toward the main? The sky at this hour is more pearl than blue and there’s a dog barking behind a fence you can’t see.

The routes that only open when something is blocked: roadwork, parade, accident, or just the bone-deep feeling that today is not the day for the usual. Some streets are narrow enough that you still brush leaves with the mirror. Others open suddenly into a view of water, or a parking lot, or a field stitched with dandelions.

Some people take detours as corrections, a necessary inconvenience. Others treat them as invitations, excuses to look too long at a house with painted shutters or to wonder what’s kept behind a row of locked mailboxes. 

Not every detour ends where you expect. Sometimes you loop back and trace the original line in reverse. Sometimes you find a shortcut by accident and never use the old way again. Some days, the sign that says “Detour” is left standing long after the road is open, and nobody takes it down.
