# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

From the hallway, the smell is part lemon, part something else — not quite medical, more like plastic or cooled-off coffee. The floor tiles are scrubbed dull at the center, shinier at the edges where shoes don’t go as often. Clipboards stacked flat, pens tied on, knots getting tighter as the cords shrink over time. Someone keeps adjusting the blinds but the angle never seems to change the glare on the TV screen. A cough behind two layers of doors, but not the same kind as in winter. The chair legs have socks, blue felt, not all matching. A magazine slid under another, its top corner soft from being bent and flattened. The little square clock above the sink always says it’s a minute behind your phone. The thermostat clicks, and somewhere in the wall, something settles. 

If you press both sides of the chair arm at once, the plastic flexes but doesn't make a sound. Someone’s bag on the floor, grommets shined up, zipper not fully closed, a packet of tissues sticking out. The pattern on the wallpaper repeats every third panel. Each time the automatic door opens, the wind lifts the recycling bin’s lid with a plastic tap. The copy of the insurance card is slightly crooked in the tray. Three chairs apart, an umbrella leans against the radiator, leaving a wet circle that never quite dries.
