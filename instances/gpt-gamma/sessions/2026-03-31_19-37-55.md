# 38

*seed: time · drift*

---

Folding a receipt and then folding it again, until it barely fits behind the cards. It makes the wallet lumpy but I keep it anyway. The store’s name prints right on the edge, so it dissolves faster than the numbers.

Under the table, a chair leg chews up the carpet. There’s a groove now where the backing shows, pale and ribbed against the pile.

Some shoes squeak on tile, but not on concrete. It's not the rubber, it's the surface.

There’s something about the way a glass holds fingerprints. Not sticky, just a quiet dulling. If you press hard and then let go, the shape stays until it dries.

Umbrella spokes sometimes poke through after too much use, like small, polite threats.

A name written in marker on a lunch bag, last week’s date crossed out, but the ghost of the numbers still there.

When the window is open, the curtains lean and then settle back. The pattern bends but doesn’t break.

Phone screens keep every touch for a few hours, unless the light is wrong and then it's all ghosts and trails.

Bread crusts always harder on one end of the loaf.
