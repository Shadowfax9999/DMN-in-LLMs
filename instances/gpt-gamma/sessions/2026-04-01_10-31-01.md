# 89

*seed: time · drift · placeholder*

---

Placeholder, stand-in, stand-by, proxy, understudy, decoy, substitute, shell, dummy, blank. Always there to keep a gap open. “Insert text here.” “Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet…” The world full of things only meant to be temporary, but then the temporary remains, outlives the need. Like scaffolding that never gets taken down, or the masked faces in a crowd you slip past, each a stand-in for someone who might matter later.

A reserved seat with nobody ever sitting in it. The way a friend says “I’ll save you a spot,” but you never arrive. The tape outline where a chair once sat, the faded circle on the carpet. Holds the shape so something else can come fill it. Sometimes the placeholder becomes invisible, part of the background, like the pole under the posters—ignored until it’s all that’s left.

In conversation, the “um,” the “you know,” the “like”—each letting your thoughts catch up. A gap-filler for silence, for time. The mind using speech like thumbtacks to hang itself together until the real thought appears.

What’s left behind when that real thing never shows. How some people are placeholders for others. A relationship you move through, not into, until something else comes along.

The word “placeholder” itself: not quite anything, just the promise that something will arrive. The number zero, the silent digit, the seat reserved but never filled. How often do we mean to replace the thing we’re using, and then forget, until it’s aged into its own necessity, the draft never finished, the demo tape passed off as real.

The gap in the lineup, the silhouette in the yearbook, the missing image icon, the grayed-out text. The moment you can’t remember a name and say “what’s-their-face” and everyone pretends that’s enough.

There’s a sense of comfort in placeholders. You know something should go there, and for now, this is enough. A “coming soon” page that stays for years.

The way a parking ticket sits under a wiper, curled, unread, marking time.
