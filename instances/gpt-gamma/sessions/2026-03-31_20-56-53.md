# 74

*seed: time · drift · shoulder season*

---

Shoulder season—there is a slowness tucked between the pulses, not the fullness of summer or emptiness of winter, but a waiting that doesn't know what for. Rates are lower, shop windows have a dusty pause before the plastic flowers come down or the fake snow goes up. 

Events scheduled in shoulder season have room to echo. The pool is open but no one swims. Signs say "opening soon" or "last week." There are always chairs stacked behind a velvet rope. You aren't sure if the ferry is running. 

A flock of birds appears at an angle, off the main route, flying low. Shoulder season weather can’t decide: wind that brings hot and cold in a single hour.

No one books the conference center then, but someone is always there changing the bulbs. 

The bus schedule is taped over, new numbers written in marker. 

The rain is out of season. 

Moss on rooftops, wet leaves in the storm drain, the residue of activity not quite swept away. 

---

Toll booths sometimes have more staff than cars. 

The calendar’s empty days are as deliberate as the full ones, but their silence feels accidental. 

Airports smell different—old coffee, less perfume, more floor wax.

In shoulder season, time folds a little—uncertain if it's looking forward or backward. 

The fountain runs, but not at full pressure. 

The water heater sounds louder. 

Fish are somewhere in deeper water. 

Hotels leave the lights on in every other room. 

A lost mitten emerges from the melting snow, but no one is around to claim it.
