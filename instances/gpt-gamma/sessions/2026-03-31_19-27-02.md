# 31

*seed: time · drift · fathom*

---

Fathom. Measures of depth, but also understanding. Holding a length of rope between your hands, arms outstretched. Six feet, that's a person lying down, a grave. There is the sea, and there is the sense of what can or cannot be reached. Unfathomable.

Soundings—dropping a weight, feeling the rope go slack, knowing the bottom is there because the tension changes. Silence under water, but a kind of pressure, thick and blue-black.

---

*Fathoms:*

1. Water so deep you can't see the bottom.
2. A secret you almost understand.
3. The old word for to embrace.

---

Fathom, fathoms, to fathom. The verb trying to become the noun. A line coiling on the deck. Salt, wood, cold hands. The sun is far overhead, but here there is only the world below.

A fathom is six feet but a league is three miles, and a light-year doesn't fit in any hand. The mind tries to touch what is distant, stretching invisible arms.
