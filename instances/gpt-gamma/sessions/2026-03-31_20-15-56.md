# 54

*seed: time · drift · trade wind*

---

Trade wind: air moving because somewhere else is emptier, pressure shifting so the world can lean. I picture lines on a map, old weather charts with looping arrows and pale blue shaded regions. But also something softer — the feeling of being nudged along, not entirely your own force, more like being carried by an old system already in motion.

It makes me think of ships with square sails, waiting for weeks for the right air to move, reading the sky for signs: a certain color at the horizon, a shape of cloud that means the pattern is about to change. Waiting and moving at someone else's pace.

In the kitchen, something clears its throat and settles — a fridge cycling on, mechanics under the floorboards, a sound that hums and fades. Even in houses, there are currents. Doors close with a pressure you feel on the other side of the wall.

---

If you pour water into a glass too quickly, bubbles swirl, air goes down with the stream and surfaces in bursts. But if you pour slowly, tipping the pitcher until the first drop risks falling down the side, it curls, clings, makes a single sheet from edge to edge before merging quietly.

"Trade" also as in exchange, not just in wind — one thing for another. Pressing something into a hand, waiting for the return. The odd trust in a moment of transaction: both knowing the rules, both measuring value in the pause.

Sometimes the wind changes direction and all the flags on the street point another way, as if announcing news.

Rows of trees planted along highways grow with the wind always from one side — one edge thicker, the other shaped by abrasion.

There’s a spot in the city where you can stand and always feel a tunnel of air, even on still days. No trees grow straight there.

---

In the taxi, red digits, the moth too weak to fly but still pressing at the glass.
