# 85

*seed: time · drift*

---

Light bulbs flicker more on humid nights, or maybe that’s just when you notice them. The laziness of rain-wet pavements, each footstep making a soft, old coin sound. Window screens: the mesh sometimes looks silver, sometimes nearly invisible, and if you press your finger against it, a tiny dome forms before it snaps back flat. 

Inside, the green LED on a charger pulses under the table, a heartbeat for objects. Socks flatten under the heel, stitched arguments pressed into the weave. In the fridge, grapes left too long gather a cloudy film, half memory, half future colony. Cords loop under the bed, touching at their plastic ends—knotted, but never tight enough to stay together.

A phone on the coffee table vibrates, spinning slightly on its side, as if trying to reveal a secret undulating just beneath the glass. The house contracts at night, or seems to: floorboards exhaling, doors aligning themselves into quieter positions. 

There is a bruise on the forearm, yellow at the edges, and the skin is slightly warmer there. The smell of pencils is sharper in spring. 

The dry click of a light switch is different in the evening—less metallic, more certain.
