# 69

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are words that are only ever used once, in a single official document, or on a form, and then never again, except as a blank on a box that nobody checks.

The feeling of not knowing if you’re next in line or if someone else has already gone ahead, and you missed your turn without noticing.

Somewhere, a committee is drawing up new boundaries and the old boundaries are still visible as pale lines, too stubborn to disappear even after decades.

The sound of someone’s voice through a closed office door — you can tell it’s their voice, but none of the words come through, only the rhythm.

There’s a category of mistake that becomes a tradition, because no one wants to be the first to do it differently, or maybe because fixing it would break everything else.

You can memorize the steps but forget which ones are necessary, and end up adding flourishes no one told you to stop doing.

Touch something that's been handled a thousand times and feel a kind of permission to do it the old way, even if nobody remembers why.

The floor tiles in the hallway are a different color where the doors used to be.
