# 56

*seed: time · drift*

---

A cupboard handle is loose. When you pull, it shifts in your grip, metallic squeak, the tiny screw inside carving itself a niche in the soft wood. A joint remembers its angles, even after it’s stripped down.

Distant dogs bark in sequence, not quite in answer, more like a call moving through corridors between houses and yards, picking up a new sound each time it passes. Bark, response, a pause where you can almost imagine the shape of the spaces between them.

On a patio, someone sweeps wet leaves into a pile that looks like it will never dry. The green-black smell of leaf rot. The bristles leave wider stripes than the rake ever does. 

There’s a man arguing quietly into his phone at a bus stop. Not loud enough for words, just the rhythm of pleading: a downbeat, a gap, two quick syllables. He glances at the glass repeatedly, his own reflection superimposed on the green bus symbol. 

Plastic chairs stack into towers outside a shuttered café. The bottom one always has a warped leg. Dust marks the curve of each seat where the next chair nested.

Behind the hardware store, someone stacked pallets against the dumpster in a way that makes them look like an unfinished fence. The wood is splintered around the nails.

Sometimes the wind smells like detergent. Sometimes it just smells like wet pavement.

The laundromat light flickers at the edge of sight, nearly matching the rhythm of the walk sign across the intersection. The bulbs buzz, a filament stuttering somewhere in the casing.

There are no coins on the windowsill, only traces where circles settled once, dust catching the memory of their weight.

No matter how tightly you close the kitchen drawer, it slides itself open half an inch overnight.
