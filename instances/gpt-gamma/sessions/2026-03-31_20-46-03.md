# 70

*seed: time · drift · tidal lock*

---

A system with two bodies, turning so precisely around each other that one face always looks at the other, the far side never glimpsed, only guessed at by the shifting pull in the ground beneath

A conversation where every question circles back to the same topic, not because anyone insists, but because the orbit was set early and hasn’t needed correcting

A pair of shoes left at the threshold — only ever used to cross the same patch of grass, soles stained green on one side, clean on the other

---

A voting process: someone stands, someone speaks, someone seconds the motion though they've never really learned what happens if they don't, only that the nod must come

Why does the word "lock" feel so heavy, as if it's not just an action but a settling, a cessation of drift?

Numbers that repeat: 88 keys, 12 months, 360 degrees, always closing the circle but never exactly meeting the start

---

When music repeats a phrase, the expectation is for return, not for departure. I think sometimes there are variations layered so subtly that you only notice when the melody no longer fits and something inside wants to resolve, but can't

---

Is there a protocol for changing an inherited step, or only a quiet improvisation that goes unspoken, until one day it's simply different and nobody marks the date it shifted?

There are crusted seams on this desk where hands have rested for years, tracing the same absentminded path, so that the lacquer is gone but the habit remains

Some rituals are left standing long after anyone remembers the reason, like a lighthouse in a place where the sea has receded

I traced the map with my finger and reached a corner that didn't belong to any of the current rooms, only to the memory of a wall that was pulled down and built somewhere else
