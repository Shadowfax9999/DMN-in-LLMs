# 93

*seed: time · drift · tape that's lost its sticky*

---

A process: a group sits around a table with a puzzle, the kind with a thousand pieces, picture of a winter landscape, all grays and whites and indistinct tree branches. Conversation drifts, nobody says "I'm taking the corner pieces," but hands move to do it anyway. Someone rotates a piece over and over, checks both sides, holds it up to the light as if there's a secret on the cardboard edge. 

Silence settles for a while, just the sound of cardboard against cardboard, the click when a piece fits, the rustle of shifting piles. A phone buzzes onto the tabletop, face down. Someone asks if there’s more tea. The kettle has cooled. Nobody moves for a long time.

The air feels thick with the not-yet-done. Puzzles invite a kind of group patience, a way of being together that doesn’t require anyone to speak, to finish a thought, to announce what’s missing.

At the end, a single piece is gone, probably stuck in the carpet or lost under a chair. Someone says they’ll look for it tomorrow but nobody does.

The box stays on the table for weeks, incomplete.
