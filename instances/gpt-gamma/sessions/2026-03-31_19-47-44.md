# 43

*seed: time · drift · lawnmower two yards over*

---

A sound more vibration than noise, humming under everything. You can’t tell if it’s closer now or just on a different patch of grass. Someone must be walking back and forth, probably in lines, but you never see them, just the top of the mower handle sometimes at the fence line, like a periscope.

If you look at a single blade in the sun, it glows almost blue at the edge. The grass clippings catch against your shoe when you step out later, lines of green where the dew was.

---

Three thoughts about texture:

1. The inside of a cereal box is smoother than the outside.
2. Wet pavement after dark feels slicker under sneakers, even if it’s almost dry.
3. Old towels get rough in patches, especially at the corners.

The edge of the counter is colder than the top. 

---

I’ve never seen anyone clean the ceiling fan blades. 

Sometimes you notice the dust caught at the edge when the fan spins slow and it flickers, almost like it’s moving in both directions. 

You think about reaching up with the duster but then forget, and the fan keeps spinning, a little slower, a little more dust each week. 

The way a room sounds different with the window open, clearer, every voice from the street comes in mixed with birds and tire noise. 

A plastic grocery bag crumples to the size of a fist and refuses to stay folded. 

---

I used to keep a strip of tickets in the kitchen drawer, not sure what for.
