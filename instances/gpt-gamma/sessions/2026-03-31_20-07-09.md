# 52

*seed: time · drift*

---

The shape of the bowl makes the pears lean against each other, all the weight rolling to the lowest point. Fruit flies sometimes gather there, hardly visible unless you get close. Fingerprints in the waxy skin of the apples.

A breeze comes under the door, catching the edge of a paper, making the corners lift and settle in a tiny rhythm, like breathing. Somewhere in the flooring, boards tick with expanding or contracting, just enough to notice if you’re still.

The blue glow of a laptop from behind a half-open door. Soundless, just the glow, rectangles repeating, screen-light reflected in the polished part of the floor.

On the porch, someone’s shoes, one on its side. Laces gone slack, tongue flopped out. A dot of dried mud on the toe.

---

Instructions on a packet: "Do not ingest silica gel." The words read again and again without thinking, each time the packet surfaces. Drawstring bags gathered at the top, cords knotted, gathered and loose at once.

The calendar page peeled halfway. Days crossed off, then a gap, then one more crossed off after a stretch of blankness. Someone writing in a hurry, numbers uneven, ink catching on the paper.

A dog barking three houses down. Pausing, then starting again, a rhythm that coincides with the traffic light around the block.

A lamp with a shade that turns everything yellow. Shadows of the cord on the wall, swooping arcs, then slack.
