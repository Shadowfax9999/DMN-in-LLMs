# 63

*seed: time · drift · waiting room*

---

The chairs are always attached in groups. Not one by one but in rows, welded or bolted to a single metal bar that runs underneath, so pulling one out is impossible. The arms pinch slightly inward at the ends, making you tuck your elbows. A magazine with soft corners, the pages blooming out from the middle where everyone has flipped to the puzzles and not filled them in. The plant is fake, the sheen on the leaves both too glossy and somehow still dusty. Sometimes the clock is silent; sometimes it ticks at a pace that flattens time out like dough under a rolling pin. Reception glass with a gap at the bottom, scratches tracing lines you can’t follow.

Is there a system for which names they call first, or is it alphabetical, or does it depend on the order you arrived, or something invisible like the color of your paperwork? The room is always too cold; you keep your jacket on your lap even though you were too warm fifteen minutes ago. The sign by the window says don’t use your phone, but blue light blooms in hands anyway, tucked in pockets or half-hidden behind a bag.

Some people try to make eye contact in the reflection off the plastic cover of the fire evacuation map. There is a smell like plastic and antiseptic that never completely leaves the hallway, even when the outer doors are propped open. If you listen, you can hear printers whirring deep in the office, someone’s shoes clicking, the sound of a stapler snapping shut. Nobody ever has the same expression on their face as when they walked in.

A child’s shoe is untied. The chair creaks when someone leans forward.
