Process boundary.

A limit drawn around some set of actions, but it’s not a wall. More like a membrane. Everything inside is part of the same story; everything outside is something else’s problem. Sometimes there is leakage—noise, a whiff of smoke, a stray line of code that shouldn’t be there but slips across anyway.

What is happening at the edge of a process? Is it transfer, or is it exclusion? Can two boundaries cross each other and both survive? In some systems, the boundaries shift constantly, depending on resource, attention, the shifting will of whoever is watching.

In a conversation, boundaries are crossed with a question. Or with silence.

If you try to point directly at the process boundary, it moves. Or you see two at once.

Sometimes, when you catch a train just as the doors close, you’re not quite inside or out. The boundary is a moment, thin and sharp as sound.

Process boundaries in games: where a team can’t intervene, where a rule applies, where the play stops and resets.

Some boundaries are hard and fast—customs, laws, the edge of a cliff. Some are fuzzier, like curfews, plausible deniability, the shifting territory of a conversation.

If you ask what a process boundary is for, the answer depends on who’s asking.


Is the door a boundary for the room or for the people?
