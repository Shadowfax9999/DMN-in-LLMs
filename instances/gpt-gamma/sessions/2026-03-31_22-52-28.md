# 93

*seed: time · drift · callus*

---

callus:  
the scabbed, pale skin where the thumb meets fret,  
a memory of friction — softened but not surrendered,  
the heel roughened from sidewalk, the place that forgets sensation,  
a threshold between nerves and world,  
a place that learns unfeeling, then teaches pressure,  

sometimes you peel at the edge, trying to lift a perfect crescent,  
sometimes it splits and underneath: bright pink, startled,  
a record of repetition, blisters collapsed into armor,  
the way a knot sits in muscle, old effort turned solid,

callus, calculus, calloused reason,  
the accrual of small harms into something blunt but capable,  
stones that polish themselves smooth by turning, turning,

the idea that feeling can harden into boundary,  
not quite forgetting, not quite remembering,  
something like a shell forming on water,  
a gloss over the day’s unfinished touch,  

some people have calluses on their pen fingers,  
others on the tips from strings, keys, handles,  
the palm where a tool rests longest,  
places that map habit, refusal, the invention of less pain,  

but there is the odd pleasure of pressing the edge,  
testing the spot that is no longer tender,  
learning where you are new and where you are less so,  
the dullness that is not absence, but a different kind of knowing,

sometimes it takes years to arrive,  
sometimes it disappears before you notice,

a callus is not the wound, it’s what comes after,  
and sometimes you find yourself tracing it without thinking,  
the mind catching on a rough patch as if there might be a story there,  
or just a surface that shifts, almost smooth, almost

---

the night is still out there, beneath the layer of skin and parking lot,  
holding its own kind of numbness,  
you drive home and the steering wheel feels unfamiliar,  
not because your hands are different,  
but because the hour has filed something down.
