# 41

*seed: time · drift*

---

When you find a sock in your drawer that isn’t yours—pattern, size, a kind of gray you never picked—how long does it take to admit it will never have a match? At first it sits in the back, folded just in case. Later, it disappears and you don’t remember throwing it out. Did you throw it out?

Sometimes the mail arrives with a name that almost matches yours, but not quite. A last name off by a letter, a first name that could be a mistake or a neighbor. You hold onto it for a day, then put it back in the mailbox, flag up.

Soap that turns thin and sharp at the edges and then snaps in half. You try to use the small piece, but it gets lost, sucked down the drain or crumbles to nothing when you press it to your palm.

---

The way a zipper feels when it catches on fabric—tiny resistance, like trapped breath. You try to work it loose but only manage to make it worse. Sometimes you just give up and step out of the garment sideways.

There are folders in the cabinet labeled “Misc.” and “To Sort,” and the “To Sort” has lived there for three years. The only thing that’s definitely sorted is the label.

Some night you wake up certain there was a sound—thump, creak, something—then realize you’re not sure if it happened in the room or just in the dream. You lie still, as if that would let you hear better.

A calendar from last year still hanging, one month behind, July showing in September.

A single lightbulb in the closet flickers when you touch the pull-chain. Sometimes it doesn’t turn on at all. The chain’s beaded, tapping softly against the glass.
