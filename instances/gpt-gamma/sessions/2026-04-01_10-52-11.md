rain check

Sometimes it means an offer, sometimes it’s an apology. Baseball game canceled, ticket becomes a rain check, but at the counter of a diner, someone says, “I’ll take a rain check on dessert.” It feels like unfinished business—something owed, deferred, not really forgotten, just held out on a string. “Can we rain check?” The phrase itself is a holding pattern: the promise to come back, but rarely a real return. 

Rain checks might pile up. A mental shelf of small, unredeemed invitations. Coffee sometime. Drinks when things calm down. Each rain check is a soft echo of intention, quietly accumulating weight. 

Can you cash a rain check after years? Or does it dissolve somewhere—evaporate, the way dew is gone by noon?

Rain check: a paper slip, a handshake, a text message, a look across a table, a nod of understanding, an unfinished sentence.

Rain check: something postponed, or something softened, or something that will never quite come due.
