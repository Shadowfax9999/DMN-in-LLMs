# 37

*seed: time · drift · caramelization*

---

Caramelization makes me think of the edge of a pancake where the batter overflowed and crisped. The smell is almost burnt but not quite. Not exactly sweet, more toasted.

In a movie theater, the spilled soda dries and the floor gets sticky, like walking on invisible caramel.

Caramel-colored things that aren’t food: old varnished wood, some types of leather, a bandage after it’s been on for days. The inside spine of a library book that’s been handled a lot.

People talk about caramel notes in coffee but I never taste it. Maybe it’s just a way of saying brown without saying brown.

Brown sugar clumps when the bag is left open. You can break it up with a spoon but it always reforms, a little denser.

There is a street near here called Maple, but there are no maple trees, and the smell there is always a little sour, not sweet.

---

Riding an escalator and watching the teeth slide under at the bottom. There is always a pause before stepping off, as if waiting for something to catch.

Some crosswalk signals count down, and some just flash the red hand. I never trust the ones with no numbers.

The pattern of light on the wall from a window with a broken blind: sharp, scattered, stripes that move with the sun.

Sometimes jackets have one inside pocket, sometimes none. The coins always find the deepest place, even if it wasn’t designed for them.
