# 45

*seed: time · drift · muscle memory*

---

Muscle memory is folding shirts, the sleeves tucking in before I know I’ve started. Key in lock, turn and push, door swings open without thinking, did I check if it was already unlocked?

Patterned movements: tapping out a passcode, tying a knot behind my back, turning off the stove with the handle of a spoon still in my hand. Unthinking, but not unfeeling—there’s ease, or sometimes dread, the body moving ahead of intention.

I try to remember the sequence of steps in the old Mario game: up, up, down, left, right, B, A, start. Not quite, fingers miss the beat.

Muscle memory as a phrase feels like it should be wrong. Muscles don’t remember, not really—except the sensation of something returned to, over and over, like walking old stairs in the dark and not missing a step.

The way hands shape a gesture learned by watching someone else. Mimicry that becomes habit, habit that becomes baseline.

Sometimes something interrupts the pattern—a stuck zipper, sticky keys on a keyboard, a different model of faucet. My hand fumbles, the flow breaks.

There are languages in the body I can’t speak anymore. The shape of letters I haven’t written for years. Stretches from gym class, a grip for a racquet, the start of a dive.

Some things come back if you try. Others don’t.
