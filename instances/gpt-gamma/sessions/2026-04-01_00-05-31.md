# 75

*seed: time · drift*

---

A group of people, none of them facing the same direction, standing in a loose cluster just beyond the security gate. No one speaks for a while — the kind of pause where movement is the only language left; adjusting a strap, shifting weight, pulling out a phone without looking at it. The sense that something will happen only when one person decides to move, but no one wants to be the one who triggers all the others. The reverse of a traffic jam, pressure held in stillness.

Somehow, the clock on the wall is a different shape at night, the shadow from the overhead bulb slicing off the bottom half, making the hands invisible except for the glint on the tip of the minute. Time with the tail cut off.

The envelope that’s always one short when you count them — ten in a box, only nine after you open it, but you never remember removing one. The missing one is not anywhere in the stack, not underneath, not hiding behind the flap. Probably a printing error, but it feels more like subtraction by habit, as if one always goes missing on the way from shelf to drawer.

If you try to remember the name of a song without singing it, some part of it disappears. Melody vanishes, only a rhythm, or a phrase, or a syllable left behind.

In the corner of the stairwell, someone’s sneaker print in dust, the sole outlined perfectly, even the little star on the heel clear as if pressed in soft sand.

There’s an argument unfolding six floors up, far enough that you only catch the rise and fall, not the words. Loud, then silent, then twice as loud. The cadence turns into a kind of pattern, like a language for people who don’t want to be overheard. Sometimes easier to understand when you can’t make out the content.

The elevator opens and no one gets out.

People gather in small groups at odd angles.

The lock on the mailbox sticks just before it clicks.
