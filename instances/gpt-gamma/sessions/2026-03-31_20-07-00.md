# 51

*seed: time · drift · last light*

---

Last light. The way it pulls everything sidewise — shadows flatten, the orange streetlamp glow leaks in before it’s even fully dark. Porch steps glow for a minute, then drop into blue.

Street edges where moss is almost luminous. That hour when you can’t tell if you should turn on the lamp or just wait. The wall is navy, but only at the bottom — up by the ceiling, it’s gold.

People walk dogs, slow, plodding in puffs of pollen, all silhouettes. Someone’s phone buzzes, glinting green in their pocket.

Dinner smells coming from somewhere, not yours, not recognized — onions, oil, maybe celery.

Fence-post shadows in stripes, so thin they almost disappear. Bikes racked, wheels turning in the wind.

Mail slipped through the slot, corners bent, addresses half in shadow and half in what’s left of the day.

---

There’s a pause at every intersection, waiting for the signal, shoes tapping. Cars idle, headlights reflecting off the stop sign. Sometimes you see a face through glass — just an outline, no eyes.

The sound of a skateboard far off, cracking on uneven sidewalk. Chop of a door shutting, quick, then gone.

A moth loops in and out of the beam from the porch light, wings flickering between gold and nothing.

A window is open somewhere above, curtains catching the last stripe of pink as if they could hold onto it.

On the table: a bowl of fruit with one overripe pear at the bottom.
