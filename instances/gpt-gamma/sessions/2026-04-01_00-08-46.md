# 78

*seed: time · drift · bluff (poker)*

---

the feeling under the skin, not quite a lie, not quite true.  
a squeeze in the chest: hold, don’t show, don’t spill.  
knuckles white over plastic, or fingertips drumming,  
the way silence gets heavy at the wrong moment.  
eye contact but through fog or glass, a shield or shimmer.  
in the hush, a pulse, not yours, but carried by the table legs,  
wobbled by sneakers bouncing under the felt.  
chips stacked — green, red, white, black —  
fingertips brush, slide, linger.

What changes between knowing and pretending to know?  
There’s a breath too long, the teeth pressed together,  
a flex in the jaw — how much is visible? When did you last look at your own face as you tried to be unreadable?

Bluff is a terrain:  
edges softened, nothing drops straight off,  
the ground seems solid until you’re already stepping into air.  
Fake it, call, fold, wait for the turn.  
Everyone invents history out loud. Everyone claims not to care about the pot, the cards, the call, the river.

Not every risk is visible.  
The best stories are silent, told in the way the chips move  
or the hand pauses at the fold of the card,  
or the silence that stretches too cleanly,  
a tension that shows up as a cough, as a throat clear,  
as a glance at the clock, at the full glass, at anything except the eyes.

Under the table, a shoelace comes untied,  
the socks have holes.  
The pot in the middle, out of reach until it isn’t.  
Painted nails tap the top card; someone nearly smiles,  
then remembers not to.

Someone always bluffs first,  
but the game’s already changed by then.  
A word arrives from nowhere:  
periphery
