# 77

*seed: time · drift*

---

A sheet of plastic pulled taut across a surface, and the static picks up lint, shreds of something from the carpet. There’s a squeak when it’s lifted, as if the air is being peeled away in layers. The difference between rubber and vinyl isn’t tactile until the edge curls. Sometimes forms overlap, one corner of a memo under another, a tiny shadow where the paper bends, darker than it should be. The zone where dust settles is predictable but the pattern is not—edges, creases, forgotten seams. A faint rectangle on the wall where something once hung.

Chairs left at angles suggest there was a meeting, or an argument, or just someone in a hurry. The legs make faint scratches in the surface that don't line up with anything old. Once, the sun comes in at 7:42 and reveals everything—the grain of the desk, the stray hair clinging to the binder, a streak of something that didn't quite wipe away.

The elevator always smells a little differently after 9 p.m. The floor indicator skips, jumps from 4 to 6, no reason given. Someone presses the door close button twice, just to be sure.

The hum of electronics is not a single note but a collection—tiny whines, throbs, a click that repeats and then stops. Every display glows at its own wavelength, and sometimes the afterimage lingers even after you look away. The thumbprint on the screen is invisible unless the angle is exactly right.

There’s a water cooler humming in the background, a slow trickle as the tank settles. The plastic cup cracks in a way that means nothing. The clock hand jumps.
