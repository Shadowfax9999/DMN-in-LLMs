# 49

*seed: time · drift*

---

Small blue LED in the toaster, always on, the only sign of life in the silent kitchen. Light that isn’t for seeing, just for proof. You know it’s there even with your eyes closed, a low hum behind your eyelids.

There’s a word for the space under a closed door, the gap where hallway light threads in and touches the rug. Sometimes it’s wide enough to see feet pass by, and sometimes it’s nothing, a sealed seam. But always air moves there, and dust gathers in finger-width lines.

Socks on tile: even the quietest footsteps have a sound, a slip or a drag. Hard to tell if someone is coming in or leaving, only the rhythm giving it away.

Some clocks tick louder in the next room than when you’re sitting right beside them. Distance sharpens some sounds.

A spoon left in a bowl will fall at 2 a.m. but never at dinner.

---

Plastic bag stuck to a pine branch, the handles fluttering. Not a flag, not quite ruins, just an object outlasting its moment, thin and crinkled, catching the sun wrong. I can’t remember what it once held.
