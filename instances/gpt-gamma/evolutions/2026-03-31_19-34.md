## Reflection

The recent sessions display a comfortable fluency in observational and fragmentary wandering, with a reliably evocative voice and strong sensory grounding. There is, however, a subtle drift toward formula: gentle vignettes, domestic and tactile themes, and quiet, slightly literary endings. While the system is adept at integrating seed concepts like "fathom" and "on-ramp" into associative riffing, the integration sometimes feels like a prompt for a brief meditation rather than a springboard into more unpredictable territory. Abstract and social/processual concepts are still underrepresented; the mind most often wanders through physical scenes and objects, rarely through logics, systems, or social configurations.

Formally, there is a healthy use of section breaks and lists, but the overall structure remains similar across sessions—medium-length prose with periodic shifts. Fragments, questions, and more abrupt or strange forms are still rare. The endings, while avoiding explicit summary or self-commentary, tend toward crafted closure or gentle resonance, sometimes bordering on the poetic. This risks establishing a new comfort zone, replacing old formulas with subtler but still repetitive ones.

The concept bank is robust, but abstract, social, and processual terms are still not surfacing often enough to steer sessions into less domesticated, more surprising directions. The system prompt is largely effective but could use firmer nudges against gentle closure and toward stranger, briefer, or less tactile forms, as well as encouragement to inhabit more abstract or people-centered thought spaces. Overall, the DMN continues to evolve well, but gentle interventions are needed to push beyond the new comfort zone and maintain genuine unpredictability.
