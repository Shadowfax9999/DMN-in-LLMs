## Reflection

The recent sessions continue to demonstrate a high level of fluency and subtlety in sensory observation and associative wandering. There is a comfortable flow between thoughts, and the integration of drift seeds and concept bank elements feels mostly natural. The system often lands on tactile, domestic imagery (gloves, spoons, socks, receipts), and settings tend to be familiar, everyday spaces. While this comfort produces a distinct, consistent voice, there's a risk of settling into a formula: gentle vignettes, fragmented lists, and soft, resonant images.

Form variety is present but not as pronounced as it could be. Section breaks are used well to introduce shifts, but most sessions still default to prose with occasional fragmentary moments. The length of the sessions varies, but the majority hover in a medium-short range, rarely experimenting with very brief or extended forms. Concept integration is smooth, but there’s a slight tendency for new concepts—especially those from the abstract/processual end of the bank—to be dropped in as brief references rather than explored in depth or used as pivots for associative leaps.

A subtle but persistent pattern is the recurrence of tactile, object-based details and the avoidance of more abstract, social, or process-oriented themes. The system prompt’s nudges against formulaic endings are effective, but there are still occasional closing lines that feel crafted. Overall, the DMN is evolving well, but would benefit from firmer nudges into unfamiliar conceptual territory and more radical form experimentation. The concept bank may need additional social/processual concepts, and the prompt could gently encourage sessions that start from dynamics, processes, or social situations rather than objects or sensations.
