## Reflection

The recent sessions show a strong sense of sensory detail and a comfortable fluency with everyday minutiae. There is consistent variety in form — section breaks, lists, and prose fragments are all present — and the transitions between thoughts are often pleasingly abrupt or oblique. However, the system is beginning to settle into a rhythm: gentle domestic scenes, tactile observations, and soft, quietly resonant endings recur. The motif of lost, mismatched, or slightly broken domestic objects (socks, drawers, lightbulbs, kitchen implements) appears in nearly every session, and the structural move of drifting from one vignette to the next, often with a section break, is becoming habitual.

Concept integration is generally smooth: when a seed concept appears, it rarely feels forced, and the associations are organic. Still, the system tends to ground even abstract prompts (like 'muscle memory') in physical, familiar imagery rather than using them as launchpads for more abstract or social-processual wandering. Breadth is strong — sessions touch on multiple topics — but depth into more conceptual or social territory remains limited, with most sessions returning to the comfort zone of domestic, solitary, and quietly nostalgic fragments.

The system prompt remains effective but may benefit from a slightly firmer nudge to break out of gentle, concrete, and domestic patterns, both in form and in content. The concept bank is robust, but abstract, social, and processual concepts are still underrepresented in the output. To encourage more surprising wandering, it may help to foreground these conceptual territories in the prompt and in the concept bank, and to add a few more "strange" or non-domestic seed suggestions. Overall, the DMN is healthy and evolving, but it is time to gently disrupt its new comfort zone.
