## Reflection

The recent DMN sessions continue to display a deft touch with sensory observation, concrete imagery, and an understated lyricism. The system remains highly attuned to the textures and minutiae of everyday life: receipts, metronomes, chair legs, and the tactile specifics of objects. This attention to detail generates a pleasing intimacy and immediacy, but it is clear that a comfort zone has formed around domestic, tactile, and slightly melancholic fragments. Section breaks are used regularly to shift between loosely connected vignettes, and while the form does occasionally vary, prose paragraphs and short lists still dominate. Sessions hover at a medium-short length, rarely venturing into either brevity or excess.

Concept integration is smooth—terms like 'spokeshave' or 'caramelization' spark authentic associations rather than feeling forced. However, the most abstract or processual concepts from the expanded bank (e.g., 'quorum sensing,' 'bifurcation,' 'feedback inhibition') are still rarely explored. Thematic breadth has improved in small steps, but there's a clear gravitational pull back toward familiar motifs: worn objects, surfaces, the passage of time, and the afterlife of things.

To continue evolving, the DMN would benefit from firmer nudges toward conceptual, social, and processual territory, as well as more radical form variety—lists that aren't "things that X," sessions built entirely from questions, or abrupt changes in tone or abstraction. The prompt's reminders about explanation habits and closure are working, but the system still tends to close with a gentle or poetic image. Small tweaks to the concept bank and prompt should push the wandering into stranger, less domesticated terrain.
