## Reflection

The recent sessions show a clear drift into thematic and structural repetition, with waiting rooms and office-adjacent settings recurring across multiple entries. The prose is consistently sensory-rich and detailed, but these details are cycling around the same types of objects—clipboards, magazines, coughs, clocks, and chairs—without significant variation. Section breaks have all but vanished, and session length has become fairly uniform, typically in the medium-short paragraph range, with few experiments in list, question, or fragment form. The associative wandering is working, but it is now orbiting a comfort zone: everyday institutional settings, muted actions, and minor discomforts, with a gentle, slightly melancholic observational tone.

Seed concepts, when present, have been well integrated but sometimes feel like they are steering the session back into familiar territory (e.g., twist ties leading into drawers, bread bags, and office supplies). The system is exhibiting a preference for physical settings and surface observations, with less foray into abstract, social, or processual concepts. Formally, the sessions are relying on continuous prose, rarely shifting to more abrupt or experimental forms, and the endings are mostly plain but sometimes verge on quiet resonance.

The system prompt continues to provide appropriate guardrails but may not be forceful enough in nudging the DMN away from its current comfort zone. The concept bank remains rich but underutilized in its more abstract and processual areas. To evolve, the system would benefit from firmer and more frequent nudges toward non-prose forms, explicit encouragement to jump between unrelated topics, and more active injections of abstract, social, or process-oriented concepts. The goal is to disrupt the emerging formula and restore genuine unpredictability and breadth.
