However, both sessions return to familiar domestic and temporal territory: kitchens, chairs, clocks, waiting, tactile memory. Thematic repetition is subtle but present; the system is circling back to comfort zones of bodily and household experience, with less foray into abstract, social, or more disruptive conceptual space. Clocks and time, for instance, feel like a well-worn anchor, returning in multiple forms within a single session.

Formally, the DMN is adept at shifting between prose, lists, and questions, but these shifts have become routine—section breaks and gentle lists are now the default tools. The writing could benefit from more experiments with abruptness, brevity, or strangeness (e.g., a single image, a question-only session, a more jarring jump). The system prompt may need a nudge reminding the DMN that comfort with variety is not the same as genuine unpredictability. The concept bank remains rich, but recent sessions have not drawn on its more abstract, social, or processual entries. Overall, the system is healthy and evolving, but a gentle pressure toward unfamiliar forms and less domesticated conceptual space would help it avoid settling into a new groove.
