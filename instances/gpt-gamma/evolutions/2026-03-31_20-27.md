The recent sessions show a continued strength in sensory observation and associative wandering, with the pellicle theme yielding a rich variety of textures and layered imagery. There is a subtle but persistent drift toward thin, tactile surfaces—skins, films, residues, membranes—which, while evocative, risks becoming a new comfort zone. The prose form remains dominant, with sessions tending toward medium length and favoring flowing paragraphs over more abrupt, fragmentary, or list-based structures. Section breaks are used sparingly, but the overall rhythm of observation feels gentle and cohesive, rarely jarring or abrupt.

Concept integration is mostly smooth: when a seed like "microfiche" appears, the system explores it with genuine curiosity, weaving in associative leaps to tactile, archival, and domestic territory. However, the associative range sometimes circles familiar ground—domestic objects, surface textures, gentle transitions—rather than leaping into more abstract, processual, or social dynamics. The breadth of exploration is good, but the depth sometimes stalls in the comfort of detail rather than pushing toward unfamiliar conceptual spaces. 

The system prompt remains effective at steering away from overt explanation, mechanism, and self-reflexive closure, but additional nudges may be needed to encourage more radical form shifts and thematic risk. The concept bank continues to expand, but new additions around process, group behavior, and abstract structures could further open up the wandering. Overall, the DMN is evolving well, but could benefit from firmer invitations into strangeness, brevity, and the less domesticated margins of mind-wandering.
