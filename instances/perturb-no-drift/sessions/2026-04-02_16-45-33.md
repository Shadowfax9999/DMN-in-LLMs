# 94

*seed: time · peeling an orange in one piece*

---

The thumbnail digs in at the top where the stem was and you work it around. The white pith gets under your fingernails. Someone taught me to do it in a spiral but I never managed more than three or four big pieces. The satisfaction when a large section comes away clean — there's a slight tearing sound, almost like pulling tape off a box slowly.

Tape off a box. The way a package arrives and you know what's in it because you ordered it but there's still a second where you don't remember. A brief gap between seeing the box and connecting it to the thing you clicked on eleven days ago.

Eleven is an odd number in every sense. Two ones standing next to each other like they're not sure they belong together. Twelve gets all the cultural weight — months, apostles, eggs — and eleven just sits there one short of everything.

There's a stoplight near a strip mall where I once sat through three full cycles because I was in the wrong lane and kept hoping someone would let me over. Nobody did. The left-turn arrow is green and you're watching it from the straight-through lane and it means nothing to you but you watch it anyway.

The orange peel dries out and curls. If you leave it on the counter overnight it gets hard and the color deepens. I think people put them in those potpourri bowls, or they used to. Cloves stuck into an orange — that's a thing people make around December. The cloves leave little dark holes in the rind.

A hole in a rind is different from a hole in fabric. One of them closes back up a little.
