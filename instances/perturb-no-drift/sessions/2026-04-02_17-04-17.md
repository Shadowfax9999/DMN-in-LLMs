Astrolabe. Heavy word. Brass-colored in my head, which is probably just because the object is brass.

There's a bakery I pass sometimes where the flour dust on the counter looks like it could be a map of something. Not a real map. Just the way powder settles unevenly. The girl behind the counter always has her sleeves pushed up past her elbows even when it's cold, and I've never figured out if that's a uniform thing or a preference thing. Probably flour on sleeves is annoying. That's the whole reason. Somebody at a dentist's office once had their sleeves pushed up the same way and it looked completely different — clinical instead of comfortable. Same gesture, different room. The room decides what the gesture means.

I can never remember whether Celsius and Fahrenheit cross at negative forty or negative thirty-eight. One of those. They meet exactly once and then diverge forever, which sounds like it should be a bigger deal than it is.

The faucet in my kitchen has a slow drip that I keep meaning to fix and the washer is probably seventy cents. The issue isn't cost, it's that I'd have to turn the water off under the sink and the shutoff valve down there is one of those oval handles that hasn't been turned in years and might not turn at all, or might turn and then not stop. So instead I just listen to it at night. It's not rhythmic enough to be soothing and not fast enough to be urgent.

Alan Turing kept his mug chained to a radiator so nobody would steal it. Or that might be apocryphal. Most of the good stories about eccentric people are.

The smell of cut grass is already in the air and it's only the first week of April.
