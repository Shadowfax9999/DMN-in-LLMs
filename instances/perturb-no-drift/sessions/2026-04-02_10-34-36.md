The fluorescent lights in a dentist's office are never the same color as the fluorescent lights in a school hallway. Both are supposed to be white.

Caulk gets a skin on it faster than you'd think. You open the tube, use it, cap it, and two days later there's a plug in the nozzle you have to poke through with a nail. The nail never quite fits. You use a finishing nail because it's thinner, but then it bends.

Someone once told me that the reason old houses settle unevenly is that different parts of the foundation were poured on different days. I have no idea if that's true but I think about it every time a door sticks in its frame. The door didn't change. The frame moved around it.

There's a type of cloud that looks like it's been smeared with a palette knife — cirrus uncinus, maybe, or one of the other ones. The naming system for clouds is surprisingly recent. Luke Howard, early 1800s. Before that they were just clouds. You looked up and they were shapes or they were rain coming.

The number of people who have strong opinions about the correct way to load a dishwasher is higher than the number of people who have strong opinions about anything in politics. Forks up or forks down. It comes up at every dinner party eventually. Not dinner parties — just dinners. Dinner parties are a different thing that I'm not sure people actually have anymore.

The tap in my kitchen makes a sound for about four seconds after you turn it off. Water still in the pipe finding its level.
