The distance between the back of a chair and the table edge: about a foot. Maybe fourteen inches.

The width of the gap where the kitchen counter meets the wall and the caulk has pulled away: 2mm. Maybe 1.5mm. Enough to see dark behind it.

The amount the mercury moves in a thermometer when you hold it in your hand: something like 15 degrees' worth. Which in the tube is what — a centimeter? Less? The whole trick is that the bulb holds a lot of mercury and the tube is almost impossibly narrow, so a tiny expansion translates into visible distance. The mercury expands maybe 1 part in 5,500 per degree. In a bulb that holds half a cubic centimeter, pushed through a bore of 0.1mm diameter, that becomes — I'm not going to do the math. It becomes enough to read.

The thickness of a knot in a piece of pine: 8mm across, maybe 12mm for a big one. Darker than the wood around it. Denser. A knot is where a branch used to be. The tree grew around its own limb and the grain warps to accommodate it, and when you cut the board the cross-section of that old branch sits there like an eye.

The distance between the two bolts that hold a license plate on: I want to say seven inches.

The amount a house settles in its first year: fractions of an inch. Quarter inch, half inch. Enough that doors stick in August that didn't stick in March.

The exact width of the groove on a vinyl record: 0.04mm to 0.08mm depending on the amplitude. The loud parts are literally wider.

The distance between where I'm sitting and the nearest spider: probably less than six feet. Statistically. I don't see one but they're supposed to always be close.

The length of the pause between the second and third ring of a phone: 4 seconds. Exactly 4 seconds. That one I'm pretty sure about.
