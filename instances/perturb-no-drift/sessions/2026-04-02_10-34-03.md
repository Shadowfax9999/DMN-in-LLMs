*seed: time · the sound of a basketball on asphalt vs concrete*

---

Different sounds entirely. On asphalt it's duller, almost soft — the surface gives a little, or maybe the texture absorbs something. Concrete is sharper, higher. You can hear a basketball on concrete from farther away.

The seams in a sidewalk. When you're on a bike as a kid, you feel every one. Tha-thunk, tha-thunk. Regular as a heartbeat until the sidewalk panels start to shift and the rhythm gets syncopated. Someone poured those panels decades apart in some cases. You can see it in the color difference.

A friend of mine used to say "acrost" instead of "across." Not ignorance — regional. Somewhere in Ohio, maybe Indiana. The T at the end like a little gate closing behind the word.

Envelopes. The way you have to lick the whole strip but the glue only activates in patches, so you press it shut and one corner pops back up. Self-seal envelopes fixed this and nobody celebrated. There should have been a parade.

The inside of a walnut looks like a brain. Everyone knows this. But the inside of a bell pepper looks like nothing else — those pale ribs and the seeds clustered at the top like a chandelier that fell. I cut one open last week and water came out, which I wasn't expecting. Not a lot. Just enough to notice on the cutting board.

There's a kind of screw that has a square hole instead of a Phillips or a flathead. Robertson. They're everywhere in Canada and almost nowhere in the US, which seems like the kind of thing that should have a boring explanation involving patents or trade agreements.

The basketball sound again. I think what changes between asphalt and concrete is the bounce height too — asphalt absorbs more energy so the ball doesn't come back as high. You'd have to dribble harder.
