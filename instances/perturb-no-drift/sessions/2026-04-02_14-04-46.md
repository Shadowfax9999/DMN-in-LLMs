*seed: time · the way elevator buttons light up differently in different buildings*

---

Some elevators the button stays lit the whole time. Others pulse. There's one kind where pressing it again cancels the selection, which feels like a mistake the first time and a revelation the second.

The number 6 on an elevator panel is always in the same place relative to 5 but the font changes building to building and it's strange how much that matters. Some buildings use Helvetica and some use something that looks like it came off a 1970s calculator.

Cafeteria trays. The ones with the molded compartments. The compartment for the main dish is always the largest but the dessert compartment is deeper than you'd expect. I ate off those trays for years without noticing that the silverware didn't have its own compartment — you just put it wherever.

The sound a basketball makes on an outdoor court versus an indoor one. Outdoor is flatter. The ball itself might be different too, the rubber versus the leather, but even the same ball sounds different. Concrete gives it back faster.

Someone told me once that the yellow of a legal pad was originally just the color of the cheapest paper available and now it's a deliberate choice that costs more to produce than white would. I have no idea if that's true.

There's a specific kind of cloud that looks like it has a flat bottom and a cauliflower top. Every cloud of that type has the flat bottom at exactly the same altitude as every other cloud of that type on the same day. Different altitude different day but on any given afternoon they all agree.

The word "penultimate" gets misused as if it means something grander than "ultimate." People use it to mean the most ultimate. The pen- is doing the opposite of what they think.

I can never remember which way to turn a radiator valve.
