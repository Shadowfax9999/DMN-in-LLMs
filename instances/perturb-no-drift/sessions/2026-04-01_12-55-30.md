# 13

*seed: time · fluorescent buzz*

---

Fluorescent buzz. The sound you stop hearing until someone mentions it.

Ceiling tiles with brown water stains that look like countries. There's always one that's slightly pushed up from where someone checked the wiring and didn't seat it back right.

The plastic chair in a waiting room where one leg is shorter. You shift and it rocks. You shift back. It rocks the other way. You just pick a side and commit.

Magazines from 2019 on a side table. A Real Simple with an avocado on the cover.

I once spent forty minutes in a place like that — maybe a dentist, maybe the DMV — reading an entire article about organizing your garage with pool noodles sliced lengthwise and I still think about it sometimes. You slit the pool noodle and clamp it over the edge of a shelf so things don't roll off. That's the whole idea. It works, probably.

The woman across from me was filling out a form with a pen that was chained to the clipboard. The chain was about eight inches long. She kept having to hold the clipboard at an angle to reach the bottom of the form.

There's a specific kind of clock that only exists in those rooms. White face, black numbers, red second hand that moves in ticks not sweeps. It's always seven minutes off from your phone.

The fluorescent tube that's dying does this thing where it gets dimmer at one end. The pink-gray end. Then it starts flickering a little, not dramatically, just enough that you're not sure if it's actually flickering or if you're blinking.

Someone's kid is kicking the back of a chair in a rhythm that's almost but not quite regular.
