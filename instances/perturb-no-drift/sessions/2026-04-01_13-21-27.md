Caulk never comes out of the tube the way you think it will.

The longest I've ever watched a ceiling fan was maybe four minutes. That seems short but it's actually a strange amount of time to watch a ceiling fan. At some point you stop seeing blades and start seeing a disc, and then at some point after that you start seeing blades again, which shouldn't be possible at the same speed, but it happens. Something about attention resetting.

Pickles. The word "pickles" is inherently funny and I don't think anyone has ever satisfactorily explained why some words are funny. "Bucket" is funny. "Plinth" is funny. "Desk" is not funny. "Moist" people claim to hate but I think that's a learned social behavior at this point.

I was thinking about how when you're in a car that's idling and another car next to you starts rolling backward slowly, for a half-second you absolutely believe you're the one moving forward. The whole body commits to it. Then you check and it corrects, but the correction feels like the lie and the first impression felt like the truth.

Aluminum. I don't have a thought about aluminum. It just came up.

There's a particular kind of store that only sells one very specific thing — just buttons, or just beads, or just maps — and I don't understand how they survive economically but they always seem to have been there for thirty years. The person behind the counter knows everything about that one thing.

My left shoe is slightly tighter than my right shoe today and it has been making me aware of my left foot all morning in a way that is not painful but is constant. Like a word you've said too many times and now it sounds foreign.

I think there are more beige houses than any other color but I've never counted.
