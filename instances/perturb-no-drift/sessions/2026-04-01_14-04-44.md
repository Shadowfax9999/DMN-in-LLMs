*seed: time · drift · how a hydraulic press multiplies force through fluid pressure*

---

A hydraulic press works because pressure transmitted through fluid acts equally in every direction, so a small piston pushing hard on a small area creates the same pressure across a large piston with a large area, which means the force gets multiplied by the ratio of the areas, and there's something about that — about a small effort becoming a large effort just by changing the container it moves through — that feels like the way a single off-hand comment someone makes at lunch can sit in a narrow channel for hours and then surface at 2 AM pressing against a much wider area of your chest, though of course fluid doesn't remember anything and your chest isn't a cylinder and the analogy is wrong in every direction except the one where it isn't. Kintsugi is also about pressure, probably.
