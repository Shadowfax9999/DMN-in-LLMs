*seed: time · the moment a streetlight turns on*

---

The moment before you notice it's on. It was already on. You just caught the edge of something shifting in the light and looked up and it was orange already, fully committed.

Parking lots have that too. You drive in during the day and leave after dark and at some point every light in the lot turned on and you didn't see any of them do it.

The word "dusk" does something strange in the mouth. It starts open and closes shut.

I used to think fireflies were synchronized and they're not, they're just close enough together that your eye groups them. Or maybe some species actually are. I'm not sure which thing I heard second.

There's a faucet in the building where I work that takes four full seconds between when you turn the handle and when water comes out. Long enough that you wonder if it's broken every single time. Four seconds is a lot of nothing when you're expecting something. You start to turn it more, which doesn't help, and then it all arrives at once.

The handle is one of those cross-shaped ones that hospitals have, the kind you can turn with your elbow.

I think the13-amp fuse is the one that goes in the plug for a kettle.
