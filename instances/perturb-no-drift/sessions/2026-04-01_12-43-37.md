# 10

*seed: time · hitchhiker*

---

Thumbs. The whole practice depends on thumbs. Which is strange because most of what you're communicating is done with the whole body — standing at the edge, facing traffic, visible. The thumb is almost ceremonial.

I've never hitchhiked.

There's a plant called hitchhiker's weed, or maybe beggar's tick — those flat seeds with two prongs that grab onto your socks. You don't notice them until you're already home.

The Voyager probes are hitchhiking, kind of. Except nobody picked them up. They're just standing at the edge of the solar system with their thumb out.

No — that's not right either. They're moving. They threw themselves.

Catching a ride implies someone else is already going where you want to go. The whole economy of hitchhiking is that the car is going there anyway. You add almost nothing to the cost. You're a rounding error on someone else's fuel.

Remoras on sharks.

The number of relationships that work like that — where one party is going somewhere anyway and the other just needs to hold on. Freight trains. Currents. A younger sibling following an older one to a party.

I used to hold the back of my friend's bike going up a hill while he pedaled. He was stronger. I don't think he noticed the extra drag, or maybe he did and didn't say anything.

There's a whole spectrum between hitchhiking and stealing a ride. Boxcar riders. Stowaways. At some point the person whose momentum you're borrowing doesn't know about it, and the ethics shift, but the physics don't.

Seeds do it. Burrs, obviously, but also the ones that ride water, or ride wind — they didn't choose the medium. Dandelion seeds don't know there's wind. They're just shaped for it.

Being shaped for something you don't know exists.
