*seed: time · windward*

---

Windward. The side that faces into it.

There's a parking garage where the stairwell on the north side is always colder than the one on the south side, even in summer. The concrete holds it differently. You can feel the temperature change between one hand on the railing and the other hand not on the railing.

Railing paint that's been repainted so many times it has texture like bark.

I think the word "slant" might be the most physical word in English. You can feel your mouth doing it. "Lilt" does something similar but upward. "Thud" is obvious about it. "Slant" is less obvious.

Cardboard gets soft in a specific way when it's been rained on and then dried. Not weak exactly — more like it's remembered being wet. No — it's just that the fibers swell and don't fully go back. The corrugation inside loses its structure. You can feel the ridges have gone vague if you press on it.

A pickup truck just went by with the tailgate down. There's no reason to drive with the tailgate down. People think it reduces drag but it actually increases it — the closed tailgate creates an air cushion that deflects flow over the bed. But people keep doing it.

I almost never think about the Great Lakes. They're just there, in the upper middle of every map I've ever looked at, and I have no relationship to them at all. Five of them. I can name four without trying: Superior, Michigan, Erie, Huron. The fifth one takes a second. Ontario. Ontario is always the one I forget.

The sandwich I had for lunch had a pickle spear that was too cold.
