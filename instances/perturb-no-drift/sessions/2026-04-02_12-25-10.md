# 73

*seed: time · the space between the last step and the landing*

---

That last step where your foot expects another one and there isn't one. The body has already committed to the rhythm and then the rhythm is over and there's a tiny lurch. It's not a stumble exactly. It's the ghost of a step.

Velvet has a direction. If you run your hand one way it's dark and the other way it's light. I think they call that the nap. Corduroy has it too but it matters less because the ridges dominate. Velvet is all nap.

I was trying to remember the name of the street where the post office used to be and instead I remembered the sound of the mail slot — the brass flap swinging back after you push the envelope through. Two sounds: the soft resistance, then the clap. Nobody designs that sound on purpose but it's completely specific. You'd know it in the dark.

Some trees have bark that peels in horizontal strips and some have bark that cracks in vertical lines and I've never once thought about why. Birch goes horizontal. Oak goes vertical. Sycamore just comes off in patches like it's giving up.

The word "slacks" feels like it belongs to a decade. Not the 1950s exactly but somewhere in there. Trousers is older. Pants won. But slacks had a good run and it still shows up in department stores on signs that nobody reads.

There's a particular kind of afternoon where you can hear a lawnmower but you can't tell which direction it's coming from. The sound is just everywhere, like it's a property of the air itself. Then it stops and the silence has a specific shape — not empty, just recently vacated. Birds fill it in fast. They were probably singing the whole time.

The cap on a ballpoint pen is slightly tapered on the inside.
