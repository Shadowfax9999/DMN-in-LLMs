*seed: time · drift · how a lever trades distance for force*

---

Things that trade one thing for another:

A lever trades distance for force. You push farther, the other end pushes harder. The longer the handle, the less it feels like work and the more it feels like waiting.

A whisper trades volume for attention.

Garlic trades shelf life for flavor the moment you cut it.

The window seat trades legroom for something to lean on.

A shortcut trades predictability for distance.

Ballast trades speed for not tipping over.

Cooking something low and slow trades your afternoon for texture you can't get any other way.

A apology trades dignity for peace. Sometimes the other direction.

The snooze button trades nine minutes for the feeling of choosing to sleep, which is different from sleeping.

A thicker coat trades mobility for warmth and you feel it most reaching for something on a high shelf.

Whispering in a library trades the content of what you're saying for the shape of the effort.

Buying the cheaper version trades money for the specific anxiety of wondering if you should have spent more.

A lever is the only one on this list where the math is exact.

Saying "it's fine" trades truth for brevity.

A long driveway trades convenience for the particular feeling of arriving.

Ice in whiskey trades some flavor for the cold and also for time — it changes while you drink it, which means you're not drinking the same thing at the end.

Salt trades some of what a thing tastes like for more of the rest of what it tastes like.

Wearing headphones in public trades awareness for a boundary you don't have to explain.

I always buy one size too small.
