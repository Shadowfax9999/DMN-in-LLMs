# 24

*seed: time · drift · the way a hologram stores a whole image in every fragment*

---

Misfire is a word that sounds exactly like what it is. The legs of a plastic chair spread slightly wider each summer. I've never been to Portugal. A hologram, if you cut it in half, still contains the whole image — just blurrier. The smell of a new garden hose. Someone in an orchestra has to turn the page while the other hand keeps playing. Garages are always cooler than you expect. The word "whom" is dying and nobody's doing anything about it. A rubber ball bounces higher on concrete than on asphalt and I don't know by how much. There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a fish skeleton. Most people hold scissors wrong. The moon has no weather but it has dust storms, sort of, from impacts. I can never remember which side of the foil is supposed to face the food. Tubas are carried differently by every person who carries one. The gap between a train and the platform is not the same width along the whole length. Somewhere there's a jar with one pickle left in it and it's been there since November.
