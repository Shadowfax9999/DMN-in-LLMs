Cardboard gets soft in a specific way when it's been rained on and then dried. Not soft like cloth. More like it forgot how to be rigid.

The longest I've ever watched a ceiling fan was probably four minutes. That's a guess. It might have been two.

Tongs. There's a motion your hand makes with tongs — that test-click before you pick anything up — that serves no function at all. Everyone does it.

I can picture the layout of a grocery store I haven't been to in eight years but I can't remember which side the milk was on. The deli counter was on the left when you walked in. I'm almost certain. The flooring changed near the bakery section — went from tile to a different tile, slightly warmer color. Why do I still have that.

A rubber band wrapped around a doorknob is supposed to help with something. Keeping it from latching? I've seen that suggested and never understood the specific problem it solves.

The word "caulk" looks wrong every time I read it.
