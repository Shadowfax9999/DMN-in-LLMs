*seed: time · the way a book smells different depending on when it was printed*

---

Paperbacks from the seventies have that sweet acetone thing. Library sales, you can sort by decade with your nose almost faster than by looking at the copyright page.

The word "foxing" for those brown spots on old paper. I don't know where that comes from and I'm not going to look it up right now.

There's a street near where I grew up that had a culvert running under it, and after enough rain you could hear water moving if you stood in the right spot on the sidewalk. Not loud. Just present. The asphalt had a dip there that never got fixed. Cars would hit it and you'd hear the suspension compress — a sound like a single syllable from a word you don't know.

Suspension bridges and car suspension are doing completely different things.

I've been thinking about how faucets work. The older kind with the rubber washer that compresses against a brass seat — you're just pushing rubber into metal harder and harder until water can't get through. Newer ones use ceramic discs with holes that align or don't. The ceramic ones don't drip. But the old ones had that quarter-turn of give at the end where you could feel the washer deforming, and you knew exactly how closed the faucet was. The ceramic ones are binary. Water or no water. There's a detent and then it's off.

A guy at work used to say "that's neither here nor there" about four times a day. Just a filler phrase for him, the way some people say "honestly" or "at the end of the day." But the phrase itself is strange if you hear it fresh — something that exists in neither of two locations. Where is it then.

The ceiling fan in this room has a pull chain and I can never remember which chain is the fan and which is the light. One is shorter. I think the shorter one is the light but I've been wrong about that before.

Cashews come from a fruit. The fruit is supposedly edible but almost nobody eats it. The cashew hangs off the bottom of the fruit like an afterthought.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs but not when I go up. It's been doing that for about three years.
