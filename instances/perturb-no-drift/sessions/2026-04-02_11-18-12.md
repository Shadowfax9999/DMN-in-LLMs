*seed: time · pumice*

---

Pumice. The stone that floats. You can hold it and feel how wrong it is — too light for its size, like a prop version of a rock.

The inside of a clarinet bell is smooth in a way that seems unnecessary. Nobody's touching it. Nobody sees it. But the air does, and apparently that matters.

I sat in a car once in a parking garage for maybe twenty minutes after arriving somewhere, just not getting out yet. Not on my phone. Not upset. Just sitting in a parked car in a concrete structure. The engine ticking as it cooled. There's a whole phase of arriving that happens before you open the door.

Caulk comes in more colors than you'd think. There's one called "almond" that doesn't look like any almond I've seen.

The way a screen door doesn't fully close — it gets to the last inch and just hovers there, then the pneumatic closer eases it shut with that final click. Two speeds in one motion. I think the mechanism is just a piston in a cylinder with a small hole for air to pass through.

Geese fly over in a V and everyone looks up. It lasts about four seconds and then everyone goes back to what they were doing. The same event, every time, and it still works.

My left shoe is tighter than my right today. Feet aren't the same size and shoe pairs are.
