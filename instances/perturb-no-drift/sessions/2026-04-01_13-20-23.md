# 21

*seed: time*

---

Staple removers look like little snake mouths.

The overhead lights in this building make a sound I can only hear when no one's talking. It's not a hum exactly. More like a pressure.

I used to think "albeit" was two words. I don't remember when I stopped thinking that. There must have been a day.

Carpet in offices is always that same tight low-pile stuff, the kind that shows roller-chair tracks. You can see where someone's been sitting by the fan of tracks around one spot. A whole day recorded in carpet.

My left shoe is tighter than my right shoe. Same size, same shoe, different fit. I read somewhere that most people have one foot slightly larger than the other. I don't remember which one is supposed to be bigger.

The vending machine across the hall has a Twix that's been sitting against the glass at a slight angle for at least three weeks. It hasn't moved. Nobody's bought one. The price is $2.25 which feels like a lot for a Twix but I genuinely don't know what things cost in vending machines anymore. There was a time when I had a strong sense of what a candy bar should cost and now I don't.

Pigeons on window ledges always face the same direction. I think it's into the wind but I'm not positive.

The font on exit signs is surprisingly specific — there's a whole standard for it. The green ones and the red ones mean different things in different countries. I think in Europe they're green with the running man. In the US they're red and just say EXIT in that blocky font. Or maybe both exist here. I've seen both.

A staple remover could also be a tiny alligator. Depends on whether you see the jaw from the side or head-on.
