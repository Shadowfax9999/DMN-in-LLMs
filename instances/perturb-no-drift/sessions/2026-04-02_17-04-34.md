# 98

*seed: time · first frost*

---

First frost in April would be late enough to matter. The tomato plants. Somebody always loses tomato plants.

The phrase sounds like it should be the title of something. A young adult novel, maybe. Or a racehorse.

There's a particular sound that a garden hose makes when it's been left out and the water inside it has gone cold — you turn the nozzle and the first few seconds come out in slugs, uneven, like the hose is clearing its throat. Then it smooths out and you forget.

Alan Shepard hit a golf ball on the moon. Two golf balls. I always forget there were two.

The number of things I know about the moon that I can't verify is probably large. Most of what I know about anything is like that. Secondhand all the way down. Even the things I've seen — I saw them once, and now I have a memory of seeing them, which is a different thing.

A receipt from a gas station, found in a jacket pocket from last fall. $42.07. That number means nothing now but it was exact once. Someone pumped exactly that much. The pump clicked off and that was the number.

Cardboard gets soft in a specific way when it's been rained on and then dried. Not wet-soft. A different soft. Like it lost some internal argument about whether it was still rigid.

There are stores that sell nothing but fasteners. Bolts, washers, cotter pins, set screws, clevis pins. I don't know what a clevis pin does exactly. It has a hole through one end for a wire to pass through, I think.
