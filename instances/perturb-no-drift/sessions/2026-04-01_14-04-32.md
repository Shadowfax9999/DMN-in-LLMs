# 31

*seed: time · matinee*

---

Matinee. The double-e at the end doing nothing, just sitting there being French.

There's a theater on a street I can almost picture — not a specific one, more like the composite theater that forms when you've seen enough of them. The marquee with black letters on white plastic, some of them slightly crooked. The carpet inside that's always the same dark pattern designed to hide stains. Probably the most thought-through carpet in any building, and nobody thinks about it.

The cold hits you different in a matinee. You walk in from bright sun and the air conditioning is already running for a crowd that isn't there yet. Fifteen people in a room built for three hundred. Everyone picks a seat like they're solving a spacing problem, which they are. Nobody sits directly in front of anyone else. Nobody sits directly next to anyone else. The distribution would probably look organic from above, like seeds on a sunflower head, maximizing distance.

Coins in a fountain don't do that. They cluster. People throw them toward the center, toward each other.

The previews are louder than the movie will be. They're always louder. I think the volume is actually set differently for them but I'm not sure that's true.

A pen I lost three weeks ago is probably inside a couch cushion. Not this couch. A different couch. I keep not checking.

The thing about a matinee is you come out and it's still light and your eyes do that squinting adjustment and for about four seconds you don't know what season it is. You left in daylight and returned to daylight but something shifted. The parking lot looks like a different parking lot.

Socks with no elastic left in them. They just slide down into your shoe and bunch up near your toes. You can feel exactly where the fabric folds over itself.
