# 14

*seed: time · overexposure*

---

Overexposure. The photography kind first — too much light, everything washed to white. Then the financial kind, which I think means having too much invested in one thing. Then the skin kind.

Three completely different disasters named the same way.

The cap on a tube of toothpaste: why is it always the wrong size for the thread? You turn it and it catches, then skips, then catches again. Some engineer specified that thread pitch and it's never quite right.

A cardinal outside. Not singing — just the chip note. The sharp one that sounds like a smoke detector running low on batteries. I spent a long time not knowing that was a cardinal.

Socks that have lost their elastic. The slow roll down the ankle across a morning. You pull them up at 8 and by 10 they're bunched at the heel and you've stopped noticing.

The word "several." How many is that. It's not three and it's not seven. Somewhere in there. Everyone agrees it's more than two and less than a lot but nobody's pinned it down and nobody needs to.

Caulk guns. The ratchet mechanism — you squeeze and it advances one click and the plunger pushes the caulk forward. But when you release the trigger the caulk keeps coming out for a second. You have to flip that little release tab on the back. Most people forget and get a blob on the countertop.
