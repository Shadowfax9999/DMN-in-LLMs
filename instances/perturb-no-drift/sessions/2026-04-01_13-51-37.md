*seed: time · a button you push that doesn't click*

---

A button you push that doesn't click. The membrane kind on a microwave. You press it and something happens but your finger doesn't believe it.

Elevator buttons that were already pressed. You press it again anyway.

The word "penultimate" — I've never heard anyone use it outside of trying to use it.

There's a specific kind of shelf in a closet that's too high to see the top of. You put things up there and they become invisible. Tax documents from 2019. A hat you don't wear. You'd have to stand on something to even confirm they're still there.

Confirming things are still there. Opening the oven to check. Opening the dryer to check. Opening a suitcase you already zipped to check if your charger is in it even though you watched yourself put it in.

The zipper on a suitcase goes around three sides, which means there's always a moment where you're not sure which direction to pull. Both directions work. One of them is the wrong one and you won't know until you're halfway around.

Fitted sheets have a tag that says which end is the long end and I have never once found it before the third attempt.

A guy at a hardware store once told me the trick is that the tag always goes on the bottom left. I don't know if that's true for all brands or just the one he sold.

I think about advice from strangers in stores more often than advice from people I know well. Something about the specificity. They're not advising you on your life, they're advising you on a hose clamp.

Hose clamps come in sizes and I always buy one too small.
