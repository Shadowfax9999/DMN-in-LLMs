Staplers have two settings and I've never used the second one.

The one where the prongs bend outward instead of inward. For temporary fastening, supposedly. I've seen it. I've never chosen it. I don't know anyone who has.

A vending machine in a hospital basement. The light inside it is the most confident light in the building. Everything else is hedged — dimmed corridors, curtained windows, the nurses' station lit from below by screens. But the vending machine just *blazes*. Funyuns at full brightness.

The word "slacks" is losing ground. My grandmother said slacks. I say pants. Someone younger than me probably just says whatever the specific thing is — chinos, joggers. Slacks covered a whole territory that's been subdivided.

I was trying to remember how a whittling knife is different from a regular knife and I think the answer is the handle is rounder and the blade is shorter but I'm not confident. There's a category of tool where I can picture someone using it but couldn't buy the right one.

Parking garages echo in a way that makes every car sound like it's on your level.
