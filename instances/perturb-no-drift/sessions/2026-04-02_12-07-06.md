*seed: time · the way tape sounds when you pull it off cardboard*

---

That sticky unzipping. Fast it's a rip, slow it's almost a voice.

The clear packing tape leaves a ghost of itself on the box — pulls up a layer of brown paper so you can see the corrugation underneath. Exposed ribs. Every box has that structure and you forget until the tape shows you.

I used to think corrugated meant wavy. It does mean wavy. But the wave is triangular, not round, which seems like it shouldn't count. Triangular waves. There's a synthesizer waveform called that. Sounds buzzy and hollow compared to a sine wave. Everything in synthesis is named after shapes you can draw — saw, square, triangle, sine. The names are perfect and also completely unhelpful if you haven't seen the oscilloscope. Somebody once had to decide that the shape mattered more than the sound for naming purposes.

A fly keeps landing on the same spot on the wall. There's nothing there. It lands, walks two inches, leaves, comes back. Whatever it's finding is invisible to me and absolutely compelling to it. Two different worlds in the same room.

The cardboard thing — when you break down boxes for recycling, the sequence is: pull off the tape, press the flaps in, stomp it flat. The stomp is satisfying out of proportion. You're standing on furniture. Temporary furniture that exists for one second and then it's flat.

My right thumbnail has a ridge in it that's been growing out for months. A dent, really. I read once that these come from some minor trauma to the nail bed — you banged it on something and forgot about it, and then six weeks later the evidence arrives. The body keeping records you didn't ask for.

Somewhere in this building the HVAC just shifted. You don't hear it turn on — you hear the change in what was already there.
