Span. Like a bridge has one, or a hand has one. Span of attention. The word does a lot of work.

Mirrors don't reverse left and right. They reverse front and back. You raise your right hand, the person in the mirror raises the hand on your left — but it's still their right hand, because they're facing you. The confusion is that we imagine the mirror-person as someone who walked around behind a pane of glass and turned to face us. But nobody walked anywhere. The mirror just swapped the axis that points into it. Front became back. Everything else stayed. The thing people think mirrors do is the thing mirrors don't do, and the thing mirrors do do is the thing nobody notices.

The tab on a soda can — you push it one way and it levers the mouth open through a hole that was scored into the aluminum at the factory. The scoring is the part that matters. Without it, the lever does nothing. The can resists and resists and resists at one thickness and then at a thickness just below that it gives way all at once. There's no in-between.
