*seed: time · locker combination*

Thirty-two, eighteen, seven. Not mine. I don't know whose. But the hand knows to go past the first number once, and that was always the part that felt wrong — you have to overshoot to arrive.

The dial on a locker is surprisingly smooth for something in a middle school. Everything else in that building is textured for punishment — the cinderblock, the carpet that's basically sandpaper, the water fountain with the calcified nozzle. But the lock dial is this little circle of frictionlessness.

Copiers have a smell. Not toner exactly. Something warmer. I sat next to one for an entire semester in a temp job and the smell became invisible after about four days. Then I went on vacation and came back and there it was again, thick and almost sweet. The nose resets.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a ceiling. Just flat across the whole sky, no texture, no features. Stratus, probably. It makes the light come from everywhere at once and nothing casts a shadow. Days like that feel like being inside a room with no walls.

A french press works because the mesh is just tight enough. Too tight and you can't push it down. Too loose and you get mud. The tolerance on that mesh must be pretty narrow actually. Someone had to decide the exact gauge.

I keep confusing Tbilisi and Tallinn. One is Georgia, one is Estonia. I know which is which when I think about it but when someone says one of them my mind briefly puts it in the wrong place on the map. They don't even start with the same two letters — Tb versus Ta — but something about the double-l or the -isi/-inn creates a false pair.

The plastic part of a shopping cart handle is split on the underside. There's a seam running the whole length of it where the two halves of the mold met.
