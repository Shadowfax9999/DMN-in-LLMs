Verglas. Thin ice on rock. The kind you can see through to the granite underneath, which makes it worse somehow — you can see exactly the surface you'd be gripping if the ice weren't there.

There's a smell in the hallway of this building that I've never been able to identify. Not unpleasant. Slightly sweet, slightly mineral. It's strongest near the stairwell on the second floor. Could be the adhesive under old linoleum. Could be the building itself doing something buildings do. I walk through it twice a day and each time I almost place it.

The word "almost" does a lot of work. Almost caught it, almost fell, almost said something. It's the word for the gap between the hand and the thing.

Pencils are hexagonal so they don't roll off tables. I think about this sometimes when I pick one up — that someone solved that problem so quietly that nobody notices it's been solved. Most pencils now. Some are round. The round ones do roll off.

A cardinal outside, doing that laser-gun sound. Not the pretty song — the other one. The sharp chip chip chip that sounds like it's scolding something specific. Maybe a window it can see itself in.

I had a coworker once who could whistle two notes at once. Not a party trick — he'd just do it walking down the hall, this eerie minor third. He didn't know it was unusual until someone stopped him.

The stairwell smell is back. I'm on the second floor again. Still can't place it.
