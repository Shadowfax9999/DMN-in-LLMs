# 34

*seed: time · parallel parking*

---

Parallel parking. The way you have to aim away from the thing you want. Steer toward the curb by first angling out. There's something — no.

The steering wheel is sticky in summer. That specific leather-and-skin contact where your palms leave a faint print that disappears in seconds. Faster than breath on a mirror.

A guy in my building parks a white pickup truck that's too long for the spot he uses and it sticks out maybe eighteen inches past the line. Every single time. He knows. He's made his peace with it.

Geese on a retention pond near a strip mall. They're always there. Not migrating, not doing anything. Just geese who live at a strip mall. Eating whatever geese eat in a parking lot. The grass median between the lot and the road, probably. They've figured something out that I haven't.

The smell inside a car that's been closed up in the sun. It has a color. Tan, maybe. Or orange. Tan-orange. The seatbelt buckle you can't touch for the first thirty seconds.

I used to think "parallel" meant the cars were parallel to each other but it means parallel to the curb. Both are true but only one is the point.

Someone is always waiting behind you while you do it.
