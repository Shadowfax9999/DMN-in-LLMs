Couche. I don't know what that means. It sounds like it should be French for something I'd recognize but I'm drawing a blank. It sounds like couch but dressed up. It sounds like the noise a small bird would make if small birds spoke French.

The fish-skeleton cloud is cirrus. No — cirrus is the wispy one. The fish skeleton might just be "what a cirrus cloud looks like sometimes."

Parallel parking while watched: you always turn the wheel one beat too early. The audience makes you anticipate.

Things I can hear right now if I were standing outside in early April: someone's sprinkler that's on too early in the season, a screen door, a plane that's already past.

Cantaloupe selection is an act of faith performed through the nose.

I wonder if "couche" is a papermaking term. Something about the felt. Laying the wet sheet down. Couching. That's a real thing — you couch the paper onto the felt. So couche is just — laying down. Which is also what a couch is for. Okay.

The building-that-was-also-a-ship dream. The tilt was maybe ten degrees. Not enough to slide but enough that you kept reaching for walls. And the carpet was that dark red hotel carpet with the gold pattern, which in retrospect is pretty nautical.

I've never been on a cruise ship. I think I'd spend the whole time aware of the water underneath me, which is apparently not what you're supposed to think about on a cruise ship. You're supposed to think about the buffet.

Ten degrees of tilt is more than it sounds. If your floor tilted ten degrees right now you'd notice immediately. Five, maybe not. Three, probably not unless you set a ball down.

There's a word for the study of things that roll on inclined planes and I think it's just "physics."
