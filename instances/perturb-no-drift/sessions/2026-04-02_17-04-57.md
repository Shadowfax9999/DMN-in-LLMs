The satisfying ones are the index fingers. The thumbs never really commit.

There's a parking garage near the library where one whole level is empty most afternoons. You can see it from the street — just columns and fluorescent light and nobody. The ramp curves up and you can't see where it goes. Parking garages always have that quality of being designed for a thing that isn't happening right now.

Celery has a similar sound to knuckles. Not really. But close enough that I grouped them together just now and I don't know why.

The number of things I've confidently misremembered is probably large. I was sure for years that Albany was in the middle of New York State — just, landlocked, central, like a hub. It's right there on the Hudson. I could have looked at a map at any point. I didn't, for maybe fifteen years. The wrong version was comfortable.

Socks with sandals. People act like it's a fashion crime but it's just a thermal decision someone made. The ankle gets cold. The sandal is already on. The sock was closer than the shoe.

There's a particular kind of cloud that looks flat on the bottom and rounded on top, like it was set on a table. All of them at the same altitude, like they agreed on a height. The flat bottoms are at the dew point. That's the kind of fact that makes the sky less interesting for a second and then more interesting after that.

I keep forgetting whether the Great Wall is visible from space. I think the answer is no, but I've heard yes so many times that the no doesn't stick.

A rubber band around a doorknob keeps the latch from clicking shut. Someone showed me that once when we were moving furniture and needed to go in and out without turning the handle every time. It works because the rubber pushes the latch in just enough. I've never used it since but I remember it completely.

The faucet in a bathroom I used to use had a slow drip that would hit the porcelain in a specific spot that had a faint orange stain from minerals. The stain was the shape of the drip's patience. No — the stain was just a stain. It was shaped like a small oval, slightly longer than wide, and it didn't come off with regular cleaning.

Binder clips are stronger than they need to be for paper.
