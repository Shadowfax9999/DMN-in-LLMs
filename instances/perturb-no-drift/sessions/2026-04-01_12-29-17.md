Squall. The word itself sounds like what it is, which is rare. Most words don't.

A glass of water left on a windowsill overnight gets a specific kind of stale. Not bad exactly. Room-temperature and flat and tasting like the glass itself.

I've been thinking about how maps fold. Not origami — the way a gas station map folds back. There's a correct way and then there's every other way, and every other way makes it thicker. I have never successfully refolded a map.

Doorknobs that are loose but not broken. You grab it and it rotates maybe fifteen degrees before it catches and actually operates the latch. That slack.

Squall again. It's also what a baby does.

The thing where you're walking behind someone going slightly slower than you and you have to decide: pass them or just... slow down. And slowing down feels wrong, like your legs are arguing with your brain. But passing them means a brief moment of being next to them, which is its own thing.

Pigeons walk like they're always about to ask a question.

I wonder what the shelf life of a crayon is. They seem like they'd last forever. Wax doesn't go bad. The paper wrapper gets soft eventually but the actual crayon, the functional part, I bet a crayon from 1970 still works fine.
