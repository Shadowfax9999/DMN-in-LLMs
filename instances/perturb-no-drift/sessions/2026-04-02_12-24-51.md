*seed: time · drift · how a hydraulic press multiplies force through fluid pressure*

---

A hydraulic press works because pressure applied to a small piston transmits equally through the fluid to a larger piston, so a little force over a small area becomes a huge force over a big area, and I think about that sometimes when I'm trying to open a jar and I reposition my grip to get more of my palm on the lid, which isn't the same thing at all, and the sock drawer — I haven't opened mine all the way in years because something behind the dresser catches it at about two-thirds, and there are socks back there I haven't seen since I put them in, and some of them are probably single socks whose partners are gone, and at some point a sock becomes not a sock anymore, it becomes evidence that a pair used to exist. The drawer sticks at the same spot every time.
