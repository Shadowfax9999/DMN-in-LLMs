*seed: time · the way a wet dog smells different from a dry dog in rain*

---

The wet dog thing is specific. It's not just water plus dog. Something gets unlocked.

Cardboard does that too. Dry cardboard has almost no smell. Wet cardboard smells like it's confessing something.

I was trying to remember the name of a street earlier and what came instead was the color of a mailbox that was on it. Not the street name. The mailbox. It was one of those oversized rural ones, the kind on a post, and it was a green that had clearly started as black. The name of the street is still gone.

Socks with sandals. People have strong opinions about that. The opinion is always stronger than the thing deserves. It's footwear. But someone will bring it up at a dinner table like it's a moral position.

There's a particular kind of cup — heavy, slightly too thick at the rim, off-white — that every church basement has. I don't know who manufactures them. They must be made somewhere. There's a factory floor where those cups are just cups, not the vessel for weak coffee after a funeral.

The word "caulk" looks wrong no matter how many times you read it.

I think the recycling truck comes on Fridays here but I'm not sure because I've never once been the one to take the bin out in time. It just appears at the curb already. I don't know when that happens.

A rubber band that's been sitting in a drawer for a year snaps the first time you stretch it. The ones in active use last forever. Something about that feels backward but it's probably just how polymers work.

My left knee clicks when I go down stairs but not up.
