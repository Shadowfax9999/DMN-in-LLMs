Missed step. Not a step you failed to take but the one that isn't there — your foot expecting floor and finding air. The lurch.

It happens on staircases you know well. That's the part. Familiar stairs. Your body has counted them and gotten it wrong by one, or gotten it right but your weight shifted early. The stumble at the top when you think there's one more. The whole system briefly offline.

Parking garages have a version of this with cars. You walk toward where you parked and it's not there and for about two seconds your brain offers you nothing. Just blank. Then you remember you parked on three, not two, and everything reorganizes. But those two seconds.

The word "lurch" is good. It sounds like what it is.

I had a coworker once who said "I'm going to go ahead and" before every single thing she did. I'm going to go ahead and send that email. I'm going to go ahead and grab lunch. As if each action needed a small runway. I think about her sometimes when I notice how many unnecessary preparations people make before doing ordinary things. The little throat-clear before speaking. Adjusting glasses that haven't moved.

Rubber bands deteriorate faster in sunlight than in drawers. You find one in a junk drawer and it's still stretchy after years, but one left on a newspaper on the porch for a week just snaps.

The sound a screen door makes is different from inside than from outside. From inside it's the slap. From outside it's more of the spring — that long, thin whine before the slap catches up.

I think most people load the dishwasher wrong but I'm not confident my way is right either.
