# 71

*seed: time*

---

The toggle on a seatbelt — the red button — is always red. Every car. Every manufacturer. I've never seen a blue one or a grey one.

Somewhere in my mouth right now there's a canker sore starting. I can feel the spot where it's going to be before it arrives. The left side, lower lip, inside. It's not painful yet. It's just different. The tissue there feels aware of itself in a way the rest of the mouth doesn't.

Cardboard has a smell that gets stronger when it's humid. A stack of moving boxes in a garage in April. There's a reason for that but I'm not going to think about it, I'm just in the garage with the boxes and the lawnmower and a bag of grass seed that's been there since last year, and the grass seed bag has a picture of grass on it, which — of course it does.

The word "several" does a lot of quiet work. It's not "a few" and it's not "many." It occupies a band somewhere between four and eight, maybe, but no one would agree on the exact boundaries. If someone says "I've been there several times" you'd picture something different than "a few times" but you couldn't say what number changed.

I think most people's left foot is slightly longer than their right. Or the other way around. One of them is longer.

The little paper sleeve they put around a coffee cup. I always take it off. I don't know why. The cup is too hot without it but I take it off anyway and then I hold the cup by the lid, which is worse. Some habits are just habits — they don't come from preference, they come from the first time you did something and then you kept doing it.

There's a specific kind of cloud that looks flat on the bottom and puffy on top, and they all have the same flat bottom at the same altitude, like they're all sitting on a glass table you can't see.

I had a filling replaced once and the dentist said the old one was fine, it was just old. Twenty-three years old. I thought about that — something in my mouth older than some of my coworkers.

Rubber bands lose their elasticity just sitting in a drawer.
