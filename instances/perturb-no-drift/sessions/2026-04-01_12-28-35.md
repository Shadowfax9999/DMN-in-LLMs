# 1

*seed: time*

---

Tongs. The kind with the spring hinge, not the scissor kind. Picking up ice.

The way a jar lid resists and then doesn't.

How many times I've read the back of a shampoo bottle. Sodium laureth sulfate. I could probably spell it without checking. I have never once looked up what it does.

A cardinal sounds like a car alarm that went to music school.

The ceiling in a dentist's office is always the same ceiling. Acoustic tile, rectangular, with that pattern of tiny holes. You stare at it long enough and you start seeing faces in the grid. They put posters up sometimes. A beach. As if that helps.

Somebody somewhere is an expert on acoustic ceiling tile. That's their whole career.

The phrase "load-bearing wall" gets used metaphorically a lot by people who have never knocked on a wall to hear whether it's hollow.

Hollow walls sound like — actually I don't know how to describe that sound. A dull thock. Not quite a drum. The solid ones just go *tok* and your knuckles sting.

Cantaloupes. You're supposed to smell the stem end. I do this every time and I have no idea what I'm smelling for. I just pick the one that smells the most like cantaloupe, which is circular reasoning.

The way people parallel park when they know someone's watching versus when nobody is.

I had a dream once where I was in a building that was also somehow a ship but nobody mentioned it. The hallways tilted. Everyone acted normal. That's the most realistic part — everyone acting normal.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a fish skeleton. I forget what it's called.
