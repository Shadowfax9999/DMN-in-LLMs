*seed: time · the way a crowd thins at the edges*

---

A crowd thins at the edges the same way sleep does, the people at the periphery already half-somewhere-else. Or no — a crowd thins at the edges because that's where the commitment drops off. The center is for people who meant to be there.

There's a intersection near a hospital I used to pass where the crosswalk signal made a different sound than any other one I'd encountered. Not the standard chirp. More of a wooden knock, like someone tapping a cutting board. I never found out if that was an accessibility feature or a malfunction.

Capsaicin doesn't actually burn anything.

I've been thinking about how when you pull a fitted sheet out of the dryer it's already balled up in a way that's structurally similar to how you're going to store it anyway. The dryer does the work of giving up for you. Flat sheets come out flat and you fold them. Fitted sheets come out defeated and you just — agree.

The number of people who have touched a particular doorknob. Not a famous doorknob. The one on the supply closet at a medium-sized accounting firm in Dayton. Eleven years of hands. The brass is shinier where the thumb lands.

A pelican's pouch can hold three gallons. The stomach holds much less. So the pouch isn't storage — it's a net. The bird is fishing with its own face.

There's a particular kind of parking lot that only exists near community colleges. It's too big for the buildings it serves. The light poles are spaced for a lot that was supposed to be fuller. At 2 PM on a Tuesday half the lot is empty and the cars that are there have clustered near the entrance like they're cold.

My left knee makes a sound going down stairs that it doesn't make going up. Only down. Only the left one. It's been doing this for maybe four years and I've never mentioned it to anyone, which seems like either a reasonable choice or a strange one depending on how stairs work.

The supply closet doorknob in Dayton is not something I've ever seen. I made it up. But there is one, and the thumb-shine is there.
