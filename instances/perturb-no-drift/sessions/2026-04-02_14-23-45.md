Barometric pressure. The weight of air, which you never think about until your sinuses do.

There's a specific kind of headache that arrives before rain. Not during. Before. The rain is still twenty minutes away and your head already knows. Somebody at some point measured this — figured out the threshold where tissue starts to notice. I don't remember what it is.

The barometer in my grandfather's house was a brass circle on the wall near the coat hooks. I don't think anyone ever looked at it to actually learn the weather. It was decorative in the way that clocks in paintings are decorative — present, round, suggesting that time or pressure exists.

Coat hooks. The kind with two prongs that curve up like horns. You hang a jacket on one prong and a scarf sort of drapes over the other and then the whole thing falls at 2 AM and you think someone's in the house.

I wonder how many 911 calls are coats falling off hooks.

The air in a tire is under about 32 PSI, which is roughly twice atmospheric pressure. That seems low for something that holds up a car. Four palm-sized contact patches, each the size of a paperback book, and that's all that's between the car and the road. The rest is just shape — the arch of the tire doing structural work the way a Roman arch does, or doesn't, I'm not actually sure the analogy holds.

There's a bird outside that's been doing the same four-note phrase for about ten minutes. It keeps pausing like it's waiting for a response that isn't coming. Probably a cardinal. They do that — they just keep going. The pause isn't waiting, it's breathing, but it sounds like waiting.

My left knee has been stiff since I sat down. Thirty-two is probably too young for that but I don't actually know what age knee stiffness is supposed to start.
