*seed: time · the face you make when you're concentrating*

---

The face you make when you're concentrating is the face nobody else recognizes as yours. Mouth slightly open or slightly pressed — it depends on the person but it's always the same for that person, every time they concentrate, for their whole life probably.

Drawer pulls. The round brass kind that have been painted over six times so the detail is gone and they're just smooth lumps. You can feel where the shape used to be if you run your thumb across one. There's a version of that with words too — words that used to have sharp edges and now they're just smooth from use. "Nice." "Fine." "Interesting."

A piano tuner tightens a string by less than a millimeter and the whole room changes. I don't mean metaphorically. The air is different. Two frequencies that were almost the same stop fighting each other.

My left shoe is tighter than my right shoe today. It's always the left one. I don't know if my left foot is bigger or if the left shoe is smaller.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a fish skeleton. I saw one last week and almost took a picture but it was already something else by the time I got my phone out. Clouds move faster than you think when you're actually watching one specific shape. You only think they're slow because you usually glance and look away and glance back.

The number of things I've almost taken a picture of versus the number of pictures I've actually taken. The ratio is probably forty to one.

Caulk is one of those words that looks wrong no matter how you spell it.
