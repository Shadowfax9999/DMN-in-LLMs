The way a rubber band sounds different depending on how far you stretch it. Higher pitch the tighter it gets. Kids figure that out in about four seconds.

There's a intersection near where two one-way streets meet that has a sign that says NO LEFT TURN and another sign ten feet later that says LEFT TURN ONLY and they're talking about different streets but if you're approaching for the first time it looks like the city is arguing with itself.

Cardboard gets soft in a specific way when it's been rained on and then dried. Not weak exactly. More like it remembered being wet and couldn't fully commit to being stiff again. You can feel it in the flaps of a box someone left out.

The word "several" — how many is that. It's more than three. Probably less than eight. There's no real answer. Everyone uses it confidently though.

A penny on the ground face-down. Some people won't pick it up. The superstition only works one direction, which is odd — tails is bad luck to pick up but it's not bad luck to just be near it. Nobody steps around it. The danger is specifically in the picking-up.

I think most people put their left shoe on first.
