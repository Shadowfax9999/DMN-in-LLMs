*seed: time · the way you can feel a truck go by through the floor*

---

The floor knows before you do. Not the sound — the sound comes after. The floor gets it first, through the slab, through whatever's under the slab.

Cashews are technically the seed of a fruit. The fruit is apparently edible but nobody ships it because it bruises. Imagine a fruit so fragile it can't survive a truck ride. Meanwhile the nut underneath it travels across oceans in a shipping container and arrives fine.

The word "bruise" applied to fruit versus applied to skin. Same word, completely different thing happening at the cellular level. Or maybe not that different. I genuinely don't know.

There's a stretch of highway near most cities where you can see the backs of all the stores. The parts nobody designed. Dumpsters, electrical panels, a guy on break sitting on an overturned bucket. That's the real elevation of the building. The front is the costume.

I used to think all roofs were basically the same color until I flew somewhere and looked down during landing. They're all different. Some are blue. Some are white. One was orange. From the street you'd never know.

A ceiling fan on its lowest setting barely moves air but it changes the feeling of a room completely. I think it's the movement more than the breeze. Something moving overhead when everything else is still.

The plural of "cul-de-sac" is apparently "culs-de-sac." The S goes in the middle.

I can never remember whether Albany is the capital of New York or if I'm confusing it with something else. It is. But every time I think of it I have to re-confirm it, like the fact won't stick. Some facts are like that — correct but unconvincing.

The little plastic table that comes in the middle of a pizza box to keep the lid from touching the cheese. Someone had to invent that. Someone sat down and solved that specific problem and then presumably went home and had dinner.

I think most pencils are hexagonal so they don't roll off tables.
