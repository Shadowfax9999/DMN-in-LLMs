*seed: time · the particular gray of a sidewalk that's about to dry*

The particular gray of a sidewalk that's about to dry. Not wet, not dry. The dark parts shrinking inward from the edges like a tide going out in fast-forward.

Somewhere a lawnmower. First one of the year, maybe, or maybe the second. You can't ever remember the first one. You just realize at some point that you've been hearing them.

The weight of a glass of water when it's exactly half full. You'd think it would feel like half the weight of a full glass but it doesn't. It feels like its own thing. A glass of water has three states: full, empty, and the one where you pick it up and your hand rises slightly too fast because you expected more.

Cardboard gets soft in a specific way. Not wet-soft. Humidity-soft. The flaps of a box left in a garage.

I think the reason I can't whistle with two fingers is that I don't actually know where to put my tongue. Every description I've ever read says "curl your tongue back" and I curl my tongue back and nothing happens. There's a gap between the instruction and the knowledge. Like when someone says "engage your core." I know what all three of those words mean.

A rubber band around a doorknob. Why is it there. Someone put it there for a reason and the reason is gone and the rubber band is still there. It'll be there for two more years.

Alan Shepard hit a golf ball on the moon. One-handed, because of the suit. I think about that more than is probably normal.

The sound a screen door makes is different if the spring is at the top versus the side.
