# 15

*seed: time · a book whose spine you recognize before its title*

---

Spackle.

The word just sitting there. Not the thing, not the act, the word. Spackle.

A thermostat in a hallway that nobody adjusts but everybody reads. 68. Always 68. You look at it and keep walking. Why do you look at it.

Forks have four tines and nobody ever asks why not three or five. Some have three. Salad forks? No, those still have four, they're just smaller. Oyster forks have three. I think. The oyster fork is the only fork that goes on the right side of the plate. That's a rule I learned from a placemat at a restaurant when I was maybe ten, the kind of placemat that has facts printed on it, state capitals and table settings, and I remember the table setting better than most of the capitals.

Montpelier. That one always sticks. Vermont.

The inside of a mailbox is a temperature you never think about. Hotter than outside in summer. The mail sits in there getting warm. Someone's birthday card slowly heating up in a dark metal box.

Caulk and spackle are different things and I mix them up every time. One fills cracks in walls and one seals edges around bathtubs. Or both fill cracks but in different materials. The caulk gun is the one with the trigger.

A ceiling fan on low doesn't move air so much as just remind you it's there. The pull chain taps against the light fixture in a rhythm that isn't quite regular.

Carpets in office buildings have that specific flatness. Not soft. Flat. The kind of carpet that exists so the floor isn't concrete.

I had a pen in high school that had four colors and you'd click down the one you wanted. The blue one always ran out first.
