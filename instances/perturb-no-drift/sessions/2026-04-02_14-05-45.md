# 80

*seed: time · drift · how a lock and key work through ward cuts and pin tumbler heights*

---

If you cut a key wrong by one thousandth of an inch, the pin sits too high and the cylinder won't turn. If you think about that, a key is just a sentence the lock already knows.

If the garage door is open the echo changes completely. If it's closed you get that flat concrete sound back.

If you hold a key up and look at the cuts along the edge they look like a tiny mountain range. If a locksmith looks at the same key they see numbers. Five or six numbers, each one a height.

If the spring pushes the driver pin down and the key pushes it back up and they meet exactly at the shear line, that one pin is solved. If all five pins hit the shear line at once, the whole plug rotates. If even one is off, nothing.

If I'm honest I don't know what a ward is. I know ward cuts are older. I know they're not the same as pin tumblers. If someone handed me a warded lock I think I'd recognize it by the keyhole shape — the old-fashioned kind, the kind you can look through.

If you can look through a keyhole the door is probably old enough that the hinges are exposed on the outside too.

If the hinges are exposed you don't need to pick the lock at all.

If you're thinking about how to get through a door you've already decided something.

If the ceiling fan is on in the other room I can hear it from here but only the lowest speed makes that ticking sound. If it's on medium or high it just hums.

If two people are walking toward each other on a sidewalk and both step the same direction to avoid each other, they'll do it again. If one of them just stops, the other one figures it out immediately.

If you squeeze a lime wedge into a glass the oils spray out separately from the juice. If you watch closely the oils hit the surface of the drink and sit there for a second before they mix in.

If a pin tumbler has six pins instead of five the key is harder to pick but not six-fifths harder. More like twice as hard. If I'm remembering that right.

If the oven has been off for an hour it still clicks sometimes.
