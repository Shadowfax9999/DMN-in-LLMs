The way a rubber band sounds different depending on how far you stretch it. You can play a whole scale on one if you hold it between your teeth and pluck. Someone must have figured that out by accident.

Caulk never comes out of the tube the way you think it will. You cut the tip at an angle, like every tutorial says, and then the bead comes out too fast or too slow and you end up with this lumpy ridge that you smooth with a wet finger and it still looks wrong. Painters do it in one clean pass. I don't understand the finger pressure that requires.

There's a particular kind of cloud that looks like it was smeared with a palette knife. Cirrus, maybe. They move faster than they look like they're moving — you think they're still but if you check back in ten minutes the whole arrangement is different. Meanwhile cumulus clouds actually do move visibly, piling up over themselves like something boiling in slow motion.

A friend once told me that the reason old books smell good is the lignin in the paper breaking down into vanillin. I have no idea if the chemistry is right but the claim is satisfying in a way that makes me suspicious of it.

The cap on a Bic pen has a hole in it so you don't suffocate if you swallow it. That's one of those facts that feels like it should be an urban legend but apparently isn't. The hole is really there. You can blow through it.

Pencil graphite and diamond are the same element arranged differently. That one still gets me.
