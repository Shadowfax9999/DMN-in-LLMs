# 58

*seed: time · the moment before a sneeze*

---

The moment before a sneeze is the closest most people get to a system crash. Everything halts. You can't talk, can't think about anything else, your eyes close involuntarily. The whole organism just — suspends.

Sometimes it doesn't come. That's worse. You're left standing there with your face half-organized around an event that didn't happen. Like arriving at a door that turns out to be painted on a wall.

Painted doors. There's a whole category of architectural trompe l'oeil where someone paints a door on a bricked-up archway so the facade still looks symmetrical. I think there are famous ones in Italy. The commitment to visual balance over function — spending money to paint a door no one can use so that someone standing across the street sees the right number of doors.

Symmetry matters more from a distance. Up close you notice the hinges are wrong.

I had to replace a hinge once and the screws had been painted over so many times they were just smooth bumps. You could feel the Phillips head shape under the paint if you scraped with a thumbnail. Layers of color in the scratch — white, then cream, then a surprising green, then white again. Someone chose green once.

The word "sternutatory" means something that causes sneezing. Pepper is sternutatory. Dust. Bright light, for some people — the photic sneeze reflex, which is genetic, which means some families sneeze when they walk outside and some don't, and neither group believes the other group is telling the truth.

A hinge that's been painted over enough times won't close all the way.
