Caulking. The word sounds like it should be about birds.

There's a specific kind of tired where you're not sleepy, you're just done processing. Like a register that's full. You sit down and your eyes are open and you're looking at the middle distance and nothing is happening in there. Then five minutes later you realize you've been thinking about whether Rhode Island has a coastline. It does. Obviously it does.

The middle distance isn't a place. It's a focal length. Your eyes are aimed at something twelve feet away but they're not reporting back.

Coat hooks shaped like animal heads. Those exist in a lot of houses built in the 1970s. Brass deer. Brass duck. Always in the entryway. The screws go through the drywall into nothing and eventually the whole thing pulls out and leaves two holes.

I think I could identify a French horn by sound but not by sight if it were disassembled. Just a lot of tubing. A trombone disassembled would be easier — the slide is the slide no matter what.

The phrase "no matter what" is strange if you slow down on it. No matter what. As in: regardless of what matter, what material, what stuff is involved. But nobody means that when they say it.
