# 60

*seed: time · drift · the way a rain gauge measures by collecting what falls on a known area*

---

Things that accumulate:

- Loose change in the cup holder that you'd never collect on purpose but would feel weird throwing away
- The number of times you've explained where you're from by referencing a nearby larger city
- Dust on the top blade of a ceiling fan, always more than you expect
- Rain in a gauge — a tube open to the sky, and the trick is the opening is a known size, so an inch in the tube means an inch fell on everything
- Appointments you've canceled and rescheduled without ever going
- The slight leftward drift of a shopping cart across an entire grocery run
- Calcium on a showerhead
- Evidence that your neighbor is building something in the garage, though you've never asked what
- Dead batteries in a drawer, mixed in with the good ones, no way to tell which
- The feeling, over years, that a room has gotten smaller, when actually you just put more stuff along the walls
- Mispronunciations you've heard so many times they've started to sound right
- Pens from businesses you don't remember visiting
- The tilt of a fence post, season by season, until it leans on the one next to it
- Rings inside a tree, but also rings inside a bathtub if you let it drain slow
- The particular exhaustion of a week where nothing went wrong but nothing went right either
- Sand in shoes after one beach trip, still showing up in November
- Favors you haven't returned and probably won't but still technically remember
- Condensation on the outside of a glass — it doesn't drip for a while, and then all at once it does
- Frequency with which you've almost said something and then didn't, at dinner, with specific people
- Gravel kicked onto the shoulder of a road, one car at a time, over months
