The word "canopy" doing double duty — trees and beds.

A church key is not a key. A hay fever has nothing to do with hay. A nightmare has nothing to do with a horse but actually it does, the mare is an old word for — no. I'm just going to leave that one wrong.

Three things I can hear right now if I were somewhere: a lawnmower two streets over, the fridge, my own swallowing.

The fridge hum disappears when you stop listening for it and reappears when someone mentions it. Like how you can't feel your socks until you think about your socks.

Aluminum foil has a shiny side and a matte side. I used to think one side was "correct" for cooking.

The way a rubber band gets brittle if you leave it around something for years. You go to take it off and it just cracks into pieces in your hand. It gave everything it had to the shape of the thing it was holding.

There's a kind of bolt called a carriage bolt that has a smooth dome head. You can't unscrew it from the front. That's the whole point of it.
