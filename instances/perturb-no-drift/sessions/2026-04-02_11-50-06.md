*seed: time · drift · how a carburetor mixes air and fuel by venturi effect*

---

- Air to fuel in a carburetor: 14.7 to 1. Fourteen parts air for every one part gasoline. The engine breathes mostly nothing.

- The venturi narrows and the air speeds up and the pressure drops and the fuel gets pulled in sideways. The ratio of throat diameter to bore diameter determines how hard it sucks.

- Water to rice: 2 to 1, or 1.5 to 1, depending on who taught you.

- The ratio of time spent looking for a parking spot to time spent inside the store. Sometimes 1:1.

- Flour to butter in a roux: equal by weight. You stir it and stir it and at some point it stops being two things.

- The proportion of a phone call that's actual information versus the parts where you're both figuring out how to hang up.

- Concrete: 1 part cement, 2 parts sand, 3 parts gravel. The cement is the smallest fraction and it's the only one anyone names.

- How much of a song you need to hear before you know what song it is. Sometimes one note. Sometimes the whole first verse and you're still not sure.

- The ratio of drawer space taken up by the thing you opened the drawer for versus the stuff that's just in there.

- Pupil to iris when you walk outside: the pupil shrinks to maybe a fifth of the iris diameter. The muscle that does this has no name you'd remember.

- Popcorn: roughly 40 to 1 by volume, unpopped to popped. A tablespoon becomes a bowl.

- The proportion of a key that actually does the work inside the lock. Maybe a third. The rest is just handle.

- How much of a conversation you remember the next day. Less than you think but more than nothing.

- The ratio of time a traffic light is green to the time it feels green.

- In a venturi, if you narrow the pipe to half its diameter, the air moves four times as fast. Area is squared. That's where the pull comes from.

- Salt to everything else in bread dough: about 2%. Two percent and you can taste it in every bite.
