# 26

*seed: time · the way someone walks differently when they're carrying something fragile*

---

Carrying a casserole dish changes your center of gravity but it also changes your face. Both hands occupied, eyes slightly down, and the walk becomes this deliberate thing where each step is placed instead of taken.

Elevators have a smell that isn't any one thing. It's the aggregate of everyone who was in there today plus the cleaning product from last night plus the specific warmth of a small box that opens and closes.

The word "aggregate" doing a lot of work just now.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a ceiling — flat, gray, uniform, no feature to grab. I never know what those are called. Stratus, maybe. They make noon look like 4 PM.

A penny is heavier than you think when you actually hold one and pay attention. Or lighter. I can't decide which direction the surprise goes. You have an idea of what a penny weighs and then the real penny doesn't match it, but I genuinely can't remember which way.

Scissors feel different in the left hand. Not worse, necessarily. Just like the scissors are suddenly aware of you.

I think I own three rolls of tape and I don't know where any of them are.
