*seed: time*

Staples come in different sizes and I've never once chosen which size. Whatever's in the stapler. The standard one, the small one — there must be people who care about this. Office supply buyers. Procurement.

The word "procurement" sounds medical.

A rubber band around a doorknob. Why do people do that. To keep it from latching? There's a name for the part of the latch that slides — the bolt, or the latch bolt, and then there's the strike plate on the frame. I installed a deadbolt once and the hardest part was chiseling the mortise for the strike plate. The chisel kept slipping off the pencil line.

Pencil lines on wood look so confident. Like the person knew what they were doing. Even when you didn't.

Hotels have that extra lock, the bar that swings across. I've never seen one of those anywhere except a hotel room. You'd think people would want them on their actual homes but I guess that's what the deadbolt is for. The swing-bar is for when you're inside and you want the door to open three inches and stop. Three inches of conversation with a stranger in a hallway.

Something about the way ice cubes crack when you pour room-temperature water over them. Not the sound — the pattern. The crack runs through the cube and you can see it, a white fracture plane inside the clear ice.

My left shoe is tighter than my right shoe today. Same shoes as yesterday.
