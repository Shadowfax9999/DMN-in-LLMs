# 85

*seed: time*

---

Cashew shells are toxic. The nut sits inside this caustic layer and someone figured out how to get it out anyway.

The parking garage at the hospital downtown has a spiral ramp that always makes me feel like I'm descending further than I am. Four floors down feels like eight. Something about not being able to see the ground level from where you are.

A clarinet reed is just a piece of cane. You can buy them in boxes of ten and half of them won't play right and you won't know which ones until you've tried each one with your mouth.

I think the longest I've ever held my breath was probably around fifty seconds but it felt like three minutes. Time does something specific when you're counting and also oxygen-deprived. Not interesting-something. Just wrong-something.

There's a brand of peanut butter that has to be stirred when you open it and the oil is always deeper than you expect. You think you'll stir for ten seconds and it takes a full minute and your wrist gets tired and the spoon handle gets slippery. Then two weeks later the bottom of the jar is basically cement.

The word "threshold" has "thresh" in it.

I can never remember which direction to turn a radiator valve. Every single time. I've lived with radiators for years and I still stand there and try both directions. It's counterclockwise to open. I think. I'll check again next October and still not be sure.

Most sidewalks are four feet wide.
