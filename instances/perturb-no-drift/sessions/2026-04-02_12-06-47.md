*seed: time*

---

The faucet in the upstairs bathroom has always dripped in pairs. Two drops close together, then a pause. Two more. I don't know if that's a washer thing or a pressure thing.

Someone at work uses the phrase "net net" and I can never tell if it means something different from "net." The word "moist" bothers people but "net net" bothers me more. There's a confidence to saying the same word twice that I find suspicious.

Orthotics. The word looks medical but the thing itself is just a shoe insert. You pay forty dollars for a piece of foam that costs maybe two dollars to manufacture and the packaging says "biomechanical" on it. The checkout person never comments on what you're buying but they always seem to with orthotics. Or maybe that's just me noticing because I felt weird about it.

I can never remember which direction to turn a jar lid when it's upside down. My hands know, but the moment I think about it consciously I'm lost. I have to flip it right-side up, remember which way is open, then flip it back. Sometimes I just hold it upside down and let my hands do it without watching.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a fish skeleton. I saw one last week and spent maybe thirty seconds trying to remember the cloud name and then gave up. Cirrus something. Or not cirrus. The fish-skeleton ones are thin and repeating.

The number of things I've given up trying to remember in the last year is probably larger than the number of things I've successfully remembered.

Cashews are expensive and I buy them anyway. Almonds are fine but they're not cashews.
