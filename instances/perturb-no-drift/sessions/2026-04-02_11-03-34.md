*seed: time · the sound of someone else chewing*

---

The sound of someone else chewing is unbearable in a way that your own chewing never is. Both are the same sound. Both are happening the same distance from your ear — no, that's not true, your own is closer. Your own is inside your skull. And yet.

There's a word for the rage version of that. Misophonia. Which sounds like it should mean hatred of music but doesn't. Hatred of sound, or specific sounds, or — actually I think it's more particular than hatred, it's more like the sound bypasses the part of you that decides how to feel and goes straight to the part that's already furious.

Pencils. The mechanical ones where you click the top and a tiny amount of lead advances. 0.5mm or 0.7mm. I used 0.5 for years and then switched to 0.7 and the line felt luxurious. Like going from a twin bed to a full. That's maybe a three-millimeter difference in line width at most. The pencil doesn't care. The paper doesn't care. But my hand knew immediately.

There's a hallway in every school that's just slightly too narrow for the traffic it carries. Two people can walk side by side but a third person coming the other way turns it into a negotiation. Someone has to go single file. The question of who drops back is never spoken about and never resolved by any rule — it just happens, and sometimes it happens wrong and there's that shuffle.

Aluminum. The British say aluminium with an extra syllable in it, and neither pronunciation sounds like a metal. Both sound like a flower.

I think cashews grow hanging off the bottom of a fruit. The fruit is on top and the nut hangs below it like a comma. Or maybe I have that reversed. The fruit might be the comma. One of them is kidney-shaped and the other is bell-shaped and they're attached at a single point.

The click when you turn off an old TV and the screen contracts to a single bright dot in the center before it goes dark. That dot lasted longer than you'd expect.
