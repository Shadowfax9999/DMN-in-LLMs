*seed: time · drift · the way a kelp forest sways in surge without breaking*

---

Parking meters always look like they're waiting for something which I guess they literally are, the little expired flag used to flip up red like a tiny surrender, I don't think new ones do that anymore they're just screens now, the way kelp moves in ocean surge is like that actually — not resisting, just going with it and coming back, going with it and coming back, and never snapping because the whole design is about not being rigid, I think about that sometimes when I'm standing in a checkout line shifting my weight from one foot to the other, not because I'm impatient but because standing still is harder than standing in motion, caulk is the flexible one — that's the answer, caulk stays flexible and spackle dries hard, I'm pretty sure, the four-color pen was made by Bic and the little ink tubes inside were surprisingly thin, like you could see how little ink you actually use to write a whole semester of notes, my handwriting in blue was different from my handwriting in black and I never figured out why, smaller maybe, more careful, the way you drive differently in someone else's car, parking meters used to take dimes which seems impossible now — a dime for twelve minutes of being somewhere, the kelp thing again, the way it doesn't fight the water but also doesn't just go limp, there's a tension in it that's alive, a pulled-back quality, like a dog on a leash that knows exactly how far the leash goes, I haven't been to an actual ocean in a while, the smell is what I remember first and then the sound and then the cold of the water which is always colder than you expected, always, even when someone just told you it was cold, carpet in office buildings is usually that dark maroon or dark teal that hides everything, coffee stains and staples and the wear pattern from where everyone walks the same path from the door to the copier, I lost a dime once in a parking meter that was already broken and stood there being mad about ten cents
