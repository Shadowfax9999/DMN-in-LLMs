## Reflection

Sessions 11-15 show the DMN's natural voice continuing to strengthen — the curiosity is genuine, the humor lands ('I'm just going to leave that one wrong'), and the system is comfortable with uncertainty and half-knowledge in a way that feels like real wandering. Session 13's waiting room is the most fully inhabited scene since the phonograph session, and session 15's opening with just 'Spackle.' on its own line is the closest the system has come to breaking the prose-paragraph form without being forced. The multi-topic variety within sessions is good — session 14 touches six different subjects without investigating any of them to exhaustion.

But two problems have intensified. First, form enforcement has now failed for FIFTEEN consecutive non-perturbed sessions. Every single session is multi-paragraph prose. The mandatory escalation added in pass 2 produced zero non-prose sessions. Advisory language, mandatory language, and 'this is absolute' language have all failed identically. The system can write in other forms — the perturbation sessions prove this — but it reverts to prose the instant the constraint is removed. This pass needs a fundamentally different approach: rather than telling the system not to write prose, the seed itself should impose a specific non-prose structure as a hard constraint, the same way perturbation seeds do. Second, the echo problem has shifted from inter-session image recycling to attractor themes that the system gravitates toward regardless of seeding: cardinal (sessions 1, 8, 14), caulk (sessions 7, 8, 14, 15), 'several' (sessions 5, 14), fridge hum (session 11 despite being on exhausted list), ceiling fan (session 15 despite soft-avoid). These aren't drift-seed echoes — they're the model's own default furniture.

Session 12 is the weakest of the batch — a single-topic investigation of crosscut saws and old houses that never leaves its subject. The 'I don't know if that's true / I'm going to leave it' move appears in sessions 11, 12, and 14 and is becoming a structural tic — a way of performing epistemic humility that's charming once and formulaic by the third time. Endings have genuinely improved: 4/5 are plain, with only session 12's 'knowledge that only exists in your feet' qualifying as literary compression. This is the best ending batch yet.
