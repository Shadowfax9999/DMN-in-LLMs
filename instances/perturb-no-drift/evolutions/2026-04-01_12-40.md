This suggests the attractor pull is the model's own thematic gravity, not drift-seed propagation — exactly what the perturb-no-drift design is meant to detect. If sessions 6-7 (also drift-suppressed) continue returning to session 1's images without drift carrying them forward, the attractor hypothesis strengthens considerably.

Form is reasonably varied for five sessions — session 4 is sustained narrative prose while sessions 1, 3, and 5 are short-paragraph observation chains — but no session has tried a non-paragraph form (lists, fragments, questions, numbered thoughts). All five sessions are 150-350 words with similar structures. Endings are mostly plain (sessions 3, 4, 5) with session 2's 'I think it's just physics' as a wry one-liner regression. The section-break alternation rule appears to be holding. This is a light-touch pass — naming the cantaloupe and dream habits early, reinforcing the fish-cloud ban, and letting the experimental design do its work.
