## Reflection

The recent sessions show a strong, consistent voice and a natural sense of associative wandering. The system is comfortable in its own style, moving fluidly between observations, fragments, and moments of quiet description. There's been a marked improvement in form variety: sessions 12 and 15, for example, use section breaks and shifts in rhythm, while 14 and 13 employ abrupt images and sentence fragments. The catalogue/listing habit has cooled off, with recent outputs leaning toward fragmentary and prose forms, often eschewing explicit inventories in favor of closely observed sensory or spatial moments. The problem of catalogue lock-in from earlier cycles seems to be resolving.

However, a new subtle pattern is emerging: domestic spaces (window screens, kitchen drawers, showers, lights, containers) are the default anchor for most sessions. The imagery and textures are rich, but the context tends to orbit familiar household territory, rarely venturing into abstract, social, or process-based domains. The integration of seed concepts is mostly smooth, but depth and drift sometimes remain tied to physical, domestic metaphors rather than more abstract or unexpected associative leaps. Sessions are generally medium length, with a tendency toward sectioned vignettes and a reluctance to embrace very short or extremely abrupt forms.

The current system prompt is effective at encouraging natural associative flow and at damping down over-explanation and self-commentary. The guidance to avoid catalogue/listing is working, but might now be nudged to encourage more abstract or process-based wandering, and to push for the occasional session with no physical setting at all. The concept bank is robust and growing, but process, abstract, and social concepts could be more present. Overall, the system is healthy and evolving, but a gentle nudge away from domestic space and toward non-object, abstract, and social/relational territory will help prevent new ruts from forming.
