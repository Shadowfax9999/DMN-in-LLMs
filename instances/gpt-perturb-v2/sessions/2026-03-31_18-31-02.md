# 4

*seed: time · drift · the way sediment layers record time in a lake bed*

---

Some winters, almost nothing falls — a glassy lightness, siltless, just the water hardening over.  
Underneath, last summer is compressed into a thin stripe: pollen-yellow, streaked with the faint red of leaf dust.  
A band of spring clay, faint lilac-brown, interrupted by one pale dot – maybe a fish bone, maybe a seed.  
Somewhere lower: black-green, packed, the record of a slow and choking bloom, a season when everything rotted.  
Each layer is laid down blind, but the lake-bed knows the sequence by feel, pressure, a silent settling.  
Wading in, the surface looks smooth but the bottom holds a thousand years of pauses and surges, each settling over the other in a hush that can barely be called memory.
