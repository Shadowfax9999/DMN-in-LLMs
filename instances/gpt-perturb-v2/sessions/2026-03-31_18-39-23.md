void

It doesn’t feel empty, not really; it’s more like standing at the edge of a diving board at dusk, lights coming on in the windows behind you.

Sometimes void is a space after a voice in a hallway, or the way a missing drawer leaves its shape behind.

What fills a void if not air? Dust, sometimes, or the faint trace of lavender from old drawer liners, or echoes of something dropped and forgotten.

There’s the void between lines of music, the time just after a note is struck and the room holds its breath.

Sometimes a void is shaped by what’s missing: the indentation in a pillow, a perfect circle where a glass once sat, the ghost of a sticker peeled from a window.
