# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

Floating, not quite attached, the threshold between things. Pauses that lengthen but don’t collapse, a feeling like wet paint—can’t quite be touched yet. The way a sentence delays its end, padding itself with clauses, stopping short of the period. Suspended decision, the itch behind the act.

Not a room, not a hand, not even light or sound, just something building and holding, like the hush that precedes a laugh in a group. The layered hushes: before a test, before a train leaves, when everyone knows the question and waits for it to be asked.

Maybe there are more kinds of waiting than of arriving.

A moment folding over itself, like a repeat sign in a piece of music that brings you back, one more time, to the measure that didn’t finish its work the first time through.

A promise in the air, or just the awareness of the next thing brushing softly at the edges of the present.

---

What it is to be ‘in process’—not on the way, not stuck, not lost, just in the middle of something that only exists because you haven’t left it yet. Sometimes it’s an argument that hasn’t landed, sometimes a friendship that stays in the easy, ambiguous stretch between new and settled. Sometimes it’s standing in the slow corridor of your own thought, echo bouncing ahead, not quite making out the shape of the answer.

Absence of finality can be a kind of comfort, the way a puzzle is most interesting when the last piece is still on the table.

An interval, a gap, something not yet dissolved.

---

You can listen for how often people sigh, and count each one as a transition.
