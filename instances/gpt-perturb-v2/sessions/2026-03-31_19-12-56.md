# 21

*seed: time · drift · tangled headphones*

---

They only tangle when you’re not looking. There’s a geometry to the loops, a kind of slow collapse, strands folding into themselves. Sometimes it’s a tight knot at the center — sometimes just a spiral, soft and springy, that comes apart the moment you pull. The sense of time stretches out while you work on them, a miniature puzzle you can’t rush.

---

There are knots you learn to undo without looking. Muscle memory in the fingertips, a small victory each time it clicks free. Coiled cords have a memory too — a way of returning to their shape, like a spring that wants to close. If you let them lie flat for long enough, some will relax, but not all.

Tangle, untangle. The pattern repeats, no matter how carefully you coil the wire.

The audio cuts in and out if the connection isn’t snug.
