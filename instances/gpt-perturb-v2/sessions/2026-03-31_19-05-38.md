# 18

*seed: time · drift · doldrums*

---

Doldrums: air too still, nothing catching, every effort a ripple that doesn’t travel far. Not even the hum of a stalled engine, more like a lake just before something drops in.

When I picture “doldrums” I think of ships, sails limp, canvas flapping like laundry in a basement—there must be a color to that feeling, maybe a dull blue, not the kind of blue that promises cold or drama, but the kind that sits on a wall in a waiting room.

It’s not boredom exactly, but the absence of the thing that makes direction feel available. When sound becomes part of the furniture. Sometimes a day at its midpoint, the part before lunch where nothing is being decided.

There are doldrums in relationships too: when you’re neither drifting apart nor surging together, just repeating the moves you know, the pattern holding steady but thin.

I think of songs that lose momentum mid-verse—something unresolved, voices still taking turns, but the chorus doesn’t come.

In the doldrums, ambition is muted. Not gone, just suspended. No urgency, but no solution.

Some words are shaped for stasis. Doldrums. Languor. Quagmire. Pause. The mouth lingers on them, as if even pronouncing them slows you down.

The cat sits with its tail twitching, waiting for something to happen.
