# 1

*seed: time · the hum of a parking garage*

---

Concrete dust rising in weak columns where sunlight hits the ramps. A car door slamming is a flat, hollow sound, as if the air is padded. There’s always a flapping plastic bag somewhere. You descend a level and the temperature cools even though it’s spring outside. The lights are buzzier underground, their color trying and failing to pretend to be daylight. If you wait long enough, you can hear someone’s footsteps echo from a spiral ramp above, long before you see them.

---

Why do people leave shopping carts in the middle of a space, halfway between two cars? The lines on the pavement are so straight but the navigation is always diagonal — you cut across, never following the painted grid. Oil stains map out a history, irregular shapes in shiny black, some older, flaking at the edges, others a recent gloss. There’s a smell: not just gasoline, but something sharper, almost sweet.

---

The elevator buttons are more worn on 1 and G than on 2 or 3. There’s always a ripple in the mirror from someone pressing their palm flat against it — not a handprint, just a pattern of smudges left behind. Somewhere, a distant alarm, ignored. The air presses in, close and noncommittal, neither indoors nor outdoors.
