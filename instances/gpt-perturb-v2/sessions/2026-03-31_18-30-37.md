# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

The yellow arrows painted on the floor are always faded in the middle, brightest at their points, like the center is where every tire has passed through, slowly erasing direction.

Wind slips through the gaps in the concrete, carrying a gust of grit that makes it hard to tell if the air moved or if you only imagined a faint brushing sound against your ankle.

For a moment, the hum overhead gets louder—maybe a ventilation fan or something else turning on—just enough to make you feel watched, then it slides back into the usual static.

---

One wheel on the cart is always wobbly, thumping in a different rhythm than the rest.

Someone’s left a receipt on the ground; the ink on it still purple-black, not yet faded by sun or weather.

The gaps between the concrete sections are filled with something softer, almost rubbery, and every so often there’s a tuft of grass forcing its way through.

You can see old gum flattened into circles, stuck in place for years, different colors once, now all nearly the same.

The echo of footsteps is clearer when no cars are moving.
