# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

Odd how dust accumulates behind things that don’t move. The strip of floor between the wall and the bookshelf always a little gray, a little untouched.

If you press your ear to the thin pane of glass, the outside rearranges: birds stutter, a car’s engine dips, footsteps blend into a kind of underwater shuffle.

The hum from the overhead light flickers just above hearing range. Sometimes it’s louder when you’re trying to listen for something else.

A peach bruise on the arm, thumb-shaped, barely visible.

You can feel when rain is coming; it’s not in your skin but in a kind of hush that the day takes on, like it’s holding back.

There are words that never feel finished, no matter how many times you say them.
