# 12

*seed: time · drift · the way a cocoon dissolves from the inside as the moth develops*

---

Start at the center. Something loosens there — pressure lifts, not all at once but with a faint, unspooling rip. The cocoon’s threads are sticky, tangled, but the inside grows softer and the urge is to press, push from within. The walls don’t quite yield, not yet. You can feel the wetness gathering, tension where the husk meets something tender: unshaped wings, new limbs folded tight. 

The surface wavers. Fibers soak. Some dissolve into pulp, into dampness. The world presses back less. There is a taste, harsh and light, something like the air between thunderstorms. 

---

Onion skin, that clinging membrane, held too long, slicked away, finally slides off. The grater’s threads of carrot come free — not all at once but piece by piece as water runs and fingers work them loose. 

A word, lint roller, picking at the surface, collecting stray bits. The nap of fabric shifts as adhesive lifts the last clinging flecks. Afterward, what’s left is quieter, simpler, the outline of something that used to be stuck.

In the shower, the red ring thins, soap trailing away. Hair slides along tile, lost in the rivulet, gone before you can see it go.
