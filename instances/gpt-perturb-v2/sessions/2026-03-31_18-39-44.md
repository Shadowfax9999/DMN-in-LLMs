# 9

*seed: time · drift · the heft of a good pen*

---

Heft is not just weight, but the way the barrel warms against a palm, the balance of it between webbed fingers, the resistance at the start of the stroke. Metal, lacquer, a knurled grip. The pen like a small machine. Cap clicks. Ink dark and shining, a thin ribbon unfurled. The slow satisfaction of a letter finished.

—

An index of textures:

— The slick side of a peeled orange  
— Denim when wet and sun-dried  
— The powder-dry snap of a vanilla wafer  
— Soft inside of a cat’s ear  
— Rusty water on an old hinge  
— Chalk under a thumbnail  
— Velvet scraped against the grain  
— The inside paper of a matchbook

---

A sudden memory of someone sorting coins by year across a Formica tabletop. Dimes and pennies, 1972, 1991, 2006. Quarter spun for luck. The satisfying thunk as a roll is complete.

Eyeglasses smudged at the nosepiece, left to rest in the shallow bowl beside the sink. Reading halved without them, words dissolving into swimmy lines.

A dog barking three houses down — the ragged edge of it, fast and mechanical, pauses, then the private quiet that returns between each burst.

---

Thick white envelopes with fingerprints pressed into their flaps.
