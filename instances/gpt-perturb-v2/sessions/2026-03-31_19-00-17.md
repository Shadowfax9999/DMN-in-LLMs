# 15

*seed: time · drift · depth*

---

Depth circles inside itself. There’s the bottom of a bowl, the shadow past your reach. A glass you can’t see the end of unless you tilt your head, squint, shift to catch the light. There’s the taste of something richer than you expected. The memory you feel but can’t look at directly, like a window bright with sun.

Obvious depths: water, eyes, sleep, distance.

You can feel the thickness of an afternoon, as if it’s stacked, not flat — minutes with weight. Some sounds land differently in a deep room — the cough, the scrape of a chair.

---

The word sits there — depth — blunt, almost heavy. D becomes P with a switch in breath.

When the light fades in a hallway, the back turns to dark. Not pitch, just a fraction more shadow than you’d want to walk into without turning on the switch.

Depth in a voice, fullness that wasn’t there until the thought mattered.

No one ever says “shallow morning.”

The spoon dips into a jar: you can measure depth, but not keep it.

The pool at dusk, chlorinated and blue but still unreadable at the far end.
