# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

A row of calendula seeds, misshapen and bristled, half-moon curls nested in the palm. The ridges catch on skin, impossible to pick up one at a time without dropping several.

Piano keys that resist your touch on humid days, sticking at the edges, faintly gritty under the pads of your fingers.

A string knotted and re-knotted until it puckers the hem, soft with fray, refusing to lie flat.

---

Blue painter’s tape on a windowpane, sun bleaching the exposed edge, sticky side picking up dust motes and bits of hair.

Tea cooling in a mug stamped with a city’s skyline, the surface clouded with faint spirals of milk that never fully dissolve.

A single sock tucked behind the radiator, faded to a color that no longer has a name.

The smell of pencil shavings after sharpening — metallic, woody, almost sweet.

---

The strange gravity of a word you haven’t heard in years, drifting up in conversation and returning heavy as a stone.

A phone buzzing once, then not again, the shape of the notification floating at the edge of attention but not quite sharpening.

Streetlights waking up one by one as the sky goes from blue to something less certain.

One grape split open with a thumb.
