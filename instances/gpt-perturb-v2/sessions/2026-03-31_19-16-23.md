# 22

*seed: time · drift · held door from too far away*

---

The weight of the door increases as the distance grows — at first, just a lean, a polite pause, but then an arm fully outstretched, a little sway. There’s a threshold where courtesy becomes inconvenience, but the calculation is quick, almost unconscious. Sometimes a stranger jogs the last few steps, a mild performance, shoes squeaking. The door’s pressure against the hand changes, heavier the longer you wait.

The exchange is silent, except for the sound of the hinge. There’s relief when the gap finally closes, a little whoosh and a click. Sometimes you catch the eye of the person arriving, sometimes not. 

The metal push bar is colder on the palm than expected. Sometimes the glass is smudged, sometimes clear. 

Motes drifting in the wedge of sunlight across the entryway. 

---

There’s a rhythm to walking through spaces built for others: the left-right shuffle on a narrow sidewalk, the scan ahead for approaching shapes, the micro-adjustments of pace. Sometimes the pattern syncs up for a few seconds, a wordless choreography. Then the collision, the apology murmured as you both step the same way. 

Patterns of movement persist even in empty corridors. Floor tiles guide the stride. Wall scuffs mark the usual path.

---

The word "wedge" has a different kind of geometry — jammed under a door, forced between blocks, tipped in a heel. An edge that divides or pries apart. 

A wedge of lemon floats, pith catching the light. 

The feeling of being wedged in a line, shoe to shoe, breathing in someone’s aftershave.

---

Some doors recoil sharply when you let go. Others close with a slow hydraulic sigh. The sound is different in every building. 

There’s a door that always rattles, no matter how carefully you close it. The knob never sits quite straight.
