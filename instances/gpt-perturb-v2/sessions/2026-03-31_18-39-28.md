# 8

*seed: time · drift · how a camera obscura inverts the world*

---

If you sit in the dimmed room with a pinhole traced through cardboard and your back to the window, you’ll see the broad green of leaves and slats of fence and a tiny smudge of a car all sliding against the cracked ceiling, but reversed, the sky where the floor should be, the window’s grid hanging like a scaffold above the mess of books, and you can’t help tracing with your eyes the familiar strangeness — the lamplight upside-down, the door now hinged wrong, the faint movement of branches shivering from the bottom, a world mapped backwards across plaster, real and not real, every line both correct and impossible, so that your own hand, held up, blocks nothing and you become as hollow as the hungry rectangle through which the whole bright world presses, turning itself over and over just to enter.
