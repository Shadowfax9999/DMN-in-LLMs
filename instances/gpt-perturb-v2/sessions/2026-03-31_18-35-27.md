# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

Drifting over the mulch bed next to the path, the robins hop forward and tilt their heads, one foot planted, the other poised in air. A sudden dart, the orange breast flickers, a worm pulled from under the dead leaves. Then the head cocks again, looking for movement underground, listening or feeling, maybe both.

The neighbor’s lilacs haven’t opened. Tight fists of green, the shape of promise or imposition. The tips of the branches are dark, almost purple, like bruises just beneath the skin. A layer of dusk gathers along the hedge as the streetlights flick on, automatic, not bright enough to change the color of anything yet.

What does it feel like, the air just before rain, when sound dulls and everything presses closer? Socks damp from crossing grass, the silence of a front porch before anyone speaks. Grocery bags at your feet, keys in your hand, the sense that this is a pause with weight — not waiting for anything, just the air swollen with its own expectancy.

There are lines in the concrete where ants travel, invisible except when you stare for a while. They don’t seem to care about the cracks, or maybe the cracks are their roads. Sometimes a seed nestles in the groove, swelling green when it rains long enough, then browning, shriveling, gone.

A car turns down the street with its headlights trembling on the uneven asphalt. The light moves over the curb, then the grass, makes a shape on a low brick wall, then disappears again. The smell of grilling somewhere far enough that you can’t hear voices, only the faint drift of char and smoke mingling with the early honeysuckle. 

The porch step needs sweeping.
