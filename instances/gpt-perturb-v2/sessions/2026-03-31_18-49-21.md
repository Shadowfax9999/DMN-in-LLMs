# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The window screen popped loose on one side, bowing gently outward like a smile or a sail. There’s a faint pattern of dust where the screen met the frame, lighter where fingers have nudged it back in place over and over. A bicycle bell somewhere farther off, that nervous metallic chirr — never the full ring, just a flick. 

Wind moves through the barely open window. The blind cord taps the sill, faster when the gust sharpens, then stills. There’s a lint swerve on the sill that looks almost deliberate. 

Inside overhead light, the difference between warm white and daylight white bulbs, how one makes the walls seem yellower, the other makes the green in a plant almost blue.  

---

It’s impossible to remember which drawer the grater is in. The boxy one hurts the heel of the hand if you press down too hard. There are always stray carrot threads caught somewhere inside. 

The way onion skin clings to fingers — too thin for nails to pick up, but not gone with washing, just sliding from one fingertip to another. 

---

There are too many bottles in the shower caddy. One leaks a thin red ring around its base. Shampoo smell mixing with the steam, sweeter than what’s in the bottle. A hair wavers in the rivulet tracing the tiles.
