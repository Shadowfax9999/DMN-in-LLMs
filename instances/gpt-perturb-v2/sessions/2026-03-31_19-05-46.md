# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a kind of static that happens between two people who expect a conversation to move somewhere and it doesn’t — the rhythm slides into neutrality, neither warmth nor conflict, and topics appear just to fill the space. Exchanges that confirm only that both are awake.

Not all pauses are waiting. Some are more like a furlough, like the mind has been given five unsupervised minutes in the middle of routine, and it’s unclear whether to rest or just idle.

Negotiation. There’s a version of negotiation that never resolves, not deadlock or surrender but a kind of circling: each side rehearses its objection, an endless rehearsal, little notes taken and lost. "Let’s revisit this later," which is another name for indefinite stillness.

In math: equilibrium doesn’t have to be balanced. Sometimes it’s only the absence of net movement. The pressure from both sides cancels but nothing is satisfied.

Some friendships exist only in this register — sustained by inertia, not by shared interest or effort, but by the difficulty of ending something that isn’t broken.

---

I once read about how ice can melt at its own surface, still solid underneath — a kind of molecular pause, halfway between two states, neither winter nor spring.
