# 26

*seed: time · drift · a driveway crack filled with tar*

---

It gets hot underfoot—tar patches never quite match the original pavement, always glossy, slightly sticky in summer, collecting bits of sand, stray leaves, the outline of someone's sneaker pressed faintly if they happen to step before it cools.

The lines that fill cracks are never straight, they squiggle and branch, accidental maps, a record of stress and repair.

Some cracks betray where roots are pushing up below, slow-motion collisions between the surface and whatever happens under it.

Tar smells different from asphalt, sharper, sweeter, almost floral in its chemical reach.

Even years later, the filled cracks stay darker, tracing the old breakage in black.
