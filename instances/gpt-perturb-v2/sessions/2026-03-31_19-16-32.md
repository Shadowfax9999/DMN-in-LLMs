*seed: time · drift*

---

There are agreements that nobody signs — the understanding that you wait until the elevator empties before stepping in, the way people fall silent when a meeting begins, clocks set five minutes fast to hedge against lateness, the pause before answering a difficult question. A hand raised means wait, but a glance away is a softer signal, dissipating the tension with an easy distraction. 

Some signals travel invisibly — a cough at the right moment, shoes squeaking in the hall, a sequence of taps on a closed laptop, the hum of a phone on vibrate shivering through a desk. There’s an order to interruptions, to who can speak next. 

Shorthand accumulates in the margins, a tiny circle to mean “revisit,” a check for “done,” a trailing question mark for “maybe.” The marks go unnoticed except by those who use them. 

Some groups always sit in the same configuration, even when there’s no assigned seating. A ritual of arrangement, boundaries policed by backpacks and water bottles. 

In a crowded document, two cursors hover side by side, each flashing in their own rhythm. If you watch long enough, they seem to sync, even though they don’t. 

The point where a conversation falters: neither party sure whether to push forward or let it dissolve. 

The chair creaks. Someone smooths the corner of a notepad. 

A new agenda item appears, and the tempo shifts.
