# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a catch in the sock, something stiff at the seam. Bend the ankle, it presses — not pain, a reminder that the body lives in layers and one shifted. Folded cloth, a slip of thread rubbing. You move, it rides along, never quite finding a place to settle.

---

The afternoon is slow. Blue light on the kitchen floor, silent except for the water’s echo in the pipes. It could be later, or earlier, the hours rearrange. Time doesn’t collect here — it flows, trailing behind like a shoelace that hasn’t been tied. 

---

Plastic container lid, slightly warped at one corner. It snaps, but never flat, never perfect. The air around it smells faintly of sweet corn, even though it’s been washed twice.

---

The moment when the screen dims to black and your own shape floats up, ghosted in the reflection, looking back without focus. There is a sense of pause, not quite waiting, not quite anything else.
