# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a window near the kitchen sink, the sill wide enough for two mugs (sun-warmed handles, water spots). One mug still holding the outline of a teabag, the other empty but not clean, a rim of beige.  

The yellow sponge is torn at one end, making a sort of open mouth. Dish soap, almost out, the bottle soft at the sides. Someone’s left a spoon with a bit of yogurt and honey, drying rough.  

The outside air is thick with pollen tonight — a greenish dust on the cars. When headlights sweep the street, you can see particles sparkling, not quite settling.  

---

A sock under the table, not a pair.  
Potholders stacked crooked, red thread unraveling.  
The faucet drips every few minutes but never in a pattern.  
Mail on the counter: coupons, a blue envelope, a folded flyer with bold red letters.  
Someone called from the hallway, but the words blurred — too many doors open at once.  

The sound of a door closing is sharper when there’s no one talking.  

There’s crust from old bread in the sink drain.
