# 18

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sidewalk splits for the roots. The place in the cement where the pattern doesn’t fit, as if the tree made a gentle argument.

Some evenings the light creeps in sideways and dazzles everything in the window, and it’s only for twenty minutes, then it’s gone. Dust floats, gold for a moment, then sifts back into forgetting.

Cicadas started up early, two weeks ago. The sound isn’t a roar, exactly, more like a soft fever just under the skin of everything, especially near the hedges.

There’s a hollow where a shoe presses in wet ground and the mark fills with water. You can see the sky in the puddle until it clouds up with the next shoe.

A glass of water left on the counter absorbs a little taste from the air if you wait long enough.

---

I keep thinking that the word “Tuesday” is blue, but not the same blue as the word “Monday.” The idea of a color for a word—not a synesthesia, but more like how some names seem to come with an imagined texture.

A list of textures that don’t match their appearance:
— Banana peel (looks waxy, feels like plush rubber)
— Velvet (looks like a field, feels like a scrape in reverse)
— Bread crust (looks hard, gives at the touch)
— Tiled floor (looks solid, cold seeps up through socks)
— Bubble wrap (looks stiff, collapses to the touch)

The way socks twist slightly when you walk, just enough to lose the heel where it belongs.

It’s still bright out but the birds are quiet.
