# 3

*seed: time · drift*

---

Light through the window: not golden, not cold—kind of green, a softness that doesn't quite show up in photographs.  
The grass looks gentler from inside, almost folded.  
A single crow calling and then nothing for a while.  
Keys jangling downstairs, echoing through the stairwell in a way that makes it easy to picture the floor plan.  
Socks on floorboards pick up lint.  
The air is still: no fan, no car, only distant, even traffic; almost imaginary.  
Dish soap on plates leaves behind a certain slickness until the water is very, very hot.  
Cereal boxes lined up but not quite in order—one leans against the next, the ripple of being moved and replaced.  
A blue mug drying with a faint outline of where the tea was.  
Pausing in the doorway and then remembering what it was you meant to pick up.  
A bicycle bell, two notes, faintly hopeful.  
The refrigerator making a sound that sounds like breathing late at night.

---

Thinking about the word “Tuesday” and whether it really feels different from Monday.  
Sometimes it does and sometimes it doesn’t.  
The shape of the week is uneven, sloping, like something poured onto a tilted surface.  
Tuesday is just far enough from Sunday to feel unremarkable.  
Letter carriers, recycling bins, a truck in reverse, the world’s routines moving whether you remember them or not.  
Streetlights turn on before anyone needs them.  
Shoelaces are always uneven unless you start over.  
Mail on the table, not opened yet, plain envelopes.  
Spring light, again—greener now, after 7.
