# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sound of sandals on pavement is different from sneakers. Softer, almost sticky, as if each step lingers half a second past its arrival.

There’s that faint smell of thawed earth, a little metallic. It gets into the sleeves of jackets left in closets too long.

What does the sky look like, when it’s clear but you can sense pollen in the air? Is it more yellow, or is that just a feeling behind the eyes?

Some birds seem to know when traffic is light enough to cross the street safely. You see a sparrow dash between car tires and wonder if it made a calculation.

---

Three numbers: 18, 39, Tuesday. If you rearrange them, they don’t spell anything.

---

The soccer fields are empty. The grass is still too short for anyone to lose a ball in it. The nets have holes. The parking lot smells different after rain.
