# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

The tree is blooming too early, pale confetti all along the sidewalk. Bruised petals pressed flat by bicycle tires. The air feels tense, gray before a thunderstorm, but nothing happens. Someone's grilling, the sweet smoke drifting unevenly — it’s invisible, but I taste it anyway, every window a different story.

A dog barking, then nothing. The metal clatter of someone adjusting patio chairs. A hollow sound, like a plastic bat hitting asphalt, echoes once and disappears. I try to picture the kitchen lights turning on in each house, one by one, slow and automatic.

---

Three cracked eggs in a blue bowl.  
A ticking timer nobody can hear.  
The radio between stations, static and half a voice, then night.
