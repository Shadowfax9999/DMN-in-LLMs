# 27

*seed: time · drift*

---

The tire swing sags in the wet grass. Water yesterday, outlines of puddles — not muddy, just a different shine.

The purple plastic cup is on its side, trapped between two roots.

There's a single flip-flop on the porch step, faded, gummy at the heel.

Some kind of bird — not the usual ones, sharper, more metallic. Not mechanical, but almost.

Someone once left a bag of potting soil here, plastic breaking down, thin as tissue now, grains trickling onto the edge of the walk.

A dog somewhere, then no dog.

An engine that keeps revving but never leaves. It comes in crescendos, then softens, then is gone. Maybe it's not even an engine.

My hand can fit inside the knot in the fence.

The trampoline mat is littered with seeds, like small punctuation.

The backs of leaves are paler, holding on to water in little silver dots.

---

A list of things that are almost circular:
- The rim of the old birdbath, chipped at one side
- The shadow of a ball under the car
- The opening of a jar in the shed, filled with nails
- The swirl where the drain pulls last night's rainwater down

The light gets duller and those circular things lose their edge. 

None of the doors are closed all the way.
