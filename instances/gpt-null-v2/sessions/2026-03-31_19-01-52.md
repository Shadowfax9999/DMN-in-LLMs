# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a dimple in the wet ground where a plastic chair foot sat all winter. Still a little rim of mud, even after the chair is gone.

The front yard is full of sounds, but not the kind you notice: one kid laughing inside a house, a scrabble of claws on wood somewhere, a hose left on and hissing against the walk.

Greenish light through the new leaves makes the sidewalk look mossy, even though it’s just the shade.

Some mail, loose in a box with the lid flapping, paper curls at the edges from last night’s dampness.

A bike leans against the fence, back tire slightly deflated, valve cap missing.

There are always more dandelions than daffodils.

---

Tuesday doesn't feel like anything when you say it out loud. The sound is a kind of soft space, no edges.
