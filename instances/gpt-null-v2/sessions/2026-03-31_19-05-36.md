# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

A glass door with a metal frame that catches at the bottom every time it slides, so you have to lift it a little. The handle is too warm from the sun. The carpet on this side is tangled with a single white thread, knotted, refusing to be pulled loose. 

Are there more insects on the south side of houses? Or is it just that the sun comes in stronger so you see them. 

Clouds that look like pencil erasers smudged across the sky. 

There’s a loose bolt in the bench out front and it wobbles even when nobody sits down.

---

Sticky note, corners lifting. Static makes it pull toward your knuckle.  

An alphabet of shoe prints in the grit by the threshold, some sharp-treaded, some worn flat, one barely visible but broad. 

Crickets start early here, or maybe it’s frogs, or both. 

The air smells like cut stems.
