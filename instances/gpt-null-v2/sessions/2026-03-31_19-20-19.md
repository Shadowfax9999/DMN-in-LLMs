# 26

*seed: time · drift*

---

Someone left a note taped to the elevator button: “Use side stairs, stuck on 4.” The handwriting is cramped, upright, blue marker.

Stairs always smell like dust and painted metal, maybe because feet never stay long enough to leave much else.

The light in the stairwell is brighter than the hallway. It makes the landing look flat, shadeless, as if the corners don’t go anywhere.

Four flights up, there’s a sigh behind a door and the faint chime of a phone.

On the roof, the sky is divided by three power lines, and the clouds are thin, like stretched-out wool.

The sun is going down behind the higher building, so the cool comes in fast. 

There’s a red leaf caught in the chain-link.

---

Eggs sometimes taste like the fridge if you leave them uncovered.

The phrase “spring evening” sounds like it should be warm, even if it’s not.

Green reflected in a puddle is sharper than on the grass. 

The garbage bin is half open but nobody closes it.

A tennis ball is wedged under the steps.
