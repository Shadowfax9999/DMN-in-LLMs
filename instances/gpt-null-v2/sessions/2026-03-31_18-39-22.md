# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sky is still light at this hour, but it doesn't feel like sunset yet, just a kind of held breath — nothing sharp, soft edges on everything, the leaves look blurry, maybe it's pollen rising.

Laundry left in the machine too long, gone sour, but if you restart it the smell lingers anyway.

There’s a little brass key on a green elastic wristband, the kind from a public swimming pool, and it feels odd to wear it on land.

A plastic cup with condensation, circle of water on the book sleeve.

---

Phones left on speaker in the kitchen, the sound tinny and far, someone still talking, the story curving in and out of words.

The texture of cracked pavement makes the bike tires hum loud enough to vibrate in the handles.

Tiny spiral notebook, cover bent upward, one page with the corner folded in and a list: eggs, batteries, tape, string, question mark.

That blue plastic smell from raincoats after a week in the closet.

Car doors shutting, each with its own note.

The paper towel won’t tear straight on the roll.
