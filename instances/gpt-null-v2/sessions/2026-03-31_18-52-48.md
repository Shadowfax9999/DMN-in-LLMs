# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

Air from outside and air from the hallway mix differently than air straight from outside. The window is two-thirds open, the screen has a tear in the corner that’s been there for years. If you squeeze a mandarin between your fingers it sprays a tiny sun.

—

I keep thinking about the word “tessellate.” Tiles, but also a kind of folding. Bees do it but so do patterns on the bathroom floor, some almost make you dizzy, especially when you’ve just woken up. Why do some repeating designs feel colder than others? The blue and white mosaics look like ice because they’re blue and also because of the star shapes, like crystals but not really cold, except on bare feet sometimes.

The clock’s minute hand is almost on the six. Somewhere a door is scuffing the floor. Socks are drying on the radiator. A plate with crumbs is balancing on the arm of the couch.

—

Sometimes when it’s almost sundown the light comes in orange and makes everything in the room look a little unfamiliar, like the shadow under the table is thicker, like maybe something is under there. 

There’s a faint line where the carpet has been pressed down by the same chair over and over. The stain near the corner looks a little like a country, maybe Spain or an animal running. 

The space above the window is dusty. 

If you touch the screen gently, all the little wires vibrate like a silent harp.
