# 22

*seed: time · drift*

---

Salt on a black surface—grains that gather in crooked constellations, none of them symmetrical, not even close. These little piles always look like maps or diagrams but they never correspond to anywhere you’ve been.

If you bring your face close, there’s a briny tang, or maybe it’s just the memory of kitchens.

The light keeps changing, but you don’t see it move. There’s suddenly more of it on the edge of the table, catching the rim of a glass. Circles of water left behind.

From the window: a narrow slice of tree, not quite enough to tell what kind, just a blur of green and the suggestion of stems or trunks. If you squint, the leaves merge into one dense shape.

A fork sits apart from the rest, prongs angled toward the edge.

---

Tuesday feels like a thin, pale gray, more transparent than other days. If you wrote it out in red marker, the ink would pool in the curves of the “u”.

Sometimes the word “evening” makes you picture a streetlamp coming on, even if you’re indoors with the lights already on.

Stacks of paper with rough edges, the kind that catch on your fingers, always seem to collect by accident.

The sound of a car going by is muffled, but the wheels pick up the gravel at just the right place, and there’s a scatter you can almost feel in your teeth.

There’s a plate with a blue pattern under the salt.
