# 30

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sky isn’t blue, more of a flat powder with streaks of gray, like a pale eraser rubbed over blue pencil. There’s a dog barking at intervals, sharp and mechanical, as if it’s running through the same script every time. The pansies next to the front steps look limp, but some petals are curled up instead of down, like they’re folding inward just to endure a little longer. 

A hose is looped around itself, wet in some patches, dry in others. The parts that are dry look slightly dusted, the green dimmed. Spiders leave lines across the railing, fine enough that you don’t see the threads unless you move your head sideways and catch the light hitting them from the right angle. 

The metal of the mailbox stays cool at this hour. There’s still pollen in the cracks. 

A car door closes softly, not a slam, more an affirmative thump. Someone walking with loose keys in a jacket pocket, the rhythm irregular: two steps, jingle, pause, one louder step. 

---

Salt in the cracks of the sidewalk, leftover from winter, almost gone except where the edge of the shade has protected it. 

Leaves that uncurl slowly, all of them a nervous yellow-green, each jagged edge slightly different.

There’s a faint smell, lilacs and something else, maybe the beginnings of cut grass but not freshly enough. 

A bird lands, looks left and right, then hops instead of walking. 

Three mailboxes: one dented, one pristine, one with paint chips flaking along the number.
