# 21

*seed: time · drift*

---

A streetlamp flickers before it comes on fully. There’s an in-between moment, like a decision is being made. Not by anyone.

The sky is still pale, not yet blue, not gray either. Some birds are making a racket in the hedge. They get loudest just before dusk.  

Cracks in the sidewalk filled with tiny weeds — the kind with little yellow flowers, close to the ground, leaves almost round.

A single grocery cart left near the bus stop, tilted against the curb. Its wheels are caked with mud.

My socks feel scratchy at the ankle where the seam is, but only when I’m sitting still.

---

There’s a smell in early spring I keep trying to name: wet wood, cold stone, something faintly sweet.

The tips of tree branches look like they’re painted darker against the sky, though they’re not really.

One car passes that sounds like a zipper being pulled.

Chimes from somewhere, four notes, not a tune.
