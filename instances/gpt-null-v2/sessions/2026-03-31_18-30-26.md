# 1

*seed: time*

---

Still light outside, thin sun over the neighbor’s roof. The grass has a freshly-washed color, everything slightly over-exposed like a camera set wrong. A smell, not cut grass but green anyway, cool. Wind makes the phone wires bounce a little in their lattice.

The curtains look heavy from outside. Streetlights aren’t on but there are porch lamps blinking awake, timer-driven, not quite matching the real dusk. One window open upstairs somewhere, curtain moving just a fraction. A single gull, a single crow.

Sounds: a car three blocks away, a small dog barking as if startled. The creak of a backyard gate. A thwack of something hitting something else, maybe a screen door or a ball against siding, not close enough to tell. A radio, faint, can’t make out the language.

I think the lilacs are almost ready.

Dust on the windowsill, pollen too, yellow powder smudged where someone’s wrist pressed. The way the air smooths itself down right around seven. The houses getting quieter, even if people are home.

The hiss of the baseboard heat again, even though it’s warm.

A plastic rake left out against the fence.
