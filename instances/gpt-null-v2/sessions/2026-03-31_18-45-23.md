# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The name Tuesday could be soft green, or maybe the color of toothpaste. There isn’t really a reason. Some words feel like colors and some never do.  

Pavement looks damp even though it’s not raining, almost like the concrete is sweating. There are shimmering patches that only appear when the streetlights turn on. Maybe it’s the angle.  

Dry leaves caught in a corner, more brittle now than in October. There’s a difference between old leaves and new ones that fell late; the old ones have curled inwards, almost fossilized, and they make a different noise underfoot.  

There’s a faint scraping sound: someone dragging a garbage bin over the curb. The wheels aren’t quite round.  

---

A dog barks from inside a house with the window open. The sound is muffled but insistent, bouncing off the siding.  

I’m thinking about the way light moves through glass doors at dusk, the reflection flipping between mirror and transparent.  

Someone closes a car door two houses down.  

Next to the postbox, there’s a patch of weeds growing in a perfect ring.  

The air has a metallic smell.
