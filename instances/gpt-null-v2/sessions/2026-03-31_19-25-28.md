# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

Still light outside and the window is open just enough for the room to have two temperatures at once. Left arm colder than right. The sliding glass door has streaks where someone tried to clean last week but only caught the main smudges, leaving a vertical trail of half-effort. The glass makes a kind of double world: inside, reflection of lamp and couch; outside, the actual trees all blurred and bending in the wind.

A squeak from the hallway that could be the door hinge or something softer, shoe on linoleum. I expect footsteps but only the squeak. 

Dinner smell: something savory, maybe onions, or the way bread smells when it's toasting too close to the broiler. If I try to isolate the smell in my mind it's just a vague warmth, not quite food, more like the background of kitchens in houses that aren’t my own.

Some pigeons land on the wire above the back fence. They settle in pairs, almost evenly spaced, but one always pushes the next one down the line until they fly off entirely. 

Tuesday is an unremarkable name for a day. The “u” feels slow, the “s” slides by, the “d” is abrupt. 

A single sock under the coffee table, curled tight as if it’s defending itself.

Somewhere a car alarm chirps, not the full alarm but that brief warning squawk, once, then silence.

The radiator ticks as it cools.
