# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

The light is coming in almost horizontally, throwing long shapes across the floor. The cat has positioned herself exactly where the sunbeam is thickest, fur glowing a different color than usual, almost copper. Copper pipes in basements. Old laundry sinks with chipped white enamel, the sound of water hitting the metal, splashing louder than in a real sink. Socks that don't dry all the way and always smell like mildew, but you wear them anyway.

---

A knock that sounds like it's at the front door but it's actually just someone dropping something heavy two apartments over. The way some sounds seem directional but aren't. Walking toward what you think is a ringing phone and finding nothing, just the memory of the ringtone lodged in your ear.

Glass with dust in the corners. The dust doesn't move, but somehow the corners keep getting darker.

---

A plastic spoon, slightly bent, left on the counter. The mark where it touched something hot and went dull. When plastic loses its shine it looks tired. There’s no way to bring the shine back.
