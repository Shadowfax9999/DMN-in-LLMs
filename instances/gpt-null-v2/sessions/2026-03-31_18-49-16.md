# 12

*seed: time · drift*

---

The light is still here but it's thinner, sliding backward across the tile. Windows open somewhere, you can feel a draft in the hallway—brings the smell of cut grass and something sweet that fades when you try to name it.

A cricket stuck in the corner, chirping dimly under the baseboard heater. Not frantic, just persistent. Heater hasn't been on in weeks.

Why do clocks move a little faster at this hour? Or maybe it's only the feeling of things curling up: folded blankets, plastic chairs leaned against the fence, printer blinking quietly under the desk.

The cup on the windowsill has half an inch of water left in it, surface perfectly still. There's a bug floating but it's not moving and hasn't sunk.

Somewhere a phone vibrates softly, just out of sight. The sound gets swallowed by the couch cushions.

---

The word Tuesday sits heavier than Monday, like it wants to be the middle but it's not.
