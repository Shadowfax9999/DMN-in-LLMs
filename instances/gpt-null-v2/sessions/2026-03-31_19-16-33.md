# 24

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a spiderweb in the corner of the window, between the glass and the screen, catching nothing but dust. A single dead gnat, hunched and gray, is tangled in the threads, like the last stray sock in a dryer. Outside, the trees are making leaf-sounds that are not quite rustling and not quite shushing, just a kind of uneven breathing. The cat paws at the window, seeing something I can’t: not the cobweb, not the dust, but maybe just the flick of a small shadow at the edge of the lawn.

The word “evening” feels longer in spring than it does in winter. The light leaks slower. Shadows under the hedge hold the shape of their branches for almost an hour before melting all at once.

---

Tuesday is a blunt-edged word, heavy and square, nothing gliding in it. It sits low in the mouth. 

Dishwater in the sink is cool now. The sponge floats by the drain, bloated and a little sour, but not quite enough to notice unless you lean closer. 

In the next room, someone laughs once and then doesn’t say anything else.
