# 9

*seed: time · drift*

---

The street is full of that pale yellow light, the kind that makes windows look soft even if they're closed. Sometimes the air just feels different, thicker, like it presses on your skin. 

A small dog across the street is barking at a bicycle. Tires spinning, spokes catching the sun. There's something about bicycles when they move through puddles: the sound is sharper than you'd expect, and the water always splashes at a slant.

The screen door creaks. There's a smell from somewhere — onions, maybe, or cut grass, or both together. 

If you look at the pattern of cracks in a driveway, some of them veer off suddenly, choosing a new direction for no reason at all.

The world is full of things that happen at 18:39:

— The sandwich shop two blocks south closes its blinds.  
— Someone's feet on a plastic playground ladder, squeaking.  
— Sprinklers ticking quietly in a yard nobody is looking at.  
— A bird lands on a wire and doesn't sing.  
— There's a car that has been parked for so long the license plate is bent under dust.  

A train goes by, but you only see one orange boxcar between the gaps in the houses.

I think the cat was sitting under the mailbox but now she's gone.
