# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

The street lamps come on earlier than I expect. Not dark yet, just the wrong color, sodium light knitted into the blue that’s left.

There’s a warmth in the air that the sidewalks haven’t registered yet; concrete still cool, holding cold from the night before.

A bus sighs when it stops. It almost sounds like a person giving in.

Somewhere inside a house, the noise of water in pipes, quick then slow. Doors open toward the yard, screens let the evening in.

A skateboard hits a seam in the pavement with a clack I can’t quite place in distance. Far away, closer, both at once.

The smell of soap trailing someone’s laundry, vanishing in patches, then back again.

Windows reflect the last light differently—some go orange, some stay pale blue.

Feet in shoes on gravel, the uneven rhythm of walking past mailboxes.

Birdsong changes after sunset—shriller, or maybe the city gets quieter.

A bike leaning against a fence with both wheels off the ground.
