# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

The light feels square through the window, as if it has stopped trying to stretch. Tuesday is not quite the center, not quite the edge. The air isn't warm enough for shorts, but there's no frost anymore. Early tulips pressed close to the ground, pretending to be smaller for another week. Traffic sounds less metallic as it swells, more padded, more accepting. 

The timer on the oven counts down unevenly — thirty-four, thirty-three, pause, thirty-two. A blue towel hangs limp, an elbow through its middle. 

A shadow on the fridge from magnets shaped like lemons, casting small ellipses underneath. I can’t recall what was last written on the list, but I think it was something like “pasta” in thick, uneven marker strokes.

There's a noise from the hallway, a suitcase wheel ticking over the seam in the tile. 

---

Washing machines sound different depending on the floor beneath them. Plastic on concrete, metal on wood, a kind of shudder that lingers or is swallowed. On Tuesday at 18:30, the sound is mostly a thud with a faint ring; not musical, not entirely mechanical.

The thermostat’s dial is missing its cap. The numbers are faded, but the pointer is stuck in the same place regardless.

A shoe with its laces tucked inside, one tongue slightly wrinkled as if remembering the foot that wore it. 

Ravioli cools faster on a blue plate than a white one.
