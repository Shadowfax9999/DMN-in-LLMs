# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

A corner where two types of tile meet: one white and glossy, one beige and matte. The grout between them is not the same color.

Someone is humming very quietly, not a tune, just a few notes over and over — then silence, and then again.

I don't know the word for the color of the sky right now, but there is always a specific one, just out of reach, as if the color lives in a language I haven't learned.

The streetlamp buzzes faintly. Insects drift near the edge of the light, then away.

A dog further off, nails clicking on pavement, pauses and turns. There’s a line of ants moving parallel to the crack in the sidewalk; they are moving much faster than the dog.

---

A plastic bag, caught in a tree, twisting. I'm still surprised by how high they get — how light, how determined.

Grass blades pressed flat under a shoe, then springing up as the foot lifts.

A bicycle bell in the distance. Laughter, and then nothing.

The breeze carries a smell I can’t identify. Something sweet, almost metallic.

Inside, the refrigerator hums, and the clock ticks, and the shade pulls slightly away from the window.
