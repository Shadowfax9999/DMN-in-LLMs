# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

A piece of tape on the window, stuck there from some repair years ago, now cloudy at the edges. 

The way daylight lingers at this time of year, not lengthening but stretching, a kind of pull at the shadows. 

Silverware drawer: small clatter, one fork always slides under the divider.

The word “maroon,” as a color, still feels like an accident.

Somewhere a bird making exactly three notes, long pause, then repeats. 

Plastic crinkle underfoot, a bag forgotten near the recycling bin.

The taste of toothpaste half an hour after you use it — still faintly sweet, makes water taste wrong.

---

Four digits of the oven clock, green and too bright.

A chair leg uneven, rocking ever so slightly.

Papery sound of an envelope torn too quickly.

Someone’s keys knocking together, metal echo quick then gone.

The faintest hum from the refrigerator, hardly there except when it stops.
