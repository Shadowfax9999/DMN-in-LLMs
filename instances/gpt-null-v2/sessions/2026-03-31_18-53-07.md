# 15

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a cold spot on the countertop, right under the shadow of the window frame, where condensation gathers into a comma-shaped puddle.

The hum of the fridge keeps slipping out of synchronization with whatever is humming outside. Not quite in rhythm, not quite clashing—just parallel.

A list of things left outside overnight:
—a child’s bucket with cloudy water in the bottom, the handle stiff with age
—plastic Adirondack chairs, faded to a different green on the arms
—a trowel, half-buried in the mulch, handle sunburned to gray
—a shoe with curled edges, laces stiffer than before

The word “evening” enters the mind as a slow fade rather than a sharp border. The air becomes less definite.

There is a space behind the trash bins where violets grow.

---

The dog barks once, pauses, barks again, with a small scraping sound after the second bark.
