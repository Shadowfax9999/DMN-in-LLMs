# 16

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tuesday. The "u" in the middle stays low compared to the arch of the "T" and the rise at the end.

Parking lots just after dinner, the way the sky holds on to its blue but dulls it so the white lines look faint, like chalk that's been walked over.

People are carrying things: mesh bags, takeout, a jacket slung over an arm. So many plastic water bottles dangling from hands.

A breeze that doesn't shift the branches but lifts one piece of paper.

Some shops are still open, the lights inside yellow and square, and there's the faintest smell of garlic from somewhere.

There are shoes left outside a door, one shoe upright, the other on its side as if peeled off in a hurry.

Traffic hum that's not quite white noise, because there are the higher whines of mopeds and the bump of a trunk closing.

---

The hardware aisle at this hour is empty. Every bolt seems very far from its purpose.

There are blue plastic bins for different sizes, but they look like they're holding seeds.

A child sits in a shopping cart with their head tipped back, staring at the ceiling.
