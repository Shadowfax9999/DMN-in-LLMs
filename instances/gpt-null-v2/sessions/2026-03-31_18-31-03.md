# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

The windows are open and the air has that uneven warmness that feels like it should draw insects in but there’s only a faint smell from outside, mulchy and green. There’s a single shoe left beside the doorway, the other one crumpled deeper into the room, toe under the radiator. 

On the kitchen counter, two glasses—the water in one is cloudy, the other is clear. The sound of neighbor kids clanking scooters against the curb, their voices bouncing off the brick in short, sharp bursts. A napkin half-folded sits in a diagonal line on the table; there’s a ring where a mug once was, still a little damp.

Light moves differently between six-thirty and seven; it tries to be gold but gets stopped by the screen and comes in softer, almost flat. There’s a fly hovering in the center of the room as if it’s trying to decide what to do next.

The fridge hums, then clicks off, and in that moment there’s a hollowness like the room just grew a fraction larger. 

A plastic bag twists on itself by the window. 

The faucet drips a single drop, very close together.

There’s no wind.
