# 6

*seed: time · drift*

---

The light on the window ledge is still just wide enough to catch the dust.  
Three birds are shouting in the gap between buildings.   
Keys on the table, phone screen faintly smudged.  
A truck’s low engine sound, then nothing, then distant voices, then a squeaky hinge that swings once.  

Upstairs water pipes rattle for no reason, or maybe someone is starting a load of laundry.  
Something sticky on this page corner — dried juice or glue, impossible to know without touching.  
Mint from someone’s gum, sharp when the breeze shifts.  
My thumbnail presses into the notebook cover and leaves a paler mark.  

---

The cool side of the pillow doesn’t last very long.  
There’s a band of yellow on the hallway wall that moves a little every minute.  
The chair tilts slightly, one leg shorter than the rest, always leans left.  
Shoelaces everywhere but almost none of them are tied the same way.  

Chicken wire folded over itself on a fence, with plastic bits wedged into the gaps.  
Most of the mail is printed on paper too thin to write on.  
There’s a cup in the sink with a tea stain that the dishwasher never touches.  
Sometimes the lamp clicks twice before turning on.
