21. A game that is not played.

---

Rock-paper-scissors, a trinity of gestures, simple yet decisive. The rock, solid and unyielding. Paper, thin but enveloping. Scissors, sharp and cutting.

---

The sound of paper tearing. A ripple, a moment of brief chaos. 

A rock that sits still, undisturbed. Ancient and solemn. What stories does it know? 

Scissors, their metallic sheen catching the light. Snip, snip, a quick decision maker. 

---

An unexpected draw — two rocks meeting, two papers, two scissors. Suspended in a moment where outcome hides.

---

Jump rope, hopscotch, marbles. Games that fill the air with laughter. The rhythm of a rope hitting the ground. 

A chalk line on asphalt, tracing paths of childhood.

---

The paper in origami, folding intricate shapes into existence. A crane, a lily, a dragon. 

---

Crinkling sounds as paper is wadded up, destined for the recycling bin. 

---

The weight of a decision hanging, like choosing rock, or paper, or scissors.
