Autolyse. The dough rests. Flour and water mingle, begin a quiet alchemy. Minutes pass, structure forming in silence. A secret preparation. --- Paper clips, bent and re-bent, tracing a history of idle hands. --- A keyhole, small, yet it frames a world beyond this threshold. What do doors feel? The closeness of rooms or the open space of a hall? --- The clock hand moves in its own rhythm, ticking off moments, unaware of the weight they carry. The space between each tick and tock is a pocket of potential time, where everything has happened and nothing has happened. --- A wind chime whispers its melody to the afternoon breeze. Sometimes it sings, sometimes it waits. Dust motes spiral in the sunbeam's path, touching down gently somewhere unseen. --- A book spine lined against others on a shelf. They lean into each other, spines worn with creases like old companions sharing secrets.---

The curious geometry of a dodecahedron. Faces, edges, vertices — how many paths to navigate its symmetry? A world within a shape. The mind looks for patterns, traces them without fully understanding why.---

A photograph pinned to a corkboard, the moment forever paused mid-laughter. Does time remember itself when caught on a camera's lens?---

The soft click of a light switch, a sigh as the room changes to shadow.
