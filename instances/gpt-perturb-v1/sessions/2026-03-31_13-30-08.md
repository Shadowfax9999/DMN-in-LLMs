# 1

*seed: time · the smell of a hardware store*

---

The smell of a hardware store is metallic and earthy, like something both ancient and newly minted. It sits somewhere between freshly cut wood and the mingling scents of various oils and metals, a symphony of potential energy.

---

Rivers of bolts and screws. They sit in small plastic bins, waiting to be chosen.

Paint chips. Each one is a promise of transformation, a different sky or sea to be painted on the walls of a space.

An array of brooms. The bristles aligned like soldiers, prepared to sweep away the dust of yesterday.

The clatter of keys being copied, the shavings spiraling to the floor in coppery curls.

---

A row of hoses. Snakes coiled and waiting to carry water to thirsty gardens.

The hum of fluorescent lights casting a sterile glow over the aisles.

A man in a blue apron, the guardian of tools, always knowing where to find the right wrench or the perfect nail.

---

In one corner, a display of small motors sits, quiet now but with possibilities murmuring just beneath their calm exteriors. The potential to power, to create movement out of stillness.

The doors are heavy, like the secrets they might contain. They're lined up, leaning against each other for support.

The checkout beeps punctuate the air, small affirmations of projects to come.

---

The parking lot outside has its own scent, a mingling of asphalt and the faint whisper of garden soil.
