# 21

*seed: time · drift · deglaze*

---

Deglaze — the word itself shimmers like a coating, a liquid gloss that disappears only to reveal more. Cooking, chemistry, layers of flavor. 

Acorn caps scattered across a sidewalk, like tiny helmets abandoned by a miniature army. Each one a perfect little bowl, collecting rainwater or dust, depending on the day's offerings.

Pebbles on the shore, the way they become smooth over time. Time isn't just measured by clock hands; it's also recorded in the erosion of stone, a silent history.

A song stuck in the head, repeating like the persistent drip of a faucet. It's curious how a melody can loop without a conscious invitation, taking residence in the mind's quieter corners.

---

A shadow slants across the floor, shifting as the sun moves. Shadows create their own map, tracing unseen paths on familiar ground. Each movement of the sun tugs the shadow along, almost like a leash.

Salt on the tongue, the sharpness of it, the way it makes you aware of your mouth in ways you weren't moments before. 

There's a distant sound of traffic, the ebb and flow of engines and tires on asphalt. It's like the city breathing, an inhale and exhale in mechanical form.

---

A thought of a friend rises, unbidden, associated with a scent or a season or a shared memory. How small fragments can conjure entire people.

Leaves rustling in the wind, a familiar whispering that seems to carry secrets between the branches.

The light switch clicks, and darkness recedes as the room fills with artificial daylight. The connection feels immediate, yet it's a simple rearrangement of electrons finding a new path.

---

The scent of a freshly cut lemon, the bright tang that fills the air and lingers on fingertips. It's a smell that sharpens the senses, almost waking them from sleep.

The texture of paper, the slight roughness under fingertips as a page is turned. It holds stories and potential within its fibers.
