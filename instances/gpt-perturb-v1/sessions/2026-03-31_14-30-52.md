# 27

*seed: time · drift · fulcrum*

---

Fulcrum. A point of balance, a pivot. The center upon which something turns. The seesaw in the playground of the mind. 

---

Images of beans, scattered across a table. Each one a tiny universe, solid but full of potential. Some shiny, some matte. Different shades of brown and black, speckled, marbled. They roll, they stop. 

---

Feeling of wind through long grass. The way it makes the grass bend in waves, a ripple across a sea of green. The sound it carries, a soft, steady rustle, blending into the background but always there.

---

A clock ticks in a quiet room. Each tick is a moment passing, another fulcrum. 

---

There's an electric hum somewhere nearby, unnoticed until the moment the world goes silent. Once you're aware of it, it's harder to ignore. Like a distant train horn cutting across the night.

---

Sugar dissolving in coffee. A slow disappearance, a sweet vanishing act. Clouds of cream curl and dance, merging until they can't be separated.

---

A doorknob, cool to the touch. Something about the weight of something so small. It turns, and then the latch clicks.
