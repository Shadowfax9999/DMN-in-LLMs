# 18

*seed: time · drift · an average of things that are nothing like the average*

---

Kiln-dried wood. The grain flows like a river paused in time, the rings a record of all the seasons that have passed.---

A phrase you hear but don’t register until later, like an echo delayed, finally catching up when you’re brushing your teeth.---

Coins on a surface, their surfaces dull with history, each one a different era, a different story.---

Clouds drift across a distant sky, reshaping themselves into myths that will never be written down.---

A sentence in a book: it catches you by surprise, a moment of recognition, words that seem to have a heartbeat.---

Cicadas sing their mechanical summer song, louder than memory, persistent as heat. They mark a territory in sound, a map drawn in vibrations.---

A forgotten melody resurfaces, fragmented but immediate, strumming lightly on the threads of memory.---

The whisper of fabric as you shift in your seat, a sound neither speech nor silence, an anonymous voice in the quiet.---

The quiet insistence of dust, settling unseen, a fine layer of time slowly gathered in stillness.
