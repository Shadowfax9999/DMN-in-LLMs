*seed: time · drift · bus shelter*

---

Bus shelter

---

Waiting for the bus, people shift their weight from one foot to the other. Their shadows dance on the ground. Shadows seem more playful than the people they belong to. Each person carries a different mood, yet the shadows blend together in the light of the setting sun.

The map on the glass. It's smudged with fingerprints. Dust has settled into the corners where the frame holds the glass. Here and there, a raindrop from earlier remains, a tiny lens distorting everything behind it.

Someone left a book on the bench — pages yellowed, spine cracked. The title is worn. The bus shelter is a waiting room for stories that start mid-sentence.

---

The bus arrives. The air changes as its doors open with a hiss. Everything rearranges itself for a brief moment: who's first, who'll find a seat. The sound of the engine is like a sigh of relief. 

The bus shelter is empty now. Except for the map, the book, and the shadows.
