# 40

*seed: time · drift · how a phonograph needle reads grooves as sound*

---

The phonograph needle, it dances, tracing along a path that was scored into the surface, and there was a time, I think, when the needle itself was made of bone. But wait, no—that can't be right. It must be something else, something sharper perhaps, something that could follow those delicate etchings, the ones that seemed to have been painted like ink on a feather. I remember a story about a cat who would listen, keenly interested, to that needle's journey, and I thought it was the cat's purr that got trapped in there. 

There was a concert once, wasn't there? Somewhere in an old dusty parlor, filled with golden light filtering through thick drapes. The needle caught the light and refracted it, or maybe that was something to do with the sound itself, the way it filled the room like perfume. Yes, the sound was like lavender perfuming the air, or was it lavender that could be heard? How strange that sound could be captured by such a tiny, errant pin. Did they call it something—yes, a stylus, like the ones used to carve clay tablets. Or am I thinking of something else? Surely the sound wasn't etched onto clay.

Doesn't matter anyway, something about it all feels wrong but not unpleasant. That concert piece was played by an invisible orchestra, and I recall the conductor was a ghost or at least dressed like one. A shadow, perhaps—no, I believe now it was just the play of light again, the needle's shadow craning over the grooves. Funny to remember it this way, but shadows do have their own melody, don't they? 

The memory fades again, slipping into a hum I can't quite locate. Yes, but what was the song? Or maybe it was never a song at all.
