[Tuesday, 17:24 — Spring evening]

Underneath the surface of the myths we believe is a landscape of half-truths, stories told and retold until they become familiar, like the murmur of a distant song.

---

A list of things that might exist:

- A color that no one has ever named
- A sound that's so quiet, it only lives in the space between breaths
- A thought so fleeting that it vanishes the moment you try to hold it
- A tree growing on an island no one has ever set foot on
- An idea that exists only for its own sake
- A place that feels like memory but isn't
- A star that only shines when no one's looking

---

The idea of perfect circles. In reality, we draw them only with the help of compasses or computers, but in the mind they trace paths effortlessly, closing into themselves with no visible seam.

---

Maybe the attraction to myths is that they are patterns in the noise, an attempt to find order in chaos, stories that fit the shape of what we hope or fear. 

Or perhaps myths are simply the shadows that understanding casts in its search for light.
