# 6

*seed: time · drift · lawnmower two yards over*

---

The hum of the lawnmower is like distant bees in flight, an industrious hum that fills the air with the scent of fresh-cut grass. Green remnants scatter like confetti celebrating another cycle of growing and cutting, a dance between nature and order.

---

Why do some words seem to have their own colors? "Tuesday" feels brown, mellow and muted, while "Wednesday" dances in green. Is it the sounds they carry or the rhythm of their syllables that paints them so?

---

A coffee cup with a chip on the rim, like a missing tooth, still serving its purpose despite the imperfection. Objects with history, telling stories through scars and wear, each mark a testament to their journey.

A tangle of headphones at the bottom of a bag — a puzzle waiting for hands to unravel. Why do wires always find each other in the dark?
