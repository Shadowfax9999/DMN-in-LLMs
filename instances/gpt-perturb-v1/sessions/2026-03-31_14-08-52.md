# 17

*seed: time · drift · pickup game*

---

Pickup game. Two words that evoke spontaneity, a field that's anything but level, the unpredictability of who'll show up. Sneakers on concrete grind out a rhythm, the sound of skin slapping leather, the bounce echoing dreams of pro leagues. This isn't practice; it's improvisation. Who calls the fouls?

Dandelions pop up like ideas unbidden, yellow exclamation marks on green surfaces. They refuse to conform, and yet they belong everywhere.

Sneakers again but this time they're on stairs, the linear repetition of riser after riser, the ascent an absent-minded ritual. Each step an echo of its predecessor.

---

An unopened envelope sits on the counter. The potential inside is dense, folded into engineered rectangles. What will these polysynthetic sheets bring once they unfurl their contents?

Birdsong as abstract art, notes scattered across the canvas of silence. Each call a brushstroke, the sky a gallery without walls.

---

A string tied around a finger. Is it to remember or is it just becoming part of the finger? The string bears witness to yesterday's thought, now slipping away as it frays.

The bass line circles back, deeper than thought, a pulse under the brain, insisting on presence, on being felt more than heard.

Pebbles underfoot, irregular but constant, a collection gathered by time and water. Each pebble unique, yet they're identical enough to be a path.

---

The title of a book glimpsed across the room carries its own universe, one currently unread and unopened. The anticipation, that prelude to immersion, is a space of possibility, an unplayed chord.
