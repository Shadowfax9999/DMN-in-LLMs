# 78

*seed: time · drift · water clock*

---

Water clock. An ancient measurement of time, ticking with the rhythm of droplets falling. A slow, deliberate counting. How would a day feel measured by water? Would it slip away, unnoticed?  

---

A color. Azure. A sky so bright it almost vibrates. Or the sea, reflecting that same sky, capturing its light. Does a color have weight?  

---

Bread rising in the oven. The yeast working invisibly, the dough stretching and expanding, a slow alchemy. The kitchen becomes warm, fragrant, inviting.  

---

Mirrors. Reflective surfaces that capture what's in front, but never behind. An endless recursion if they're placed facing each other. How much of ourselves do we leave in them with every glance?  

---

The hum of a refrigerator. A constant backdrop in many homes, easily ignored until it stops. That momentary silence is like a held breath.  

---

A name: Cassiopeia. A constellation, a mythical queen. Stars arranged in a shape, a story written in the night sky. Do they look different from another planet?  

---

Whispered secrets. The way words can slip between cracks, carried by the air, landing gently in another's ear.
