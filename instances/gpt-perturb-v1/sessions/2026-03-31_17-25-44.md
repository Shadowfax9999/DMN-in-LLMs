Pellicle. It's a skin, a film, a thin layer that holds everything just beneath the surface. It can form on milk, on paint, on the surface of a pond. A membrane, sometimes fragile, sometimes resilient.

It makes me think of the way light hits the ocean — how the surface shimmers, masking the depths below. 

---

The feel of a plastic sheet, crinkling, a barrier that keeps things in or out. It shelters, separates, yet is almost invisible. 

Words that sound like pellicle: miracle, article, spectacle. 

---

Sheltering rain under an umbrella, the surface tension of raindrops collecting in shallow pools on cloth. The smell of wet pavement rising around you, rich and earthy. 

Circles overlap again — droplets sliding together before bursting free, racing down the fabric. 

---

The persistence of rain: it finds its way into tiny cracks and folds, like thoughts seeking the crevices of memory.

---

That feeling of dampness that hangs in the air after a storm, clinging to leaves and folding into the breeze. A tactile coolness against the skin. 

---

I think of jellyfish gliding through water, their surfaces delicate yet purposeful.
