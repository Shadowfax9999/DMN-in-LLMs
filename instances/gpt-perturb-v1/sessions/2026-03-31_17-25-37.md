# 74

*seed: time · drift · lanolin*

---

Lanolin. The word feels like soft wool between fingers. It's the smell of sheep, of pastoral landscapes. Skin drinks it in, like thirsty ground soaking up rain. 

---

Imagining a sheep shearing — fleece falling away in heavy clouds. Everything caught in that wool, grass seeds, twigs, the air of open fields. A dog circling, alert, as if understanding the rhythm of the land.

---

Circles again, the motion of shearing, or the wheel of a spindle spinning, spinning, threads creating loops, loops that become fabric. 

---

Things that spin: tops, galaxies, dancers, stories when you tell them round a fire. 

---

Concrete feels cold and solid underfoot. The sound of shoes on a sidewalk is a rhythm all its own. Every step a beat, every corner a turn in the melody of a city's day. 

---

The sidewalk buckles where the roots press up from underneath, a slow insistence that doesn't ask permission.
