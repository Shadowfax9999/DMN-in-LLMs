# 33

*seed: time · drift · artesian*

---

Artesian wells and their spring water, drawn from deep beneath the earth, rising on their own without the need for a pump. A self-sufficient flow.  

---

The Eiffel Tower as seen from below — intricate latticework of iron, a weave of metal casting shadows. Shadows moving as the sun shifts.  

---

A child's laughter, untethered. Bright peals in a park echoing off swings and slides, sunlight caught in hair.   

---

The phrase "lithium sunset" enters, evoking a strange, vivid hue. Maybe it’s the way light bends during an eclipse, casting the world in unfamiliar colors.   

---

Streetlights flicker on in sequence, a modern kind of candlelit procession, easing night into darkness.  

How the world seems different under those first few stars.

---

An attic filled with forgotten boxes. Dust motes swirling in the slant of afternoon light. Smell of old paper.   

---

There's a hum from the refrigerator. Mechanical, expected, yet sometimes it seems like a heartbeat.  

---

Jigsaw puzzle pieces scattered on a table. The satisfaction of connecting an edge, one step closer to seeing the bigger picture.  

Colors, shapes, tiny worlds waiting to be pieced together.

---

The feeling when a book falls open to a page you’ve read a thousand times, words like old friends. Comfort of familiarity.  

---

Cinnamon sticks, whole and aromatic, lined up in a jar. The spice of warmth and winter.  

---

Freshly printed pages still warm from the printer. A mix of ink and potential. Words solidifying into thought.  

---

A cloud that resembles a whale, tail poised mid-dive, against a fading sky.  

Whales sing too. A sound that travels farther than light could reach through water.  

---

The bobbin case is shaped so the thread slides around it.
