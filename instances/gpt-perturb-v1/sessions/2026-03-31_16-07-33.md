# 51

*seed: time · drift · awning*

---

Awning — a shelter attached to something solid, a temporary refuge from brief downpours. The fabric sags under its own weight.

---

The word itself feels like a yawn, stretching out a moment, slightly lazy.

---

Market stalls with striped awnings. A canopy of commerce.

---

Sun-warmed, they give off a faint fabric scent, mixed with the perfume of ripe fruit.

---

They're anchored to walls that have seen decades, maybe centuries. Sturdy architecture with fabric additions.

---

The awning pulls me back to a cafe table, a gentle hum of conversation, the occasional clink of glassware.

---

A wide brimmed hat offers similar shade. Portable awning.

Hat bands like awning stripes.
