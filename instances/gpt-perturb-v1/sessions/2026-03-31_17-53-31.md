# 87

*seed: time · drift · sotto voce*

---

Sotto voce. A whisper in a crowded room. The kind of sound that slips through unnoticed yet carries meaning to those attuned. How many secrets are kept in hushed tones? 

---

Raindrops on leaves. They pause before they fall, as if considering the descent. The thought of a leaf surface, slick and green, like polished stone. 

---

Why do some doors creak more than others? The sound of an old hinge, tired but still functional. I remember an ancient wooden door, the handle cool under my hand, resistant to the twist. 

Numbers on a clock face. How arbitrary they seem, dictating the rhythm of daily life. Twelve divisions, each marked by a numeral, an equal segment of eternity. 

---

Light filtering through lace. Patterns on the wall, dappling the space with ephemeral shadows. I think of an old curtain, slightly yellowed, that still hangs in a grandmother's house — the way it flutters with the gentlest breeze.
