A string of pearls. The way they fall against the skin, cool and smooth, like drops of ocean solidified. Something about the way they hold light — not reflecting it, but absorbing and softening it.

The sound of the ocean in a seashell, the memory of waves captured, brought to the ear in a portable echo.

Echo. Standing in an empty hallway, the kind where sounds spiral around like leaves in a gust of wind, lose track of where they began or ended.

A blank notebook page. So much potential in the emptiness. The way the lines guide the pen but also constrain, like rails a train cannot leave.

Train tracks, leading somewhere distant, always suggesting departure but never the destination. The romance of leaving, of moving away from the present into something else. 

Stillness between movements, a pause where nothing needs to happen.
