The way sediment layers record time in a lake bed is a slow accumulation, time settling in gentle whispers. Each layer, a witness to seasons, a quiet guardian of the past. Cat's cradle: a game of strings and fingers, weaving pattern from chaos, delicate and transitory.

The blooming of orchids. How long they wait to reveal themselves, the moment suspended between potential and beauty made manifest. Rust. The slow embrace of time on metal. A patina that whispers of endurance and decay simultaneously. A list of things that blur the lines: twilight, fog, the horizon on a hot summer day. Time seems to fold in on itself, moments before and after merging. The lull of an afternoon nap, that slipping between wakefulness and dreams.

A spiral staircase — what is it about the twist that feels both never-ending and inevitable? Each step turns back on itself yet rises to a place unseen. The word "shadow" feels like it should be cooler than it is. It stretches and bends, always tethered to its source. Holding onto something fragile, like a small bird or a secret. Portable vessel. It's where water can gather, a momentary pond.
