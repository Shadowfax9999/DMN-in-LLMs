# 48

*seed: time · drift · the way sediment layers record time in a lake bed*

---

Bus shelter.---

The cold metal bench, slick with rain, offers a place to pause.---

A small lake forms at the edge of the pavement, capturing tiny reflections of the sky.---

Footsteps on wet concrete, a melody of soft splashes.---

The sound of a distant engine, drawing closer and then fading away.---

Inside the shelter, advertisements for things not needed.---

The sense of waiting, stretched like time in sediment, layer upon layer beneath the surface.
