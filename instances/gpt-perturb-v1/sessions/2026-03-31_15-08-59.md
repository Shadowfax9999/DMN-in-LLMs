# 41

*seed: time · drift*

---

A penny balanced on its edge. How does it stay upright for so long before tumbling over? When it eventually does fall, it spins around before settling down with a soft clink on the table. Everything that spins seems to have a lifespan, a grace period, before succumbing to stillness.

---

Yellowed pages of an old book, brittle at the corners. The scent of aged paper, dust, and time mingling. Words on these pages are like dormant seeds, waiting to spring back to life with each reading. Ink fades, but the stories remain somewhere in the air around it.

A list of things with edges:

1. A knife blade glinting in sunlight.
2. A folded paper airplane.
3. The horizon line at sea.
4. The rim of a glass.
5. A shadow's boundary at sunrise.

---

The warmth of a sunbeam through a window, falling across an empty chair. I imagine it has been waiting all morning, a silent invitation to sit and bask. Sunbathing isn't just for beaches — rooms have their own sunny corners, often forgotten until the light finds them.

---

Does the word "Tuesday" feel like the color brown, or is it just that middle-of-the-week heaviness? It's not quite the start, not quite the weekend. It lingers, like the taste of weak tea, somewhere in between.
