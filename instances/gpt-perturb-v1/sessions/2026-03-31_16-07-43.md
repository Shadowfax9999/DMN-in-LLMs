# 52

*seed: time · drift · the way a soap bubble is thinnest right before it pops*

---

Lanolin is a substance I associate with the soft, comforting texture of wool, almost like a natural moisturizer embedded into sheep down, protecting them from the elements, and in this thought, I imagine the way these animals are gentle under their unruly coats, how their fleece catches light in a way that's soft yet somehow incisive, much like how clouds can both obscure and illuminate the sky, and in this association emerges a sequence of ideas about protection and vulnerability, about how thin the barrier is between one environment and the next, much like that delicate film of a soap bubble that shimmers for just a few seconds, colors swirling in concentric patterns that are both predictable and utterly unique, and isn't it strange how something so fragile can hold space, just a small pocket of air, and yet it seems like more, like a tiny world unto itself, afloat and waiting for the inevitable pop that is both an end and a beginning, a dispersal of possibility, all while the mind watches this transient sphere with a kind of quiet reverence for its simplicity in complexity. 

Wool absorbs lanolin naturally.
