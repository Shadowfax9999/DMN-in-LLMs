# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

Barcodes. Black and white lines, patterns readable by machines, holding information in their stripes. What if you could read them with your eyes?

---

A single bath tile on a wall. Smooth, cool to the touch. They multiply into patterns, covering the room. Each one, a small square, a part of a larger picture.

---

The smell of rain on asphalt. A scent that rises with the first few drops, the earth drinking in the water. There's a name for it, isn't there?

---

Coins left on a table. Silver and copper disks, each with a weight and worth. They lie scattered, the remnants of change from a purchase.

---

The sound of a page turning. Paper sliding against paper, breaking the silence of a room. A small sound, yet so distinct.

---

An empty chair at a dining table. The anticipation of someone yet to arrive, or the memory of someone who's left.

---

Kaleidoscopes. Tiny mirrors and colored beads, twisting and turning. An ever-changing mosaic inside a tube.

---

A single thread pulled from a fabric. How it travels, unraveling a surface, a line drawing attention to a hidden structure. 

---

Plastic cutlery, lightweight and disposable. Forks and knives that mimic metal, but without its heft. They wait at picnics, ready for use, but are soon forgotten.
