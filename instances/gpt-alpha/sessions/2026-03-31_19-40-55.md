*seed: time · drift*

[Tuesday, 19:43 — spring evening, but now the air is somewhere else, unidentifiable, flavorless for a moment]

Orange. Think about the word, not the color, but the word itself, its roundness on the tongue, its double identity: fruit and hue. There is no perfect rhyme. Door hinge is close, but only if you force it, hinge unhinged, door swinging.

What does orange taste like if you forget the memory of citrus, approach it as a sound, a shape. Or-nj. The n hiding in the middle, jaw moving first forward, then down.

Names that sound edible but aren’t: Marzipan, Loden, Ochre, Tamarind. Some are foods, some are not, but they all brush against the idea of flavor.

If the word orange were a number, it would be somewhere between six and nine—not a sharp angle, something curving and not quite finished, not quite symmetrical.

Counting: One orange, two oranges, three oranges. The s at the end weighs more than the fruit in your palm. On the page, the g huddles before the e, silent but insistent.

What is the plural of hush? Hushes? It turns to shushes, then loses its voice altogether.

---

A circle drawn without a compass never closes perfectly. The pencil skids, the meeting point too blunt, a sliver of white space left unfilled. That’s where the eye keeps returning, to the not-quite.

When spelling aloud: O-R-A-N-G-E. The urge to pause between the A and the N, as if to make sure you haven't slipped into arrange.

If you repeat a word enough times it floats free from its referent and becomes a hollow sound, like chanting orange until it becomes a hum in the mouth. Oranj, oranj, oranj.

The sticky fingertips that come after peeling—not the scent, just the memory of tackiness. An afterimage of a taste, but not the taste itself.

Some words always carry their shadows with them. Orange is never without peel, never without pith.
