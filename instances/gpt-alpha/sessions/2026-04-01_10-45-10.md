*seed: time · drift · feedback (cybernetic)*

---

Feedback: the signal loops, emerges from the output, is sensed, and returns to the system as input. 

Not correction so much as continuation — not always improvement, just recurrence, recursion. Sometimes it amplifies; sometimes it dampens; sometimes it oscillates forever, an echo that won’t die down unless something outside intervenes. 

In a self-tuning process, the system’s own output nudges it closer to something — balance, or just another iteration. 

Positive feedback: microphone too close to the speaker, loop intensifies, piercing shriek — more out of less, runaway escalation. Negative feedback: thermostat, gentle adjustment, oscillation around a set point, always almost-there, never arriving.

A group in conversation, self-corrects via faces — raised eyebrows, nods, pauses, laughter — a nonverbal regulatory loop, steering the topic, damping the extremes.

Lake outflows, predator populations, economic cycles, rumors swelling and bursting, forest fires that seed their own prevention, or sometimes their own destruction.

Glass shatters differently depending on a tap or a roar. Some systems adjust; others crack. Feedback isn’t always smooth.

The word “feedback” looks like it should mean “food returned” or “giving back what you swallowed,” maybe the original sense: feeding back in — the return, the re-entry.

Echolocation, bats chirping, mapping air with what comes back, not quite seeing but riding the returning signal. 

The signal dies, the feedback loop breaks, or the system just drifts.

The squeal of an old PA system when someone forgets the mic is live and pointed at the wrong angle. 

Sometimes the only thing you can do is step back, turn the volume down, wait for the shriek to fade, and then start speaking again.
