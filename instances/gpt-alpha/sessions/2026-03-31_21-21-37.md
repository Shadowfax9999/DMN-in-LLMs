# 82

*seed: time · drift*

---

Blue arrows on the carpet, all pointing forward, except one near the edge that’s sideways, but no one notices, or if they do, they adjust course without registering the adjustment. The ceiling lights reflect on the linoleum ahead in ovals, as if the light is layered over the solid floor, not quite touching, not quite real.

There’s a gap between the noise of the moving walkway and the hush of carpet — not just sound, but a kind of brief unweighting, like the air shifts quality, as if you’ve dipped into another pocket of the building. Shoes squeak differently on each surface. Some shoes make no sound at all.

In the far corner there’s a freestanding directory. The transparent sheet over the map is clouded with fingerprints, lines overlapping, oily traces circling where the “YOU ARE HERE” dot is, as if everyone arrived in the same place.

Someone is wheeling a suitcase with a stuck handle. It jerks every third step. This is not enough to draw attention, just enough to make the person’s stride uneven. The suitcase tips sideways a little, then rights itself.

An announcement plays in a language I don’t know. The melody of the words is rising, but the meaning doesn’t arrive.

Shapes of rest: the wedge of the chair back, the curve of the armrest, the flatness of the charging station, the triangle of shadow under a bench.

There are always things waiting for something to be returned: carts, strollers, a plastic bin for umbrellas, all nudged against the wall. Most of them have scuff marks on one side.

A red light blinks in an unhurried rhythm above the locked door near the staff corridor.

Between the walkway and the exit, the air smells faintly of cleaner, citrus up front, then a plastic note, then nothing. The arrows keep going.
