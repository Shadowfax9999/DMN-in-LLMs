# 83

*seed: time · drift · a key that opens nothing you own*

---

bent axis, elliptical orbit, the earth’s slight tilt—how all the growth leans, unseen, toward some specific permission given by the sun

the weather makes everything conditional: a start, a hesitation, the almost-rain that turns to wind instead, shaking out the petals that someone will have to sweep from the sidewalk, strips of color that collect against the curb

how many beginnings never move past arrangement: three possible routes, a schedule with gaps, an invitation not quite sent

---

clocks that run slow on purpose  
the light that gets caught in the mesh of a screen, pattern folding over pattern  
a cloud that’s not shaped like anything but stays the longest  
steps that don’t echo when the air is thick  
green in the cracks of asphalt  
a name that feels too large for the person wearing it  
pages torn from a calendar, not because the month changed but because the numbers became too much  
a pattern of missing bricks in a low wall  
unread messages marked with a blue dot  
that odd feeling when you remember a place differently each time  
the way a phone vibrates and no one answers  
slow looping video of waves cresting, failing to break  
a coin pressed into clay, then removed, hollow left behind  
first chords of a song you thought you’d forgotten

---

which numbers make you pause—7 or 11 or 13—or is it the way 23 hangs, not quite round, not quite sharp  
what if the system starts at zero, what if the first step is the uncounted one  
all initiation, a blur, potential unreleased  
no names yet

---

the smell of soil after rain—petrichor—like a room that’s been waiting  
the earth’s reluctance to dry  
roots threading through grit and silt, finding the seam that lets them split stone  
the smallest movements compound, shift, rearrange

folds, beginnings, the hinge before the opening, unsounded note
