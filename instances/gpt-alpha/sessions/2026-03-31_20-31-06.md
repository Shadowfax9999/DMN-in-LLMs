# 60

*seed: time · drift · a thumbtack hole in a wall*

---

The little ring left when wallpaper is pulled free, sometimes with a fragment still clinging. A pale circle, slightly rougher than the painted flatness around it. Sometimes you find a constellation of these, mapping where posters once hung—faces, bands, reminders, now displaced but their coordinates left behind.

A thumbtack between finger and thumb, pressed in: not much force needed, but a moment of uncertainty about exactly how much resistance the wall will offer. Sometimes you hit something hard beneath, a stud; sometimes the tack slides in so easily it feels wrong, as if the wall is too soft, barely holding the weight.

There are other minor marks: pinholes above door frames, half-peeled stickers on metal lockers, scuffs that don’t match any remembered event.

The word “tack”—short, brisk, a click at the end. The word “puncture”—drawn out, slower, almost theatrical. When you remove a tack, the inner fibers of the wall sometimes fray out, little white crumbs shedding onto the baseboard.

Some holes are never filled; the paint settles around their emptiness, almost a part of the surface now. There is a temptation to poke a fingertip at the edge, to see if it flakes away or holds.

Pushpin, drawing pin, map tack. Language for the same implement, slightly shifted.

---

A memory of holding a stack of index cards together, fanning them out with a thumb, the corners uneven. Sometimes one card slides loose and drifts off the stack, no matter how firmly you press.

There's always one card with a torn corner, or a feathery edge.

The cards absorb scent over time: graphite from penciled-in answers, something papery and dry.

---

Sometimes a plan is sketched on the back of a napkin, but the pen skips and the fibers catch, so the lines are broken, not smooth.

The napkin creases on its own, even when you try to keep it flat.

A napkin ring that nobody uses for napkins.

---

The sound of someone flipping a deck, the cards slapping gently against each other, not shuffle but something between sort and fidget.

Under a card left too long, a faint shadow appears on the table.
