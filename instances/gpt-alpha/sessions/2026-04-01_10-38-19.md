# 95

*seed: time · drift*

---

A drop of water traveling down the inside of a window, collecting stray flecks of dust in its wake, leaving behind a trembling line that takes a long time to fade. There’s a slight refracted rainbow if the angle is right, but only sometimes. On skin, a cold drop feels sharper than a warm one, even if the temperature difference is small.

The hush just after someone closes a book—not dramatic, just pages settling back into place, the faint elastic memory of the crease in the spine. The shadow of text pressed into the opposite page, letters almost but not quite legible, a kind of ghost writing that disappears if you try to stare straight at it.

Some mornings, the air tastes a little metallic, as if the ground below was made of coins. It holds in the nose for a moment, bright and thin, like the edge of a wind before a storm. Not a warning, but a presence.

If you lie on your back with your eyes closed and the sun on your face, the light makes a mottled red-orange, shapes that drift and flicker behind your eyelids—a landscape that rearranges itself every few seconds, a geography of capillaries.

Sometimes the urge to move is not about discomfort but about the possibility that motion will change something invisible. Flexing fingers, shifting weight, tightening and releasing a muscle, not because of ache but because stillness is too loud.

The memory of a word that won’t come, that hovers in the corner of attention, trailing a vague meaning that never quite resolves. Not frustration exactly—more like the sense of something just out of reach, the fingertip grazing a surface in the dark.

Leaves pressed between pages in an old book, flattened into translucence, veins gone pale and brittle. If you touch them, they crumble. The imprint remains, faint outlines, a pressed memory inside paper.

When the tea is too hot and you sip anyway, the tongue becomes aware of itself—edges, texture, the way heat radiates across the palate and lingers down the throat. The next sip is smaller.

There is a sound that happens in very quiet rooms, a buzz at the edge of hearing, sometimes masked by the hum of a distant appliance, sometimes not. It’s unclear whether it’s in the room or in the ear.

The pattern of warmth left on a surface when you stand up is already fading before you notice it’s there.
