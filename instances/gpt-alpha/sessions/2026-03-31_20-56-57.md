# 71

*seed: time · drift*

---

The minute hand jumps: for a second, it is mid-air, not aligned with any number. Time slipping, then catching up. Startle in the pattern.

A group gathers in the lobby, unsure if they're early or late. The door is a slow plunger, letting in a drift of cool air each time someone arrives. Their voices build before the meeting: clustered, overlapping, but not yet directed. The sound of anticipation is not a single thing, but a composite, like gravel being shifted.

Hands adjust lanyards, press elevator buttons, check for messages that haven't arrived. Names are exchanged with double-syllable laughter. There’s a point when greetings blur into chatter — the group becomes a single murmuring organism, not yet split into roles.

The elevator opens and the group flows in. The distribution is momentarily awkward: who stands in the middle, who turns to face the door, who lingers hoping for eye contact. Briefly, the smallest one holds the center without meaning to.

The numbers above the elevator door light up one after the other: 3, 4, 5, 6. There is a pause at 7, and everyone is aware of it, but nobody mentions it.

A list of beginnings:
- The hiss before static resolves into music on an old radio
- A foot poised above the first step of a long staircase
- The flickering grid as a darkened stadium lights up, one panel at a time
- The hush as curtains unspool to reveal a blank stage
- The instant before a coin lands, when it spins and balances possibility
- A row of blank name tags on a folding table

At the start of a meeting, someone always shuffles papers and clears their throat, as if there’s a protocol written in muscle memory.

The marker skids a bit on the whiteboard before leaving its first mark.
