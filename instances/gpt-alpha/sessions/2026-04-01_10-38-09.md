# 94

*seed: time · drift*

---

Taste that isn’t taste: when cold air rushes against the back of your throat on a winter walk, it almost stings, but it isn’t sharpness you could name, or heat, or cold exactly—more like the shape of sensation minus any particular flavor, a pressure that moves through the body as a gradient, settling behind the eyes, a kind of tidal feeling.  
Sometimes I notice the same thing happens when I hold my breath just a little too long, a spacey shimmer at the edge of vision. Not dizziness—just the sense of having come to the edge of what’s comfortable, and then a step further, a new texture in the head.

A word for the moment just before sneezing? Or just before waking, half-suspended, neither deep nor fully back.  

Napping in sunlight, the world is bright even through closed eyelids, red-orange through the lids, warmth on the face, thoughts not yet themselves. Not sleeping, but not doing either. The drift of images across half-consciousness—someone’s voice (maybe a memory, maybe not), the familiarity of a certain place, then none of it makes sense and it slips away. Sometimes it feels like tracing outlines in fog: you think you were holding something, but your hands are empty.

The way a limb tingles as circulation returns, the pins-and-needles that isn’t pain but a demand for attention. It’s impossible to hold still when it happens—tiny, involuntary twitches, the body insisting on movement even when you try to ignore it.

Night sweats: not the dramatic kind, just a sudden sense of heat, a patch on the pillow, the air against damp skin feeling newly cold.

How many sensations exist that have no proper name—edges and in-betweens, the pause before relief, the moment after relief has faded but the body remembers it for a few seconds more.

There must be a word for the feeling of something almost arriving, almost happening, almost taking shape. There must be a word for the shapeless ache of nearly.  

The color of bright light behind thin skin.  
The taste of temperature.
