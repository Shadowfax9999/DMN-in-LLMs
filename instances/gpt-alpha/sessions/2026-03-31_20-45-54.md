# 66

*seed: time · drift · singing in the car alone*

---

The sound is different in there. The interior softens it, the plastic and fabric absorbing the sharper edges, everything a little round at the top and bottom. Sometimes you harmonize without thinking about it. Sometimes you try to follow exactly, but your voice doesn’t quite reach the same note, so it slides under, makes its own path. The windshield fogs up if you keep the air too low, a drifting cloud in the upper corners.

Hands on the wheel, tapping the beat, sometimes left hand, sometimes right, depends which turn is coming. The song ends and you’re not ready, so you replay it. Sometimes you catch yourself singing with more force because you think nobody can hear, but then you pull up at the light and someone in the next lane looks over. You mouth the chorus anyway, lips barely moving now, face going neutral at the bridge.

Sometimes the song calls up an image — a neon sign on a diner, rain hitting the roof, a stretch of highway, specific or not. You picture the music video for a song that never had one. Sometimes the voice trembles on a high note and you decide it’s fine to leave the wrong note hanging, carried forward by the next line.

The seat belt presses across your chest in a way you only notice when singing, because you take a deeper breath, and it resists. There is a particular point where your breath runs out in the phrase, earlier than you expected, so the last note falls away quietly.

The wiper blades click in time, almost syncing, almost not. The car, a kind of imperfect auditorium, made of spilled change, receipts, crumbs in the seam between gear shift and vinyl cup holder, but the song fills it anyway.

Sometimes, for a moment, it feels like the chorus could be the whole world, all of it inside that shell of moving metal and glass. The turn signal ticks, the song goes on, there is nothing to remember or forget.
