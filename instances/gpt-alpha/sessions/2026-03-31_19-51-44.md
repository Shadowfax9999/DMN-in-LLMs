# 41

*seed: time · drift*

---

The ridges on your thumb, pressed into smooth plastic. There’s a faint click, but it isn’t the sound of anything starting, just the springiness of the mechanism. A small twist in the wrist, the way it feels when you pick up an object without looking — the edges surprise you, cold or warm, heavier or lighter than you’d decided in advance. The shape arrives through your skin in fragments, not all at once.

The smell of shoe rubber on a gym floor is different from the smell of kneeling near the door, peeling off wet sneakers onto a textured mat. There’s a kind of hush made from all the little squeaks, a shuffle-noise that only happens in wide open rooms. The laces drag against the tongue — soft, then stiffer, then the snap when you tug them tight.

---

Sometimes you stand perfectly still, not because you are waiting, but because your weight shifts until it finds a place and the rest of you follows. In that pause, the inside of your mouth notices a trace of mint, or something left behind from before. Saliva thickens, or thins. A breath moves through the nose and cools the top of the palate. The world outside the body can feel like it’s wrapped in gauze.

There’s a dull ache behind the right knee, reminding itself to exist.

The surface of the desk is a little sticky, but only where your wrist rested too long. If you run your finger over it, there is resistance, a small drag, then smoothness again.

A light flickers, unnoticed at first, then insistently, as if it means something more than interruption.

---

There’s a pulse at the base of the thumb that doesn’t match the beat in the chest.
