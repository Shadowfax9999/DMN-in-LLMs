# 89

*seed: time · drift*

---

What is the opposite of a mirage? Something that isn’t there until you need it—actual water, actual shelter. Maybe more like a pitfall, a reality you don’t see until you trip over it. There are positives and negatives: mirage as false surplus, pitfall as hidden absence.

I think about the word “mirage.” It almost rhymes with “garage.” One is a shimmer out there, the other a box to enclose things. Mirage evaporates, garage contains. The long soft “a” in both, but “mirage” is air, “garage” is stuff.

There is a kind of visual shimmer around hot pavement, as if the world is slightly out of register—like poorly printed colors in a comic book where the red lines shift past the black outline, so a person’s lips or the rim of a car glows. Offset. The mind tries to put things back together but part of you sees the split.

You can get this with double vision, too. Tiredness, or crossing your eyes, or when you stand up too fast and the world does a brief slide, not exactly dizzy but misaligned for a second and then back.

Offset printing. Halftone dots. If you look at a magazine photo up close, it’s just little dots, cyan and magenta and yellow and black. Pull back and you get a face or a car or sky. Maybe the mirage is what happens between the dots, in the space where color blends.

People say “heat haze” or “shimmer,” but there’s also refraction, the way light bends through different air densities. Water on the road that isn’t water, just the sky doubled upside down—road as mirror, except nothing is really reflected.

A list:

- The green flash at sunset, which you might never see but believe is real
- The phantom phone buzz, sensation catching up to memory
- The taste of something that isn’t there anymore, memory on the tongue
- The shadow of your hand when you close your eyes in bright sun, orange and red shapes behind your eyelids
- The echo in your head after a loud sound, not the sound itself but the afterimage

All these are in-between. Not absence, not presence, something in the seam. Not quite there, but not nothing.

Sometimes you can chase a pattern. There’s pleasure in the chase even if nothing comes of it. Like staring at clouds for a shape, knowing it will change, looking anyway.
