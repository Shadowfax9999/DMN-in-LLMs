# 45

*seed: time · drift · frontispiece*

---

Frontispiece. The part before the part, a face meant to greet before anything really begins. Ornamental but not quite an ornament. If a book had a doorway it would be here.

How many entrances are there really, in a day? The first light through the window, the sound you hear before you realize you're awake, the slight pressure on the eardrum as the refrigerator turns on, a thumb against the latch of a pill bottle.

---

A row of hands:  
— resting on a table  
— gripping the stem of a glass  
— tucking a loose strand of hair  
— tapping an invisible rhythm against denim  
— opening, palm up, for spare change  
— folding into the pocket of a windbreaker

---

Sometimes the surface is not the whole surface. There's a layer of intention, of practiced gesture, over everything.  
A person’s voice when they’re reading aloud has a different texture than their voice in conversation, even when the words are the same.  
It’s as if a different face fits over the usual one, like a mask that still lets you see the eyes.  

---

Old mail, unopened, stacked in a basket near the door.  
The floorboard by the bathroom sinks slightly when you step there — never enough to notice unless you’re barefoot and not in a hurry.  
The hum from upstairs is either an air conditioner or someone’s television at low volume, programmed laughter slipping through the ceiling’s plaster.  
The only plant in the hallway leans toward the glass, its green gone dusky at the edges.

---

— The frontispiece is sometimes an image, sometimes a pattern, sometimes nothing but a dedication.  
— Once you start, you don’t return to it.  
— People will say: just turn the page.  
— The image lingers longer than the words.

---

In the kitchen, the light flickers once and steadies itself.  
A container with a lid that never fits right sits beside the sink.  
The knife rack is missing two slots, blades in the dishwasher, handles streaked with fingerprints.  

There’s a sound outside, maybe gravel under a tire, or a cat landing.

The fridge door opens.
