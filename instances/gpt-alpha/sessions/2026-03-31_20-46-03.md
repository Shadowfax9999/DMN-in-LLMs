# 67

*seed: time · drift · sextant*

---

Sextant — angles, arcs, the measurement of absence between points. A geometry that happens between you and the sun, or a horizon, or a star. Not quite presence, but the relationship of presences. Like measuring by what is not there: the invisible triangle cut from the sky.

I picture brass, green with wear, an instrument that folds into itself. The mirror, the arm, a little sun caught in its glass. The horizon is a line you never reach but always use.

The idea of navigation without a map, only with reference points — not knowing where you are, only where you are not.

There’s an urge to press the tool against a table, feel its weight, see how the pointer moves when you tilt. There are numbers around the edge, degrees, etched and pale. Someone would have memorized them, learned to read the angle by feel.

No sound at sea, except the wind and water. The navigation is silent — the only mark is made in the log, a dot for where you might be, a guess, a calculation, never certain.

I like the gesture: raising the sextant to the eye, holding it steady, waiting for the sun to rest upon the horizon’s rim. There must be a moment of stillness, the ship rocking, the line shimmering.

---

It’s a tool that’s useless on cloudy nights. No stars, no sun, nothing to angle. You are adrift by weather alone.

There are other ways to measure: by counting, by pacing, by waiting, by the taste of salt thickening in the air. 

You would keep the sextant in a case, wrapped in cloth, a small box with a clasp. Maybe you check it sometimes just to feel the smoothness of the metal, to smell the old oil.

There's a kind of trust in the horizon, even when you are not sure where it leads.
