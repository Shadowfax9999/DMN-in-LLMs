# 96

*seed: time · drift · collapse*

---

Collapse: the syllables fold in, the mouth shuts down at the back, lips close. Strange that "collapse" is a word for falling inward, but saying it pushes outward: co-laps.

There are things that only come apart once. Rice cakes—press them and they crumble, not shatter. Chalk dust in the palm.

The feeling of knees buckling, not from exhaustion but from some unknown impulse, like your body just rehearsed the end of a sentence.

A stack of cards tipping over. Sometimes they splay out in a fan; sometimes just a clump, nothing graceful about it.

Paper lanterns after rain, the way the thin walls give way, shape becoming slack, color sinking into the wrinkles.

---

“Collapse” has a sister word, “implode,” quieter but more violent.

There are buildings that lean for decades before falling, but a sentence can collapse in an instant, just by taking a turn you didn't expect.

Breath, at the very bottom of exhale, ribs folding in, the soft pause before the next inhale. Maybe even there, a tiny collapse.

There’s a way a night collapses, not just the noise, but the possibilities. Hours you thought you'd have, suddenly missing, air pressed out.

Plastic straws after biting: the walls cave in but hold the crease.

The opposite of collapse is not always "holding up"—sometimes it's simply spreading out, pressure finding its way to the edges.

Collapsed star, black hole, gravity swallowing everything, but that’s too far, too big.

Eggshell in the hand, pressure distributed just right, unbroken until the pressure points find each other.

A sweater, taken off and thrown, soft heap in the chair. The shape it makes is never the shape it had.

When you stop spinning, there’s a moment the world folds in on itself and then, slowly, resumes its place.

There are days that feel like a structure held up by one thin beam, but the beam is a mood, or a phone call, or the certainty that the kettle will boil.

After a storm, the air is heavier, as if the sky itself collapsed a little, pressing close to the ground.
