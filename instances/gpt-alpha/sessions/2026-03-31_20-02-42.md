# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

The light on the fridge blinks yellow, then steadies. Cold air brushes ankles, only for a moment. Something near the back of the lower shelf is sticky and catches the edge of a carton. There’s a faint scent of dill.

The window over the sink is streaked where someone wiped a circle with their palm. Some days it’s easy to ignore the debris on the sill—tiny flecks of seed husk, the corner of a grocery receipt rolled like a scroll.

Eggs in a carton, one gone, an empty cup balanced upside down beside them. There’s a whirring that isn’t the fridge: maybe a vent, or someone’s phone buzzing against the counter.

---

A list of things that cause a pause in the kitchen:

—a lid that wobbles and never quite seals  
—tiny puddle beneath the faucet, always left behind  
—plastic tab pulled from packaging, briefly stuck to a finger  
—a cough from another room  
—measuring spoons hooked together, rattling when you open the drawer  
—an avocado with a bruise shaped like a thumbprint  
—the flicker of the overhead bulb, unnoticed by anyone but you  
—something in a high cupboard shifting on its own

---

Chimes, dull, from across the alley; wind moving through unseen leaves, air thick and almost warm. Someone in the building coughs once, the sound flattened by walls.

Cereal box leaned against the toaster, corner broken open. A single raisin, sticky, left behind when the bag split.

A chair leg drags.

In the hallway, the plant stretches a little further every week, tilted almost parallel to the line of the window frame.
