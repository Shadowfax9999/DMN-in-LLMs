# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a tidemark left by winter boots just inside the door, salt finding seams in the floorboards, white crystals mapping the places where feet pause. Nothing ever lines up quite square — baseboards, doorframes, always some slight separation. The gap under the door whistles when the weather shifts. The last echo of something cooked hours ago lingers at ceiling height, higher up than you’d think, not really garlic or onion anymore, just drift. 

The top of the radiator is warm, but dust gathers in the narrow slots, fuzzed over, gray. If you press your thumb into the carpet, the pile smooths out instantly, but flecks of lint collect at the border where it meets the wall. Corners of a room are always darker than the rest, no matter how many lamps you use.

The window latch is stiff. Paint has sealed part of it in place, a reminder of a season when the sash wouldn't stay put, or maybe when the draft became too much. The sound of someone moving above you is muffled but definite, each step a rearrangement of possibility — here, not here, going, returning.

Sometimes, when voices carry down the hallway, you catch only the melody, not the words, sentences ribboning past. 

On a clear evening the same square of light creeps across the floor, always starting in a different place as the calendar turns. 

The smell outside after dark changes suddenly — sharper, almost metallic, as if the grass itself is holding its breath. 

The clock on the wall marks time with a softened tick, easy to lose in the hush, but it is always there, touching every minute as it passes.
