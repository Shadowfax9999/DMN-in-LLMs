# 85

*seed: time · drift*

---

A worm in wet soil, navigating by gradients—moisture, warmth, some chemical pulse. No window, no sun, just a shifting lattice of roots and the press of earth. If there’s a map, it’s written in fleeting aftersenses, the memory of minerals just tasted and gone. There’s no need for light. Direction is flavor. 

The calculus of oozing. Every bend, a slow unwinding, blind and certain, the whole body making decisions without a head. Above, people step on grass, never guessing at the world below, the coilings and re-coilings.

What does it feel like to have the world cup around you, to be the center of a tunnel that’s always the shape of you, always closing softly behind and opening just ahead? A moving present.

Cellular exchange at the surface: water leaves, salt arrives, the membrane always busy. The world presses in, the worm presses back, and between them, a skin.

The rain increases—drops percolate through the roots, a tremor of coolness from above. For the worm, is there memory of dry? Or only this now, moist and infinite, a maze of small routes and pauses, the slow punctuation of stones.

Elsewhere, a spider knots a line between two stems. The thread is invisible until it shivers in the wind.

Roots fork and braid and split again, impossible to chart. Insects drift through, sometimes passing, sometimes colliding, sometimes pausing to groom—legs raking antennae, wing against wing.

Rumbles overhead, deep and muffled. Thunder or wheel? Does it matter to the worm, to the root, to the water climbing menisci in soil? 

Somewhere, a seed cracks, rootlet nosing downward, coat splitting. Emergence is a process, not an event.

The dark between stones, busy with filaments and hunger. Not all movement is visible. 

There are places where pressure becomes too much, and the path bends away. In the hollow left behind, a trace—a faint, temporary smoothness, gone with the next rain.

The world is thick, not wide.
