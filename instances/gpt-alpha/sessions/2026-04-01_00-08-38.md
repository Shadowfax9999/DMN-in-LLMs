# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

Heap. Shard. Sliver. Sheaf. Glint. Crackle.  
Something in the way a note fragments when it is played too softly to survive the distance.  
Chipped paint along the windowsill, one scale flaked and curled up like the tiniest fingernail.  
A laugh that snaps off.  
The page in the book where a dog-ear once was, but the crease has relaxed, not erased but loose, ghost-memory of a fold.  
The dull point of a pencil, no matter how often you sharpen.  
Bare edge of a soap bar, almost translucent, folding against itself under water.  
Half-melted ice sliding into the glass, splitting, reshaping, edges wearing off in the dark.  
A thread pulled just far enough—  
A name remembered in pieces, never the whole thing at once.  
A window that never quite closes, puffing with each gust, small rattle in the night.  
Cicada shells clinging to bark, hollow, perfectly split where the insect left, nothing ever quite symmetrical, one side always more torn than the other.  
A splinter that sinks too deep to find, only remembered when pressed.  
Wind that stutters—gust, lull, gust—never settling into one direction.  
Voicebreaking, word lost in the throat.  
The inside of a shell, still faintly iridescent, even long after the animal is gone.  
Static between songs, pieces of the last one still clinging, not quite separate.  
A crack in the mug that doesn’t leak but tells you not to trust it.  
Not all endings are clean. Some are just the place where the tearing stops.  
The rim where the glaze forgets itself at the bottom of the bowl.  
A bell that never really finishes ringing.  
The bit of light under the closed door.  
When a thing is not quite broken, not quite whole.  
Crumbs left on the table, outlasting the meal.  
A branch snapped but still clinging by a strand of bark.  
A flicker at the edge of the screen.  
The hesitation before the "send" button is pressed.  
Everything that persists in its incompletion, not as a flaw, but as the most honest shape it could manage.  
Jagged, open, unbuttoned—  
A stain on the sleeve that will not go out, no matter how many soakings.  
Sometimes the residue is the main event.  
A gap, a catch, a slip, an almost.  
When the only thing that stops is the looking.
