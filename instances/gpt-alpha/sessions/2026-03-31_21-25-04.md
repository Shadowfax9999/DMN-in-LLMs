# 84

*seed: time · drift · train delay*

---

Train delay, platform hum, digital boards click to a new time, 19:42, then 19:55, then blank.  
A pause thickens the air, not static but pressed, all that assembled motion arrested, nowhere to stream out.  
Some people check their phones again, as if the update will rupture the impasse, as if knowing means movement.  
Some stay in the cloud of their own music, headphones on, a rhythm contained, marking time in their private measure.  
Shoes shift, a bag unzips, zippers like small metallic insects.  
The track number repeats in the air, tinny and automated, a voice both robotic and weary.  
A girl draws a shape on the fogged glass, then rubs it away with her sleeve.  
Light from the overhead fixtures makes everything seem more tired, surfaces shining with the effort of reflection.

Something flickers in the corner: a rat moving quickly, then gone.  
The sound of a distant announcement blends with the scrape of a rolling suitcase.  
No one speaks directly about the delay, but people look up at the same moment, faces tilting toward the possibility of a change.  
Paper cup, crumpled receipt, a bouquet of tickets held together with a paperclip.  
The smell of sugar and old coffee, metal rails cooling in the dark.  
For a second, everything is still.  
The doors open but nothing boards.  
A breath held not in lungs but in the space itself.

One woman paces always to the same square tile and back, an orbit dictated by anxiety or habit or both.  
A teething baby gnaws at a keychain, drool silvering the car-shaped fob.  
Someone sneezes, sharp and echoing.  
The clock above track 3 is running fast, or maybe it just seems that way.  
A large digital ad cycles through faces: smiling, smiling, smiling, always inviting but not for you.

A plastic bag gets caught on a breeze, lifts, settles near the yellow warning line.  
A man closes his laptop with a soft, padded clap.  
Birds outside the glass dome peck at leftover crumbs, undeterred by human waiting.  
The boards update all at once, like an array of eyes blinking in relief.

The train pulls in with a rush of wind that tastes metallic and cold.  
There is a moment when everyone is almost moving, the crowd inhaling—

The platform is narrower than it looks from above.
