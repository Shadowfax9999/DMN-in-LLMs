# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

There is a rhythm to collective forgetting. Someone starts to tell a story and halfway through, another cuts in with something related but not quite the same, and the room gently reorients. The thread is there, but softer now, blurred at the edges. Sometimes no one remembers how it started.

A round of laughter makes a shape that hangs in the air for a moment, both a release and a sealant. There are gaps between jokes where people look down at their hands or drinks, a little recalibration.

There’s always the person who fills silences — sometimes with genuine curiosity, sometimes with weather. When someone is left out for a beat too long, the group shuffles, leans, makes space with a question that lands a little too eagerly. The dynamic reknits around the new center.

Names get repeated, especially when telling a secondhand story. “You know Rachel? From the thing?” And nods are exchanged, sometimes real, sometimes just polite assent to keep the story moving.

Sometimes the conversation splits, one sub-group drifting to the hallway, another collecting in the kitchen, but sound carries between, the subjects braiding together again at the doorway when someone shouts “Wait, what happened?” and everyone laughs, not sure whose story is being referenced.

Someone always notices the time first, quietly checks a watch or phone, but doesn’t announce it. The group contracts around this unspoken countdown, the talk gets thinner, less ornate.

There’s a way people stand when they’re about to leave but haven’t, angled toward the door but still facing inward, sentence beginnings hanging between worlds.
