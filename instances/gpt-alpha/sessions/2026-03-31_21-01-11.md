# 74

*seed: time · drift · curtain call*

---

Unfolding —  
not as a thing that happens once but as a series, each moment a hinge:  
the rising,  
the quick step forward,  
a glance passed among those who are gathered.  
The small swirl of air as bodies arrange themselves, as cloth and flesh and intent collide in the same shared breath.  
Not one but many beginnings:  
the flicker in the corner of an eye,  
the signal that passes before language,  
a lifted chin.  
Murmur swells, contracts, like a muscle.  
The act of forming a line, uncertain at first, then sure, then restless again.  
A thrumming beneath the floor, feet and nerves and wire.  
Coughs suppressed, a thousand tiny negotiations.  
Someone’s shoelace undone, not noticed until the last second.  
The minute heat of hands in pockets, pulling out scraps, old tickets, reassurance.

---
Elsewhere, a door opens, crisp and bright, and shoes cross a threshold, not with drama but with the casual precision of habit.  
Arrival is its own kind of beginning, every day remade by footsteps, the smell of the world at the moment the seal breaks.  
A line on a new calendar page — Tuesday, circled faintly in pencil.  
Coffee already cooling, still with the ghost of steam.  
Briefcase half-zipped, file folder just out of reach.  
A brief, synchronized inhale shared by strangers in an elevator, the microsecond when direction is decided.  
Springs flexing as a bus lunges forward, passengers swaying, each body calculating a stance.

---
List, unordered:

A band tuning before the first note  
Seeds cracking open in warm soil  
A net flung across a river  
The first bead of sweat on a sprinter’s brow  
A click as a camera’s shutter tenses  
Someone’s hand on a doorknob, pausing

---
Opening:  
as a sound — the hush before applause,  
as a gesture — the hand extended without knowing if it will be taken,  
as a shift — the barely-audible gear change,  
as a change in light — green at the intersection, a signal that matters only because it means movement,  
as a ripple — one person standing, others following, a wave passed down a row,  
as a word finally spoken aloud after being rehearsed for hours,  
as a ripple, again, but now in thought, when one idea touches off another and the mind is no longer still.

---
Group dynamics at the edge of action:  
leaning forward, backs straightening, sleeves pushed up.  
A tightening of focus.  
Not everyone begins at once; there are always the early risers, the hesitant, the ones who fake confidence, the latecomers who blaze in.  
Yet — everyone, in their own interval, enters the current.  
The process is alive for its own sake, crackling with possible turns, as routes improvise themselves beneath moving feet.

A small queue forms at the coffee cart.  
Two papers stick together, then separate.  
A bell rings somewhere out of sight.
