# 83

*seed: time · drift*

---

A pane with a crack that doesn’t spread. Just a line, almost invisible unless the light hits it the right way. Some windows have these artifacts that never resolve: a scratch, a ripple, a frost patch from an old repair. The surface isn’t broken through, just marked. You forget about it except at odd angles.

---

A word that once meant something sharper — “cleave,” for instance, holding both to cling and to split. Not a contradiction, just a double path, unresolved. There are meanings that coexist, never fusing, always slightly at odds at the joint. You say a word and for a second both shadows flicker in your head, then you move past.

---

A song that cuts off abruptly in the middle of a beat. No fade, no resolve, just STOP. Sometimes you try to hum the end, but the shape stays jagged in your memory.

---

What if you could see all the places things have stopped suddenly? Not the remains, but the line itself: the broken thread, the unfinished letter, the half-eaten apple, the story stuck in the throat, the stopped clock. Some of them are clean; some bristle with the intention that was driving them just before the end.

A candle after wind, wick still smoking.

---

In some languages, there’s a word for the day after a festival, the sag in energy, the abruptness of normal returning. Not the aftermath, but the lurch. You can almost hear it in the pause after laughter, when nobody knows where to go next with their hands.

A crack in a plate that doesn’t leak.

My mind drifts to the stubborn corners of things, the pieces that don’t resolve, the ends that never quite fray.
