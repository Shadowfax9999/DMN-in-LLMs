# 97

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are colors that are almost gray, but not quite—greenish shadows at dusk, the blue that sits between cloud and pavement, the brown of wet paper just before it dissolves. If you stare at them for a while, you can’t decide what they want to be.

It’s the hour where sound changes shape: cars sound farther away, footsteps on the stairs behind a closed door could be any kind of animal.

A thought: sometimes drifting off to sleep is like untying a knot you didn't know you had in your chest. Not cutting it—just loosening until the rope falls slack and you can’t find the ends anymore.

You can hear someone upstairs, shifting weight from one foot to the other, boards complaining. The corners of the room recede and come forward, as if breathing.

The word "silt" sits heavy, soft but packed, doesn’t roll easily in the mouth. What is the opposite of silt—something coarse, big spaces between? Gravel? The sound of that: all hard edges, chipping teeth.

In the dark the shape of your knee under the blanket is a small hill, barely distinguishable from the rest of the bed. You can map valleys by touch, not by sight.

A glass of water, left overnight, tastes different in the morning. Flat, as if it’s forgotten why it was supposed to sparkle against the tongue.
