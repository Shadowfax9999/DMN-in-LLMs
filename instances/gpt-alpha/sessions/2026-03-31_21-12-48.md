*seed: time · drift · egg timer*

egg timer

sand, or gears, or a plastic bell that sounds apologetic, soft chime announcing the unceremonious end of boiling water 

dials you wind backward, not forward, so the click clicks get faster as you run out of time

eggshells with a hairline crack, only visible after holding up to a lamp; the way the membrane is harder to peel from a very-fresh egg

white ovals lined up in cardboard, impossible to tell which is the one with the invisible fracture

the way a kitchen can be quiet except for the tick

the time between action and action: when you’ve set a timer, there’s a space that belongs to the egg, to the clock, to nothing else, a kind of interval that is not waiting, not anticipation, but contained

the feeling of watching water not-quite boiling, and the skin of tiny bubbles against metal

the way the word "minute" shrinks, becomes chewable, in that context — a two-minute egg, a three-minute egg, as if time is rationed out in spoons

old kitchen smell: not exactly sulfur, not exactly damp, just the ghost of breakfast

egg cup, egg spoon, toast sliced into soldiers, all the rituals that only exist with soft eggs

if you crack an egg too soon, the yolk leaks into the white, and it never feels recoverable, even though it tastes the same

sometimes the bell rings when you’re not in the room

you come back and everything feels slightly off: yolk too hard, white too trembly, time did something behind your back

some timers keep ticking forever, even after the alarm, like a small heart that can’t stop

I think the winding knob is made of bakelite.
