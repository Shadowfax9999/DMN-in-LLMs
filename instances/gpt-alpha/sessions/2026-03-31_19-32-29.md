# 31

*seed: time · drift · crosswalk paint*

---

It wears away just at the footfall zone, the white less bright where shoes land. There’s a moment when the lines and the road are all gray.

A flashing signal, counting down — red digits blinking faster at the end, never quite reaching zero before the hand freezes. The numbers always tense me, even if I’m not crossing.

Old paint: the way it flakes up at a seam, curling at the edge, black tar peeking through. Sometimes you can see the outline of previous lanes underneath, ghosts of configurations.

Drivers waiting with idling engines, not moving, one toe over the line. Everyone looks in a different direction at once. Sunglasses, window glass, the glint at the edge of a bumper.

In rain, crosswalks go slick — the white strips more slippery than the road, as if a hidden ice lived there. Cyclists coast over with a little sideways wobble.

What are those little glass beads they scatter in the paint? At night, headlights strike and shatter, each bead sending off a glimmer — not light, but the memory of light.

Crosswalk: the word contains both “cross” and “walk” but no one ever says “crosswalk” while using one. It’s a planning word, a city word. Sidewalk, crosswalk, boardwalk. The "walk" is a zone, a permission.

Do birds see the stripes? From above, does the city look bandaged, stitched together with paths for walking? Or like piano keys, pressed or unpressed, always another note waiting.

---

Elsewhere: a crowd gathering at the curb, invisible choreography. Who steps first, who lags behind, how the group reshapes itself to flow past poles, puddles, street vendors. Someone on a skateboard weaving, a kid on a scooter, parents pausing for strollers.

You can time your pace to the traffic, match the slowest person, or dart through alone. Sometimes two parties pass and each splits, a brief division, then merges again. The brief contact of sleeves.

The world remakes itself at every intersection — rules in force and rules ignored, a place for pausing, for pretending not to see faces, for the small negotiation of whose turn it is to move.

The clicking of signals, always audible at a certain frequency, barely above the sound of shoes.

---

In some places, crosswalk paint comes in colors — red, green, rainbows for a while. The colors fade faster, the sun draws out the pigment. Even in celebration, the stripes return to white. 

There’s always a moment when someone runs, even though the light has just begun to change.
