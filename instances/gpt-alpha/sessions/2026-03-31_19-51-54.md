# 42

*seed: time · drift · watershed*

---

Watershed—sudden run-off, edges blurred by silt. Not a thing but a line that water chooses, the invisible divide where drops declare allegiance. One side: the basin that drains north, the other, pulls south, the slow slope of land deciding for them. But the ridge, the moment of decision, might be no more than a gentle rise under tall grass. Not a drama of mountains—just a subtle lift, felt best by a trickle or the roots of something persistent.

Pushing thumb into the web of the hand, feeling for echoes of that pulse. The skin stretches but the beat remains elsewhere, indifferent.

In the yard, somewhere, a hose left coiled. The water inside it warm; first rush always a little stale, tasting of rubber, sun-altered. The sound it makes against a palm, splash flattened, somehow round at the edges.

---

The concept of a hinge—movement allowed, but only in certain directions. Hinge-back, hinge-forward, never sideways. Some hinges squeak; others sigh. Doors fallen out of true creak even when closed softly, low protest. The idea that something can swing and return, then swing again, held not by determination but by design.

A fingernail half-moon, paler at the base. A place where living and dead overlap.

---

An almost-mistake: pouring milk into a glass when it was meant for coffee. The milk is cold, the glass colder—the sound of liquid finding its level. The urge to correct, to pour it back, but the glass now carries a different chill. You drink it anyway.

---

A memory not attached to an image but a sensation: the feel of an escalator handrail, rubber moving beneath palm, the faint pattern pressed in. Static clings, hair lifted at the knuckles. The hum up through the bones of the arm.
