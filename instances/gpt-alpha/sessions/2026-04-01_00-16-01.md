Things that cut off:

— The little tick of a lamp switch, a snap, night suddenly edged in a different color  
— The sound of your own throat while swallowing in a silent house  
— The way a black-out curtain swallows headlights, the beam sliding up and gone  
— Wi-Fi cycling out, buffering spiral snagged mid-spin  
— A mouthful of water, cold, then empty  
— The second before a sneeze that never arrives  
— A pencil lead breaking mid-sketch, graphite wound catching at the page  
— The last blip of static as a radio station fades, voices dissolving into textured nothing  
— Letters half-erased, ghost-grid of a message that existed only for a moment  
— The hiss right after a soda can opens, a burst, the air reorganizing  
— A memory stopping short, the face present but not the voice  
— A refrigerator compressor shutting off, the soft hush that follows  
— The impulse to call someone, phone already unlocked, finger pausing

Noises sometimes keep going in your head even after they stop. The buzz of clippers after a haircut, phantom vibration at the hip, the echo of a door closing. Maybe breakage is not always loud. Sometimes it's just a shift, a missing thing. The air not disturbed. The breath held, the muscle slackening.

There’s a kind of comfort in the cutoff, a definition to the boundary. You know where the outside stops. Rooms become more distinct when the hum drops out. You start to hear the clock. The fabric of things is more ragged at the edge.

The strip where the sticker peeled off the window is still a little sticky, dust clinging, bare but holding on.
