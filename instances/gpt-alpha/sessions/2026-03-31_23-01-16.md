# 95

*seed: time · drift*

---

If you blink deliberately, you can feel the pause behind your eyelids like a screen pulled down for a breath. There is the afterimage of the room: red spot, window ghost, floating blue square.

Some numbers stick in the mind as if magnetized. 23. 108. 42. 17. I don’t know which ones are lucky, only that they feel charged, as if they would light up on a keypad if pressed at the right hour.

In the sound of a fan, you can listen for patterns that aren’t there, like phantom rhythms under white noise. If you listen long enough, the patterns seem real, then slip away when you try to count them.

There is a particular tension in waiting for bread to rise — the dough swelling under a towel, warm and slow. The surface shifts: bubbles, a faint sheen, small invisible muscles gathering in the silence.

Late at night, the air cools quickly near the floor but lingers warm at the ceiling. Heat sorting itself by height, quiet negotiation. Socks off, skin to tile, the difference is immediate.

When you are just about to fall asleep, sometimes your body slips — one startled jolt, the floor not holding for that instant. Then the rest of you catches up.

The word “dusk” feels like a soft landing, while “dawn” feels sharper at the edges.

Some letters are easy to write without looking: o, c, l. Others stumble if you try to draw them blind. S always wavers; m sometimes leans too far left.

If you stare at a ceiling long enough, the texture starts to move, shifting between bumps and valleys, never repeating exactly. 

One ant crosses the same patch three times, each path carried like a thread.
