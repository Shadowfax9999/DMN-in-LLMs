# 52

*seed: time · drift · alluvial fan*

---

alluvial fan  
spread fingers at the edge of a sleeve  
a splay, a fanning out, gravel arranged by invisible rules  
wider at the mouth, narrowing upstream, the traces of a flood carried and then set down

Water comes turbulent, then slows, then drops what it can’t suspend  
Coarse stones first, then finer silt — a gradient left behind by energy waning  
The pattern is not symmetrical. The debris gathers in arcs and lobes, sometimes braided  
Sometimes the fan is a delta, sometimes just a pale mark on the desert where rivers only flow in rain

Depositional logic: why fine dust blows further, why gold settles out here and not there  
Why fields are green where the fan reaches, how the earth thickens before the flat.

---

The phrase “zone of deposition”
the opposite of erosion but also not  
both happen at once, in different places  
A mouth where things collect, accumulate, rest

Which cities are built on fans? Are the bones of certain neighborhoods shaped by sediment, old paths of water disguised as grid and avenue?
Some streets that turn abruptly, as if dodging a headland buried beneath fill

You could live your life without knowing the ground remembers flow  
That the map beneath the map is a pattern of unrest and settling

---

A list of zones:

tide pools  
bus terminals at midnight  
the apron beneath a mailbox  
the mouth of a storm drain  
the vestibule outside a theater  
the spot where wind-driven snow drifts in a fence’s lee  
the gap between two escalators where debris collects  
the rim of a pond after the rain has receded

Not every zone is visible on a map  
Some are only a feeling — the sense that things accumulate here  
or that history is thick, splayed out, invisible but present

The sediment that makes a place, thin-layered, granular, not quite the same as memory

---

The gravel at the bottom of a bottle when you rinse it and pour it out  
The line of silt beside a gutter after winter melts  
A screen that catches lint, a shelf that gathers receipts

The pattern is always a fan, even when you can’t see it  
The pattern spreads, quiet, shaped by energies you may not notice until they stop
