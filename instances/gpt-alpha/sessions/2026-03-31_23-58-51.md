*seed: time · drift · dead end (driving)*

---

Dead end: the car slows instead of stopping — a crawl to the barricade, then the moment you have to turn around, three-point or two if the street is wide. That slow exhale of momentum trailing off, headlights brushing a patch of fence, grass pressed by tires where others have tried to force a shortcut through the edge. Asphalt peters out, gravel, maybe a sign. The feeling in the stomach is not frustration, just a kind of soft blankness. 

A dead end in a subdivision always seems more temporary than it is, like they're just about to connect the street to something bigger. Trees grow taller than the orange barrels, the road still ends there years later.

Not all endings declare themselves. 

—

There are words that feel like dead ends: "however," "nevertheless," "anyway," placed at the beginning of sentences as if the thought will continue, but sometimes nothing follows. Or emails that sign off with "let me know if you have questions," and the conversation dies there.

—

What about a dead end in a maze? The relief of certainty — nothing to figure out, just the wall, the clear instruction to turn back. No ambiguity, just the end of the path. Contrast with a fork: so much is undecided, but at a dead end, the mind empties, options collapse.

A map with streets that just stop. 

Cul-de-sac, circular: a kind of soft dead end. Not abrupt, but enfolded, an ending that loops.

There's a difference between being stopped and coming to an end.
