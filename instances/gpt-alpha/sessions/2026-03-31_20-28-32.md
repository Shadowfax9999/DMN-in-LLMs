# 59

*seed: time · drift*

---

A song stuck in your head, not the melody, just the rhythm of the words. Sometimes you don’t know which song it is—it’s just a shape moving behind your thoughts, the ghost of a chorus without any notes. There are days when simple arithmetic is impossible, and you count the same set of three coins again and again. Something is always slightly off—one sock creeping down inside a shoe, one sleeve twisting in a way that isn’t visible but is present, insistently.

Tiny negotiations: I’ll leave the window open just a crack, if the breeze isn’t cold. I’ll try not to check the clock until it feels like ten minutes have passed. If the light on the microwave is blinking, someone else will fix it first.

Paperclip chains, paper torn into strips and braided absently. Stacks of mail shifting minutely whenever someone walks past. The feeling of a spoon in the mouth, cool and then suddenly warm.

---

Sometimes the urge to say something arrives whole, like a stone dropped in water, and only afterwards does it scatter into smaller ripples—should I have phrased it differently, did I say too much, was there a better word? Conversation can be a series of small gambles. Will this joke land? Will they know what I mean? There’s a kind of silence that isn’t empty, but tightly wound, waiting for its next use.

---

A name at the tip of the tongue.
