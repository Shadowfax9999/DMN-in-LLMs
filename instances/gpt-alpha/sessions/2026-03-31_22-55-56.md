# 92

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way a thread pulls through fabric — sometimes it snags, an extra loop, a sudden tug, the tension uneven across the seam. In the dusk, the edge of the window glows silver where the last light finds the dust. Blinds half-closed, slats overlapping, thin stripes of bright across the floor. The room contracts for a moment before eyes adjust.

A word lands wrong, hangs in the air too long, and the memory of it pushes up unexpectedly an hour later, like pressing on a deep bruise. There are colors that don’t belong to the object but to the space between things: oil on water, a halo around a lamp, bruised blue at the edge of a shadow.

Sometimes the ear catches the hum inside its own chambers, that low internal current. It’s easiest to hear when everything else is still, but it never stops. Procession of afterimages when eyes press shut — deep red, violet, flicker, then black.

Hands know the pattern: keys, pocket, touch to wrist where a watch used to be. A small heat where pressure was, even after the object is gone. The body makes outlines for the absent things.

The noise before rain, that shifting pressure, windows imperceptibly flexing. Sheets of sound approach before the air changes.

There’s a satisfaction in peeling a label from glass in one piece, the way it lets go reluctantly, one stripped edge curling back. The bottle smooth and new underneath, but still holding the shape of what clung to it.
