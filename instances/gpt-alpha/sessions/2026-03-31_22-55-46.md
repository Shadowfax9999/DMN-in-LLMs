# 91

*seed: time · drift*

---

Vapor trails in morning light have that way of not-connecting, thin dissolves, tracing lines that almost meet but evaporate. Sometimes the mind wants to connect them, to finish the pattern overhead, but the sky ignores this, lets the lines shred into unrelated wisps. 

There’s a kind of satisfaction to seeing something almost join and then fade, like a melody that hints at resolution and coils away. Unfinished bridges, half-rhymes, the unsaid thing on the tip of the tongue. That static of potential — the sense of having nearly caught it.

If you hold two magnets close, you feel both the pull and the resistance. The muscle in your forearm shakes, tiny spasms you barely notice unless you look at your hand.

An itch between the shoulderblades, unreachable, more intense for its remoteness. A memory you can’t touch directly, only skirt with images, fragments.

In math, sometimes the answer is “not defined.” Division by zero. An asymptote you approach, never reach. The closeness becomes its own kind of distance, defined by not-arriving.

A puddle in the dark — you don’t see it, but the sudden chill up the ankle tells you you’re in. Water clings above the sock’s edge, then spreads, warmth replaced by prickling cold.

The word “almost” is heavier than it needs to be. Carried in the chest, like a stone. 

If you try to hum with your mouth closed, the sound buzzes the bone. 

Something in the next room ticks — not the clock, a pause, then again. Not regular enough for seconds. 

Some plants open at night, folds unspooling in darkness, no witness but moths. 

Salt left behind when water flashes away, white lines, ragged, marking where something used to be. 

A rhythm that loses time, comes back a beat late, never quite settles again.

There’s a gap in the fence behind the garden, just wide enough, but the grass there is flattened.

A tune keeps looping in the back of the head, but one note is missing every time it plays.
