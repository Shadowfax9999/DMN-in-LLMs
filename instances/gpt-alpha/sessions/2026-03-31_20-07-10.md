# 49

*seed: time · drift*

---

The weight of an earring pulls the lobe into a crescent, a soft fold stretched out and left to swing. The chain of a necklace leaves a faint print along the neck, a sequence of ridges, pink and temporary, slowly fading as skin returns to its usual smoothness.

There is a line of trapped air beneath cling film, a bulge that distorts the covered leftovers in the fridge. Pressing it with a fingertip, pressure equalizes, the bubble vanishes, replaced by a small circle of condensation.

A knuckle pops if the hand tightens fast and then opens again. The brief sting, then the sensation gone. Sometimes a pulse is visible just above the wrist—a short, thick line flexing with every beat, small movement beneath a patch of skin.

The edge of a book pressing into the thigh when read cross-legged on the floor, the skin there marked by a shallow groove for minutes after getting up.

The places where the body meets the world leave marks, never quite symmetrical, never in exactly the same spot twice.

---

A fruit fly slides along the inside of a water glass. Its path is erratic but the curve of the glass surface slowly guides it back to the waterline.

A crumpled receipt, unfolded, retains the memory of the first fold: the crease, even when smoothed, interrupts the flatness, the paper never quite returns to unused shape.

At the heel of a sock, individual threads pull and knot into a soft mound. After hours of wear, the pile flattens, then regrows in the wash.

The pattern a spoon makes in yogurt—spiral, concentric, then broken as the mouthful is taken. The next dip never follows the last.
