# 48

*seed: time · drift · convex*

---

Convex: bulging outward, curving like the shell of a beetle, like the surface of a licked clean spoon, like a forehead touched in thought. Sometimes fingernails are convex. The thumbnail especially, catching the light in a crescent.

The soap dish: water beads along the lip, pooling in the center. The convexity only visible at a slant.

The lens in reading glasses, thick at the middle, thinner at the edge. Objects behind it swell, bending light inward. There’s a tiny distortion when you move your head too quickly—an edge of the book warps then snaps back.

---

A dog’s rib cage, expanding on each inhale, convexity beneath fur, the rise and fall marking time.

Plastic clamshell packaging, unopened, resisting fingers. Pressing down, it yields only slightly, springing back when the pressure lifts.

A hillside visible from the window, grass just coming up after winter. Convex even in shadow, its shape obvious from the way rainwater streaks the soil.

---

Sometimes the inside of the wrist feels both hollow and rounded, the curve shifting when the hand rotates. There’s a pulse there, soft and regular, unnoticed except when specifically sought.

Coins: the older ones pressed so flat by years that their original convex surface is worn almost featureless, just a suggestion of former roundness.

The back of an ankle, where the tendon curves and the skin draws tight. The outline of the bone makes a gentle arc against the floor when sitting cross-legged.

---

A drinking glass fogged from cold water, the surface tension bows droplets outward, meniscus curving, light bending. The rim itself is round, but the liquid inside shapes itself convexly, swelling just above the edge before it spills.

When you press a thumb into fresh bread, the crust cracks outward from a single point, the indent convex at first, then flattening as the crumb yields.

The button on a remote: glossy, slightly domed, glinting in the television’s reflected light.
