# 94

*seed: time · drift*

---

Fold, unfold: a napkin, a map, an origami bird. There’s always a moment where the crease is new, sharp, deliberate — then, after unfolding, the memory of the fold lingers, a faint line, never quite erased. The idea that something once doubled, then opened, leaves a ghost of itself.

Click of a pen: on, off, on again. The spring settling, the point disappearing, reappearing. Mechanical reversibility. Fingers remembering the number of clicks.

A seesaw: one end up, the other down, brief balance, then trough and peak switching places. No true center, only passing through it. The air under the seat on the way down, the muffled thud against sand or grass.

Mirror-writing, practiced out of boredom: shapes reversed, letters unfamiliar, a slow-motion skewering of the familiar line. The hand trying to unlearn direction. Mirror image of a signature: recognition flickering, but not quite landing. The difference between left and right is felt, not just seen.

---

Accordion doors, folding in sequence, pleats multiplying and collapsing — a sound like rainstick beads, tracks guiding their path. The sound of a door halfway closed is not the same as halfway open.

A zipper, teeth interlocking, then parting — not always smooth. When one tooth is out of place, everything stalls. Sometimes the pieces won’t rejoin, even if the slider traces its steps.

Buttonholes stretching, losing their shape with repeated undoing and fastening. The fabric thins, remembers stress.

---

A pendulum returns, but never precisely — the air slows it, gravity leans in. The swing arcs are echoes, fainter, as if memory is friction.

Sometimes a word spoken backward feels like it belongs to another language. There are syllables that don’t want to run upstream.

The center of a palindrome is the hinge. In “racecar,” the “e” is alone, flanked and protected, not turning, just holding everything together. The axis is silent.

Unfolded paper, the shape of the mountain crease, the valley crease — neither quite disappears. Even after smoothing, the memory of symmetry lingers as a kind of secret scaffolding.
