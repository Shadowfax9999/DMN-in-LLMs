# 51

*seed: time · drift · alloy*

---

A mass of something fused from more than one source. Brass, bronze, solder, amalgam. The mind drifts to coins: the old ones with the copper rising through, ghosting silver into brown-green halos at their edges. The sound coins make, tipping into a cup or tray, sharper than pure metal, as if tininess gives resonance.

Alloy as mixture: not just metals but crowds, languages, river water with silt. Something indistinct at the boundary. In the air after a fireworks display, everything is haze: sulfur, smoke, sweetness, burnt paper. That blend lingers above the field long after the crowd goes home.

---

Numbers in sequence, and the way sequences assemble different qualities with each addition:

1 – single
2 – pair
3 – cluster, triangle, dynamic
4 – grid, square, foundation
5 – tension, imbalance, the odd foot

No individual number is alloy, but the sequence becomes one, each number inside the next, each property fusing, losing itself. There’s a purity to a single thing but almost nothing is ever left untouched, unalloyed.

---

A song that is neither major nor minor but something twisting between, made from borrowed chords. Registers in the bones as uncertainty, as if the music is always almost about to resolve. On the radio in the car, you’re not quite sure if it’s happy or sad. The alloyed mood.

Saltwater at the mouth of a river. The invisible swirl where it mixes, fresh eddies within the brackish volume, a layered thing you can’t see but could taste, if you drew a line across the water and sampled along it. The shape of that transition is not a line, but a zone.

---

The keys on a commuter’s keyring, some old and brassy, some flat and steel, one coated in plastic, tiny differences in weight and feel. Each impression in the hand a composite, an imprint layered from mornings and evenings, uncertain which cut fits which door until tried.
