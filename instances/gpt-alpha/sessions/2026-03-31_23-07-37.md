# 98

*seed: time · drift*

---

Resonance lingers under the skin, vibration with no external note—just leftover shimmer from something earlier. There’s a kind of quiet you can hear pressing on the inside of your ears, the hush that arrives after a loud day, the kind that settles on your bones. Fingernails against palm: you can measure time in crescents, faint lines pressed deep, pale for only a moment and then they fade. When you blink, sometimes shapes persist, a negative bloom ghosted in your vision—like the afterimage of a bright lamp when you close your eyes too fast.

There’s a flavor for every kind of tired. The metallic one, hard at the back of the throat, is worse than the soft, woolen tired that wraps around your head. Some exhaustion is granular—it gathers behind your eyes in a fine sediment you can almost feel shifting when you move.

The drift of thought before sleep isn’t linear, more like stepping stones half-buried in mud: you can’t see where the next one is until you’re already moving your foot. Sometimes the distance between two thoughts is like the break between radio stations, static filling up the silence until something else comes in—never quite the song you expected.

A single loose thread at the hem of a sleeve can become the whole of your attention, finger tracing the spiral of unraveling, wondering if it will ever stop or if the fabric will simply dissolve, bit by bit, into its own origins.

Light from a distant streetlamp, filtered through curtains, does not reach the far wall—it fades before it gets there, turns into a vague patch of not-quite-dark that reshapes the room’s edges. 

There are words that only make sense at night. "Liminal" is one—barely spoken, softer in the dark, a word for in-between moments, in-between places, in-between breaths.
