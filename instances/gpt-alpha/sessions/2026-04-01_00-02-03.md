# 76

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word “conclusion” with its hard “kloo” sound—clunk, shut, boxed in. “Resolution” spreads softer, but implies winding down. “Termination” clinical, without nooks. “Culmination” swelling, more a summit than a stop. Endings with their own textures.

A record needle finishing the last groove, turning and turning in silence against the label, the gentle repetitive click. After a while, you forget there was ever music.

Things that end abruptly:
—a blown fuse mid-sentence
—a sudden summer downpour cutting off the picnic laughter  
—the snap when the elastic breaks  
—tears in a loaf of bread where the knife didn’t go straight  
—the phone line going dead, tone replaced by silence

The feeling, not of relief, but of blankness. The mind scrabbling for the next thing and finding none.

The end of a roll of tape: gummy, ragged, clinging  
The end of a pencil, chewed, hard to hold  
The end of a lit fuse, the waiting

Last page of a book, lingering before closing it, not ready  
The way the screen fades to black—uneven, sooty grayness, not sudden, not bright  
A sentence that stops, not with a period, but by trailing  
The limpness of a flag with no wind

After a performance before applause, the soft sucked-in quiet when everyone is suspended  
A tide that doesn’t so much stop as turn imperceptibly toward going out  
The scent of something burned long ago, just a hint, not the thing itself  
Endings that echo inside, like sound in a shell

Sometimes thinking of the word "defunct": slightly dusty, more befuddled than mournful, still around the edges  
A drawer you haven’t opened in years, the objects inside out-of-date, inert

The way a party ends: not with a speech or a clear signal, but after enough people have left, the air is changed, the room is just a room again

An egg carton with one egg left  
A blank space at the bottom of a form  
A thread hanging from a cuff

The road at the edge of town that just stops, gravel spraying into weeds
