# 92

*seed: time · drift*

---

“Mnemonic” sticks in the mouth like a piece of lint — the silent “m,” woolly, awkward, wants to be pronounced but isn’t. Same awkwardness as “gnome” and “pneumonia.” Silent consonants hiding in the word, vestigial.

If you repeat a sequence long enough, it becomes opaque. Thirty days hath September, April, June, and November. It’s just a litany after a while. The real meaning recedes, the words become beads on a string, sliding, smooth, untethered.

Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, violet. ROY G BIV. The indigo doesn’t really belong — crammed in to fit, more for the mnemonic than the spectrum. Indigo is almost never named outside the list.

Some codes never get used. You make one up for a password, forget which phrase you based it on, now it’s a trapdoor with the key thrown away.

---

The way numbers have their own colors, just for you. Four is always green, six is brown, but only in your head. The number form that’s a private hallway, numbers stacked like mailboxes. Synesthesia as an unshared mnemonic that doesn’t help you remember anything, just makes the world more textured.

The shape of the answer is sometimes all you have — you know you underline the verb, you circle the noun, but the words themselves blur.

Flashcards: the slow, shuffling rhythm, paper softening at the corners. That feeling of almost remembering, the answer on the tip of your mind, trapped behind its own curtain.

Sometimes a rhyme is comforting, like a warm-up phrase before a performance. Lefty loosey, righty tighty.

Acrostic poems, the first letter of each line spelling out a word you’re meant to remember, but the poem itself is so forced, so awkward, all the energy in squeezing the meaning into the form.

The calm of knowing you have a trick, even if you don’t use it. The trick is a backup, like a spare key in a drawer, reassurance.

Even when you forget the mnemonic, sometimes the rhythm of it lingers, a pattern with no content, just a shadow of remembering.

The words “never eat shredded wheat” still return uninvited, even when you don’t need to find north.
