# 47

*seed: time · drift*

---

Noticing the difference between steam rising from a mug and steam sliding along a lid—one drifts upward, the other pools and runs in small beads, circling the rim before gathering itself and falling.

A loose thread on the oven mitt, catching the sleeve every time, until the thread is gone and something is missing from the feeling of picking it up.

The sound of a slow exhale through the nose, mouth closed, fogging up the inside of glasses for a second, clearing again.

A faint sourness near the edge of the fridge door. Not inside, just at the seam.

There’s always a spot where the tile grout is darker than the rest, not because it’s dirty, but because it’s where the light never falls.

Three crumbs migrate across the table for days, nudged further by elbows, sleeves, the breeze from someone walking by, until finally they disappear, one at a time, and no one saw it happen.

---

Moving through the bedroom: a slip of sock fabric underfoot, barely enough to notice but enough to pause; the stretched shadow of a lamp cord following the baseboard.

A finger pressed just above the eyebrow, smoothing an imaginary line, as if there’s something to shape.

Pillows redistributed, one always heavier than it looks, the other collapsing immediately under the back of the head.

There’s a small pain at the heel—a strip of skin roughened by walking, not enough to limp, just enough to remember when putting on shoes.

A line of light under the door, thinner some nights, wider when the hallway bulb is on late.

---

Sometimes the silence at 2:13 a.m. feels wider than usual, as if even the street outside is holding its breath.
