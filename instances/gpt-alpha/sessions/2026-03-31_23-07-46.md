*seed: time · drift · stub*

---

stub: a remnant, something meant to be finished but left as a suggestion—ticket stub, pencil stub, tree branch hacked off, the spongy end healing over, scarred.

Stubbed toe: pain radiating upward in a pulse, dizzy moment of contact, the body’s immediate focus narrowing to that exact point. The sound in your mouth: stub, abrupt, stopping itself.

A coupon torn clean along the dotted line, the stub left behind to be filed, or maybe lost in a coat pocket, forgotten until the lining frays.

Ticket stubs collected in a jar, colors fading, perforated edges softening. The memory of entry—proof that a moment happened, that you were allowed inside. But the main part is always gone, only the remnant allowed to be kept.

Stub as a beginning—stubbled jaw, rough with growth, not yet a beard but no longer smooth. Unfinished, in the process of becoming.

A page with just a heading, the rest empty—PROMPT: Write something here. Sometimes stubs linger for months: empty outlines, promises made to yourself in code or margin notes.

A candle stub, wax pooling around the wick. Tiny, functional, insufficient for a whole night but too stubborn to throw away.

Words with "stub" hidden inside: stubborn, stubble, stubby. All slightly awkward, a little unfinished, an edge instead of a smooth line.

Some trees end in blunt stubs after a storm, bark peeled back to show the pale inside. Sometimes the break is clean, sometimes splintered.

Stub as a placeholder, as if you could always return and finish it, as if everything you leave unfinished might be completed someday, if you just had more time or more light.

A pencil worn down to a stub is awkward to hold, but the lead writes darker, softer, smudging the page.
