# 58

*seed: time · drift*

---

Someone laughs too loudly in the next room. The sound is a shape — round, forced out, then falling in jagged pieces. It doesn’t belong to any face I can see. I think of how laughter sometimes feels like a trick, how joining in can be automatic or effortful or neither, just something that happens through you or around you.

Is there a kind of silence that comes after laughter? The quiet after a joke that lands perfectly is different from the quiet after a joke that fails. Both are measurable, but only in sensation, not in seconds.

Repetition without rhythm: tapping on the desk, then stopping, then starting again but with a different beat, unsure whether it’s annoyance, boredom, or just the need to mark time.

The covers on the bed still look slightly rumpled hours after getting up, the impression of a body not quite erased. If you smooth the fabric, there’s a moment when the lines resist, as if they remember.

—

Words that feel like they should be heavier: plinth, grommet, vault.

Words that are unexpectedly light: kite, velvet, hollow.

Idle hand-to-hand exchanges: passing a pen, passing a receipt, passing a secret. Hands remember the texture of these moments more than the mind does.

Sometimes a plan evaporates halfway through the first step, leaving the motion behind but none of the intention. The urge to write something down and the sensation of the pen against the paper can be separate.

A phone charger, looped and slack, lying behind the chair.

The question you didn’t ask still feels like a pebble in the shoe.
