# 44

*seed: time · drift*

---

Odd how the backs of knees are always cooler than the rest of the leg, walking, waiting, a bit of breeze curling under the hem. There’s a place just above the ankle where the sock elastic pinches and then later you see the pale ridge it leaves, like an afterimage of standing.

Someone’s cart rattles on the cracked sidewalk—three wheels in line, the fourth catching every seam. The sound is uneven, like the half-skip of a heartbeat that surprises you sometimes at night.

The smell of warm bread floats sideways out of the grocery door, blending with exhaust and the sweet, drifting static of someone’s headphones leaking notes into the corridor of dusk.

A window above half-opens; two hands rest on the sill, wedding ring turned inward, knuckles pressing small hollows in the paint. The rest of the body invisible, only suggestion and gesture.

Light pools on the curb, leaking from a sign’s yellow plastic, and for a moment it makes the puddle look deeper, like a small golden hole punched through the street.

---

Stillness gathers at the edges where parked cars define the limit of movement. Headlights, tail lights, reflections that double and blur in puddles and shop glass.

A dog barks from somewhere behind a fence. The sound comes out flat, more announcement than alarm.

Someone’s phone vibrates in a coat pocket and they hesitate, shifting weight from foot to foot, balancing presence and distraction.

A woman bends to tie her shoe, right on the curb, elbow almost brushing a bus bench. She’s mouthing the word “loop.” The laces are thin, soft, a kind of cloth that always comes undone if you don’t double-knot.

Farther down, a scooter lies on its side, wheels still spinning a little, abandoned or dropped or waiting.

The voices from the storefront are layered: someone giving change, someone laughing, someone asking for a receipt.

The sky is less blue now, more like a memory of blue, pressed thin between rooftops.

Someone is whistling — a tune without melody, just a rise and fall, shaping the air into something almost familiar.
