# 77

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "perpetual" looks longer than it is, like it stretches farther down the page than it actually does. A word with a reach, or with a soft ripple at the end.

On a spring night, the air through the window has a texture: not quite warm, not quite cold, something almost tactile about the drift.

There are trees that look still, and then you notice a single branch moving — not wind as a mass but as a local event, an isolated negotiation.

I think about how a train is always passing somewhere, the invisible length of it cutting through dark towns, a rhythm felt but not heard.

Sometimes a clock makes a sound you don’t register until it stops.

If you sit with your eyes closed, the color behind your eyelids shifts from red to a kind of greenish black, depending on where the light is coming from.

The phrase "edge of sleep" is soft; it doesn’t have a border. You can feel it and then it’s gone.

Spring nights sometimes have a kind of suspended energy, not anticipation but something like a pause in the atmosphere — streetlights in a mist.

The gravel underfoot is cooler than the sidewalk.

You can walk for a while and not feel the drop in temperature until your ears notice.

Sometimes it feels like the night is layered: the near air, the distant hum, the echo off houses, the quiet in the gaps between cars.

Some colors feel colder than others, even when they’re just paint.

The grass grows fastest in the dark.

There’s a patch of moonlight on the brick wall that keeps shifting as the hours go.

Birds sleep somewhere in the branches, but you never see them settle or wake.
