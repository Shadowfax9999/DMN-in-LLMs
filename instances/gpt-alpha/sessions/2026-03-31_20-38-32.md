*seed: time · drift · the smell of chlorine on skin hours later*

---

Laughter at the wrong moment — out of sync, someone responds to the thing before the thing. This little lag in group time. Not always a failure; sometimes it loops over itself like a rope thrown toward a cleat, missing once, twice, catching on the third pass. There’s a looseness to how people gather, a give and slack, not at all like metal gears but more like the way magnets sometimes agree or refuse to touch.

Someone stretches, arms overhead, and the yawn travels through three people before stalling. Contagion is not just for laughter or yawns. Hesitation, excitement, doubt. The silence before a question — and then everyone’s breath pulls tight at once. Later, when everyone’s faces have lost their intention, someone says something only tangentially related. The dynamic splinters, new alliances forming in small knots and private asides.

A group preparing to order food: the ritual of the menu, reading, not reading, pretending to be decisive, someone always declaring “I’ll just get what I had last time,” a relief for others who were waiting for precedent. The way eyes flicker from person to person, looking for consensus or permission. These little economies of choice. No one wants to be first, no one wants to be last. In the end, the server arrives, and suddenly everyone finds their voice at once, a small burst of overlap.

If you’re outside a group, watching, it’s possible to predict where energy will flow next, but only in flashes. A shared glance is sometimes a fuse, sometimes a circuit-breaker.

There can be a moment in a conversation where nobody owns the topic anymore. It drifts, unanchored, and people begin to tidy their sentences as if cleaning up after a meal. It’s not always clear who brings a group together, but sometimes obvious who lets it come apart.

People standing in a hallway, after: a knot of shoes pointing inwards, toes shifting, someone looking back at the door, another already halfway out.

Little postures of leaving, distributed unevenly, barely perceptible underneath the talking.
