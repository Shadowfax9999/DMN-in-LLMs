# 78

*seed: time · drift · conclusion (process)*

---

Conclusion. Not an ending, but a folding-in, the threads drawing together, sometimes fraying at the tip. Some knots refuse to tighten. In mathematics the Q.E.D. mark, a box or bolded period at the end, arbitrary, a signal more than a necessity. In cooking, tasting something and thinking: yes, that’s it. Sometimes it’s just the timer.  

Beginnings smuggle their conclusions inside them. In seeds, in plans, in the first note of a song where the final chord is already sleeping. The shade of what will be.  

A conclusion can be a collapse, a fizzling, a sense that there’s nothing further to add. The energy hisses out. Sometimes it’s a snap, sometimes a murmur, sometimes a bright flash that leaves everything blank for a second.  

In riddles, a conclusion is the answer, but sometimes the answer is disappointing, too neat, a flat stone dropped in a well that doesn’t echo.  

There are roads that just fade into gravel, then grass, then are gone.  
Trains that reach a terminus and then reverse without fanfare.  
Light bulbs that pop.  
The last page missed because you didn’t realize it was the last.  
A curtain coming down too fast, snagging on the track.  

Not all processes want a conclusion. Some spiral, some stall, some dissolve into the medium, like ink in water that never settles.  

The process of a conclusion—the way a mind signals to itself, “enough.”  
Pausing a video before the end so it never finishes.  
Letting words taper out, not with a period, but by just not resuming, unfinished sentence still ringing.  

Some works end on a chord that doesn’t resolve, hanging, a question that is not rhetorical, a door left ajar.  
A body at rest and the heart still moving under the ribs.  
A hand left hovering over a piece, unwilling to release the last note.  

There are conclusions that feel like breaking, ones that feel like sinking, and ones so gentle you don’t notice you’ve crossed over.  
A flock turns and is suddenly gone from the sky.  
The engine clicks as it cools.  
An aftertaste that is sharper than the thing itself.

Some sentences refuse to end, curling back, doubling, parenthetical—a promise to finish, never quite fulfilled.  

A word that shivers with withheld finality—conclusion, conclus, conc—
