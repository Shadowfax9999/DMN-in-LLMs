# 70

*seed: time · drift · graveyard shift*

---

Graveyard shift — the hours that hollow themselves out, become their own country to inhabit. Not night, not morning. The clock’s hands circle in a silent ballet for those awake: nurses, janitors, bakers, the people who drive the streets empty of traffic, headlights making slow pools on signs. Time feels stacked, not linear — hours on hours, like plates, or geology, or unread mail.

—

Fluorescent light makes skin look violet, or gray, or sometimes greenish at the wrist, especially when you’re tired enough that your body feels like someone else’s. The insistent hum of the lights is steady, unbroken even when you stop hearing it.

A coffee cup leaves a small ring on a counter that will not be cleaned until morning.

There is a crispness to paper handled at 3 a.m., sheets sticking and peeling apart, as though they know they shouldn't be disturbed.

—

Things that are most themselves in the hollow hours:

- Refrigerators
- Worn-out shoes
- Loose change in a pocket
- Crumpled receipts
- The blinking cursor on a login screen
- Steam rising in empty rooms
- The hum of vending machines
- The slick feeling of a paper towel out of the dispenser
- A small knot in a shoelace

—

The ticking of a wall clock is steady, but sometimes the minute hand jumps forward in a way you weren't expecting.
