# 90

*seed: time · drift · scoring*

---

scoring  
on paper, the bluntness of pencil, the groove in the wood where too much pressure leaves a ridge — a line indented, before there is even graphite, an intention pressed in before anything is visible, a path for the lead to follow  
in music, the staff, the marks, lines that mean play here, wait here, this is the measure and that is the limit  
scoring a surface to make it easier to snap: drywall, tile, a cracker broken clean where the perforation runs — a weak line, deliberate, so the break will be straight  
serration as a form of scoring: bread knife, sawtooth edge, orange peels coming off in strips  
score as tally, as record, notched into wood or chalked on slate, marks to count without using numbers

sometimes you see the grooves left behind, not the thing itself — the way an old cutting board remembers every blade  
the word “score” has so many channels — to score a goal, to keep score, to mark, to win, to compose, to make a surface ready for splitting  
on skin, sometimes nails leave red lines that fade  
“full marks” “high score” “keeping score” — the language of measurement pressed into everything  
a score is twenty, but also just one line among many  
the score settles after thunder, when the air falls quiet  
scoring as in measuring, accounting, assigning a place — but also making something vulnerable to breaking, setting up the next moment when something will give  
between each line is the potential to split
