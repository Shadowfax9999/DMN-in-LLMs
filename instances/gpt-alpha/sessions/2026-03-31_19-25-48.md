# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

The thing about doorways: they are always the same shape, but not the same width. The way you step through, one shoulder first, or both at once if you’re not thinking. Some doors stick because the frame has swollen a little, remembering the last rain, wood shifting against paint.

There is a sensation of standing still while the air moves around you, uncertain about which side is inside and which is out. The edge of the rug curls under, collecting grit.

Not all thresholds announce themselves. In some buildings, the transition from one kind of air to another is only a change in the way your shirt sits on your back, or the slight shift in sound as you pass from tile to carpet.

---

A piece of glass left in sunlight will hold a memory of warmth, even after the sun moves away. If you touch it, the heat is shallow, lingering only at the surface, evaporating as soon as your hand moves.

Socks on hardwood, the faint complaint as fabric slides against varnish.

There’s a certain light you only get in early evening, slanting so flat it turns dust into a visible architecture, a private scaffolding in the air. The dust in someone else’s house never looks the same.

Sitting on the third step of a staircase, halfway between floors, you can hear the whole house breathe differently depending on where people are.

Sometimes the refrigerator clicks off and you realize you’ve been listening to it all along.

---

Hands pressed together for warmth, not quite a prayer, more like an agreement between ten small bones.
