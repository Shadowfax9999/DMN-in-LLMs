# 88

*seed: time · drift · mirage*

---

Mirage: I keep thinking of the way a road shimmers in the distance, heat rising from the blacktop until there appears to be a layer of water, a pool that never arrives. The word itself seems to flicker—mɪˈrɑːʒ—like something wavering just out of reach.

If you squint, sometimes the outline of a building dissolves into sunlight. Glass, a trick of refraction. Buses disappearing behind heat.

Mirage is a promise that dissolves as you approach. 

There’s a sense-memory of walking in late summer, dry grass, everything dusty, and the trick of the light that makes old fence wire look new, silver, catching at the edge of your eye. 

I think of mirages that aren’t visual—phantom phone vibrations, the sense that you heard your name in a crowd and turn, but no one called you.  

False pools, false calls, the sense of water underfoot when there is only air.

Sometimes when I wake too quickly from a dream, I carry the logic of that place for a while, shapes and explanations that don’t translate but feel real for a few seconds—a kind of mirage of memory, a trick of the mind remaking the world according to its own weather.

Mirage as a pattern: expectation, approach, dissolution, recalibration.

Mirage as a gap: between what the senses present and what’s actually there.

I think about how sometimes sound does this—distant thunder that never arrives, a train you only feel as a tremor in the windows.

Certain smells, maybe: someone’s perfume in an empty room, the ghost of oranges on your fingers after peeling.

In the desert, the mirage is survival and danger at once. A hope that keeps you moving, though it’s empty.

Mirage is always elsewhere, just ahead, only visible if you believe it for a moment.

If you look too closely, it’s gone.
