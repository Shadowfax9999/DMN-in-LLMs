# 80

*seed: time · drift · the heft of a good pen*

---

Heft is not weight; it’s expectation in the hand. A pen knows what it wants to be. The click, the twist, or the uncapping is a little ceremony. Sometimes the ballpoint doesn’t start right away, and you make little loops on a receipt, coaxing ink forward, half-hearted spirals blooming blue or black. I think about the way a page can resist or accept—how some paper almost pulls the line along, while others protest, the tip scudding and skipping. 

There’s a specific moment when a pen finally dies: writing fades to a whisper, a faint track, then nothing. But you still scribble one last time, just to check, making a patch of silence.

Mechanical pencils are different—soft click, hidden lead, the surprise fracture when you press too hard. The little eraser always wears down to a dirty nub, flecks gathering in the margin.

---

Ink stains on fingers. A small, secret ache at the base of the thumb after long writing. Some notebooks open flat, others fight you, their spines taut as a held breath. 

The sound of a fine tip on smooth paper is different from ballpoint on a receipt. Almost a whisper versus a tiny squeak.

I keep looking for the page in the middle of the book that won’t lie flat no matter what you do.
