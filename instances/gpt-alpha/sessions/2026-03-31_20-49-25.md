# 68

*seed: time · drift · the way a room feels bigger when the furniture is gone*

---

The emptiness is not just space—somehow, the shape of the room changes. Sitting on bare floorboards, the echo is different; footsteps sharper, voices thin and far away. You notice dust in corners, the scars pressed into the wood where a table leg always stood. Air moves differently—drafts snake under the window, unimpeded.

There are shapes in the absence: the outline left on paint after a picture is removed, a dim rectangle on the wall. Light finds places it could never reach before, leaving long stripes and odd angles. Sometimes it feels like you forgot what the room really was, until it was emptied.

The smell is less—no fabric, no old paper, just plaster and wood and air that hasn’t quite taken on any new memory yet.

---

The sound of a single car passing is louder when the apartment is empty. I think the keys dropped onto the floor will bounce more, maybe skid a little further.

---

It’s easier to imagine possibility in an empty room than in a full one. But harder, too—sometimes the blankness is more confusing than possibilities arranged in their old forms.
