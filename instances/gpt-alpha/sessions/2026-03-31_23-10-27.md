# 100

*seed: time · drift · nascent*

---

nascent — not just beginnings, but beginnings with pressure behind them, a bud pushing, not the gentle kind, but newness straining against a skin. An eggshell, the exact moment it cracks but before the chick fully emerges. Nascent doesn't want to stay nascent. The word feels like tension, the in-between.

Not quite at the surface, but wanting. Like steam pressing at a pot lid, or the sting right before tears break through. 

A match just struck, the first curl of smoke, sulfur. 

Sometimes it feels like dawn, not the sunrise itself, but the lightening sky behind closed eyelids. 

The word "nascent" is awkward in the mouth, hesitant, a little thick. 

You can miss the moment something ceases to be nascent. When does a leaf stop being new? At what size, what shade of green? 

Music, too: the note before the phrase begins, the intake of breath from a violinist, bow paused on string.

There are seeds that fail, sprouts that yellow and collapse before making a mark above the soil. 

Sometimes the nascent never leaves that state, like letters begun but abandoned, ideas that never collect enough gravity to condense.

There is a pressure in nascent, a gathering, an urge that can feel like impatience, or like hope, or like the ache of wanting to see form rise out of formlessness. 

Even the word itself feels unsteady, as if unsure whether to root or to vanish.
