# 61

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sometimes a group seems to drift into agreement without ever speaking it aloud. The feeling of tacit alignment — glances, small nods, the tilt of a head as if to say, yes, we’re moving this way. Nobody announces the decision and afterward, no one is quite sure who steered.

A group text thread where the tone shifts subtly. The jokes become more elliptical, a phrase gets repeated, and suddenly it's the thing everyone references for weeks. Language accrues in this way, something sticky about in-jokes and the way they pool in the cracks of conversation.

When people sit around a table, energy moves in small ripples: laughter that echoes in miniature, a shared breath, the physical orientation of knees and shoulders that map out alliances or distances more than anyone claims. Sometimes one person’s shift in posture cues a general movement — chairs scrape back, everyone stands. It can feel choreographed.

There are conversations that are mostly interruptions, each sentence dovetailing into the next, no topic ever fully closed. Sometimes this feels like chaos, but sometimes it has a rhythm of its own, like the overlapping call of birds in the early morning, half-listened, half-ignoring.

---

Patterns that cluster in groups:

— The topic that always returns no matter where the conversation started.
— The laugh that’s louder than the rest and pulls others with it, like orbit.
— The question nobody wants to answer, passed around like a hot object.
— The story retold with variations, as if the group is editing it into final shape.
— A silence where someone is expected to fill in, and nobody does.

The feeling after a gathering, when everyone leaves and the room is suddenly still — but not empty, exactly, just holding residues of voices, footsteps, the vague heat of having been used.
