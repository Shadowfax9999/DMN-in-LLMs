# 53

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are diagrams with no lines — only densities.  
Places inside other places. Light caught in a stairwell, dust held aloft for a moment, swirling, then gone.  
A field that seems empty until the air changes and a flock rises, unfolding from the grass. Not hidden, just distributed.

The places where things cluster:
— the edge of a field where the soil darkens  
— behind a row of bleachers after a game  
— just beneath a ledge, where rain is interrupted  
— the hollow beside a signpost, the roots exposed  
— the shadow under a swing set, packed tighter than the rest of the playground  

Maybe a city is mostly made of these: thresholds, interruptions, slow floods of everything that doesn’t fit anywhere else.

---

Unmapped roads that appear when heavy trucks pass through, the gravel rearranged, a path not planned, only worn by enough weight over time.

The way moss finds the cracks in brick walls — not a geometry of design, but of persistence and accident.

Some clouds hang without moving for hours, a weather that never quite arrives. The waiting is a kind of accumulation too.

---

There are gatherings that don’t declare themselves:  
A pattern of footsteps over months, grass pressed flat in the same arc around a corner.  
The routes that emerge because everyone avoids the same puddle year after year until the earth gives way and the detour becomes the path.

Not every pattern is written. Some are only pressure, habit, a slight thickening at the margins where things slow down.  

The off-ramp that’s barely used except on holidays.  
A mailbox with the paint worn off the side the courier always touches first.  
Where the plastic bags tangle in the branches by the river.  

Not all maps use lines. Some are only weight, memory pressed into ground, air, water, waiting for the next layer.
