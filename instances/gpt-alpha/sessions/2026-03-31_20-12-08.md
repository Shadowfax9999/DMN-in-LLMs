# 50

*seed: time · drift · a sticker on a stop sign*

---

Some stickers lose their corners first. Others begin to bubble from the center, air creeping underneath. Sun fades the colors, red bleeds out of thin vinyl, black stays longest but chalks at the edges. If you trace your finger over the roughness of layered stickers, the ridges tell an order, older stickers sealed beneath newer, but not always. Sometimes a fresh sticker is placed deliberately beside an old one, a companion or rival.

On the back windshield of a neighbor’s car: one ghostly oval where a decal was peeled away, adhesive residue collecting dust, the outline reading clearer in pollen season.

---

Ankle itches suddenly, sock seam has rotated underneath, a twist inside the shoe that demands correction. Sharpness of fingernail through woven cotton, a brief relief.

Keys in the pocket shift, pressing a shape into the thigh, metal edges rearranged by the breathing movement of walking, by sitting, rising, climbing steps.

There’s a spot behind the right ear where hair collects after a shower, damp and cold against the skin, never drying as quickly as the rest.

---

A single grape in a bowl, spinning when nudged by fingertip, impossible to make it stop with one push. Tiny scratch on its surface where it brushed against the wire of the colander, visible only in the exact slant of the evening light.

There are always at least two napkins left over after takeout. They stay folded, quietly becoming softer each day, their sharp manufactured creases opening just enough that they don’t lie fully flat.

---

Fingertip presses into the fabric of a cushion, indent deepening, then rebounding slowly, a faint warmth left in the wake of the pressure.
