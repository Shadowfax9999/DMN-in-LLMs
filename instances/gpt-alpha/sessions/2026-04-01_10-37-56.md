# 93

*seed: time · drift · amalgam*

---

Amalgam. The sound is soft at the edges, like gum pressed into a coin slot, pliable and metallic at once. There’s a sense of mixing, but also sealing—strange that it’s used for filling teeth, restoring hollows, plugging the absences that sweets or time have carved out. I think of mercury, silvery liquid, droplets skittering if you break a thermometer, hard to catch, always splitting further instead of coming together. How can something so elusive become sturdy when bound to something else? Metal plus metal makes a hybrid, but the word feels gentler than alloy, as if it’s less about fusion and more about accommodating difference, letting parts remain distinct inside the mix.

The way some memories are amalgams, never purely one thing—half a conversation, half the weather that day, a mosaic with bits from several years fused together by the pressure of remembering. Sometimes there’s an ache when you touch a tooth filled with amalgam, the tiniest trace of temperature sinking down the root. Nerve endings waking up. Sometimes I think about how much of the body is already replaced, patched, reinforced, inside and out, but you forget unless you look closely or bite too hard on a cold spoon.

The taste of something metallic at the back of the mouth, not flavor exactly but a sensation, as if memory could have an aftertaste.
