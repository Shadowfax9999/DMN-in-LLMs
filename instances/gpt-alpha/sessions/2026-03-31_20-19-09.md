# 54

*seed: time · drift · weeping (cheese)*

---

A soft collapse at the edge of structure: the weeping rim, moisture collecting between curd and air. Not quite surrender, not quite process. The surface glimmers and beads, as if it is remembering water. Some cheeses refuse to weep, others lean into it, a kind of daily sadness, or relief. The humidity of a room becomes a silent ingredient. Salt travels outward. A rind turns tacky.

Sometimes the word weeping appears elsewhere — in fruit left too long on the table, in concrete after too much rain. A seep, a slow release.

The idea of boundaries that leak: walls that sweat in summer, ink that ghosts through thin paper, the way the body always negotiates what escapes and what is held in.

---

Not just cheese. There are other forms of weeping that aren’t about sadness.

A roof at night after the first thaw. The condensation inside a plastic wrap. Varnish on a table not quite set, oozing at the edge where it pools.

Some trees leak resins in early spring, amber before it's hardened, catching small things on the way down.

A window that fogs and drips, tracing invisible drafts. The cold spot in the room always gives it away.

---

The word weeping is heavier than sweating, somehow. Not the urgent salt of the forehead, but something surrendered slowly, a yielding.

A weep hole in masonry, meant to allow drainage. The necessary escape so pressure doesn’t build. Design that allows for softness, for give.

Some bricks darken under the rim of a gutter, marking time with their own slow stains.

---

Dew on spider silk, bead by bead, an alphabet of loss or recovery depending on the angle.
