# 88

*seed: time · drift*

---

Paper towel torn along its perforation never quite neat, always a ragged lace edge. The habit of folding it once before wiping a spill, as if that makes it better at absorbing, more honorable. Watching water bead up on the surface first, resisting, then sinking in.

The word "spill" is gentle for something that happens fast and then sits there, unmoving, a small lake on laminate. Surface tension lets the puddle hold together longer than you'd think.

Sometimes a spill soaks out and leaves no sign, but if you look at the right angle, there’s a dull patch, a print left on the counter. Later, the edge will gather grit, become visible, a border of dust clinging to the shape of what used to be wet.

A rip in the towel halfway through wiping, fibers catching on the corner of a mug. The urge to press harder, make clean be certain. But that always pushes liquid to the very rim of what you’re trying to contain.

Towels left overnight in the sink start to smell, an edge of sweetness first, then something woolly and sour. That smell clings to your hands even after.

Sometimes two sheets come off at once, fused by a drop of something invisible, and the next time you reach for one you get more than you meant.
