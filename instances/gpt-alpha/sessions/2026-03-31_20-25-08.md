# 56

*seed: time · drift*

---

Rankings that don't matter but keep resurfacing:
- Which flavor of gum is most forgettable.
- Most likely spot for a pen to vanish on a crowded desk.
- The order in which you remember classmates when seeing an old group photo.
- Which cupboard handle feels least smooth.
- Songs that you can’t recall until you hear the first two notes.

A single strand of hair on a sleeve, silvery against navy, moving a fraction with every shift of the arm. Something about the curve it makes, like handwriting, but not forming a letter.

The overhead fan wobbles slightly, a rhythm that doesn’t match the ticking of the clock.

Plates stacked not by size but by whatever was clean first.

Some evenings, the hallway feels longer.

---

The word “between” does not belong to either side. 

Inside a joke, there is one person laughing because they remember, and another laughing because they want to.

You can see the shape of dried soap in the bottom of a mug, like cloud outlines, not quite round. 

Yesterday's list of things to do sits next to today’s, neither crossed out, both half-smudged.

A fly landing on the rim of a glass, pausing as if considering the edge.
