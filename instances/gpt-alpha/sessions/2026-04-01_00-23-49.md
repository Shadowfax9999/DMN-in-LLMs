# 86

*seed: time · drift*

---

Continuity. Not sameness, but persistence. The river running through the same city for hundreds of years, and none of the water the same. You walk the same route every morning, but you are not the same person, not quite, and neither is the street, not really.

---

How many ways can things join?

A weld, molten and cooled.
A knot, friction and intention.
A splice, where thread overlaps thread.
Fusion, in the soft belly of a star.
Roots tangling underground.
Molecules brushing against each other, caught by charge.

There is also overlay—leaves pressed in a book, glass stacked, transparency on transparency until nothing is clear.

---

Elsewhere, two magnets, impossible to keep apart once aligned just right. The pressure between your thumb and a smooth stone: always a certainty, but never quite the same certainty twice.

Some joins are temporary. Velcro, snap, clasp. There is a sound to separation as well as to union—sometimes louder, sometimes near silent. The zipper undone in the dark, teeth separating with a faint hush, or the press of a lid onto a tight plastic container, air whooshed out.

---

Some continuities can’t be seen. The faint warmth left in a chair after someone stands. The echo of a bell after it’s stopped vibrating. That sense of something still present, though you can’t point to it.

When cloud cover breaks, sunlight returns—not the same sunlight, but the same process, ongoing. The horizon does not break, only our perception of what is beyond it.

Things joined by continuation: line to line in handwriting, a single unbroken loop on a string, the way your heart keeps beating without noticing the gap between one and the next.

---

You can’t see the moment where the blue of the sky becomes the black of night. It happens anyway, every day.
