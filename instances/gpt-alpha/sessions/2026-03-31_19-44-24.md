*seed: time · drift · a coin on the sidewalk you didn't pick up*

---

Mousetrap, blister, rosehip, pageantry. Sometimes a stray word like “pageantry” drags up others from the same mud — scepter, velvet, ruff, powder, glare. These words feel heavy with costume, more like fabric than meaning. I want to touch the word “velvet” just to see if it’s soft, or if it’s sticky, or if it stains.

Words that taste purple:

- amethyst
- bramble
- dusk
- lilt
- syrup
- lozenge

A lozenge always arrives with a sense of waiting. The slow dissolve. There is a kind of patience you only practice with something sweet and medicinal pressed against your tongue. The word “medicinal” makes a hollow in the mouth, a space for something sharp or herbal.

Herbal is not the same as green. Herbal is cool, astringent, maybe dry — green is sap, is alive, is reckless.

Sometimes the mind flips through words like nail polish swatches. You know the color, but you reach for the name. “Petroleum blue” is not a color I’ve seen but I know what it would feel like on my hands — slick, chemical, with a sheen that almost slides away from touch.

There are words I never say out loud: “sartorial,” “pliant,” “stanchion,” “vermilion.” They stay inside, like objects locked in a display case. Some words are just for thinking.

---

The difference between a list and a collection is permission. A list expects completion. A collection welcomes drift, repeats, omissions.

Words you keep mispronouncing in your head, even though you know better: epitome, chthonic, paradigm, indelible.

There’s always a word that lodges behind the teeth and refuses to come out clean.

---

Some words arrive already tired.
