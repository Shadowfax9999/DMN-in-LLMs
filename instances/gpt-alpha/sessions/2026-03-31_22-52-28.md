Quantum leap: not a leap at all, not bounding, not gradual, but a sudden shift, a jump between discrete possibilities. The electron is somewhere, and then elsewhere, and the in-between is not a place you get to see. The metaphor slips away from its physics — in conversation, a quantum leap means huge, dramatic, but in the original it's minuscule, indivisible, mysterious.  

A leap is leg, is calf, is tension in the foot, is the moment before — the coiling, the weight shifting — but quantum leap is no midway, only a before and after.

Does a thought jump, or slide? Sometimes there’s a sense of falling, but sometimes it goes dark, then light.

The click of a light switch, the absence of a dimmer.  

On/off. This or that. No shading, no blending: just a state and another state. But still, from the outside, things seem to blur — is there ever a perfect switch? Or does the eye always chase after the hand, inventing a motion in the moment of change?

If you close your eyes and press gently, colors will flicker, not fade.  

The word "leap" hangs in the mouth longer than "jump" — open vowel, lingering aspiration.

Once, there was a set of stepping stones, and one was missing. The gap looked too wide, but you had to cross. Not a quantum leap — just a human one. A space you measure, a risk with distance. After, wet shoe, cold sock.

In the language of stories, leaps are progress, escapes, revelations. But the leap can also land you in the same place, just out of breath.

Atoms jumping shells, the body bracing without moving.

The smallest leap is invisible, but the feeling of decision is unmistakable — that sudden, irreversible pull toward one thing, not the other.

A frog tenses beneath a leaf.  

Somewhere, an electron changes its mind.
