# 32

*seed: time · drift*

---

The logic of a line, or the attempt at one, flows through a crowd. Each person believes in their direction. The tangle is only obvious from above — lines thread and break, double back, a pulse of purpose within chaos. Nobody sees themselves as the obstacle. 

In the hush of an elevator, people rearrange themselves with tiny steps. A cough, a shuffle, a polite withdrawal into phones. The floor numbers glow, and everyone pretends not to count. The doors close with a sealed sigh, a brief pause in the world’s hum.

Sometimes, waiting is its own gathering. Bus stops in rain, shoulders hunched, bags hugged close. The blur of traffic seen through fogged glass. People cluster at the edge of shelter — water tracing paths off umbrellas, dripping steadily, a slow pooling at the threshold.

There's a choreography to the escalator: stand right, walk left, but no one enforces it. A stranger brushes past, bag grazing a sleeve, apologies murmured low. Arriving at the top, the step flattens and for an instant bodies hesitate, recalibrate, and then disperse.

The sound of coins in a vending machine: anticipation in metallic notes, the pause before the can clatters into the slot. People leaning in, peering, choosing. Sometimes a snack gets stuck and two or three gather, offering advice, a collective engineering of the inevitable shake. Brief alliances, dissolving as quickly as they appear.

A crowd after a concert, everyone funneling toward exits, voices half-lost, sharing fragments of the same song as if to keep it alive a little longer. Tickets shoved deep in pockets, or collected as souvenirs, slick with sweat and confetti dust. 

In the morning, the bus is always quieter. Sleepy nods, the faint hum of headphones leaking rhythm. A book held open, page forgotten. Each stop announced by a soft ding, a practiced hand pressing the button just before. The world outside slides past, barely noticed.

A group forming for a photograph — the subtle pull in, arms around shoulders, the lean and tilt so faces fit in the frame. Awkwardness distilled into a pose, then released. The moment after, everyone steps back, checks phones, disperses, the picture already beginning to forget its own moment.

Someone leaves a scarf behind on a bench — its shape keeping the memory of a neck, a last warmth before the chill sets in.
