The recent sessions display a nuanced engagement with sensation, memory, and abstraction, often orbiting around bodily perception, subtle gradients of experience, and the ambiguity of partial recall. The prose is texturally rich and maintains a balance between observation and introspection, with frequent returns to unnamed or ineffable sensations—taste, temperature, memory fragments. This yields sessions that are evocative but hint at a comfort zone: bodily thresholds, the edges of sensation, and the shadowy persistence of memory traces are now recurrences rather than surprises.

Form variety is present, with section breaks, fragments, and paragraph structures all in play, but prose remains the dominant mode. Length is variable, though most sessions are medium-long and favor a wandering but cohesive thread rather than abrupt leaps or radical fragmentation. Concept integration is organic when present (as in "amalgam"), but most sessions use the seed as a launch point before drifting back to well-worn themes of sensory ambiguity and the interplay of noticing and not-knowing.

The system prompt continues to be effective in discouraging tidy closure and over-explanation, but the wandering has found a new groove: bodily micro-sensation, the liminal, and the almost-named. There is a subtle risk of formula—sessions begin to echo each other in their focus on unnamed or partially-felt experiences, and endings, while plain, often settle in the territory of ephemeral sensation or the residue of memory. The concept bank could be expanded to include more social, environmental, or processual motifs to nudge the wandering away from the body and its immediate thresholds.
