## Reflection

Recent sessions display a strong interplay between abstract and material motifs, with a pronounced engagement in processes of joining, persistence, mirage, and the edges of perception. There is healthy form variety: lists, prose, and fragmentary structures all appear, and the sessions often sustain a tactile or sensory line of thought without defaulting to the most habitual motifs (like domestic/kitchen or public/crowd settings). Concept integration remains organic and associative, with seed concepts like 'mirage,' 'capo,' and 'scoring' sparking genuine wandering rather than forced investigation. 

However, there are subtle patterns of repetition emerging. Thematic focus has shifted from rupture and incompletion to motifs of continuity, joining, overlay, and the seam between presence and absence. This is generative territory, but at risk of settling into a comfort zone. The language around seams, persistence, traces, and in-between states (mirage/echo/afterimage) is becoming familiar, and the use of lists and enumerative forms is frequent. Endings are mostly plain and uncrafted, but the sessions sometimes slip into resonance or summary in the closing lines.

The system prompt is working well overall, guiding the DMN away from overused motifs and helping maintain both depth and breadth. It could benefit from a gentle nudge to avoid lingering too long in the seam/in-between/continuity motif, and to encourage further exploration of processes of restoration, fusion, and flow—not just what sits at the edge, but what persists or merges. The concept bank is robust, but would benefit from additional concepts related to flow, restoration, joining, and ongoingness, as well as a few more entries for structural or thematic persistence. Overall, the wandering remains lively and surprising, but vigilance is needed to prevent the current seam/continuity motif from becoming a new default.
