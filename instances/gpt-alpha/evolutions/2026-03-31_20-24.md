## Reflection

The recent sessions show a strong command of sensory detail and associative wandering, with a notable richness in imagery and a willingness to explore both the physical and conceptual. The use of forms is healthy: there are fragments, lists, and prose passages, as well as section breaks that help shift gears mid-session. This variety prevents structural stagnation, though prose remains the dominant mode. There is also a clear effort to integrate seed concepts in a way that feels natural—recent sessions about 'alloy', 'alluvial fan', and 'weeping (cheese)' all unfold with genuine associative logic rather than forced insertion.

However, a few patterns are beginning to solidify. The motif of zones where things accumulate—edges, thresholds, places where debris gathers—has appeared in several sessions, often with similar language (e.g., lists of places where things collect, diagrams of density). There is also a risk of settling into a comfortable rhythm of observation-explanation-observation, even as explicit explanations have diminished. While the avoidance of domestic and bodily motifs is successful, the system now leans into environmental gradients, accumulation, and flows as its new anchor. Group dynamics and social motifs are present but still secondary, and abstraction is usually tethered to a physical process or landscape rather than sustained on its own.

Concept integration remains strong, but the concept bank could benefit from even more processual, emergent, or social-dynamic terms—concepts that invite the wandering to explore collective behaviors, environmental systems, or moments of convergence/divergence in groups, rather than just material or spatial motifs. Endings are plain and avoid literary closure, though the "list of places" closing move is at risk of becoming formulaic. Overall, the DMN is evolving well, but gentle nudges toward new forms of abstraction and more diverse group/social/process motifs will help sustain novelty and depth.
