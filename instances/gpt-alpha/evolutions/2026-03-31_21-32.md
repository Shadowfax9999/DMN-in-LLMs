## Reflection

Recent sessions reveal a strong, mature engagement with both sensory detail and associative wandering. There is a notable breadth in settings and perspectives: transitions in public spaces (moving walkways, platforms), abstracted lists, and deep dives into subterranean/bodily perspectives (the worm in soil). The writing is rich in texture and mood, with associative leaps between the physical, the social, and the abstract. Form variety is present, including fragments, lists, and prose, with occasional section breaks and shifts in focus. Endings are mostly plain and unobtrusive, though a few ("the world is thick, not wide") verge on quiet resonance.

Despite this overall health, certain motifs are at risk of settling into new grooves: public transport and transitional spaces (walkways, platforms), the choreography of group or crowd movement, and environmental process metaphors (gradients, thresholds, emergence). The domestic/kitchen motif has successfully receded for now. There is also a tendency for the form to gravitate toward mid-length, multi-section prose, with lists and fragments appearing but not dominating. Concept integration is natural and generative, with seed ideas sparking genuine associations rather than feeling forced. However, a few closing lines approach a literary resonance that should be gently watched to prevent formulaic closure.

The system prompt is doing effective work, but could use a subtle reinforcement against overuse of public/crowd/transit settings and a gentle nudge toward solitary, bodily, or purely abstract wandering. The concept bank remains robust, but could be expanded with more solitary process, interior sensation, and transition/beginning concepts to further support breadth. Overall, the DMN is in a healthy exploratory phase, but vigilance is needed to prevent new habitual anchors from forming, especially around public/conveyance motifs and environmental process metaphors.
