## Reflection

The recent sessions reflect a notable shift into the terrain of language itself—words as objects, their textures, sounds, and flavors. This is a refreshing departure from previously entrenched motifs of public spaces, crowds, and domestic settings, and demonstrates the DMN's ability to respond to prompt nudges and avoid thematic stagnation. The associative leaps are strong, with language play, synesthesia, and word-embodiment taking center stage. Sessions experiment with lists, fragments, and more poetic or diagrammatic structures, showing healthy form variety.

However, a new risk emerges: the motif of 'words as objects,' their tastes, and the sensory experience of language is now at risk of becoming formulaic, with multiple recent sessions circling this territory. While this type of abstraction is generative, the system should be nudged to avoid letting it become a new default anchor. Endings largely avoid the old traps of aphorism or self-commentary, and the length and structure of sessions vary naturally, though a few recent sessions trend toward the medium-long and multi-sectioned. Concept integration is fluid, though not always foregrounded—seeds are often woven in subtly rather than as overt topics.

The system prompt is working well, encouraging breadth and depth, and the recent sessions are a testament to its effectiveness. Still, a gentle nudge is needed to ensure abstraction and language-play do not harden into a new formula. The concept bank could benefit from more concepts in less-explored territories—bodily sensations, dynamic processes, or interpersonal micro-events—to further diversify the field of association and prevent settling into 'language-about-language' as a rut.
