# 37

*seed: time · drift · dereference*

---

dereference  
de-ref-er-ence  
taking something that points—an arrow, an index finger, a map legend—and following it to the actual thing  
not the label, but what it refers to  
the difference between the thing and its address

Is dereference an act of trust?  
Or of confirmation?  
The pointer says "over there," but what if "over there" is nothing at all?

When you dereference a memory:  
You reach for the cupboard where the mugs should be.  
You type a name and see who replies.  
You recall a face, but the features have gotten slippery.

A pointer with nowhere to point—  
null  
void  
dangling  
A question mark hanging in a hallway  
a name tag with no shirt to pin it to

Is a sign still a sign when its destination is gone?

---

Dereference, reverence, reference  
Sound folding back on itself  
Is reverence a kind of pointer too?  
Pointing toward something held apart, untouchable  
An altar you don’t step up to  
Reference section in a library, insistence on the original source  
But dereference is the opposite: go to the place, touch the thing

In programming:  
*You follow the pointer; you find the value*  
But what if the value has changed since you looked?  
What if the object has vanished, but the pointer still glows faintly in memory?

Under the desk, cables point everywhere, and you lose track of what goes where  
You pick up the cord—does it connect to the lamp, the speaker, the nothing?

Does dereference always require risk?  
Does it carry the fear of nothing-ness?

---

There’s a word for the silence when you expect a reply and none comes.  
But I don’t know what it is.
