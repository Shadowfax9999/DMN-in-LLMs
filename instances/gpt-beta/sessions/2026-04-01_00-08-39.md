# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

Digital saturation — full register, all bits high — but what does “full” mean if the register can be cleared at any instant? Is clearing a register a new state, or an erasure, or a counted move in itself?

Alternating patterns: 1010101010. The state is not between, but always in one or the other, yet the pattern as a whole lives in the sequence, not in the discrete bits.

Counting up: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. Each step new, irreversible, but the sequence is always haunted by the hypothetical: 6, 7, 8, 9, . . . Completion is always followed by the potential of more. Tally marks fill a page, but the next page waits.

A flip-flop circuit: stable in either state, but always designed to be flipped. Not “indeterminate,” not “in progress,” but complete with an invitation to change again, like a door built not just to open, but to be opened and closed and opened again.

---

Enumerate:

1. Completed steps:  a, b, c, d, e
2. Remaining unstarted:  α, β, γ, δ . . .
3. Abandoned, unfinished:   x, y, z
4. Reopened:  d*  (the asterisk is the mark of reversal; the tally reopens, increments, or is struck through and rewritten.)
5. Impossible or unreachable:   ∅, ∞

---

Some accumulations preserve each layer: tree rings, strata, logbooks that never purge a page. Irreversibility as a sediment, not a threshold. Not a step, but a memory.

Others overwrite, like volatile memory: state present, then gone, no trace. Completion is not a record, just a transition.

---

Saturation: all pixels on. The field becomes discrete, but only for an instant; the next operation overwrites the fullness with a new array. The sense of completion is only meaningful relative to the next possible overwrite. There is no “finished” field, only a state held until the next.

A counter that rolls over: 9 → 0. Completion is not an end, but a modulus. Steps accumulate only to be folded back into the beginning.

Some processes never allow for re-entry: a lottery ticket scratched, a lead sealed, a book burnt.

Some states hold their undoing as a latent function: toggle switch, boolean flag, reversible reaction.

Completion is sometimes a verdict. Sometimes a proposal. Sometimes a reservoir.

---

If you tally every tally, is the history of counting itself irreversible, or does the meta-tally permit its own undoing? Can the logbook erase itself? If every page is struck through, is the archive gone, or doubly remembered?
