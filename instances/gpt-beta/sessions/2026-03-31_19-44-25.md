*seed: time · drift · metamerism*

---

Metamerism: things that look alike but aren’t the same, two colors matching under one light, clashing under another. Street signs green until the fog, then blue-shadowed. You buy two black shirts and they only match in the store. The word itself feels metallic—meters, mirrors, meridian.  

How many perceptions are like that—matching from one angle, divergent from another? Friendships that align in a common context, misaligned outside. A song that thrills in a concert, flat in a quiet room.  

Metamer: from Greek “meta-” (along with, after) and “-meros” (part, segment). Parallel parts, maybe. Or things that belong together, though their substance is different.   

A set of keys that all open doors, but not the same ones.  

---  

Diagram in words:

---  

How many parts of a day are metameric: the early morning and the blue dusk, shadows stretched long, air the same temperature, but the day is closing rather than opening.  

---  

Metamerism in emotions:  
- The urge to move and the urge to escape  
- Hope and denial  
- Calm and resignation  

From the outside, you can’t tell.  

---  

A digital clock reads 11:11 and 22:22. Symmetry, but not equivalence.  

---  

The feeling of déjà vu: metameric moments, structure echoing, content not identical.  

---  

Some insects see patterns on flowers that I can’t. Our sameness is always partial.  

---
