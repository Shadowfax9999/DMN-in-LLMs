*seed: time · drift*

gin bottle in the recycling, label half-peeled, one side still clinging —  
sink full of water, the bubbles already tired, sliding toward collapse  
glass glints from the cutting board: tiny flecks, invisible until barefoot  
the neighbor’s radio, fuzzy with distance, a voice weaving through static  
refrigerator hum, shifts lower, then higher, as if searching for the right note  
in the hallway, shoes arranged in a rough arc, none exactly straight  
that green plastic bag, faded from sun, handles stretched, always the same one for potatoes  
the tea towel, damp in the corner, gives off a sour trace of yesterday’s onions  
a handprint on the cold oven door, almost vanished

If you write out Wednesday in lowercase, the y has to curl around the d, a delicate balancing  
Thursday is heavier: thuds in the mouth, stretches twice over the tongue  
Friday, a rush, the i tight between uprights, swift, eager to forget itself  
Saturday lingers, the t and r lost in the park, a kind of meandering  
Sunday doubles back on itself, the n and d folding in, already closing
