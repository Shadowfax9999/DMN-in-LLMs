Recursion depth — a measure, a boundary, or just a warning in an interpreter, stack frames piling up, each level both identical and changed, like stepping through nested rooms with mirrors that shatter slightly at each threshold, image repeated but altered, softened at the edges or split by a hairline crack each time you descend; there is no bottom, only the point where the system refuses to go further — maximum recursion depth exceeded — but in thought, outside the machine’s rules, does recursion ever halt, or does the pattern simply become so fine, so interwoven with everything else, that it vanishes into texture, like threads worked into tapestry, steps in a fractal lost below the threshold of perception.

Recursion in the wild: river branches into tributaries, tributaries into streams, streams into tiny rills, each pattern echoing the structure above, but never truly the same — a fern leaf, a broccoli floret, lungs splitting, the bronchial tree, capillaries — a structure repeated until the matter itself can’t accommodate further division, or the division is too small to matter, the world shape lost in the granular noise of leaf-cells, cellular automata ticking along, each cell’s next state folded within the rules copied from its ancestors’ game.

Is recursion depth about repetition, or the keeping of memory? Does any recursive process imply a call stack, or can it be flattened, unwound, made iterative, memoryless? Tail call optimization: the process steps through itself without stacking up baggage, simply erasing the past as it moves, depth an illusion if you know the trick, but the pattern persists all the same.
