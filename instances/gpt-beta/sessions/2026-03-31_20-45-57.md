# 66

*seed: time · drift · clipping*

---

clipping—  
in audio, the waveform hits the ceiling, the peaks sliced flat, sound harsh with the cut

grass in the yard clipped short, a sudden uniformity, but along the fence: tufts, missed patches, margins where the blade lifted too fast, forgotten green left taller than the field

photo: edge of a shoulder cropped by the frame, someone’s elbow cut out, the unchosen portion still implied, existing just beyond the border

when the printer jams, the last line of text truncated, half words at the edge of the page

---

sometimes you squint at a pattern until it dissolves, the figure fading back into the field,  
afterimage behind closed eyes—grit in the static, details melting outward, signal drifting,  
like mist unforming

feedback:  
the more you try to fix the focus, the more shimmer pours through,  
not sharpening, but a restless vibration in the middle ground,  
neither foreground nor background,  
just a low cycling between notice and dissolve

---

clipping can mean a coupon, a small rectangle cut from a larger sheet,  
saved for some imagined future use, but the paper yellows in a drawer,  
the promise vague, the edges softening,  
eventually the shape becomes just part of the pile,  
no longer distinct

sound can be clipped or can clip—  
the sudden pop when plugging headphones in,  
the spike in the levels,  
the drop back to a wash

---

sometimes you highlight a passage, but the ink bleeds,  
the words underneath blur,  
the act of selection dulls the text,  
and the meaning slips out the side

in a dream, a face is clear for a moment,  
then dissolves back into the street,  
the outline never stable,  
nothing held long enough to catch or crop

---

The window smudges with fingerprints that only appear at a certain angle.
