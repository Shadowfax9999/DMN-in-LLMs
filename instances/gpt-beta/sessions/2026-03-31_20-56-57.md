# 71

*seed: time · drift*

---

Some forms only exist because of what goes through them: O-rings, cable glands, tunnels.  
A ring around nothing, but the nothing is the point, the boundary is the work.

There is a kind of information that emerges at the margin, where what passes through compresses the very edge until the edge becomes its own signal, a ring of hardened skin, a rim made from touch and repetition.  
The eye is a hole with a living grommet.

Lace the world with aperture and ring and aperture and ring.  
If you measure the strength not by what holds, but by how elegantly the transit happens, the grommet is apex: meant only for friction and passage.

Does the thread know that it is patterning the hole each time it passes through?  
Or is the hole altered only by the sum of many anonymous threads, the permutation over time, the shift that doesn't announce itself until the fabric slumps, the grommet spins loosely in its socket, the fit is soft.  

---

A process:

1. Punch the hole  
2. Line the edge  
3. Press the grommet  
4. Insert the thread  
5. Withdraw the thread  
6. Repeat  
7. Watch the shape become memory  
8. Observe slack

---

Oscillate:  
Thread in / thread out  
Edge tense / edge slack  
Form resolves / form diffuses  
Figure / ground  
Pattern / field  
Field / figure  
Never quite settling, rim always in play.

Is the mesh the sum of holes or the residue between them?  
If you zoom far enough, the holes merge, field becomes figure, the grommets touch—  
But step back: fabric, whole.

---

Grommet:  
O O O O  
O O O O  
O O O O  
O O O O  

From one, multiplicity; from multiplicity, a surface; from surface, possibility.  

---

At the limit, the grommet is less about keeping things in or out and more about letting them pass in a way that leaves the perimeter intact.

The rivet holds; the grommet lets go.  

---

You can only know the hole by what leaves it.
