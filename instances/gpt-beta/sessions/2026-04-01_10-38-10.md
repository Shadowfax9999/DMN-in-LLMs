*seed: time · drift · moduli space*

moduli space: the space not of points, but of forms  
forms of forms  
all possible configurations, but each configuration is itself a kind of summary  
a surface where each coordinate is an entire geometry; not a position, but the class of all positions indistinguishable under some rule

what is it like to wander in moduli space?  
there is no single object—each "here" is a universe of equivalent objects  
to move is to deform, but also to reclassify:  
   — now a torus stretched long  
   — now a torus squashed flat  
   — now a sphere with two handles, the handle pinched so tight it’s nearly a point, but still a handle  
the points blur, because the objects blur  
the space itself is fuzzy; distinctions can dissolve if the equivalence relation shifts

to count is to slice; to enumerate is to partition  
in moduli space, slicing is itself a choice of lens

a field of teapots, all slightly different, all "the" teapot  
what does it mean to remember a particular teapot here?  
it’s both present (as a region) and dissolved (as a member of a wider class)  
memory in moduli space is a matter of zones, not points  
you cannot make a tally; you can only map a neighborhood

the structure is not a list, not a tally, not a stack or a ledger  
it is a terrain, everywhere thick with variation  
distance is meaning: how far must you travel before the object is truly changed?

---

Permutation of moduli:  
1. The set of all lattices in the plane, up to scaling and rotation  
2. The set of all polygons with n sides, up to similarity  
3. The set of all curves of genus g, up to homeomorphism  
4. The set of all arrangements of three points on a line, up to translation and scale  
5. The set of all complex structures on a donut  

"Up to" is the hinge—the point at which counting becomes continuous, at which particularity dissolves into family  
A process that can finish, but never resolve to a sequence of digits  
Counting stops making sense when every point is a cloud

---

You walk in moduli space, but you don't step—  
you nudge a parameter, you merge two ends, you twist a handle, you let a neck shrink or swell  
You don't leave footprints because there's no single surface  
You might return, but the neighborhood is different now—the equivalence has changed, the moduli have shifted  
A record is impossible unless you choose a cross-section, a gauge  
But the section you choose is arbitrary; you could have sliced otherwise  
No act of logging survives the next deformation

---

To know a thing in moduli space is to know it as part of a family  
To lose a thing is only to change its equivalence class  
There is no last page of the record, only a new region of the field  
The teapot turns into a vase, then a cup, then a closed curve with two mouths  
Nothing is counted; everything is adjacent  
You can only say, "Here is the kind of thing that could have been,"  
and move on.
