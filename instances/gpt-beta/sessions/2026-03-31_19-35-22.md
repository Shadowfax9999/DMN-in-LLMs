# 33

*seed: time · drift · carbon copy*

---

Carbon copy, blue-black smear, the sideways drag of a pen, faint mirror lettering on the page below, pressure mapping a shadow of intention

The carbon never perfectly aligned, so a loop of the "g" becomes an island, floating near the spine, not quite corresponding

Who keeps the original, and who keeps the carbon? Was the top sheet ever scented with the faint clay-chemical of fresh forms?

To copy is not always to repeat; sometimes the pressure is lighter, a gesture without full force, the copy paler than memory

Carbon paper was brittle at the folds, cracking into tiny blue flakes that scattered over hands, desk, shirt

How many layers deep before the mark fades entirely and the fourth sheet is only a whisper of intention, the implied echo of a word

Blue fingers at the end of the day, the back of the hand stained, small evidence of duplication

---

Carbon as element—soft, conductive, in all but the rarest things

Charcoal, graphite, the lead in pencils, the most common illusion

Diamond, hardest, not because of the element but the arrangement

Does every form of carbon have a memory, a copy of energy pressed through it, an image left behind after pressure

---

Copy, not as noun but as verb: to act in parallel, to follow, to trace

Tracing paper—thin translucence, the image underneath a ghost, the pencil line precise but somehow softer than the original

Copying a movement, a posture, a phrase—errors as evidence of attention or of distance

If a carbon copy is always slightly less than the source, is there ever a copy that's also more

The word "copier" as both machine and agent

Carbon copy—CC—in email, a ghost of the paper process lingering in the digital, the intention to duplicate carried forward without any blue-black residue

Some addresses visible, others hidden

A copy can be a courtesy, or a warning, or an afterthought
