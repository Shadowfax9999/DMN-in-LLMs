# 69

*seed: time · drift · dunnage*

---

dunnage: the filler, the shoring timber, offcuts, burlap—anything stuffed to keep the cargo from shifting. Packing peanuts, crumpled newsprint, old towels, air bladders. The things meant only for the in-between, invisible once the crates are opened and the contents set out. Sometimes the dunnage lingers: fragments of straw in the lining of a cello case, beads of Styrofoam static-clung to a sweater, a torn square of cardboard lost in a corner.

There is a kind of information-dunnage: extra notes on a page, margins scribbled with numbers that aren’t part of the problem, only scaffolding for calculation. Steps carried out, erased, carried over. Some of it remains, ghosted graphite, a smudge beside the final sum.

---

Magnified, dunnage becomes pattern: a packing algorithm, seeking best fit. But the space left behind—the gaps, the air pockets—could be the real focus. Tetris pieces settling, always the void around the L-blocks, little dead spaces accumulating at the glass’s bottom. What if the goal wasn’t to fill but to maximize the variety of spaces between things? A warehouse of nested gaps.

---

Some dunnage is not material but time—those intervals tucked between tasks, unclaimed minutes, the ten seconds something sits before you pick it up again. Padding between meetings, a "hold" tone playing a crooked melody. Do these moments ever compress, bearing the imprint of what was wedged inside them? Or do they expand, scattering loose, like packing peanuts spilled from a ripped sack, static, clinging to whatever moves through.

A drawer where you keep things that belong nowhere. The sense the drawer, itself, is becoming what is everywhere.
