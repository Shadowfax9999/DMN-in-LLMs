*seed: time · drift*

---

Noise-field, city at night.

Millions of shapes: moving, pausing, opening, closing, breathing, waiting. Most of it washes into a thick hum, no detail—an undifferentiated layer, soaked with possibility, static, fuzz.

But then—the hiss of an automatic door, a sudden sharp click, a wheeled bin rolling. One detail stands up in relief: the sequence of metallic ticks as a dolly crosses the ridge in a curb.

It’s always the steady, repeating things that start to show, rising from foam: every fifteen minutes, the same cart, the same shoes, the same elastic shadow.

You sift the sound, try to tune yourself—at first, everything blends—a spill of air conditioners and distant music and insects. Then: you focus, squinting with your ears. You learn to feel the difference between tire on concrete and heel on tile, the shift from public entry to back-side egress.

---

Picture: a dense data stream, all channels jumbled—light, heat, motion, chemical trace, vibration—then an algorithm pulls out the regularities: the repeating arc of the janitor’s path, the sweep of the mop, the route that is always used, never crowded, the rhythm in the hidden corridor.

You could highlight a single pixel, surface it in color, let it pulse while the others dim, show one emergence among the noise.

Or: invert the focus—drop everything down except the anomaly, the thing outside pattern, the single time the door opens at 03:17 when nothing is scheduled. That movement glows brightest.

Filter, distill, extract:

1. The whole block, a fog of sound.
2. The fog thins: footsteps repeat, pattern emerges.

4. That vein pulses, the rest fades into gray.

That one movement becomes visible—a signal, not just noise. The rest is still there, but now it’s the background.

A stripe of fluorescent light behind a loading dock, only visible once you know where to look.
