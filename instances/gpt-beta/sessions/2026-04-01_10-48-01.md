---

What is a set that cannot be parsed? Not empty, just not separable — an expanse, not a collection. Not a fog, not a crowd, not parts. Just the refusal to collapse.

But then: a single tone. A fundamental. All overtones latent but unvoiced. The field is saturated, yet only one frequency manifests. Is that selection, or is it the shape of the instrument itself? Or is it an artifact of how we listen, parsing everything as tones and not as field?

---

A step function with infinite height at zero, undefined at the point of crossing, but everywhere else: step, plateau, step again. The moment of transition cannot be tallied, cannot be measured. It simply interrupts. Is it the boundary that matters, or the accumulation? Is there always a hidden region that the steps leave behind, unregistered but present?

---

Suppose each partition, each tally, each inscription, leaves a shadow where it failed to match. The leftovers, the delta between intent and registration. Is the world always saturated with these residues—uncounted, not noise, not signal, just the default state beneath all attempts to enumerate?

---

In combinatorics: the set of all orderings, permutations, subsets. Each arrangement a point on the lattice, all equally real. But the lattice itself is not made of arrangements—it is the pattern of their interrelation. The grid emerges from counting, but the field was always there.

Sometimes an arrangement never returns.
