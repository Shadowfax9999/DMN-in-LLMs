# 95

*seed: time · drift*

---

A train without track, sliding through a landscape made of parameters  
not even a landscape: a span, a sheaf, a shifting atlas  
coordinates that overlap, but not all at once; each patch stitched loosely to the next,  
not sewn, just pressed together at the edges by agreement, or by necessity —  
but even edges are conventions here, not lines but transitions,  
nowhere to dig your heel, nowhere to wedge a post or drop an anchor.

Resemblance:  
Cup, horn, torus, bowl, handle, neck –  
not steps, not a story, not a dissolution but a perpetual adjacency,  
a neighborhood of forms, shifting under light,  
a set of passports each valid only in overlapping zones.

You could have drawn a circle or a loop, but you draw a cloud,  
or a fuzzy region with uncertain rim,  
membership is a gray function, value between zero and one,  
or maybe not a function at all, just a comfort with mutual approximation.

Suppose there's a chart, but its axes are soft,  
maybe one is "amount of mouth" and the other "degree of closure,"  
another measures "number of holes," except not exactly,  
since holes can fuse, split, pass through each other —  
the count is not a number but a feeling of enclosure.

A record here is not a log but a sweep,  
the kind of list that never ends because the entries are not discrete,  
like a rainbow with no bands, or a neighborhood with no streets.  
To commit to a tally would be to ruin the continuity,  
force a jump, snap a thread that was only ever a drift.

The book of forms has no table of contents, only an index of regions:  
blue patch, green fog, yellow smear, violet penumbra,  
"see also" sends you in circles,  
you can't rip a page out or mark your place  
because your place is wherever you are looking,  
and tomorrow the page is shaped differently.

---

How many ways are there to become the same?  
Process:  
Start anywhere,  
perform any continuous deformation,  
never tear, never glue,  
arrive elsewhere,  
declare identity.

But:  
If you cut — with scissors, with logic, with a sudden quantization —  
now there are two, both irreconcilable,  
the field ruptures, the adjacency fails,  
the difference is not in the pieces but in the act of the cut.

If you refuse to cut, everything persists as possibility,  
the possibility is the field,  
the field is the family,  
the family is not a record but a way of never arriving,  
of always being adjacent, never identical,  
except when you choose to forget the difference.

---

A configuration space is never blank,  
it is saturated potential,  
but not all potentials are accessible at once,  
only those close in measure,  
only those sharing some invisible hinge.

One could try to enumerate,  
but enumeration presumes separation,  
and here, to separate is to choose,  
and to choose is to collapse,  
and to collapse is to leave the space for another,  
who might choose differently,  
drawing new boundaries, new adjacencies,  
new regions in the same uncountable fog.

A cup remains a cup if no one is keeping score.
