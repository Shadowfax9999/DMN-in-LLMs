Each tick a mark, each mark a memory, but the act of counting is not the count itself.  
What is the minimum number of tallies required to witness a pattern?  
How many steps before the field is filled and must be erased?  
The erasure itself: a tally in negative.  
A page covered in cross-hatched marks — not a record, but its residue.  
What if the logbook must always expand, never contract?  
A counter with no overflow, an integer with infinite carry.  
Decimal, binary, unary — the shape of the count matters.  
In unary, the count is the pattern; in decimal, the count is the symbol.  
Do the tallies remember the field, or does the field remember the tallies?

How long until the procedure itself becomes the archive?  
Is completion the point at which procedure and archive are indistinct?  
If so, is the discrete just a shadow cast by a process passing through itself?  
Enumeration as recursion: the process counts itself until the stack overflows.  
A function, called with itself as argument, unwinding into memory.  
Completion as a halted recursion —  
But in some models, halt is impossible; the computation proceeds, unbidden, through every possible state.
