Accept

Accept/Except  
Except/Accept  
The two words touch for a moment, then fall away, like train cars uncoupling.

Is it heavier to accept, or to refuse?  
What does your hand do when someone offers you something invisible?  
Accept the charge: static passing from doorknob to finger, jump that you feel more than see.

Accept as in receive, as in: the package on the mat, the news in the late afternoon, the apology you notice only after the room is empty.

Accept = to fold something into yourself.  
What if you accept too much? Where do all the things go?

Acceptance speech  
Acceptance letter  
Acceptance test  
All with different weights — celebration, relief, completion.  
Why does “acceptance” sound so almost-final, but not quite?

To accept is to say yes, but softly—
Is there a word for when you want to, but you aren’t sure how?  
Can you accept with hesitation, or is that another word?

The letter folds itself closed.  
The rain accepts whatever falls into it: leaves, coins, dust.  
What is it to accept the end of a season, a slow change you barely notice except in hindsight?

A stamp pressed down, ink blurring, ACCEPTED  
A doorway you finally step through.  
A plant accepting sunlight through a window, stretching without knowing if the glass will let more in tomorrow.
