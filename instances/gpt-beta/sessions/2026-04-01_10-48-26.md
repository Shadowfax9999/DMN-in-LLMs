# 100

*seed: time · drift*

---

A block of memory, sixteen cells long. Each written, overwritten, claimed, zeroed, then inscribed again. The fullness here is not abundance, but address—the way a number becomes an endpoint. No more cells left means no more writing. The act ceases not because of fatigue or choice but because the structure gives out: pointer reaches highest value, increment would wrap, so instead, it stops.

---

Counting to one thousand on an odometer. 999 clicks to 000, and in some cars, the mechanism resists, the digit refuses to roll again, locked in a cycle that only pretends to return. The digits keep moving but the tally cannot exceed itself; it overwrites what it is, not what it was.

---

Firm closure, but not abrupt. A spreadsheet with no more columns. Column AZ, then BA, then BB, and so on, but eventually system-limited, a geometry of naming running out. The field is not infinite, just indexed elaborately enough to seem so, until the edge appears—not an edge of meaning, but of enumeration.

---

Is a field ever truly saturated, or always just refusing to admit more? A honeycomb filled: the last drop slides in, surface tension domes the cap. No more can be taken without spill, and yet nothing in the pattern says "finished" except the bulge, the sense.

---

What if the capacity is not known in advance? The process continues until resistance, overflow, denial. Like typing into a form that suddenly rejects more characters—character 256 is simply refused. You notice the ceiling by failing to exceed it.

---

Discrete tokens, finite slots. Not a continuous field, but a structure of yes/no, present/absent, filled/vacant. Each step toward completion is an irreversible act; you cannot un-write the ink once it dries, cannot un-mark the square once checked.

---

A puzzle with all pieces in place: the absence of vacancy, every shape snugged against its neighbor, nothing left to do. There is no *signal* in the completion, only the silence of done.

---

Some endings are not events, but constraints made visible.
