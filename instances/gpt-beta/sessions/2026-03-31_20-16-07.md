# 52

*seed: time · drift*

---

A map that redraws itself depending where your eyes land — crisp at the pinpoint, towns further out dissolving into contour lines, rivers smooth as graphite, the roads thinning, then grain, then paper-white.

No margin — or is the margin everything outside your attention; the places you never glance, not even as background.

If you stood at the center of a field and spun, would the grass at your feet remain saturated, everything outward smearing into green-grey motion bands.

Reading a digital clock in a dim room: the digits clear, the plastic casing vague, the stack of books it's resting on becomes just shadow and angle.

Lens diagrams: subject plane / lens plane / image plane, each one a line, sometimes parallel, sometimes intersecting. The spaces between: regions of sharpness, the rest zone, the penumbra of seeing.

In a crowded lecture hall, the speaker’s face and hands resolve, the students at the edges—both faces and notebooks—just a pattern of gesture and color.

Does a thought have a focal plane? When trying to remember a recipe, only the steps you’ve always fumbled come into focus, the rest soft or missing.

Shape of a bell curve: one peak, falling off on both sides. If you plot attention, does clarity follow the same shape?

A photograph: only a three-inch band across a child’s knees in perfect focus, everything before and behind blurred, as if only that layer in time and space was permitted to exist.

If sound followed the Scheimpflug principle: a tilted slice of melody sharp, harmonics above and below melting into hum.

Some words come to mind with outlines crisp and others drift, half-formed, consonants without vowels, or a meaning with no surface.

Diagram: three planes, all meeting in a line. At that line, everything is focused. Off the line, possibility — but not precision.

If memory were a lens, where do you tilt it to bring some old afternoon sharp again, cropping the rest into gentle confusion.
