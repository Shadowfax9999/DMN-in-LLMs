# 67

*seed: time · drift · molt*

---

Molt: the outer casing loosens, the shell or skin begins to detach at the seams, underneath something soft, unfinished, pale; the boundary that was once pattern or color or armor is now a relic, a husk on the branch, still bearing the ridges and whorls, but empty, curled, the interior gone

The moment before the molt—an unease, a pressure, something not quite fitting, the old shape resisting, then splitting; the process never witnessed straight on, only the aftermath, the shed lattice on the bark, or a feather drifting loose, more absence than event

Sometimes the old skin clings, tattered at the edges, not fully released—ghost traces, doubled outlines, the new blurring with the residue of the old; you touch the leaf and the casing flakes away, dust on your fingertip

In books, exuvia: the cast-off, the left-behind container

---

Surfaces: A chalkboard after erasure, the faint palimpsest of words persisting, never fully black; the eraser smears more than it removes, each pass layering the history, a fuzz of past lessons not quite gone

Voice memos stacking in a phone’s memory, none deleted, all with identical timestamps at first glance, fragments of melody, reminders for tasks that never resolve, their significance fading, the original purpose unclear, just noise accumulating, every play compressing the meaning

A receipt in a pocket, text blurred from too many washes, numbers running, subtotal merging with the creases

---

If you try to clean a mirror with the wrong cloth, streaks appear, dividing reflection from distortion, and the face only comes into focus between the smears, clarity and cloud alternating as you move your head

Molt/remainder: Is there a version where the emerging form is not clear, where the process never completes, always half-in, half-out? The field of cicada shells under the oak—so many discarded patterns, more empty than full, the noise of them crunching underfoot

Blurring as a kind of molt, the old signal breaking apart into field again; radio static after the song fades, the melody lingering as an afterimage, then dissolving entirely, field growing over figure

Sometimes you find a single line of text highlighted, but by now the meaning is gone, the words just more paper, the ink part of the softening

The lamp throws a shape on the wall, but every hour the shadow moves, shifts, thins, until the room is just the room again
