# 48

*seed: time · drift · a book with someone else's bookmark still in it*

---

The spine is cracked, a little, and the bookmark is a postcard from a festival in 2018. The image side faces in. Who held this last, and what sentence were they saving.

Page edges dust-furred. The corner of one page folded, then unfolded, the crease soft as an old bedsheet.

---

A keyring in the pocket of a winter coat, the keys long forgotten, but the weight still familiar.

---

Cardboard box with masking tape doubled over for an easy pull, but the tape has sealed down so long the tab is just another surface, glossy with age.  

---

Some numbers turn out to be too round to remember — 1000, 5000, always slipping away, but 912 sticks, clings, a house number or a time on a clock that means something.

Syllable clumps: bookmark, doorstop, doormat, slipcase, all carrying an object and a threshold.

Plastic bag knotted tight, not by you, the knot pressed flat, still holding a tangle of rubber bands inside.

---

A place to pause inside something ongoing: a bookmark, a comma, an unwashed spoon left by the sink, not finished, not left behind, just held.
