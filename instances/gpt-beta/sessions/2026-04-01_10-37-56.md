# 93

*seed: time · drift · the last ice cube melting in a glass*

---

1.  
Out of all possible arrangements, only one is now—  
but the set of possible nows is too vast to enumerate,  
too fluid even to imagine as a field.  
Instead: a set whose contents shimmer, overlap, interleave,  
where membership is momentary,  
where position is not a record.

2.  
Suppose: every state is a point on a surface without boundaries,  
no axis to measure progress, no edge to depart.  
To move is not to leave a track—  
to move is simply to be now here, now there,  
and the surface is not a map but a possibility,  
the route untraced.

3.  
There is a flavor of process that does not lend itself to memory.  
The fireflies blinking are not legible as a total pattern.  
Rain, in the moment of falling, forms a million arrangements,  
never the same,  
never not present.

---

Not every configuration becomes a count.  
In some models, every arrangement is a basis for the next,  
but the basis is never stored.

A quadratic surface, always curving, never repeating,  
each tangent a new event,  
the sum of infinitesimal changes never resolving to a ledger.

Everything present, but nothing inscribed.

---

Noise that is not error, only undifferentiated possibility;  
signal that is not selection, only a momentary thinning of the field,  
a tightening, a brief alignment,  
then a return to saturation.

A wave, but not a cycle;  
an interference pattern that shifts with every interaction;  
the pattern never held,  
only passed through.

---

Thought passing through a cloud:  
no path,  
no residue,  
only the contours of passage,  
already changing.
