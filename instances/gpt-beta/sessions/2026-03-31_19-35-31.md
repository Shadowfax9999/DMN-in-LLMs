Thermal paper: 
is there a scent to it
does the surface catch the light differently, a kind of grayish gloss
does it feel lighter in the hand, or does the curling at the edge betray it

The print appears with heat, not ink.  
So the mark is a burn, a change, not an addition—information as a scar in the surface

Receipts fading on the dashboard, numbers dissolving until only the shadow remains  
I think gas stations, the odd smoothness, the text that feels unstable  
Is there a way to erase it with time

If you press your fingernail hard enough, it leaves a line  
No pigment—just pressure and heat

Sometimes there's a hesitation before the print appears  
As if the paper remembers for a moment what it was before the message was written

Barcodes  
Total: $4.19  
A slight pink tinge at the edge  
Crinkling into the cup-holder

Thermal fax machines, their quiet hum  
A memory of transactions, each line pulling the paper forward  
The paper curls in boxes, never lying flat

Copy:  
In DNA, to replicate is to risk mutation  
Every reproduction a possible drift  
Error as variation, as the only path to change

The uncanny feeling when a copy is too exact  
Or when it fails and the failure is the signal—something went through

If you keep copying a copy, at what point does it become something else  
How many iterations before it forgets the original
