# 72

*seed: time · drift · fond*

---

fond  
sounds like found  
sounds like pond  
the smooth ellipse, the open middle, vowels that linger  
i hold the word, let it pool behind teeth,  
fond: not just liking, but a warmth that seeps out sideways,  
not fixed, not hard, not certain—  
something gentle, almost a blur at the edge of wanting

fondant: a paste, pliant, shaped and remade  
to be fond of is to let yourself be changed by the thing  
to soften—  
fondness as a slight slack in the line

---

between fond and found:  
what is lost, what is held, what is never quite discovered  
the difference between having and loving  
possession, but with room at the edges for something to slip away

---

there are objects i think i am fond of but can’t recall the names  
sometimes the feeling is sharper than the image  
sometimes the image hovers, blurry, but the feeling is clear  
a glass with a chip at the rim  
a sound made in another room  
a smell left on a scarf

if you are fond of an absence, is that just longing  
or is it the shape the missing thing leaves behind

---

fond, fonder, fondest  
but never fondle—  
the word stumbles, the feeling curls back  
not all derivatives carry the source

words stretch thin, reveal the holes where meaning presses through  
fond is an edge: not love, not indifference  
a mesh between

O O O O O O O O O O O O O O O O

sometimes i can’t tell if i’m remembering the thing  
or just the feeling of liking it

fondness can outlast contact  
linger like scent in a hallway  
wash up, recede, come again

---

a word with soft corners, never quite closing  
fond, pond, sound  
vowel after vowel, ringing  
a grommet for the voice

fondness is never the whole field  
just the trace around one part of it

---
