# 62

*seed: time · drift*

---

First: everything is there, all frequencies humming — the hiss before the music starts, lines waiting for one to thicken.

A page covered edge to edge with penciled numbers: 79320419210313407. No columns, no sums. Looking becomes searching. You find 13, because your eye lands on it. Now the page is organized around a gap, the space just after.

Shadow on the bathroom tile is not part of the tile. You catch yourself arranging the shapes, tracing edges, pretending there’s a pattern while your other mind just lets the light move.

There are processes that make order: convolution, filtering, extraction. A photo before sharpening; after. The edges leap out, boundaries that were always there, just not chosen yet.

On the bus, the seats all the same until you need one. The one with the cracked vinyl, the one nearest the exit, the one under the only working light. All become signal; all the others melt into background. You never think about the ones you don’t notice.

--- 

Stones in a river, most hidden below the surface, but one breaks the water and the current splits. That’s the beginning of pattern.

Sometimes you sort coins for the tiny dings, the one with the blue mark, the one older than you. They accumulate, a pile transforming by the act of picking.

Through the window screen: every hole the same, but your eye catches one bent out of shape. Now the screen becomes a field with a singularity.

Erase everything but what the pencil missed. A ghost of letters, not the message itself.

A kitchen drawer: dozens of utensils, but you reach for the one with the red handle.

Each heart monitor beeps, but your ear waits for one arrhythmia, one skipped note, and then everything else is quiet.

--- 

Somewhere in the silt, a shard of blue glass, colder and sharper than the riverbed. 

I think the lamp is just a little too bright for this room.
