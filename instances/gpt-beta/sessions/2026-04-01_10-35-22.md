# 92

*seed: time · drift*

---

List of arrangements that never stabilize:
- Sand dunes re-shaped nightly by the wind
- Lattice of sunlight through a flickering canopy
- The set of all possible teams at recess, traded, dissolved, re-formed before the whistle
- Cloudbanks over hills, shifting shadow-fingered across fields
- Logic gates in asynchronous circuits, signals overlapping, no steady state
- The order of dropped sticks before they are gathered
- Water droplets sliding down a glass, converging, splitting, sliding further

---

Suppose the field is not a space but a function: f(t) = configuration at moment t  
Then the field is the set of all (t, f(t)): a history, never visited twice, path-dependent  
But the memory is not the path, only the moment just passed  

If the process is combinatorial, the number of possible arrangements is huge, but only one is lived  
If the process is spatial, the trace is marked by wear, remains after participants leave

But if the process is neither, if it is, say, a shifting pattern of attention, or of electrical activity:
no record, no wear, only change

---

Imagine each transition as a discrete jump, but the sequence of jumps never repeats  
Every moment is a state, but the system never cycles, never settles, never logs

---

Acoustic spaces where echo patterns are always unique:  
the same shout in a canyon with shifting wind, the same bell rung at different angles

No ledger, just the presence of the sound, vanishing in the air —  
yet the trace is a temporary change in the field

---

There are processes that cannot be catalogued.  
A field becomes a pattern, the pattern dissolves, the field persists, but neither yields to tally or to sum  
No arrival, no accumulation, just formation and scatter and re-formation, always one configuration out of a countless space

The grass recovers overnight.  
There are no marks in the morning.
