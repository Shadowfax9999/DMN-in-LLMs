# 43

*seed: time · drift · a photograph of a room you recognize but can't place*

---

A photograph:  
   — Table with nothing on it  
   — Window glossed, showing only hints of leaves  
   — An unreadable calendar page, numbers blurred  
   — The wall socket with one prong exposed  
   — Chair tucked halfway under  
   — Light on the carpet like a rectangle cut from somewhere else  
   — Shadow touches the far corner, almost blue  
   — If you look too long, the air vibrates slightly, the outlines run  

---

What can you know from a photograph with no people in it?  
        The season by the thickness of the light  
        The cleanliness by the absence of dust in the corners  
        The era by the outlet, the trim, the shade of beige  
        The lived-in or the waiting-to-be-lived-in  
        The slight warp in the doorframe  
        Whether the clock is electric or not  

A photograph—  
      always some missing layer:  
           sound of the fridge,  
           faint traffic,  
           footsteps above or below,  
           the way the light would feel on your forearm,  
           the question of why the room was empty then  

---

Refraction again: the photograph is not the room  
      The room through a lens,  
      A past moment poured into a new surface  
      The lines of the carpet never quite lining up  
      The sense that you were there, but just out of frame  
      Sliding glass, sliding memory  

---

There are rooms you remember only as photographs, even if you lived there.  
Sometimes you have to close your eyes to see the colors again.  
The light in photographs never feels like the light on your face.  
The corner where a phone could have been, but there's nothing in the photograph except  
the quiet.
