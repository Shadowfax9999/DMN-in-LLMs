gamut  
a word that doesn't sit still, spreading both up and down, range and array, but also a color chart, keys stretched across a clavichord, a sequence and a field overlapping

If gamut is a span, is span a line, or a shelter—rafters overhead, or a simple bridge from here to there? Span as a verb: to cross, to hold together without touching the middle, weight carried at the edges

Array, range, gamut, span—do they differ?  
Array: order imposed, placement with intention  
Range: two ends marked, territory between them, wild or measured  
Gamut: the whole scope, but also the vibration, everything that can be struck

Color charts and keyboard scales: a spectrum isn't a set of pegs to hang something on, it's a smear, a fullness, but then we name the intervals—red, orange, yellow, green—the illusion of discrete steps on a continuous slope

But "run the gamut": to traverse all states, to move through the whole possible field  
Does the world ever run its own gamut, or does it hover, oscillate, skip, refuse to visit every place in turn?  
Is there a process that insists on using the entire range, not just sampling, not just flickering along one ridge?  
Or does the mind merely imagine the fullness, mapping the seen onto the possible?

Chromaticism: every note between two points, steps so close they blur together, until the scale is a ribbon  
But a ribbon is also a kind of border, a way to mark the finish, or the full circuit of a boundary

---

A list, not of things that run the gamut, but of different ways completeness appears:

1. Every element visited, no repeats  
2. The field filled at once, instantaneous saturation  
3. A process that leaves a trace on each possible state  
4. A sum in which omission is impossible  
5. The sense, not of fullness, but of nothing left to add—no gaps  
6. A sweep, a scan, but some pockets are always missed  
7. A fixed roster, a page of checkboxes, each ticked in order  
8. Gamut as gesture: hand wide, encompassing  
9. Gamut as residue: the mark after every color has been used  

---

In the old sense, gamut was the lowest note in medieval music—gamma ut—one anchor, and then the whole scale: an origin, not just a boundary

So completeness isn't always the outer edge; sometimes it's the foundation, the note so low it holds the others above it

Anchors, origins, saturations, traces, fields, spans, arrays, ribbons, residues, rosters

---

A spectrum is not a log.  
A field is not a tally.  
Gamut, traversed, becomes memory, but before it is run, it is only readiness, all possible steps latent and humming.
