# 89

*seed: time · drift*

---

Some processes don’t move forward or backward.  
They hover, trembling in the region where a step could be taken but isn’t, or is taken and then instantly un-taken.  
A logic of alternating permission and withdrawal:  
    Allow, retract, allow, retract—  
But not as a rhythm that stabilizes, more as an instability that sustains itself.  
Like the cursor blinking in an empty field, insisting on presence by interruption.  
But the interruption is the presence: no letter, only the eligibility of letterness, on and off, visible and not, never both, never neither, but always oscillating in the space between.  

---

How many cycles do you need before the oscillation becomes the thing?  
Does wavering, repeated, become a structure?  
Or is the form always escaping itself, a recursion whose signature is not the trace but the trembling edge of the trace, never resolved into signal or erased into field?  

---

If you tried to map undecidability itself:  
    It would not be a region, or a border, or a difference,  
    but the reciprocation of conditions, each one held open by the return of its opposite.  
Not indeterminacy-by-absence, but indeterminacy-by-persistence.  
A hover that never lands, and never leaves.  

---

If you listen to a tone wobbling just around a note—  
    Not vibrato, not detuning, but the feeling of approach and retreat,  
    almost settling, almost departing—  
the ear starts expecting the ‘aboutness’ to last.  
Not expecting arrival, but expecting the not-arriving to continue.  

Some structures are built entirely from the expectation of not resolving.  
A logic of poised midairness, always at the inflection of being about to.  

---

If you repeated the word “wavering” enough times, would it become a state or a field, or just an echo?  

---
