*seed: time · drift · sundial shadow*

sundial shadow  
sun on a flat stone, the line shifting, everything slow and certain  
a place to put your finger and imagine the turning  
on the wall, a rectangle of light moves through the morning  
shadows in a stairwell, blue in the early part of the day, gold later  
the way a glass cup leaves an oval of shade with a bright core  
window blinds make stripes across the table, sharp-edged at noon, blurred by evening  
sometimes a shadow looks detached, as if it belongs to a different thing  
the hour hand is thicker than the minute hand, but the shadow thins as it stretches  
light leaks around the edges of a closed door, a band of brightness along the floor  
old clocks ticking in quiet rooms, the hands not quite lining up with their own shadows  
a branch outside flickering between sunlight and wind  
pavement warm where the sun touched it, cool underneath cars  
dappled shade under trees, circles overlapping in a pattern that moves when nothing else does  
walking east, your shadow long and in front of you, sometimes mistaken for someone else  
the word "gnomon" is easy to forget, but the shape is everywhere  
shadows colorless, but sometimes you want to call them blue  
the shadow moves fastest at the edges of morning and evening, slowest at noon  
on cloudy days, no shadows at all, only gray underfoot  
something left out too long becomes two colors: the part that sees sun, and the part never reached
