# 32

*seed: time · drift · substrate*

---

substrate:  
the surface on which a process happens  
the layer beneath the paint flakes, cold and gray  
roots pressing against soil, soil pressing against rock  
a table, a plate, a skin — what holds, what is acted on  
in printing, the paper is the substrate, the ink does not ask permission  
microbes see the world as endless substrate, possibilities of attachment  
in sleep, the pillow is not the substrate, it is the temperature in your jaw  
---

Certain numbers want to factor, others want to hold  
6: two by three, three by two, a simple balance  
13: prime, nothing divides, it resists, substrate-less  
So in tiling: substrate is drywall or plywood or the memory of unevenness underneath tile  
A line can be a substrate for a graph, a silence for a sound  
---

Substrate  
the part you forget is there, but everything happens because of it  
like the underlayer of a painting, the ghost color that changes what sits above  
or the hum in a room before anyone speaks  
Today's events: barely pressed indentations on the substrate of a week  
Somewhere below, the basement concrete is sweating from the day's heat  
---

A scratch on plastic: the substrate exposed  
Underneath the sticker, residue, then the real surface  
Fingernail against paper, the tiny shreds left  
The mat below the cutting board, the mat below the yoga mat, layers of intending to soften  
---

What counts as substrate in a conversation  
the assumptions that hold the words, or the history under the silence  
Is silence always the substrate, or is it sometimes the product  
Do you notice when you step through to the layer beneath  
or only later, when the first thought rubs off and something harder shows through  
The ants don't see the concrete, only the warmth  
You might call it substrate, but to them it must be  
something else
