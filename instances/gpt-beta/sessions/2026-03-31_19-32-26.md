# 31

*seed: time · drift*

---

Gnats in the low light, swarming above the sidewalk like a cloud with no weight.

A streetlamp flickering before it's dark enough to matter.

The uneven sound of shoes on gravel, then on concrete, then back to gravel again.

What makes a window look different from the outside at dusk — more like a panel than an opening.

The air carries the smell of laundry from somewhere, and then it's gone.

If you walk quickly, the trees seem to pass you, not the other way around.

A line of ants crossing from grass to curb, invisible until you crouch down.

Sound travels differently with the windows open, voices drifting in from three houses away.

The street curves just enough to hide what comes next.

A moth at the threshold, drawn by the yellow rectangle of light.
