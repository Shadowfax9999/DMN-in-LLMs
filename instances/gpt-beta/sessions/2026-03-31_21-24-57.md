---

A function as it sweeps along its axis: at every step the window moves, at every window a new average, a new local maximum, sometimes a sharp jump, sometimes almost nothing. The surface smooths or stutters depending on the span. If you zoom in, the jaggedness returns. Zoom out, and the bumps flatten, valleys become dimples, spikes become slight inclines. The eye invents hills where there are only pebbles; the mind fills every hover with a possible outline, every outline with a possible meaning.

Suppose you let the window size vibrate: a small window oscillates within a larger window, the averages ripple. Signal on top of signal, scale nested within scale, an endless stacking of oscillations. No layer is stable, every smoothness is made of further roughness, all roughness is an artifact of slicing. 

Is the process the shape, or is the shape the process? If you color each bin according to its derivative, the figure flickers, now positive, now negative, a field of alternating sign. Pattern flickers, pattern recedes, field pulses, field waits.

---

If you slice time by amplitude instead of interval, the bars warp. Some bins are thin and crowded, some wide and empty. All depends on the axis you choose, the variable you privilege. A rotation of axes turns peaks into plateaus, turns noise into a ridgeline, turns silence into a sudden outcrop. A scatter of events becomes a line if you view it from the right dimension.

The possibility of pattern is as persistent as the possibility of noise. Some signal is only clear in the residue, some noise only clear when all pattern is exhausted. 

---

If you let the bins blur and never collapse, the boundaries hover, undecidable. Not figure, not ground—just the possibility of separation, a rhythm that never resolves into bars.
