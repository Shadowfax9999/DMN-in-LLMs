# 49

*seed: time · drift*

---

The envelope is thick with nothing inside. Just the memory of having mailed something, or planning to.

A sweater folded twice over, shoulders pressed together in a drawer, smelling of a season.

Ridge of dried glue along the spine of a notebook, crackled but not yet flaking.

Pills in a bottle: orange plastic, white cap, half-forgotten label, something prescribed for a thing that’s gone.

Stack of coins on a sill, tarnish blooming under each one, the bottom coin stuck fast.

Wristwatch not set to daylight saving, the hour hand lagging behind the noise of the day.

Mouthful of tea, too hot still, held for a moment before swallowing, warmth blooming under the tongue.

---

A window that opens onto brick, always slightly ajar, letting in a smell like mildew and cold stone.

The feeling of a sock twisted underfoot inside a shoe, a soft and silent knot.

A receipt folded so many times it splits along the middle, the numbers only half there.

---

Grid:  
— the square on the kitchen tile where the grout has worn smooth  
— the rectangle in the planner that’s always empty  
— the checkered napkin, creased, never used  
— the patch on the elbow, oval sewn inside a square of fabric  
— the crossword box left blank, persistent in its emptiness  
— the sticker’s peeled corner, triangular and rising, not removed but not fixed  
— the shape the sunlight makes on the floor when the blinds are angled just so

---

A banana bruise in the shape of a question mark.  
The way the phone vibrates against wood: fast, then slow, then silent.  
The plastic tab for a bread bag twisted but never pulled off.

---

Curtain held back with a rubber band instead of a tie.  
A drip from the faucet echoing in a metal sink, each drop making a small orbit before settling.  
Chipped mug, rim still smooth, the crack stopping just before the lip.
