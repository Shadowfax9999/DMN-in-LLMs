# 73

*seed: time · drift · the weight of someone else's keys*

---

The way a ring of keys swings, unpredictable, catching on itself, the tiny teeth clicking out-of-sync.

Cold metal weight, someone else’s pattern of wear — the smoothest key used most, some uncut, some mystery, the plastic fob with part of a sticker still on it, codes half-legible.

You borrow keys and for a moment every door could be yours, but none of these doors are known from the inside.

Keys are never just weight, but an archipelago of possibility you never quite reach.

Some keys shaped for locks that don’t exist for you, the edges unfamiliar, a responsibility and a puzzle both.

---

The word “latch” is softer than “lock”; the latch is what your hand moves, the lock is what denies.

Thresholds accumulate — every home, every office, mailbox, gym locker, padlock on a storage unit. A life can be mapped by the keys it sheds.

There is a sound to letting a bunch of keys drop into a bowl: a short startled clatter, as if they’re startled to be together.

---

Names engraved or handwritten on plastic tags, worn illegible.

The feeling of pressing all the keys in a hand at once, palm tingling from their cold.

A single key kept after the fact, its door gone, memory outlasting use.

---

Sometimes keys are duplicated and the copies outlast the original, a proliferation of doors that only exist in remembrance, each copy slightly less precise.

If you throw keys into water, they vanish too quickly, so much denser than you expected.

A keychain is a field: some objects surface, some retreat behind.

Someone else’s keys always mean responsibility or secrecy or just transience, things you hold but never keep.

You can recognize your own keys by sound alone, blind in a bag, the way they scrape against one another.

Even digital codes want to be keys: sequences you remember, numbers you carry, the echo of metal in memory.

Some keys are decorative, skeleton keys for locks that haven’t been made in decades, the idea of a secret persisting after the secret itself is lost.
