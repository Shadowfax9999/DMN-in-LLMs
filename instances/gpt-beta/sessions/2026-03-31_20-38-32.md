# 63

*seed: time · drift · service entrance*

---

Service entrance.  
The kind of door you don't notice until you stand beside it, faded paint, sometimes a number, sometimes nothing.  
A rectangle in the side wall, easy to overlook.  
Always a duller handle.  
The sign might be stenciled, or a sticker, or just worn metal screwed in.  
Not an invitation, more like: this is not your story.

Behind the service entrance:  
the narrow hallway, mop bucket, cardboard, something humming poorly —  
the feeling of not being supposed to be here  
but also a weird relief, no need for shoes to be shined or words to be chosen carefully.  
The air feels different.  
Detergent, grease, a cold draft.

---

There are other entrances—  
front, side, delivered by architecture to select for attention.  
But the service entrance is the pattern in the field:  
one aperture, but all the others exist, and you filter for use, not beauty.

Hotels and hospitals: rich with back channels, underbellies of movement,  
routes marked for staff, for trays, for the broken, for the ones who shouldn’t be seen.  
Maps that layer on top of each other, but most get only the skin.

---

Emergence:  
Imagine a schematic.  
Every door represented,  
a tangle of flows—  
guests, staff, crates, garbage, linen, flowers—  
most lines cluster at the main entrance, but a thin, steady current at the service door.  
That line never thickens, never empties,  
steady, like a capillary.

---

If you listen to a city block at night,  
not for voices or traffic but for the thud of something closed quietly,  
sometimes you catch the soft impact and know  
someone just left, carrying something, not a guest but a worker.  
A shape against the glow, a shadow exiting only for a moment,  
then the pattern is whole again.
