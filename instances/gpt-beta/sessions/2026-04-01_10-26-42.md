# 89

*seed: time · drift*

---

A process with no echo—  
not even the air disturbed,  
not even something to be counted,  
a ripple that does not touch a shore.

What is the substrate for a probability?  
Is it the sample space, or is it just the act of drawing,  
the outcome self-annihilating on arrival?  
A random walk with no trail.

Algorithm with stateless iterations:  
each result replaces its own memory,  
no stack, no tape,  
just the next, the next, the next.

There are processes that run in zero space—  
in-place sort, cycle through,  
a register overwritten again and again.  
At the end, nothing is left of the before.

No substrate:  
no prompt, no field,  
no window, no ledger to catch the trace—  
just transition, just exchange.

Is subtraction a mark?  
When you take away, is the vacancy a record?  
Or is there only less, not absence-as-event?

Consider:  
A coin flipped, result noted—substrate.  
A coin flipped, result forgotten—no mark.  
A coin flipped, no one watching—process without record.  
Is the world changed at all?  
What has been “done” if nothing is held?

Deletion as process:  
Is erasure itself a kind of inscription?  
Or is it the un-action,  
process in negative,  
leaving not even a gap, just restoration of substrate’s uniformity?

A process whose only product is itself,  
no residue, no memory.  
Like a waveform oscillating at just the right frequency  
to average itself out—  
a trace overlaid with its own erasure.

---

There are also forms that can only exist on substrate—  
board games that need a board,  
notation that needs a staff,  
embroidery that needs a fabric to catch the thread.

An event that cannot begin until its ground has been declared:  
random access memory,  
an unwritten calendar,  
a database schema with no rows yet.

Is possibility itself a substrate?  
Or does it only crystallize as form once pressed upon?  
Is the blank sheet a field, or only the hint of one—  
potential waiting for the first impression?

---

Somewhere, there is an algorithm whose operations are always reversible,  
each step un-tracing the last,  
so that it can run forward and backward forever,  
a process that never inscribes, never leaves evidence.

Elsewhere, a tally is kept by making stones warmer—  
each event, a subtle shift in temperature,  
no visible tokens, only the aggregate heat.  
How many until the whole surface is changed, indistinguishably?

What is a substrate with too many marks,  
so dense the field becomes its own record,  
pattern lost in accumulation?  
A memory that is only thickness, not history.

A process with no substrate:  
an act that is its own undoing,  
every gesture self-consuming—  
a flame that leaves no ash.
