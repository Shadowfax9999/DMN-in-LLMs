# 41

*seed: time · drift · rime*

---

Rime — cold edge. Frost collecting on a branch, clinging. The word lingers, not as rhyme but as boundary, a crust of ice. There is rime on telephone wires in the morning sometimes, a delicate coat that dissolves before anyone notices. The world starts by accumulating: moisture in the air, settling, becoming visible.

Rime also floats at the margin of the tongue, like a word you almost remember. Letters crusted together, a little stiff, reluctant to thaw.

I think about the difference between frost and snow. Snow falls; rime grows. One descends, one collects from the invisible. The process is gentle, slow, barely perceptible. A pencil left by a window can feel rime, a coldness that is not quite wet.

Sometimes rime makes a thing visible only by hiding it. A fence becomes softer, less in focus. The world with rime is quieter at the corners. There is less echo, less clang. Metal loses its ring.

A glass of water beside the bed gets rime if the room is cold enough, just a fringe, a hesitation before the surface fogs.

Rime is not absence, but extra: a layer, a fringe, a fullness that obscures and adorns.
