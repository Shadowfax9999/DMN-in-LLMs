# 78

*seed: time · drift · metastability*

---

Metastability—states that endure, but only provisionally. Stable, yet not final. Prepared to shift; rest on a cusp. Not readiness, not hovering, but a form that permits arrival and reversal both. There’s the digital sense: a flip-flop circuit, left holding a value, one nudge away from flipping. There’s the physical: a droplet balanced on a waxed surface, not spreading, not rolling, but neither fixed nor falling. 

A metastable archive would not catalog finished things. It would catalog held-ness—states sustained against collapse, values kept pending by force or habit, arrangements liable to sudden revision, dominoes set upright but not yet tipped. 

1. The tray balanced on fingertips, not put down, not dropped  
2. The login session, open, still valid  
3. The candle flame, windless, not out, not blown  
4. The unsent message, drafted, awaiting a finger  
5. The chemical mixture, supersaturated, clear, until seed introduced  
6. The consensus, held as long as no one objects  
7. The vote, tallied but not yet announced  
8. The book, bookmark in place, mid-sentence, paused  

An archive of prolonged suspension, each record a poised persistence—susceptible, and yet enduring. Enumeration without closure.

---

What kind of process only ever advances by irreversibility? Step, click, notch, ratchet—never to be redrawn, never to blur backward. The pawl drops into the gear; the counter increases; the stack grows, never shrinks. History as a monotonic function. 

What is the shape of counting where regret is not permitted? What kind of memory is that? 

---

Two lists.  
1. States that remain possible, but lean toward one outcome.  
2. States that, once counted, cannot return.  

There’s no middle list.  

---

Is the number of completed things always less than the number of things begun? A tally grows, but the field of potentialities is uncountably wide.  

---

The metastable is not the incomplete. It is the completed, held open for reversal; the solid, with an invitation to switch.

---

A key hung on a nail:  
If lifted, the door opens,  
If left, it’s neither used nor lost—  
a “ready” that isn’t hovering, but a condition that stipulates what the world *could* be, if nudged.  

---

Not every process erases its shadow. Some histories preserve the possibility of flipping. Some records keep both the signature and the cancelation in reserve—ink that might dissolve, circuits that might still toggle, events that keep their negation latent, just off the page.
