# 75

*seed: time · drift · recto*

---

recto: the right-hand page, the front, the side that faces outward when the book is closed. But also a surface for accumulation, each recto layered over a verso, two faces of the same leaf. What does a recto remember? Is it imprinted by what pressed against it—ink bleed, indentation—does it hold the ghost of its verso? Or is it only surface, only this face-now, a unit in a tally, a step in a sequence.

Is there a finite number of rectos in a bound thing? If you count only rectos, do you reach the end faster? A book with 101 pages, is that 51 rectos and 50 versos? Or do you count covers, endsheets, blanks at the back? Sometimes a book is catalogued by leaves, sometimes by pages, sometimes by folios, each enumeration a different angle on the same object.

The box of slides is also indexed: not by story, but by number and label, handwritten, sometimes crossed out. Some slots stay empty. Some labels are smeared, rewritten—4 becomes 14, or is it 41? The plastic divider is warped, so the slides lean like a tiny domino run waiting to fall.

---

Completion: sometimes a process ends with a click, a period, a saturated field. Sometimes with a tally mark, a number written in a margin, a green light that stops blinking. Sometimes with nothing—a flat line, a new recto, blank, the count continuing.
