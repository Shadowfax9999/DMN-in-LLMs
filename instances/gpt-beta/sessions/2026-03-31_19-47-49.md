# 40

*seed: time · drift · the gap between a curtain and a wall*

---

Diagram (in words):  
         CURTAIN  
          |  
     (gap)  
          |  
        WALL  

Not wide, hardly a space for a finger, but the air there is colder, or warmer, depending which side of the day you’re on.

A single bead of light squeezing through, no matter how carefully the cloth was fixed, always the vertical stripe—dawn, or streetlamp amber, or just a thin dust.

What lives in the gap:  
- The insistent rectangle of morning  
- Drifting pollen, almost invisible  
- A moth pausing on the fabric  
- The sound more muffled, as if the curtain absorbs certain voices  
- The scent of pressed cotton, sharper at the edge  
- Sometimes a cat’s paw, uncertain, curious

Is the gap a border or a seam? Or is it an invitation?  
Is that where the old heat goes, pushed by the radiator, hovering, unable to cross?

Curtains billow when the window is cracked open, but the gap remains, anchored, the one place the air is always different.

If you look through the gap from side-on, the outside becomes a sliver: a car mirror, a tree half-remembered, the blue edge of sky.

Nothing really happens in the gap, but it draws attention.  
Why do children want to peek through, why does the eye keep returning?

There are so many edges in a room, but this one seems to breathe.  
If the sun moves, the gap is the first to change.  

The wall is cool, always, unless the day is high.  

The gap narrows at night, or maybe the darkness fills it in.  

---

How does a clock parse the difference between a moment and the shape of a day?  
If you measured the gap by time—how many seconds does the beam last before the curtain settles, or the day turns?  
Why do gaps seem to last longer than the things around them?  

Is every gap a kind of threshold, even if no one passes through?  

The edge of the curtain frays, but the gap keeps its line.
