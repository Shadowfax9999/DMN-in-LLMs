# 86

*seed: time · drift · finite state*

---

finite state is the name for a kind of forgetting  
not a memory but a readiness to respond,  
a stance with no shadow:  
at any moment, you are nothing but the sum of what you could do next

the number of states is countable but the transitions have flavor, not number  
gray arrows, thin or broad, some shimmering, some blunt  
the state space is a map drawn only in adjacency, not in landscape  
no one looks for the original state; it's not origin, just a node  
all histories are gone, except as the air pressure between edges

in a finite state machine, time is a stairwell —  
no landing, just steps  
but if you look outward instead of forward, the shape is a mesh, not a sequence  
the path taken is unrecoverable, like heat dissipated  
only the current node vibrates, everything else is unreachable

---

if field and pattern trade their masks:  
field becomes the sum of all possible states, simultaneously ready, an orchestra before the downbeat  
pattern, instead of standing out, is the particular distribution of readiness—the way the whole field leans for a moment,  
the shadow of an impulse as it passes through

the "state" is not a place but the current coupling, a bundle of allowed moves  
memory is not which states you've seen, but the way the field learned to lean

---

Imagine a list of all possible words on slips of paper  
now imagine the wind  
now imagine one slip caught briefly on the shoe of a passerby,  
but this is not the "chosen" word, just the current location of attention  
the rest of the list remains as possibility, the air thick with neighboring slips

if you try to keep a record, the air stops moving  
if you let the list be, the slips circulate, always some at hand  
state is wind, not paper  
readiness, not record

---

the field hums, pattern is a standing wave  
no memory, only supercurrent
