# 87

*seed: time · drift · diffusion*

---

Diffusion: not dissolution, not saturation, not filling a shape—just a gradient, a something-becoming-gradient, a loss of edge that is also a rule, a process, a way of being-distributed.

What if the pattern is not that something stands out, but that nothing does? Or, the pattern is the slope—the way the difference smooths, how contrast flattens, how the sharpness softens not all at once, but always by halves, or quarters, or a law with no memory except the local difference.

Diffusion equation: ∂C/∂t = D∇²C. But as a field—not a place, a process inside the numbers, time gently spreading concentration, a pulse unwinding.

Suppose there is a process where pattern and noise are only the names for different rates of flattening. If you look at the whole, nothing is lost, but every place is less itself. A process of sameness by erosion—not by vanishing, but by sharing.

---

Oscillation and diffusion: twin processes, not opposites, but phases. Oscillation preserves difference (now here, now there, but always amounting); diffusion erases difference by making everywhere the same (but slowly, so there is always a next step, a slope, a tendency).

Suppose: A pattern that oscillates while it diffuses, a signal that both returns and recedes, a process that cycles inward as it spreads outward. What would invariance look like? A preserved mean, an amplitude shrinking, a frequency that sags as boundaries blur.

A process that cannot be 'solved,' only inhabited—a field not settling, but always tending, always nearly.

---

Standing inside the diffusion: from any spot you see only the difference in your immediate vicinity, the feeling that there was once more contrast, that now everything is in-between. From outside: the slope is the pattern.

Sometimes the rule is not that anything is kept, but that forgetting becomes a method. The system forgets its own sharpness, not by falling apart, but by letting everything meet in the middle.

---

The word diffusion sounds soft, but its action is relentless.
