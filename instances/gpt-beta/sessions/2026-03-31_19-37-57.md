# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

1. Is there a perfect circle in the mind, or only approximations made sharper each time you redraw it?

2. When a voice memo is replayed, does the air remember the first sound, or does it begin again each time, thinner and thinner?

3. What of the digital copy, untouched by hands, its zeros and ones unwavering—does it drift when no one is looking?

4. A photocopied hand, veins blurred into rivers, each version fainter—eventually the lines float alone, belonging to no body.

5. To copy a face in memory: cheekbones sharper, laugh lines shallower, the eyes fixed in expressions that never quite happened.

6. A word traced over and over, pencil pressing deeper, the page beginning to tear, the meaning harder to read.

7. The faint echo left behind after a song ends, like a print in air—do you notice the silence thickening?

---

The way mildew writes over the pattern on old tile, layers of life on life, green threading into white.

There are movies I only know through summaries, never the real thing—copies of story, without the picture.

---

If a clock ticks without hands, how does time know it’s passing?

When you hear your own name, and it sounds like someone else’s voice.

A wish repeated until it fades into superstition, the details sloughing off, just the shape of wanting left.

The image on a coin worn flat by fingers.

In the morning, the imprint of the pillow still on the cheek, already starting to vanish.

Somewhere, the original is gone.
