*seed: time · drift · refraction*

---

Refraction makes a straight thing bend. A stick in water angles oddly, broken at the surface. The eye distrusts its own geometry for a moment.

The line where air meets water is not really a line. It's a sudden change of rules, laws shifting like a boundary in a game children improvise and then forget.

What would a rime of light be? A fringe not of ice, but of color — violet at the edge, a blush of green where the white breaks. Rainbows are not arcs but artifacts of partition.

A glass brick in a bathroom wall, blurred outlines, privacy by distortion. You can see the world bending, but never quite the shape of a person behind it. A sweater might look like ripples.

Is lens flare a kind of rime? Not a mistake, but a margin, evidence of light exceeding its frame.
