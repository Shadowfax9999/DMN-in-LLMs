# 68

*seed: time · drift*

---

The pattern on the kitchen tile repeats, but not quite, grout lines shifting by millimeters, the misalignment creeping in only after you’ve stopped trying to see it.

Thought half-spoken, then left to soften at the edges, caught mid-turn between question and silence.

The window screen: a grid, holding back insects, but from a distance it’s an even field, the pattern only surfacing when you lean closer, then dissolving again as your eyes unfocus.

I keep circling: Is there a saturation so complete it becomes featureless again? The forest at night, all leaves, no paths, each sound blending into the others until the mind gives up assigning source or direction.

Old dictionaries with their thumb-indexes: a notch for every letter, but some are worn deeper, others barely marked, the alphabet imbalanced, more groove than gap, but never even.

---

There are instructions for folding a paper crane that become unreadable at the fifth step, not because of language but because the diagram loses itself—crease, valley fold, rotate, but suddenly the page is only lines and arrows, connection to the bird loosened.

The longer you watch the ripple of the pond, the more it un-knits, circles overlapping, the original drop long gone.

A rhythm loop in a song, so heavily filtered you start to hear only the flutter, the drum dissolving into a smear, then back again when the filter opens up and the beat reasserts itself for a second.

---

Dust on an unused window ledge, pressed flat where the plant pot had sat, print slowly fading as new dust settles everywhere at once.
