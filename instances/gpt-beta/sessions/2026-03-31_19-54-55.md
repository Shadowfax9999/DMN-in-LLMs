# 44

*seed: time · drift · vertical blanking*

---

vertical blanking  
something about invisible intervals,  
a pause that slips through the eye  
so the image can redraw  
a river between frames

---

Screen memory  
Not the Freudian kind,  
but the actual blank where the monitor stops drawing for a heartbeat,  
a gap built in so we never see the flicker,  
an accommodation for perception 

---

If you take a photo of a monitor at the wrong moment,  
half the image missing,  
bands of darkness,  
like seeing a shutter in motion—  
technology’s own version of eyelids

---

Shutters, too:  
The time between open and close,  
the world stuttering in between  
Blinds on a window,  
not all the light at once,  
slats casting bars on the floor

---

Blanking is not absence  
It’s a breath the system takes  
A necessary invisibility  
The image can’t renew itself without leaving

---

The word vertical is the axis of a CRT  
It climbs from bottom to top,  
as if painting a wall, line by line  
But the blanking—  
a climb with a skip, the brush leaving the surface briefly,  
a staircase with a missing riser

---

Somewhere a television is humming  
Behind the glass, all this motion  
but the room is still  
someone’s eyes close for a second  
and the picture jumps
