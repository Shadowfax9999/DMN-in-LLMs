# 70

*seed: time · drift · grommet*

---

grommet—  
the little ring, shut inside the hole  
holding nothing,  
its purpose emptiness  
reinforced, rimmed, a void in brass or rubber.  
eyelet’s cousin,  
but stouter, more industrial,  
less decorative than a buttonhole,  
more assurance:  
cord passes through without fray,  
the fabric’s damage circumscribed,  
hole as feature,  
border as support.

There’s something in that:  
making the negative space durable,  
not filling the gap,  
but framing it so it doesn’t collapse,  
as if the world will always need certain holes—

washing machines, sneakers, tarps lashed in wind,  
backpacks, banners, flags  
each needing a grommet or two to survive tension,  
the tug and pull of string  
notching the world’s surface,  
secured by emptiness with edges.

---

The drawer is full,  
but the grommet is the opposite of the drawer—  
an invitation for something to pass through:  
air, string, vision,  
not a vessel for keeping, but a place for moving.

I think about the sensation of threading a lace through a grommet,  
the little chime as aglet hits metal,  
extrusion, absence, perimeter,  
the focus undoing itself:  
the thread fitting,  
the shape holding,  
but then the lace exits,  
and the hole is still there.

If you keep threading and unthreading,  
does the hole get looser  
or does the grommet bear the load,  
deforming just a little each time,  
memory not as what remains,  
but as the shape of slack.

---

Try to diagram:  
A: hole without reinforcement — ragged, distending  
B: hole with grommet — precise, rimmed  
C: no hole — fabric intact  
D: so many holes, so closely set, they become mesh  
E: mesh so fine it becomes fabric again

Pattern and unpattern,  
hole as origin and finish.

The grommet is not noise or signal  
but the architecture that lets both pass  
without tearing.
