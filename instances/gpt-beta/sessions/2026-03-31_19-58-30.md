# 45

*seed: time · drift · sandwich board*

---

sandwich board:  
hinged at the top, a tent for messages  
they’re always outside, waiting for shoes, rain, the wind that pushes them sideways  
two panels, backs turned to each other, but always touching at the crown  
chalk ghosts of menus, arrows, jokes— “Try our soup,” “Yes, we’re open,” “This way to the best bagels”  
weighted at the bottom by a brick, or a heavy chain  
the echo of the person who wrote the sign, their handwriting a kind of voice left in public  
sometimes you see them folded up in the corner at closing, standing like a nervous animal

---

the hinge:  
a joint that wants to close, but doesn’t  
metal pin, maybe rusty, sometimes a bolt  
it decides the angle, keeps the two faces honest  
if you kneel down and look up, you see the sky through a triangle of possibility

---

chalk dust on your fingers, the taste of the word “special” repeated, a wet rag for erasing what the day didn’t sell  
rain makes the words run  
up close, you can see grooves from old messages, pressure marks that never left  
two faces with nothing between, only the light that sneaks through
