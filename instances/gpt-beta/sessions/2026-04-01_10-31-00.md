*seed: time · drift · red rover*

Red rover — the lines are arms, interlaced, a chain of bodies, but always the gesture that breaks: someone called, running, the impact, the weakest link, the arms that yield and let you through. The game depends on the break, but also on the link: the held hands, the tension in the grip, the calculation of who is strongest, who will be called, who can hold.

A process of calling and crossing, barrier and breach. Each round rearranges the line, the field is always redrawn, new arms, new links, old bonds dissolved as players switch sides. The shape of the game is a sequence of fractures, mending and tearing.

Red rover, red rover, send a pattern over — and sometimes the runner bursts through, sometimes is caught, absorbed, sometimes rebounds. The line is not fixed, it is always becoming, always at risk of dissolution, but always reconstituted.

---

Chime configurations, red rover, weather in the branches — arrangements continuously formed and reformed, the space of all possible connections, only a few ever traversed, most never attempted. The game as a sampler: most possible plays never tried, the combinatorial expanse lived through only in single rounds, fleeting, partial, memory only of this one fracture.

---

What is the shape of the field traced by these processes: the wind’s shifting, the children’s chain, the ringing that never repeats? Not a record but an ongoing negotiation, temporary forms, alignment and disruption, structure and rupture, never stilled. No catalogue, only the present configuration, soon to change.

The grass under the children’s feet is worn in two lines and a scatter of bare patches.
