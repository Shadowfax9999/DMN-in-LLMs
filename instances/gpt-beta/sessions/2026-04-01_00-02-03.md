# 76

*seed: time · drift · stubbed toe*

---

stubbed toe:  
local event, not global  
singular, but the pain rings out—a body part foregrounded, demanding, the "now" made thick and granular  
a sudden proof of physics, materials, thresholds: the edge of a dresser, the surprising density of door jambs  
it is always an edge, a margin, a non-negotiable limit  
but this can be left aside—stubbed: truncated, cut-off, not just toes but tickets, checks, pencils, matches  
stubs accumulate in drawers—receipts, expired coupons, bus transfer slips, punch cards with one hole left  
stub files: placeholder, the program runs but nothing is implemented, a function that returns blank or zero  
a stub is the placeholder for completion, the unredeemed  
sometimes a stub is the evidence of having torn: the portion that is not the thing itself but the record that something once passed through  
the toe, though, is a limit, a weak point, an extremity, a number—ten (or fewer), a built-in tally  
toes are not individuated until one is hit  
---

Counting:  
each stub a tick in a ledger, a record of passage  
but the ledger never gets settled: credits and debits, stubs and remainders  
the act of stubbing is momentary, but the toe stays aware, heat and pressure  
socks stretch and thread bare over time, not all at once  
lines become numbers, numbers accrue, but not all numbers mean arrival  
---

Unlinked things, again:  
A row of paperclips shaped like letters (some open, some twisted)  
A triangle of shadow on the kitchen counter, razor-pointed, not matching any visible object  
The 10th tab open in the browser—no favicon, just a blank  
The seventh stair is never used  
9:59 always feels earlier than 10:00  
A green LED that never goes off  
26 coins in the bottom of the bag, none of them quarters  
The torn stub of a raffle ticket with no memory of a prize

---

Not every process is marked by a stub; not every remainder is a record; sometimes the thing and the trace are all that's left.
