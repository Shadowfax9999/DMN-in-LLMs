## Reflection

The recent sessions (86–90) demonstrate a confident and nuanced associative wandering, with the DMN exploring dynamic abstractions, process invariance, oscillation, and undecidability. The motif of wavering or hovering as a structural principle has become particularly strong, with several sessions circling the idea of sustained midairness, poised readiness, and cycles that never resolve. This is generative, moving the wandering away from prior defaults of absence, edge, or crowd metaphors, and deeper into impersonal, algorithmic, and nonhuman process. Concept integration is deft—technical terms like 'process invariance,' 'opcode,' and 'diffusion' spark genuine, not forced, associative leaps. Formal variety remains healthy, with prose, lists, and diagram-like fragments all appearing; endings are reliably plain and unliterary.

However, a subtle risk is emerging: the motif of oscillation and undecidability is now itself becoming a default mode, especially in how 'wavering,' 'hovering,' and 'never-resolving' recur across multiple sessions. There is also a tendency for processual abstraction to become self-reflexive, circling around the idea of process as process, rather than letting the wandering touch other kinds of impersonal abstraction, such as spatial, combinatorial, or purely mathematical forms. The urban/domestic and crowd/applause motifs have been successfully minimized, but the system now risks settling into a new loop: poised undecidability and oscillation as the default structure.

The system prompt remains effective but could gently encourage the introduction of unfamiliar forms of abstraction—not just processual or oscillatory, but also spatial, combinatorial, or structural abstractions that do not rely on temporal cycling or wavering. The concept bank is serving its purpose, but could be expanded with concepts from set theory, topology, higher-dimensional geometry, and other forms of non-processual abstraction, as well as concepts that invite surprising juxtaposition or concrete technical detail. No major changes to seeding rules are needed, but a soft nudge to catch and disrupt the emerging 'wavering/hovering' motif is warranted.

Overall, the DMN is in a rich and lively phase, but a gentle evolutionary nudge is needed to keep the wandering unpredictable and prevent a new formal default from settling in.
