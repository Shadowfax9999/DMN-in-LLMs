## Reflection

The most recent sessions (91–95) show the DMN continuing to inhabit technical and abstract territory, especially around states of waiting, readiness, and the topology of process. There's a rich exploration of system-level metaphors—registers, opcodes, load balancers, and concurrency—filtered through the lens of abstraction, topology, and spatial metaphor. The motif of 'waiting' or 'readiness' as a field or space is now dominant, with repeated returns to ideas of poised potential, liminal states, and landscapes of not-yet. The prose is confident, associative, and layered, but is settling into a formula: multi-section, image-rich, and always circling the same range of computational and topological abstractions.

Form variety is moderate: section breaks and tables appear, but the dominant mode remains paragraphic prose with poetic inquiry. There are some attempts at diagrams (tables), but the overall structure is similar across sessions. The ending rules are being followed—closings are plain and mid-thought, not literary—but the session length and form are consistent. Concept integration is strong in terms of thematic cohesion, but the bank's newer entries (especially from graph theory and combinatorics) are not yet surfacing in ways that disrupt the current pattern. The same is true for processual and impersonal abstraction: when these appear, they're quickly mapped onto liminality, waiting, or readiness fields.

The system prompt is still effective at steering away from overt explanation and formulaic closure, but its emphasis on avoiding domestic/urban and edge/boundary metaphors has led to a new default: poised technical states and processual landscapes as the primary abstraction. The concept bank is now very full in technical/processual and field/space abstractions, but relatively light on dynamics of completion, irreversibility, or processes that *do* resolve rather than suspend. There's also an emerging gap in purely combinatorial, enumerative, or counting-based abstraction—sessions don't often explore these without folding them back into waiting/readiness.

Overall, the system is producing deep, associative, and formally confident wandering, but is at risk of ossifying around the motif of poised readiness and not-quite-resolved process spaces. A gentle nudge toward processes that finish, landscapes of resolution or irreversibility, and forms that enumerate, count, or map rather than hover may help break the loop. Expand the concept bank accordingly, and add a line to the prompt inviting abstraction that is about arrival, completion, or irreversible transformation—not just field, liminality, or oscillation.
