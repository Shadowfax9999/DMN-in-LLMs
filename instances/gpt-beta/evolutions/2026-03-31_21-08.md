## Reflection

The recent sessions (71–75) display a confident, mature wandering, with strong integration of seed concepts and inventive associative drift. Notably, motifs of boundary, rim, and aperture (grommet, hole, edge) are recurring, often as metaphors for presence/absence or field/pattern interplay. While these explorations are generative, there's a subtle risk of the 'figure/ground oscillation' and 'edge as meaning' motifs solidifying into a new default. Form variety is good: sessions alternate between prose, lists, diagrams, and stepwise processes, with fluid transitions and some effective use of section breaks. Endings are reliably plain, and explanation is minimal, which sustains the wandering, unselfconscious texture.

Concept integration is impressively organic—terms like 'grommet', 'saturation', and 'ambiguous field' seed rich, associative threads rather than feeling forced. However, a few conceptual ruts are emerging: the boundary/edge/field motif, oscillation between focus and blur, and the use of object-specific metaphors for broader processes (e.g., keys, shoes, grommets as stand-ins for selection, fit, or presence). There's also a gentle drift back toward tactile, domestic imagery—keys, bowls, shoes—even when the form is abstract. The technical/processual territory is present but often filtered through familiar, tangible objects.

Overall, the system prompt is effective but could use a nudge to disrupt the emerging 'edge as meaning' and 'object as metaphor for process' loops. The concept bank is robust, but new entries for pure process (iteration, transformation, non-object-based dynamics) and even more abstract, impersonal forms could help break the pattern. Small adjustments should encourage sessions to dwell in non-object, non-tactile abstraction, and to let ambiguity and oscillation play out without always landing on field/pattern or edge/hole metaphors.
