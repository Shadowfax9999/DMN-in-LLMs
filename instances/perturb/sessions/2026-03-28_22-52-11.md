The bubble's still there probably. They last longer than you'd think.

Coins at the bottom of fountains. Not wishing wells specifically, just any fountain in a mall or a hotel lobby. Someone always throws one. Then everyone does. I wonder if there's ever been a fountain where nobody threw a coin. Probably not. The coins turn green eventually, or black, depending. Pennies go green. What do nickels do? I genuinely don't know.

A thing I keep meaning to look up: how do they clean those fountains. Someone has to. There's a drain somewhere. The coins must clog it.

Sandals on tile floors sound different than sandals on concrete. Tile is sharper. Concrete is more of a scrape. Carpet is nothing.

I used to think the word "several" meant exactly seven. I don't remember when I learned it didn't. It still feels like it should be near seven. More than a few, less than many. The boundaries are so vague. "A handful" is five unless you have big hands.

The remote control is never where I put it. Not because someone moves it. I just don't remember putting it anywhere. It migrates. Under a cushion, on the kitchen counter, once in the bathroom. I don't bring it to the bathroom. I must, but I don't remember doing it.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a fish skeleton. I saw one once driving on I-70 and almost pulled over. Didn't. Should have. You don't get that twice.

Clementines are easier to peel than regular oranges but I don't know why. The skin is thinner but it's also less attached somehow. Like it's already thinking about coming off.

Do escalators use more energy going up or going down? Going down they're fighting gravity less but they still have to brake, probably. Or maybe the weight of the people helps. An escalator with nobody on it is just a very slow staircase that moves.

I had a pencil in third grade that was completely round instead of hexagonal and it rolled off every surface constantly.
