# 16

*seed: time · drift · how a seed knows which way is up when it germinates*

---

Monday I sat at a piano bench that was too high and every key felt like a ledge. Tuesday the roots go down and the shoot goes up regardless of how you orient the seed, which means gravity is being sensed at a cellular level, by little starch granules settling to the bottom of special cells like sand in an hourglass. Wednesday I realized I don't know what my own front door sounds like when someone else opens it. Thursday seems like the day most people quit things. Friday the starch granules are called statoliths, and I keep thinking about how even a seed in total darkness, buried wrong, knows which way is down because something inside it is falling. Saturday evening and the pencil thing is still true — I just tried to sketch a cup and suddenly the pencil weighs something. Sunday I planted basil seeds once and half of them came up in a curved shape, like they'd started wrong and corrected.
