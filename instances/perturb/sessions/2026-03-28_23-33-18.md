The thing about pressure being 101,325 pascals — that means if you could somehow remove one pascal of pressure, one single newton over one square meter, nobody would notice. You wouldn't feel it. Your ears wouldn't pop. Nothing would change. But it did change. You just can't detect it. There's probably a threshold somewhere, some number of pascals you'd need to remove before a human body registers anything. I don't know what that number is.

Guitars tuned by harmonics. You touch the string lightly at the twelfth fret and pluck it and you get this bell-like tone an octave up. The fifth fret harmonic of one string should match the seventh fret harmonic of the next string. I think. I've watched people do it and their heads tilt like they're listening for something between the notes — a wobble, a beating frequency that slows down as the strings get closer to matching.

The word "beating" for that pulsing interference pattern between two almost-matched frequencies. Like a heart is in there somewhere.
