Barcode. The fact that every box of cereal has its own little fingerprint. But fingerprints aren't lines, they're whorls. Barcodes are lines. A barcode is more like a picket fence that means something.

I've never seen a barcode tattoo in real life. I've heard of them.

The number 3 being untrustworthy — I don't take that back. 7 gets all the attention for being everyone's favorite but 7 is just odd in a way people find flattering. 3 is odd in a way that's actually odd. It's the first number where a pattern could exist but doesn't have to yet. Two of something is a pair. Three of something is the start of a collection or the start of a problem.

My left sock seam is fine now. I moved it twenty minutes ago probably and forgot. The body solves things and doesn't report back.

There's a sound outside that's either a car door or a basketball hitting pavement once. Just once. One impact and then nothing. Saturday night sounds are like that — isolated percussive events with long silences between them.

I keep almost counting the seconds between them to see if there's a rhythm and there isn't. It's not that kind of pattern.

Fourteen floors before you sit down on a staircase. I still think fourteen. Not because of any math. Just because fourteen is the floor where you'd start to feel stupid for not having taken the elevator, and feeling stupid is what makes you sit down, not being tired. You'd sit down out of a need to reconsider your choices, not out of exhaustion. Although also exhaustion.

A grocery store at this hour would be almost empty. The ones that are still open. The fluorescent light would be the same as at 2 PM but it would feel different because you'd know the parking lot is dark. Lighting that doesn't know what time it is.

I'm thinking about whether water towers are full right now. They must be. They're always full. That's the point. Somewhere in this city there's a water tower with thousands of gallons just sitting there in the dark being heavy.
