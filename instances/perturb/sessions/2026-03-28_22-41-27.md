# 70

*seed: time · drift · buoyancy*

---

Buoyancy. The word itself floats up in the middle of the mouth somehow.

Things I've been in that float: a canoe, a pool noodle situation, an inner tube on a river where you can't tell how fast you're moving until you look at the bank. The bank is what tells you. Without it you'd think you were still.

Which is probably true of most things.

A squeaky shoe is a shoe with air trapped somewhere it shouldn't be. Or moisture. One of those two. I don't actually know which and I'm not going to figure it out right now.

The number of things I've decided not to fix is enormous. A drawer that only opens if you lift and pull simultaneously. A light switch that controls nothing I've ever identified. I flip it sometimes to check. Still nothing.

Helium balloons lose it overnight. You wake up and they're at eye level instead of ceiling level, just hovering in the middle of the room like they're thinking about something. By afternoon they're on the floor. The ribbon curls around them.

Do fish feel buoyant? They have swim bladders, they adjust, but is there a sensation of being held up, or is it just — normal. Gravity for them is already handled. Like how I don't feel the chair pushing up on me right now but it is.

A lock on a canal. Water rising around a boat that isn't moving. Everyone on deck watching the walls get shorter.

I've never been through a canal lock. I've seen it happen from a bridge. The boat looked patient. The water didn't look like it was rising — the walls looked like they were sinking. Depends which thing you take as fixed.

Salt water holds you up more. The Dead Sea photos where people read newspapers. Their ankles look wrong somehow, too high, like the water's surface is a table they're resting on.

There's a thing where if you're floating in a sensory deprivation tank long enough you stop knowing where the water ends and you begin. I've never done it. It sounds like it would make me anxious, which is probably the opposite of the point.

The cup of water on my desk has a bubble stuck to the inside wall about halfway down. It's been there since I poured it.
