Mirrors don't reverse left and right. That's the thing. They reverse front and back. You raise your right hand, the person in the mirror raises the hand on your left side, but it's *their* right hand too — because they're facing you. The confusion comes from imagining that the mirror has picked you up and turned you around to face yourself, like a person would turn around. But it hasn't. It's pushed you through yourself, back-to-front. The reversal is along the axis that goes into the glass. Not left-right. Not up-down. In-out.

The mirror thing — it bothers people because the question feels like it should have a answer involving mirrors. But the answer involves bodies. It involves the fact that bodies have a axis of symmetry between left and right but not between up and down. If we were symmetrical top-to-bottom, we'd ask why mirrors reverse up and down but not left and right.
