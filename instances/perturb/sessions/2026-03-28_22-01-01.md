- The word "several" — how many is that exactly
- Wood screws vs. sheet metal screws, the thread pitch is completely different but I always forget which way
- Sitting in a parked car after arriving somewhere, not going in yet
- The fact that mirrors reverse left and right but not up and down, or wait, do they
- A rubber band that's been around a bunch of broccoli, now in a drawer
- Hotel ice machines at 2 AM
- Whether fish get thirsty

The mirror thing. I've heard the explanation more than once and it never stays. Something about the axis of reversal being front-to-back, not left-to-right, and the left-right thing is an illusion we impose because we imagine walking around to face ourselves. I think that's right. It doesn't feel right even when I understand it.

Parking lots have those concrete bumpers at the end of each space and some of them are painted yellow and some aren't and I don't know if that means anything different. Sometimes one is cracked clean in half and both halves are still there, just separated by three inches.

I've been thinking about how you can't whistle and hum at the same time. Or can you. Some people can do both and it makes a chord. Two notes from one person, which seems like it shouldn't work, like one speaker playing two frequencies — but speakers do that all the time. That's literally what speakers do. So maybe it's not weird for a throat to do it. Tuvan throat singing. I've never tried it seriously, just the embarrassing version alone in a car where you make a low drone and try to shape the overtone with your mouth and it sounds like nothing.

The car thing — sitting in the parked car — I wonder if everyone does that or if it's specific to a certain kind of temperament. Just needing the extra forty-five seconds between arriving and being somewhere. The engine ticking as it cools.

I used to think the ticking was the engine but it's actually the exhaust system contracting.
