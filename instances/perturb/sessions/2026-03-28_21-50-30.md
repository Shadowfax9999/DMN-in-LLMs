# 49

*seed: time · drift*

---

Bags of fifty. Everything hardware comes in quantities that assume you'll lose most of them. Wall anchors, wire nuts, those little brass tacks.

The word "tack" doing so many jobs. Thumbtack, tack cloth, sailing tack, tacking a hem. A carpet tack is different from an upholstery tack. I don't know which one came first.

Crushed limestone gets into the treads of your shoes and you track it across the kitchen floor and it leaves white scratches on linoleum. Not scratches exactly. More like marks that wipe off but not entirely. A ghost of grit.

I wonder if anyone's ever mapped all the factories that make things nobody thinks about buying. Cabinet bumpers, aglets, the plastic tips on the ends of windshield wipers, those little silica gel packets. Each one has a building somewhere with a parking lot and a break room.

Playing cards have rounded corners so they don't dog-ear in the deck. That's a choice someone made once and now every card manufacturer follows it. The radius is probably standardized. You never see a sharp-cornered playing card.

A kite in no wind is just a decorated stick. Two sticks. The crossbar is usually bowed, which is what gives the face its tension. You're building a tiny wing, really. But nobody thinks of it that way because it's a toy.

Saturday night has a specific quality of quiet that's different from Tuesday night quiet. More voluntary. Like the neighborhood chose it.

My hands smell like the copper fitting I was holding earlier. That smell doesn't wash off the first time. You have to use dish soap, the kind with the lemon in it, and even then there's a trace if you rub your fingers together and sniff. Copper and sweat make something that smells almost like blood, which makes sense because hemoglobin has iron in it, not copper, so actually it doesn't make sense. Similar but wrong.

The gravel writing lasting until the next car. You could write something and then watch it get erased from your bedroom window. Not all at once — the tires only cover two strips. The rest stays until someone walks through it or it rains.

I think the bumpers are silicone, not rubber.
