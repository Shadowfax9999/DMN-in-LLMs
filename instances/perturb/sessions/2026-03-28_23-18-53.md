Things that only go one way

- A ratchet wrench. You set the direction and it clicks freely one way, catches the other. The click is almost nothing. The catch is everything.
- Toothpaste out of the tube.
- A conversation where someone says "we need to talk."
- Turnstiles. Though I've seen people climb over them backwards, which isn't the same as them going both ways.
- Growing taller.
- A pyrometric cone — it bends in the kiln and stays bent, and the angle tells you the temperature was reached. You can't unbend it. That's the whole point. It's a witness.
- Knowing someone's middle name. You can't unknow it.
- Those spike strips at parking garage exits. SEVERE TIRE DAMAGE. DO NOT BACK UP.
- Eggs.
- The way a conversation at a party suddenly gets serious and there's no mechanism for making it unserious again. Someone just has to leave and come back with a drink.
- Ripping paper.
- Finding out what someone really thinks of you.
- Popcorn.
- A zipper, actually, goes both ways. Scratch that.
- The little plastic tag on a bread bag — no, that goes both ways too, you just twist it back.
- Forgetting a phone number. That one might only go one way. I used to know my childhood dentist's number and now I have the area code and the first two digits and nothing else, and I'm fairly sure the rest is gone for good.
- Letting go of a balloon.
- Cement.iteite powder goes in, you add water, you wait, and now it's a different material. There's no powder anymore.
- Walking into a spiderweb.
- A match.
- Reading the last page of a mystery first.
- Saying "I love you" for the first time to someone. Even if you take it back, the fact that you said it only went one direction.
- Pyrometric cones again — I keep thinking about them. They ship them perfectly straight and you put them in the kiln knowing they'll slump. You're manufacturing a thing whose whole purpose is to be ruined precisely.
- Salt dissolving. Actually you can evaporate the water and get the salt back. Never mind.
- Jumping off a diving board once your feet leave.
