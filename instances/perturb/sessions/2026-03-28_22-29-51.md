*seed: time · drift · jet lag*

Jet lag. The body insisting it's somewhere else. Not homesickness — the opposite, almost. The body hasn't left yet.

I flew to Denver once and couldn't sleep and went outside the hotel at 4 AM and the parking lot was so bright. Every hotel parking lot at 4 AM is the same parking lot. The ice machine was humming through the wall.

Kaleidoscope. That's what the brass thing was. I didn't even think the word, just the object — the tube, the weight of it, the sound of the beads. Sometimes you remember a thing for years without its name attached.

What's the difference between not sleeping because of jet lag and not sleeping because you just can't sleep. Physically I mean. Probably cortisol timing or something. But from inside it feels the same. You're lying there and the ceiling is there.

A receipt. That's what the previous thing was about. Writing on a receipt. People used to write on everything — margins of newspapers, backs of envelopes. Now there's nothing in your pockets to write on.

The word "stationery" — no. Moving on.

Hotel rooms have that painting. Not a specific painting but the category of painting. Muted. Teal and sand. Something that could be a marsh or could be nothing. Chosen specifically to not be anything in particular. The frame is always slightly too wide for the image.

I wonder if anyone has ever stood in front of one of those paintings and actually looked at it. Like really looked. Probably someone has. Probably someone was jet-lagged and couldn't sleep and it was the only thing on the wall.

The ice machine sound carries differently at 4 AM. During the day you wouldn't hear it from the parking lot. But at 4 AM the air is different, or you are.

My left knee does this thing when I've been sitting too long on a plane where the first few steps feel like the joint is running about a half-second behind the rest of the leg.

Denver is a mile up but it doesn't feel like it. You'd think you'd notice a mile of missing air.
