# 80

*seed: time · drift · the way a compass needle finds north by aligning with Earth's magnetic field*

---

Things that point:

- A compass needle, but not at true north. At magnetic north, which is somewhere in the Canadian Arctic and drifting.
- Weathervanes. They point where the wind is coming *from*, not where it's going. Which is the opposite of how you'd design it if you were starting fresh.
- The hour hand at 4 a.m. when you can't sleep. It points at something you said in 2019.
- Sunflowers, before they're fully grown. They track the sun east to west during the day and reset overnight. After they mature they stop tracking and just face east permanently.
- A dog's nose.
- The thing your eye does in a crowd where it goes straight to the one person who's already looking at you.
- Stalactites.
- That gesture where someone uses their chin instead of their hand. Lips pursed slightly, chin tilted. It means "over there." It works in every country I've ever heard of.
- Roots point down and stems point up and if you flip a seedling upside down both will curve to correct themselves within hours.
- The wear pattern on a carpet points toward the door people use most.
- A rip current. You can see it from shore — a darker channel in the surf, pointing straight out.
- The finger you hold up to test wind direction. You're not really feeling which side is cold. You're feeling which side dries faster.
- Every branch on a tree is pointing at a patch of sky where the light was.
- Street signs.
- The way, in an old paperback, the spine cracks open to the page someone kept returning to.
- Iron filings near a magnet. They don't just clump — they arrange into lines, each one a little arrow in a field you can't see. The field was always there. The filings just make it legible.
- A queue points at the thing everyone wants.
- The crease in a folded map points at the place the owner kept refolding to check.
