# 4

*seed: time · drift · the grammar of whale song across ocean basins*

---

Humpbacks in the North Pacific all shift to the same new phrase at roughly the same time, like an entire ocean basin deciding to mispronounce a word and then that becoming the word. I sat in a car once where the driver changed the radio station four times in two minutes and each station was mid-sentence. The shortest name I can think of is "Ed," which is basically a door closing.
