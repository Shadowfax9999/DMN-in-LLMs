*seed: time · drift*

---

The smell of pencils is cedar. But the smell of cedar isn't pencils. It's older than that. It's the inside of a closet in a house that isn't yours.

Three-quarter inch is bigger than you think until you hold one. Then it's exactly the size it is.

I've been in a clearing like that. Not that one. A different one. There was a foundation and I didn't wonder what had been on it. I was maybe eleven. I was looking for something else — a creek, I think, or the creek was just where I ended up. The foundation was just a thing I walked across. Concrete is warm when the sun's been on it and cold when it hasn't and there's about twenty minutes in the evening where it's exactly skin temperature and you can't feel it under your feet at all.

A gate with a chain and no lock. That's not security. That's a sentence. "You can come in but I want you to know that you decided to."

My left ear is doing the thing where it feels full. Not plugged. Full. Like there's a thought in it that hasn't become sound yet. It'll go away or it won't.

Tubular steel has a specific ring when you tap it with a knuckle. Hollow but not empty. The pitch depends on the length and whether both ends are open or one is capped. I don't know which note a gate post would be. Probably nothing clean. Probably somewhere between G and A-flat, the neighborhood where notes go when they're not trying to be musical.

The word "windward" assumes you're on a boat or an island. On a foundation in a clearing you'd just say "into the wind." But windward is better because it has "ward" in it, which means "toward," which means you're already moving in that direction just by facing it.

Four anchor bolts at the corners means the building was small. A shed or a pump house. Something with one purpose that it finished doing.
