*seed: time · drift · the difference between forgetting and never having known*

---

The difference between forgetting and never having known. There's a version of you that knew something and lost it, and a version that never had it. They look the same from the outside. You'd answer the same way on a quiz. But one of them has a hole shaped like the thing.

I wonder if anyone has ever confidently remembered something that never happened and then forgotten it. So you'd have a memory-shaped hole for a thing that was never real. That seems like it should have a name but I don't want to go looking for it.

Packing peanuts. Who decided that shape. Not the S-shape ones, the figure-eight ones that look like little bones. Dog treats look like little bones too. A lot of things look like little bones. The letter S doesn't look like a bone though. The letter S looks like exactly what it is, which is unusual for a letter.

There's a particular feeling of being in a car that's parked but still running. Engine doing its thing underneath you. You're not going anywhere but the car doesn't know that. Or — it's not that the car doesn't know. The car is just doing what a running car does. The going-somewhere part was never the car's idea.

I used to think the word "whatnot" was two words. What not. Like a question about negation. It's actually from the name of a piece of furniture — a whatnot, a shelf for displaying small things that don't have a category. Things that are not what. Things that are not anything in particular. So you put them on the whatnot. I said I wasn't going to do that.

Bubble wrap was originally invented as wallpaper. That didn't work. Most things that become ubiquitous were first a failed version of something else. I don't know if that's actually true as a general claim. It just felt true for a second.

My left sock is thicker than my right sock. Different pairs. I don't know when that happened. At some point today I put on two socks from different pairs and didn't notice and now here I am at the end of the day with mismatched socks. The left foot has known all day.
