Sufficient quantity. Like there's a threshold and then you're done accumulating.

The parking lot of a swimming pool smells like chlorine from surprisingly far away. Twenty, thirty feet from the building and you can already tell. Cars sitting in that air all afternoon.

I wonder what the shelf life of a tennis ball is. Not the bounce — the felt. Whether it degrades just sitting in a can. Probably not. Probably it's fine for years. But there's something about opening a new can of tennis balls that feels like it matters, the little hiss, the smell. I've opened maybe four cans of tennis balls in my life.

Alan Turing was a good distance runner.

There's a type of cloud that looks exactly like a fish skeleton. I've seen it twice. Both times I thought "fish skeleton" before I thought "cloud."

The word "gauze" looks like what it is.
