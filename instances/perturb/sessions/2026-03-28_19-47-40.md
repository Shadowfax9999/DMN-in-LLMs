# 17

*seed: time · drift · ligature*

---

Ligature — the little bridge between two letters in typesetting, the f and the i leaning into each other, sharing a stroke. Also a thing you tie off a blood vessel with. Also in music, the metal clamp that holds a reed to a mouthpiece, which means the thing that lets the vibration happen is itself held in place by something that doesn't vibrate. I played clarinet for two years in middle school and I remember the ligature screws were always slightly too small for my fingers, or my fingers were slightly too large for the screws, and tightening them felt like a task designed for a different species. The reed would slip. You'd overtighten and the sound would go flat and choked. There was a kid named David who could assemble his whole instrument while walking down the hallway and I never understood how. Ligature also just means tying, binding, from the Latin, but I said I wouldn't do that. The thing about the clarinet ligature is that it's the most boring-looking part of the instrument and if you get it wrong nothing else matters. I wonder if that's true of a lot of systems — that the least interesting component is the most consequential. No, that's too neat. Sometimes the boring part is just boring and also unimportant. The little rubber feet on the bottom of a toaster. Although actually those keep it from sliding off the counter so maybe that's a bad example. I had a toaster once where one of the feet fell off and it sat slightly tilted for three years and I never fixed it. The toast still came out fine. It just sat there, crooked, like a dog with one ear up. Saturday evening and the light is doing the thing where it comes in low and turns everything on the counter into a still life, the salt shaker throwing a shadow twice its height. I don't know why long shadows feel more dramatic than short ones, they just do. The basil I planted that time — I think it was in a windowsill pot and I overwatered it immediately because that's what I do with plants, I care for them too aggressively, like attention is the same as conditions. The ligature on a clarinet is usually nickel-plated brass.
