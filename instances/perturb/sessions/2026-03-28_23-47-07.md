# 92

*seed: time · drift · bioluminescence in deep ocean trenches*

---

1. The clear-on-clear problem is also a deep ocean problem. Below about 200 meters there's nothing to see by except what makes its own light.

2. Anglerfish look fake. Like something a committee designed to be scary for a children's movie and then someone found one.

3. Most bioluminescence is blue. Not because blue is special but because seawater lets blue travel farthest. The light adapted to the medium.

4. A torpedo level is just a small level. I didn't know that either.

5. The space between two notes has a name in music but I can't remember it. Interval. That's not what I was reaching for though.

6. There are creatures at hydrothermal vents that have never experienced a photon that didn't come from chemistry.

7. I can hear the neighbor's wind chimes through the window right now. Five notes, always the same five, never the same order.

8. The backs of playing cards, the edge of packing tape, the symmetry of a bicycle wheel — things designed so you can't tell which way they're oriented.

9. Some deep-sea shrimp have eyes that detect the thermal glow of volcanic vents. Not bioluminescence, just hot rock barely radiating into the visible spectrum. They're seeing temperature.

10. The washing machine has stopped. I can tell because the floor feels different under my feet.

11. Salt flats are white and flat and when they're wet they reflect the sky perfectly. I've only seen photos.

12. Counterillumination: some squid light up their bellies to match the light coming from above so they cast no shadow. Camouflage by becoming a light source.

13. My thumbnail still has a dent from trying to find the tape edge earlier.

14. Five notes from the wind chimes again. Different order.
