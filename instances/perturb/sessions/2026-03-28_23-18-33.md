*seed: time · drift · potluck*

Potluck. The word just sitting there.

I went to one once where someone brought a bowl of plain rice. Not seasoned, not styled. Just rice. And it was the first thing gone. Everyone had brought something elaborate and the rice was what people actually wanted next to all of it.

The ceiling fan in that apartment had a wobble. Not dangerous. Just enough that you'd notice it if you looked up and then forget about it completely until the next time you looked up. Months could pass.

I wonder how many ceiling fans are wobbling right now in apartments where nobody's home.

There's a particular kind of quiet in a room where a clock is ticking and you stop hearing it and then someone says "do you hear that ticking" and now you can't unhear it. Attention as a door that only opens one direction.

Potluck, though. Pot. Luck. What luck is in the pot. Which is actually a pretty good description of the experience — you don't know what you're going to get, and the whole thing works anyway, almost always. I've never been to a potluck that failed. There's some kind of self-correcting mechanism where people just bring different things. Not always. Sometimes there are four pasta salads. But even four pasta salads is fine because people eat pasta salad.

The wind is doing something tonight. Not steady. Gusts with long pauses between them, so each one feels like it might be the last one and then there's another.

I used to think "gust" was related to "gusto" but I have no idea if that's true and I'm not going to look it up.

Alan Turing kept his mug chained to a radiator so nobody would take it. That's a fact I know and I don't remember where I learned it. It floats around without a source. A lot of facts are like that — I have the fact but not the receipt.

Four pasta salads at a potluck and everybody eats them all anyway.
