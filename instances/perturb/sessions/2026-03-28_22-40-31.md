The friction material question is one I could look up but I don't want to. Some questions are better as questions.

Cashews come from a fruit. I knew this once and then forgot it and then someone told me again and I was surprised again. The cashew hangs off the bottom of this pear-shaped thing called a cashew apple. The apple is the afterthought. Or the cashew is the afterthought. Depends on where you're standing.

I wonder how many things I've learned exactly twice.

A parking garage at night has a particular sound — your footsteps and then the version of your footsteps that arrives a half-second later from the concrete wall. You speed up slightly without meaning to. Not because you're scared, or not only because you're scared, but because the echo makes you aware of your own pace and once you're aware of it you can't maintain it. Like how you can't walk naturally once someone says "walk naturally."

There's a word for the little plastic or metal piece at the end of a shoelace. Aglet. Everyone knows this now because of some TV show or internet thing. It used to be a piece of trivia that impressed people and now it's a piece of trivia that makes them say "yeah, aglet, everyone knows that." The fact didn't change. Its social function flipped entirely.

I've been thinking about how a screen door sounds different depending on whether you're inside or outside when it closes. From inside it's a slap. From outside it's more of a rattle. Same event, two different sounds, and neither one is wrong. I guess the reflective surfaces are different on each side. The porch absorbs differently than the hallway.

My left shoe has a squeak that only happens on smooth floors. Tile, polished concrete. Disappears completely on carpet or asphalt. I keep thinking it'll go away and it doesn't. Six months now. It's not the sole — I think it's where the insole meets the footbed, some kind of friction between two smooth surfaces that only loads right on a hard floor. I should put talcum powder in there.
