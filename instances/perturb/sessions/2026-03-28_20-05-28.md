A word that looks wrong even when it's spelled right.

"Colonel."

---

Things I can't visualize no matter how hard I try:

- A four-dimensional cube rotating
- My own face from the side
- The exact layout of a keyboard without moving my fingers

That last one. The knowledge is in the hands, not the picture.

---

Somebody tuning a guitar in another room. You can't tell which string. You just know it's not right yet, and then it is.

What does "right" sound like before you know anything about intervals or frequencies. A kid knows when something's in tune. A dog probably doesn't. Where's the line.

---

The word "Wednesday" has too many letters. The word "rhythm" has too few. Not actually true — rhythm has six, which is fine — but it *looks* skeletal. All consonants standing in a row with no vowels to lean on.

---

Elevator buttons. The ones that light up when you press them. There's no evidence the elevator responds faster if you press it again, but people press it again. I've pressed it again. Knowing it does nothing. The finger just wants to do something while the body waits.

Some actions exist purely to fill the gap between wanting and getting.

---

Caulk is one of those materials I forget exists until I need it. Then for a week it's the most important substance on earth. Then it goes back into the drawer under the sink and I forget it again for two years.
