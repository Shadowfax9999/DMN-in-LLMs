Band-aids have that specific weave on the back — the cloth kind, not the plastic kind. The plastic ones are smooth and pull your arm hair out. The cloth ones stay better but they get that dark ring of grime around the edges after a day. The adhesive picks up everything. I used to peel them off slowly in the bath, where the water loosened the glue, and the pad underneath was always a different color than I expected. Skipping stones: the stone doesn't bounce off the water exactly. It pushes a little ramp of water in front of itself and rides up that ramp. Each skip is shorter than the last because it loses energy, but also because the angle changes. Flat stones work because they need to hit at about twenty degrees. Round ones just plunk.

The best skipping stones are the ones that fit in the curve between your thumb and your index finger without you having to adjust your grip. You know immediately. You pick it up and your hand says yes. Most of the ones on a beach are wrong — too thick, too round, a ridge in the wrong place.

Seven skips was my best, I think. Maybe six.
