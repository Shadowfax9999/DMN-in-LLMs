Salt flat. The word "flat" doing so much work there. A salt flat isn't flat the way a table is flat. It's flat the way something is flat when there's nothing left to take away from it.

I've never been to one. I've seen photographs where the sky and the ground mirror each other and people look like they're standing on nothing.

Mirage. The heat shimmer that makes the road look wet ahead of you. I remember being in a back seat and asking why there was water on the road and being told there wasn't. That's a strange thing to learn as a kid — that seeing something doesn't mean it's there.

Rental cars smell different from owned cars. Not better or worse. Just neutral in a way that feels slightly wrong, like a room with no books in it.

The seatbelt buckle gets hot enough to burn you if the car's been sitting in the sun. You learn to check with the back of your hand first. The back of the hand is where you test temperature — bathwater, irons, seatbelt buckles. I don't know why the back and not the palm.

Palm. Palm tree. Palm reading. Three completely unrelated things sharing a word.

I wonder how many words I use in a day. Not unique words — total words. There must be studies. Someone's counted. The number is probably between ten thousand and twenty-five thousand and I have no real basis for that guess.

Guessing the number of jellybeans in a jar. The trick is supposed to be estimating volume — how many layers, how many per layer — but nobody actually does that. You just look at the jar and a number appears in your head. The number is almost always wrong but it feels right, which is the whole problem with intuition.

Jars with metal lids that pop when you open them. The pop means the seal was good.
