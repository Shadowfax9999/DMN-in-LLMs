Forfeit.

That's the word. Not the game-show kind. The older one — something you lose because you didn't claim it in time. A forfeit used to be a fine. A penalty. Something taken from you.

---

The quarter I stepped over this morning was face-down. Tails. I noticed that I noticed.

---

Things that come in pairs from the same source, like mace and nutmeg:

- Cork and cork oak acorns
- Cream and buttermilk
- Coke and slag

Actually slag comes from smelting, not from the same plant or animal. That's not really the same. But it felt like it belonged on the list.

- Lanolin and wool
- Whey and curds

Whey is the part nobody wanted for centuries and now people put it in protein shakes. Curds are cheese. Same bucket of milk, one becomes cheddar and the other becomes a gym supplement.

---

I keep thinking about marbled. The inside of nutmeg is marbled. Marbled paper — you float ink on a tray of thick water and drag a comb through it and lay the paper down. The paper on the inside covers of old books. That swirl pattern. It's called Turkish marbling, or ebru. Each sheet is unique because the ink floats and you can't control it precisely. But they all look like each other.

Mass-produced uniqueness. Like fingerprints.

---

Saturday night and I'm thinking about cloves in oranges. 

The grater for nutmeg is usually made of stainless steel with tiny punched holes. The holes have a burr on one side — that's the cutting edge. Same principle as a cheese grater, just smaller. Some of them are shaped like little cylinders with a compartment to store the whole nutmeg inside when you're not using it.

I had one of those. Silver-colored, about four inches long.
