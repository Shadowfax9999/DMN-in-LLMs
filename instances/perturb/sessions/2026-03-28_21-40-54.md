The sound an air conditioner makes from outside is completely different from the sound it makes from inside. From inside it's a hum you stop hearing. From outside it's a rattle and a fan and water dripping somewhere.

Dripping. There's a faucet in every building I've ever been in that drips. Someone knows about it. Someone has decided it's not worth fixing yet.

What does Saturn look like from Titan. You'd barely see it. The atmosphere is so thick — orange haze, nitrogen and methane — the sky is just a murky glow. You wouldn't know you were orbiting anything. You could live your whole life on Titan and not know Saturn was there unless someone told you. That's strange to think about. A whole world that doesn't know what it's orbiting.

1. The way a rubber band sounds different depending on how far you stretch it
2. Cinnamon is bark
3. The number of people who have been alone in a room in this building before me
4. Whether fish notice currents the way we notice wind or the way we don't notice air pressure
5. The phrase "by and large" — I have no idea what that actually refers to
6. Pencils used to make me anxious. The sound on paper. I don't know when that stopped
7. Most ceilings are eight feet high and I've never once thought about that while standing in a room

Fish and currents though. A trout holding position in a stream, adjusting constantly, the way you lean into a turn on a bike without thinking about it. Proprioception but for water. They have a lateral line — a row of sensors down each side — but what it feels like from the inside, whether it's more like touch or hearing or something with no analogy.

I bit the inside of my cheek earlier and now my tongue keeps going back to it.

The ceiling height thing. Eight feet. That's not based on anything structural. Lumber comes in eight-foot lengths. The standard is downstream of the material. I wonder how many things are like that — where the shape of life follows from something industrial that nobody chose for human reasons.

Keys. Why do I always put them in my left pocket. I'm right-handed. You'd think I'd want them accessible to my dominant hand but apparently at some point I decided otherwise and now it's just where keys go.
