Gel. Gelatin. Gelato. All from the same root, freezing, but gelatin jiggles and gelato is smooth and gel is neither. Three textures from one word for cold.

I wonder if there's a language where the word for "cold" and the word for "stiff" are completely unrelated. They probably feel related in most languages because cold things *are* stiff. Your fingers get stiff. The garden hose gets stiff. But that's not a linguistic universal, that's just weather.
