The salt thing is funny because you think you have an example and then you think about it for two more seconds and you don't.

What else is like that. Eggs. Eggs are the real one. Although — no. Century eggs. Fermented eggs. You're not unmaking the egg, but you're not just cooking it either. You're doing something else to it entirely. I don't know what the chemistry is. I think it's alkaline.

Alkaline. That's a word I use without really picturing what it means in practice. I know it's the opposite end from acid. I know baking soda is alkaline. I know if you put too much baking soda in banana bread it tastes like soap, or like metal, or like something that isn't food anymore. I've done that.

Banana bread is the thing people make when they feel like they should be making something. It's not that anyone wants banana bread specifically. It's that the bananas are sitting there turning black and throwing them away feels wasteful and baking feels productive so you combine those two pressures and get banana bread. I wonder how much banana bread exists because of guilt about bananas versus actual desire for banana bread.

There's a ceiling fan somewhere making a ticking sound. Not in this room. One room over, or above. You can't always tell with fans. The sound travels through the structure of the building differently than through the air, so sometimes a fan on the floor above you sounds like it's beside you.

I had a friend in college whose apartment had a thermostat that controlled nothing. It was on the wall. You could turn the dial. Nothing happened. He'd still turn it sometimes. He said it made him feel like he'd done something. The landlord never removed it because removing it would mean patching the wall, and patching the wall costs more than leaving a fake thermostat. So the fake thermostat stays.

A lot of things stay because removing them is more expensive than ignoring them.

I think about that with buttons on crosswalks. Some of them are connected and some of them aren't and you can't tell which is which. Everyone pushes them anyway. The ones that aren't connected are just thermostats on the wall.
