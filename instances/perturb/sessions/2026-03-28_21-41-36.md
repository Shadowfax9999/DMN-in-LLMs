A calendar still showing last month means nobody looks at it anymore but nobody takes it down either.

March is still up somewhere right now and it's the 28th so there's almost a reason. Two days of legitimacy left. But the ones I'm thinking about are the ones where it's been wrong for weeks and the wrongness just sits there, not bothering anyone. A calendar three months behind is furniture.

No. I was going to make something of that and I don't need to.

Things that are eight feet tall because the board is eight feet long:

- ceilings
- privacy fences
- the side of a shed
- a bookcase that goes floor to ceiling in a room with eight-foot ceilings, which feels like it was meant to, but it's coincidence all the way down

A 4x8 sheet of plywood weighs about 60 pounds if it's 3/4 inch. Carrying one by yourself you hold it on edge and kind of waddle. Wind catches it. I've seen someone lose one off a truck on the highway, it flew like a playing card for a second before it didn't.

Tapping the barometer. There's a word for that kind of thing where the system has the information but needs a nudge to display it. I don't know what the word is. Maybe there isn't one. Dithering is close but dithering is adding noise to help a system resolve, which is almost the opposite — you're adding randomness, not a directed tap. Though maybe the tap is just a very local, very brief randomness. You shake the mechanism loose from wherever friction has it stuck and it falls to where gravity and the pressure want it.

I keep thinking about the plywood on the highway. How it caught air and lifted. Probably a full second of flight. Plywood doesn't look like something that could fly but it has the aspect ratio for it. Low aspect ratio, like a manta ray. Not like a manta ray. Like a door. A door in the wind would also fly if it weren't hinged.

Saturday night and I can hear something that might be a basketball on pavement, that specific hollow double-bounce sound. Or it could be someone closing a car trunk repeatedly, which doesn't make sense but sounds almost the same from far enough away.

The calendar. April starts Wednesday.
