Train horn at a distance — you can never point exactly to where it's coming from, it just exists in a direction, somewhere south or southwest, and the two notes sit in an interval I should know but don't. Freight trains at this hour. The graffiti on boxcars passes through towns at whatever speed and nobody asked to see it and nobody chose to display it, it's just there for a few seconds, someone's name in bubble letters riding from Oakland to who knows where. I played clarinet for one year in fifth grade and what I remember most is the taste of the reed after soaking it in my mouth, woody and faintly sweet, and the specific embarrassment of squeaking during a rest when everyone else was quiet. The ligature screws — if you overtighten them the reed can't vibrate and the whole thing goes flat and dead. I think about overtightening a lot actually, not clarinet ligatures specifically, but the general problem of holding something too firmly for it to do what it's supposed to do. My left knee is doing the thing. Saturday and I haven't eaten, which I'm aware of the way you're aware of a lamp that's on in a room you're not in. There's rice in the cabinet, there's always rice in the cabinet, the bag getting imperceptibly lighter each time until one day you pick it up and it weighs nothing. Shadow of the salt shaker on the counter looks like a chess piece, something about how the proportions stretch makes familiar things into other things. I had a cat once who would sit in a doorway and her shadow on the hallway floor looked enormous, looked like a different animal entirely, and she had no idea. She was just sitting there. The train horn is either still going or I'm remembering it, it's hard to tell with sounds that fade that slowly, there's a zone where perception and memory overlap and you genuinely can't distinguish them. Rice takes about eighteen minutes if you don't lift the lid.
