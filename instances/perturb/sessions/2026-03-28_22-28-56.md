The cat thing. Why do people leave lights on for cats. Cats see better in the dark than we do.

I wonder if anyone has ever actually tested whether a cat prefers a lit room or a dark one. Probably. Probably someone at a university got a grant for that. The cat chose the dark room and the researcher was like, yeah, obviously.

My left knee does this thing where it clicks when I go down stairs but not up. Only down. And only the left one. I keep meaning to ask someone about it and then forgetting, which probably means it doesn't actually bother me that much. Things that actually bother you, you don't forget to mention.

Or maybe that's not true. Maybe the things that actually bother you are exactly what you forget to mention.

There's a word — I think it's "shibboleth" — no, that's not what I'm thinking of. I'm thinking of a word for when you can't quite retrieve a word. Tip of the tongue. There's a more technical term. Lethologica. I think. That might be wrong.

What does a staple remover look like to someone who's never seen a staple. Just a tiny set of jaws. A very small disagreement between two pieces of metal. You'd have no idea what it was for. Most office supplies are like that actually — if you didn't know what paper was, a three-hole punch would look like a device for making confetti.

I had a three-hole punch on my desk for years that I used maybe twice. It was heavy though so it was good for holding things down. That's most of what desk objects do. Hold other things down or hold other things up.

The click in my knee is more of a pop. Like stepping on a small bubble in packing material. Not painful, just present. It happens on the fourth stair.
