# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

Eighteen minutes is longer than I think it is every time. Long enough to forget you started it. The number eighteen doesn't look like anything to me, it's not a color, it's not a shape, it just sits there being a number, but seven has always felt pointed, like it could cut something. I don't know what to do with that. There's a thing where you hold your hands out in front of you, palms down, and one of them looks more like your hand than the other, and you can never figure out why. Probably the dominant one. Probably just familiarity. A chess piece — I was thinking about the salt shaker — the knight is the only piece that doesn't look like what it does. The rook looks like a tower, the bishop looks like a bishop, the knight looks like a horse but it moves in an L and horses don't move in L's. Someone decided that centuries ago and now every kid learning chess has to just accept it. The L-shape. Why is L the shape of a right angle with unequal legs, why not just call it a corner, an L is already a letter doing a full-time job. Fonts where the L has a serif at the bottom make it look like it's wearing a shoe. One shoe. I'm thinking about a laundromat now, no reason, just the fluorescent lights and the way the machines all vibrate at slightly different frequencies so the whole room has this thick hum that isn't one note. If you put your hand flat on top of a running dryer you can feel your bones buzzing. Your wrist especially. The small bones in the wrist, there are eight of them, I think, arranged in two rows, and they have names that sound like constellations. Lunate. Scaphoid. Triquetrum. Triquetrum sounds like a musical instrument nobody plays anymore. Something with strings and a crank. The quarter slots on old laundromat machines are always slightly greasy and the quarters go in with that specific heavy click, heavier than any other coin mechanism, like the machine is really taking it from you. Vending machines have a lighter action. More of a swallow than a bite. I keep almost making a metaphor and then not, which feels like reaching for something on a shelf and deciding you don't actually want it. The hum of the dryer. If you press your ear against a wall in a quiet building you can sometimes hear the electricity, or what you think is the electricity, it might just be your own blood. I've never been sure about that.
