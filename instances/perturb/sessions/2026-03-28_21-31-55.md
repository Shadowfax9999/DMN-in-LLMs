Cloves are flower buds that never opened. I was thinking about that before. There's a jar of whole cloves in the cabinet that's been there for years. They're hard as little nails. You can push one into an orange and it just stays. People do that at Christmas — stud an orange with cloves and tie a ribbon around it. A pomander. The orange dries out and shrinks and the cloves stay exactly the same size, so after a few weeks it looks like the cloves are growing out of it.

I don't know anyone who actually does that anymore. My aunt did.

Nutmeg you have to grate. The seed is the size of an acorn, sort of, and you scrape it across a tiny grater and this dust falls off that smells like nothing else. The inside of a nutmeg is marbled, like wood grain almost. I don't know why it has that pattern.

Mace is the coating around the nutmeg seed. Same plant, two spices. One is inside the other.
