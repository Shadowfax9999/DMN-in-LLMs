Pockets hold last season's receipts. Gum wrappers. A parking stub. You wore this in February. The coat remembers February for you. Not remembers. Holds. A vinyl record holds sound similarly. The groove is one long spiral. Not separate circles for each song. One continuous path inward. The needle just follows the curve. If they were concentric circles the needle would need to jump. It would need to know where. The spiral solves that by never deciding. It just keeps going slightly inward. That's elegant in a lazy way. The engineer chose the path of least resistance and it worked. Most good designs do that. The coat zipper is like that. One motion. Everything closes at once. Someone had to invent the zipper. Before that, buttons. Buttons take individual decisions. Each one a small commitment. I button my coat from the bottom. Some people start from the top. I don't know why I start there. Hot showers. Whether mine feels like yours. Whether my bottom-up buttoning feels like yours. There's no way to compare. You can describe temperature in degrees. You can't describe sensation in anything. The word "warm" is just a label. The feeling underneath it is private. Seven words is a strange constraint. Thoughts want to unspool longer. Like a record's spiral. Like a zipper pulling.
