Manhole. Why not womanhole. No — I'm not doing that.

The covers are heavy. Like, genuinely surprisingly heavy if you've ever had to lift one. Cast iron, maybe 80 pounds? More? The pattern on top is usually a crosshatch or a geometric flower, and every city has a different one. Someone had to design that pattern. No — I caught that.

Things that are heavier than you expect:
- manhole covers
- a gallon of milk when you thought the jug was almost empty
- bowling balls, every single time
- a sleeping child

Things that are lighter than you expect:
- dry sponges
- those big rolls of fiberglass insulation
- the moon (relative to — no, I don't actually know if that's true)

Saturday night and the copper smell is still on my hands. That's real. The way a smell outlasts the activity that caused it. You're done with the plumbing but your hands aren't done with the plumbing.

Manhole covers are round so they can't fall through the hole. That's supposedly the answer to an interview question at Microsoft or somewhere. It's one of those facts that feels like it should be more interesting than it is.

The word "round" looks round. The word "flat" looks flat. The word "tall" doesn't look tall though. It's short. Four letters.

I wonder if there's a name for words that look like what they mean. Not onomatopoeia — that's sound. This would be visual. Like how "bed" looks like a bed if you squint at the letters. The b is a headboard, the d is a footboard.

My daughter would like that. She's at the age where letters are still partly pictures.

The copper smell. I keep going back to it. The fitting was a ½-inch 90-degree elbow. Sweat fitting — you clean it, flux it, heat it, and the solder wicks in by capillary action. The solder turns shiny and then dull as it cools. There's a moment right after you pull the torch away where the joint is still liquid and you can see it settle, like a meniscus forming around the whole circumference at once.

I should check that joint tomorrow for leaks. You don't always know until the water's been running through it for a while.
