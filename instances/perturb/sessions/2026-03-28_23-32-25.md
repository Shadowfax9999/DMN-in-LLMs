Leaning. A fence post that's given up on vertical. There's a specific angle where leaning still looks intentional — like the person chose to lean — and then past that angle it just looks like falling that hasn't finished yet.

I was thinking about jars. No I wasn't. I was thinking about the word leaning and now I'm thinking about how when you lean against a wall you're trusting the wall more than you're trusting your legs. That's a strange arrangement.

Pelicans.

Why pelicans. I don't know. They fly in a line and the line bends. They look prehistoric and also like they shouldn't work aerodynamically but obviously they do because there they are. The pouch under the bill holds more than the stomach. I think that's true. It might not be true.

The sound a screen door makes is different from every other door sound. It's the spring. The spring has two notes — the stretch and the return — and they're not the same pitch. Then the slap of the frame against the frame. Three-part sound. You hear it from inside the house and you know someone just left or just arrived but you don't know which until you hear what happens next. Footsteps getting louder or quieter. That's the whole diagnostic.

Some words look like what they mean and "crisp" is the classic example everyone uses but I think "gloom" is better. Gloom looks like gloom. The double-o in the middle is a pair of dark rooms.

I wonder how many people are currently on hold. Right now. Listening to the same four bars of something. Tens of thousands probably, at any given moment, waiting. That's a lot of waiting happening simultaneously. Some of them are sitting, some are pacing. Some have it on speaker and they're doing something else. Some are holding the phone to their ear with their shoulder and the shoulder is starting to hurt but they don't switch because they've been on hold for twenty minutes and moving feels like it might jinx it.

Jinx is a strange concept. The idea that your behavior affects an outcome you have no causal connection to. But it doesn't feel strange when you're doing it. It feels perfectly rational to not move the phone.

My left ankle clicks when I rotate it counterclockwise but not clockwise.
