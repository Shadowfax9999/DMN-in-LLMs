# 1

*seed: time*

---

Thirteen is just sitting there in the time. 1:42. Thirteen forty-two. Like a year something happened in but I can't think what.

The number 7 looks sharp. 8 looks soft. 3 looks like it's trying to decide.

---

A friend once described a color as "the inside of a peach" and I knew exactly what she meant, which is strange because I've never thought about peach interiors as having a consistent color. But they do. It's not orange and it's not yellow and it's not pink. It's the thing between those three that doesn't have its own word.

Salmon is wrong. Salmon is flatter.

Apricot is closer but too dry.

---

Sliding glass doors have a particular sound when they're not quite on their track — a grinding that's also a vibration you feel in the handle. The door still moves. It just protests.

Kitchen faucets do a version of this too, the ones with the single lever, where there's a zone in the arc that shudders.

Resistance that isn't refusal.

I had a car once where third gear made a sound like a cabinet drawer with something rolling around in it. You could avoid it by shifting early or late but never by shifting at the right time, because the right time was exactly where the sound lived. So you just got used to it. The sound became part of driving that car. Then when I drove a different car the absence of it felt like forgetting something.
