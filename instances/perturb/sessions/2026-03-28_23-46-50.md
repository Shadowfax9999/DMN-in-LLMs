# 91

*seed: time · drift*

---

Paraguay did win the Chaco War. Bolivia wanted the oil underneath. I don't know if they ever found much of it.

The ceiling fan in a hotel room always has one speed that's wrong — too fast for sleeping, too slow to actually cool anything. You end up on the middle setting which is just the fan admitting it can't help you.

Carpet in hallways is always darker than carpet in rooms. Even in the same building, same floor. Hallways get darker carpet. There's probably a reason but I'm just noticing it.

The number of things I know about Bolivia could fit on an index card. La Paz, high altitude, salt flats. Tin. That's almost the whole card.

Sometimes when I'm trying to remember a name I can remember exactly where it sits on the page — left side, near the bottom, short word — but the word itself is just gone. The spatial memory outlasts the verbal one. Like the shelf stays after the book falls off.

A washer that's unbalanced during the spin cycle walks across the floor. You can hear it from two rooms away, that grinding migration. Someone told me you can level a washing machine with a torpedo level and adjustable feet and I nodded like I'd do that someday.

The backs of playing cards. The pattern is always symmetrical so you can't tell if someone's holding a card upside down. That's the whole reason for the symmetry. I never thought about that until embarrassingly recently.

Refrigerator hum cuts out and the silence is louder than the sound was. You only hear it leaving. Like you'd been leaning on something and it stepped away.

There's a particular kind of packing tape that's almost impossible to find the edge of. You spin the roll and run your thumbnail along it and nothing catches. The clear-on-clear problem.

I think the salt flats in Bolivia are where they get lithium now.
