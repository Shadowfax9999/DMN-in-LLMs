Mishima pottery — you'd carve the line, press the contrasting slip in while it's wet, let it dry, then scrape. The scraping is the part that requires nerve because you could go too deep and lose the line you just made. The whole technique depends on stopping at the right depth twice: once when you carve, once when you scrape.
