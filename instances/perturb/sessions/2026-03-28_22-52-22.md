# 72

*seed: time · drift · the reason shadows have soft edges at distance and sharp edges up close*

---

The shadow of a fence post is razor-sharp at its base and by the time it reaches the sidewalk six feet away it's just a smudge, because the sun isn't a point, it's a disc, and the further the shadow gets from the object the more the light can sneak around from different angles on the sun's face. I've probably driven through the intersection of Route 9 and Elm four thousand times and I couldn't tell you what color the building on the northeast corner is. There's a kind of knowledge that only exists as "I would recognize it if it changed." My pencil — the round one — I remember the color was yellow and the eraser was worse than useless, just smeared graphite into a shiny gray streak. I think they make pencils hexagonal so they don't roll but also so your fingers have somewhere to land.
