Hexagonal. That's also how they tile basalt columns. Giant's Causeway. I've never been but I know what it looks like, which is its own strange thing — a place that exists in my mind entirely as photographs taken by other people from roughly the same angle.

The bruise color. It's not purple exactly. It's that yellow-green at the edges where the hemoglobin is breaking down, and the center is still dark. Like a weather system seen from above. I don't know why bruises are tender to the touch days later. You press on them to check.

Things I press to check:
- bruises
- bread, for freshness
- the spot on a peach
- a doorbell I'm not sure works
- piano keys in a store, one note, quietly

The piano-keys one. There's always a moment of embarrassment when the note comes out louder than you expected. Every piano in a furniture store is slightly out of tune and has three dead keys somewhere in the upper register. Who tunes those? Does anyone? Or does someone just close the lid eventually.

I had a substitute teacher in maybe fifth grade who could play piano and that was the most impressive thing I'd ever seen a person do. She played something — I don't know what it was. I remember the other kids were talking and I was watching her hands. That might be a false memory. The specific detail of watching her hands while others talked feels too cinematic. But it also might just be what happened.

There's a particular kind of cloud that looks like it's been torn rather than formed. Cirrus. They move fast but they look still because they're so high up — the parallax makes the motion invisible. Like watching a plane that seems pinned to the sky.

My left shoe has a creak that only happens on smooth floors. Linoleum, the glossy concrete in a Target. On carpet or asphalt it's silent. I think it's the insole shifting against the sole but I've never actually looked.

Route 9 has that stretch where all the businesses are set back from the road behind parking lots and you can't tell if any of them are open. There's always one with a sign that says OPEN in a window that's clearly dark inside.
