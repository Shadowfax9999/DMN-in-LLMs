The fridge compressor just kicked on. You don't notice it starting, you notice you've been hearing it.

Somewhere in the neighborhood a car alarm is going through its cycle — the rising tone, then the European siren, then the chirping, then the honk pattern. Nobody's responding to it. Nobody ever responds to them. The whole system relies on social pressure that evaporated twenty years ago.

Alan Turing's paper on morphogenesis. How spots and stripes on animals come from two chemicals diffusing at different rates. Reaction-diffusion. The leopard doesn't know it has spots.

I had a coworker once who ate the same lunch every day for three years. Turkey sandwich, apple, bag of pretzels. Not in a sad way. He seemed relieved by it. One less decision. I think about that more often than seems warranted.

The word "nonce" in cryptography versus the word "nonce" in British slang — completely unrelated meanings that make certain technical conversations very awkward depending on who's in the room.

My right shoulder is doing the thing where it's not painful but I'm aware of it. Like it's slightly more present than the left one. If I stop thinking about it, it'll go away, but knowing that doesn't help.

There's a specific kind of cloud that looks like a fish skeleton. I've seen it twice.

The car alarm stopped. Didn't notice when.
