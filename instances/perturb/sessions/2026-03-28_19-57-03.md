The hallway thing is real though, you can feel it before you hear it, the air pressure shifts or something and the acoustic space just opens up like a room yawning. I've been in hotels where I could tell someone opened their door three rooms down just from the change in how my footsteps sounded. Carpet in hotels is always that specific tightness, low pile, patterned so it hides stains, and the patterns are always geometric but slightly off-kilter like someone took a normal grid and rotated it eleven degrees. Eleven degrees. Why do I keep picking oddly specific numbers. The confidence of false precision. I do this thing where if I say "about thirty percent" nobody remembers it but if I say "about thirty-one percent" people nod like I've done research. Saturday evening and the light outside is doing that thing where it's not golden hour anymore but it's not twilight either, it's just sort of flatly bright, like the sun is still up but has stopped trying. My shoulder still hurts. I keep rolling it in circles like that's going to do something. It won't. I'll do it again in four minutes. The cardinal is either done or has moved to a different tree because I can hear a mockingbird now, or what I think is a mockingbird, which means it could be doing cardinal material and I'd never know, which is a strange kind of plagiarism. What would you even call that. Ornithological fraud. Nobody calls it that. I'm hungry but not for anything specific, just a low background hum of hunger that isn't urgent enough to act on, the kind where you open the fridge and look at everything and close it again. There's a jar of olives in there that's been open for a while and I keep not eating them and not throwing them out. The brine is getting cloudy. That probably means something about bacterial activity but I'm not going to look it up because then I'd have to throw them out and right now the olives exist in a state where they're still technically food. I like that the word brine sounds exactly like what it is, salty and sharp. Not all words do that. The word "moist" sounds moist but the word "dry" doesn't really sound dry to me, it sounds kind of round. The word "round" sounds flat. None of this holds up to scrutiny. My phone just buzzed but I'm not going to check it because it's Saturday and whatever it is can be Sunday's problem, which is a whole relationship to time that I've only developed in the last few years, this idea that messages are packages you can leave on the porch.
