*seed: time · drift · the mathematics of origami and Huzita-Hatori axioms*

---

Things that are not symmetrical but look like they should be:

- Your own face
- The way a fitted sheet folds no matter how careful you are
- Handshakes — one hand is always doing more work
- The number 8, actually, if you look closely at most fonts
- A paper crane, if you folded it honestly

Things that are not decisions:

- Which shoe you put on first
- The side of the bed you sleep on
- Saying "I'm fine"
- The first fold in a piece of paper — it's always in half, always toward you, nobody decides this

Things that are not as strong as they seem:

- Padlocks
- Ultimatums
- The top of your foot
- A single fold — but a folded edge becomes a new axiom, a new line you can reference, and by the sixth or seventh fold you've built a geometry that can solve cubic equations, which Euclidean straightedge and compass can't, which means a piece of paper is technically a more powerful mathematical instrument than the tools the Greeks gave us

Things that are not the color you remember them being:

- The Statue of Liberty
- Old pennies
- The sky during a tornado — people say green but it's more like the color of a bruise on someone with olive skin
- Yielding signs are not yellow anymore, they're red and white, and I don't know when that changed
- Your childhood bedroom walls

Things that are not empty:

- Water towers at night
- The space between your tongue and the roof of your mouth
- A room you just left
- A folded crane — there's air trapped in the body, a small pocket, and if you don't blow into it to inflate it it just looks like a flat thing with wings

Things that are not patience but get mistaken for it:

- Not knowing what else to do
- Being tired
- Sitting on a staircase on the fourteenth floor
