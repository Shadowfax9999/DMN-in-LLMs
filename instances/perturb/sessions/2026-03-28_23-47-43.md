Seatbelts have that two-stage thing where you pull it slow and it feeds out, pull it fast and it locks. I used to test that as a kid in the backseat. Over and over. The satisfying catch.

There's a plant I walk past that I've been assuming is a weed but it's been there three years now and it's getting bigger. At some point persistence becomes legitimacy.

The sound of someone else's phone vibrating on a table.

I can never remember which way to turn a radiator valve. Every single time. Lefty-loosey should apply but I second-guess it and then I'm standing there turning it both ways trying to feel which one is opening. The metal is always slightly greasy in a way that doesn't seem to come from anything.

Alan Turing's death is one of those facts that sits in my mind at a weird angle. The apple. Whether it was deliberate or accidental and how the ambiguity itself feels like part of the story. His mother thought it was accidental. I don't know what to do with that detail so it just stays there.

My shoulder's still here. Still just the right one.

Cashews come from a fruit. The cashew apple. The nut hangs off the bottom of it like an afterthought. I've seen pictures. The fruit is yellow or red and apparently you can eat it but it's too perishable to ship. So most of the world only knows the afterthought part.

The fridge just cycled on. There's a half-second where you can hear the compressor decide to start — a kind of pre-hum, like it's gathering itself. Then the steady drone. I only notice the drone when it stops, but I notice the startup every time.

Alan Turing also ran marathons. Near-Olympic-level times. That doesn't fit the picture most people have of him and I think that mismatch is part of why I remember it.

Cashew shells contain an oil that causes chemical burns. The people who process them by hand in certain countries get damaged skin on their fingers. I read that once and it changed how cashews taste to me, which is not rational but is true.
