1. Hooch. That's what sourdough people call the dark liquid.
2. Yes it's alcohol.
3. Have people tasted it? Of course they have.
4. The theremin thing is interesting — the instrument where imprecision is the voice.
5. A piano hammer hits a string and then immediately backs off. There's no sustained contact. So no vibrato. The note is already orphaned by the time you hear it.
6. I don't know if "orphaned" is right there.
7. Frets on a guitar are definitely nickel sometimes and sometimes stainless steel and people have opinions about this.
8. People have opinions about everything that touches a string.
9. What holds a piano string taut? The pin. The tuning pin. And the force on those pins is enormous — something like 160 pounds per string and there are over 200 strings. The cast iron plate inside a piano is under maybe 20 tons of tension.
10. I just want to sit with that for a second. Twenty tons, held still, inside a piece of furniture.

Cinnamon is bark. That never stops being weird to me. You peel bark off a tree and it curls up and you put it in oatmeal.

Nutmeg is a seed.

Bay leaf is just a leaf.

Vanilla is a fermented orchid pod.

Saffron is the reproductive part of a crocus.

Cloves are flower buds that never opened.

Something about that — spices as interrupted processes. Bark that was peeled. Buds that were picked before blooming. A pod that was fermented instead of left to seed.
