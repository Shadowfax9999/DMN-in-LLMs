# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

1. The hollow double-bounce. I can almost feel it in my palms.
2. April starting on a Wednesday means it ends on a Thursday. I don't know why that matters but I checked.
3. Plywood on a highway would tumble, not glide. The center of pressure is ahead of the center of gravity so it pitches up, stalls, catches again. Repeating.
4. A manta ray actually flaps.
5. Something about the phrase "spring night" that already sounds like it's being remembered instead of lived.
6. I wonder how many car trunks get closed twice because the first close didn't latch. The little rubber bumpers compress and then the whole thing bounces back up an inch.
7. Hinges as the thing that keeps doors from being kites.
8. The basketball sound stopped.
9. There's a kind of bolt called a carriage bolt that has a square section just under the head so it locks into a square hole and can't spin. You tighten it from the nut side only. I installed a gate latch with them once and stripped one because the square section wasn't seated.
10. The word "seated" doing work in both bolt contexts and chair contexts and somehow meaning the same thing — settled into the place shaped for it.
11. Nine-something at night and still not fully dark.
12. Cork expands after you pull it from a bottle. You can't put it back the same way it came out.
