Back-of-knee. That soft ditch. The place you forget about until you kneel on something or a mosquito finds it.

Seven things I can't explain why I know:
1. The back of the knee is called the popliteal fossa
2. You can feel your own pulse there if you press right
3. Chairs that are too tall cut into it
4. It's one of the first places to sunburn if you're face-down
5. Babies sweat there before almost anywhere else
6. I'm not sure about number 5
7. The word "fossa" just means ditch

I was going to say something about how it's a hinge and hinges are always the vulnerable part but I don't think that's true. Door hinges are the strongest part of the door usually. It's the latch side that gives.

Saturday night and I'm thinking about knees. There's a specific kind of quiet that happens when you're the only one still awake in a building. Not silence — the refrigerator is running, something in the walls is ticking, there might be a car outside with its bass on. But the human quiet. Everyone else opted out.

Do fish sleep. I think some of them do but they don't close their eyes because they can't. That must be a different kind of sleep. Or maybe closing your eyes isn't the point of sleep and we just do it because the lids are there.

The number 4 looks heavier than the number 7. Not the quantity — the digit. The shape of it. It sits lower. 7 is leaning and thin, like it might blow somewhere. 4 is planted. I don't know if everyone sees this or if it's just me or if I'm making it up right now and it'll seem stupid in five minutes.

Popliteal. It sounds like it should be a type of small tree.
