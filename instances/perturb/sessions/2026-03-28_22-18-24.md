The back of the knee is such a strange place to have thin skin. You'd think evolution would have armored that spot better. But then again nobody's attacking the back of the knee. Except chairs.

I can hear something right now that I can't identify. It's not mechanical. It's not wind. It might be nothing — might be the auditory system inventing signal from noise because it's too quiet.

Things I've never successfully visualized:
- the back of my own head
- a four-dimensional cube (I just rotate the three-dimensional one and pretend)
- what my voice sounds like to other people
- the actual size of a whale when I'm not looking at one

That last one. I've seen whales. I've watched footage. I've read the tonnage. But when I try to hold the size in my mind without any visual reference it just... defaults to large. Generic large. School-bus-and-a-half large. Not *whale* large.

Peanut butter is sticky in a way that nothing else is sticky. Honey is sticky but it's a different project. Honey wants to stay on surfaces. Peanut butter wants to stay on itself. It's cohesive rather than adhesive. Or both. I don't actually know the difference precisely and I'm not going to look it up.

Saturday night has a specific feeling even when you're not doing anything. It doesn't feel like Tuesday night where you're not doing anything. On Tuesday the not-doing-anything is neutral. On Saturday it has a shape around it — the shape of all the things that could be happening. Bars exist right now. Concerts are starting right now. Someone is putting on shoes and checking their hair one more time. And here I am thinking about peanut butter.

The number 3 is the one I trust least. I don't know what that means. 8 is reliable. 3 is always up to something.

I wonder how long a staircase would have to be before you'd stop and sit down in the middle of it. Like if it were one continuous flight. Thirty floors? Twenty? There's probably a floor where most people would just stop climbing and accept where they are. Fourteen feels right but I have no reason for that.

My left sock has a seam that's sitting wrong across my toes.
