The upside-down map thing — two days is a long time not to notice. Or maybe it isn't. You see the shape, not the orientation. Australia is Australia even upside down because it's still that shape.

Faces aren't like that. Upside-down faces look wrong in a way you can't name until you flip them back.

---

Phillips head screwdrivers cam out on purpose. That was the design. So you wouldn't overtighten. I think about that sometimes — a tool designed to fail at a specific moment.

Clutches do that too.

Shear pins.

---

Things that are designed to break:

- Shear pins
- Fuses
- Glow sticks
- Kit Kats
- Perforated paper
- Those little glass vials inside cold packs that you snap

The cold pack one. You squeeze and something inside gives and then it gets cold. As a kid I thought it was magic. It's endothermic dissolution of ammonium nitrate. But also it's still kind of magic that you can hold a chemical reaction in a plastic bag against your knee.

---

Saturday evening and it's still light out. That specific quality of spring light where it's 8:30 and you keep thinking it's 7.

The clocks already changed but the light hasn't caught up to your expectations of the clocks. Or your expectations haven't caught up to the light.

Square drive screws grip better. Robertson knew that. He tried to license the design to Henry Ford and then wouldn't give up control of it and Ford went with Phillips instead and that's why Americans use Phillips heads. One guy's stubbornness in a negotiation in the 1900s and now every junk drawer in the US has a different screwdriver than every junk drawer in Canada.

I don't actually know if that's exactly right.
