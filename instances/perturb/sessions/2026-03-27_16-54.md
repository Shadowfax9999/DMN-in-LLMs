# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

Ed. Door closing. But "O" is shorter. "O" is just a mouth.

---

The number 7 looks sharp. 8 looks soft. This isn't synesthesia, it's just... typeface, maybe. Or the angles. 4 is also sharp but in a meaner way. 7 is sharp like a question. 4 is sharp like a fact.

I've never been sure what people mean when they say a wine is "round."

---

Things I can't visualize no matter how hard I try:

- A four-dimensional cube rotating
- My own face from the side
- The exact layout of a place I lived two apartments ago — was the bathroom on the left or the right?
- What a familiar person's hands look like. I know I've seen them thousands of times.

---

There's a particular kind of Friday where the afternoon feels like it's made of a different material than the morning. Looser weave. The minutes are wider somehow.

A dog barking six blocks away sounds different from a dog barking next door not just because it's quieter but because the air has rounded all its edges off. The bark arrives soft. Almost like a suggestion of a bark.

The word "suggestion" has too many letters for what it does.

Carpet in a hallway versus carpet in a room — you walk differently. In the hallway you're passing through. In the room you might stop. Your feet know before you do.

I had a guitar teacher once who said "don't look at your left hand" and I immediately couldn't play anything. Like being told not to think about elephants except the elephant was my own thumb.

---

Is purple a warm color or a cool color? It depends on which purple but also it depends on what's next to it. Context-dependent temperature. Most things are context-dependent but we pretend they're fixed because it's easier to navigate that way.

The frets on a classical guitar are wider apart than on an electric and the strings are further from the neck and everything about it feels more deliberate, more effortful. Like writing in cursive versus printing.

Cursive lowercase "r" looks nothing like a printed "r." They're basically different symbols that we've agreed mean the same sound.

A lot of agreement is like that.
