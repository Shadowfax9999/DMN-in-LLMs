The double-text thing. It's not really about the second text. It's about the gap between them. You send the first one and there's this window where you're still the person who just asked a normal question, and then you send the second one and now you're the person who's worried about how the first one landed. The second text is a map of your anxiety about the first text.

Which is probably why nobody ever sends a triple text. Two is nervous. Three would be a different thing entirely.

Cartography and lying. I keep thinking about how every projection has a thing it sacrifices. Mercator keeps angles true but wrecks area. Peters keeps area true but everything looks stretched. You don't get to keep everything. You pick what matters and let the rest distort.

I think most conversations work like that too but I said I wasn't going to chase it.

Nickel is one of those metals that doesn't smell like anything until you touch it, and then your hands smell like metal for an hour. But the smell is actually your skin oils reacting with the nickel, not the nickel itself. So the smell is neither yours nor the metal's. It's the reaction.

I had a guitar with nickel frets once. The E string always wore a groove into the first fret before any of the others because that's where barre chords sit.
