# 3

*seed: time · drift · armillary sphere*

---

Armillary sphere. The word "armillary" sounds like it should be about arms, and it is — those metal bands circling each other. There's one in a garden somewhere I'm thinking of, green with oxidation, tilted wrong because the post settled.

Friday is still orange.

The four spices. Mace sitting there since 2019. Seven years of a spice not being used but also not being thrown away. There's a whole zone of the kitchen like that — the tahini with the oil separated on top, the fish sauce from a recipe you made once.

---

Armillary. Artillery. Capillary. Words that end in -ary and have an L in them somewhere.

- Ancillary
- Corollary
- Maxillary
- Fibrillary
- Axillary

Axillary means armpit-related.

---

The rings of an armillary sphere are meant to represent orbits but they don't move. It's a frozen diagram. A globe does the same thing — fixes the continents so you can point at them, but the pointing is the whole point, not the spinning.

I'm thinking about the plastic bag pulling the glass off the table. The handle hooking the stem. That's such a specific small disaster. The moment between hearing the sound and knowing what broke.

The clove is the one that smells like a dentist's office if you've ever had a dry socket packed with clove oil. Sharp and medicinal and warm at the same time. Whole cloves look like tiny burnt flowers.

A ceiling fan clicking on the third rotation. Not every rotation — just the third one. You stop hearing it and then you hear it again.

---

What does the color brown taste like. Not what brown foods taste like. What the color itself would taste like if you could put it on your tongue. Dry. Slightly bitter. Room temperature. Like the inside of a walnut shell, not the walnut.

Green would taste cold and thin.

Orange — the color, separate from the fruit — would taste like nothing but feel fizzy.

I haven't thought about synesthesia in a while. The Tuesday-brown thing. It's not real synesthesia, it's just an association that stuck early and never came loose. Lots of people have those. Letters having genders. Numbers having personalities. Five is reliable. Seven is difficult.

Three is young.
