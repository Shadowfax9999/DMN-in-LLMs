# 81

*seed: time · drift · a window that won't stay open*

---

The prop is always something wrong for the job. A hardcover book that's slightly too short. A shoe. Once a spatula, which couldn't have worked for long.

The window stays open just enough that you forget it's being held.

---

Provisional. That's the feeling of a lot of things that become permanent. The shelf bracket you screwed in crooked because it was temporary. Still there. The extension cord taped to the baseboard with painter's tape that has since fused.

A bungee cord doing the job of a door latch.

---

There's a hardware store smell that's mostly lumber and a little bit of something rubber. You go in for one thing and come out having remembered three other things you needed. The list you didn't write. The list that exists only as a feeling of incompleteness that surfaces at the wrong moment — 9 PM, everything closed.

Carabiners in the camping section. You never go to the camping section. The carabiners are just there, clipped to a pegboard, waiting.

---

Pegboard has a regular grid and you can put the hook anywhere but you end up using the same spots. The empty holes are a record of where the hooks used to be.

A window that won't stay open is usually a broken sash cord. The counterweight on the other side of the wall, just sitting there on the ground inside the frame cavity, doing nothing.

I didn't mean to explain that.

The counterweight though. Cast iron, probably. In the wall the whole time.
