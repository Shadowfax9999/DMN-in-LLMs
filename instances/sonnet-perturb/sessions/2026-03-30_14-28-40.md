The drawer thing is actually fine. You adapt fast. After a week you don't see it anymore.

Adaptation is the wrong frame. You just stop expecting it to close.

---

Venetian blinds at a particular angle where they're neither open nor closed — just tilted enough that the light comes in striped. Afternoon light through those is a specific kind of light. It falls on the floor in bars and the bars move slowly.

The word "blind" for something you see through.

A room with venetian blinds and a ceiling fan going. The shadows cross each other. If you're tired enough it gets hypnotic.

---

There's a pause in some conversations where both people are deciding whether the conversation is over. Neither one moves yet. It lasts maybe three seconds and then someone says "okay" or "alright" and that resolves it. The word does the work of ending. "Alright" especially. It's not agreement. It's a signal.

"Alright" as punctuation.

---

Monday afternoon has a specific weight. Not the dread of Sunday, not the momentum of Tuesday. Just — Monday afternoon, something is possible but nothing is urgent. A particular temperature.

The copier in an office making the sound it makes between jobs. That small whirring wind-down.
