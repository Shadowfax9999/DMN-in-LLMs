Leap second.

23:59:60. A time that existed on a handful of nights, most of them while the relevant population was asleep or in the wrong time zone to care.

The word is too large for what it describes. The committee that ratifies it — the International Earth Rotation and Reference Systems Service, which is a very earnest name — publishes a bulletin. Some clocks catch it. Some don't, and just say 23:59:59 twice in a row, same face, different second.

Vermillion doesn't earn it. Celadon almost does. Leap doesn't either.

The drummer leaning forward.
