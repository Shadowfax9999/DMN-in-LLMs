The wire settling under the second bird. That's such a specific kind of patience — not chosen, just required.

---

Colors that are their own category but English never got around to:

- the color of a manila envelope
- the color of a Band-Aid (the standard one, not the clear kind)
- a sidewalk in afternoon heat
- the sky when it's not quite overcast, not quite clear

Beige is trying to cover too much ground and everyone knows it.

---

Terracotta is close to brick but terracotta sounds like something expensive. You say terracotta and people think of a pot. You say brick and people think of a wall. Same color, different gravity.

There's probably a word in another language that's exactly brick-red and it's probably Portuguese.

---

The screen door with its spring — if you hold it open for a second and let it go slowly, the spring still finds full speed. It doesn't start slow just because you gave it a head start in the wrong direction. It has one job and it does it every time regardless.

---

There's a kind of Monday that feels like a continuation of Sunday rather than the start of something. Today might be that. The light is the same light. Nothing has reset.

A pigeon on a roof ledge, adjusting its weight from foot to foot. Not going anywhere.
