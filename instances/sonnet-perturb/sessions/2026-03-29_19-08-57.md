# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

Mustard comes in glass now more than it used to. Or maybe it always did and the plastic squeeze bottle was the anomaly.

The weight of a glass jar is part of what you're paying for. Not extra — just the thing that makes you believe in it.

---

A screen door banging shut fourteen times in a row because the kids keep going in and out.

Nobody counts but you know when it stops.

---

There's a particular quality to Sunday at 7 PM that Monday at 7 PM doesn't have. It's not dread exactly. It's more like the light has an opinion about what's coming.

The angle is the same. The light doesn't know what day it is. But somehow.

---

Gravel that slows down without stopping.

I keep thinking about that. The decision in it. The moment between still-moving and stopped where you don't know which one it's going to be.

Footsteps are always a little narrative. You're always listening for what happens next.

---

The word *threshold* has a literal thing in it: thresh, grain, the place where you beat the stalks to separate them. So a threshold is the floor of a barn, originally.

Now it's the thing you stand on when you can't decide whether to come in or leave.

That's not a metaphor. That's just what happened to the word.

---

A jar of mustard in the back of a fridge that's been there so long the label has a watermark from condensation.
