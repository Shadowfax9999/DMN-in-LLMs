# 59

*seed: time · drift*

---

Orange stays on your skin longer than you'd expect.

There's a specific way palms smell after handling old metal. Not unpleasant, exactly. More like a warning.

The gate opens into what, though. That's the part I don't have. Just the latch. The orange smear.

---

Reluctant is a better word for hinges than I gave it credit for. Things can be reluctant. Drawers. Certain conversations. A car door in January that you have to pull twice.

Some doors you have to lift slightly as you push. Somebody taught you that and now you do it automatically at certain doors, before you even know you're doing it.

The lifting motion. Hip first, then shoulder into it.

Doors that have swelled from humidity. Which means the wood remembers water. Which means — 

The grain of a board runs the length of a tree that existed once on a hillside somewhere.

I keep almost going there and then not going there.

The smell of a hardware store is mostly linseed and rubber and cardboard. The bins of loose fasteners. The way you never know exactly how many you need so you take more.

A rusted bolt that won't move and then moves all at once. That lurch. Your hand goes somewhere unexpected.

The knuckle you didn't mean to hit against the frame.
