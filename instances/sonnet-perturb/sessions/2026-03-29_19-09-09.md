# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

The mustard is probably still good. The date on the label is a suggestion.

---

Shoes lined up at a door. Someone's house. You can read the household from them — the worn-down heel, the child's sneaker with the velcro flap half-detached, the one pair that looks like they've never been outside. You take your own off and add them to the story.

Someone's always the last one awake. In every house, every night.

---

The word *condiment* feels vaguely Victorian to me. Like it should be in a sentence with *shall* and *parlor.* But you find it in the drive-through, the truck stop, the gas station rotating rack of little packets.

Honey mustard. Yellow mustard. Spicy brown.

Spicy brown sounds like a description, not a name.

---

A parking lot where one light is out and the shadows fall differently and your car looks wrong for a second and then right.
