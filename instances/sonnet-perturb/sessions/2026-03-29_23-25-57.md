# 37

*seed: time · drift*

---

The gas station light has a hum or there's always something humming near it. Compressor. Refrigerator case inside, visible through the glass. The snacks arranged by a logic that shifts at midnight — what's at eye level at 3 PM is not what you reach for at 11.

A foil sleeve of gum. Push-through or peel. The small blister, the resistance, then not.

---

Pushpins.
Rubber bands of unknown age.
A battery that might be fine.
A coupon.
A key to something.

The coupon still looks like value. Only the date changed.

---

Sunday night has a specific pressure that's not anxiety exactly. More like a faint awareness of adjacency. Something waiting in the next panel. You're not thinking about it. It's just there, in the peripheral of the hour.

The gas pump receipt unfurling whether you pressed NO or not. The wet receipt, specifically — it goes translucent. The ink holds but barely.

Two pipes under the street that never touch. The one for rain, the one for everything else. At some point someone decided they shouldn't mix and that seems right. There are rules like that — obvious once stated, invisible until they're violated.

Thermoplastic at the edge of a yellow line, where it lifted. Small ridge. You could catch a wheel on it if you were going slow enough to notice.

The clerk probably doesn't look at the pump receipts either.
