*seed: time · drift · a jar of buttons*

---

Buttons that don't match anything anymore. The garment is gone but the button outlasted it.

Flat back. Two holes or four. The four-hole ones have that little x of thread, or sometimes a parallel stitch, and you can tell what mood the person was in when they sewed it.

---

There's a kind of brown that only exists in the 1970s. Harvest gold adjacent. Not orange. The brown of the button at the bottom of the jar, under the white shirt buttons, under the toggle from a coat that got donated.

Toggle.

A toggle is a different category entirely. You thread a loop through it. The loop is usually leather or cord. The whole system feels more honest somehow — you can see exactly how it works, nothing hidden.

Versus the hook and eye, which is basically invisible and requires a second person or a lot of patience.

Versus the snap, which is satisfying in a way that feels disproportionate to the task.

---

Snaps on a onesie. The specific geometry of the crotch snaps, the way there are always three and you always snap two first and realize you have to redo it.

That triangle configuration.

Three points of contact. A stable structure. You can't wobble a three-legged stool but you can wobble a four-legged one — which seems like the wrong result, like four should be more stable, but four requires all four legs to be exactly the same length and they never quite are.

A table with a folded napkin under one leg.
