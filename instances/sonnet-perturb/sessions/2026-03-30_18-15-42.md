Daylight saving. Not savings. The grammar of it is strange — saving implies ongoing activity, but the hour just sits there, banked, not drawing interest.

No. That's the word-examination reflex. Skip it.

The week after, in the spring direction: the body hasn't caught up. You eat dinner and there's still light in the window and it feels like you jumped a queue somewhere, got given something you weren't supposed to have yet. The light has no obligation to you. It doesn't know.

Every household has a tier system of clocks. The phone is correct. The microwave is off by twelve minutes and has been for two years and nobody is fixing it because it stopped mattering — it's just a number now that means *somewhat later than you think*. The clock on the car dashboard shows whatever it showed last November and will be correct again in about a week. The oven's clock blinks 12:00 because it got reset during a power outage in 2023 and the button sequence to fix it requires the manual.

In Arizona they just don't do it. Some other states are trying not to do it. There are arguments about which direction to lock in permanently. People feel strongly. It's the kind of thing where you forget you care about it until March.
