# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

The stepping second hand. You already know it's coming and it surprises you every time.

Tambourine is three syllables for something you shake once and it tells you everything it knows.

Things that count as percussion:
- tape dispenser
- a cup set down on a counter
- someone's pen
- car door, two rows over in a parking garage

The parking garage thing. When an engine starts and for a second the whole structure knows about it.

---

Seven feels sharp. Four is blunt. Nine takes longer than it should. Eleven is a name for a person, not a number — someone quiet, goes by Ellie.

The flat light of Monday afternoon. Not bad light, just indifferent. The shadows are in the wrong places, or not in the wrong places, just in places you don't have a feeling about.

A friend of mine used to say *yep* the way other people say *goodbye* — final, door-closing, like that was the whole conversation. I still hear it when I read the word.

Concrete getting warm in the sun is a smell that doesn't have a name. Not the rain version. The dry version, afternoon, first warm week of the year. Just the concrete sitting there absorbing it.

The difference between a tap and a knock. I keep thinking there's something there and then not finding it.
