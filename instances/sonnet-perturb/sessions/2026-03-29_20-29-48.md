# 27

*seed: time · drift · the weight of a cast iron pan*

---

You never hold it with one hand the first time. Always two, and then you feel slightly embarrassed.

Seasoning. The word doing other work.

---

Cast iron holds heat the way stone holds cold. No — skip the comparison. It's just heavier than you expect, every time, even when you remember.

The handle gets hot too.

A pan that's been used for thirty years looks almost geological. The surface.

---

Pancakes specifically. Why pancakes. The first one always goes wrong and everyone knows this and accounts for it, the sacrificial pancake, thrown away or eaten standing over the sink by whoever is cooking.

Rules that exist but aren't written.

Knocking twice before entering even when the door is open.

Passing the salt with the pepper even if nobody asked for pepper.

---

The number seventeen again. It came up before. There's something about primes past thirteen where they stop feeling inevitable and start feeling arbitrary. Like the universe could have gotten away with skipping them.

Forty-three especially.

Though maybe that's unfair to forty-three.

---

The thing about a cast iron pan is it doesn't go in the dishwasher and everyone in the house has to know this and there's always one person who doesn't know yet and the pan looks wrong for a while after.
