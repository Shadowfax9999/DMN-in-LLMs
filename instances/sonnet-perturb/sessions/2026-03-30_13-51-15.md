*seed: time · drift · rumble strip*

---

The sound before the sound. You're already off the road when you hear it.

---

Braille for cars. Though that's the kind of thing that sounds clever and then you sit with it and it's just a description.

The texture of it through the steering wheel. Through the seat. The whole car becomes a nerve.

---

There are roads that hum at specific pitches when you drive over them. Someone laid the rumble strips at intervals tuned to a melody. America the Beautiful, on a stretch in New Mexico I think. You'd only hear it at 45 miles per hour. Too fast: too high. Too slow: nothing coherent. The road as an instrument that grades your compliance.

---

Monday afternoon in the middle of a week that started this morning and there's still four days of it. That's not a complaint. It's just a shape. Four days sitting there in a row like freight cars.

A freight car is enormous up close. You forget. You're at a crossing and one goes by and each car takes longer than you expected and then another one, and you're late, and the car idles.

The gate arms at railroad crossings are surprisingly thin. You could break one. People do. The arm comes down and someone drives through it. The arm breaks and the car drives away with a piece of it somewhere on the hood probably.

---

Thin things that stop you anyway.

The velvet rope. The orange cone. The sawhorse with the one reflective stripe.

None of them could actually stop anything. They're requests. Very confident requests.
