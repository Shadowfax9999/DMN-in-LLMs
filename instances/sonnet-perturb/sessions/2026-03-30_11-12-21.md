*seed: time · drift*

The hem just stops the fabric from having ideas — that's still sitting there.

Hems and selvages are different. The selvage is the edge that always existed. The hem is the edge you made because you had to stop somewhere.

A coast is a selvage. A border is a hem.

Something in a junk drawer that you've moved to three different houses. Not kept — just never thrown away. A warranty card for something you don't own anymore. A battery that might be dead. A thing you can't name, plastic, with a clip on it.

The clip suggests it belonged somewhere. Clipped to something. Organized. And then whatever it organized stopped mattering and here it is, still clipped to nothing, still certain about its purpose.

Parking structures smell the same everywhere. Oil and concrete and something else. Cold even when it's warm. The air doesn't turn over. You're always walking through air that's been there.

The attendant booth, sometimes empty. Little TV inside showing something. A space heater. A calendar.

Twelve months of whatever the calendar company thought would appeal to parking structure attendants. Mountains, probably. Or puppies.
