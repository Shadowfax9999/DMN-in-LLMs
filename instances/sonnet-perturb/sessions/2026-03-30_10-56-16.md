# 51

*seed: time · drift · household knowledge someone doesn't have yet*

---

The first time you use someone else's shower you stand there for a second too long figuring out which way is hot.

Every household has a thing like that. The drawer that only opens if you lift slightly while pulling. The toilet that needs a second flush. The light switch that's behind the door instead of beside it — you slap the wall twice before you remember.

Temporary amnesia of the familiar.

---

Saffron costs what it costs because of the harvest. Each flower has three stigmas. You pick them by hand. Someone told me this once at a dinner party like it explained something about the person who told it.

Actually, I don't know if it explained anything.

---

The word "threshold" is doing something structural in a house. Not metaphorical — actual threshold, the strip of wood at the bottom of a door frame. People step over it ten times a day without registering it's there.

Then the threshold metaphor got so big it ate the physical thing.

---

Spring morning specifically: the light comes in at an angle that makes dust visible. All winter it was there. Now you can see it.

The dust wasn't waiting for spring. The light was.
