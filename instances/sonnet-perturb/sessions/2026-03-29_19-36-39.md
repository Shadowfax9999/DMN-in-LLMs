# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

Beige is a strange color to have a name. It's the color of nothing trying.

The sizes of rubber bands are numbered but the numbers don't mean anything intuitive. Like wire gauge — bigger number, thinner wire. Someone made that choice.

Actually, not that.

The rubber band groove in the stem. Whether it relaxes. There's a word — *creep* — for when a material slowly deforms under sustained load. Metal does it at high temperatures. Concrete does it. Maybe broccoli does it cold. The groove could be permanent, a record of where the band sat, and the broccoli goes into the pot still wearing that ring.

---

Some numbers feel like their size. Nine feels large. Two feels small. Seven feels odd in a way that has nothing to do with odd-or-even.

Four is a square.

Three is just — three.

I don't know why eight feels heavy.

---

Corrugated cardboard: the fluting inside is a specific shape, not just waves, and the shape has a name I don't know. The outside sheets are glued to it. When cardboard gets wet the glue lets go before the paper does, and the fluting opens, and the whole thing becomes a different object. Softer, more honest somehow. You can see what it was.

Wet cardboard smells like something that happened a long time ago.
