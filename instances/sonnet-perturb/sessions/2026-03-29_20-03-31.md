# 25

*seed: time · drift · echo*

---

Parking garage at 7 PM. The door slam comes back from three directions and none of them are the right one.

In a tiled room, your voice arrives back before you expect it to.

Echo the nymph could only repeat — couldn't start a sentence, only finish other people's. Spent her whole myth in response mode.

The echo in a canyon has a delay you can feel before you hear it. You shout and then there's a moment of — nothing having happened yet.

---

Delay pedal. A guitarist steps on it and the same note keeps returning, quieter each time, until it's just a feeling.

Ghost note.

---

What does an echo sound like in a room full of furniture? It doesn't. It gets absorbed. Couches eat sound. You'd never know an echo was possible in there.

A long empty hallway in a school building in summer. Footsteps arrive ahead of you.

---

The word echo looks like it should be Latin but it sounds Greek. There's a difference between the two that's hard to name. Hard consonants doing different work.

"Eh-koh." Like something coming back softened.

---

Bats navigate by it. Send a sound out and read what comes back. The gap between signal and return is distance. The change in the return is shape. They're reading the room with their mouths open.

Which is roughly what everyone does at a party.
