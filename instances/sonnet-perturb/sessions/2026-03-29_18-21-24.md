Paint swatch names do that — "Agreeable Gray," "Accessible Beige" — as if the color needs to be introduced gently, talked up a little before you see it. You'd never name a color "Confrontational Taupe" even if that's what it was.

The word *fleet* is three different words. Fleet of ships. Fleet-footed. Fleeting. They sit in the same sound and don't touch each other.

A coin slot on an old parking meter — the slot is just barely wide enough, and there's something satisfying about that precision, the quarter dropping clean without wobble. Though sometimes it doesn't drop. It just sits there in the slot, half in.

Accordion files. The way they collapse flat and then open out. I had one once and never used it the way it was meant to be used, just shoved things in the front pocket.

The number seven is supposed to be everyone's favorite number. I've read that somewhere and never known what to do with it. Seven doesn't look like a favorite. It looks angular. Uncomfortable to write by hand — that little crossbar some people put through it and some people don't.

The people who put a crossbar through their sevens probably also put a loop on their twos.
