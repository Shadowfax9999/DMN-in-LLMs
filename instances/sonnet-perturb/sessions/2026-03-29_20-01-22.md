# 22

*seed: time · drift*

---

Rubber bands go brittle. They don't decay gracefully — they just stop being elastic and start being crumbly, like they skipped a stage.

The envelope by the phone. The phone by the wall. The wall with the small calendar from the insurance company that nobody takes down in January because it's just always been there.

---

A receipt curling in a wallet. The thermal paper fading to nothing. You saved it for something and now the numbers are ghosts and you can't remember what the something was.

Folded paper develops a memory. Fold it the same way enough times and it wants to go back there. It's easier to close the second time. The crease is a path worn into it.

A map refolded wrong. Everyone has done this. The map that won't go back. You're fighting it at a rest stop in the rain.

The legend is always in the corner you need.

---

I don't know why receipts curl toward the printed side specifically. Toward the ink, or toward where the heat was.

A blank receipt is just a strip of slightly waxy paper. There's nothing on it until something hot touches it.

Carbon paper is a different thing entirely — you push through, you don't heat. The impression goes down through pressure. Three copies, each one lighter. The bottom one barely legible, carbon-gray, faint like something overheard.
