# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The minute hand on a waiting room clock doesn't sweep, it lurches. Each second a small defeat.

---

Somewhere there's a word for the specific orange of a road cone that's been out too long. Faded to almost pink. A construction orange that gave up.

The seam on a tennis ball. You can feel it before you see it. The hand finds it automatically.

Tupperware lids that almost fit. The ones that are clearly *from* something, you just can't remember what. A whole cabinet of them.

Mint condition. What does mint mean there. Something about coins, the facility, the freshness of new metal. But now it just means: untouched. Mint condition action figure still in box. A sadder thing than it sounds.

---

The way a car door sounds different when you close it from inside versus outside. Both hollow but differently hollow.

String cheese peels in one direction. If you go the other way something goes wrong, structurally, and the piece is suddenly wrong.

There's a specific kind of afternoon in early spring where the light goes long and gold at around 5pm and everything gets a yellow tinge and it feels like a different year entirely. Not this year. Some other one.

Dial tone. There are people who've never heard a dial tone in a context where it was just the normal beginning of a phone call. Not a novelty. Just: the sound before the numbers.

A broom closet that's also where the fuse box is.
