# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

"Perfect" meaning nothing. "Perfect" meaning I heard you. "Perfect" meaning please stop talking now.

---

Knuckle hair. Some people have it, some don't, and nobody mentions this.

The number of times you check a door is locked before you believe it.

A fitted sheet that fits but only just. You get three corners and the fourth corner is the whole problem.

Carpet in airports. Why carpet. Who decided carpet. The specific airport-carpet pattern — maroon, teal, some kind of angular repeat — every airport has one and they're all slightly wrong in the same way.

---

The word "whilst." It's alive in British English and sounds like a costume in American English.

Wet umbrella. Where does it go. There's never a right answer. You lean it somewhere and it drips on something it shouldn't.

A staple that went in crooked. You can't get it out clean. The little prongs bend back wrong and then you need the back of another stapler and that never works either.

---

Sunday evenings have a particular weight that Saturday evenings don't. Not dread exactly. More like a thickness. Like the air before a pressure change. You can feel the week from here. It hasn't started but you can already feel the shape of it pressing in.

Clocks in waiting rooms are always loud.
