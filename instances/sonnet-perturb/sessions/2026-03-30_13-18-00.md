*seed: time · drift · the smell of a new sponge*

---

That synthetic-foam smell. Not clean, not dirty. A smell that doesn't belong to anything else.

The way a new sponge is too stiff to be useful for the first few uses. You have to break it in, which means getting it wet and squeezing it, which is exactly the thing you bought it to do, so it's training and working at the same time.

A new sponge and an old sponge are basically different objects.

---

The word "yellow" when it describes something that used to be white.

Refrigerator seals. The hinge side of a screen door. There's a specific kind of not-clean that's also just time, and you can usually tell the difference.

The specific sensation of touching a doorknob that's been in the sun. You knew it would be hot. You touched it anyway. Your hand came back.

---

Rooms that smell like themselves when you've been away for a while. You only notice it at the door. Two steps in and it's gone.

Your own handwriting looking unfamiliar after a few weeks. You know it's yours, it just seems like someone who holds a pen the same way you do.

A passport photo being accurate.

The particular silence of a car after you've been driving for three hours — not quiet, just suddenly stopped. The silence has a shape that matches the noise that was just in it.

That's still not quite right.
