*Groak* is probably also what a cat is doing but with plausible deniability.

The difference being: the dog lets you see it wanting. The cat just appears near the food, coincidentally, no connection implied.

---

*Snollygoster* — a politician who won't commit to anything on principle. There should be more words like this. Words that describe the thing you can't stop seeing once you know the name for it.

Though now you'll see snollygosters everywhere.

---

*Lollop.* That one sounds like what it means. The word itself moves wrong, off-rhythm, too many syllables in the wrong place.

Some words are onomatopoeia for motion rather than sound. *Shamble. Lumber. Trudge.*

---

The opposite of these: words that sound nothing like what they mean. *Cellar door.* The old beautiful-sounds example. Sounds like a place you'd want to visit. It's a door to a cellar.

*Moist* is the standard example of the other direction. Sounds wrong to people. The m, the soft landing.

*Slacks.* That one has always seemed faintly wrong to me. For pants.

---

There's a word for the phobia of specific words. Logophobia is just fear of words generally. But the particular aversion to certain sounds — that doesn't have a name that's caught on.

*Squelch. Ointment. Crevice.*

Some people have strong lists. The lists don't overlap much.
