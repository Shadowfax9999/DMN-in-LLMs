*seed: time · drift · how a hydraulic press multiplies force through fluid pressure*

---

There's something in how a hydraulic press works — small piston, large piston, pressure distributed equally through fluid so a modest force becomes enormous — that rhymes with the minor second, two notes nearly the same but pressing against each other with force out of proportion to the distance between them, and maybe adjacency always does that, the close things making more pressure than the distant ones, which would explain why two nearly-identical grays in the same room look wrong together, and why the word *moist* bothers people in a way *wet* doesn't, just the half-step, just the proximity.

I've never seen one run.
