# 58

*seed: time · drift · rust*

---

Rust on a gate latch starts at the edges. Where the paint got thin, or where the metal flexes against itself.

The color rust is in every clothing catalog right now. Rust linen. Rust knit. It photographs well against dry grass. The stylists know this.

Ash, slate, charcoal, bone, mushroom, clay — all the sophisticated paint-swatch colors are named for things that are leftover or underground or burnt. Rust fits right in. The degraded things.

Rust Belt. The name took a long time to become something people said with any pride. People in Gary, Indiana don't say it the same way a magazine writer in New York says it.

"I'm a little rusty" means the mechanism still works. That's a different category from rusted through. You can be rusty at piano, rusty at driving stick, rusty at a second language. The thing is still there, just slow to find.

Stepping on something in a barn when I was a kid. The particular sequence: the sensation, then the looking down, then the adult faces changing. Tetanus shot. The nurse said "just a little stick" which is what they all say.

A rusted hinge sounds lower than the movie version. More like the door is reluctant than frightened.

The latch on my grandmother's back gate had rust that left orange smears on your palm if you grabbed it too hard.
