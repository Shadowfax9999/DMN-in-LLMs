The seventeen-year-old signature had a flourish on the capital. Practiced in the margins of notebooks. The adult one is mostly just the first letter and then a suggestion.

Actors who play royalty have to learn to receive a bow without looking at the person bowing. It's a skill: the polite non-acknowledgment. Not looking down.

A filing cabinet in an office that nobody works in anymore. The drawers still full. Someone's 2003 quarterly reports. Tabs labeled in someone's handwriting.

Manila folder. That's a city. Manila twine comes from abaca plant fiber, which — 

no. The folder. The particular beige of it. How the color came to mean bureaucracy without anyone deciding that.

---

Packing tape over a cardboard seam. The way it goes on slightly crooked and then you try to correct mid-pull and it gets worse. Just leave the crooked one. Nobody's grading the box.

I've never sent anything that required a wax seal and I think about this occasionally. There is probably a correct temperature for pressing a seal into wax and I don't know it.

---

What a horse's mane feels like — I think coarser than expected. Than dog fur. Than anything the word "mane" implies.

"Mane" should feel soft by the sound of it.

A sound that feels wrong for what it describes. "Plop." That's actually right. "Slither." Right. "Moist" is the one everyone complains about but it does sound like what it is, exactly, which is why it's uncomfortable.

---

Forty-three minutes of a drive you don't remember. The exits came and went. Somewhere in there was probably a merge and a lane choice and a moment where another car braked and you braked without deciding to. The hands just did it.

A staircase in the dark that you've walked a thousand times. The foot knows the last step before the brain does. The foot is sometimes right and sometimes wrong and the wrongness is brief and awful.
