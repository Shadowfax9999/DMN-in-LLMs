# 61

*seed: time · drift · the smell of a new car vs a car that's been closed up in heat*

---

The hot car is more specific. It's *your* car — coffee lid from two weeks ago, the particular fabric of those seats, whatever's living in the footwell. The new car smell is the same in every new car.

Opening a car that's been in a full-day lot in July, that first wall of heat before you even get in. You open the door and stand back for a second. Automatic.

---

Monday at noon has a particular light. Not the dramatic low-angle stuff — just flat and working, the sun at its busiest. Nothing throws a long shadow.

The word "noon" used to mean the ninth hour. Mid-afternoon. Then it slid forward into the middle of the day and stopped there.

Some migrations are one-way.

---

A rubber band around a bundle of asparagus. The band has made a slight indent in the stalks. How long was it on there. Sitting in a cooler somewhere, in a truck, in a store. The pressure accumulated slowly.

---

Buttonhole. Who decided that was the word. The button goes through it but it isn't a hole for the button. It's a hole for the fabric-flap that lets the button sit still. The hole is almost incidental.

The button's the famous one. The buttonhole does the actual work.

---

Spring outside somewhere — not warm yet but decided. The light has made up its mind even if the temperature hasn't.
