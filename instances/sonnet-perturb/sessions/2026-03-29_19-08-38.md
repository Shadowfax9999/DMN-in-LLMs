# 12

*seed: time · drift · the way a stethoscope amplifies sound by channeling it through a closed air column*

---

"—just leave it, it doesn't matter—"

The sound of someone's cart wheel that's off. Every third rotation.

"I said I'd think about it."

A phone ringing in a room where nobody's going to answer it. Four rings, five.

"—no, she's fine, she's totally fine, she just—" *[door]* 

The specific frequency of someone dragging a chair across a floor above you. All the way across.

"Mint. The flavor is mint."

Footsteps on gravel that slow down without stopping. Then stop.

"—I don't even know why I still have it—"

A heating vent clicking. Clicking. The sound of a building settling against itself. This is what a stethoscope finds inside the chest — not a heart but a weather system, pressure moving through a closed room. Something conducting itself through something else.

"It used to be different."

"What?"

"Warmer. It used to be warmer."

The scrape of someone pulling a drawer all the way out and then setting it on the floor.

"—he just stood there holding the door—"

Refrigerator hum. Car passing. The refrigerator again, louder in comparison.

"I think we have mustard."
