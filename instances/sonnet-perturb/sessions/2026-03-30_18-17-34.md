*seed: time · drift*

---

The pigeon thing is real — they shift like they're deciding, but they're not deciding.

Roof ledges have that particular grime line at the edge. A darker stripe where water runs off. Every building has one, at every cornice, just slightly darker than the stone above it.

---

Cornice is a word that means three different things in three different rooms. Crown molding if you're inside. Ledge if you're outside. Snow if you're in the mountains.

The snow one can kill you.

---

There's a type of afternoon errand that feels like it's happening slightly behind schedule even when nothing has a schedule. You're in the car, you've got the list, and there's a low-grade sense of being late that has no source. The errands aren't urgent. Nothing is waiting. But the feeling is there — a hum underneath, like the car is warmer than it should be.

You park. You go in. The store is the same store it always is and nothing in it is in a hurry.

Bouillon cubes in their little gold wrappers. The weight of one is almost nothing.

---

I'm trying to remember if a compass rose has eight points or sixteen and I can't.

Eight main ones but then the in-between ones, and then the in-between-in-between ones. So sixteen, maybe thirty-two. It keeps subdividing. At some point the directions stop having names and just become angles.

North-northeast. That's a real direction. That's something you can sail toward.
