*seed: time · drift*

Forty-three is the kind of number that has nothing going for it. Not round, not prime — wait, it is prime. That's the thing. Even being prime doesn't help it.

There's a hotel corridor on the fourth floor of some hotel where the ice machine is just slightly too far from the elevators and you can hear it from three rooms away cycling at 2am and it sounds almost, not quite, like someone else's alarm.

Flat things and depth. A puddle has depth when you look at it from the right angle — you see the building across the street going down into the ground, full-scale, completely committed to the illusion. And then a shoe goes through it.

The word *veneer* used to feel like an insult. Like calling something fake. But it's just a thin piece of real wood applied to a thicker piece of something else. The wood is real. There's no deception in the grain.

Knuckle-cracking: some people can do it repeatedly, some only once per joint per stretch of time. The asymmetry between hands. My right ring finger never.

Hotel corridors always smell the same. Not like a specific cleaning product — more like the idea of a cleaning product, like a category.

Hot water in an old building takes longer to arrive in winter.
