# 3

*seed: time · drift · interval (music)*

---

The interval isn't the notes. It's the distance between them. You can measure it — a third, a fifth — but measuring doesn't tell you why a minor third sounds like it's about to cry and a major third doesn't.

A perfect fifth is hollow in a specific way. Sounds like a bell that hasn't been rung yet.

---

The word *interval* works in music and also in running ("take intervals") and also in theaters ("fifteen-minute interval") and also in mathematics and also just generally — a gap between two things. Most words don't do that. Most words stay in their lane.

*Rest* does it too. Medical, musical, structural.

*Pitch.*

*Key.*

*Scale.*

All of music's words are borrowed from elsewhere. Like the vocabulary was filled in secondhand, things pressed into service.

---

The sleeping dog's nails clicking on nothing — that's REM. The whole body convinced of a world that isn't there. Not the paw moving, exactly. The dog running in it.

A dog's sense of time is supposed to be bad but that seems wrong based on nothing specific, just a feeling. The way a dog knows when it's 5pm.

My grandmother's kitchen had a clock that ticked and you only noticed it when the room got quiet. Then it was enormous. Then you couldn't stop.

The interval between ticks. Also an interval. Also measurable.

A minor second is almost dissonant enough to be wrong. Two notes leaning against each other. You hold them long enough and they start to sound fine.
