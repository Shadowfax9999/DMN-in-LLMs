*seed: time · drift*

The foil wrapper is the issue. They make it too tight. You're already in a hurry because the broth is boiling and you need the cube now, not in forty-five seconds of peeling.

Smoke alarms have a little green light that blinks once a minute. Proof that something is awake.

The shuffle at the baggage carousel keeps happening. Happens at elevators too. The doors open and people arc backward to let people off, even when there are no people to let off. The arc happens anyway. Pre-emptive courtesy for a crowd that didn't materialize.

Red meaning stop and red meaning emergency and red meaning love. The same color told to do all of that.

Vestments have specific names for specific pieces and none of them sound like English. Chasuble. Alb. Cincture. A whole vocabulary that belongs only to the getting-dressed-for-Mass sequence and nowhere else. There must be someone whose job it is to know all of them, to iron the right one.

The iron. The ironing board that folds back into the wall in older houses. Springs so strong the thing could take your arm off if you weren't careful about the latch.

Latches that need two hands.

The light goes on when you're hit and the referee doesn't have to decide. The referee still has to decide some things. The touch on the arm that doesn't count. The argument afterward. Even wired certainty has edges that blur.
