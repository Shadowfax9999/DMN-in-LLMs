# 66

*seed: time · drift · lint in a pocket*

---

Lint in a pocket. The particular accumulation of it — gray even when the fabric isn't gray.

A pocket that's been used. A pocket that's been empty a long time. Those are different pockets.

---

The word *accumulate* is doing something.

No — just, there are things that build up without being added. Lint. Sediment. The number of times you've taken a particular route. At some point the route is just the route.

---

Pockets in jeans versus pockets in a coat. A coat pocket can hold something for months. You reach in in November and there's a receipt from August, a pen you forgot, something wrapped in foil that you can't identify. Time travel, but only one direction and only by accident.

The foil thing. You don't unwrap it.

---

My grandmother kept tissues in her sleeve. Not a pocket. The sleeve itself, folded back once at the wrist. I don't know when that stopped being a thing people did.

---

A pocket protector is its own kind of pocket inside a pocket.

---

There's a word — *fob* — that used to mean a small pocket, specifically for a watch. Now it means the remote key for a car. The pocket shrank until it was just the thing that used to be in it.

---

Change at the bottom of a bag versus change in a pocket. The bag change is feral. The pocket change has somewhere to be.
