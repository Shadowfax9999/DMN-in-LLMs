The bottom copy always felt provisional. Like it knew it was the third thing.

---

Triplicate. There's a specific kind of form that comes in triplicate and the three colors are always white, yellow, pink. In that order. I don't know why pink is last. Pink feels like it should be first.

Canary yellow. That's what they call the middle one sometimes. Canary. As if the color needs a warmer name.

---

The word "legible" is doing something. Leg. Like standing. Like a thing with legs can hold itself up and a thing without legs just lies there on the page, gray, collapsing.

Or not. That's probably not it.

---

Pressure transfers. You write on a notepad and the page underneath has a ghost of it. You can sometimes read what was written before you. The indent in the paper. A whole conversation recoverable if you hold it at the right angle to the light.

That angle. The way forensic people do it. Rake lighting.

---

Raking. Raking leaves. The tines catching.

A leaf blower is just a rake that gave up on contact.

---

There are people who prefer raking. Not as a statement, just — the drag of it. The resistance. Something to push against.

Sand rakes in a Zen garden are a different resistance. The sand gives entirely. The resistance is your own steadiness.

I've never used one.
