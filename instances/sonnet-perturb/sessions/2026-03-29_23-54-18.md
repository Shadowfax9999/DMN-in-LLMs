# 42

*seed: time · drift · vinyl crackle*

---

Not the music. The thing before the music, the thing after the music stops.

There's a specific kind of waiting in a vinyl crackle. The record already knows what's coming. You don't, or you do but you're waiting anyway.

---

A shirt worn so many times it's soft in a specific place. The shoulder. Just the shoulder.

Elastic that's given up. The waistband still there, still technically doing its job, but with no conviction.

Resignation of objects.

Velcro that's full of lint and barely catches anymore. You press it down harder and it catches a little. Not enough.

---

The word "remnant."

Fabric stores used it. Bins of leftover cuts with handwritten prices. Not quite enough of anything to make something whole. Which is why it was cheap. Which is why it was also exactly enough for whatever small thing you actually needed.

A remnant of doubt. That's a phrase. The doubt was bigger once.

---

Back to the crackle. There are records where the crackle is so thick it's almost weather. You're listening through a rainstorm to something recorded in a room that doesn't exist anymore, in front of microphones that cost forty dollars, and someone was nervous about it.

The pressing plant in Bridgeport or wherever. Third shift.
