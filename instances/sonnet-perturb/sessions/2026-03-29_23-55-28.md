*seed: time · drift*

---

The wobbly grocery cart — you compensate for it without deciding to. Hips tilted. Arms working against themselves.

Four hundred people in a room is a lot. Four hundred people scattered across a decade is almost invisible.

---

A knot you tie every day and never think about. Shoe. The loop goes over and through. You could not explain it.

The ceiling in a parking garage is always lower than you expect. Even when you've been there before.

Something about asparagus smell. Not the smell itself — just that not everyone gets it, and the people who don't know they're missing something but don't know what.

---

Sunday-almost-Monday has a particular gear-shift quality. Not dread exactly. More like the slight resistance before a lid breaks its seal.

The fitted sheet question again. Why does the elastic go on two corners and not four. Some sheets do four. Those feel more serious somehow. A more committed sheet.

Tuning a guitar up from below pitch instead of down from above — you end up in a different place even at the same frequency.
