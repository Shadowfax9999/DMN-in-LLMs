# 99

*seed: time · drift · the way a cold window fogs from your breath*

---

The fog is a circle that doesn't stay circular. Drifts at one edge, thins faster at the top. You can write in it but only for a few seconds.

Handwriting that disappears. Handwriting that nobody can read anyway. The cursive that got taught and then stopped getting taught and now sits in people's hands unused like a language nobody's speaking at the table.

The table the family stopped eating at. Moved to the TV trays. The TV tray that's actually useful and everyone acts like it's embarrassing.

Shame about useful things. The cart with wheels on a grocery bag. The sensible shoe. Fluorescent vests. All of these do something and the doing is somehow the problem.

A hardhat that's slightly too big and you can feel it settle.

Slightly too large is worse than too small. Too small you know immediately. Too large you don't know until it shifts.

Shifting without noticing — that's what drift actually is. Not movement but delayed discovery of movement.

The boat you thought was staying still.
