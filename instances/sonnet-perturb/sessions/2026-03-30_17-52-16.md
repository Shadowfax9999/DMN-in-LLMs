# 90

*seed: time · drift · foxing*

---

Foxing. The brown spots. Old paper going back to something.

Also: a fox making the hounds lose the scent, doubling back, crossing water. The word doing two jobs and neither one looks like the other.

The paper kind is a stain that comes from inside. Not spilled on. Not burned. Just the paper deciding, slowly, over decades, to mark itself.

There's a word for wood doing something similar. Checking — when it splits along the grain from drying out. A board checks. A fox foxes. Intransitive.

---

Brown paper bags have that smell. Something about the inside of the bag specifically, not the outside. You have to push your face in a little.

The word "musty" is doing a lot of work and not doing it very well.

---

A fox at the edge of a field, very still, looking at something in the grass. Not the direction you'd expect. Everything about the posture says *I am not about to do anything* and then it does.

---

The receipt is still under the table leg. Probably soft now, from humidity. Probably the ink on the back is spreading a little, foxing into the paper fiber. Holding the table up while quietly becoming illegible.
