The napkin method works but it always feels temporary, like an admission that something is slightly wrong with the world and you've chosen to live with it rather than fix it.

Sugar packets too. Or a matchbook, in places that still have matchbooks.

The word "shim." Very satisfying. Does exactly what it describes.

---

A door that doesn't quite latch unless you lift the handle slightly while closing it. Every person who lives in that house learns this without thinking about it. A visitor always fails. The sound of the door bouncing back open is a specific sound, not alarming, just indicating — you're not from here.

Houses have these small initiations. The light switch that's in the wrong place. The shower that takes exactly forty seconds to go warm. The step that's shorter than the others.

The short step. That's almost always the top one or the bottom one. The one where the builder ran out of exactly the right amount. Your foot knows before you do and when it's wrong your whole body lurches.

Proprioception just failing for half a second. The body's confidence undermined.

Reaching for a glass in the dark and it's not where you put it.
