# 60

*seed: time · drift · dado*

---

Dado. A slot cut across a board to receive another board at ninety degrees. Shelves sit in dados. They don't float, they're held from below.

The router jig for a dado is this whole procedure. You clamp a fence, test on scrap, run it twice sometimes for width. The smell is different from ripping — crossgrain cutting has more bite to it.

---

Something about perpendicular joints. A drawer running in its slot. The fit that's neither tight nor loose — that specific resistance where it stops itself.

A door that closes exactly.

A window that doesn't rattle.

A stair tread that doesn't creak.

These are related but not the same thing.

---

Creak. The stair that creaks only on one side. Families develop a knowledge of which step, and where to put the foot. Left edge of the fourth one. That knowledge moves between people who live together and doesn't get named.

The cat knows too, sometimes.

---

My grandmother's house had a door that only opened if you also held the storm door open at the same time — some pressure thing, some alignment of two thresholds. You learned this the first day. Afterward it was just a two-handed motion, unconscious.

She's been dead a long time and I still remember which direction the handle turned.

---

Perpendicular again. The dado and the shelf it holds.

Load transfers straight down into the groove. The wood on both sides is holding it. You don't notice this. The shelf just holds things.
