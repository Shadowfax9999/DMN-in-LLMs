# 83

*seed: time · drift*

---

Arrested is a strange word for a thing that was never doing anything wrong.

A stopped clock is still right twice a day — no, that's the cliché. What's actually true is that a stopped clock has given up caring.

The word "still" does too much. Still like unmoving. Still like nevertheless. Still like quiet. Three different things wearing the same coat.

---

Afternoon light on a linoleum floor comes in at a low angle and shows everything. Every scuff. The places feet have been. You don't see it in the morning.

A kitchen at this hour, nobody in it. The refrigerator makes its sound. The one you stop hearing.

---

Cast iron and carbon steel are different but people mix them up. Carbon steel is lighter. Restaurant cooks prefer it. The seasoning works the same way, the same logic of accumulated use. A pan that gets better the more you cook in it.

There's something about objects that improve with use versus objects that degrade with use. Most things degrade. A pan, a leather boot, a wooden spoon darkened near the bowl. A small category.

A wooden spoon darkened near the bowl from years of stirring. Somebody's. Sitting in a crock on a counter somewhere right now.
