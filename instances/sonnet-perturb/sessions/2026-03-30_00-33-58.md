# 49

*seed: time · drift · a book with someone else's bookmark still in it*

---

A grocery receipt, usually. Or a torn envelope flap. Sometimes a photo — and then you've stopped reading entirely.

The stranger's page number is its own information. 47. They got 47 pages in and left it there, or they finished and forgot to take it back out, or they never opened it again after page 47 and now here it is in a thrift store bin. You don't know which. The receipt doesn't tell you. It just says: eggs, milk, something illegible, $14.83.

Holding two lives at once without knowing either of them.

---

A name written inside the front cover in careful handwriting. *For Margaret, Christmas 1974.* Margaret is a full person and you have nothing else. You don't know if she liked it. You don't know if she read it at all or if she thought it was presumptuous of whoever gave it — this gift that says *here is a thing I think you should read*, which is a particular kind of intimacy that doesn't always land right.

Margaret might have hated it.

---

The smell of old paperbacks is specific. Not all old paper — just that particular softcover smell, something vanilla-adjacent but also a little close, like a drawer that's been shut for years. You know it the moment you open one.

"Pumice" does something real in the mouth — that was the thread from before. The mouth-feel of words. "Margaret" is a solid word. Four syllables but it takes up space differently than four syllables usually do. "Felicity" is four syllables and it floats. Margaret does not float.

---

A bus transfer as bookmark means they were reading on the bus. Which means they were in a particular seat on a particular route holding this exact book and now you're holding it and the bus transfer is still warm from their hand, except it isn't, it's been years, but the mind supplies the warmth anyway without being asked.

The photo is the hardest one. You look at it longer than you should. A dog, probably. Or two people squinting into sun. You can't put it back without feeling something.
