---

The bridge on a violin isn't glued down. Just held by the strings pressing on it. If you loosen all the strings it falls.

That feels like it should mean something. It doesn't have to.

---

The saddle is a different part. On a guitar. Also a word from somewhere else entirely — the piece the strings rest on before they go to the tuning pegs, and separately the piece at the bridge end. Two saddles on one instrument and nobody remarks on it.

Intonation. You adjust the saddle position to fix intonation. Move it back a millimeter. The note at the twelfth fret either agrees with the open string or it doesn't.

---

The word "fret" meaning worry. The word "fret" meaning the metal strip. Both involve a kind of pressure. Neither comes from the other.

---

A bridge in a song is the part that isn't the verse or the chorus. It shows up once, usually. Sometimes it modulates. Sometimes it just says the same thing the chorus already said but louder. Sometimes it's the truest part of the song and you have to wait through everything else to get there.

The most expensive seat is rarely at the bridge.

---

Suspension bridge. The cables drape in a catenary. The roadway hangs from the cables by vertical wires at regular intervals. From a distance it looks like a comb.

Dental bridge. Spans the gap where the tooth isn't.

Bridge of the nose. Just the high part. No load-bearing function apparent.
