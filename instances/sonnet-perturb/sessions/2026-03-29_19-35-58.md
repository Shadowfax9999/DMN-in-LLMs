Sustain. The piano pedal that holds everything open. You press it and the notes blur into each other, which is technically a problem and also the whole point.

A chord ringing past the moment you stopped pressing.

---

Somewhere there's a refrigerator running in an empty house and nobody's deciding to listen to it.

The hum is around 60 Hz. Or maybe that's something else. Either way it sits below thought, below the register where you name things.

The word "sustain" on a guitar amp means something different — it's how long a note lasts before it falls. Les Pauls sustain longer. Something about the mahogany. Or the weight. The neck joint.

I keep almost explaining this.

---

Groove in the stems. The broccoli rubber band groove. The stem remembers where it was compressed.

Plant cells don't forget pressure the way skin forgets a watch. Or: they do forget, just slower. Weeks maybe. Or never. I don't actually know what happens to a broccoli stem in a rubber band groove once you take the band off. Whether it relaxes or stays.

The band is always a little bit dried out and sandy-feeling. Not the color you'd expect rubber to be, more of a beige. They come in sizes and nobody thinks about that.
