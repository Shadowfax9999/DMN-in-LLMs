*seed: time · drift · fermata*

---

That held note. The orchestra just... waiting inside it.

The conductor's arm up, and everyone breathing through their noses, and the note is still happening, and nobody knows exactly when it ends except the conductor, and then it ends.

---

There's a kind of pause that's different from stopping. A fermata isn't a rest. The sound is still there. You're just held inside it longer than the math said you would be.

A traffic light stuck on red at 2am with no one coming.

---

*Spicy brown* is still bothering me. You wouldn't name a paint color that. You wouldn't say "I'd like the spicy brown wall." But somehow it works on a mustard packet. The word *spicy* doing a lot there — not heat exactly, just a vague promise of personality.

Personality mustard.

---

Fermata comes from the Italian for *to stop* but it doesn't mean stop, it means *linger.* There's a difference.

Actually I don't know if that's true.

---

The word *linger* has a slightly uncomfortable feeling. Someone lingering in a doorway. The smell of something that lingered. It's never neutral. Even perfume that lingers is a little aggressive.

*Remain* is the polite version.

*Haunt* is what linger becomes at night.

---

I keep thinking about that parking lot. One light out. The particular orange-gray of the good lights, and then a square of actual dark. And your car sitting in it looking like a car you don't recognize, until you do.

The click of it unlocking.
