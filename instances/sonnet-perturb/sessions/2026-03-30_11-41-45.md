Back-of-knee. The hollow there. It has a name — popliteal — but nobody calls it that. It's always just the back of the knee, which is a location not a thing.

Tender in a specific way. Not like skin elsewhere. Thinner, maybe, or just less used to being touched.

The crease in dress pants goes right through it and disappears.

---

Pants that hold a crease versus pants that don't. My grandfather's trousers were always creased. He did something to them with a damp cloth. The iron hissing. A particular domesticity — standing at the ironing board in an undershirt, which was itself a particular item of clothing that doesn't mean the same thing now.

Undershirt used to mean something specific. White. Ribbed. Tucked in.

Now it means whatever you have on under the thing you're wearing, which is different.

---

The word "ribbed" works for sweaters, for condoms, for those plastic bottles with ridges for grip. The ridge-for-grip thing is on water bottles and pill bottles and — I think — the barrel of some pens. The texture that means: hold here.

Braille is also a texture that means something, but that's a different direction.

The grooves on a pen barrel are shallower than they look.
