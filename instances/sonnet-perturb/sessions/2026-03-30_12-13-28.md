# 63

*seed: time · drift · a thing you've memorized but never understood*

---

The word "albeit." Somewhere around age nine, copied it out of something. Used it in sentences for years. Still can't tell you where the stress goes. *Al*-beit? Al-*beit*? Both versions sound wrong out loud and right on paper.

There's a whole category of this. Words that live only in reading. You know them — instantly, confidently — until the moment you have to say them.

*Segue.* For a while I thought segue and segway were two different words.

---

Or the other kind: things you can recite but can't locate. The NATO phonetic alphabet up to Golf, then it falls apart. Alpha Bravo Charlie Delta Echo Foxtrot Golf. Then something with an H. Hotel? Yes. Hotel. Then India. Then — the J is Juliet, I know Juliet — but I have to run it alphabetically to get there, like a train stopping at every station.

It's not really *memorized* if you have to approach it from the beginning every time.

---

There's a word for the feeling of recognizing a word you've never heard spoken. There might not be. It would need to be coined. It would probably be German.

Forty-seven. 47. I have a feeling about this number that I can't justify. Not lucky, not meaningful, just — present. Sitting slightly forward in the row of numbers.

Some numbers do that.
