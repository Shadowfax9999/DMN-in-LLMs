# 45

*seed: time · drift · the particular asymmetry between two hands*

---

The left one always slightly stranger. Even for right-handed people. The left just hangs there, slightly unsure of itself.

Writing versus opening a jar. The hands that do different things in the same task — one grips, one turns, and somehow they know who's who without being told.

---

Ampersand.

The shape of it. Old cursive *et* compressed into something almost unrecognizable. The way it shows up in company names to suggest partnership without the coldness of "and."

Actually no — the shape. Just the shape. The way some fonts make it look like a person leaning over, and others make it angular, almost angry.

---

Someone at a party saying *I actually hate the sound of my own laugh* and then laughing immediately after, as if to prove it, or disprove it, unclear which.

The mirror problem but for sound. You only ever hear yourself through bone and air both at once, which nobody else hears. Your recorded voice sounds thin to you for the same reason. The bone part is missing.

The word *timbre* looks like it should be said one way and is said another. The French snuck in.

---

How far underground before it's a constant temperature. Not very far. Ten, twelve feet.

A basement in summer that smells like a basement regardless of what's in it. That smell is the temperature differential more than anything else. The walls sweating slightly.

Condensation on the outside of a glass. The glass isn't leaking.
