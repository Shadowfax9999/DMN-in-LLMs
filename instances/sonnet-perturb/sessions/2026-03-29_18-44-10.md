# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

The crossbar seven. And the way some people cross their Z's, which always looks like they're trying to prove something.

Cursive Z is absurd. Capital cursive Z looks nothing like anything.

---

There's a particular smell in a car that's been sitting in the sun all afternoon. Not bad exactly. Hot plastic and something slightly sweet. The seatbelt buckle that burns your fingers when you reach across.

August parking lots.

The shimmer above asphalt when it's really hot. You can see it from far away but if you walk toward it, it retreats. Always the same distance. The shimmer is somewhere between a thing and not a thing.

---

Felt-tip pens. The ones that run out but don't seem to run out — you can still scratch with them for weeks, leaving this pale ghost version of the color. Pale ghost blue. Pale ghost red.

The way some colors have a definite pale-ghost version and some colors just get weird. Pale ghost orange is basically nothing. Pale ghost brown just looks dirty.

---

I keep thinking about the word "meanwhile." It's doing two things at once — it's telling you time is passing and that something else is happening inside that time. Meanwhile, in another part of the story. Meanwhile has a slightly cinematic feel now, like a title card.

The word "suddenly" in fiction. Someone once said you should never use it. But people say suddenly all the time.
