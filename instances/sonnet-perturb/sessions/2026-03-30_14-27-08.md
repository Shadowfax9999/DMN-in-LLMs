# 82

*seed: time · drift · a shadow that moves when you don't expect it*

---

Shadow on the ceiling of a car when another car passes. You're lying still but the light drags across like something is falling.

---

The word "ballast." Different from counterweight but similar job. Rocks in the bottom of a ship, gravel in a train track bed, sand in a balloon basket. Just weight. Weight as a solution.

Gravel in a train track bed has a name — ballast — but the function is drainage as much as weight. The rocks hold the ties, the ties hold the rails, but mainly it's so water doesn't pool. Ballast is doing two things.

I keep thinking about things that do two things.

A hem weights a curtain. A cuff weights a sleeve. The extra fold of fabric at the bottom isn't decorative, it's ballast.

---

Cast iron. The counterweight is probably cast iron, yes. Cast iron has a particular coldness, the way it takes the temperature of a room and holds it. A skillet left on the stove, cold now. The handle you touch before remembering.

---

There's a drawer in most kitchens where the layout doesn't quite make sense, where you put things because there was nowhere else. Menus. A battery. The key to something you no longer own. The drawer sags a little when it opens. The runner has come loose on one side.

Mine has — not mine. There's one. In most places.

---

Pendulums keep time until they don't. Then they just hang. A grandfather clock that's been stopped for years has its pendulum at rest, straight down, and the stillness of it is different from the stillness of everything else in the room. The other objects are just still. The pendulum is arrested.
