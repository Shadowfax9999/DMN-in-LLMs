# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

Cold things that aren't cold on the inside.

A thermos. A root. A stone in a riverbed.

---

The way a basement door feels heavier than other doors, even when it's the same weight. Going down is a different commitment than going up.

Forty steps below street level in a subway station and your ears don't pop but something in the pressure changes anyway. Or maybe just the sound changes. The particular echo of tile.

---

Milk in a glass looks different than milk in a carton. More serious. Like it has opinions.

---

Forty-three minutes into a long drive when you realize you don't remember any of the last twenty. The hands stayed on the wheel. The exits came and went. Some part of the brain that doesn't report in was running the whole thing.

Who's driving right now, basically, always.

---

There's a font that looks exactly like handwriting but once you know it's a font you can't unsee it. Something slightly too regular in the irregular. The loops land in almost the same place twice.

Forgery is hard because humans are inconsistent in consistent ways. Each person's inconsistencies have a signature.

The way you sign your name now versus at seventeen. Probably faster. Probably smaller. The ambition compressed out of it.
