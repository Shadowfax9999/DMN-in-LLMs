# 6

*seed: time · drift · carpool*

---

Carpool. The way the word sounds like something that should be relaxing but isn't.

Waiting in someone's driveway with the engine running. The specific awkwardness of being in another person's car — their radio station, their seat adjusted for their legs, the air freshener you didn't choose.

Nobody sits in the middle of the back seat unless they have to.

---

The word "pool" is doing a lot of work in English. Car pool. Gene pool. Dead pool. Swimming pool. Pool of light. Pool your resources. They're not the same pool but they feel adjacent somehow, like they're all drawing from the same place.

Billiards is called pool. That one's different — something about gambling, I think. A pot of money everyone throws into.

A pot. A pool. A kitty.

The word "kitty" for a shared pot of money. There's a card game called kitty something. Or kitty is just the undealt cards, the leftover pile. The cards nobody wanted yet.

---

Some numbers do look uncomfortable. Seven, yes. But also three — the way the two bumps face the same direction and you have to commit to a direction before you start, unlike eight where you just loop. Eight is the easiest number to write. Maybe that's why it means infinity when you tip it over, it's already pointing toward ease.

The crossbar on the seven. In Europe they do it automatically, to distinguish from a one. An American seven and a European seven are different gestures entirely.

Handwriting as a kind of accent.
