The tea thing is true of soup too. There's a narrower window with soup.

A spoon left in a cup changes something. Even if the tea is still hot. Something about the metal in there.

---

Grout between tiles. The color it starts as versus the color it becomes. Nobody talks about that transition — it's just slow and then it's the new normal. Bathroom tiles that were trendy once. Pink. Avocado. Harvest gold. All of those bathrooms still exist somewhere, fully functional, just waiting.

The word "avocado" applied to a refrigerator.

A refrigerator that still works. Older than most of the people who open it.

---

There's a kind of shim you put under a wobbly table leg. Usually a folded receipt. The receipt from something months ago. Suddenly functional again. Preserved.

Paper's tensile strength is weird. You can fold a sheet of paper enough times that you can't fold it anymore. Eight times approximately. But in the other direction — pulling along the grain — it's actually pretty strong. Not noticeably strong. Just more than you'd expect from paper.

A paper cut from a piece of paper that didn't matter at all. The most ordinary sheet. The betrayal is proportional to how mundane the source was.

---

Monday afternoon specifically has a light that Tuesday doesn't. The week is still real on Monday afternoon. There are still enough days for things to turn around.
