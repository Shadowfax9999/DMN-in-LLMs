Things that look like mistakes but aren't:

The angled pickup.
The way a door in an old house doesn't quite hang plumb.
Intentional fading in denim.
Venetian red.

---

A door that doesn't hang plumb might actually be a mistake. Hard to know from the outside.

The word "plumb" comes from lead. The plumb bob. You drop it and gravity tells you what's straight. There's something in that — using a falling thing to find the fixed thing.

The fixed thing being a concept, not a physical line anywhere in the room.

---

Venetian red is a pigment. Slightly orange-brown. Not what you'd expect from the name. I keep thinking it should be deeper, more cardinal. The name pulls in one direction and the actual color sits somewhere else.

The gap between the name and the thing.

Celadon. That's another one. The word is cool and glassy and the color is — it's close, actually. Celadon almost earns it.

Vermillion does not earn it.

---

The drummer leaning forward. I keep coming back to that. Nobody saying anything. The tempo is a social fact and everyone agrees to it without speaking and then everyone agrees to the new tempo without speaking and the moment of change is both obvious and deniable, you could claim afterward it was always that fast.
