# 24

*seed: time · drift · how a theremin turns hand distance into pitch*

---

A tan line from a watch. Pale strip at a wrist. A record of years.

A theremin makes pitch from proximity — hands wave near two antennas, and distance becomes sound. No touch. Just: near, not near. Players learn to hold very still in sustained notes, because any small movement changes pitch. A discipline of hovering.

A heron has this. Total stillness above water, then — not there anymore.

Hospital bracelets leave a ghost too. Papery, printed with a name or a bar code, a color for risk category. Red for penicillin. Yellow for falls. You tear one off and your wrist looks wrong for a day.

Sometimes a hand goes to a wrist, finds skin, moves on.

"Fall risk." A judgment about how well a body knows its own position in space — how much it trusts a floor. Proprioception failing somewhere. You reach for a doorframe.
