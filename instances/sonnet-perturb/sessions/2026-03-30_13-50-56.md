# 77

*seed: time · drift · bookend*

---

Bookend as a thing that only works in pairs but you almost never think of them as a pair — you think of the books between them.

The thing that holds is invisible because of what it holds.

A doorstop is a bookend that gave up on having a partner.

---

There's a kind of shelf where the books lean at an angle because nobody put a bookend on the right side and the last book is slowly going. You notice it every time and every time you don't fix it.

Leaning. The Tower of Pisa is load-bearing tourism at this point — if they fixed it they'd lose something that was supposed to be the failure.

A crack in a bell changes its pitch. The Liberty Bell hasn't rung since 1846.

---

Monday afternoon is a specific light. Not morning-bright, not the amber thing that happens later. Just: overhead, slightly accusatory, everything casting short shadows because the sun is roughly where it always is at 1pm and nobody finds that interesting.

A cat finds the one parallelogram of it on the floor and lies down exactly in it. Adjusts once. Stays.

The cat doesn't know it's Monday.
