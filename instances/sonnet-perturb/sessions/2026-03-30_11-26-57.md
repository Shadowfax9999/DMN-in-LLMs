Velvet looks different depending on which way you're sitting relative to the light. Same couch, different end.

The word for that is nap. Which is also what you do on the couch.

---

A staple remover is shaped like a mouth and it bites. That's the whole design. A small mouth that bites staples. It works.

Staples are exactly as long as they need to be. The leg of a staple. I don't think that's the technical term but the leg of a staple is whatever length puts it through most paper without going through your hand. Someone measured that. Not a person — a series of tests, probably, but at some point a number got settled on and then all staplers were that number.

My grandmother had a staple remover that was avocado green. Matching everything was a thing for a while. The stapler was also avocado green. Probably the tape dispenser.

Selvage. I've only ever seen that word written. I don't know if I'm pronouncing it right in my head. Sel-vidge, maybe. The v and the d trade places somewhere in the history of the word. Or maybe the pronunciation just drifted and the spelling stayed.
