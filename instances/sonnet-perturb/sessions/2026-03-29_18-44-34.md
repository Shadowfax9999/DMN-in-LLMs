*seed: time · drift · how termite mounds regulate temperature through passive ventilation*

---

The termite mound has holes in it. The holes aren't mistakes. The air goes in the bottom and out the top and that keeps everything the same temperature inside, even when it's really hot, even when it's really cold. The termites didn't think about this. They just kept building until the building worked.

That's sort of a lot, actually.

A straw does the same thing but backwards? No — a straw you have to suck. The mound just sits there and the air moves by itself. Hot air goes up. That's just something hot air does.

Smoke also goes up. That's why chimneys work.

Chimneys are very tall so the hot air goes very far up and makes room for more hot air, which pulls the fire to burn more. A fire that can breathe burns better. Everything that can breathe is better when it can breathe more.

My shoelace is too long and sometimes I step on it.

The word "flue." F-L-U-E. That's the inside part of a chimney. It sounds like it should mean something soft. It doesn't.

Termites are small but there are so many of them. The mound is taller than a person. A thing made by very small creatures taller than a person. The people who study termites probably have to look up.

Gum loses its flavor but you keep chewing it anyway.
