*seed: time · drift · staccato*

---

Staccato. The word itself is the opposite of what it means — all those vowels running together, *staccato*, smooth in the mouth, a long word for short notes.

A piano teacher tapping the student's wrist to make the notes stop.

---

There's a gas station on the corner of a road I used to drive that had a light out in one of the pump bay signs. So it read UNLE ED. For years. UNLE ED. I kept waiting for someone to fix it.

Some lights flicker before they go. Some just stop.

The sound a fluorescent tube makes in the second before it dies — not quite a hiss, not quite a tick.

---

Staccato rain on a skylight is different from staccato rain on a car roof. The skylight transmits. The car roof contains. You're inside the percussion in a car.

I've been in a car during hail and it was the loudest thing that has ever happened to me.

After, walking around to look at the dents. Each one a small dome, a depression, a record. The hood looked like someone had been at it with a ballpeen hammer in a very patient mood.

---

*Patient mood.* 

Patient as in waiting. Patient as in someone in a hospital. Same word.

The etymology is probably — no.

The checkout line at a pharmacy is its own specific atmosphere. People looking at reading glasses. A rack of things that are technically candy but no one is happy about.
