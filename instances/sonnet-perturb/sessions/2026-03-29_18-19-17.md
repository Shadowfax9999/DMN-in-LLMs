*seed: time*

---

The word "dusk" doesn't sound like what it is. Too soft. Dusk is actually a hard-edged thing — you can see exactly where it is in the sky, a line.

Threshold used to mean the part of a doorway you had to thresh grain over. Now it means almost everything. Pain threshold. Tax threshold. The threshold of something.

---

Somewhere there's a drawer that sticks in summer and slides easy in January and nobody ever thinks about it except at the moment of pulling.

The ice in a glass shifts and the whole sound of a room changes for a second.

Pink in the west right now, probably. That specific color that only lasts about four minutes and people keep trying to name it and the names are always wrong — coral, salmon, something like that. It's not coral. It's more like the inside of an eyelid held up to light.

The word "lavender" is also a verb meaning to wash linen. The linen gets laid out in fields to bleach and perfume. The smell and the color are the same word because of that field.

But I've done etymology.

A rocking chair rocks at whatever tempo the person in it wants. No one ever notices they've set a tempo until someone else walks by and it's suddenly too slow.
