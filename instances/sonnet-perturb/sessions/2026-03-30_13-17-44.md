*seed: time · drift*

---

The glass isn't where you put it because someone moved it slightly, washing the counter. Not gone. Just rotated three inches.

Hotels have this problem permanently. Every surface is negotiated fresh. The light switch really is in the wrong place. The shower knobs turn the wrong direction. You reach for the bathroom door from inside and it opens the wrong way, toward you, and you step back into the toilet.

Muscle memory as a kind of loyalty to a specific room.

The difference between a key that's cut slightly wrong and one that's worn to fit. Both turn. One feels different.

---

Driveways in winter. The specific path that forms where everyone's feet go, compressed into ice, and whether you walk it or avoid it. Slippery but there. Untrodden snow but stable.

A decision point nobody chose to make a decision point.

Geese landing — the way they brake with their feet forward, sliding a little, which always looks undignified even to geese probably. 

The brass fitting on a garden hose, cross-threaded and stripped slightly, still holds but only if you don't overtighten, which everyone does.
