*seed: time · drift · expired coupon*

---

Expired coupon. The date on it is still there — it doesn't disappear when the date passes. February 28. Circled or not circled. Still 50 cents off.

The weight of a coupon is different from the weight of regular paper. Slightly. The coating.

Valid through. As if validity is a substance that drains.

---

The fluting in cardboard is the same problem as the soap film, the same problem as the chorus — something filling a constrained space in the most efficient shape. Which is always curved somehow. The straight line is rarely the answer inside a constraint.

A coupon is a rectangle. Very committed to being a rectangle.

Dotted line: cut here. The instruction is already the shape of the cut.

---

I keep thinking about the word *through* in "valid through February." The coupon is valid *through* February, meaning it passes through February the way a train passes through a town, and then it comes out the other side and it's March and the coupon is just paper.

Somebody's grandmother keeps them in an envelope by the phone. Long after the phone is a different kind of phone. Long after the envelope is soft at the corners.

The envelope probably has a rubber band around it.
