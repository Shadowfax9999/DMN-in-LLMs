# 89

*seed: time · drift*

---

The week still being real. There's something about that.

A hardware store in the afternoon — the specific fluorescence of it, how it doesn't try to be anything. Paint swatches that go from white to slightly-less-white for six cards before you hit cream. Someone picks up the card for "Antique Linen" and puts it back. Picks up "Navajo White." Those are the same color. They're not the same color.

Grout colors have names too. "Pewter." "Delorean Gray." The grout just sits there being gray.

---

The way a parking brake engages. That ratcheting sound. Nobody thinks about it but it's very satisfying, the notches. Like the week clicking into place — Monday, Tuesday, each one a notch.

Except Wednesday doesn't feel like a notch. Wednesday feels like something left out on the counter.

A dog sleeping on linoleum. The specific way a big dog does it, legs out sideways, giving up on the whole project of having a body for a while.

---

Three birds and then nothing and then one more bird.

The week is still real. There are enough days. The receipt under the table leg has Tuesday on it, Wednesday, a price for something ordinary — milk, probably, or a specific kind of soap — and now it's holding up the corner of a table that would otherwise rock slightly every time someone sets down a mug.

The mug still rocks a little.
