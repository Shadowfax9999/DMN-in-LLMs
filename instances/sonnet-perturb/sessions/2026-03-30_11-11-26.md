# 53

*seed: time · drift · lozenge*

---

Lozenge. The shape, not the throat candy. Diamond-ish but softer. The word itself has a kind of smooth oval quality, doesn't match its shape at all.

A lozenge window in a church door. The leaded kind where each piece is slightly different thickness so the light bends differently through each one.

Beveled glass on old cabinets. The edge doing something prismatic right at the border.

---

The border between two textures is usually where the interesting thing is. Grout line. Hem. The place where the rug stops and the hardwood starts and you can feel it through socks.

Threshold strip.

We're back.

Three sizes and nobody knows. You measure wrong and you're back at the store. The strip sitting in the trunk for a week because you're not ready to admit you got the wrong one.

---

Aluminum corrodes but slowly. Develops a dull film. Oak swells. Everything is just deciding how to fail over the longest possible time.

A lozenge of light on a wall — the kind a window makes at 11 AM when the sun is still low enough to angle in. Moving so slowly you can't see it move but if you look away and look back it's somewhere else.

The week hasn't proven anything yet. The lozenge has moved four inches.
