# 80

*seed: time · drift*

---

Graffiti on freight cars is the only writing that moves through towns that don't have bookstores.

The word BONE in six-foot letters on a gray boxcar. Just that. BONE.

A whole car painted silver with something that might be a face or might be a building on fire, hard to say, gone before you can decide.

---

There's a crossing in Ohio — I don't know where in Ohio, just Ohio, the way Ohio holds still for things like this — where the signal arm comes down and the light blinks and the bells start, and then nothing comes. A minute. Two minutes. Sometimes a train that had been held somewhere finally moves and it gets there eventually. Sometimes it was a false trigger and the arm goes back up and everyone drives through feeling slightly cheated.

The bell pattern is always the same. Dit-dit-dit-dit. It doesn't change based on whether the train is close or far. It just rings as if urgency is a setting you pick once.

---

Corridor. A word that has weight on one end. *Cor-ridor.* The front end is nothing, the back end drags. Like how *umbrella* front-loads and then tapers.

*Umbrella.* There are seven letters and four of them do very little.

---

Monday at 1:51 is a particular kind of stuck — not early enough to still be morning, not late enough that anything is winding down. The afternoon just sits there. A ceiling fan on low. The clock ticking somewhere in a room you're not in.
