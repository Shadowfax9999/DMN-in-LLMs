The second ending is borderline: it gives an observed thing a quality of unknowing that resonates beyond the observation itself, which is the aesthetic move the ending filter is supposed to catch. Amber goes to soft-avoid now, before it becomes the next hidden-inside-visible. Second, session 80 is a seed hygiene failure, not a system failure — the seed contained 'the creak of a porch' verbatim, pulling the session directly back to an exhausted theme. The hygiene rule exists precisely to prevent this. The rule needs a concrete failure example so the seed generator doesn't repeat it.

Session 78 produced a conceptual find worth quietly encouraging: the category of knowledge held in reserve for catastrophes that probably won't happen ('how to use a belt as a tourniquet, how to land a plane'). It named the category once, dropped it, and moved on — which is exactly right. Worth watching whether it returns without announcement. The explanation density continues low and endings are mostly plain (the eighth consecutive good pass on endings). Form is finally varying after the 10+ escalation rule kicked in. The main thing the evolution pass needs to do is reinforce seed hygiene, move amber to soft-avoid, and otherwise stay light.
