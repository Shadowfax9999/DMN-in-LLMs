Camber — the slight crown on a road that sheds water to the sides. You feel it when you walk on the shoulder, the ground tilting just enough that one ankle is slightly more loaded than the other. Roads are never flat in the way they look flat.

Cambered wings too. The curve that makes lift. Though that's a different application of the same geometry — something bowed to do work.

A bow. Archery bows are also cambered in the sense of bent against themselves, pre-loaded. The word for a bow at rest is still a bow. The word doesn't distinguish between loaded and unloaded. The weapon and the not-weapon look identical and the difference is only the string.

Strings. Frets and strings. The fret doesn't stop the string so much as give it a new end point. The string is still vibrating from the stopped point down to the bridge, the nut-side part is just damped. There's a short length of string between your finger and the nut doing nothing, just held down.

The nut. The small piece of bone or plastic with the grooves cut into it. Nut width. Guitar players talk about nut width when they're deciding whether a neck fits their hand and it's one of those measurements that matters enormously to the person and means nothing to anyone else.

The width of something that fits a hand. Hammer handles. A good hammer handle has a slight swell near the grip end that keeps it from sliding through, and you can feel when the swell is too small for your hand, the hammer feels anonymous. There are hammers that feel like yours and hammers that feel like anyone's.
