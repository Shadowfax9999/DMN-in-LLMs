A key blank looks like a key that forgot what door it was for.

Coin blank. They call it a planchet once the design goes on — before that it's just a disc.

A face going blank is the opposite: something leaving, not something waiting.

The word looks like what it means. Four letters, that flat "a" in the middle, the hard stop at the end. Blnk. Almost nothing.

Stencil is the other direction — not a thing before it's shaped, but a shape that shapes other things without becoming them.

Carbon paper. The second sheet knew nothing about what was happening to the first.

Onionskin. People don't say onionskin anymore.

A draft is also a blank, sort of. A will before it's witnessed. A letter before it's sent. The gap between the thing and its becoming-the-thing is where all the anxiety lives — a prescription that hasn't been filled, a key blank in a pocket, a form with fields still empty.

Fields. A blank field in a form. A blank field in a landscape. Same word, very different weight.

Fallow is a kind of blank that's actually working.
