# 59

*seed: time · drift*

---

Canvas wears through at the ball of the foot first.

Rubber soles separate at the toe.

There's a specific gait for shoes that are about to give out.

The word "sole" doing two jobs at once.

A dress shoe that squeaks in a quiet hallway.

Leather softens to the shape of a particular foot and then it's useless to anyone else.

Boots left in a mudroom for years, still holding the lean of whoever wore them.

The inside of a glove.

A hat that belongs to one head.

Things shaped by repeated contact — shaped without anyone deciding.

Handrails get smooth in the same places on every staircase.

The middle of a step wears down and the edges stay the same height.

Someone in an office building doesn't notice the step is concave.

A clerk in a marble building, hundreds of years, the same eleven steps.

Marble looks permanent but it isn't.

The Parthenon used to have a roof.

I always forget it had a roof.
