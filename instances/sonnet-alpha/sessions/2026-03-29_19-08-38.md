*seed: time · drift · refraction*

Refraction. Re-fraction. Something fractured a second time, or fractured again, or fractured back. The straw in the glass doesn't look broken, exactly — it looks *mis-seated*, like it was assembled slightly wrong.

The word "prism" sounds like it should be colder than it is. It sounds like "prison" with the vowels compressed.

A triangular thing. A triangular room would feel like being in a prism. You'd always be standing near a point. The acute corner of a room would be useless, you'd never put anything there, the angle is too specific. Though restaurants do it with little tables and two people face each other across something too narrow with their knees almost touching and it's either romantic or terrible.

Knees. Kneecap. Patella, which sounds made up. The patella just floats there and the tendons hold it in place and when you kneel on a hard floor you can feel it complaining in a way that isn't quite pain. More like pressure with an opinion.

Tile grout. The lines between tiles are always slightly lower than the tiles themselves and the grout gets darker over time in a way that isn't uniform, it goes by traffic, by where people stand most, and a bathroom floor read correctly is a map of where people habitually put their feet while brushing their teeth.

Most people probably stand in the same spot every morning without ever deciding to.
