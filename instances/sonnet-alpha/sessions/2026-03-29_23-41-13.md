# 41

*seed: time · drift · peripheral vision*

---

peripheral vision catches motion before the center does — the body knows something moved before you know what it was

a cat on a fence at dusk, right at the edge

the astronomer trick: look slightly away from the faint thing to see it — the rods out there in the margins, more sensitive, less precise

averted vision, that's the actual term

something about the edges being older — the part of the eye that's been around longer, before there were things worth looking at directly, when what mattered was knowing something was moving in the grass to your left

fovea is the sharpest, the center, the high-resolution part, and it's also the newest, evolutionarily, relatively — the luxury part, added later, for reading and faces and small print

the eye built for running from things, then retrofitted for art

---

the word *peripheral* has a soft landing, the -al pulling it down gently

not like *fovea* — fovea is hard and medical and slightly unsettling, like something you'd find labeled in a diagram

the faint star disappears when you stop trying to see it from the side
