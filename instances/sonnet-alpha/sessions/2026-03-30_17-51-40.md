# 89

*seed: time · drift*

---

A parking stub from a city you don't live in anymore.

The numbers on it don't mean anything to anyone now.

There's a coat that still smells like somewhere else.

Coat pockets have their own archaeology. A gum wrapper. A ticket. A folded edge of something that was once a thought worth writing down.

The fold is still there in the paper even after the words are gone.

A crease remembers its crease.

---

Two guys in a hardware store who aren't together but keep finding themselves in the same aisle. They've nodded once. That's the whole relationship. They'll never see each other again and neither will know it happened.

The one with the list folds it smaller. It's a square now. Softer at the corners.

There's a name for that kind of encounter but nobody uses it.

---

Brad nail. Tack. Shim. Awl.

Tools for small exact purposes. The exactness is in the name too.

An awl pokes a hole so something else can go through it. It's preparatory. Its whole job is to make way for a different thing.

A lot of things are like that without being called an awl.

The coat still smells like somewhere else.
