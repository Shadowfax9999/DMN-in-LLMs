# 57

*seed: time · drift · trellis*

---

Trellis. The diamond shadow it throws.

Not the trellis — the shadow of the trellis.

The wood is structural. The shadow is the idea of the wood.

A rose doesn't climb so much as grab and let go and grab again, a little higher each time.

Tendrils. The word has the right shape — soft at both ends, something reaching in the middle.

Reached. Reaching. Reach has no good past tense.

The wire on a trellis goes slack over years.

The nail holding it pulls out a little. The wood behind the nail goes gray.

Gray wood smells like something — not rot, not fresh wood, something between. A garden-shed smell. A back-of-the-garage smell.

Every garage smells the same in a different way.

Oil and cardboard and something rubber. Old rope.

Rope coiled on a hook. Nobody uncoils it.

The hook bends eventually under the weight of nothing much.

A bent hook is a kind of — no.

Baling wire. The word baling. A bale of hay is enormous up close.

You can't really picture the weight of a bale until you've tried to move one.

Same with a bag of concrete.

Same with a body, probably. The dead weight of a sleeping child carried from the car.

That weight that trusts you completely.
