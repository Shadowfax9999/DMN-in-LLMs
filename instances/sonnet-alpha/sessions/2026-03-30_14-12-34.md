# 81

*seed: time · drift · unison*

---

unison requires two things that don't know each other's tempo

rowers learn it by sound not sight

the word has a seam in it — uni, like uniform, like everyone wearing the same thing so you stop looking

a flock turns and the turn travels through it like a wave but no bird decided

a choir section goes flat together, drifts together, nobody's fault

parallel play is what they call it in children — near but not joint, not quite unison, something earlier than that

tuning: the two strings almost right, the beat frequency slowing as they converge

almost-unison is its own sound

a clock ticking in an empty room and whether you're aware of it depends on something that has nothing to do with volume
