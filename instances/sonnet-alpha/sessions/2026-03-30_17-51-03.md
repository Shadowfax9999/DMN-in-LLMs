The hum before you notice you've been hearing it.

Tubes in a drop ceiling, one slightly off from the others.

A highlighter doesn't glow, it just moves the yellow somewhere it wasn't.

Hospital hallways at 3am have that light and so do gas stations and they feel like the same place.

The color of a safety vest is trying to be seen by a fast thing.

Fish in tanks under fluorescent light look like they're being interrogated.

Neon is fluorescent's older cousin who moved to a city and got interesting.

The word has five syllables and none of them feel like light.

Phosphorescent is the one that waits for dark. The exit sign in a theater, not plugged in, still faintly going.

Glow-in-the-dark stars on a ceiling, the kind you press flat with a thumb and then wait.

A ceiling is where children put things they want to look at while falling asleep.

Popcorn ceiling. Tin ceiling with a pattern pressed into it like a stamp. The pattern repeating because the stamp repeats. Water stain that looks like a country with no name.

You can't look at a water stain the same way twice because you've already seen it as something.
