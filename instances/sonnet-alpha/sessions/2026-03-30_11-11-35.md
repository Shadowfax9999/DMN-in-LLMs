Sword. The w.

Gnaw — the g is doing nothing. Knife. Knight. The k that used to be said and then just stopped being said but stayed in the word anyway, watching.

Psalm. Island. The whole s in island — that one was never there to begin with. Someone added it. Added a letter that makes no sound and wasn't even in the original. Just put it there.

The thing about a parking lot at noon is the heat coming up from below and above at the same time. You're inside it. The car handles are hot enough that you grab and let go.

A parking lot in March is a different temperature problem. Cold coming up from the asphalt, sun on the shoulders. Split down the middle.

---

A glass that's been sitting too long — the water tastes like where it's been sitting. Like the air in that room.

---

Hedge. I keep seeing hedge used as a verb and it sits differently than the noun. "Hedging." Something you do with finances, with language. The bush got there first.

Couch can be a verb. "Couched in softer terms." The furniture version is so dominant now the other one sounds borrowed.

Cleave means itself and its opposite. You can cleave to something or cleave it in two.

---

A zipper at the top of its run makes a small click that you don't notice until you're in a quiet room.
