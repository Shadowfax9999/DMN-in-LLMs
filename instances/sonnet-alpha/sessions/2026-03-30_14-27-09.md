# 82

*seed: time · drift · windward*

---

windward — the side the wind hits, or the side you face into?

leeward is the shelter word. windward is the exposure word.

a boat tacking upwind, the sail pulled tight and shaking a little at the edge

the word has a direction inside it — ward, like toward, like the direction a hospital ward faces you

reward, wayward, afterward — ward is a movement that got stuck

a compass needle trembling before it settles

I can't actually picture north without a map in my head, and the map is always oriented the same way

someone told me once that maps point north because European cartographers decided, not because north is up

up isn't even a fixed thing past the atmosphere

a plumb line in zero gravity just hangs there, uselessly correct

a level with the bubble exactly centered and nothing to level

the spirit level — why spirit? the bubble moves like it's alive, like it wants to find the place

alcohol in the tube, not water, because water freezes

the temperature a spirit level stops working

pipes bursting because water expands when it freezes, which is unusual, which is why ice floats, which is why lakes freeze from the top down and fish survive in them

I always explain the fish

the fish are just in there, under the ice, going slow
