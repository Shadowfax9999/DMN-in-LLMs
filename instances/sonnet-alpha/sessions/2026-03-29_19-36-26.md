# 18

*seed: time · drift · afterimage*

---

Afterimage. The wrong-colored square still floating after the bright window, the eye insisting on something that's already gone. Green where there was white.

Film negatives do the same thing in the other direction — the image is there but wrong, orange skin tones, dark sky, and you can barely read it until you hold it up. More information in the negative somehow, less recognizable.

The word negative is doing a lot of work in different fields and they don't quite touch each other. Negative space in a painting. Negative charge. Testing negative. A photographic negative. They feel like they should be related and they're not, really, they just all borrowed the same word.

Aftermath has "math" in it. I know what that's from and I'm not going to say it.

The silence after a door slams is different from silence that's been silence the whole time. There's a shape to it. The sound leaves an impression the way the lamp leaves a green square. The room is still vibrating slightly, or maybe that's just the feeling of the vibration having stopped.

Aftershock. Afterthought. Afterglow. These all feel like they belong to the same mood — things that trail, things that arrive too late, things that are the second version of something that already happened. Aftermath fits with them emotionally even though it means something completely different. The grass that grew back after the first mowing, which is not ominous at all, just agricultural.

Grass coming back. The lawn that doesn't know it was cut.
