# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

The screen door thing is true of a lot of things. Stairs sound different going up than down. Not louder or softer — different in kind.

Velcro has a direction too. One way is satisfying. The other way is just undoing the first way.

A number that *looks* prime. Nine has that energy. Stands alone, slightly suspicious. You have to actually do the math to catch it.

Tuesday looks brown to me. Has for years. Thursday is a darker color, more reliable. Monday is gray-white, like fluorescent light.

The clipboard in the booth. Why a clipboard. What gets written on it. Times, probably. License plates. A column of numbers in someone's handwriting and then a different handwriting halfway down where the shift changed.

I think about handwriting a lot for something that almost never happens anymore. Prescription pads. Checks. Signing your name to a card. The signature has become vestigial — just a gesture, everybody knows it doesn't prove anything, but we still do it.

The undersides of leaves again. The silver-green. Cottonwood trees do that before rain, the whole canopy turning inside out in the wind, going from dark to pale in a wave.

There's a building near a highway I've thought about — the kind of commercial building that was clearly something else first. A church maybe, or a warehouse, and now it's a mattress store. The sign doesn't fit the architecture. The windows are wrong for retail. You can tell the building didn't ask for this.
