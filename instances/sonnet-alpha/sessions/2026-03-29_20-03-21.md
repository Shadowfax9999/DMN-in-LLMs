# 25

*seed: time · drift · wood shaving*

---

The curl of it. That specific tight spiral that comes off a hand plane. It smells like nothing else — not like wood, exactly, more like the inside of wood, the part that wasn't supposed to be air yet.

A woodshop floor covered in them is a different kind of mess than sawdust. Sawdust is fine and settles. Shavings stay where they land, light, holding their shape.

A ribbon of orange peel does the same thing if you cut it right. Wants to spiral. Something about how the outer layer is longer than the inner layer and they can't agree.

The word "shaving" is doing double duty and nobody thinks about it. Razor shaving. Wood shaving. Both are about thin removal. Both leave the evidence behind — the shaving itself is the evidence.

Barbershop floor. Different smell entirely. Talcum and something synthetic.

There's a kind of Japanese plane that pulls toward you instead of pushing away. The shaving comes up through a slot and curls back over your hand. I don't know if that changes the curl or not.

A lathe shaving is longer, comes off in a continuous strip if the wood cooperates. Sometimes it goes for a foot, two feet, before it breaks. The long ones are satisfying in a way that's hard to explain.

The word "cooper" means barrel-maker. Coopered staves have to be cut at an angle on each edge so they'll pull together into a curve. That's a lot of very precise shaving.
