# 85

*seed: time · drift · the weight of a wet newspaper*

---

A wet newspaper is heavier than you expect it to be every single time.

The gray of the ink running is a different gray than the gray of the paper.

Newsprint. That word is doing a lot of compression — news, print, the whole industry, the smell, the blackened fingertips.

Fingertips. The ridges hold ink differently than the flat of a finger does. You can press a thumbprint into a wet page and it stays.

Pressing something into something else to make a mark that stays — wax seals, dental impressions, feet in fresh concrete.

Concrete takes about twenty-eight days to reach full strength. You can walk on it in a day.
