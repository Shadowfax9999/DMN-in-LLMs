*seed: time · drift · capillary*

capillary

the word is too long for what it describes

crack in the sidewalk filling with rain — the water doesn't fall in, it gets drawn

something about that, the upward pull, the idea that narrow enough is a kind of wanting

a glass tube thinner than a hair and the water just climbs

a bruise spreading under skin the same way, following paths that are already there

the body has a lot of routes that only show when something goes wrong

the blue lines at the wrist, the ones you can press and feel the pulse knock back

knocking back — a door does that too, slightly, if the frame is tight

tight-grained wood versus open-grained, the difference visible if you cut across it

a cross-section of anything is always a surprise

the inside of a geode looks nothing like the outside

the inside of a leek

the inside of a head of cabbage, that dense pale layering, the leaves so compressed they've gone almost white

white things that used to be green: the bottom of celery, the part that was underground, blanched by the absence of sun

plants doing this on purpose, the asparagus mounded with dirt to keep the light off

the dirt still warm from the day when you put your hand in it
