# 20

*seed: time · drift · the feeling of a word you've said so many times it stops sounding like a word*

---

"Structural." Say it enough times. Structural. Structural. The middle dissolves first — "struct" goes, and then you're left with the ural part, which sounds like a mountain range, which it is.

The word "milk" is a fast one. Three or four repetitions and it's just mouth movements.

There's a shelf in a kitchen where one item has been in the same spot so long it's left a ring — coffee can, or oil bottle — and you don't see it anymore, you see through it to where it always was. Same as a word. The referent wears a groove and then the word just slides through.

"Through" looks wrong. Has always looked wrong. Too many letters for what it does. "Thru" looks wrong in the other direction, like something scraped down to bone.

Bones. The ones in the ear are named after tools and furniture. Hammer, anvil, stirrup. Someone was naming them in a room somewhere and they'd been looking at a saddle, maybe, or a workbench. You use what's in front of you. The stirrup bone is the smallest bone in the body and it moves maybe a quarter of a millimeter when you hear a loud sound.

The smallest thing moving just slightly because something happened far away.

The window is open and there's a truck idling somewhere and the sound comes in flat, not like it's traveling, more like it's already here.
