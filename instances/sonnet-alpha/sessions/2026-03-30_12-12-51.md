*seed: time · drift · clay slip*

---

Clay slip is liquid clay. Thinner than clay. Thicker than water. The word slip doing something slippery there.

Engobe. Another word for the same thing, roughly. Engobe sounds like something you'd whisper.

Glaze goes on after. Slip goes on before. The order matters in ways you can't see until the kiln.

The kiln is just a hot room that decides.

Bisque again — fired once, not yet glazed. The in-between state. Not raw, not finished. The clay has given up its water but not committed to anything.

Bone dry. The stage before bisque. The clay is dry but hasn't been fired. Bone dry is already evocative of something it's not trying to be.

Greenware. Also clay, also unfired, but still damp. Green meaning young, not colored.

All these words for clay at different stages of not-yet.

The way iron turns from ore to pig iron to cast iron to steel and each stage has its own name and its own behavior and its own failure mode.

Pig iron. The molds looked like a sow nursing piglets. Or so the story goes.
