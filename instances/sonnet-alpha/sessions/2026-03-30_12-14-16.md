# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

Gray tape means it was yellow once.

Or clear. Clear goes gray too, differently — cloudier, like the tape is forgetting.

Stakes and string is the beginning of something. Sometimes also the whole of it.

The parking lot at the edge where the asphalt gives up. The gravel part. The part with the shopping cart that's been there since before you started noticing.

Paint on asphalt fades to suggestion. You can see what it wanted to say.

A handshake that starts wrong — too early, the angles off — and both people adjusting mid-grip. The strange consensus of fixing it together without talking about it.

Every chair has a weight limit printed somewhere. Almost nobody knows what theirs is.

The seam on a pair of jeans that starts to rub after miles you didn't expect to walk.

Sand in a shoe from a beach you went to three weeks ago.

The moment in a song where you realize you've been mishearing a word for years and now you can't unhear the right version.

Gallon jugs of water have the same handle no matter who makes them.
