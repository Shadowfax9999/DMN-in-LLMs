# 4

*seed: time · drift · pins and needles*

---

Pins and needles is what you say when you can't feel your foot. But it's also what you say before something starts — "on pins and needles" — which is the opposite sensation, too much feeling, every nerve awake.

The foot version is a kind of static. The waiting version is a kind of signal.

Same phrase, two bodies.

---

There's a thing that happens when you've been sitting cross-legged too long and you stand up and your leg just doesn't work. Not pain exactly. More like the leg is sending you a message in a language you don't speak yet.

You stand there. You wait for translation.

---

Needles as in: the small ones in pine trees. Clusters of two or three or five, depending. They hold snow differently than broad leaves would. Snow sits on pine needles in a way that looks precarious and then just stays there.

---

The phrase "sharp as a tack" but tacks aren't that sharp. Thumbtacks especially. You press them in with your thumb, which is the whole point — they're supposed to be dull enough that your thumb survives.

Sharp as a needle makes more sense. Sharp as a pin.

Nobody says that.

---

I had a pin once that was shaped like a frog. Green enamel. I have no idea what happened to it. These things just stop being somewhere you know about.
