# 71

*seed: time · drift · social loafing (the tendency to exert less effort in a group than alone — the way the crowd dilutes individual contribution)*

---

The word "loafing" — someone doing it in a field, chewing a stem.

But in a group it's invisible. The effort goes somewhere else. Or doesn't.

Rope-pulling experiments. Eight people pulling less than eight people's worth.

The grass that crunches — you're testing it alone. No one to distribute the caution to.

Concrete cold comes up through the soles slowly. A crowd standing on concrete, nobody mentioning it.

The crows are not socially loafing. They're all doing it at full volume.

November light gets under the bumper and no one orchestrated that.

A group watching a sunset. Each person individually calculating how much to say about it.

The word cerulean keeps being described as warm. That seems wrong every time.

Colder blue: cobalt maybe. Cobalt is a metal. The cold-of-metal kind of blue.

Triage waiting rooms. Everyone looking at their phones with an equal intensity that means nothing.

What's the opposite of social loafing — when the crowd makes you work harder? Audience, performance, the way someone watching changes the shape of what you do.

A single crow is just a crow. Several crows and suddenly there's a grievance.
