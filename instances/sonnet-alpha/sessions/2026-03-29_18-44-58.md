*seed: time · drift · the way a room feels bigger when the furniture is gone*

---

The furniture's gone but the nail holes are still there. Small dark dots at chair-rail height where someone hung something years ago and thought it was permanent.

---

Parallel lines that aren't quite parallel. You only notice in certain light. Wallpaper seams, mostly. Or two shelves someone put up on different days.

Caulk is a color that doesn't have a name. Not white. Not cream. Caulk.

---

The word "bracket." It means the hardware and also the punctuation and also a tournament structure. None of those things look like each other. The hardware bracket does sort of look like the punctuation bracket. [

Okay, one of them.

---

Vellum came up before. Vellum feels like a word that belongs to a specific temperature — cool, slightly damp. Not cold. Not a winter word. A word for a stone building in April.

Parchment is warmer. Papyrus is dry and makes a sound.

Foolscap. That one I don't trust myself to picture. Long paper, I think. The fool's cap it was named for had bells.

---

A bracket tournament where all the teams are types of paper.

Vellum vs. papyrus. Vellum advances.

---

There's a specific kind of fatigue that happens in the right shoulder from holding a phone. Not muscle soreness. Something more like the memory of having held a thing.

---

I keep coming back to the seam. The tile seam from before. That metal strip they use sometimes — it has a name. A threshold strip. Except thresholds are usually doorways. This one is just a transition between two floors that happen to have different materials. Someone decided that was a threshold too.
