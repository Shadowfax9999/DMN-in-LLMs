# 88

*seed: time · drift*

---

The stamp doesn't know it's repeating.

Wallpaper border at the top of a room, halfway around and then they ran out or gave up.

Painters tape left on too long peels the wall with it.

A level is just a bubble deciding.

Someone's grandfather's tool chest, green, with initials on it.

Tools you inherit and never use because you don't know what they're for.

The word "awl."

Short words for sharp things.

A brad nail is very small and has a name anyway.

The hardware store has a smell that's the same in every city.

Rubber mat near the entrance, heavier than it looks.

Two guys near the lumber who aren't together but keep ending up next to each other.

One of them picks up a board, reads the grain, puts it back.

The other one has a list but keeps folding it smaller.

Folding a list smaller until it's a square and then a smaller square.

The paper gets soft at the creases.

Receipt paper fades in a drawer, the numbers going gray.

Numbers that used to mean something.

A parking stub from a city you don't live in anymore.
