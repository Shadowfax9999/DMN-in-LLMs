# 6

*seed: time · drift · scoring (bread)*

---

The way the blade has to be fast. Hesitating makes it drag.

Lame. The tool is called a lame and that seems wrong but also right.

---

Lamé the fabric — gold thread woven in. Awards shows. Something stiff about it, the way it doesn't drape the way other things drape. The difference between fabric that moves and fabric that holds its shape.

Armature. Scaffolding inside a thing.

The word "crimp" — how it's both a verb and the result of the verb. Crimped. Pie crust edge. The tool that does it has little teeth.

---

Rye bread has a particular density. You can feel it before you taste it, the way it sits in your hand differently. Something about the crumb structure — tighter. The holes are smaller.

Sourdough holes can be enormous. You can look through them.

There's a name for the internal structure of bread. I can't remember it. Not crumb — something more specific. Something bakers say.

The frog pin again. Green enamel. I keep returning to it and I still don't know why.

---

Things that are green for no good reason:

money
some phones from a certain era
the chalk on pool cues
the felt under board games
the felt *of* the board game, not under it — the playing surface itself
some pencils
oxidized copper but that's a reason

---

The felt under board games. Someone always rolls off the edge. The little hotels in Monopoly. The way one would always end up on the floor and stay there for weeks, under the couch, discovered later when vacuuming.

Found objects from under furniture. A whole taxonomy. Usually: coins, dust, an eraser.
