1816 was the year without a summer. Tambora. So that ring is thin, probably — a stress year, the tree barely grew, and it's in someone's floor looking exactly like all the other rings except narrower, and nobody knows.

A thin year. A year that shows up as a gap.

Growth rings in a cross-section look like a target. Which is funny because a target looks like a tree cross-section. One of those things where you can't tell which borrowed from which, or whether neither did, whether circles inside circles just land in both places independently.

The word "annual." Annular. Annular eclipse — the moon slightly too far away to cover the sun completely, so there's a ring of fire around the edge. Someone described that to me once and I still find it hard to picture correctly. The moon is the same moon but it's just a little further out in its ellipse so it looks smaller and the sun leaks around it.

An ellipse is almost a circle. Almost. The difference is just eccentricity, which is also what you call a person who's almost normal but the sun is slightly off-center.

Off-center. The plank from the middle of the tree, not the edge. The pith would have been somewhere in that plank, or near it — the oldest wood, the first wood, the wood from when the tree was a sapling and everything around it was different.

I keep thinking about the 1816 ring but really I'm thinking about something being inside something without being visible from outside. The ring is in there. You'd have to cut the board in half to see it, and nobody does that to a floor.

There are things in walls too. Time capsules in cornerstones. Newspapers from 1887 folded into the hollow space between plaster and brick. A child's shoe, which apparently was common — people hid a single shoe in walls for reasons nobody is entirely sure about anymore. Not superstition exactly, or maybe superstition, but the specific belief has dissolved and the shoes remain.

A shoe in a wall is stranger than a shoe on a foot. Stranger than a shoe in a box. The same object, the same leather, but the context makes it feel like a message from somewhere you can't go.

The 1816 ring is the same. Still there, probably. Running the full length of the board, past the point where someone nailed it down, past the heating vent, under the refrigerator if it's a kitchen floor.
