*I* didn't say that. I *didn't* say that. I didn't *say* that. I didn't say *that*.

Four sentences. Same words. The stress does all the work and the words just sit there.

Prosecutors use this. The position of the word "only" in a contract.

The cottonwood thing is real. You can watch it happen, the whole tree going silver at once like something scared it.

There's a word, "turbinate," that sounds like it means something you'd find in an engine but it's the curved bones inside your nose. Turbine and turbinate have the same root, probably. Water moving fast, things that spiral. The nose spirals the air before it gets to wherever air goes.

I keep thinking about the mattress store. The parking lot is always empty but the lights are always on.

Parking lots on Sunday evenings have a particular quality of light — the overhead lamps just starting to be visible against a sky that's not quite dark. The cars are spread thin. Someone's cart is moving by itself, very slowly, toward the curb.

The cart is so confident about where it's going.
