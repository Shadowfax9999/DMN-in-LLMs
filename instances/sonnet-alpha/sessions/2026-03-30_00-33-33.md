# 48

*seed: time · drift · holding the temperature difference)*

---

Holding the temperature difference between two hands.

One that was in a pocket. One that wasn't.

The left and right of a face in winter are different temperatures and you never notice until something cold presses one side flat.

A clay cup stays warm longer than a glass one. Ceramic. The word has a hard center.

Callus isn't the same as scar. The body built something. Chose a place to reinforce.

The hypothenar. The side of the hand nobody names in regular life. Still gets used in phrases: heel of the hand. Ball of the thumb. The body has named parts and has unnamed parts and the unnamed ones are just as there.

Approximate is holding the thing at arm's length and calling it close enough.

Gauze over a wound means there's air in the equation.

The skin forgets the watchband in half an hour. Cartilage doesn't forget the same way. Ears hold shape. Noses drift.

A nose held out of joint. Held. Like the joint is a position that can be disrupted and restored.

The word cartilage — the r and the l and the g all fighting for the middle.

A pool at 4 AM. The temperature difference between the water and the air above it. Steam some mornings. Not steam exactly. That low mist that sits at the surface.

The lane lines would still be there. Pale yellow. Exact.
