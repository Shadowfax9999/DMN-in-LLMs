# 63

*seed: time · drift · the way you know someone is behind you without looking*

---

The back of the neck knows first.

Not warmth. Not quite sound. Something that happens before you've decided to notice.

A crowded elevator and both people staring at the floor number.

There's no word for knowing where other people are without looking. Proprioception only covers your own body.

The way someone else in the kitchen changes your path through it before they've moved.

A door open behind you that should be closed.

The waitress who appears the moment you've put the menu down.

Shadows arrive ahead of the person.

Probably the ear doing something under the threshold of hearing. Or the skin. Or the way light shifts slightly when a body interrupts it. It's not one thing.

A deer at the edge of a field, head up, not looking at you.
