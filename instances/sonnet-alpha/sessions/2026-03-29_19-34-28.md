*seed: time · drift*

The k in knife. The k just stands there at the front, doing nothing, having done something once.

Knave. Knight. Kneel. They all have that k. There must have been a whole cluster of words that started with a hard kn- sound and now they just... start with n, visually lying about themselves.

Gnat. Gnu. The g in gnu is funny because gnu is already a funny word regardless of the g. The animal is funny. It looks like it was assembled from leftover parts and the name sounds like someone sneezing.

There's a category of animals that seem embarrassed. The gnu. The cassowary. The proboscis monkey, obviously. The blobfish, but only out of water, which seems unfair — judging something by how it looks in the wrong medium.

A fish out of water looks wrong. A human underwater looks wrong too, just more briefly.

The word "briefly" is strange to look at. The ie in the middle. Brief. B-R-I-E-F. Five letters and two of them are doing the same vowel job together, and then f just ends it abruptly. Brief brief brief. It starts to look fake after a few repetitions. Most words do.

Cellar door. Someone somewhere decided that was the most beautiful sound combination in English and now it gets repeated every few years like a law. It doesn't sound that beautiful to me, it sounds like a place you keep potatoes.

Potato is a good-looking word. The round o's. It looks like what it is somehow, bumpy and round. Tomato too. Those two rhyme and it always feels slightly accidental, like they were named by different people who happened to land on the same ending.

The tomato was considered poisonous for a while. People just looked at it and thought: no. Red and shiny, grows on a vine, related to nightshade. Reasonable conclusion, actually.
