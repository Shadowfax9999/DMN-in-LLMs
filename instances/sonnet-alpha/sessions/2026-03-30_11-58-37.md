Primer gray is the color of waiting to become something else.

No — primer gray just *is* a color. It doesn't wait.

The car doesn't care.

A parking garage with the lights half on. That particular flicker where one tube is almost dead. The hum it makes.

Concrete floors painted battleship gray. Gyms. Garages. The same gray. Someone decided that was the floor color for serious work.

Rubber mats over that, sometimes. Holes punched in the rubber in a grid pattern. The holes don't go all the way through.

Grommets. The word grommet.

A sail with grommets. A tarp with grommets. The grommet as the thing that keeps a hole from becoming damage.

A hem that keeps an edge from fraying. A frame that keeps a photograph from being just paper.

The jig that holds a thing steady while you drill into it.

Bisque is also a chess term. A handicap stroke. A point held in reserve. You can use it when you want it. Some people never use it at all.
