# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator moves. You don't think of it as something that moves but it walks, slowly, over years, vibrating itself a few inches toward the center of the room. Appliance repairmen know this. There's probably a term for it.

A shoe in a wall is weirder than a shoe in a box but a shoe in a box is already pretty weird if you think about it for too long. The box is just waiting. The shoe inside is in a kind of suspension.

Suspension. Suspense. Same word stretched out.

Things under refrigerators: a pen, always. A bottle cap. One of those small rubber bands that comes around broccoli. The pen dried out before it got there.

The word "cornucopia" looks wrong. Too many letters for what it is. It should be shorter, rounder. "Cornucopia" looks like it should mean a legal procedure or a genus of beetle.

The child's shoe in the wall — just one. Always just one. The other shoe is somewhere, presumably. On a foot, in a trash heap, in another wall across town. A matched pair separated by whatever logic governed that. Someone carried one shoe to a construction site and pressed it into a gap in the plaster before the lath went up, and then walked home, or wherever, in both shoes or one, and that was that.

Socks get separated in laundry and people keep the single one for a while, in a drawer, a provisional drawer, in case the other one turns up. The shoe version of this but permanent and architectural.

Plaster. The smell of old plaster is specific and I can't describe it except to say it's gray. The smell is the color gray.
