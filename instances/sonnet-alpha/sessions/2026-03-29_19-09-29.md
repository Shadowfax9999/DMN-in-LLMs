# 15

*seed: time · drift · compost*

---

Compost. The word is soft in the middle. Com-post. Like something that used to be a post.

The pile at the back of a yard that nobody really tends but everybody maintains a vague relationship with. You add to it. You don't turn it. Occasionally you feel guilty about not turning it.

Kitchen scraps in a bag in the freezer so they don't smell. The bag gets full and you forget it's there and then you find it in February. Eggshells and coffee grounds and something that used to be a lemon.

February in a kitchen is its own kind of thing. The light comes in at the wrong angle and hits the counter and shows you everything you haven't cleaned.

Counters collect. Not dirt exactly — a film. A record of meals. There's a particular kind of buildup around a toaster that is just toast, essentially, distributed. Crumbs and dust and a slight grease-haze from everything that ever cooked nearby.

The inside of a cabinet above a stove.

Sticky in a way that's never fully cleaned. You pull out a jar and the jar lifts slightly and there's resistance, like the jar has been slowly fused to the shelf, and this happens with every jar, and you never think about it until it happens.

Glass and wood in a long slow conversation.

Some trees are stickier than others. Cherry bleeds. Pine bleeds. You can smell a pine board before you see it sometimes, if the cut is fresh. The smell of a fresh-cut board is a different smell than the smell of lumber sitting in a yard. The yard smell is dry and a little sour. The fresh cut is just — itself. Resin and something green still in it.

Green takes a while to leave wood. A green board moves. It warps while you're using it, while the project is underway, which is either a disaster or just a thing that happened.
