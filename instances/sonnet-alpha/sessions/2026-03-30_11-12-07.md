# 54

*seed: time · drift · denim*

---

Denim sounds tougher than it is.

The word has some give in it. Den-im. The second syllable soft.

Jeans that are too stiff to sit in. You have to break them.

A color that goes lighter where you bend.

Indigo is one of those words that's also exactly its color. Ochre does that too. Mauve doesn't — mauve looks like something else.

Seam on the inside of a leg, thick enough to feel through a chair.

The back pocket wears a white oval from a wallet. Specific to the person. Like a thumbprint almost.

Coveralls are denim's more serious version. Nobody says coveralls without meaning work.

A jacket that doesn't keep you warm enough but you wear it anyway because it's the right weight for the season you want it to be.

The way new denim smells is not a good smell but it's a specific one.

Thread used on denim is usually orange. That choice is everywhere and almost invisible.

Stiff at the knees when it's cold. You can hear yourself walking.

Patches. Iron-on patches with the adhesive that gives out. A patch on a patch.

The word dungaree. It went somewhere.
