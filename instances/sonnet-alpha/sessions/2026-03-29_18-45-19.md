# 10

*seed: time · drift · a pill you can't swallow on the first try*

---

The second try you tip your head back too far and then there's just the ceiling and the pill is still there.

Aspirin is the worst. The coating is supposed to prevent that but the coating is the first thing that fails.

Capsule vs. tablet. Capsule is easier — slippery. Tablet has that dry chalky mass.

There's a word for the texture of something that absorbs saliva instantly. I don't know what it is. Maybe there isn't one.

---

Things that are harder the second time you try:

parallel parking in a space that's obviously fine
starting a sentence you abandoned
folding a fitted sheet back the same way it came
getting the bobby pin to catch

---

The pill thing is partly about expectation. The first time you just swallow. The second time you're supervising the swallow and that's the problem.

You can't supervise a hiccup out of existence either. Attention makes it worse. There's probably a name for this — the category of things that fail under observation — but the name doesn't matter.

The pill is on the floor now. That's the third option no one mentions.
