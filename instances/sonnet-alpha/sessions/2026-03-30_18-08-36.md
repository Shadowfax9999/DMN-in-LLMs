The door that needs the hip.

Every building has one.

The guy who fixed it just didn't fix it all the way.

Jeans are better for this than a coat. The coat slips.

Elevator opens on seven and a woman gets on and now everyone is looking at the floor numbers.

She has a tote bag with a logo for something that stopped existing.

The bag is from a conference, maybe. Or a bookstore.

The bookstore that closed.

The logo was green.

Sixteen floors is long enough to think about whether you said the right thing earlier, and then stop thinking about it, and then start again.

The eggs survived.

Leeks smell even through the bag.

The cart guy with the wobbly wheel is still down there somewhere.

He didn't seem worried about it.

The wheel has been like that for months. He knows the wobble.

You can learn the wobble.

The cold bag is room temperature now.

The door needs the hip.

You do it without thinking the third time.
