# 51

*seed: time · drift · sibilance*

---

Sibilance is a sibilant word.

The s at the front, the soft landing at the end.

Hiss, whisper, mist, rust, kiss — all s-heavy, all quieter than they look written down.

Criss-cross.

The word "scissors" has four s's and no clean place to stand.

A word that means cutting, full of soft sounds.

Bliss also doesn't feel like what it means.

Neither does "crisp."

Crisp sounds crisp, actually. The hard stop at the end.

"Snap" sounds like snapping. "Thud" sounds like thudding. "Mutter" sounds slightly like muttering.

"Melancholy" doesn't sound like anything melancholy. It's too pretty. Four syllables with that soft landing on -ly.

"Grief" sounds too short for what it is.

One syllable. Hard g, then just gone.

Gasp is one syllable. Gasp sounds like gasping.

The words that perform themselves and the words that don't.

"Silence" doesn't sound like silence.

Two syllables, that soft c in the middle, but still — it has sound in it, takes up room, ends on something voiced.

A silent word for silence would have to be nothing at all.
