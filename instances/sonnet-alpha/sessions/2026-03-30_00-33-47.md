# 49

*seed: time · drift · newspaper box*

---

Newspaper box. The coin slot facing you at chest height.

The hinge at the top, not the side.

You pull up, not out.

The smell when you open one — ink and metal and something stale from yesterday's edition still in there.

Quarter. Used to be a quarter.

The ones bolted to a pipe. The ones that just sit there. The ones cemented into a concrete base nobody thought would outlast the newspaper.

Pale yellow. Exact. Still thinking about the pool.

The lane lines on the bottom of a newspaper box would make it make more sense to me somehow.

Gumball machine also has a coin slot at chest height. Also dispenses something. Completely different object.

Slot. Slot as in groove. Slot as in opening. Slot as in time slot. The word doing three different jobs and none of them feel related until you look at them all at once.

Bolted-down things. Park benches. Those half-spheres on ledges. The pipes under sinks in public bathrooms.

There's a word for those half-spheres but I don't have it right now.

The coin lands flat somewhere inside and you never hear it.
