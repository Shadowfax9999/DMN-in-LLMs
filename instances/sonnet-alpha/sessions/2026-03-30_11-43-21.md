# 60

*seed: time · drift · bisque (ceramics)*

---

Bisque is the stage before glaze. Before it decides what it is. Porous enough to scratch.

The word sounds like soup. Cream-colored. Both things, actually — the ceramic and the soup — cream-colored, which seems like too much of a coincidence and probably isn't.

A bisque doll face before the eyes are painted on.

Unfinished things that look finished. A gray primer coat on a car that stays that way for years in someone's driveway.

The Parthenon primed and never glazed. The roof went on and then eventually it didn't.

Floors that wear in the middle. Roofs that don't last. Marble that looks like it will outlast everything and won't.

Hardness isn't the same as permanence.

The sole of a shoe, the sole of a foot. Bisque before it's glazed. The Parthenon before it had a name.

Everything is in the middle of becoming something else or stopping.

A primer-gray car just sitting there.
