*seed: time · drift · a rule you follow without knowing why*

---

You don't step on the seam between sidewalk squares. Not every seam. Just some. There's a rule but you don't know the criteria.

Knocking on a table after someone says something bad.

Touching the doorframe.

The specific way you arrange things on a desk before starting — even when you're not going to start, even when the work won't happen — you still move the cup to the left and close the other tab.

---

Vellum. That word just arrived.

Not because of anything.

Vellum.

---

The rule about which mug. There's a hierarchy and it isn't written anywhere. Some mugs are for serious mornings. Some are for afternoons when nothing's going right. One is never used but also never thrown away. The mug you can't name a reason for keeping.

Every kitchen has one.

---

The seam between bathroom tile and floor tile — sometimes there's a metal strip, sometimes they just meet, sometimes there's grout and sometimes there's a gap. I always notice the gap. I've never thought about why I notice it.

The gap is maybe a centimeter. Sometimes less.
