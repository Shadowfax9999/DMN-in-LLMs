# 39

*seed: time · drift · poolish*

---

poolish — the word is too relaxed for what it is

something left overnight to do its own thing

the yeast count doubles and doubles in the dark

---

garlic paper again. the session before carried it in

a shallot in cross-section, the diagram of something medical — then poolish arrives and the bread is already there underneath the vegetables, the flour is already there, you can't get away from it

the starter that some people feed for years like a dependent

sourdough kept alive through moves, wrapped in a towel on the passenger seat

there are people who have inherited starter

---

inherited yeast

the word *culture* doing two jobs at once

a petri dish and also everything

---

poolish is French but sounds like it's describing the surface of something, a texture, a color

a poolish sky

the word has the wrong weight for bread

*levain* has the right weight — you can hear the rise in it, the *lev*, the lift

*biga* is sturdier, more Italian, lands flat at the end

these are all just wet flour sitting in bowls in the dark
