# 38

*seed: time · drift*

---

warm dirt, the temperature of a hand

a potato grown in a basement versus one that saw light — the basement one is longer, stranger, reaching

the word "eye" for what grows on a potato when it sits too long and decides to try again

a turnip looks like it's hiding something. the shape of it. the way it tapers

celery: the part that was buried is the part people throw away

a parsnip is a carrot that got scared

root vegetables are all slightly wrong-looking. nothing about a beet is reassuring

—

fennel underground versus fennel above: the bulb goes dense and pale, same blanching, and then it just opens into those thin green threads going everywhere, the above-ground part can't decide what it is

the anise smell is in both parts equally

—

the word *bulb* for the light fixture and for the onion. both round. both enclosing something. the onion enclosing more onion, tighter and tighter toward the center where it gets sharp and wet

the center of an onion is almost translucent

a shallot in cross-section looks like a diagram of something else, something medical

—

the garlic paper. thin enough to read through. dry but not crisp — it doesn't shatter, it compresses
