*seed: time · drift · the creak of a porch*

---

A creak that only happens at a certain weight, a certain spot — the board remembers where

Boards remember differently than metal remembers

A screen door's spring has a specific tension and a specific sound and when it's gone someone replaces it with something that sounds slightly wrong for years

The latch that doesn't catch unless you lift and push at the same time

Someone in that house knows the sequence, visitors don't, the door swings back open behind them

A porch without furniture is a different kind of porch

Plain porch

The word "stoop" is small and blunt in a way "porch" isn't — stoop you sit on the edge of, porch you sit back in, the posture is different

A stoop faces out, a porch receives

No — a stoop also receives, just differently, more forward-leaning

"Veranda" is a word that wants columns

There's probably a word for just the concrete slab in front of a door that isn't really any of these and people just point at it and say the step

The step, the landing, the slab, the stoop — these are all doing the same job and none of them are really the same

The creak is the porch announcing weight, announcing presence, the house knowing someone has arrived before anyone inside does

The three boards before the door are a kind of sentence
