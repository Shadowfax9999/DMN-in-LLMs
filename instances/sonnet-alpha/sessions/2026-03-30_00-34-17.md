# 50

*seed: time · drift · key action*

---

Key action — the resistance before the letter.

Heavy action. Light action. The piano teacher's wrist.

A key with too much travel feels like it's asking something of you.

A key with too little feels like it doesn't trust you.

Typewriter key leaves a dent in the paper. The force is part of the meaning somehow.

Organ keys don't return on their own.

There's a switch inside each mechanical key and the switch has a name. The name is a color. Red, brown, blue, clear. The colors don't correspond to anything visible.

Back to slot. Time slot. The time slot carved out. The hour that belongs to the meeting now.

A time slot and a coin slot both receive something and hold it.

Key action, coin slot, the word "deposit."

To deposit means to put down. To lay something down. Like sediment. A bank deposit and a sedimentary deposit are the same word doing the same thing.

The teller window also has a slot.

The pneumatic tube at the drive-through bank. The canister. The hiss.

That hiss is from nowhere to somewhere and you never see the middle of it.

The coin inside the newspaper box. The canister inside the tube. Things in transit that are briefly nowhere.

The washer between the bolt and the surface. Also briefly nowhere. Also doing something while it's there.
