# 70

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "crisp" has a hard ending that the thing itself doesn't have.

Shadows in late fall go somewhere they weren't before — not longer, different. Aimed differently. Like they shifted their weight.

A metal fence post in October. The cold goes into your palm and stays there for a minute after you let go.

Ice on a car but not the next car. The angle of a roof, the albedo of one color versus another, whether someone ran the engine at 6 AM. Tiny differences compounding into whether you scrape or don't scrape.

There's a word for the blue of a clear cold sky that I can't locate. Not cerulean. Cerulean is warm somehow. Something harder.

The grass doesn't crunch until it does — and then you know, and you start walking differently, testing, and you're wrong about half the time.

October light and November light. The sun is lower in November. Same direction, lower. It gets under things. It finds the underside of a car bumper and lights it specifically.

The cold of concrete is a slower cold. It takes longer to feel it. The cold of glass is immediate. Metal is somewhere between but sharper.

Crows in November sound like they're talking about it.
