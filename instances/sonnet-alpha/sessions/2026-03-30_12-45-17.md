# 68

*seed: time · drift*

---

Envelopes have that smell when you lick them.

The word "lick" is uncomfortable in almost any context.

A staple in the corner of something means it used to be part of a stack.

Phone booths are gone but the footprint is still in some sidewalks — a square of slightly different concrete.

You can't whisper in a gravel parking lot.

Ice in a gas station cup is different from ice anywhere else. Louder. Hollow.

Someone's voicemail message is still their voice even after.

"Noon" is a strange word for the brightest part of the day. It sounds like a closed room.

A receipt in a coat pocket from a different season.

Permanent marker on skin fades faster than on paper.

The weight of a full backpack is different when you're tired — same number of pounds, different feeling in the shoulders.

Cafeteria trays have a particular flex to them. A little too much give.

There's a specific anxiety in returning a shopping cart from far away and watching someone take your spot while you walk back.

Cardboard gets soft in humidity before it actually shows any damage.

The second time you hear a song in a day you notice different things.
