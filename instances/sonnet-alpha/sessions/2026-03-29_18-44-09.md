*seed: time · drift*

---

The eraser that's been there long enough to get gray on one side.

Not gray from use. Gray from the floor.

---

There's a kind of pen that has a rubber grip that gets sticky after a few years. Not all of them. Just some. You can't predict which ones. It goes tacky and then you don't want to touch it and then you throw it away even if there's ink left.

Pens with ink left.

The guilt of that is small but real.

---

Hotel. The word. The H is doing a lot. Ho-tel. Two equally weighted syllables. Most words have a preference — one syllable leans heavier. Hotel doesn't. Both land the same.

Motel is different. Mo- is lighter. The stress falls back.

Botel. Is that a thing. Boat hotel. I think that's a thing somewhere.

---

The little Monopoly hotel is red, not green. I had the green ones backwards. The houses are green. Hotels are red. The upgrade is a color change. That's how you know things got more serious.
