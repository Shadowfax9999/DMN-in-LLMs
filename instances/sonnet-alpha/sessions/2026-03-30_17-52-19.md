# 90

*seed: time · drift · the weight of a full grocery bag*

---

The weight settles into the fingers unevenly. One handle always worse.

Plastic stretches. The bag gets longer as you walk.

You switch hands at the corner without deciding to.

A carton of broth is heavier than it looks. A bag of chips weighs nothing and takes up everything.

The specific muscle that starts complaining. Upper palm, below the knuckles. It doesn't have a common name.

If you put the bag down to rest, the handles are still warm when you pick it up.

There's a moment of commitment when you grab all the bags at once from the trunk. Redistribution after that is hard. You've made your choice.

Inside: leeks sticking out. Something round rolling. The eggs in their fortress.

She's at the elevator pressing the button again even though it's lit.

Man with the cart that has the wobbly wheel, doing it slow, not bothered.

The cold from the frozen section is still in one of the bags. It'll be room temperature by the time the elevator opens.

Sixteen floors is a long time to hold a bag that's stretching.

The door that needs the hip.
