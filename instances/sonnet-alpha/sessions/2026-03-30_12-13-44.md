# 64

*seed: time · drift*

---

Head up, not looking at you, and yet.

The posture of a person who has just been told something they already knew.

Concrete gets hot faster than grass. Standing barefoot on a driveway in July, you relearn this.

The moment after the alarm goes off and you're not sure if you actually heard it or dreamed it.

Drywall has a particular weight when you carry a sheet of it. The whole sheet flexes. You feel like you could snap it.

There's a temperature at which a car door closes differently. Too cold and the seal grabs wrong.

Tinnitus sounds like a room that's very far away but also directly in your ear. Both at once.

A hotel key card that doesn't work the first time, and then does. Nothing changed.

The exact moment a dog decides to trust you and your hands are still.

You can hear fluorescent lights if the room is quiet enough, but only once you've noticed them.

Grass seed on a muddy patch at the edge of a parking lot. Someone taped string between two stakes. The string is still there. The tape has gone gray.
