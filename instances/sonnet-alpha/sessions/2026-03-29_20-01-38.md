Tape hiss is the sound of almost-nothing. The sound of a medium waiting. Before the music starts — that particular warmth in the silence that isn't silence.

The word "hiss" is doing what it describes, which not many words manage cleanly. Buzz does it. Murmur almost does it. Whisper sort of does it. Crunch completely does it.

There's a frequency range for hiss. High, thin, like something escaping. Snake hiss, steam hiss, tape hiss — all in the same register. The body treats them similarly. A small alertness.

I keep thinking about the leader tape — that clear section at the beginning of a reel, before the brown magnetic tape starts. Completely silent. You'd watch it go by on the spindle and know the sound was about to begin. The silence had a color.

Digital silence is just zero. No color. Tape silence is — not silence, it's the floor of the medium. The lowest sound the tape can make while carrying nothing.

That floor. The word "floor" for the lowest point of something. The ocean floor. The forest floor. The market hit the floor. It always means: the thing that holds everything else up. Except in the ocean the floor doesn't hold anything up, it's just the bottom of a hole.

Holes don't have floors, usually. You say the bottom of a hole. A well has a bottom. But a room has a floor.

The difference is probably whether you're standing in it.
