# 14

*seed: time · drift · déjà vu*

---

Déjà vu. The vu part is the seeing. Déjà is the already. Already-seen. But when it happens it's not visual exactly, it's more like a pressure behind the eyes, a brief wrongness, and then it's gone before you can locate it.

There's a different one — the word for the opposite. Jamais vu. Never-seen. When a word you know suddenly looks wrong, foreign, like it belongs to another language. Say "door" enough times. Door. Door. Door. It goes slack.

The hammer handle swell in the grip. There's a name for that, probably. The ferrule is something else, that's the metal ring. The swell is just the swell.

Some words look like what they mean and some words look like they should mean something else entirely. "Grain" looks right. "Pulp" looks right. "Elm" looks wrong for a tree that big. Elm should be something smaller. A fastener. An elm is something you use to join two boards.

The boards in an old floor are never quite flat anymore. They cup toward the center or cup away and the seams ridge up and you know which ones will creak before you step on them, eventually. The body learns the floor of a house. You stop thinking about it.

A door frame that's settled out of square. You know it's happened because the door starts catching, then one day won't latch, and you plane a little off the edge, and six months later it's catching again.
