# 5

*seed: time · drift · a callus you didn't notice forming*

---

The skin just decides. Thickens up where friction keeps happening. You didn't file a request.

Callous with an *ous* is the same thing but emotional. A thickening against feeling. The two spellings are the same root, which makes sense — they're both about not registering what's touching you.

Guitar strings leave ridges on the fingertips. Or maybe they just make the ridges that were always there more pronounced. You can hear fingers moving on wound strings in recordings sometimes — that small squeak. The callus moving against metal.

The frog pin keeps coming back. Just the image: small, green enamel, a particular kind of not-quite-glossy shine. It was a brooch, or maybe just decorative. I pinned it to something and then didn't. That's probably all there is to it.

Tack has too many meanings and they don't connect to each other:
- the thumbtack kind
- the sailing kind (changing direction)
- horse equipment — the tack room, the smell of leather and oil
- tacky meaning sticky
- tacky meaning wrong somehow, off, tasteless

The tasteless meaning is interesting. A thing can feel tacky and you know it immediately. Before you've reasoned about it. The callus forms before you notice, and the sense of wrongness lands before you name it.

There's a specific dried-out feeling to old leather.
