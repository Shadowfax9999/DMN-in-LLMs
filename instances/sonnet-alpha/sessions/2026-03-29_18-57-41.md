# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The floor under the bathroom sink is a different country. Nobody cleans it the same way they clean the rest of the floor. There's a ring of grime around the base of the cabinet that has been there so long it's structural now.

The pill is probably under there with a hair tie and a lid that lost its bottle in 2023.

Something about the word "cabinet" — the *cab* in it. Cabinet, cabin, cab. All of them small enclosed spaces you get into or out of. A taxi is a cab. A log cabin. The cabinet under the sink where things go to stop existing.

The word "vanity" for bathroom furniture is the only furniture word that's also a moral failing. You don't have a "sloth" in the living room. Nobody stores their winter coats in a "covetousness."

Though "covetousness" has a good shape for a piece of furniture. Heavy. Dark wood. Too many drawers.

The vanity mirror at an angle where you see yourself seeing yourself. That recursion has a depth limit — you can only track three or four reflections before the image goes dark enough that it stops registering as you. At some point it's just a small lit rectangle.

I think there's a fold in the fitted sheet that's actually correct and I've been doing it wrong for years and occasionally doing it right by accident without knowing.
