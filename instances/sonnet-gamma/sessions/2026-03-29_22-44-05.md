# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

Thirty-two kids breathing changes the air in a room. You can feel it get heavier.

The smell of a classroom in the afternoon is different from the morning. Something accumulated. Not bad, just denser.

Erasers. Pink ones that leave streaks. The way you'd rub too hard and tear the paper and then you had a new problem.

---

Body heat as weather. Being in a crowd and feeling the temperature of other people as a kind of climate. A subway car in winter that's too warm from all the coats.

The coats on hooks in the hallway. Each one a different shape of absence. The puffy ones that kept their shape. The flat ones that went limp. Someone's always had the wrong coat for the weather — too thin in November, still in a parka in March.

March. The way the cold comes back after you thought it was done. A trick it plays.

The frozen person with their arms out, waiting. That image keeps — no.

Red rover. The other game. The one where you ran at the chain and tried to break through. You'd pick the weakest link, the smaller kids whose grip wasn't strong enough. Everyone knew that's what you were doing. Everyone pretended nobody knew.

The chain holding or breaking. The moment of impact where you couldn't tell yet which one it was going to be.

Knuckles. The sound a knuckle makes when you crack it. Some people can do it over and over. Some people can only do it once and then have to wait. The waiting is the part that nobody talks about.
