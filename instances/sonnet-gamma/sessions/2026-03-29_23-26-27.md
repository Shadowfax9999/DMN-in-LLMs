*seed: time · drift · hapax legomenon*

---

A word that exists exactly once in the surviving record. Not rare — singular. The entire apparatus of a language producing one instance and then never again.

Which means: if the document burns, the word is gone. Not lost. Gone, as if it never was.

---

The opposite would be a word so common it stops being a word. "The." Does anyone actually hear "the" anymore or does it just pass through like a breath between real syllables.

Threshold words. Below a certain frequency you start to notice them. Above a certain frequency you stop.

---

Some words you've never said out loud. Read them, understood them, never spoken them. Is that a different kind of knowing.

Tincture. I've never said tincture out loud in a sentence to another person. I've used it internally, in reading, plenty of times.

Whereas some words you've only ever spoken, never seen written. Certain sounds a specific person made for specific things. Those don't have spellings. They're only ever hapax in the other direction — no text, just the one voice, one context.
