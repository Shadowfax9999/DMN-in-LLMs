---

Parking lot drain — the grate has a pattern but I can't picture which one. Either parallel slots or a grid. One of those things that's in front of you constantly and you've never actually looked.

The word "grate" also means something unpleasant. And also the thing you do to cheese.

Cheese grater holes are uniform, which is the whole point, but the first few passes are always slightly off.

---

I keep thinking about the break room board and what it would feel like to be the person who finally takes it all down. The rummage sale from 2019. Someone puts it up and then — nothing. It just stays. Nobody's the person whose job that is. The expired CPR certification printed on cardstock, laminated maybe, because someone thought it would last.

Lamination does preserve things past their usefulness.

There's a particular smell to old lamination. Slightly chemical. Like something sealed itself off and then baked.

---

A cork that's gone granular. If you pressed it hard enough in one spot the pieces would just — release. Little cork beads. The way foam does when it's old enough.

Seat cushions from the 80s. The ones that yellowed and then crumbled at the corners first.

Something about corners. They always go first. Paint peeling, foam crumbling, fabric fraying. The corner is where two stresses meet and neither one fully handles it.

---

Seam holes. The thing from before. I didn't finish thinking about that.

A garment with the seams ripped out — you can see where it used to be a different shape. The holes in a grid, evenly spaced, and the cloth on each side slightly compressed where the thread pulled through. That compression doesn't come out even after washing.
