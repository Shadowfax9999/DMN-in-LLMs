Queue. The silent letters just sitting there. Q-U-E-U-E — three of the five letters are doing nothing.

A line of people where nobody wants to be a line of people. Everyone pretending the queue isn't what it is.

The British use it as a verb. "Queue up." Americans say "get in line," which is a command. Sounds different. More impatient.

---

The way a queue moves: not smoothly. In bursts. Long stillness and then everyone takes three steps at once, like the information that space is available takes time to travel backward through the bodies.

Which it does. It's just a wave.

No — not explaining it.

---

A queue of tabs I'll never read. A queue of emails. A queue of things I meant to look up once and then the moment passed and now I don't care but the tab is still there representing an intention I used to have.

Print queue. The job sitting there waiting. The document that already printed an hour ago but the job is still listed, status: unknown.

---

Cue versus queue. Same sound, different jobs.

Cue: a signal to begin. Queue: a waiting to begin.

Both involve readiness. The coiled thing.

A musician waiting in the wings. That's a cue. A musician in the green room half an hour before showtime. That's a queue.

---

Snooker cue. The chalk.

The chalk leaves a blue mark on the tip and sometimes on the player's hand and sometimes on their shirt and they don't notice until later.
