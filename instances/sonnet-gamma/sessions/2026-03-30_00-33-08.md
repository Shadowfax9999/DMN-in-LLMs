Hyperbola looks like it should mean something excessive. Hyperbole. The orbit shape named after the rhetorical figure, or the other way around — both from the Greek for throwing beyond. A comet comes in on a hyperbolic path and leaves and never comes back.

Things that don't come back:

- comets (some)
- the specific way a room smelled
- whatever you were about to say before someone interrupted
- boomerangs, sometimes

The boomerang thing is embarrassing when it happens. You throw it and it just goes.

---

Weir is a good word. Sounds like the thing it describes — that slight wrongness, the water behaving strangely. A weir is just a small dam but the water goes over it differently than it goes over a regular edge, it smooths out, pulls into a sheet. The nappe.

Curtain of water. Curtain of —

No.

There's a thing where a stream hits a flat rock and spreads into a disc shape for a moment before falling off the edge. Like it's considering the geometry. I've seen this and I can't remember where.

Trabecular. The word for the inside structure of bone, the foam part. Trabecula — little beam. So the inside of your femur is little beams all along, at angles, following the stress lines. They remodel. The beams shift over years to match how you walk.

That's actually remarkable but I'm not going to —

The angle you carry weight at. Your whole life.
