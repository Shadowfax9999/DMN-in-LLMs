*seed: time · drift · a mark on a wall at hip height from a doorknob*

---

The paint is worn through to something yellowish underneath. Or maybe the wall was always that color and the paint on top is the later decision.

Every door in the house does this eventually if you don't put a stopper down.

Doorstoppers. The spring kind that you flick and it makes that sound — boing, that actually is the sound, no better word for it. Kids can't leave them alone. You can't really leave them alone either.

There's a whole category of damage that only happens slowly. The groove where a ring sits on a wooden dresser. The dip in a mattress. The path across a lawn where everyone cuts the corner.

Hip height is about where you stop thinking to hold the door.

---

The number 11 looks like something standing next to itself. The number 1 is just a line but two of them together feel like company. I don't know why 11 feels different from 1 1 with a space. It does, though.

Some numbers have weight and some don't. 7 has weight. 4 is flat. 9 has something going on in the back of it.

400 feels like a large room. 400 feels like more than four hundred things. I don't know what to do with that.

---

The forsythia from last time. Still thinking about it at the edges going yellow.

Yellow is such a fast color. It gets there before you're watching.
