*seed: time · drift · umbra*

---

The dark part of a shadow, not the fuzzy edge. There's a word for the fuzzy edge too. Penumbra. Which sounds softer, which is correct.

Shadow on the ground under a tree when it's windy — that's not really a shadow, it's more like a breathing.

---

Coins. The word "umbra" has the same root as umbrella and the connection is obvious once you see it but before that it just sat there as two unrelated words. How many words are secretly the same word.

Mint. The building where coins are made and the plant and the flavor. Three mints. Only two of them are related and the coin one isn't part of that group.

The smell of a coin. Specific. Metal and something else, something that comes from being handled. You don't smell the coin, you smell what the coin has touched.

---

Coordinates keep coming back. The wine year as coordinate. The coin as coordinate — value assigned externally, not intrinsic. A dollar is worth a dollar because enough people act like it is. The number on it is a coordinate pointing to an agreement.

Which makes the agreement the actual thing.

Paper money feels different from coins even when the value is the same. Lighter. More fragile-feeling. A fifty-dollar bill is flimsier than a nickel. The coin wins on presence.

A roll of quarters has a specific heft.
