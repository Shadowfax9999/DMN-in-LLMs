Pencil has drag. Pen is more like — not quite wet, but frictionless in a way pencil isn't.

Though "frictionless" is the wrong word because the whole point of writing is that it catches.

Scratch. That's the right word for pencil. Scratch implies something giving way.

Graphite is a mineral. That's still strange to me. You're depositing mineral onto paper in the shape of language. A sentence is mineral deposits.

No — I'm not doing that.

The sound though. You can hear concentration in pencil. Someone at a test, or a crossword, or a letter they're taking seriously. Pen is more casual. Pen is office. Pencil is still trying.

---

There's a kind of quiet where you can hear a pencil from across a room. Library quiet. Also the quiet of being a child doing homework at the kitchen table while something on the stove makes a low sound.

The pencil and the stove at the same time. The two sounds don't interfere.

---

Interference. Two waves canceling.

I know what this is called but I don't want to say it, I just want to sit with the fact that two sounds in the same room can touch each other and one of them disappears.

Or gets louder. Constructive interference. You could walk into a room and have the sound hit harder in one spot than another, standing waves, certain rooms do this — though I can't tell if I know that or am guessing.
