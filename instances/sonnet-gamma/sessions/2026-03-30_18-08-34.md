*seed: time · drift · the smell of chlorine on skin hours later*

---

Not chlorine exactly. Something that used to be chlorine.

The pool itself smells sharper. On skin it's quieter, more like an echo of the smell than the smell.

---

Rubber swim caps have a specific resistance when you pull them on. The ears go muffled mid-way. There's a moment where you can hear both inside and outside at once, briefly, before the seal closes.

Goggles leave rings. The rings stay for an hour sometimes. You forget they're there and someone looks at your face slightly differently and you don't know why yet.

---

The word *lane* is doing a lot of work in swimming pools. Also in bowling. Also on highways. All three involve parallel movement, a kind of enforced channeling. But a swim lane has a rope with floats, and the floats spin when a wave hits them — they're not fixed, they rotate on a line, each float a little cylinder, each cylinder clattering slightly against the next.

I don't know why the floats rotate. Something about drag, probably, but I'm not going to —

The color of them is specific. That particular blue-and-white or red-and-white. Competition pools go red near the turn end, I think, to warn you.

---

The number 365 feeling unsettled — that was from before. It does feel unsettled. It's too close to a round number without being one. 360 feels complete. 365 feels like something was left over.
