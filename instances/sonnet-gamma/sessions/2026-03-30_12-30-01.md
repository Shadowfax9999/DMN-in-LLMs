---

Bevel gauge. The name describes exactly what it does and somehow that still doesn't help you picture it.

The sliding arm. You lock an angle and carry it across the room to something else. You're not measuring — you're transferring. That's different from measuring.

---

Things that transfer rather than measure:
- carbon paper
- a rubbing of a gravestone
- the bevel gauge
- when you hum something to someone so they can hum it back

---

Taupe is probably somewhere near the color of a filing cabinet in a building that hasn't been renovated since 1987. Not gray. Not brown. The color of a decision nobody made.

---

"Bevel" sounds like it should be related to "level" and maybe it is, maybe it isn't. The words sit near each other like they share a wall.

A level is also a floor of a building. A bevel is not a floor of anything. That asymmetry feels wrong somehow.

---

The thing about transferring an angle: you have to trust that the material will cooperate. Wood moves. Metal is more honest about its edges. You carry the angle across the room and the room has already changed it slightly, by the time you get there.

A bevel gauge is calibrated for a perfect world and used in this one.
