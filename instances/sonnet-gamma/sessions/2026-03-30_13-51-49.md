# 79

*seed: time · drift · loading dock*

---

Loading dock. The specific smell of them — cardboard and cold concrete and something oily that doesn't have a name.

The yellow bumpers bolted to the edge. Chipped. Paint so old it's more gray than yellow now.

---

A hydraulic lift making that sound. The particular groan of it.

Pallets. The weight of empty pallets is surprisingly — no. Just: pallets stacked fifteen high against a brick wall and the way that looks somehow wrong, like furniture stacked in a flood.

The word "dock" doing two different jobs. Water and commerce. You pull up to both of them. You transfer things at both of them.

Ships get loaded. So do questions.

A loaded question is heavier than it looks. You're carrying it and then partway through you realize.

---

The back of a restaurant at 2am, someone pulling garbage. That's also kind of a loading dock situation. The same concrete, same cold, same sense of the inside of a thing turned briefly outward.

A kitchen in the middle of service is completely invisible from the dining room. Different temperature. Different noise. Different rules about where to stand.

The pass-through window between them — food going one direction, plates coming back the other. The two worlds touching at that rectangle.
