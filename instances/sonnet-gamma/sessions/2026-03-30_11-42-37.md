# 58

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "suit" doing double duty — what you wear, what you hold in your hand. A flush is also a flush. The deck has all this extra language living in it.

---

Hearts is the only suit that's obviously about feeling. The others got away with being shapes or objects. Someone decided one of them would be about the interior life of a person and then nobody questioned it.

You can have a broken club. A broken spade. Those mean nothing. A broken heart is a whole other category of event.

---

Rain filling a hole. The hole doesn't know it's being undone. The dirt falling back in from the sides is just gravity finding an easier path.

There's a specific smell when rain hits dry dirt. Not petrichor — earlier than that, the first few seconds. A kind of sharpness.

---

Four suits, two colors. That's a strange compression. You'd think four categories would want four colors. But red and black, and then you split each in half. Hearts and diamonds share red without sharing anything else. They don't feel related.

Maybe that's the point. The color is just a team. Not a meaning.

---

Knee depth.

I keep thinking about that. Knee depth and then what — the shovel goes back in the shed, the hole gets rained on, the ground levels back out. A few weeks later you can't find the spot. You remember it was near the fence post but the fence posts all look the same diameter.
