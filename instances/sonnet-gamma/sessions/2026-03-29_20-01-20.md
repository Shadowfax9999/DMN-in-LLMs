The green room is green, allegedly, because it calmed actors' nerves. Or it isn't — the etymology might be wrong. Either way the name stuck and now there are green rooms that are beige and nobody finds that strange.

Waiting rooms in general. The dentist's waiting room where the magazines are six months old and everyone is performing not-looking-at-each-other with intense concentration.

The ones with the fish tank. The fish tank as a task for the eyes so you don't have to do the harder work of staring at your phone or the middle distance.

Readiness. The coiled thing.

A spring is a coiled thing. Spring — the season — comes from a word meaning to leap or burst. The ground bursting open. It does feel like that sometimes, the way forsythia just goes all at once.

Though "burst" is wrong too — it's slower than burst. More like a pressure building until it's suddenly visible.

The window is open and the air coming through is 58 degrees and smells like wet bark and something else, something green and sharp.

Not explaining the sharp green smell. Just: it's there.
