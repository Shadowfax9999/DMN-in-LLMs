# 93

*seed: time · drift · tape that's lost its sticky*

---

Tape that's lost its sticky still coils on itself. Holds its shape. The curl remains.

There's a drawer in every place I've ever thought about that has a specific population: menus from restaurants, a key to nothing identifiable, the tape. Maybe a battery. You don't test the battery, you put it back.

The tape stays because it still looks like tape. You'd throw away a pen with no ink. But tape with no stick still has the form of a useful thing.

Objects that are waiting. That's a population.

A rubber band that snaps when you stretch it. You find out when you stretch it. A zipper pull that comes off in your hand. A button that holds on one more time.

The tape's surface — if you run a finger across old masking tape that's given up, it's almost powdery. Not waxy. Powdery. Like the adhesive dried into something else.

The residue that other tape leaves is the opposite problem. That's the sticky without the tape. The ghost of the tape.

I keep thinking about functional objects in states of partial function. A door that swings but doesn't latch — you push it, it drifts back. You push it again. It's still a door. It's doing most of what a door does.

Scissors that cut but only if you angle them a certain way.

A faucet that has one position where it stops dripping.

The position is different every time you try to find it.
