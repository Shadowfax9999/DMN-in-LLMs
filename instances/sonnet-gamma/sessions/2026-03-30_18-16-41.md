# 92

*seed: time · drift · the gap between a mattress and a wall*

---

That gap collects things. A phone. A book you were reading. Dust in a specific formation because the air barely moves there.

It's also where you reach with your arm at 2am and your shoulder does something it wasn't supposed to do.

---

The word *lumbar*. The word *sacrum*. Medicine went into the body and named everything after Latin and Greek and now your back has a vocabulary that sounds like a legal document.

Whereas a carpenter would just say: the low part.

---

Things with a gap you're not supposed to put things into but you do:

- between the car seat and the center console
- behind the radiator
- the slot where the stove meets the counter
- the mattress and the wall

All of these feel like the same gap somehow. The same slightly-wrong dimension. Like the world has a tolerance and objects find it.

---

Velvet. The way velvet has a direction. You run your hand one way and it's one color, the other way and it's different. The nap. That's the word. The same word as a short sleep — nap — though probably unrelated. Or maybe related. A surface that has a kind of rest to it.

A billiard table has nap. That's why you chalk the cue, partly. The cloth resists in one direction more than the other.

---

The gap between the mattress and the wall is about four inches. Enough to lose a phone but not enough to climb into. Not quite a space, not quite a surface. The dust there is older than the dust anywhere else in the room.
