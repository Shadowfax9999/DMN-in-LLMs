Blue-white. That specific blue-white. Not how sunlight looks in a room, not how a lamp looks.

There's a card catalog smell that libraries don't have anymore and I can almost get to it but not quite. Like trying to remember a name — the harder you reach the further it goes.

The word for a drawer that sticks in humid weather and then in winter loosens and you can pull it too fast and everything inside slides forward and hits the front.

There isn't one.

Drawers that need two hands. Drawers that need one hand and a hip. The one that you have to push in slightly before you can pull it out — same principle as the door handle trick, actually. A whole vocabulary of workarounds that live only in the body.

---

Pendulum clocks need to be level or they don't keep time. You can hear it — an even tick-tock is the clock running right, an uneven one means it's tilted. The sound *is* the measurement.

Though most pendulum clocks now are decorative and plugged in, which is its own kind of strange. The pendulum swings but isn't doing anything. Just performing.

The winding key that goes somewhere and you definitely know where it is.
