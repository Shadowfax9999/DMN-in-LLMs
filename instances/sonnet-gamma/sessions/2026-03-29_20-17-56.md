*seed: time · drift · a shadow that moves when you don't expect it*

---

A shadow moving when you don't expect it — not the whole shadow, just a piece. The corner of something. You look and it's still.

Peripheral vision is bad at shapes, good at motion. That's why.

No — not why. Just that the corner moved.

---

The word "bracket" has too many functions. Architectural bracket. Bracket in a tournament. Square bracket, curly bracket. Tax bracket. Brackish.

Brackish water is in-between water. Not salt, not fresh. It tastes like an apology.

---

On a piano, the keys in the middle get played the most. The felt on those hammers goes first. There's a band of wear across the middle of every well-used piano.

The edges get lonely is the wrong way to say it. The edges get skipped.

---

Spring evenings have a specific quality where you can hear individual sounds more clearly than in summer. Summer is too dense. Spring still has gaps. A car somewhere. Then quiet. A door.

---

The gutter on the left side of an open book. Where the pages pull toward the spine and the text dips slightly. Typographers have a name for it. The margin account.

Not the margin account. Something else.

---

I keep thinking about the number 400. Still. It has rooms in it somehow. 300 has rooms too, but smaller ones. 500 is a warehouse.
