Blue hour. The light that makes parking lots look like paintings of parking lots.

Every car a slightly different shade of wrong. Red becomes a dark bruise. Yellow becomes nothing.

The word "crepuscular" exists. Animals that come out in it. Deer, mostly. Rabbits. The word sounds like what it means — something creeping, crawling out of a hinge.

Hinges. A door swinging through its arc. The exact creak changes depending on how far you open it. There's a pitch at about forty degrees, then a different one near ninety.

I keep thinking about animal skeletons because of the fish. The way a bird skeleton is all hollow shafts. You could snap a bird's wing bone probably without trying very hard. The lightness is the whole point. Something designed to the absolute minimum.

Minimum. The word for the smallest amount, but also a smooth curve at its lowest point. Both things at once.

A dip. A valley. A trough. Trough is an ugly word for what it describes — the low place water collects, the long box horses eat from. The word is all mouth and throat sounds.

The sound of a horse eating grain from a metal bucket — that particular hollow resonance. I don't know why that's in there but it is.

---

A phone screen in blue-hour light is somehow more intrusive than it is at noon. The brightness doesn't change. Something else does.
