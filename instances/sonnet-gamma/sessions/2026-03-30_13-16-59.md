The light in the window from outside is either: someone home, someone forgot, someone always leaves one on, someone who read somewhere that it deters. Pick the one that fits the story you already had.

*Notice.* A bulletin board notice. But also to notice something. The corn disease notice and whether anyone noticed it. Same word doing two different jobs and neither one helping the other.

Fusarium. Gray ear rot. Something with "blight" in the name — there's always one with blight. I retain almost none of this but it left a mark somewhere, the way a name will sometimes surface years later with no context, just floating there, available.

The pamphlet was probably laminated. County extension offices laminate things.

---

The thing about a light left on in a window is that you only think about it when you're outside looking in. From inside you just stop seeing it. It becomes background.

There's a whole category of things like that — not absence-things, just things that require a particular angle.

A price tag on the bottom of a vase. The hinge on a cabinet door. The join in a countertop where two pieces of laminate meet at a seam you can feel but not see until the light catches it exactly right.

I think the seam one is closest to the light-in-window thing. Both are about: the angle, and whether you happen to be there.
