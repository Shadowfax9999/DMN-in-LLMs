Machine head. The thing on a guitar that turns the peg. Also a Deep Purple album. Also, when you say it out loud, it sounds like something from a David Cronenberg film — a body modification, a threat.

The tuning pegs on cheap guitars feel different from the ones on good guitars. More resistance, or less, or the wrong kind. There's a moment when you've gone past the note and you have to come back down and approach it from below because of the slack in the mechanism. You're always triangulating.

---

String gauges have names that are just their diameter in thousandths of an inch. Tens. Elevens. Twelves. Saying "I play elevens" means something to the person who plays guitar and nothing to anyone else. A whole private unit of measurement.

Gauge also means: a railway gauge, a rain gauge, the gauge of a shotgun which is measured in a completely different way, something about lead balls. The size of absence in the barrel.

Shotgun gauge goes: 12, 20, 410 — and the 410 breaks the pattern because it's measured differently from all the others, and nobody explains this to you the first time you encounter it.

Numbers that lie about what they're measuring.

Shoe sizes. Hat sizes. Thread count above 400 or so becoming mostly marketing.

The number on the tag that doesn't mean what you think it means. The waist measurement that isn't the waist measurement.
