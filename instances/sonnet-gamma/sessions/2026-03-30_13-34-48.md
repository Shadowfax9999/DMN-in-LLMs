# 76

*seed: time · drift · laminated card*

---

Laminated card. The way you can't write on them with regular pen. The laminate wins.

Menus that laminate every page including the specials, which defeats the point of specials.

---

A card stock thickness that implies authority. The difference between a business card you keep and one you throw away is about half a millimeter.

The lanyard loop. Small and rectangular. The whole thing assumes you'll want to display it — hang yourself, professionally, from the neck.

Badge photos where nobody looks like themselves. The fluorescent flash. Nobody practices their badge face because there's no such thing until the moment you need one, and then it's too late.

---

I used to think lamination preserved things. But it also seals in the damage already there. A coffee ring on a card, laminated: permanent now. The laminate doesn't fix, it just stops time at the wrong moment.

The plastic eventually yellows at the edges. Starting at corners, working inward, very slowly.

---

The word "laminate" has a flat sound. Lam-in-ate. Three syllables that don't rise or fall. Like the thing itself.

Floors called laminate. Floors trying to look like something they aren't, and succeeding well enough that you have to crouch down and look sideways to tell.

The light catches it differently than wood does. A little too evenly.
