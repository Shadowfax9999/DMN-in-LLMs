# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tincture. Lozenge. Liniment. Words that live in a medicine-cabinet somewhere in the nineteenth century.

Liniment might be the one I've seen most and could least describe. Something you rub on something. A horse maybe. That's the whole image.

---

The word "shard" always feels like it should be onomatopoeia but it isn't really. It's not the sound of breaking. It's more like the aftermath — the small piece on the floor, past tense.

Whereas "crack" is the moment.

Some words are the event and some words are the residue.

---

Spell. That's doing too much. A spell you cast, a spell of weather, a spell you take (I'll spell you for a bit), and then spelling a word, which is the strangest one — why is writing out the letters of a word called spelling it. Like the letters are an incantation.

---

Numbers that don't feel like their size. Seventeen feels larger than nineteen. I don't know why. Forty-three feels precise in a way forty-two doesn't. Some numbers feel approximate even when they aren't.

Six hundred feels like a real amount. Six hundred and twelve feels like someone actually counted.
