There's a low frequency in this room right now. Refrigerator hum, or something in the building's bones. You get used to it and then it stops and the absence is louder than the sound was.

Consonance.

No — that's the wrong word. Dissonance is the tension. Consonance is when the intervals rest. Furniture sitting on a floor is consonant. Nothing is in tension.

Except furniture is always in tension. Compressed. The legs pushing down, the floor pushing back. The table doesn't know it's at rest.
