Bracket as in the thing on the wall holding the shelf. Bracket as in the punctuation. Bracket as in tournament — which is a strange use of the word. A bracket is a support structure and in tournaments it's... also that, kind of. Holding the shape of who beat whom.

Sixteen teams become eight become four become two become one and the bracket is just the record of that collapsing.

I always fill mine out wrong in the first round. Everyone does. That's not an insight, it's just what happens. The whole enterprise of the bracket is to be wrong about it.

The spade in a deck of cards looks like an upside-down heart with a stem. Which means at some point someone mirrored a heart, added a stem, and called it a different thing. Now they feel completely unlike each other.

Clubs look like a three-leaf clover but nobody says that. Nobody says "I've got the ace of clovers." The name stuck wrong and we all just live in it.

Diamonds is the only suit that's just a shape with no pretense of being an object.
