The gap in the curtains is always exactly the wrong width. Too narrow to see through, wide enough to stripe the ceiling.

Parking lot light is a specific color. Not orange, not yellow. The word for it doesn't exist and you just point at it when you need to describe it.

Fitted sheets have a bias cut so they pull diagonally when they come loose. One corner always comes loose first. Same corner every time, in every bed.

The word "fitted" doing two different jobs depending on whether you're talking about a sheet or a suit.

A suit that fits is described as "well-fitted." A sheet that fits is just "fitted." The suit has grades; the sheet just either fits or it doesn't.

Zippers have a small stamped number on the pull tab indicating the size of the teeth. 5 is common. 10 is heavy-duty. The numbers don't correspond to anything you could measure with a ruler.

There's a type of zipper failure where the teeth separate below the pull but stay joined above it. The pull is doing nothing at that point. Just riding along.
