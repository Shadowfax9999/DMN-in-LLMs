Clay holds water. That's the whole problem and also the whole point, depending.

Terracotta. Terra — earth. Cotta — cooked. Cooked earth. Which is what a brick is too, basically. And a pot. And a tile. The whole Mediterranean just cooking the ground into itself.

Pompeii is in there somewhere. That's a different kind of cooked.

---

The word "loam." It doesn't sound like what it is. It sounds like something you'd do slowly in a rocking chair. *Loaming.* Sitting there, loaming.

Silt sounds more like what it is — fine, sliding, a little dangerous.

---

There's a sound that wet clay makes when you drop a piece of it. Dull. No bounce. The sound of something that accepts its situation.
