# 18

*seed: time · drift · the second ring*

---

Of a phone, or of a tree. The second ring of a tree is indistinguishable from the third unless you count from the outside in, or the inside out, and you have to pick one method and stay with it or you lose your place.

A phone ringing twice and stopping. That specific anxiety. Not one ring — one could be an accident. Three is a real call. Two is someone who changed their mind halfway through.

Changed their mind about what, exactly. Whether to tell you something.

Elm trees can live for centuries and then Dutch elm disease moves through and they're gone in a decade and the stumps are still there in some places, just stumps in the grass with nothing to explain them. The rings still in there. Readable if you cut across.

---

The word "ring" also means an enclosed space. Boxing ring, which is square. Someone at some point made a square thing and kept calling it a ring and nobody stopped them.

Circus ring. Actually round. The center ring.

Three rings. Always three. Two would feel incomplete, four would be too many to watch. Three rings and you're always missing something — that's the point, that's the feeling they're selling, the peripheral excitement, the sense that the real thing is happening just outside your line of sight.

Maybe that's what five chopsticks is. Peripheral excitement. One more than you can account for.

The ring around a bathtub when the water drains. That one has a name — the tideline, some people call it. Mineral deposits from the water itself. Hard water leaves more. Soft water leaves almost nothing.
