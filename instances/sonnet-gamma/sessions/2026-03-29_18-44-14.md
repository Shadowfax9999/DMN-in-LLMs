# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

Neighbors who've never spoken. You can live next to someone for years and the closest you get is a nod at the recycling bins. Not unfriendly. Just never.

---

The recycling bin has a lid that doesn't quite close. The neighbor's.

Actually I don't know whose it is. It's just there, at the edge of a driveway, lid slightly raised. A pale yellow that used to be a brighter yellow.

---

Yellow is a strange choice for a durable object. It fades visibly. You watch it become wrong.

Whereas blue —

blue holds for a long time. Blue fades but stays blue. Yellow fades into a color that isn't really a color.

---

The word "chartreuse" is always surprising. I know what it is and then I see it and I think: that's more yellow than I expected. Every time. Like the category resets.

The word sounds green. That's the problem.

---

Puce.

What does puce look like. I genuinely couldn't say with confidence. Something brownish. Purplish. It doesn't matter.

Some colors exist mainly as words. You reference puce, you don't really see it.

---

Mauve. Ecru. Taupe.

These are colors that live in fabric stores and nowhere else. Taupe is everywhere actually but no one calls it taupe in real life. You just say "kind of brownish gray" and everyone knows what you mean.

---

The recycling bin is taupe now. That's what happened to the yellow.
