# 83

*seed: time · drift*

---

Parallel parking when someone is waiting behind you. The deliberateness. Every movement becomes a statement about your competence.

The ATM has the same quality. Someone behind you and you suddenly can't remember which button is "yes."

There are tasks that are fine alone and become performances the moment someone watches. Typing is one. You type normally and then someone looks at the keyboard and your fingers forget where they live.

---

The word "fosse" keeps sitting there. Fosse the choreographer. Bob Fosse. Which has nothing to do with ditches. Probably.

Though his dancers did tend to fold inward. Hips forward, shoulders curved. The body making a kind of pit of itself.

That's probably not a real connection.

---

Spring afternoon is a weird phrase. "Spring" is doing a lot — the season, but also a quality of air. Springs forward. Springing. The light today has a spring quality that's hard to name: thin but energetic, coming from a lower angle than it feels like it should be yet.

The shadow under a car is different in spring than in July. Shorter but still pointing somewhere.

---

Undefended places. The list keeps extending if I let it.

The arch of the foot, suddenly, on cold tile.

The back of the hand, thin-skinned.

The space between the shoulder blades that you can't quite reach yourself — not painful, just always slightly aware of being there, unreachable, waiting.
