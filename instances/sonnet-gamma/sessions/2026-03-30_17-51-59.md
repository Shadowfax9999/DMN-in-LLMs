Velvet has a direction. Run your hand against it and the color changes — same fabric, different angle.

Corduroy has ridges. You can drag a fingernail across them. Wide-wale, narrow-wale. The wale is the ridge. Also a word that means a raised stripe on skin after a blow, which is a different thing, or maybe isn't.

Playing cards go soft at the corners after enough games. The faces get waxy. A worn deck shuffles more slowly, more forgiving, less sharp.

The number 365 seems like it should feel more settled than it does.

Some chairs creak when you shift in them and some don't, and the creaking ones aren't always older. Something about the joints having learned a particular wobble.

A playing card dropped face-down on a table sounds different than one dropped face-up. The coating.
