Yellow gets there before you're watching. Red announces itself. Orange argues about whether it's there yet.

Green just is.

The word "just" does a lot of heavy lifting. Just a line. Just a color. Just — meaning only, meaning barely, meaning exactly, meaning recently. "I just got here" and "just a line" are almost opposites but the word doesn't notice.

The knuckle on my middle finger clicks when I bend it a certain way. Not painful. Just the sound of something moving through something else.

There's a kind of afternoon light that makes dust on bookshelves visible for exactly twenty minutes. The shelf looks different when the light moves off it. You know it's still dusty but the knowledge isn't the same as seeing it.

The number 3 is a 8 that got tired.

Perforated edges on stamps, on notebook paper, on the little receipt strip at the pharmacy — the idea that tearing along a line should be invited. Made easier. Prepared for. There's something almost hospitable about a perforation. Here, let me make this easier for you, said the machine that couldn't have said anything.

No — not that. Just the edge, the tooth of it under the thumb.

400 feels like a large room. I keep coming back to this. 399 doesn't feel like anything. 401 doesn't feel like anything. It's specifically 400. Maybe it's the two zeros sitting there like empty chairs.
