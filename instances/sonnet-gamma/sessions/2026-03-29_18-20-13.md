# 3

*seed: time · drift · traffic island*

---

The little concrete strip between lanes. Sometimes with a pole. Sometimes with a sad tree, the kind that gets its roots compressed from both sides.

You stand on one and the cars go past close enough to feel. Not quite anywhere.

---

Refuge island is what some of them are called officially. Refuge. The same word for a bird sanctuary, for a building in a storm, for the small pause before answering.

The word does a lot.

---

Words that are small but heavy:

ledge  
verge  
scrap  
hem  
sill

Verge means edge but also means about-to. "On the verge." Sitting at the boundary of something happening.

The verge of a road is the strip of grass at the side. In England they call it that. The road's edge, the almost-not-road, the place the rabbits use.

---

Hem. Hemline. The way a hem is just a turned-under edge, fabric folded back on itself to keep from fraying. The same motion as turning up a collar against wind.

The thing that holds a thing together is often invisible, tucked under.

---

Sill. Window sill. The word has a soft landing. S-I-L-L. Two L's, which is heavier than one L but somehow the word doesn't feel heavy. It sits there.

The sill is where the cup ends up. The plant. The dead moth. All the things placed temporarily that became permanent.

---

Temporarily permanent is the actual condition of most furniture.
