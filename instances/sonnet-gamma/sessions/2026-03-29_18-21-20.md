# 5

*seed: time · drift · callus*

---

The word and the adjective diverged at some point but you can still feel them together. Callus. Callous. Hardened skin, hardened feeling. The body and the personality doing the same thing for different reasons.

A guitarist's left fingertips. The side of the middle finger from thirty years of pen grip. The heel of a carpenter's palm.

Places the world pressed repeatedly until the pressing stopped hurting.

---

There's a kind of knowledge that's under the threshold of language. Not muscle memory exactly — more like: the material conditions of a place, filed somewhere between the body and whatever pays attention. The weight of a particular door. Whether a stair creaks on the left side or the right.

You can be in a house for a week and not know any of it yet. Two months and you know all of it and don't know you know.

---

The body's filing system is organized by repetition, not importance.

I know exactly how hard to press the coffee maker's button. I know less certainly the names of my neighbors on the right side.

---

Scar tissue is denser. Less flexible. Holds its shape longer.

The metaphor is right there and I'm going to leave it.

---

Corns. The word "corn." A corn on the toe. Completely different word family. Grain. Kernel. How did it end up meaning that small hardened circle on the foot. Probably from the Latin for horn but it still sounds wrong every time.

The toe knows the shoe.
