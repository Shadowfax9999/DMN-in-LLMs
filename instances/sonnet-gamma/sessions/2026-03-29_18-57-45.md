# 11

*seed: time · drift · a library pencil*

---

Flat. No eraser, or the eraser is that brownish color that means it's been there since before you.

The stub length that says *someone used this seriously.*

---

Card catalogues had a smell that's gone now. Not the cards — the wooden drawers, the brass pulls, the particular compression of that many paper edges in a small space. You'd open a drawer and get a face full of it.

The Dewey Decimal System makes more sense than it has any right to. 641 is food. 641.5 is cooking. 641.59 is cooking by region. All the way down until you're standing in front of one specific book about Sicilian fish stews, and the number got you there, and you never had to understand the number.

---

Return slot. The satisfying thunk of a book going through.

Where does it go after the thunk? Some chute, a cart, a room nobody sees. The book disappears into the back of the library the way luggage disappears at an airport.

---

Reference books you can't take home. There's something about being made to read something *there*, in that chair, under that light. Forced proximity to the thing itself. The encyclopedia stays in the building.

I used to like the maps in the back of atlases more than the maps. The index of place names. Columns of names with coordinates. *Abilene, TX — D4.* A grid address for a city.
