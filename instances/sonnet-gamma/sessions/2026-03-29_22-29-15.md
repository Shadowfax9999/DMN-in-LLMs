# 31

*seed: time · drift · blister*

---

Blister pack. The pills sealed in foil and plastic, each one in its own little dome. You push and something gives. That small pop.

The ones that don't give cleanly — you push too hard and the pill crumbles inside.

---

New shoes are a calendar. You can tell how long someone's been walking by where the blisters are.

The word itself: blister. The bl- at the front. Bleat, blunder, blot. Something about that combination feels swollen already.

Blister steel. That's a thing. The surface bubbles during the process and that's what it's called.

A water blister versus a blood blister. Two different colors, two different causes, same shape. The body using the same architecture for different problems.

The one you're not supposed to pop. The specific restraint of that. Leaving it.
