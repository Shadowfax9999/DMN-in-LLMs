*seed: time · drift · spigot*

---

Spigot. The g doing nothing you'd expect a g to do.

The handle that crosses perpendicular. You turn it and water happens. You turn it back and water stops. The logic is completely exposed — nothing hidden in the mechanism. A child understands it immediately. That's rarer than it seems.

---

A faucet is different from a spigot somehow. More formal. Indoor. A spigot is outside, threaded, attached to brick. You screw the hose on.

The hose has a kind of memory of being coiled. It wants to go back. You stretch it across the yard and it curves away from you the whole time, leaning toward its resting shape.

Rubber does that.

There's a word for that — the way a material wants to return to its original form. I can't think of it right now. Not elasticity exactly. Something more specific.

---

Rubber bands on a doorknob, left there by someone, no one knows why, everyone's afraid to remove them. They're probably in every office in the world right now. Faded to orange.

The specific orange of an old rubber band.
