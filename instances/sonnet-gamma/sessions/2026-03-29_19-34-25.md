*seed: time · drift*

---

The battery could be good. That's why it's there. Not because anyone plans to test it but because throwing it out is a small irreversible act and keeping it costs nothing.

Irreversible. There are so many small irreversible acts that feel reversible right up until they aren't. Deleting a contact. Throwing out a mattress. At some point during the disposal the decision becomes real.

---

Rubber bands degrade just sitting there. In a drawer, in the dark, not being stretched. They just go brittle. So the rubber band in the drawer is probably not a working rubber band anymore.

The chopstick lengths. One is slightly fatter than the others. One has a texture near the top, little parallel ridges for grip, and the others are smooth. You'd notice in use. You'd keep reaching for the ridged one.

---

Something about the number five. Five chopsticks in the drawer, five different provenances. Five feels like accumulation. Four feels like coincidence. Six feels intentional. Five is just... life happening to you.

Not sure that's right.

Pentagonal numbers. You can't tile a floor with pentagons without gaps, which seems like it should be more famous than it is. Bees know this. Regular hexagons, no gaps. Pentagons, always gaps. Five is almost a tiling number and isn't.

There's a bathroom somewhere with a floor tiled in tiny hexagons. White hexagons. The grout is gray now but it was probably white originally. The tiles themselves are slightly uneven, not factory-made, and if you look at one tile long enough you start seeing the glaze has small craters in it, little surface events from when it was cooling.
