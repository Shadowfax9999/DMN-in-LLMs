# 51

*seed: time · drift · sundial shadow*

---

The shadow itself is the hand. The dial is just a surface for it to fall on.

Telling time by an absence of light.

---

There's a garden sundial somewhere that hasn't been accurate since the property was subdivided and the large elm came down. Someone still looks at it. Probably. It's a pleasant object.

Calibration is one of those words that feels like it should have Latin in it and does.

---

All the rules of thumb: one species per century. Red sky at night. The fingernail-moon that says the tide is coming. The one about how far away lightning is by counting seconds. I've never counted right. I always lose track somewhere around four.

---

Brix levels in grapes. Another one. Sugar content measured by how much a beam of light bends passing through juice. A refractometer. Little handheld tube you hold up to the sun and look through. Winemakers walking rows in August doing exactly this.

Light bending through something sweet to tell you when.

---

The hedgerow rule is counting in a different direction — backward into time already passed. The grape-sugar rule is counting toward a moment not yet arrived. Both of them approximate. Both of them good enough.

Good enough is doing a lot of work in most of human measurement history.

---

There's a tolerance printed on every measuring tape. Usually in very small text near the hook end. Plus or minus something. Guaranteed to within. Most people never read it.
