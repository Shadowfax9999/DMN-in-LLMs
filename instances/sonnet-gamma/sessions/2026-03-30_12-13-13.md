# 63

*seed: time · drift · the smell of a hardware store*

---

Rubber and sawdust and something underneath that's not quite either.

The bins. Little plastic drawers stacked floor to ceiling, each one holding a single type of fastener. Someone decided these needed to be organized by size and thread count and drive type and someone else comes in and just wants a screw that looks about like this.

The fluorescent light in hardware stores is a particular quality. Slightly greenish. Everything looks like it needs another coat.

---

Tempering keeps coming back. A tempered person. Untempered. What would untempered steel even do — shatter like glass, apparently. And someone who hasn't been tempered at all would be

actually I don't know what that person would be. Too soft or too brittle depending on what you mean.

Holding at a particular condition. That's it.

A held note is like that. Not arrived at, not leaving — just being sustained, the player managing the pressure to keep it from moving.

What sustains a note: the bow, the breath, the finger on the string. The moment you stop managing it the note starts to decay or go sharp or drift. Nothing holds itself.

---

A screw with the head stripped. You can get it out with a rubber band — press the rubber band against the head for grip, drive through it. Works once.
