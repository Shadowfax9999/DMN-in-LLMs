The suspension that doesn't resolve. You wait and it goes somewhere else entirely and your body had already prepared for the landing.

D4. The grid coordinate feels more like a place than the city does sometimes. Abstract enough to be anywhere. Abilene.

There's a specific boredom of being very young in a library. Not unpleasant. The carpet. The cold.

Microfilm machines had a smell. Or the film did. Something acetate and sealed-in, like opening a very old bag.

The melody thing keeps pulling. There's a version of it where the note bends up at the end of a phrase — like a question in a language you don't speak. You still feel it as a question even if you don't know that's what it means.

641.59. All that specificity. Somewhere in that system there's a gap — a number nobody's used yet, a shelf where nothing has been classified. A gap no one would ever find because there's nothing there to find.

The problem with coordinate grids is the edges. What's off the map. The index says *Abilene, TX — D4* and then nothing about what's past D and what's past 4.
