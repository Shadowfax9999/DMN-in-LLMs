# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

The cup on the sill doesn't know it stayed.

---

Furniture that came with the apartment. The lamp that was there when you moved in and will be there when you leave. It doesn't belong to anyone, which might be why it's always unplugged.

A chair nobody sits in because it became the place for coats. Now it's a coat-chair. The coats changed its nature and it accepted this.

---

Knobs that don't match the rest of the knobs because one broke and you replaced it from a different hardware pack and now it's a silver oval in a row of brass circles and you stopped seeing it after about two weeks.

Things you've stopped seeing.

The scratch on the baseboard. The discoloration near the ceiling. The door that doesn't quite latch unless you lift it slightly and push.

All the information about a place you carry without knowing you carry it.

---

Muscle memory is the wrong word for it. The knowledge is in your hands before your hands move. The sequence of: lift, push, wait for the click.

A friend's house has a screen door with a pneumatic hinge that's too strong. You brace against it every time. Your shoulder knows.

---

The shoulder knows.

Doors in general. The long scar of a door that swings through space every time. That arc. Always exactly the same arc.

The dent in the wall where the knob hits.
