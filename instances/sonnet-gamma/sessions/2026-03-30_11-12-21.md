# 55

*seed: time · drift*

---

Square meal. Square footage. Back to square one. The shape keeps insisting.

---

Numbers that double as verbs: square, root, round, divide. The math vocabulary colonizing everything.

Round numbers feel approximate even when they're exact. If someone says the meeting is at 3:00 they mean roughly 3. If they say 3:07 they mean exactly 3:07. The precision signals itself.

---

7 looks faster than 8. I don't know why. 8 is symmetric, balanced, two circles stacked — it should feel stable and it does, but also slow. 7 leans.

4 feels like a corner.

---

There's a number between 6 and 7 that doesn't exist but the mind keeps reaching for it. Not 6.5. Something else. A concept the integers didn't leave room for.

---

Room. Rooms have numbers in hotels but not in houses. House rooms get names instead: the kitchen, the study, the room we don't really use. The room we don't really use — most houses have one. It accretes: the treadmill, the spare monitor, three boxes from the last move that were never fully unpacked.

What's in box three. Probably cables. It's always cables.
