Unique. That's the classic answer. But "common" isn't the opposite of unique, it's just... more of a thing. The true opposite would have to be something that exists in all instances simultaneously, which isn't a category, it's everything.

What's the opposite of Thursday.

Not Wednesday, not Friday — those are adjacent. Not Sunday. There's no axis to flip Thursday across. It just sits there in the week without an opposite, not because it's special but because position isn't polarity.

What's the opposite of a spiral. An expanding thing going inward? That's still a spiral.

The word "penultimate" gets used as though it means the opposite of what it means. So often that the actual meaning feels like a mistake now. There's something happening there with words that drift toward their own negation through sheer social pressure.

Lukewarm has no opposite. Tepid has no opposite. There's hot and cold and then there's this middle zone that isn't the opposite of anything, just the absence of commitment in either direction.

The opposite of the present moment is both directions at once, which means it's everything except the thing you're in.

The back of a spoon is curved the wrong way to reflect anything correctly.
