*seed: time · drift · tacking (temporary stitch)*

---

Tacking. Long loose stitches, maybe half an inch, holding the seam in place so you can sew it properly. Then you pull the tacking out. The thread leaves small holes that close up.

So the real seam passes through the same cloth the temporary one was in.

---

Tacking in sailing is something else. You come about — the bow crosses the wind — and now the sail's on the other side. Zigzagging upwind. I can see the geometry of it but I've never been on a boat doing it. Whether it feels abrupt or gradual.

The wind doesn't change. You change your angle to it.

---

Thumbtack. The head is for your thumb but it's also just — a small disc. The disc so you can get it out again, or so it doesn't go all the way through your thumb by accident. Both, probably.

I'm thinking of a bulletin board with so many thumbtack holes the cork is just pockmarks all the way down. Some corks get granular after enough of this. The board in a break room somewhere that nobody maintains, notices for a rummage sale from 2019, a CPR certification that's long expired.

---

Temporary things that leave marks. The tack holes. The seam holes. The water's disc shape on the flat rock that I still can't remember where I saw — might have been concrete, actually. Might have been a parking lot drain.
