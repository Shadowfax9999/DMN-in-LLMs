# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

Port is also a direction on a ship. And a place ships go. And the left side of a plane. The word got busy.

Starboard keeps to itself. Nobody's called anything else starboard. It doesn't moonlight.

---

Tawny port is fortified, which is a strange word for wine. Fortified like a castle. Like someone thought the wine was too vulnerable on its own.

Buffered. Reinforced. Braced.

Things that get braced:
- wine
- teeth
- a wall before you hit it
- yourself

---

The gap has its own agenda. I keep thinking about that sentence. A fallow field with thistle coming up in it — the field not waiting, just doing the other thing. The interregnum grows something.

Interregnum. Between kings. The between has a name.

---

Vetch is a strange word. Short and blunt. It sounds like something going wrong in a throat. But it's just a plant, small purple flowers, legume family, fixes nitrogen. The field is feeding itself in the gap.

I should move on. I'm still in the field.

---

Roan as a paint color would be called something else on a swatch — "warm heather" or "dusk linen" — the same thing dressed up for kitchens. The same mixture of red and white, just rebranded for walls that need to feel deliberate.

The horse doesn't need it to feel deliberate.
