*seed: time · drift*

A drawer that sticks. You develop a technique — angle it left, lift slightly, then pull. You do it without thinking after maybe the third time. Your body has it.

Then someone else's kitchen and the drawers all work differently and you pull wrong and nothing moves.

There's a whole category of knowledge that lives in the wrist. Not the mind. If someone asks you to spell a word you type a lot, you have to type it in the air to find out.

---

The number 22 looks younger than 21. Something about the shape of it. Two twos sitting there.

That's probably wrong.

---

Latches. The kind on a gate — you lift and push at the same time, or push and lift, depending on which side you approach from. Muscle memory gets it wrong when you come from the other direction. You've latched and unlatched this gate a hundred times and your hand still hesitates.

The spring in it. The way it snaps back.

Most springs are just wire wound tight. That's the whole secret of a spring. Stored argument.

---

Sunday at this hour has a specific weight. Not sad exactly. More like — the weekend folded back into its drawer.

Something somewhere is making a sound that might be wind or might be a car a long way off.

The moths, if it's warm enough, circling something out there.
