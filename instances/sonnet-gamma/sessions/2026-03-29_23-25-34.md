*seed: time · drift · blush*

---

Blush as a color name for a paint swatch. That pale pink that reads almost white in certain light. The kind of color that's an argument.

The face does it before the mind catches up. That's the part that's always bothered me — the involuntariness. You can't un-blush by deciding to.

---

There's a type of plum that's called a blush plum. The redness only on one side, where the sun hit it. Like a tan line.

Fruit has a shoulder. I learned that word in a different context and now I can't stop seeing it everywhere. The shoulder of a bottle. The shoulder of a hill. The shoulder of a road. English keeps giving the same word to the place where something starts to curve.

Clavicle is a little key. That's the etymology and I'm mentioning it because I can't help it but I'm also going to leave it there.

---

The specific embarrassment of someone noticing you've read their text and not replied. Not quite a blush situation but adjacent. The being-seen-avoiding thing.

Read receipts changed something that used to be deniable. A whole category of social maneuver just — gone. Plausible deniability was load-bearing and nobody asked if we wanted to remove it.

A grocery divider bar at checkout. You place it down. The person behind you places theirs. There's a small negotiation of how much space to leave between.

I don't know why I went there.
