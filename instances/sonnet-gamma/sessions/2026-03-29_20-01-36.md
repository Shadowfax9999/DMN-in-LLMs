*seed: time · drift*

---

58 degrees is the exact temperature where you can't tell if you need a jacket or not and you stand in the doorway for a moment doing nothing.

Forsythia is such an ugly word for what it is. All those s's and the hard stop at the end.

---

Pressure building until it's suddenly visible — that's also how you find out you're angry. Not in real time. The thing is already happening by the time you notice it.

There's a kind of thinking that works the same way. You're not aware of working on something and then the solution is just there, sitting on the counter when you come back into the kitchen.

The kitchen isn't going anywhere. Still: cold tile, the sound the refrigerator makes around 2 AM, the drawer that doesn't close all the way and you've never fixed it.

---

I keep thinking about the waiting room fish tank. The fish don't know they're doing a job. They're just doing whatever fish do — which is mostly hang there, make small adjustments, hang there again.

Hanging there making small adjustments.

That's most of it, probably. For most things.

The forsythia out front is already going yellow at the edges where it's getting less light.
