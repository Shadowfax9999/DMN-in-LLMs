---

Rhombus. Not diamond — diamond is rhombus with ambitions.

Though diamond is also carbon and also a baseball field and also a shape on playing cards that isn't quite a rhombus because the suit symbols have curved sides. The diamond suit is soft. A rhombus isn't soft.

The word has the right shape for what it means. Heavy in the front, then the -bus, which is somehow civic. Rhombus sounds like it should have a route number.

---

Parallelogram is the word nobody wants to say. You learn it in sixth grade and then you spend years avoiding situations where you'd have to.

Trapezoid at least has some personality.

---

The four-sided shapes and their strict hierarchy of specialness:
- quadrilateral (anything)
- trapezoid (one parallel pair)
- parallelogram (two parallel pairs)
- rhombus (parallelogram, all sides equal)
- rectangle (parallelogram, all angles equal)
- square (both)

The square at the bottom, maximally constrained, most specific. Winning by having the most rules applied to it.

---

The word "square" doing about sixteen jobs. Fair deal. Boring person. Ninety degrees. Town center. A number times itself.

The boring-person meaning — when did that happen, when did the shape become an insult. The square has four equal sides and four equal angles and apparently that's suspicious.
