# 8

*seed: time · drift · kintsugi*

---

The word sounds like what it is, almost. The K, the soft landing.

Though I've mostly seen kintsugi in photographs, never in a hand. Which might be the only way to actually understand a bowl.

---

Celadon. That's another one. A glaze color from somewhere specific. Korea, maybe, or China. Celadon sounds like a character in a novel you haven't read.

The color is a pale gray-green that has no reason to be as beautiful as it is.

---

Grout turns dark when it's wet and then comes back. This is not the same thing as kintsugi but the mind went there.

There's a tile floor somewhere with one cracked tile and nobody fixed it and now the crack just lives there, part of the floor, not gold, just dark.

---

Things with cracks that aren't fixed:

- that tile
- the sidewalk square pushed up by a root, which the city marked with orange spray paint and then apparently forgot
- the mug on the second shelf that everyone uses anyway
- the word "cleave," which means its own opposite

---

Cleave has been bothering me for years. Not years. It comes up and bothers me and then I forget about it.

The way two meanings can occupy a word without crowding each other. They live in different rooms of the same house. You always know which one you mean by context and the ambiguity never actually causes a problem and yet.

---

Sanction. Same thing. Permit or penalize, depending on nothing obvious.

Left. Remaining, or the direction. Though those don't feel as violent a split as cleave.
