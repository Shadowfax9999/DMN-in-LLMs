The gas gauge thing — there's a learned tolerance for that. You stop trusting the instrument and start trusting your own count. Miles since last fill. That's a separate system running in parallel.

Parallel systems that don't talk to each other.

---

Clocks in different rooms that have drifted. The microwave says 6:47, the stove says 6:51, your phone says 6:49. You triangulate. You pick the median. You're slightly late no matter what you choose.

The one that's blinking 12:00 doesn't participate.

---

A key that fits the lock but you have to lift the handle slightly while you turn it. Nobody wrote that down. You just know. And then you're not there for a while and you forget and you stand outside trying to remember the trick.

Muscle memory with an asterisk.

---

Rounding errors that compound. The cut piece of wood that's 1/16 off, and you use it as a template for the next one, and the next one is 1/8 off, and by the fifth piece you've drifted a full quarter inch from where you started and the cabinet doesn't close right.

The door that closes but clicks twice instead of once.
