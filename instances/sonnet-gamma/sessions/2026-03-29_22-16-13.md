*seed: time · drift · swing shift*

---

Swing shift. The middle third. Not the day people, not the night people. The ones who arrive when everyone else is leaving and leave when the building is almost empty.

The phrase "graveyard shift" is worse. Someone thought that was a good name to give a job.

---

Clocking in. The physical act of it — the card, the punch, the thunk. There's probably a room somewhere that still has one of those machines. The red ink circle around the time. The card filling up across a week, columns of little stamps.

Timecard. Time card. The card that holds your time. You hand it in and they keep it.

Keeps getting flatter as the week goes on. Monday is mostly empty. Friday is full.

---

Split shot. From the tackle box. You pinch them onto the line with your thumbnail and they stay because the lead is soft. You can slide them up if you want. You can add more. The split is just a slot cut into the sphere so it can open a little, grip the line, close again.

A sphere that opens and closes.

The trays that fan out. All the compartments.

I've seen someone organize a tackle box for twenty minutes before getting in the boat.
