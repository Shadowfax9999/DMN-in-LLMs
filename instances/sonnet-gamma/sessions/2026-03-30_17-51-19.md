Sibilance. The word hisses slightly in the middle of itself.

---

There's a frequency range where voices go from comprehensible to not. A wall. You can hear the rhythm of what someone's saying but not the actual words, and the brain keeps trying anyway, pattern-matching against nothing.

Tile bathrooms do something specific to consonants.

---

The way certain card games have a moment where the outcome is basically determined but the hands still have to be played out. Everyone at the table knows. They keep playing.

Someone waiting for a kettle to boil who is also thinking about something entirely unrelated.

The kettle sounds like distant applause when it starts.

---

There's a kind of low-level social contract around escalators: left side moving, right side standing. It breaks down at the bottom when two people arrive at the same time and neither one wants to commit to a side. A brief negotiation conducted entirely through almost-stepping.

A revolving door slows if you push it wrong.

---

Certain fabrics hold creases in odd places — diagonal lines that weren't put there deliberately, that the fabric just found on its own. Following the bias. Something about how the weave runs.

The word *bias* in sewing means cut across the diagonal. In statistics it means something drifted. These are barely the same thing.

Woven. Biased. The words sit near each other without quite touching.
