*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "bobber" is regional. Some people don't know what you mean if you say it.

Waiting for a fish is a specific kind of waiting. Not like waiting for something to download. The float could move at any second and it might not move for an hour and both are equally possible and you have to keep watching anyway.

The line goes straight down and then bends toward where the hook is. You can't see the bend. You just know it's there.

---

Carp. The word looks like it should be a rude word or an abbreviation for something else. Carp. It's a heavy, slow fish with a wide back and a permanent expression of mild offense.

Bass sounds like a respectable fish. Perch sounds small and efficient. Trout sounds cold.

---

Tackle boxes have a specific smell. Not bad. Just: oils, and something metallic, and old plastic. The trays that flip out in a fan. Tiny compartments for split shot. A hook buried in a piece of foam.

I've never understood how treble hooks don't catch on everything all the time.
