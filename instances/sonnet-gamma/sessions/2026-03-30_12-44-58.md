*seed: time · drift · the sound of a key turning that isn't yours*

---

The whole body hears that before the ears do.

Different alert from a key that *is* yours. Yours is just mechanism. Theirs is — the word "intruder" doesn't fit because it's usually the neighbor, but the body doesn't wait to find out.

There are sounds that skip the brain entirely. Some of them are social. A name said at the wrong volume in the wrong room. A child's laugh that pitches up into not-a-laugh.

The word "thresh" is in threshold and also in threshing and those two things feel like they shouldn't share the word. One is grain separating from hull. The other is a strip of metal on the floor that gets worn down to bare silver in the middle where everyone steps. The worn part is oval. It's always oval.

Hotel room carpets have a path worn from the door to the bathroom. Always the same path. Like everyone who ever stayed in that room had the same 2 AM.

The number of people who have slept in any given hotel bed.

Not thinking about that.

There's a style of window latch where you push a little lever and it swings a crescent-shaped piece of metal into alignment with the one on the frame. The crescent. Someone named that part. It's in a catalog somewhere with a part number.

Ceilings in hotel rooms are always textured. Not for any reason you'd want to explain. The texture catches the light from the parking lot coming through the curtains that don't quite close.
