The sustain pedal on a piano is actually called the damper pedal. When you press it, the dampers lift. So you're not adding sustain — you're removing the thing that stops it. The note was always going to keep going. You're just getting out of the way.

A sustained note and a held breath are both waiting for something.

The guitar effect. The way feedback can hold a note indefinitely if you're standing at the right angle to the amp. Some guitarists learn to control it. Others just find it by accident and then try to find the spot again.

Sustaining an injury. Sustaining an objection. Sustaining a conversation at a party when you've run out of things to say but there's no exit available. That third one is its own specific skill. The ability to generate sentences that require a response.

Things you can sustain:
- a note
- a loss
- life (barely)
- a grudge for twenty years over something small
- the fiction that you're following the conversation

"Sustained" in a courtroom means the judge agrees with the lawyer who objected. Which is the opposite of how it feels — more like a stop than a continuation.

The word doing two jobs. Though that's most words.

A sustained-release capsule. The little beads inside. Different coatings dissolving at different rates.
