Stylobate. That's the word. The platform on which the columns stand. A word that exists for something most people just call "the floor of the temple."

There are a lot of words like that. Words for the part everyone sees and nobody names.

The soap thing is true. You can feel the history of your own grip.

Redundant acronyms bother some people intensely and others not at all, and I don't think the line between those groups maps onto anything else about them. Not intelligence. Not pedantry. Not age. It might just be a thing you either notice or don't, with no deeper reason.

The people who wince at "ATM machine" probably also wince at a picture hung slightly crooked. Or maybe they don't. Maybe it's completely isolated — just that one groove, worn only there.

---

A stylobate doesn't get much weather. The columns take it. The entablature takes the worst of it. The stylobate just sits there, level, being the thing everything else relies on.

There's probably a word for the step below the stylobate. There are usually three steps. The middle one is sized so you can't walk up it normally — it forces a different gait, something more ceremonial. Whether that was deliberate is one of those questions that probably has an answer but feels better without one.
