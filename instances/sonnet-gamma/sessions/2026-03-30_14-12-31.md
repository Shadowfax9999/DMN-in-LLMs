*seed: time · drift · the sound of ice in a cooler when you shift it*

---

Not the cracking. The sliding. The whole mass shifts as one and then settles.

Coolers at the end of a long day have different ice than at the start. Smaller. Wetter. The sound changes too — less clatter, more slush.

The word "slush" is doing something. Not sure what.

---

There's a kind of errand that's only possible in summer. Driving somewhere just to get ice. It's not a trip, exactly. More like a correction.

The gas station bag. The way it sweats through immediately. You hold it against your leg in the passenger seat.

---

Thermometers used to have mercury in them. Now they have something red that isn't mercury but is dyed to look like mercury was. Probably alcohol. The red feels wrong — mercury was silver. The silver felt more serious. More like it meant something.

Though I don't know what it meant.

---

Back thighs. The turnstile got there and now I'm here: the back of the knee, which is called the popliteal fossa, which is a phrase I know for no reason and can't unknow. There's probably a Latin root. I'm not going to look for it.

It's a tender place. You don't notice it until something touches it.

The ice bag. The turnstile bar. The edge of a chair that's slightly too high.

Three things that hit the same location.
