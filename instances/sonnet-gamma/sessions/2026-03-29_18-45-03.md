*seed: time · drift · fallow*

---

Fallow as a color. Pale brownish yellow — the color of a field that isn't doing anything right now. There's a whole category of these: dun, tawny, roan, sorrel. Colors that only attach to animals. You can't paint a wall roan. Nobody's kitchen is sorrel.

Roan means two colors mixed in at the fur level, not blended — still themselves, just adjacent. Red hairs and white hairs. The eye blends them from a distance.

---

Sorrel is also a plant. Tart, green, nothing like a horse.

Dun rhymes with done.

"Fallow" and "fellow" — one letter.

---

A fallow field isn't empty, it just has different things in it. Thistle, vetch, whatever comes up in the gap. The gap has its own agenda.

I keep almost connecting this to cleave and I'm not going to.

Tawny owl. Tawny port. Those are probably different tawnies — one's warm brown, one's deeper. The word doing double duty across two unrelated things, not bothered at all.
