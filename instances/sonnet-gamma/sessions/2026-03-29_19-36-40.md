Soft water is a strange phrase. Water that's soft. As opposed to what it actually means.

The rings inside a bell. Not the tree kind — the actual harmonic rings, the way a bell keeps sounding after you stop hitting it. The sound has a shape and the shape is round. Obviously. The shape is always round when the thing is round.

A bowl does the same thing if you wet your finger and run it along the rim. You have to press pretty hard and move at the right speed. Too fast and it squeaks. Too slow and nothing. There's a speed where it locks in and the tone rises and stays.

Somewhere between too fast and too slow.

The word "annular." Ring-shaped. Annular eclipse, annular groove, annular ligament around the elbow. The radius bone rotates inside it when you turn your palm up and down. You can feel something in there if you press on the outside of the elbow and rotate your wrist. Maybe a slight friction. Maybe just the pressure of your own finger.

The palm-up position is called supination. Palm-down is pronation. There's a mnemonic — supination is when you're holding a bowl of soup, pronation is when you're pouring it out. I've always found that mnemonic backwards-feeling.

Bowls again.

The bathtub ring is technically called soap scum in most cases. The tideline is what people call it because tideline sounds less bad. Soap scum is accurate but nobody wants that in their bathroom.
