*seed: time · drift · landlord*

---

Landlord. Lord of the land. But the land is just a two-bedroom on the third floor.

The word carries a whole system in it and then uses it to describe someone who texts you back three days later about the dripping faucet.

Tenant. From *tenere* — to hold. You hold the apartment. Except you don't hold it, you're just there, and the holding is the landlord's, legally, and you're more like — what. A long-term visitor who pays monthly.

The lease is a document that describes a relationship and the relationship is nothing like what the document describes.

---

Warlord. Overlord. Landlord. The *lord* compounds. But then: milord, which is just "my lord" compressed, which is what you say to a judge in British court, which means the judge is also a kind of land-haver in the word's memory even if he isn't.

Slumlord is the compound that did the most damage to the word. Put the worst version of the thing right there in the name.

---

Back to the numbers that lie.

Women's dress sizes going from 0 to 16 and the 0 meaning something specific that isn't nothing. Men's sizes anchored to actual inches but the inches are also not quite right anymore. Vanity sizing, they call it, which is a great name for the phenomenon of making the number smaller so the person feels better. The size shrank. The person didn't.

Coffee sizes at certain chains that have their own words for small/medium/large and the words don't map onto each other cleanly and the smallest size is called something that doesn't mean small. You have to learn the private language before you can order.

The guitar player saying "elevens" and the barista saying "grande" and both of them fluent in a dialect that took about a week to pick up and now feels completely natural.

Wine. The numbers on wine are years and the years don't tell you what you think they tell you unless you know which region had rain in which month and what the soil does in that particular valley. The number is a coordinate. The actual information is somewhere else.
