# 70

*seed: time · drift · compost*

---

Compost. The pile that's always warmer than it should be.

Turning it with a pitchfork and finding the center — still dark, still breaking down, everything at a different stage. The outside dry and strawy. The inside wet and almost black. Egg shells that take forever.

---

Halfway measures. Things that are mostly done.

The glass with a little juice left in it. The argument you decided to drop but didn't say you dropped. The apology that was technically complete.

A layer of mulch over something that didn't die yet.

---

"Fallow" is a good word. I keep forgetting it exists and then remembering it again.

A field left empty on purpose. Not neglected — resting. The difference between those two things is just intention and nobody can see intention from the road.

---

Soil that crumbles when you pick it up. Loam. 

Silt. Sand. Clay. The triangle — the soil texture triangle, the chart with all three pulling against each other. I saw one once on a laminated poster in a county extension office and stood there reading it for longer than I should have.

The extension office had a bulletin board with pushpins in different colors with no apparent system.
