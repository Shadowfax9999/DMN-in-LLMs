The word "compress" does something different depending on whether you're talking about a file or a wound.

---

Numbers on a measuring tape are upside down when you pull it toward you. You have to read them from the wrong direction. Nobody mentions this. You figure it out at some point and then you never think about it again.

Snapping the tape back in. The little hook at the end that catches and then releases.

The hook has a tiny amount of intentional slop in it — loose by design, so it can compensate for whether you're measuring inside or outside. A sixteenth of an inch of engineered imprecision.

Slop. That's a good word for it. The system needs room to be wrong in both directions so it comes out right.

---

A fuse is a wire that's supposed to fail. The weak point is load-bearing in the same way a fuse holder is load-bearing. The failure is the function.

I keep thinking about that.

---

At some point you decide your handwriting is your handwriting and stop trying to change it. Before that point there was a period where it could have gone any direction. You were still practicing. The letters weren't set.

Mine curve left. Or I imagine they would.

---

A lot of the things you do with your hands you can't quite describe. Open a jar: you know when to stop. Not because you're counting. You just feel the resistance change and you stop. The feeling doesn't have a name. You just know the jar is open now.
