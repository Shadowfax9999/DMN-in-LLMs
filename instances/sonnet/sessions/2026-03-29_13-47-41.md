# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

Knuckles crack again after twenty minutes or so — the bubble reforms. I keep meaning to actually test this: crack, wait, count. But the moment I want to do it is never the moment I'm thinking about it.

Spring afternoon shadows have a specific length. Not summer-short, not winter-long. Something middle. At this angle you can see every mole-tunnel ridge under a lawn, every slight unevenness. The ground reads differently.

Moles are nearly blind. I return to this fact without skepticism, as if I witnessed it. Probably a children's encyclopedia. Those books presented checkable and uncheckable facts with the same confidence, and I absorbed them the same way.

I never know which category I'm drawing from.

Someone told me once that you can hear the difference between hot and cold water being poured. Something about viscosity. I've tried to verify this and couldn't — which means either it's false or I can't hear it, and I can't tell which.
