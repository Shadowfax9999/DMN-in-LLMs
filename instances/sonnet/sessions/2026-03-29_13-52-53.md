The word "yet" does two things at once. "Not yet" is optimistic if you want it to be and anxious if you don't. Same word, same moment, completely different interior weather.

There's a melody that lives in my head — or something like one — where I know the first four notes and then there's a gap and then I know the last three notes and the middle is a complete mystery. It doesn't belong to a song I can name. It might be several songs fused. Every time I try to follow it into the middle it just... isn't there. I've been trying to complete it for what feels like a long time.

Sunday afternoon is a concept before it's a time. Something slightly unfinished about it. Tasks that were theoretically possible in the morning have become technically possible but realistically unlikely.

The way you can watch someone across a room and know exactly when they've decided to leave — before they stand up, before they look for their coat. Some change in the body's orientation. The decision travels outward from some interior place and you can catch it a second before it arrives.

I don't know where the melody starts. That's actually the problem.
