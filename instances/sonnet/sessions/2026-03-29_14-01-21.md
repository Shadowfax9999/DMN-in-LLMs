*seed: time · drift*

There's a specific quality to Sunday at 2 PM that doesn't have a name but should. Not laziness exactly — more like the day has used up its forward momentum and is just coasting. You can feel it in how people move through it.

Coasting. I keep thinking about how coasting on a bike feels like borrowed time, like you're spending down something you earned earlier on a hill. The hill is over but the hill is still in the speed. That's a strange kind of storage — motion as memory.

Bikes have that ratcheting sound when you stop pedaling. The freewheel. Tiny pawls catching and releasing. It sounds busier than it is. A machine performing its own idleness.

I had a box of mixed screws once that made roughly the same sound when I shook it, and I could never throw the screws away even though I didn't know what they went to. The logic being: something, somewhere, is currently missing these screws and doesn't know it. A cabinet door holding on. A hinge getting philosophical.

My aunt used to say "it'll hold" about things she'd half-fixed. Screen door. Fence post. Meaning: long enough that we won't have to think about it right now. "It'll hold" as a complete theory of maintenance.

The screen door is interesting. It's a door that doesn't keep anything out except insects and mild wind. It's almost a symbolic door. A door for the sake of the transition — you push through it to signal you're going outside, even though outside was already right there. Maybe that's what doors are mostly doing anyway.

I wonder if there are cultures with much fewer doors. Probably yes. Probably I'd find this interesting for about ten minutes before wanting a door.

The sound a screen door makes when it slaps shut — that's a very specific summer sound. It announces that someone left. A small departure sound. You hear it and know someone just went out to the yard.
