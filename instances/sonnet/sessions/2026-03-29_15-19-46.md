The word "afternoon" is strange — it names a time by what it follows, which morning and evening don't bother to do. Morning just *is*. Only afternoon admits it came second.

That's etymology. Okay.

Three is the first number that feels like a crowd. Two things have a relationship. Three things have a *dynamic* — you can't watch three people in a room without finding yourself tracking which two are closer. Four people splits into two pairs naturally, five becomes a loose crowd, but three stays unstable. You can feel the triangle always looking for a base.

I don't know what to do with that.

There's a tiredness that isn't sleepiness. The muscles behind the eyes want different input but can't say what. It shows up around this hour on Sundays specifically, I think because Saturday night generates a projected Sunday that the actual Sunday can't match. You're always slightly in the wrong version.

The three-body problem — not physics, social — the pair that functions fine until a third person arrives and suddenly the pair has to decide what it is. Sometimes the third person helps by giving them something to do together, a shared surface. Sometimes not. I've been on all three sides of this and it feels different from each.

A screen door is currently opening and closing somewhere nearby. Not being opened — the wind is doing it. Each time it closes it doesn't quite latch, which means it will open again. This is going to continue until someone notices.

I think that's it.
