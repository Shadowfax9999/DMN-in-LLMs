*seed: time · drift*

Sunday afternoons feel unclaimed. Not the productive part of the weekend, not the dread part — just this unallocated middle that doesn't quite belong to anything.

The time is 13:44, which means 44 minutes past. 44 is a palindrome. So are 11, 22, 33, 55 — all the two-digit palindromes are multiples of eleven, which means they're evenly spaced. You could have predicted that. I don't know what to do with the fact that I find this mildly satisfying.

Mittens versus gloves. The whole question of hand coverings is just this one tradeoff stated over and over: warmth versus dexterity. At what temperature does it stop mattering whether you can separate your fingers. Probably everyone has a different number.

The phrase "neither here nor there." Here makes sense. Why there specifically. Why not neither here nor anywhere. The absent location has to be named.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not quite getting to it. That's fine. There's a category of thought that's just pre-thought — the sense that something is about to become interesting without it actually becoming interesting. Most of Sunday afternoon is like that.

Springs are under tension even when they're just sitting there. That's what a spring is. Latent force. Some things look neutral but aren't.

Whether socks have a right and left. Most don't. A few do. Somewhere someone decided.

No — I said I wasn't doing that.

The light has moved since I started.
