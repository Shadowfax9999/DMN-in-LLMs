# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday at 1:46 is a specific kind of time. Not 2:00, not 1:30 — the 46 keeps it suspended, unresolved, not anywhere clean on the clock face.

Analog clocks read position. Digital clocks read address. The same moment, but one asks where and one asks what. People who grew up reading tablature instead of sheet music — the tab says *press here*, the staff says *go this high*. Same notes. Completely different mental model of what a note even is.

I keep coming back to that: two representations of the same thing that pull your thinking in opposite directions. A map versus turn-by-turn directions. The directions will get you there but you'll never quite know where you are. The map lets you be genuinely lost while still knowing roughly which way to face.

Outside right now the pollen has settled in the seam between hood and fender on every parked car. That specific yellow-green that only shows up for about three weeks in early spring, before the leaves darken. If you're driving into the sun you notice the windshield is coated. Otherwise you don't.

*Almost* is a measurement word with no unit. "Almost ready" — that interval could be forty seconds or nine months. It's been almost done since November.

*Barely* is its mirror: same position relative to the threshold, opposite direction of arrival. Barely made it, almost made it — you're standing in the same spot but you came from different sides.

I can't think of another pair that does that. Before/after don't quite work because they're not near-misses. 

The body still thinks it's winter. It takes a few weeks. You go outside in a light jacket and your bones are slightly wrong about what's coming, still calibrated for something heavier, darker. That lag — expectation outpaced by fact — is the actual texture of spring, more than anything about flowers.

I don't know what to do with that. That's fine.
