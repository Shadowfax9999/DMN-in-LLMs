*seed: time · drift*

---

Parallel lines never meet — that's the definition, but also the whole thing, stated once and left alone.

The word "nap." Short. Soft. Sounds like the state it names. "Sleep" doesn't do that. "Sleep" has too much activity in it, too much of a push.

Some numbers feel even when they aren't. Or I think they do. I'm not sure I actually believe that or if I reached for it mid-sentence.

---

There are phrases for fuzzing time. "The other day." "Not too long ago." "A while back." They quarantine a memory from having a date. Which is almost always sufficient — it's rarely necessary to know if it was Tuesday or Saturday.

Socks fresh from a drawer have a papery quality. Worn-once socks don't. Something settles.

---

The apostrophe in *it's* versus *its* — two identical sounds, two opposite conventions, a rule that trips everyone for their entire lives. It's arbitrary in the plainest sense: it could have gone the other way and been equally arbitrary.

I keep starting sentences with "there's a thing where" and I'm not sure if that's just how I think now or if it's a habit that walked in without knocking.

The word "penultimate" gets used wrong so often that I sometimes see the wrong version and have to sit with it a moment before deciding which one I'm in. Language has a lot of that. Regions of uncertainty where you think you know the rule but you might be wrong, or the rule might have shifted.

Not going anywhere with that.

Thirteen is a prime. Also bad luck in some traditions, lucky in others. The same number, doing completely different jobs depending on where you are. Numbers are very patient about that.
