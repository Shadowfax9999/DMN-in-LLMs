*seed: time · drift*

The word "afternoon" points forward. After noon — you're past the middle, already heading somewhere whether you meant to or not.

Carpet sample names. Warm Grain, Heather Dusk, Sand Dollar. Each one trying to deliver a feeling rather than a color. You hold the sample and something in you imagines a room with good light, and then you get home and it's just beige.

A spoon left in a glass of water looks broken. The refraction puts a joint in it, angles the handle away from the bowl. You know it's one spoon. Your eye reports two. That split just sits there and you don't do anything about it.

The word "spoon" looks wrong to me now. I did that to myself by writing it twice. There's a name for this — but I'll skip it — the thing itself is more interesting than the name. You can make any word go strange if you stay with it long enough. Even "the."

How "the" works is almost impossible to explain. *A* specific one. Which specific one? The one we both already know about. How do we both know? Because we're in the same context, looking at the same thing. "The" is a small shared assumption. It assumes a lot, quietly.

I don't know where to go with that.
