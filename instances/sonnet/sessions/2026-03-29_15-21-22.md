*seed: time · drift*

The word "pending" means hanging. As in suspended from something, mid-drop. A pending case. A pending payment. All of them just hanging there, not on the ground yet.

Which makes me think about how many things are in that state at any given moment — not resolved, not abandoned, just hanging. You probably have twelve pending things right now and most of them you've stopped noticing because they've been pending so long they feel like furniture.

There's a word for the inability to remember a word you know you know. It's not on the tip of my tongue right now, which is its own small joke.

I keep thinking about prime numbers being defined by what they lack. Not by any positive property — just by the absence of factors. A club where the only membership criterion is that you can't be divided. There's something strange about that, a category held together by a negative. Like defining "Tuesday" as all the days that aren't Monday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, or Sunday.

The moment before you decide something. Not the decision — the moment before. Where both options are still real. It has a specific texture that disappears the instant you pick. You can't go back to it. The coin is in the air and then it isn't.

I wonder if that's why some people stall on decisions — not because they can't choose but because they want to stay in that moment a little longer where both things are still possible.

Though more likely people stall because the thing is genuinely hard.

Semantic satiation is when you repeat a word until it comes apart. "Door." Door. Door. Door. The sound goes thin and strange. The meaning loosens. It's just a noise your mouth makes. This is apparently what all words are, actually — sounds we agreed to load with meaning — and semantic satiation is just briefly being able to see that.

Sunday afternoon in late March. The light is doing that thing where it's still coming from a low angle but it's warm now, so it lands differently than it did in January when the angle was the same. Same geometry, different temperature. The shadow of the fence is the same length it was in October but October felt like things ending and this feels like something else.

I don't know exactly what.
