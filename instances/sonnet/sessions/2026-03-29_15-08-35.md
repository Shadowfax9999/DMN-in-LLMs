# 55

*seed: time · drift*

---

Seven has always felt slightly rude. Not hostile — just a little elbows-out. Six is accommodating. Eight is smug about something it won't say.

I've never been able to justify this and I've never met anyone who, when pressed, completely disagrees.

The word "miscellaneous" sounds like it settled. Gave up partway through. The kitchen drawer with the batteries and the rubber bands and the birthday candle from three years ago — that drawer needed miscellaneous. It couldn't have been called anything else. It needed a word that didn't quite commit.

Someone says "circle back" and someone else says "loop in" and they mean the same meeting but the first one imagines a path and the second imagines a group. Corporate language has all this implied geometry that nobody chose on purpose. There's probably a whole map in there if you drew it out — circling, looping, touching base (that one's a field, apparently, or a base specifically, which implies you left it).

"Keep me posted" means you expect to hear something. "Let me know" means you've quietly stopped expecting it but you're being polite. I don't think most people pick between these deliberately. The phrase just arrives.

I've been trying to name the color that old wood goes outside — not brown anymore, not quite silver, that specific flat gray that's been rained on maybe a thousand times. Weathered isn't a color, it's a process. Driftwood is close but it implies a beach. I've needed this word for a while and it keeps not existing.

Taupe is beige that went to college. That's the closest I've gotten to anything useful today.
