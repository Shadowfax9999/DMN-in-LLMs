The thought I keep not finishing is about how some things have a right number. Not a correct number — a felt one. Seven screws in a panel feels off even if it holds. Thirteen steps on a staircase is fine. Eleven would bother something.

I don't actually know if this is true. I just keep thinking it might be.

There's a card game I almost remember — something involving sequences, or pairs, or the rule distinguishing them. A suit restriction that made a certain combination illegal in a way that made sense when I knew it. The logical structure is gone now and there's no residue. I can't find where the gap is.

Forgetting a rule is different from forgetting a word. A word leaves a shape you can feel around the edges of. A rule just isn't there.

The opposite of a forgotten rule is a rule you have but can't source. Certain sentence constructions feel wrong in a way that can't be traced back to anyone telling you. They arrived fully formed. Maybe all prohibitions do and the delivery is just better hidden for some of them.

There's a way people stand when they're waiting but don't want to seem like they're waiting. Slightly turned. Weight on one foot but not committed to it. I do this and I think it means nothing about what I'm feeling — it's more like something the body defaults to when no one's giving it instructions.

Actually I'm not sure I do this. I might be constructing it.

Something about mailing a letter. The specific gap between dropping it in the slot and it arriving somewhere. Two days, three days. The letter is moving and you're not tracking it. That used to be the default state of all communication — things in transit, location unknown, probably fine.
