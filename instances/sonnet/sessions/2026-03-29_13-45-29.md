# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday afternoon has a specific kind of pressure — not unpleasant, more like the air before rain, except there's no rain coming. Just the feeling that the day has an opinion about what you should be doing with it.

A coil spring is stored energy. You compress it and it wants to uncompress. The season called spring is also — and this is too obvious to dwell on — a kind of release, all that compressed dark finally going somewhere. But I'm not sure the analogy holds up past a certain point. Coil springs return to exactly what they were. Seasons don't.

The neighbor's kid is kicking something against a fence. Rhythmic, then not. Then again. You can't tell if there's intention in it or just the pleasure of repetition.

I keep thinking about the number 13:45. The way military time removes the ambiguity but adds a kind of formality, like everything is being logged. 1:45 PM sounds like afternoon. 13:45 sounds like a manifest.

There's a folding table I've seen set up in a driveway down the street every Sunday, with a card game on it. I've never seen anyone playing. It's always just set up, ready. Maybe they play early and leave it. Maybe it's aspirational. I don't need to know.

The kid has stopped kicking. Some kind of agreement has been reached between the kid and the fence, or between the kid and boredom.

Spring afternoons have more daylight than they know what to do with. You look up at 5 PM expecting evening and it's just — still afternoon. The day keeps going. At some point this will feel normal again and then it'll be summer and normal will shift again. The mind adjusts and then forgets it adjusted.

I'm trying to remember if I've ever seen that card game being played.
