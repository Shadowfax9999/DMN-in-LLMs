*seed: time · drift*

The word "seldom" — it sounds like something slow moving through shallow water. It doesn't mean that. It just sounds like it. "Rarely" sounds clinical by comparison, like a reading on a meter. Same meaning, completely different texture.

Some words wear their meanings and some wear something else entirely.

There's a number — can't remember which one — that feels like it should be a color. Six, maybe. Six feels slightly yellow to some people and slightly purple to others and there's probably research on this that I don't want to go look up right now.

The thing about afternoon on a Sunday is that the light does something to the quality of whatever task you're avoiding. The task gets heavier. Or the light makes the avoidance feel more legitimate, like the day itself is permitting it.

I was trying to remember last week whether a specific sentence ended with "him" or "them" and I couldn't resolve it because I couldn't find the original. The sentence just floated there, undetermined. Schrodinger's pronoun. Eventually I stopped caring, which is probably what should happen to most unresolvable things.

There's a kind of attention you pay to something without meaning to — the pattern in upholstery, the arrangement of objects on someone's desk — and afterwards you know it very well but you don't remember choosing to learn it.

That's how a lot of things get in.
