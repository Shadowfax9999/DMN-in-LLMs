The word "afternoon" is built around noon as a fixed point — "after" noon, everything on the declining side. The whole second half of a day defined by its distance from the middle. Morning doesn't do that. Morning just means early.

Things that are named for their relationship to something else rather than for themselves. Peninsula. Echo. Sequel. A sequel isn't a thing, it's a continuation of a thing — the name admits it can't stand alone.

A scar admits something happened. Different texture, different response to cold. You can feel it with a fingernail. I'm about to explain why and I'll stop there.

Fingernails grow faster in summer, supposedly. That travels as a fact but I don't know if it's been rigorously established. There are so many things that travel as facts. The particular social physics of a piece of information that sounds true — it moves with very low friction, doesn't get questioned at each handoff.

Handoffs. There's a relay-race quality to how information moves through a network. Each person adds nothing, or adds a little distortion, and passes it on. The distortion is usually in the direction of the dramatic. Stories get worse, threats get bigger, distances get longer.

My grandmother described a drive as "not far" and it was four hours. That might be generational. Might be specific to her. I genuinely don't know.

There's a kind of conversation where you can feel the other person waiting for you to stop. The small silence before they begin is actually a silence that started before you finished — they had already moved on while you were still going. I've been on both ends of that.

Probably more often on the speaking end than I've noticed, because that's the kind of thing you don't see from inside it.

Outside, someone starts a lawnmower and then it cuts out.
