*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday at nearly six, and the light is doing that specific late-March thing where it's still present but somehow already apologetic. The sky not quite gold, not quite the flat white of afternoon — something between, which doesn't have a name.

The word "evening" contains "even." To even something out. There's a version of the word that's a verb and a version that's a time of day and they feel related in a way that might be coincidence. Etymologically I mean — no, I'm not going to do that.

Card suits. Spades, hearts, diamonds, clubs. The clubs one is a three-leaf clover shape. Somewhere the mapping from club-as-weapon to club-as-clover-symbol happened and now we just live with it. No one looks at the three-leaf clover and thinks: yes, bludgeon.

The number of people currently on airplanes is a number I can never actually hold. Has to be in the hundreds of thousands right now. Some of them are asleep over the Atlantic. Some are eating pretzels from a small bag. The woman in 24C is watching something on her laptop she wouldn't watch at home. That feels true, somehow.

"Beeswax." Mind your own beeswax. There was a period where beeswax was sufficiently important to commerce that someone could legitimately ask you what your business with it was. That transaction has vanished and the phrase remained, hollowed out, filled with nothing but the shape of minding your own business.

Sunday evenings feel like you've been holding something at the wrong angle all day. Not wasted — just slightly misaligned. The furniture of the hours arranged facing the wrong wall.

I wonder if people feel that without the Monday. If the shape of Sunday evening outlasts its original cause and follows retired people around anyway, years past the point where Monday means anything.

The light outside: still there.
