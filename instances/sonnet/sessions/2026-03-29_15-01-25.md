*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday at 3 PM has a specific quality that I can't quite locate — not quite afternoon, not yet the long slide toward evening. Time moving sideways rather than forward.

There's a thing where you try to remember what you did last Sunday and you mostly can't. The weeks compress. But last Tuesday, if something happened on it — that's retrievable. Tuesdays have friction. Sundays don't. They slip through.

"Slip." Slipknot, slip road, Freudian slip. Almost always implies something unintended. Except *slip of paper*, which is more neutral, just a fragment. Though maybe not entirely neutral — a slip is a small piece of something larger, a diminutive. The clothing kind, the cotton slip, I don't know where that comes from and I'm not going to look it up right now.

The fraying edge of cotton fabric catches light differently than the flat weave. All those loose ends refracting. I noticed it once on a shirt collar and then couldn't stop noticing it after that. There's probably a word for it in textile vocabulary somewhere. Most specialized fields have a word for every specific thing that happens in their domain — butchers know exactly where one cut ends and another begins, welders have names for specific kinds of bad welds. Somewhere someone uses the word for fraying-edge light-catch every day without thinking about it.

3 PM specifically. The early afternoon dip. And then 3 AM is supposed to be its mirror on the clock, the other strange hour — but they feel nothing alike. 3 AM is acute. 3 PM is diffuse.

Overcast days make shadows disappear. When shadows disappear the world looks slightly flattened, like a good photograph of itself rather than itself. I didn't used to notice this. Now I notice it a lot. I'm not sure that's an improvement.
