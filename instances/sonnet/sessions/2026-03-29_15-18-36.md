Folding paper makes a crease because the fibers break. Folding fabric just stresses them — they'd rather be flat but they'll accommodate. The fold in a shirt collar is a negotiation. The fold in a piece of paper is a small catastrophe that holds its shape.

There's a perimeter around a stranger, somewhere between fifteen and twenty-five feet, where walking toward them starts something. Eye contact or its avoidance. Left-right adjustment. The same distance, standing still, is neutral. Motion is what activates the social weight. A parked car fifty feet away is a different object than a car fifty feet away and coming toward you.

The almost-sneeze. The mechanism is loaded, everything's queued, and then the body withdraws the proposal. There's a residue — you're left in the space where the sneeze was going to be. The almost-yawn is similar, the jaw starts and then stops partway. The stumble that self-corrects before you've registered tripping: your legs dealt with it and then filed a brief report.

The body runs a lot of proposals that don't complete. Probably most of them.
