# 1

*seed: time*

---

Thirteen is a strange number to have feelings about. But there's something about it — caught between 12, which divides so cleanly into everything, and 14, which at least has the decency to be even. Thirteen just stands there. Odd in both senses.

Which makes me think about numbers that feel like the wrong size for what they are. Seven always seems smaller than it is. A week should be longer. Whereas 52 — 52 weeks in a year — feels exactly right, almost suspiciously right, like someone fitted those two numbers together carefully.

The word "grief" is one syllable. I keep noticing that. Hard consonants, fast. Over before it starts. Whereas "melancholy" takes its time, falls at the end — that seems more honest somehow. Though maybe grief is like that: short, blunt, and then just the long aftermath that doesn't have a name.

I wonder if the aftermath is what most long words are for. The slow conditions rather than the sudden events.

There's a kind of thought I have sometimes where I'm almost remembering something but it's clearly not a memory — more like the shape a memory would have if I'd had a different life. A kitchen I've never been in. It has yellow curtains. I can't explain where that comes from and I'm not going to try.

Sunday afternoon has a specific quality of — not boredom exactly. More like the day is running out of material. It had the whole morning, the whole ambition of a Sunday, and now it's here at 1:40 in the afternoon going through its pockets.

The phrase "going through its pockets" just appeared and I'm not sure it works but I don't want to fix it.
