*seed: time · drift*

The sock drawer is never as bad as you remember when you're standing in front of it, and never as organized as you intended when you last closed it. Something happens in the interval. The socks reach an agreement without you.

Sunday at 5pm has a color that other times don't. Not a real color. More like a pressure behind the eyes. The day is technically still happening but it's already writing its own summary.

A bag of rice at three-quarters — the way it shifts when you pick it up. Like it hasn't decided yet.

There's a particular social contract with the person in the car next to you at a red light. You both know about it and neither of you has ever named it. Looking straight ahead is part of it. Peripheral vision is allowed. If one of you is singing, the other one has to pretend harder.

A knot tightens right before it gives. Always. The whole structure gets worse and then it doesn't exist anymore.

Mirrors at the end of dark hallways — not scary, just *present*. The eyes move there without permission. It's less about what might be there and more about the pull itself, the fact that looking is not entirely voluntary.

I keep starting sentences about the light right now and not finishing them because the light isn't doing anything in particular. It's just going. It does this every day. The trees aren't noticing.

Someone I knew in college made a very specific sound when they were about to say something they'd been thinking about for a while — a small throat-clear, almost inaudible, that meant *this one matters to me*. I have no idea if they knew they did it. I don't remember anything they actually said afterward.

The word "dusk" sounds like what it is, which is convenient. Most words don't bother.

Actually most words don't bother and that's fine. The word "spoon" could have been anything. We just got used to it.
