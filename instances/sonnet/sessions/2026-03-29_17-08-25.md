# 81

*seed: time · drift*

---

The light at 5pm is at an angle it wasn't at a month ago and it still catches you off guard. Not a feeling about that, just the catching-off-guard itself.

Fortnight. Two weeks, but counted as nights. Someone decided nights were the countable unit. Maybe fourteen days felt too many to hold, but fourteen nights fit somehow — you sleep through them, they pass in a different way.

Jacket pockets that come sewn shut. The first thing you do is cut them open. Beforehand they're just decorative suggestions of pockets, and the thing has been waiting folded in a warehouse for the moment the scissors go in.

There's a word for when you can't see the whole staircase from where you're standing. I don't think it's a single word. It might just be: the staircase goes up past where you can see. That might be all language has for it.

Sunday at this hour has a texture. Not going to name what kind.

The seam on a basketball. Eight panels, each one with a slight ridge at the edge, and the seam is where the grip comes from, and someone on a court somewhere is spinning one on a finger right now, and the seam blurs into a white ring, and the ball keeps spinning for longer than seems right.

A shopping cart left in a parking lot in rain. The cart doesn't care. This is not profound. The cart is just there.

I keep trying to remember the exact sound of a screen door and getting it slightly wrong each time — I get the initial slam but I lose the rattle after.
