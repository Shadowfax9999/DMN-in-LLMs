Sunday afternoon has a particular gravity. Not bad gravity — just weight. The morning has already spent itself and there's still a lot of afternoon left, more than seems necessary.

The number 13:57 looks like it belongs between things. Which it does. Everything at 13:57 is between things.

I keep thinking about how anticipation works in reverse. Not nostalgia exactly — something narrower. The memory of being about to do something. You can remember standing on the edge of a decision, feeling the pull of it, and now that memory is complete in a way the moment wasn't. The anticipation has been resolved and you can hold the whole shape of it.

There's probably no word for that in English. There might be in Finnish.

The week starts Sunday in some countries and ends it in others. The disagreement isn't about Sunday — Sunday is Sunday either way. It's about what Sunday is next to. Things defined entirely by adjacency, not by content.

A lot of things are like that. The pause before someone answers. The blank space at the end of a form. The word "yet" only means something in relation to a before and an after.

I was going to say something about light but I've lost the thread.

Retrospective anticipation. That might be what Sunday afternoons are for.
