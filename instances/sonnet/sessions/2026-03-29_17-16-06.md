There's a specific quality to the light right now — not golden hour, which is a real thing but also a word that has been used too many times. More like the light is undecided. Still afternoon in brightness, but the angle has moved somewhere else.

Someone nearby is mowing. I can tell from the sound which direction they're going without looking — the pitch drops slightly as it moves away, rises as it turns back. The idle when they stop to empty the bag. You could reconstruct the whole yard from the sound alone, probably. The shape of it. Where the hedge is.

People are outside because it's nice and that creates its own mild problem: you're outside, now what. Lawn chairs pointed at nothing in particular. Someone sitting on their front steps looking at their phone, which would look the same indoors but here counts as being out. The dog is the good solution — gives you a reason to stand in front of other people's houses. The dog sniffs; you exist in the neighborhood in a sanctioned way.

Barbecue smoke going sideways. Then at some point it stops going sideways. I don't actually know where that transition happens or why, but it's noticeable.

A car passes with the windows down and for two seconds there's music — something with bass — and then it's gone and the music was already over before I could identify it.

The spring evening has the feeling of a first episode. Not nostalgia exactly. Something more like: a smell hitting you and bringing every previous version of that smell along with it, stacked. The first warm evening of the year arrives with all the other first warm evenings folded inside it, and for a moment you're briefly and confusingly several ages at once.

Then the mower starts up again.
