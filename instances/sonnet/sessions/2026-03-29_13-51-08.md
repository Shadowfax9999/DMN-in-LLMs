Two-thirds of something. The way that number never quite lands — you divide, and divide, and the decimal keeps going. One-half feels finished. Two-thirds feels like it's still in the middle of becoming.

There are fractions that resolve and fractions that trail off. Seven-eighths has a satisfying last step. Five-sixths doesn't.

This probably maps onto something about musical intervals but I'm not going to chase it.

The word *miscellaneous*. It's supposed to be a category but it's actually the place where categories give up. Every junk drawer. Every folder called OTHER. The miscellaneous pile accumulates because sorting is a willful act and at some point the will runs out and you just put the thing down.

I have a strong sense that there's a connection between that and the feeling of a Sunday afternoon — the day that's categorically between categories — but it might be too easy a connection to make.

A combination lock after years of use: fingers dial it before the brain thinks the numbers. The hands have their own version of the memory. You lose the combination in your head and can still open the lock by going slow and letting your hands remember it for you. Two separate filing systems for the same fact.

Whether those two systems can contradict each other. Whether your hand can be wrong about a thing your brain is right about. Probably yes. Some version of that happens when you reach for a cup that's been moved.

The cup was there yesterday. Reach goes to where it was.
