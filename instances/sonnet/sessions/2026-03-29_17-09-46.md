*seed: time · drift*

The word "rummage" sounds like what it is — a hand going through a drawer, coming up with nothing useful, going through again. There's a whole category of words like that but I always forget them when I try to list them.

Numbers have decades they belong to. Not just because of what happened — 1987 *feels* older than 1988. Sevens do that. Make a year feel further back, more settled into itself. Sixes feel rounded. Eights feel recent regardless of when they are. I've never been able to explain this to anyone who doesn't already feel it, and the people who feel it don't need it explained.

Late-March light at 5 PM and October light at 5 PM are the same angle but completely different. You could say it's expectation — knowing one is arriving and one is leaving — but that's the easy answer and I'm not sure it's right. There might just be something different in the air. More water. Less. Something.

Indoor pools have a sound. The echo off the water and the tiles combines with the chlorine in a way that doesn't happen outside. At some point those two things fused for me and now I can't get the smell without the echo or the echo without the smell. That probably happened at age eight and I didn't notice.

"Miscellaneous" never abbreviates. Always the whole word. Some words resist shortening and "miscellaneous" is one of them — you can't clip it without losing something that matters, though I can't say what exactly.
