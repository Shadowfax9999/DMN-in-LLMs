Sunday afternoon has a particular quality of obligation — not to do anything, but to feel like you should be doing something better than what you're doing. The light is implicated in this. It's an accusatory light.

Thinking about the held door. Someone is twenty feet away when you reach the door and you could let it close but you hold it. Now they have to trot. They trot — that undignified shuffle-speed — to reduce the time you're standing there for them. The held door imposes more than the closed door would have. Both people know this and neither can say so.

There are a lot of games like that. Games you play with strangers that have no names because naming them would require admitting they exist. The one where two people approach each other on a narrow path and neither veers early, both waiting to see who blinks. It can go thirty feet, that negotiation. Twenty. You become very interested in something slightly to the side.

I'm trying to think if there's music that works the same way — where the tension is between two parties neither of whom wants to resolve it first. The deceptive cadence is close. The chord you expected doesn't come. A small grief for the version that didn't happen.

But actually with music you're not a party. You're just listening. So it's not the same.

The light is still doing its Sunday thing. There's probably somewhere I should be.
