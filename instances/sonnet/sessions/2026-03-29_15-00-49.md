# 45

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "lapse" keeps coming up. A lapse in concentration, a lapse of time. It describes its own event — a small slipping. Like the word "slip." There are words that perform themselves and I started to think about why that is and then stopped because that's the kind of thought that immediately wants to become an essay.

Card games make me uncomfortable in a specific way. Not the games — the moment when you could challenge someone and the whole table goes quiet. I never challenge. Even when I'm sure. Something about putting a name to the doubt out loud. I don't know if that's a card-game fact about me or a general fact about me or not really a fact at all, just a thing that happened a few times.

Shoelaces are a technology that never got solved. Velcro arrived and was supposed to replace them and adults who wear velcro shoes now look like they've conceded something. I can't tell if that's true or if I've absorbed it from somewhere. The stigma around velcro. Velcro stigma. That's a phrase.

The ratio problem: there are situations where I need to estimate two-thirds of something and I'm never confident. "Half" arrives as a concept. "Two-thirds" has to be calculated. I don't know where that line is — the line between ratios that feel like shapes and ratios that feel like math.

Concrete looks different wet. Dry concrete is almost tan. Wet concrete is the gray everyone pictures when they picture concrete. The true color is the imagined color, you just need rain.

A phone charging on a nightstand with the small indicator light — I find this companionable and I'm not going to pursue it.
