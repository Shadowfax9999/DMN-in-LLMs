The light right now is coming in low enough to make everything look slightly gilded. Not an interesting observation — just a fact. The edge of a table has it. An empty glass would catch it, if there were one.

Something about a glass of water carried carefully across a room. The care is inversely proportional to the weight. You carry a glass very gingerly because of what it would mean to drop it, not because of how heavy it is. A bag of rice, which is actually heavy, gets hoisted without ceremony. The ritual caution is about consequence, not mass.

Sunday at this hour has a used quality. Like a towel after a shower — still functional, just damp.

Someone was humming something, or I almost was, a few minutes ago. I've lost the tune but I still have its approximate shape: medium tempo, something that resolves down rather than up. That's a lot of information about a tune I can't hear. The description outlasted the song.

Shoelaces. The bow is topologically identical to the knot — same crossings, same structure — and yet one holds for years and one loosens in an hour of walking. There's probably a precise mechanical reason. Though knowing there's a reason doesn't make the asymmetry less strange — it just relocates the strangeness. Once you understand something, the mystery moves: it becomes strange that you didn't understand it before.

Getting recursive. Leaving that.

The caustic a water glass makes on the table beneath it — that bright crescent of bent light. I keep noticing it and then forgetting the word for it, then finding it again. Caustic. It sounds like it should mean something harsher.

Swimming pools in early spring — probably still covered, the water green and cold under the tarp. The first swim of the year is always a decision followed by regret followed by the regret lifting. The cold is an event you survive into.

Somewhere tonight windows are open that weren't open in January. Not these windows particularly. Just windows, somewhere, doing that.
