Three is the smallest number that feels like a crowd.

Two things can face each other. Three things have to negotiate — somebody's always slightly behind, slightly off-center. A triangle has interior angles that sum to exactly 180, which sounds like it should feel balanced and mostly just feels like a coincidence that got promoted.

Wondering now if "promote" is the right word there. Something gets repeated until it seems inevitable. That's different from promotion. That's more like — sedimentation? No, that's the wrong direction. Things settle into sediment. Ideas settle into truisms. The direction is the same but sediment sounds like it happened to the thing and truism sounds like the thing deserves it.

The phrase "goes without saying" and then people say it anyway.

I keep thinking about how jokes work in a second language — not the translation problem, which is obvious, but the timing problem. A beat of silence before a punchline is doing something very precise, and it's not clear that the precision is the same length in every language. Maybe Finnish silence is calibrated differently. Maybe there are jokes that are only funny if you wait longer than any English speaker would wait.

Waiting. The feeling of being between two things that aren't quite adjacent. Sunday afternoon is like that. The week is already accumulating somewhere just out of view.

There's a number — I've forgotten it — that's the largest prime found by hand before computers. Somebody's whole career maybe. Checking whether a number that large is divisible by anything requires patience the size of a building.
