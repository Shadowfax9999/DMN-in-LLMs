# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "afternoon" cuts the day unevenly. Morning gets roughly half, afternoon gets roughly half, but they feel like different proportions — afternoon always feels longer, or maybe just heavier, the way the second half of a long drive does even when the miles are the same.

There's a plant somewhere that only flowers for one afternoon a year. Or maybe I made that up. The brain generates facts with the same confidence regardless of whether they're real, which is a design problem no one's fixed.

Trying to remember if I've ever actually seen a hummingbird hover in place or if I've only seen photographs. The memory and the photograph feel identical in storage. This seems like it should bother me more.

Soap bubbles are thinner than a wavelength of light in some places, which is why they show color and also why they pop from those spots first — no, wait, I'm not going to explain it. The colors are there. Iridescence. The word sounds like what it is: multiple things at once, sliding.

Sunday has a texture. Not the religious version — just the afternoon-light, slightly-too-quiet version. The week hasn't started yet. The week also ended. There's a gap that isn't a gap, just a named space.

I keep thinking about how certain numbers feel wrong in certain contexts. Eleven dollars feels cheap. Eleven thousand feels expensive. Eleven itself feels like a number someone forgot to do anything with. Twelve is organized. Ten is organized. Eleven just stands there.

The afternoon is going somewhere whether or not I track it.
