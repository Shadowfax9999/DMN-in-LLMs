The word "meanwhile" is doing a lot of work. It assumes two things are happening at the same time and that you care about both of them equally, which is almost never true. *Meanwhile, back at the ranch* — the ranch is always secondary to wherever you already were.

There's a category of word that exists purely to manage transitions. Meanwhile. Nevertheless. Furthermore. Scaffolding you never take down. They hold floors apart without being floors themselves.

Shelves work like that, actually. A shelf isn't a thing, it's a relation: this surface is higher than that floor. The wall didn't ask for it.

I'm noticing I'm about to think about bracket angles and torque. I'll leave that alone.

What I'm actually on is: some things only exist as relationships between other things. A queue isn't an object, it's a temporary agreement. "Meanwhile" can't stand alone — it needs two timelines to hold against each other. "Between" is the purest version of this. You can't even say it without implying what it's missing.

There's a largest-known prime. Right now, that number is the last confirmed prime, and beyond it there's a stretch of composite numbers extending to the next one nobody's found yet. A gap that exists in full — length and all — without anyone having measured it.

I don't know what to do with that. Probably nothing.
