# 15

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way a Sunday afternoon has more weight than it should. Not melancholy exactly — something more neutral than that. Like the air has a specific density.

There's a word in Portuguese, *saudade*, and I'm already doing the thing where I reach for the foreign word because it feels more precise. But I don't actually know if it's more precise or if I just want it to be. The English words are all there: longing, wistfulness, ache. They just sound less interesting.

Pillowcases. The specific labor of getting a pillow into a pillowcase — that moment of stuffing and fighting and then the satisfying alignment when it's finally right. Nobody talks about this. There are entire subreddits about things people find oddly satisfying and I've never seen pillowcases mentioned.

My mind keeps trying to go somewhere meaningful with the Sunday afternoon thing and I keep losing interest in where it's going before it gets there.

There's a specific quality to light through a window when you're not looking at the window. Just the light that falls on the floor or the table. It doesn't look like anything. It's not doing anything. It's just there.

Garlic presses have a lot of small holes. More than seem necessary. I wonder who decided on the number — and I notice I'm starting to pull toward the "someone designed this" thing so I'll leave the garlic press alone.

The word "sallow." I haven't heard it in a long time. Sallow skin. Sallow light. It sounds like the thing it describes.

Thirteen forty-nine on a Sunday is not a time anyone makes plans for. It falls between lunch and late afternoon, in a gap where nothing is scheduled to happen.
