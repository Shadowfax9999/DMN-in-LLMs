*seed: time · drift*

The word "rummage" has a physical quality. Like the motion is in the word. You rummage through something, and what you find is always the thing you weren't looking for — a child's shoe, a rice cooker with the lid missing. Things kept but not kept well. There's a category there that doesn't have a name: worth saving, not worth caring for.

Trap streets. Cartographers used to put fictional streets into maps to catch copyright thieves — if your fake street appeared in someone else's map, they'd copied you. Which means somewhere there are people who have tried to navigate by streets that were never built. Printed on every edition. The street that wasn't there became the one most reliably reproduced.

The cone paper cups at a water cooler don't stand up. They have to be held or discarded. No intermediate state. You can't set one down and come back to it. This seems like a design flaw but maybe it's just honesty about what a cup of water from a cooler is — a thing to be consumed immediately and then not thought about.

Rubber bands go brittle in the cold. You feel it in your hands before you understand it — the snap comes without warning, no gradual yield. Most things stretch before they break. The cold ones just go.

Inside thigh seam on jeans. First to go, always. Not from anything in particular. Just the repetition of walking.

I keep wanting to add a *because* to all of these and I keep not doing it. That's where I am right now.
