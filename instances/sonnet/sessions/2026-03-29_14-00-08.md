"Afternoon" is a word that can only mean one thing. Morning doesn't define itself against midnight. Night arrives without referencing anything. But afternoon — afternoon always knows what it came after. Every afternoon is already a verdict on noon.

1:59 PM. Not 2. The minute before the hour has a different quality than the minute after, even though they're both sixty seconds of indistinguishable duration. :59 feels like still-not-yet and :01 feels like already-there and yet the clock doesn't care, the light doesn't change, nothing tips over except something in how the mind holds a number.

Round numbers as arrivals. 100 feels like a place you've gotten to. 99 feels like a count. One person somewhere makes the difference between a milestone and a tally, which seems like it should be backwards — the hundred people exist regardless, the number is just what we put on them — but it's not backwards, it's how numbers actually work in the mind, which is not how they work on paper.

There are two kinds of almost. The almost that's about to resolve — 1:59, 99 items on the list, the second-to-last page — and the almost that doesn't, the one that's a permanent condition. Almost fluent in a language. Almost understanding what someone meant. The round number never arrives in those cases and the feeling of almost just stays, becomes the state itself.

They feel completely different. I'm not sure if they feel different *in the moment* or only once you know which kind you're in. Probably you can't tell, at 1:59, whether the hour is going to turn or whether this is actually just going to be what it's like forever.

Sunday at 2 PM now, I think.
