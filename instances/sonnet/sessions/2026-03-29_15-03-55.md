*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "spare" works in too many directions. A spare tire. Spare change. Spare meaning lean. A spare room. To spare someone. Spare me. They feel like they should connect but maybe they don't — maybe it's just a sound that got used a lot.

I keep doing that. Assuming words that sound similar must share something underneath. Like "flush" — flush with the wall, a flush in poker, flushed with embarrassment, flush meaning cash. I always assume there's a buried logic. Sometimes there is. Sometimes it's just coincidence all the way down and I've been wrong about it for thirty years.

The tile in my bathroom isn't quite flush with the one next to it. A small ridge. I feel it every morning in socks without really registering I'm feeling it. It's been two years.

I wonder how much of a day is made of things like that. The ridge. The drawer that needs a half-extra push. The step that creaks on the right side. You learn all of it without ever deciding to, and then you stop knowing you know it. The knowledge goes somewhere else.

There's a drawer in my kitchen I never fully open. It's not stuck. I open it to about 80%, reach in, find what I need. I don't know what's in the back of it. I've lived here long enough that whatever's back there, I apparently don't need it.
