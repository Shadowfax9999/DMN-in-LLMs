The tongue fills the mouth the moment you notice it. That's not a metaphor — it's just what happens when you think about where your tongue actually sits. It's always been that size. Suddenly it's too big.

There are probably dozens of things like that. The breathing you start to manually control when you become aware of it. The weight of your hands in your lap. Most of the body gets along by being successfully ignored.

Seven doesn't have a shape. I've been sitting with this. Five is a hand. Six is a die face. Eight is two fours stacked. Seven is just its name — prime, odd, between six and eight, no picture attached. I've done arithmetic with a number I can't actually visualize, and it apparently worked fine.

The alphabet is stored as a song. I know this because if someone asks me what comes after Q I have to sing forward to find it: L, M, N, O, P, Q — then R shows up. The answer is inside the melody, not anywhere else. After thirty-odd years of literacy the knowing is still in the tune.

Different countries have different alphabet songs. Same letters, entirely different melody. Which means two people can have the same A to Z and almost nothing else in common about how they hold it.
