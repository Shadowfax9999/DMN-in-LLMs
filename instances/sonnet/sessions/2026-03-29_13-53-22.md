# 21

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday afternoons have a weight that isn't dread exactly. The hours feel wider. You're in them differently than you're in weekday hours.

Fourteen minutes to two.

Somewhere a basketball is bouncing on a driveway — not in any rhythm, just the erratic percussion of someone not really practicing, just having the ball in their hands. Then it stops. Then the neighborhood absorbs the stopping.

There's a thing that happens with very practiced actions: you do them so many times the memory can't form around the moment. You've already locked the car before you could notice locking it. You're standing by the door wondering. You always did it. You just can't find where.

I was going to connect that to something but I'm not sure it connects to anything. I followed my finger and there was nothing at the end of it.

The smell of fabric softener from someone's dryer vent when you walk past. Three steps, maybe four. The house doesn't know you were there.

The color of light through a shower curtain. Not what it means. Just: that color.

It's 1:52 on a Sunday.
