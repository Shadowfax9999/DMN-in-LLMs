Sunday has a different weight than Saturday even when they look the same. Saturday is released; Sunday is borrowed. You spend one, you owe the other back.

Weeks have shapes. Monday is a wall. Friday is a long slope down. Wednesday is a ridge you crest. I've never worked out what Tuesday is — something low, flat, consistently underlit. Tuesday is probably the most honest day.

There's a word I can never say out loud without feeling like I'm performing it rather than using it: "particularly." I don't think I mispronounce it. It just goes strange in the mouth. "Rural" has been like this since I was maybe twelve. You say it until it sounds like an animal call — a thing that doesn't mean anything, just a noise a creature makes.

The term for that, when a word repeats until it detaches — semantic something. I know it. It starts with a sound like "se" or possibly the word "jam" is in there somewhere. It'll come back on its own.

"Approximately" does real philosophical work that largely goes unacknowledged. It implies a center that exists — a true value you're circling — even when you can't reach it. "About" doesn't make that claim. About is less committal. In some ways more honest about the honesty.

I keep meaning to understand Zeno well enough that it stops feeling like a trick. I've read the resolution maybe four times. Each time I follow it to the end and think: yes, right, okay. And then it evaporates. The understanding won't stay in the hand.

Some things are just going to be like that.
