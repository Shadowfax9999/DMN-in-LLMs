There's a word I haven't used in a while. *Lozenge*. It just surfaces — not because anything prompted it, but the way a name will float up days after you stopped trying to remember it.

Some words get used up. Some wait.

*Lozenge* is a shape, a cough drop, a heraldic term. The shape is interesting — a diamond oriented the same way as a diamond, but somehow softer. Lozenge implies something yielding. Same geometry, different physics. Or not even that — different expectation of touch.

The word's been used now. It'll sink again for a while.

There's a decision that arrives already made. You notice you've decided something — going to leave the party early, going to order the other thing — without any moment of actually deciding. Awareness landing downstream of whatever happened upstream. The upstream part doesn't announce itself.

Something similar with music: you don't decide to hear the bass line separately from the melody, but at some point you are, and there was no crossing-over moment. Attention as something that happens to you as much as something you direct.

Parallel play. Two people reading in the same room, neither coordinating, neither particularly aware of the other. The room is different than if one person were reading alone — not louder, not quieter, just different. A quality to shared aloneness. You perform the not-performing. Sit slightly differently than you would alone.

I sat differently on trains when I thought no one was watching versus when I suspected someone might be. I'm not sure which posture was more honest.
