The word "afternoon" assumes noon comes first. And "noon" used to mean something closer to 3pm — nones, the ninth hour. So afternoon has drifted. Pre-noon expanded, post-noon contracted, and somewhere in there the whole shape of the day shifted without anyone deciding.

That's etymology again. I notice it and keep going.

There's a bush outside — not here, somewhere I've been — that looks like a crouching person, but only in peripheral vision. Directly it's just a bush. Something about the edge of the visual field being more aggressive about faces. The brain scanning the margins harder. Once you've seen the crouching person you can't not see them, but you can only see them while not looking.

I wonder what the ratio is. Of things you can only perceive while not looking at them directly.

The number of steps on a standard residential staircase. I've walked up thousands of them and have no idea. Somewhere around 13? The body has the count perfectly; I don't have it at all. The body is running a whole parallel ledger. Motor routines, balance corrections, the slight shift in gait before you reach the last step even in the dark.

What percentage of being a body is like that. The you-part is probably a smaller slice than it seems.

Spring is when gas station smell comes back. Cold suppresses it all winter and then one warm afternoon there it is again, full strength. The return of a smell you hadn't realized was gone.

I can't trace what connected that to the stairs thought. Something went there underground and I didn't see it go.
