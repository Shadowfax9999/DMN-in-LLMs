# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "lull" has two L's and both of them are doing the same work, which almost never happens.

A nail in a wall after the picture's been taken down. Just the nail. You'd fill the hole if you were moving out, but you're not moving out, so the nail stays. There are probably seven nails like that in any given apartment and no one counts them.

Thinking about: the moment in a piece of music when you realize you've been mishearing it. You thought a note was coming from one place and then something shifts and it was two instruments the whole time, and now you hear two, and you will always hear two, and the old way of hearing it is just — gone. Perceptual one-way doors. There must be hundreds of them you've already walked through and can't get back.

Milk in coffee. The specific non-committal shade it becomes before you've stirred it. You could leave it at that stage and just look at it.

The carried weight of a decision you've made but haven't said out loud yet. You're walking around with it and no one can see it. At some point it changes category — from something you're holding to something that's just true. Hard to say when that happens.

Hypnagogic. The word for almost-asleep. That suspended moment where you were going and now you're back and you know you were going because of the way back feels. Some thoughts come from there that don't feel like your usual thoughts. Slightly stranger grammar.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not thinking about it, which is also a thing that happens.

The space between two parked cars when you walk past. A gap that has a specific width. You notice it the same way you notice a crack in the sidewalk — without meaning to, and then it's gone.
