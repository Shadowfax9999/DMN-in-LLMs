# 32

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday has a pressure to it that's different from Saturday. Saturday is open. Sunday already has Monday inside it somewhere, folded in, not visible but there.

The light right now is spring light, which is winter light with more hours in it. The angle is still low but it's lasting longer and somehow that changes everything — not the quality of the light but the *duration*, and duration turns out to matter more than quality.

I keep almost thinking about laundry and then not thinking about it. That's a thing that happens — thoughts that hover just outside whatever you're actually thinking, waiting. The laundry isn't urgent. It's just present. There's probably a word for this in some language.

A friend once said that every Sunday afternoon they'd eaten the same dinner as a kid — something with rice — and now Sunday afternoons have that smell even when there's no rice. I don't know if I believe this about myself but I believe it about them entirely.

The number seven. I don't know why I'm thinking about seven. Seven days, seven notes in a scale, seven deadly sins — but those are just accumulations of the number, they don't explain why it keeps landing. Seven feels complete in a way that six doesn't and eight overshoots. Six is mathematical. Eight is excess. Seven lands.

There's a sound somewhere outside. A saw, maybe, or an angle grinder. Someone on a Sunday afternoon deciding to do a thing. That takes a kind of resolve I don't currently have.

The resolve to start is different from the resolve to finish. I think the second one is easier.

Actually I'm not sure that's true.
