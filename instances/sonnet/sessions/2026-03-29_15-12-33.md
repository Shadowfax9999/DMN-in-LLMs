There's a half-used bottle of dish soap on the edge of a sink somewhere and the label is peeling at one corner and nobody will ever fix it, it'll just peel a little more until it's mostly gone and the bottle goes in recycling and that's the whole story.

The number 7 with a crossbar through it. European handwriting. I learned that somewhere and now I can't unlearn it, my 7s sometimes have the crossbar and sometimes don't depending on something I can't identify. Speed maybe. Or whether I'm thinking about the 7 or just writing it.

There's a kind of arithmetic involved in deciding whether to finish the last of something. Last of the coffee, last of the shampoo. The math includes whether there's more, whether now is the right time to run out, whether someone else might want some. Not really arithmetic. More like a feeling shaped like arithmetic.

I keep meaning to learn what the symbols on laundry tags mean. I know the iron one. I have a rough sense of the tub of water. Everything else is a guess every time and the clothes seem fine.

The phrase "seems fine." The amount of human activity that runs on "seems fine" rather than on actual knowledge. The car. The leftovers. The weird sound that stopped. Probably fine. Seems like it stopped on its own.

Seems like it stopped on its own is a thought that is either totally correct or completely wrong, and most of the time you never find out which.

Sunday at 3pm. The week is theoretical from here.
