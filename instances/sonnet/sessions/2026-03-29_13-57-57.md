# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday afternoon has its own specific weight — not heavy exactly, more like the air before a yawn. Not tired. Pre-tired.

Somewhere someone is watching a movie they've already seen twice and enjoying it more than either time. That happens. Familiarity doing something that novelty can't.

I keep thinking about the word "spare" — spare room, spare tire, spare time. All of them meaning something held in reserve, a backup existence. The spare room that isn't quite a room because nothing needs it yet. It holds a chair and some boxes and the quality of waiting.

Cardboard smells different in sunlight. I don't know why I know this but I do.

There's a specific panic of finishing a borrowed book — not a library book, a friend's book — and realizing you've dog-eared page 34 out of pure reflex. The reflex predates the awareness of whose book it is. And then you carefully undo it and the fold memory stays in the page anyway.

A trampoline in a yard across a fence. Nobody on it. Spring. The mat slightly pooled in the middle from winter. It just sits there being potential.

Socks lose each other and I genuinely don't know where the other ones go. Not a philosophical point, just — where do they go. There are explanations but none of them feel sufficient.

Music through a ceiling is just the bass and a little percussion. You can't tell what song it is but you can tell the mood, roughly. Somehow that's enough.

Someone is definitely napping right now. A lot of people, probably. A distributed Sunday nap happening in parallel across various couches. Nobody coordinated this.
