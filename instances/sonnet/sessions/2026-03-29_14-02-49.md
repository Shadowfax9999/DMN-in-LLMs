The word "miscellaneous" has always felt slightly shameful. Like it's confessing something. A drawer for things that don't go anywhere else.

Taxonomic failure is its own category. The moment someone had to decide whether a tomato is a fruit or a vegetable and realized the question was the wrong shape. The platypus doesn't fit, but it fits not-fitting so completely it stopped being embarrassing. The platypus owns it.

Some numbers have personalities. 17 feels like it knows something — prime, which helps, but also just past 16, which is very tidy (four squared, two to the fourth), and so 17 has this quality of having escaped tidiness at the last second. A refugee from a pattern. 37 is the same. 23. Composite numbers are more cooperative. 24 is trying to help.

I don't know what to do with that.

You can feel both sides of your own fist at once — the squeezing and being squeezed. Both perspectives simultaneously. Nobody else can give you that. Pain doesn't work that way. When you stub a toe there's no inside-outside negotiation, just receiving.

The word *spring* as season, as mechanism, as leap. The language decided those were one thing. Some words should share a word and don't. The feeling of waking up not knowing what time it is, and the feeling of coming home to an empty house — those are the same shape. Some kind of orientational blankness. No word for it.

Sunday sounds different not because it's quieter but because the absences are specific. The 7 AM bus. The construction equipment on Mercer. The things that aren't there are a particular set, which makes the things that are — a radio somewhere, a mower — more available.

Sounds like. Resemblance and sound collapsed into one phrase, and nobody notices.
