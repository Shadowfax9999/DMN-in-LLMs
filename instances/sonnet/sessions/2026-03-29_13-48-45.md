# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

The ceiling fan is on its lowest setting and the shadow it makes on the wall is less a rotation than a slow suggestion that something might be rotating. You have to look away and look back.

There's a cat somewhere in this neighborhood — not barking, obviously, but there's a quality to Sunday afternoons where you could imagine hearing anything and it would fit. A piano. A lawnmower starting and giving up. The specific frequency of a child being called inside.

Thinking about: the word "afternoon." After noon. Everything after noon is named in relation to noon, which ended hours ago. Morning names itself. Evening names itself. But afternoon just keeps hauling around this reference to a moment that's already gone.

The light at this angle hits the dust on the windowsill and makes it look like something you'd see in a geology museum behind glass. Which is roughly what it is.

I keep almost thinking about a thing I was thinking about yesterday and then not quite getting to it. Something about how reversible processes work in theory — no, I'm not going down that. Something else. The way certain fabrics hold their shape and others don't. How you can fold a cotton shirt perfectly and it comes out with those same creases, but linen just does whatever it wants. Linen has opinions.

The Sunday feeling is real and nobody fully explains it and maybe that's fine. It's not just that work starts tomorrow. Animals feel it too, or maybe they don't, there's no way to know that. The quality of 2 PM on a Sunday is different from 2 PM on a Thursday and part of that is just you but maybe only part.

Somewhere out there a screen door just closed. That's the sound of spring more than anything else.
