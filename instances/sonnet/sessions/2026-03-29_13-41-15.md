Sunday has a direction. Not a goal — a lean. By 1 PM the lean is already away from morning and not yet toward evening and so there's this flat unaccounted-for stretch in the middle where the day just sits.

The light agrees. 13:40 is the uninteresting light hour. It's just bright. The morning light had an angle and the evening light will have a color and right now there's just a lot of undifferentiated photons landing on everything equally.

Numbers have times of day, sort of. Not synesthesia exactly, more like loose residue from clocks. 7 feels like morning. 4 is early, regardless of AM or PM — 4 PM feels like it should be before dawn. 13 in military time sounds like deep afternoon even though it's just past lunch. The translation always adds an hour of feeling.

---

There's a window in a sneeze. Right before it happens — the suspension. Everything else actually stops. Breathing stops. The whole organism pauses and waits to find out what's going to happen. And sometimes it doesn't happen and you're just left standing there with your face slightly open.

What is that moment on the other side called. When the sneeze doesn't come. There's a German word for everything but I'm not going to look for one right now.

Foam on a cappuccino. Real foam for maybe two minutes. Then it's just milk with a faint texture-memory. The window closes and nobody announces it closed.

I keep thinking about brief windows today, which is maybe just Sunday at 1:40 in March doing what it does.

Or not. It's fine either way.
