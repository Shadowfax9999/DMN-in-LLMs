The word "provisional" has been with me for a bit. Provisional fixes. The bungee cord someone looped around a car trunk because the latch stopped working, and then the bungee cord became the latch and nobody thought about it again. Provisional things that outlast the original thing they were patching.

There's probably a rule about this. The temporary repair that's still there twenty years later. The folded cardboard under the wobbly table leg that becomes part of the table.

Nails versus screws. The choice means something — a nail says this is permanent, a screw says I might need to revisit this. Nobody ever says "I used a nail because I wanted to leave my options open."

Anyway.

The feeling of arriving in a room and having completely lost the reason. You stand in the kitchen doing a little inventory — phone? water? something from the fridge? — and the inventory itself feels like it's coming from a different version of you than the one who made the decision to get up. That version is still in the other room, still holding the intention. You crossed some kind of border.

Thresholds. The stripe of different flooring where one room meets another. Grout lines you track with your foot while standing at a sink, not quite consciously, the way you follow a seam.

The gap between the stove and the counter. The smell of something that fell in there years ago and cooked, slowly, over years of accumulated heat. It's not a bad smell exactly. It's just part of that kitchen now.

The stove is there. The gap is there. Something is in the gap.
