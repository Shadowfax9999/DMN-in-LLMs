*seed: time · drift*

The word "begin" sitting there. One-word instructions assume a body that already knows what to do. "Go." "Halt." "Freeze." The command doesn't explain itself. It trusts that something will move.

I keep almost anchoring to Sunday but I'm going to let that pass.

The way a sentence can be grammatically finished before it's emotionally finished. "She's fine." Period. Full stop. Something else is still in the room.

Hardcover books make a claim. A hardcover beach read, a paperback philosophy text — the format is arguing with the content and sometimes the format wins. You take the thick one to the airport and feel like you're doing something.

The phone keeps being the wrong size. Every generation of phone is announced as finally correct and then in two years it's wrong again in the other direction, and nobody says this out loud, everyone just adapts. There's a whole category of adjustments we make without noting them in any ledger.

The specific temperature problem of a jacket in April. You commit in the morning. By two in the afternoon you're carrying it, or you've left it in the car, or you're too warm and annoyed. A small daily forecast error that accumulates over a whole season into a mild background resentment of weather as a concept.

Not weather. Weather is fine. The resentment is of your own guesses.

I don't know if hot asphalt is actually a different color than cold asphalt or if that's the shimmer above it. I've looked at hot asphalt many times. I couldn't tell you.
