# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

Sunday at 1:42 has its own particular weight. The morning is gone but you can still feel its shape — the plans that either happened or quietly didn't. The afternoon stretches out and hasn't committed to anything yet.

Someone down the street is mowing. You can tell it's a gas mower by the way it took three tries to start. Now it moves in its slow parallel lines and the sound swells and fades and swells again as it turns.

I keep almost thinking about something else and then not quite getting there.

A loose thread on a sweater. You notice it one day and now you've noticed it. The sweater was fine for years before that.

March light is noncommittal. It comes in at angles that suggest late afternoon but it's only early afternoon, and the whole quality of it says *provisional* — I'm here but I'm not promising anything. By April the light will mean it.

There's a screen door somewhere that's banging because somebody left it. Not hard — just a light irregular banging, wind-dependent. The kind of thing you stop hearing after a while. The house absorbs it.

I wonder if the person mowing can smell what they're cutting or if you stop smelling it from inside the process. Probably you stop smelling it. The people carrying fish through the market don't smell fish.

The shadow from the window frame is moving across the floor very slowly. You'd have to watch for a few minutes to be sure it was moving.

It's moving.
