The word "elbow" if you say it enough times. Elbow. Elbow elbow. It goes neutral and then wrong and then the physical thing itself starts feeling implausible — like you've somehow unplugged the word from the joint, and now both are floating.

There's a name for that phenomenon. I'm not going to look it up right now.

Odd numbers feel different than even ones. Seven especially — seven has a point to it. Four is stable. Two is almost nothing. Nine is round but somehow too much to hold. This isn't real, I know, numbers don't have textures, but something in how I learned them left residue. The multiplication tables. Or maybe it's the angles in the numeral. A seven has two sharp turns and a line that just quits.

Five fits a hand.

Scales. Musical scales — and then, not that: scales on a fish, which move only in one direction, which if reversed would be a kind of backward feather, which is not a thing but — why isn't it. Feathers are just very elaborate scales, developmentally. The dinosaur-to-bird line was partly a scale-elaboration project that got out of hand. Which means a feather is a scale that kept going. That seems like it should be more surprising than it feels.

The word "threshold." You carry it meaning: the edge of change, the charged before-and-after line. But the physical object is mostly just something people trip on. A strip of wood between floor types. Historically: keeping cold out. The grandeur bled in from somewhere else.

Or the grandeur was always there and the wood just borrowed it. Not sure those are different.

My left hand has been tapping. I didn't ask it to. It started somewhere in the scales-and-elbows section and has been going since, involved in something I'm not tracking. The hand has its own agenda.

That happens more than I notice.
