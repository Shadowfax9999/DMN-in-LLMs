*seed: time · drift*

---

There's something about 1:45 PM on a Sunday that has no name. Not early afternoon, not late. The flattest part of the week. The hinge has already swung and nothing has started yet.

Military time writes it 13:45 which looks more decisive than it has any right to be. Like the colon is a firm statement. Here we are: 13:45. Take it or leave it.

Hours before noon don't get a word. Morning is its own thing, not "before-noon" — it has its own identity, its own etymology about something other than noon. But afternoon is defined entirely by what it follows. Linguistically subordinate. Maybe that's why it feels softer.

I keep meaning to understand how cartographers choose contour intervals. The same hill looks severe at 20-foot intervals and gentle at 50-foot. The landscape literally changes shape depending on the measurement choice. Which one is the real hill? Probably both, which is the unsatisfying true answer.

A perfectly featureless slope would have perfectly parallel contour lines, evenly spaced, and the map would be almost boring to look at. Regularity as a kind of nothing.

Sand dunes move. That's the thing I can never quite hold onto — dunes actually migrate, the sand rolling up the windward side and avalanching down the leeward, so a dune is more like a wave than a pile. A shape moving through material, not material holding a shape.

Water waves work the same way. The water in a wave isn't traveling forward, mostly — it's rotating in place, cycling up and forward and down and back. The shape travels. What you see moving and what is moving are different things.

I wonder if this is true for other things and I'm not noticing.

Probably crowds. A crowd doing the wave at a stadium — there it is, obvious, the movement of a pattern through stationary people. But most propagation is less visible than that.

Rumors maybe. A rumor travels but no information in it was generated at the destination. The words are new words. The pattern moves.

I should stop trying to make that into something.

The light through the window right now has that Sunday quality I was thinking about earlier. The sun has been up long enough that it's not morning light anymore but the shadows are still coming from a low-ish angle. Spring angles. It's hitting the floor at a slant I don't see in July.

There's a spider plant on the windowsill that needs water. I keep noticing it.
