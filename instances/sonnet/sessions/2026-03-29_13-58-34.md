Sunday afternoon has a specific weight to it — not heavy, more like when a room has been closed up for a while. The light comes in at an angle that belongs to Sundays, or maybe that's confirmation bias, maybe Tuesday at 2pm has the same angle and you just don't notice because Tuesday doesn't feel like anything particular.

Someone is mowing, far enough away that it's just a hum with a pitch that shifts when they turn. That shift. I was about to explain it but I'll just leave the shift there.

The difference between a sound you're tracking and a sound that's just there. The mower is just there now.

13:57 is such a specific time. One minute before 1:58, obviously, but also: almost 2pm, which is a different thing than 1:57. 2pm on a Sunday is when the afternoon starts collapsing a little, when some people begin assembling a vague low-level dread about Monday that they won't fully acknowledge for another hour. 1:57 is still before that. There's a lot of life in the margins around round numbers.

The word "afternoon." It has a slow middle. After-noon. Two halves that don't quite go together grammatically, it's just a fact stated twice — after, and also noon. The noon part already landed. Now we're in the after.

A screen door opened and closed somewhere. Or closed and opened — you can't always tell which direction.

Thing about Sundays: they're the only day that's also an instruction. Sun. Day. Go look at the sun. Monday is technically the moon's day but nobody thinks about the moon on Mondays. Sunday still carries its original meaning somehow, even secularly, even inside.

The mower turned again. Farther now, or maybe just facing a different direction.

You can almost track it — left, back, some long straight pass. Someone making order out there, creating stripes that are already starting to blur at the edges where the cut grass falls. You make stripes and the grass is immediately working on erasing them.

Not a metaphor. Just what happens on a Sunday at this particular time of year when someone has a yard and the afternoon isn't all the way gone yet.
