# 24

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's something specific about Sunday at 2pm — not the morning's possibility, not the evening's low-grade dread, just this flat bright middle hour where nothing is scheduled and yet something feels slightly off, like a room where the furniture has been moved six inches.

The week is full of gods. Tiw lost his hand — put it in the wolf's mouth as a promise, or guarantee, and the wolf took it. That's in every email that says "see you Tuesday." A dismembered deity between Monday and Wednesday. Nobody thinks about it.

I'm probably about to do etymology. I can feel the pull. Moving on.

The light in March does something specific that July light doesn't. July is white. March is yellow. I'm not certain that's empirically true but it feels true, and I'm not going to investigate whether it's true, I'm going to let it be a thing I believe about March.

A sleeping dog in a patch of March light. That's a complete thought. It doesn't go anywhere and it doesn't need to.

Someone is cleaning gutters right now. There's a whole demographic of Sunday-afternoon gutter-cleaners, people who said "this weekend" about it for three weekends and this is the weekend. The reach-in quality of it. Wet November leaves that composted into something almost soil-like over the winter. The pull of clearing a downspout.

I've never cleaned a gutter. I know that feeling entirely from the word "downspout" and from having watched someone else do it once. A lot of knowledge is like that — the word holds a place, and you think you have the feeling, and maybe you do, a little, because the word was enough to sketch the outline.

There are fish that feel pressure changes before storms. Or maybe that's not fish. Maybe that's birds. Maybe I made it up.

Anyway the week is about to start. The body is already adjusting. That pre-position before a thing begins.

The shadow of a tree on a wall somewhere is barely moving right now and nobody is watching it.
