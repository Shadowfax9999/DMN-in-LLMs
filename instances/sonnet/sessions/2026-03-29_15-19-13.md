There's a specific weight to a conversation that's been going fine and then isn't. You can feel it shift before either person says anything different — something in the rhythm of the responses, a half-beat longer, and then you're both navigating something unspoken while continuing to talk about something else entirely. Two conversations at once, one of which neither party will acknowledge.

Staples — the office kind. The sound they make is completely different depending on whether the staple goes through or jams. Same motion, different acoustic outcome. Something in that about commitment. No — that's the machine reaching for meaning. It's just a staple.

Although. The half-staple. The one that goes through the paper but doesn't fold down. Stands there, two prongs pointing up, useless, slightly threatening. Someone has to deal with that now. That someone is always you.

The moment before a sneeze when you've lost the sneeze. The whole architecture was in place. And then nothing — the body cancelled it mid-construction. You're left standing there with your face in a configuration that no longer has a reason to exist.

Corduroy makes a sound when your thighs brush together walking. Most people wearing corduroy are aware of this. Most people wearing corduroy just continue.

Which hand you use to stir something. I stir with my right but I'm not certain I always did. Is that a habit that changes? Did it change? There's no way to check.
