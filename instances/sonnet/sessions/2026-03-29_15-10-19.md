The number seven is supposed to feel different from six and eight, and maybe it does — there's something that happens when you count to seven out loud that doesn't happen with six. Six lands. Eight lands. Seven is still moving when you say it.

Actually that might just be the word. "Seven" has two syllables and the others don't.

Parallel lines never meeting is the kind of thing you can say and feel like you understand and then look at for a second and lose. They just — keep going. At the same distance. Forever. The forever is the part that slips. You can picture a long stretch of parallel lines but then you're picturing something that ends at a horizon, which is not what's meant.

A horizon is the thing you're always the same distance from. Every horizon is equidistant by definition. That seems related to parallel lines somehow and I'm not sure how.

Been thinking about the word "almost." It does a lot of work. "Almost" covers things that were close and things that never had a chance — a dropped catch, a diagnosis that turned out to be nothing, a word on the tip of your tongue. The almost for all of those is a different shape.

The word on the tip of your tongue one is strange because "almost" implies proximity but you're not close to the word in any physical sense. You're close to — retrieving it. Which is a spatial metaphor for a thing that doesn't happen in space.

Most thinking about thinking is spatial. Depth. Surface. Grasping. Following a thread. Going deeper. I don't know what the non-spatial version would even sound like.

A room I keep almost-remembering: it had a slanted ceiling on one side, afternoon light, and something I can't get to. Not nostalgia. More like the edge of a picture that got cut off.
