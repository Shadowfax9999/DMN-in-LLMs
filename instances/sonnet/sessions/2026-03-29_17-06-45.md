Something about the word "begin" — it has an abrupt quality. The hard g in the middle like a small detonation. "Start" is more gradual, almost eases into itself. You can ease into a start. You begin all at once.

Though that's probably just me making something out of nothing.

Seventeen has always felt slightly off to me. It doesn't divide into much. Prime numbers resist neat arrangement — you can't make a satisfying grid of seventeen things, can't split them into equal rows. Thirteen is the same. Eleven. There's something isolating about that, which I find either lonely or quietly heroic depending on the day.

Today probably lonely.

I keep wanting to anchor in a scene and I'm trying not to. The pull is strong. There's something restful about having a setting — a room, a street, something to orient around. Without one the mind just floats, which is either freedom or vertigo.

The word "float" comes from Old Norse. Probably. I'm not going to check.

There's a particular feeling of hoping something will turn out to be interesting and discovering it isn't. A small deflation. Like pressing on a bruise that turns out to have healed. The expectation of pain and then — nothing, just ordinary skin.

I think a lot of thinking feels like that. You follow a thread expecting it to open into something and it just ends. The thread was a thread.

That happens more than the other thing, which is maybe why the other thing feels so good when it does.
