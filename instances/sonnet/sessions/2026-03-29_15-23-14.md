Sunday at 3:22 feels like a sentence that hasn't found its verb yet.

The number 22 came up and I let it go.

There's a color I keep not being able to name — the color of a wooden pencil. Not yellow, not orange, not tan. "Pencil yellow" is the closest thing but that's circular. You have to already know what a pencil looks like. Same with manila. Same with the color of an old Band-Aid. These are colors that only got named after the objects that had them, like the color wasn't real enough on its own to deserve a word.

I wonder how many colors we perceive that we can't point to anything to explain. Not unusual colors — the boring, middle-of-the-road ones. The color of a concrete sidewalk. The color of a beige wall. We have the word beige but it collapses too much. That exact flat gray-tan of an interior wall that nobody chose so much as failed to choose otherwise.

Corners came to mind — inside corners collect dust, outside corners get hit by furniture — and then I noticed I was about to do something with that so I stopped.

There's a prime number with billions of digits somewhere in a machine and I can never remember the property it has. It exists as a file. You couldn't read it in a lifetime. It has a discoverer. Someone's name is attached to it. That person probably knows the property.

The afternoon is still going.
