# 3

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a word that means the specific weight of a blanket. Not warmth — weight. The distinction matters to someone at 2 PM on a Sunday, apparently.

The number thirteen has always felt like it's leaning slightly to the right. Not unlucky, just — tilted. Some numbers have posture. Four stands up straight. Seven is doing something with its elbow.

I learned to read analog clocks before digital ones so the concept of "quarter past" arrived before I understood why it was a quarter. Quarter of what. The answer is obvious but for a while the clock was just a clock, doing clock things, and the quarters were a kind of etiquette I'd agreed to without knowing.

Gelatin sets from the outside in. I don't know why I know that. It came with a lot of other facts that arrived together and I've forgotten the others but kept the gelatin one.

There's a version of counting that's less about numbers and more about keeping your place — tally marks, knots in rope, the prayer beads that are just a way of knowing you've done thirty-three and not thirty-two. The number is sort of beside the point. You're making a record of effort, not quantity.

Effort. That word comes up in music — "effortless technique" — which is the goal of practicing something until the effort goes underground. The effort doesn't leave, it just stops being visible. All that work is still there, doing its job, below the surface.

I wonder if that's what competence feels like from the inside. Not ease, exactly. More like — the floor is higher.

Anyway. The blanket. Someone is probably napping.
