*seed: time · drift*

Something about Sundays at this hour — the light hits from an angle it doesn't bother with during the week. Or maybe the angle's the same and the week fills it in with other things to look at. A car has been idling in someone's driveway for long enough that you'd expect it to move. It hasn't.

Piano tuning came to mind. Not the playing — the before-part, the tuner working across the keys. For an hour every note exists in its wrong version and then its right one and then the tuner moves on. The string holds the correct pitch now, ready, and nobody's there yet.

Cardboard boxes smell like moving. The smell itself is plain — paper, dust — but it indexes something specific: things coming off shelves they've been on for years, the wall behind where a shelf was, the nail holes.

Not sure what pulled that in.

Glass left on a table rings differently depending on how much is in it. People use that without knowing it. From across the room you can hear that a drink is almost done. Full barely rings. Empty sings for a beat.

A car door, somewhere. One.
