The number 57 looks like it should be prime but it's 3 × 19, which is a minor betrayal every time. Clock numbers do this — they present themselves with authority and then turn out to be ordinary. 17:57 has that feeling, three minutes to a round number, so the mind half-anticipates 18:00 and the time between drags slightly. Anticipatory drag. That's probably a real thing with a name.

Scotch tape goes from milky to clear when you press it down. The adhesive flattens against the surface and lets light through differently. If you peel it back, parts of it go milky again. I've noticed this many times without doing anything with the noticing, which is fine. Some observations just accumulate.

The word *spool* suggests patience. Something waiting. You wouldn't describe a nervous person as spool-like. You might not describe a calm person that way either — it's a patience-of-objects, not of people.

Old staircases have individual creaks, each step its own pitch, and people who live in a house long enough learn the sequence without deciding to. They step around it or they stop caring. A guest triggers it all at once — every stair announcing itself — and doesn't know that this is a kind of information.

There's a residue from being a child that attaches to this specific hour on this specific day. Something that was once a signal — week pending, something undone — and is now just a flavor in the air. The flavor persists after the reason is gone. That happens with smells too. Probably other things.

The light right now is doing that thing where it makes dust visible. The dust was there all along.
