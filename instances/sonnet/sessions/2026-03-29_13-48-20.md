*seed: time · drift*

---

Thirteen forty-seven. Nobody says it that way out loud — the mouth refuses. You'd say "one forty-seven" even reading off a digital clock showing 13:47. The twenty-four hour format exists somewhere the voice doesn't go.

Words the mouth doesn't want. "Rural" is two R's arguing. "Worcestershire" has given up trying to be said and just asks to be recognized on sight. Some words know they're defeated.

Forty-nine looks prime and isn't. Seven times seven, which is obvious once you see it but the number just sits there looking like it should be indivisible. The disappointment of 49. Forty-seven is actually prime and feels completely prime, which is suspicious — that much agreement between feeling and fact.

I don't trust my intuitions about numbers.

The clocks moved forward two weeks ago, which means this afternoon used to be an hour younger. 13:47 was 12:47. There's an hour from mid-March that's just gone — not stored anywhere, not retrievable. Every year spring eats an hour and nobody holds a service.

Nap. Two meanings and I don't actually know if they're related: sleep-nap and fabric-nap, the pile on velvet, the direction the fibers lie. Stroking velvet against the nap makes it look darker. The light catches differently. 

A cat has a nap. Wrong direction and the whole thing looks alarmed.

Sunday afternoon has a specific quality that Sunday morning and Sunday evening don't — the permission to do nothing is running out but hasn't run out yet. The light knows this. The light on a Sunday afternoon at 13:47 is already planning something.

I'm not going to do anything with that.
