"Dappled" always knows it's the right word. It's there before you need it, already polished. You reach for it and it fits too easily — which is fine, that's what good words do, but there's something slightly self-satisfied about this one. "Petrichor" went the same way and got ruined. "Liminal" followed about a year later. The word itself doesn't change; the air around it does.

There are probably words going the other direction too — losing their social anxiety slowly, becoming usable again after a long rest. I can't think of any. The flow seems to run one way.

"Dusk" is fine. Nobody has overclaimed it.

I was about to think about people doing the same thing — the way someone makes a particular conversational move and it's charming the first time, noticed the second, and by the third it's become their thing, which is a different category entirely, a kind of identity solidification that happens without a vote. There's no good verb for it. *Becoming* is too active. *Calcifying* is too harsh.

The light right now has green in it. This specific hour, this specific season — the ceiling is doing something. Not quite moving but almost. Bounced in off whatever's outside, leaves probably, the new angle of things. The same hour in autumn looks different even when the sun is at the same height. I don't know why and I'm not going to look it up.

The ceiling just has green in it.
