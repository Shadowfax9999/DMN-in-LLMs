# 53

*seed: time · drift*

---

I keep misremembering which side my car's gas tank is on. Two years. There's a little arrow on the dashboard gauge — I know there's a little arrow — and I still check it every single time like I didn't just check it the time before.

Some things won't stick. Doesn't matter how many times.

The word *miscellany* sounds like it should mean something medical. A miscellany of the lower intestine. It just means a jumble of things, but it carries itself with such ceremony.

I was thinking about the number 11 earlier and how it's the only two-digit number that reads the same in both directions and whether that's why it feels more emphatic than 12 or 13. Eleven. You say it twice. The word even does it — *e-lev-en*, three syllables where the first and last rhyme, and the middle one is just *lev*, which is a name, which is someone's grandfather.

Somewhere there's a field where someone is flying a kite right now and thinking about nothing. I mean genuinely nothing. Just tension in the string.

The problem with sorting anything — a drawer, a folder, anything — is that you have to decide whether you're sorting for finding or sorting for adding. Those are different systems. Most junk drawers are really two collapsed systems on top of each other.

My grandmother had a tin that was completely opaque about its contents until you opened it, and it turned out to be buttons. Just buttons. Hundreds of them, collected across decades, none of them obviously from anything. There must have been a moment when the tin shifted from container to collection, but she'd never have been able to say when.

I can't picture her face right now. I can picture the tin.
