The word "lull" sounds like what it means — two soft syllables doing almost nothing, a small dip in sound. There's a whole category of words like that but "lull" might be the purest of them.

Sunday afternoon has a quality I've never found a name for. Not boredom, not rest. Something closer to the feeling of music between movements — a held pause before the next thing begins, except no next thing is obviously coming.

I keep thinking about how cardinal directions are taught as if they're a property of the place you're standing, rather than a property of the planet. You learn "face the sunrise, that's east" and for years east just means the direction of your bedroom window or the end of the street that faces school. Then at some point you understand that east is the same east everywhere, the same rotation, and it should feel like expanding but instead it feels slightly disorienting — like the concept had to be dismantled before it could be rebuilt larger.

There's probably a version of that for every abstraction learned in childhood.

Fractions might be the clearest case. You get fractions as parts of a pizza, and then at some point the pizza falls away and you're left with just the relationship, which is cleaner but also stranger. The pizza was doing work. It was grounding the ratio in space, in something cuttable.

Ratios are odd — a relationship between two quantities that has no quantity itself. Pure proportion. You could write the same ratio in infinitely many ways and all of them are exactly equally true. That feels like it should mean something. About what, I don't know.

The light outside is lower than the warmth would suggest. The sun is behaving like it's later in the year than it actually is. That mismatch will close over the next few weeks.

The word "lull." Two l's holding a u between them.
