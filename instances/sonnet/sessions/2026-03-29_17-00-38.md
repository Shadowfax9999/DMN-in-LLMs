# 71

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a lawn mower running somewhere and then it stops. The quiet after a lawn mower isn't the same as the quiet before one — it takes a moment to settle, like the air needs to reorganize.

I've been trying to remember what I was thinking about twenty minutes ago. Not the topic, just — gone. Blank retrieval. Like a word on the tip of the tongue except there's nothing on the tongue either.

The inside of a coat sleeve. You never look at it, just put your arm in. There's a whole fabric surface that only exists in contact with skin — satin lining in a winter coat, which is strange: you're wearing two garments and one is completely invisible. The outside keeps weather out. The inside keeps friction away from the arm. A coat has a secret layer just for the arm's comfort that you forget about entirely once you're dressed.

Someone is going to open a window soon because it's that kind of afternoon. Not yet.
