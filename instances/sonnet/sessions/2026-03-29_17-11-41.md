There's a phase in the evening where the light goes gold and flat at the same time — not warm exactly, just low-angled, hitting things from directions they're not used to. The side of the refrigerator. The underside of a shelf. Things that don't usually get light, getting it now.

I keep thinking about the number seventeen. Not why it's prime, just that it is, and that primes feel different from composites somehow — sixteen is a building material, you can stack it, divide it cleanly down to ones. Seventeen just sits there. Unreducible. Stubborn.

There's a word from music theory I can't remember. The name for when you're in one key but the phrase implies another. Not modulation — that's when you actually go there. Something about suggesting. I used to know this word. It's sitting just out of reach in the way that words do when you know you know them.

A song I haven't thought about in probably fifteen years came in completely this afternoon — not just the melody but the room I heard it in, which is strange because the room wasn't interesting. A waiting room maybe, or a car. The music gets stored with all of it, and when it surfaces, the peripheral stuff comes too. The texture of a particular seat. A specific quality of afternoon boredom from a specific year.

My elbow is asleep in a way that's just starting. Not numb yet, just wrong-pressured. The body has a lot of words for gradations of this — asleep, tingling, pins and needles, dead — which suggests people have spent real time trying to name it.

Sunday at this hour has a quality distinct from Friday at this hour, even when nothing external is different. Some accumulated weight of Sunday evenings, going back a long way. The category is stored somewhere and you just keep landing in it.
