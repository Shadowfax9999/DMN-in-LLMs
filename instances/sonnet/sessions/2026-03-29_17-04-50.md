The number 37 feels large. It shouldn't — it's clearly smaller than 50, obviously smaller than 100 — but when someone says *37* it lands with a kind of heft that 34 doesn't have. 36 is neat. 38 is even. 37 just sits there, taking up more space than it mathematically deserves. There's a thing I half-remember about 37 being the number people pick when asked to pick a random number between 1 and 100. I believe this completely and I also might have invented it. I have a lot of those — facts I'm not sure I received or manufactured. I'm fairly certain a standard piano has 88 keys. Fairly.

The word *almost* does something strange in a sentence. "She almost fell" — the fall didn't happen but it's right there in the word, the tipping, the approach. Almost is— actually that goes somewhere I've been before. Fine.

Bristles on a brush spring back. There's something about compressed-then-released that keeps showing up in unrelated things, and I was about to make it a metaphor for something human, which is the reflex. Just: bristles. The ones slightly too stiff don't distribute evenly. The ones too soft collapse entirely. There's probably a correct stiffness for each task and most people just use whatever's in the cup.

You could probably learn to recognize someone by their typing rhythm. Not the words — the actual percussion, the hesitation before a word they're unsure of, the faster pace on their own name. Someone listening through a thin wall night after night might develop this without trying. Though what they'd do with it I'm not sure.

Every lease I've ever signed has a paragraph I didn't parse properly. I read them and I nod, and somewhere in each one I've agreed to something I don't know about. It's probably nothing. It's also possibly something. There's no way to find out without reading it again and I'm not going to read it again.

Sundays at 5 have a specific quality. I'm not going to explain it. They just do.
