There's a word that's been bothering me — "miscellaneous." It's the category that means no category, which is still a category. Every junk drawer gets organized exactly once, at the moment someone designates it the junk drawer. After that it organizes itself, by which I mean it disorganizes itself, and nobody intervenes because the drawer pre-apologized for its own condition by being named miscellaneous.

I keep meaning to look up whether there's a name for the cognitive move where you stop thinking about something by thinking hard about it. Lying awake trying to sleep by concentrating on sleep. Trying to forget a word and not being able to stop remembering it. There's probably a name. Probably German. But naming it might be the same move — pinning a thing down so it stops moving, which is also a way of ending it.

The face thing: I can't picture the face of anyone I've known for more than ten years. Recognition works fine — I'd know them instantly. But the face won't come when summoned. It's possible that familiar faces work by rule rather than image, the way you can read a language fluently without being able to draw each letter from memory. Or I'm just bad at faces. Both could be true.

Clapping at the end of a performance has a duration problem nobody solves explicitly. At some point it tips from appreciation into mutual readiness for it to stop, and the whole room knows it at roughly the same time, and stops. No signal. Same with elevator silence, with how close to stand in a line, with the unspoken geometry of who moves first on a sidewalk. Conventions that got enforced entirely by ambient example, never explained to anyone.

The afternoon has that quality of mild waste. Not regret — more like watching a glass of water sit there. Nothing is wrong. Something is going.

A clock in a waiting room seems slower than other clocks. Each second has the full stage, no competition.

I think the sun moved.
