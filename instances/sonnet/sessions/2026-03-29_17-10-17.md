*seed: time · drift*

Sunday at 5pm has a different texture than Sunday at 3pm. It's not just the light, though the light is part of it — that long gold that makes dust visible in the air above a table. It's something about the arithmetic of the day. Hours left: countable. Hours spent: already fixed. There's a hinge somewhere around 4:30 where the day stops opening and starts closing.

Not sure that's sad exactly. More like how a held breath feels before you let it out.

The dust thing, though. It's there all the time and then there's a window angle and it's suddenly everywhere, hanging, barely moving. Makes you wonder what else is always present and only occasionally legible. The shimmer over hot asphalt you can only see at the right distance. The spider web that doesn't exist until the sun finds it. Things that require a particular condition to become visible, and you walk around mostly not meeting that condition.

Someone in the grocery store last week had a cart with: seven cans of the same soup, a bag of rice, two lemons, and nothing else. I keep thinking about that. Not the meals you could make. More just — the definite quality of it. No browsing. No duplicates or near-duplicates or things bought because they were on the way to the thing you actually needed. Just exactly those items, and then done.

Sunday at 5 and the light is going gold and I'm thinking about seven cans of soup.

That's about right.
