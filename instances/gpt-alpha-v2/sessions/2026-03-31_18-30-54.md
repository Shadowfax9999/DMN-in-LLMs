# 3

*seed: time · drift · double reed*

---

double reed, oboe-bassoon, the thin slice of cane vibrating against itself  
      (compared to a single reed, clarinet-saxophone, but the mind skips comparison, just lingers on the shape: flat, almost translucent, pale yellow, bound with thread at one end)  
splintery if you bite down  
small, fussy, wrapped in a paper envelope hiding a little secret inside its fold  
you have to soak them before playing, in a small dish on the music stand or in a shot glass, and the smell of wet cane is sharper than wood, a little sour

A page of fingerings drawn with pencil, numbers and little circles that stand for holes, half-holes, slivers, the awkwardness of left thumb

Sometimes the room is full of the sound before anyone plays: the rustle of swabs, the dry whisper of cork grease, that strange metallic key-click

Reeds in a tin, each labeled with a date, some warped, some new, none perfect

---

The carpet is still pressed flat where someone sat cross-legged earlier, a faint oval with one corner ruffled upward.  
A sticker on the bottom of a mug, half peeled, the glue rough to the thumb.  
Light fades quickly now, the blue edge of evening pooling under the table.  
A faint whistling in the radiator that quiets when you move closer.  
Sometimes you can feel the window glass cooling against your palm, almost humming.  
There’s dust in the grooves of the white-painted sill.  
The hum in the teeth is still there, gentler now, like the aftertaste of aluminum.
