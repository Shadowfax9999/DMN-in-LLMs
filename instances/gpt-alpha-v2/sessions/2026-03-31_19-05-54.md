# 18

*seed: time · drift · bridge (instrument)*

---

Bridge, instrument. The arch of wood pressed against tension, string crossing over. It lifts the note above the body, holds things apart, connects nothing to nothing, but something hums through it anyway. Some bridges are flat, some are curved. The violin bridge stands on its toes, not glued, just pressed and held by force and balance. The guitar bridge lies flush, anchored, but the strings still rise above, only touching at the points that matter.

Bridge also in the mouth: the dental kind, filling the gap where something was meant to be. Sometimes porcelain, sometimes something that shines when you open wide. Bridge in music, the section that’s neither verse nor chorus, a detour, a crossing, sometimes so brief you barely notice you’ve passed over.

Bridge, as thickness in the nose, glasses perched on it, leaving faint marks that fade after minutes, sometimes after hours. The bridge of a song, the interruption. The bridge between one day and the next, if you’re awake for the crossing.

---

There’s a smell in the hallway, not good or bad, just unfamiliar detergent. A scuff mark on the wall at knee height, maybe from a shoe or a box being carried sideways. Something small and blue wedged under the radiator, not quite in sight. Quiet except for the hum that comes and goes, hard to tell if it’s the fridge or something outside carried in through the walls.

The word “scuff.” How it sounds soft, like a shoe against tile, the abruptness softened at the end. Sometimes you don’t notice the scuff until sunlight makes it visible, gray against white paint.

A click in the throat, so quiet it might only be felt, not heard.

Fingernails still have the faintest line of yellow at their ends, even scrubbed. Not dirt, maybe from something touched hours ago.
