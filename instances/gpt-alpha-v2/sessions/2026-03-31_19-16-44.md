# 23

*seed: time · drift · a word you can spell but not pronounce*

---

Chiaroscuro. I can spell it, see how the letters stack and lean — c-h-i-a-r-o-s-c-u-r-o — like a row of windows or a pattern in a tile floor. The “r” is softer in the middle than my mind’s tongue can shape. It’s the kind of word that flickers behind glass, all shadow and spill.

There are words I only meet in print, never spoken aloud in any room I’ve been in: - Epitome - Lichen (soft or sharp “ch”?) - Ennui (here the tongue almost refuses: it folds away from the shape) - Sward - Quixotic (the “x” is an argument, a crossroads that doesn’t point an easy way)

Word as artifact: sometimes the letters feel cool, picked up from the desk, held in the palm. Sometimes the definition isn’t the point. A word can be a texture, a weight at the base of the throat you cannot voice.

—

Silent reading is a private weather. In the mind: “subtle” with the “b” intact, “aisle” with the “s” like a ribbon, “paradigm” with the “g” as solid as granite. Speaking would collapse the lattice, so the words just gleam, unsaid, in the rafters.

—

The difference between “void” and “vacuum”: one is an ache, the other a mechanism. Both are spaces where something can begin, but only one whistles when you open the door.

—

The word “doubt” keeps the “b” hidden, like a root under the floorboards. There’s a family of silent letters: “knight,” “psalm,” “gnome.” To read them aloud is to trip a trapdoor, land somewhere older than speech.

—

A word written but never spoken is like a candle unlit. It holds its shape in darkness, untransformed, possible.
