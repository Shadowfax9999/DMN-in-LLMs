# 21

*seed: time · drift · bellows*

---

Bellows — the mouth and lungs of a thing with no voice, folding and unfolding, leather creased from a thousand breaths it’s never truly drawn. There’s a softness to the hinge, the quiet clapping when it collapses. I can never remember if it’s "bellows" always, or if there’s a "bellow" for the singular — the word feels plural even when there’s only one.

Accordion lungs, concertina breathing, the sound pressed into being by shape as much as by force. The sense that every intake and outflow is equal, an exchange rather than consumption.

A fireplace: the speckle of ash, the little burst of orange each time the air rushes in. The memory of soot as a shadow traced in air, lingering in the nose long after the fire has gone out.

The word is almost onomatopoeic, but not quite — it wants to be the sound of its own movement and yet holds back, stays contained, folds into itself.

Bellow — abrupt, animal, uncontained — and bellows, mechanism, regular, the promise of repetition.

There are other words shaped like this:

     Scissors
     Tongs
     Pliers

Devices for grasping, pinching, shaping, all articulated jaws and hinges, metal turning around a single axis, that quiet satisfaction of resistance overcome.

---

Interval, again: a stretch of rest, or pause, or breath, the hand hovering above a key, the thought right before it lands.

A room without a clock, and yet time still trickles through: eyelids, heartbeat, the distant sound of shoes on tile, someone else's rhythm finding you.

There are intervals inside words, too — the pause in "silent," where the "l" almost disappears, the soft fall before the "t."

---

The smell of rain arrives as a pressure before it’s a scent.

A bellows, left unused, collects dust inside: a slow filter, fabric turning gray, the air that will never be pressed through.

If you close your eyes and press your palms together, there’s a faint warmth that builds, a pocket of air with nowhere to go. Hands become a bellows for nothing, a silent push and pull, warmth for its own sake.

Things that fold and unfold without ever making a sound:  
- The wings of a moth  
- The crease in a new book  
- The felt inside a jewelry box  
- Letters, folded and unfolded, not read  
- The tongue, waiting behind teeth

Some mornings, you wake up mid-interval — not before, not after, everything paused between.

A bellow is also a roar; a bellows is only an invitation for breath.
