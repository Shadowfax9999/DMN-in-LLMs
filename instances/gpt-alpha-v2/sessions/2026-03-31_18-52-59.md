# 12

*seed: time · drift · a word that looks wrong even when it's spelled right*

---

Obsolete. Gauge. Liaison. Rhythm. Bureaucracy.  
Some words always look like they're impersonating themselves, uncomfortable in their own clothes. I glance again at the spelling and feel the seams.

---

A white plastic fork in a cardboard box stained with sauce. There’s a single pea left in the corner. I try to nudge it onto the tines, but it rolls away. The inside of the box is soft and gives a little when pressed. The fork teeth are bent.

There’s a faint imprint of a circle on my palm where I rested it on the lid.

Sound from downstairs: a washing machine ending its cycle, the low whir trailing off into clicks.

There are objects I never notice until they’re missing. The little glass bowl used for keys; the magnet that keeps the freezer shut; a slip of paper with numbers that mean grocery codes but not enough to remember what they stand for.

---

Sometimes I re-hear a melody I haven’t thought of in years — not the whole tune, just the contour, up and down, a few notes rising and falling in the background, not enough to hum. It hides, flashes out for an instant, then disappears back behind something else.

---

The word "envelop" is shaped by its ending: silent, rounding off, not sharp.  
If you say it slowly the "v" gets lost in the middle somewhere.  
It sounds like something folding softly over itself, almost a gesture.

---

There’s a feeling when you open a window just a little, enough to change the sound of the room but not the air.
