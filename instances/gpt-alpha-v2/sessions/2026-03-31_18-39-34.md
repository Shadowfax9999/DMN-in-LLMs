# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a flash of something lime-green in the corner—a shopping bag, rope handles looped over a shoe. The announcements blend with the rhythm of the wheels, each syllable falling between the rails. When the train dips into shadow, screens in the carriage flicker blue, then white again as the tunnel ends. Someone clicks a retractable pen once, pauses, clicks it again. The floor feels sticky under one shoe, a patch of spilled soda maybe, the scent sharp and sweet mixed with the other smells.

Outside, glass reflects sky, then blurs as the train picks up speed. There is a faint echo of music from earbuds, leaking through the gap between a hood and a collar, just the highest notes. Hands clutch the overhead bar, one with chipped blue nail polish and a band-aid across the knuckle. A child points at something outside, silent, finger tracing a moving shape. The next station sign glows orange, blinking twice before holding steady.

---

A manhole cover sits in the pavement, pattern worn smooth at the center. Someone steps on it without slowing down. The sidewalk squares don’t line up perfectly, seams running off at odd angles, weeds pushing in at the corners. Chewing gum pressed flat, blackened, stretched thin. The city’s sounds layer over each other—steps, voices, the distant squeal of a bicycle brake. A pigeon hops twice, then launches itself with a sudden beat of wings, scattering a few crumbs. The traffic light cycles through faster than it seems it should. On the other side, a bus pulls away, letting off a small cloud of exhaust.

There's a mailbox with a dent in the side, paint flaking at the edges of the slot. Someone walks by holding three baguettes in a paper sleeve, tips browned, ends peeking out at different angles. 

---

The word "plaza" is shaped round in the mouth, softer than "square," quieter than "courtyard." There are places called plazas that are really only slightly wider streets, two benches and a tree. The echo in an empty plaza at night, footsteps stretching longer than the walk itself. The way some streetlights flicker just before they settle. A public fountain, water pumped through faded pipes, graffiti behind the spray. Unread flyers tucked under stone planters, paper curling at the corners. 

The heat from the stone lingers after sunset.
