# 9

*seed: time · drift · eddy*

---

eddy—an interruption in flow, a sideways pause, current doubling back on itself, holding leaves or foam in a quiet spiral. Standing at the edge of a river, there’s always a patch where the water isn’t hurrying downstream. A place that keeps the small things for a while.

---

Pigeons picking at bread by the metro entrance, scattering as someone drags a rolling suitcase over uneven paving. The low groan of the suitcase wheels, sometimes skipping in the crack between stones.

---

Someone opening a metal thermos, steam puffing out, the smell of soup or tea rising briefly above the street smells. Coins drop into the plastic tray by a newsstand and roll a little before settling.

---

Orange plastic fencing around a hole in the sidewalk, the netting half-collapsed, making a small bright eddy in the flow of people.

---

Some tiles near the curb are always damp, thin moss beginning to spread, almost slippery if you aren’t careful.

---

A child running up the steps, one shoe untied, trailing the lace behind.

A patchwork of posters on a closed shop front, overlapping dates and band names, the corners softened by weeks of weather.

A dog, brown with a white stripe, nosing at a paper cup.

One bike parked by a lamppost, back tire flat.

The dented mailbox is on a different corner here.
