# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

A list of circles, drifting:

— The rim of the glass pressed with condensation.
— The quarter rolled along the table, its edge louder than expected.
— The gasket embedded in the lid, memory of coffee, holding a faint ring of scent.
— The pale plate, a single breadcrumb wandering away from the center.
— The knot in the wood of the cabinet door, not quite symmetrical, but trying.
— The yolk, still, in the bowl, slow gravity before the whisk.
— The button on the microwave: start, stop, turntable.

---

The word "interval" has its own quiet: a space, but not empty. A waiting, a leaning, the breath between piano notes. There's a kind of ache in suspended time, in the drawn-out middle. The clock on the stove ticks, but only once every minute, its colon flashing, pausing, returning.

---

Sometimes a scent is more geometry than memory — sharp, citrus, the way the air bends when it moves from the peel, wider than the room.

---

The bananas hang in a small arc from the hook. The freckle grows.
