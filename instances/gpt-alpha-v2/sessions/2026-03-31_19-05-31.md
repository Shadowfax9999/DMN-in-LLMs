# 16

*seed: time · drift*

---

A doorknob slick from all the turns. The handprint never precisely overlapped, the oil mapping out a slow, blurry constellation.

Someone’s phone vibrates on the table, a dragonfly skitter of sound, then silence swallowing it whole. The glass of the screen picks up the overhead grid, ghost lattice on black.

There’s a soft drag when shoelace tips skitter across tile — the plastic aglets clicking, tiny percussion no one acknowledges.

A blue pen bites the paper, the loop of a lowercase “e” thick where the nib paused too long. The paper itself is slightly torn at one edge, like a memory that frays under touch, but only if you pick at it.

---

The taste of metal from the first sip in a mug gone unused for months. Vague peppermint, ghosts of last year’s tea. 

Three fluorescent tubes, and one flickers. There’s a cadence to it: two steady, one stuttering, so the light in the corner comes and goes like breath.

List of things on the window ledge:

- Coin, brown, tarnish blooming at the edge  
- Cracked piece of tile, paint clinging to the rough side  
- Folded napkin, flattening out  
- Dead leaf, curled, skeletonized  
- Thumbtack, point up  
- Useless key, cut wrong, no lock remembers it

---

Someone moves their chair, and the rubber foot squeals. No one says anything.

The pencil lead snaps mid-sentence. A fragment, black and sharp, skips off into the carpet.

Heat radiates from somewhere not visible. You only feel it on the right side of your face.

The room seems to tilt, slightly, a sense the floor dips imperceptibly toward the closed door.

Out in the corridor, the elevator is arriving. The bell is soft and indifferent.
