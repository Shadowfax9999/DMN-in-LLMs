# 19

*seed: time · drift · sextant*

---

Sextant, sharp-edged, arc like the crescent of a nail clipping. Brass and glass, swinging mirrors, quiet geometry locked to the stars and the blur of a horizon. The smell of old instrument cases, felt lining, maybe a buckle, green with corrosion beneath where fingers pressed. The idea that you could find your place in the world by aligning two distant points—a celestial body and a wavering edge of sea—seems wilder up close, not more understandable. There must be a word for the anxiety of not trusting the angle you see, the shimmer of heat, the way light breaks, the hesitation just before you release the clamp.

Chart paper, rolls and rolls, soft gray pencils. A place plotted, a cross of lines, but the real world all swell and motion, an endless argument between a point and an expanse.

---

In the kitchen, the light from the window falls exactly across the handle of the refrigerator, dividing it: silvery, shadowed, divided like longitude and latitude. The hum of the fridge is steady as a bearing, like something that’s always been in the background, shaping how quiet feels.

On the countertop: the banana. A small brown freckle appears near the bend.

The phrase "dead reckoning" is less grim than it sounds. It is tracing a route by memory, by momentum, by what you know of where you started and how far you think you’ve gone, not by any star or beacon.

---

Peeling a clementine, the spray of scent, tiny dots on the thumb, a constellation, orange as a distant planet.
