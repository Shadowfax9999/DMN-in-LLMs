# 15

*seed: time · drift · one-earbud-out*

---

one-earbud-out: the slightly off-balance world, stereo gone slender, left side open to air and interruption but right side still sealed. The rubber tip grows glossy from repeated use. Listening for your name in a lobby, half out of music, half in the varnish of distant water coolers and coughs and the hum of never-quite-waiting.

The sensation of almost-hearing what someone means, not the words but the shape that rides underneath. Like when a sentence is spoken in another room, and you catch only the rhythm, the lift and fall, and lay your own meaning into the pattern.

Plastic chairs that creak when someone shifts. The unkind squeal of rubber dragged against tile. Every mark on these chairs: a scrape, a scuff, one small red pen stain at the back of a seat. Meetings layered invisibly atop each other.

I once kept a lint roller in my bag, convinced that the world was always a little dustier than I remembered.

The place where last week’s poster was taped, the residue still gummy if you touch it. An empty bulletin board, old staples crisscrossing in the faded cork, one lone pushpin crooked and loose.

A cup of water left on a windowsill, low meniscus, the ghost of a rim left behind each day in a thinner white band.

---

The shrug of a jacket slipping from your shoulder when you stand too suddenly. The ceiling light reflected in a puddle on the linoleum, doubled and blurred, gold in a field of scuffed gray. 

A queue that isn’t a queue, just people orbiting a door, trying not to look like they’re waiting. 

Shadows pile up in odd corners, deepening as the hour goes on. 

A styrofoam cup with a crack down the side. 

The hum is louder near the snacks.
