*seed: time · drift · mumble*

mumble

the mouth reshaping sound, lips pressed, the sense of almost but not quite — friction and fog at the back of the teeth  
not murmur, not ramble, not mutter: mumble is round, caught between beginning and end  
tumbled pebble in the cheek — masc, fumble, jumble, grumble —  
the u a soft cup, the m’s doubled cushion, the b a lip-shutter, the l an exit  
sound that never quite signs itself

A mumble is what a stone would do if it could try to pronounce its own name underwater  
or the blur in the tape when the string of words is too heavy for air  
at the edge of waking, voices through walls, shape without edges

---

There are languages where consonants ride under the tongue, never surfacing  
A mumble is a refusal to declare, a shelter for the unfinished word  
Some days the world itself feels mumbled — half-light, newsprint soft, meaning dampened  
Fog on a field, shoes in thick grass, a promise half-remembered  
What sound does patience make when left alone?

Sometimes clarity is just a kind of violence
