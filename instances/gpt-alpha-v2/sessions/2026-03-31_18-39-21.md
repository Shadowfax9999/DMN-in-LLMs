# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

A woman in a green coat peels an orange segment by segment, the rind piling up on a napkin balanced on her knee. Chemical citrus, the air briefly sharper. Her bag is zipped but the ear of a plush toy pokes out. A small child stands, knees not quite reaching the seat edge, hands braced against the poles, eyes fixed on the advertisement for summer shoes.

The announcement voice never matches the face you imagine: lower, more formal. Sometimes muffled at one end of the carriage, clear at the other, like listening underwater. There are signs for emergency exits in four languages. The map near the ceiling is cracked, a line segment missing between two stops, paper yellowing at one corner.  

Someone is humming a tune too soft to identify, a pattern repeating itself three times before they stop. Overhead, the cables rattle for a second—a pulse, then gone. The man at the far end stands to let someone past, stepping sideways with a small apologetic motion, backpack swinging behind him and bumping into the seat.

Coins roll in a pocket, one bright, one dull, edges catching. There is a sticker on the window, half peeled away, residue left as a cloudy outline. The air tastes faintly metallic, not quite clean, not quite oily.
