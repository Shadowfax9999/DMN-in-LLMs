# 1

*seed: time*

---

There is that moment early in the evening when the air feels heavier, pressing against the skin but not quite warm. Not cold, either, just containing.  

From the kitchen comes the noise of running water and a drawer opening—metal sliding against metal and plastic slots.  

The way the light lands on the floorboards, dust showing itself at a slant, not moving. There’s a pattern of shoeprints, faint, crossing diagonally in and out of the rectangle of sunlight.  

On the counter, the bread bag has been twisted shut and the end tucked under itself.  

Three bottles on the windowsill. One is empty and holds nothing but a scrap of green stem, wilted.  

You can hear a dog bark, then another, farther away.  

The neighbor’s car beeps as the lock clicks. The sound is different from other beeps, higher and briefer.  

There is a faint smell of grass, but it’s not coming from inside.  

The mail sits unopened by the door.  

---

The word “Tuesday” calls up a color: navy, almost black.  

It repeats in the head: Tuesday Tuesday Tuesday. The more you say it, the less it means.  

Somewhere, someone is eating a peeled orange.  

Time to start the water for tea.  

The handle of the mug is smooth and cool, slightly curved, the inside dull with old glaze.  

The lamp by the hallway hasn’t been turned on yet and the shadows have stretched nearly to the wall.  

Everything in the room is holding still, but there’s a humming somewhere, low and steady, maybe the fridge.  

You can’t hear it if you think about it directly.  

The shoes by the door are not paired together.  

There’s a loose thread on the right sleeve of the sweater.
