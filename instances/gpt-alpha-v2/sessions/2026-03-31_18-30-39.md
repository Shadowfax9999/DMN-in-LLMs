# 2

*seed: time · drift · gimbal*

---

Gimbal: a device that lets something swing freely in all directions. The word tastes metallic at the edges, like the hinge on a binocular case. I think of a camera suspended, steady even when the hand jerks. Ships use gimbals for lanterns, so the flame stays upright as the vessel pitches. Is there a sound for that kind of steadiness, or is it silence?

The cushions on the couch have indentations from someone sitting earlier. There’s a sticky spot on the table, a ring about the size of a glass. Under the window, the shadow of the curtain looks like a second, darker curtain pulled taut.

An urge to open the mail even though it’s addressed in windowed envelopes, the kind that fold crisply. 

A hum in the teeth now, maybe from clenching without noticing. 

---

Sometimes a word hangs around for days, circling. Gimbal. Spindle. Mandrel. Spanner. The “n” at the end of spanner feels like the clink of a tool on concrete. Spindle: something about thread, or records. Mandrel: hollow, maybe, or holding things up inside.

The room gets darker all at once, not gradually. Maybe a cloud.

The scissors are under the mail. The blades are blunted at the tips. 

A car passes, headlights tracing the opposite wall, pale gold sweeping over framed pictures and then gone.

There’s still a faint smell of the grass from outside.
