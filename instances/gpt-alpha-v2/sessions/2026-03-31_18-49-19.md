# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

A distant siren muffled by the leaves just starting to push out of the plane trees. Car windows down: bass thumming, a fragment of conversation, someone’s laugh snapped off by the stoplight. The crosswalk’s blinking red hand, ignored by four jaywalkers and two pigeons. There’s a pattern of old gum stuck to the pavement, a constellation if you look at it long enough.

Half a sandwich, foil unwrapped, left on the low wall. White napkin under a pebble. The air is thicker near the bus shelter, warm with a hint of engine and something doughy from the bakery farther down. The bus lurches to the curb, exhaling, door stuttering open and shut.

A woman in lime green sunglasses checks her phone, shoves it away, and wipes a smudge from her skirt. A cough from behind the glass. Sunlight catching the gold chain on the man’s wrist as he adjusts his grocery bag, shifting weight from foot to foot.

---

The clang and whirr of a recycling truck as it crushes bottles, blue bin tipped sideways, a splash of something sticky on the asphalt. The truck’s hydraulic hiss trails behind, a ribbon of sound.

A delivery bike idling in the gutter, its box plastered with fading stickers. The driver flips his helmet up, scratches under his chin, glances at the storefronts as if looking for a number that doesn’t exist.

Some graffiti tags under the bridge — black and red, layered, scratched-out in parts so the paint peels into curls. Above, shoe laces dangle from the power lines, faded nearly white, turning slightly in the late sun.

There’s a cat under a parked van, its shadow longer than its body, eyes slow-blinking at the traffic. No one notices. The walk signal changes. The flow shifts again. 

The traffic light makes a small metallic click when it flips.
