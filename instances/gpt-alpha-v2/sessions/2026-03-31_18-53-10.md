# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

A door that clicks but doesn't latch, so you have to press it all the way, or else it drifts open again, a slow retreat. Floorboards with a spot that sinks a little, not enough to see, but you know it's there. I think of the green digital numbers on the stove, too bright at 3 AM, pooling on the tile.

Curtains that always tangle together at one end of the rod, no matter how evenly you start them. The stack of unread mail, slumped but unmoving for days. The pencil with so many eraser bits stuck to the ferrule it won't mark cleanly.

A glass of water that tastes like the shelf it was left on, not quite clean, not quite dusty.

---

Cloud cover so thin the sun is everywhere, not visible, just diffused, hard to tell the hour by the color of the sky. Shoes on power lines, looking deliberate from one angle but accidental from another. White paint on the curb that flakes off in thick curls; when you peel one off it leaves a perfect line of gray concrete underneath.

The sound of a bus stop bell that doesn't match the pictogram on the sign.
