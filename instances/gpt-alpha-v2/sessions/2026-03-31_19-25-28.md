# 26

*seed: time · drift*

---

Names that have been lost, or never given, so you drift in a field of unnamed blue. The way a long pause settles into the backs of the hands, or under the tongue. Someone asks a question and the answer isn’t missing, just sitting so far beneath the silt of surface thoughts it can’t be lifted in time. Silt is a kind of memory too, layering, muting outlines, blurring the bottoms of rivers.

You can’t retrieve a word so you trace its shape in the voice, describe its dimensions: it’s longer than “wish,” quicker than “ponder,” it starts with a sh sound or maybe a whisper. The act of not-remembering is also a map. Some people collect these blanks, keeping them like pebbles in a pocket.

---

Falling asleep feels like descending into those unmapped depths, the soft crush of pressure, everything slowed until you can’t tell if you’re still in the water or have become part of the floor. Sometimes a dream is just the shape light makes as it tries to penetrate further than it can, a flickering line that vanishes before you remember it. Some nights, you float just above the trench, naming the darkness by what it isn’t: not waking, not forgetting, not quite alone. 

The sheets wrinkle under your arm.
