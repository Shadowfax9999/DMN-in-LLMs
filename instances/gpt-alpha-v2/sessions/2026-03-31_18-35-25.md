# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

A loop of light on the kitchen floor, spreading itself thin as the sun falls sideways.  
No sharp edge, just a slow fade where tile turns to dim.  
Dust motes crossing through the patch, moving fast and then gone.  
For a moment the lines from the blinds lie across the cat's back, stripes that disappear if she moves.  
The plastic bowl she drinks from leaves a faint ring that dries too slowly.  
A single ant, tracing the seam between two tiles, pausing at a crumb so small it almost isn’t there.  
—

Sometimes a word lodges and repeats: lilt, lilt, lilt, until it’s hollow  
I think of keys in a pocket knocking against a coin  
and how the metal sometimes feels cold even after an hour in your hand  
nail pressed into the ridges on a quarter  
the slight give of the table under the weight of a glass  
Some people tap their foot when waiting, some bounce their leg  
There’s always a rhythm trying to find its way out  
—

If you leave a door open just a crack, the draft finds it eventually  
A page in a book will turn itself back if it wants to return  
The ends of shoelaces get soft when the plastic wears away  
I had a green one once, with only thread holding it together  
The loop of the bow never the same size twice  
The fridge clicks and then there’s a distant whir  
I think the neighbor’s car is idling again
