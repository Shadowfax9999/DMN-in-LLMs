# 14

*seed: time · drift · loading dock*

---

Loading dock: not at the level of the street, not at ground but up, open, a platform for what is shifting, in-between, unsorted. Pallets with the ghost of labels, dark tape, splintered edges. A rubber bumper that yields, leaves a mark on your hip if you lean back too long, not quite clean, always slightly soft, like the surface remembers each truck. The painted lines are thicker than parking-lot stripes, and the yellow sometimes doubles back over itself, wider at corners, never straight.

A bell or buzzer: not quite timed with the door, sometimes a clatter, someone calls out unseen. The echo is flat, concrete-walled, a coldness that persists through summer. Metal roll-up doors with windows you cannot see through, just shapes moving, arms raising, backs turning.

---

The feeling of a word on your tongue that refuses to resolve into a taste. "Sluice." "Barrow." Words that live near each other in some dark, corrugated pile.

---

I once saw a plastic bag caught halfway under the heavy lip of a loading dock, shivering when the wind tunneled beneath, still as paper the rest of the time.

A calendar page taped above a phone, days already marked off.

Somewhere above, the sound of pigeons that you can't see, only the small powdery mess they leave in corners. 

Someone eating an apple, the sound too sharp for the space. 

A dent in a steel door from a time no one will name.
