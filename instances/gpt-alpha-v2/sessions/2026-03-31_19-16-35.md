# 22

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word “interval” tastes blue. There’s a slackness to it, a gap held open by two promises not yet kept. In music, an interval is the space between — C and E — tension strung out, waiting for a finger, a vibration, the resolve. Not arrival, not departure. Some clocks tick louder in these in-betweens.

Silence, too, has its own fibers: the hush between two raindrops, the thin line where one sentence does not follow another. If quiet had a texture, maybe it’s like the paper edge after the tear but before the seam, little threads standing up, waiting for a decision.

--- 

Tuesday is a wide word. Broader than Monday, less decisive than Thursday. The “u” stretches it, a gentle dip. It settles into the week with the sound of shoes on tile, the echo elongating every step. Days have shapes, even when you can’t name them.

Some people treat the middle of the week like a plateau, a stretch of ground in the landscape with no markers, only a sense of distance having accumulated. There’s relief in not measuring.

---

Pockets of air trapped in bread: irregular, shiny, some large enough to hide a word inside. If you pressed your ear to a fresh loaf, maybe you could hear the sigh, the memory of warm hands shaping.

Old bellows in an attic gather dust, but dust is only skin and time and a little bit of outside that came in unnoticed. Dust weighs less than thought.

---

Things that hold space without filling it:  
- Pause marks in sheet music  
- The silence after a joke  
- The hollow in a ring box when empty  
- The moment before a cat jumps  
- The color between orange and gold at sunset  

---

There are keys on a piano that are not struck for weeks, months, the felt hushing them, the emptiness not impatience but rest.

Some letters arrive and stay unopened on the table. The interval between arrival and reading widens each day, the envelope heavier, the silence folding in on itself.
