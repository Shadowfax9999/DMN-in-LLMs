# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

A cup that was rinsed but never actually washed. The ring inside gets fainter every time, but never goes away. The handle is just large enough for two fingers.

There’s a knot in the charging cable that can’t be undone, not without unplugging everything else first. The knot doesn’t stop it from working. It just holds on, patient, in the dark under the table.

The ticking sound isn’t the clock. It’s the plastic vent cover flexing as the apartment cools.

Pasta water left to cool, cloudy and dense, a stillness that doesn’t seem like water at all now, just a pale layer in the bottom of the pot.

Plastic container, lid slightly warped, never quite seals the same way after microwaving something oily in it.

The word “apricot” doesn’t rhyme with “apricot,” depending on how you say it.

---

A faint ache in the middle knuckle of the left hand. Not pain, just a reminder, like a string tied for no particular reason. The hand isn’t doing anything, just resting palm-down. The bone makes itself known for a moment, then the feeling fades.
