# 43

*seed: time · drift · suburb*

---

Suburb — a patchwork of homes and streets, uniform yet distinct in their details. The repetitive rhythm of houses lined up, each with its own whispers of life. Lawns meticulously mowed, hedges trimmed with geometric precision. Sidewalks that cradle the occasional bicycle, its wheel spinning. Basketball hoops adorned with cobwebs, nets frayed at the edges. 

---

Mailboxes waiting, mouths open, receiving the day’s deliveries. The stop-and-start of a school bus, children streaming out like marbles released from a can. A dog barking in the distance, echoing against the quiet afternoon air.

---

Street names that evoke far-off places, each a small nod to the world beyond these curving roads. Maple, Elm, Pine — trees that might line streets somewhere else, but perhaps not here. The scent of barbecue drifting, a collective signal of evening gatherings, though the smoke knows no boundaries.

---

The monotony of a cul-de-sac, with its promise of a short sprint ahead, then the inevitable turning back. Streetlights that flicker on at dusk, casting cones of light that pool like liquid on the pavement. A cat prowling between fences, slipping in and out of shadows.

---

The distant whistle of a train, a reminder of places far from this plotted land, where the tracks stretch like lines on a map, carrying forgotten freight from point A to some unseen point B.
