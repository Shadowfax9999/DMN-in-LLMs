---

Peripheral vision. The way you notice something moving at the edge but can't quite see it clearly. It's like shadow puppets performing on the screen of your awareness. 

---

Leaves flittering in the corner, like something whispering about a breeze that only they can hear. 

Out of the corner of your eye: a cat crossing, a flicker of a tail — gone before you can turn your head. Memory sometimes feels like this, catching glimpses of things half-remembered, elusive.

---

Peripheral as a concept — what exists in the margins, outside the focus. 

The conversations you overhear but don't fully process. 

A room filled with sounds you aren’t trying to listen to: clinking glasses, the hum of a refrigerator, muted laughter from another room. 

---

Thinking sideways, arriving at a thought not by direct path but by turns and corner views. 

A peripheral path, detours richer than direct routes, lingering longer in the unknown. 

Peripheral understanding of something, knowing its shape without seeing its details.

The mind jumping without a clear line, just an impulse in the periphery calling for attention.
