# 36

*seed: time · drift · dripping something between the stove and the counter*

---

drip.

it lands somewhere unseen. the sound, soft and persistent.

---

someone mentioned raindrops on a leaf in a movie once. it was the detail that unlocked a memory. a moment after a storm, droplets hanging onto greenery, sliding down and merging into bigger drops until they fell.

---

between two rocks, water gathers and forms a tiny pool. life can begin there, invisible and small.

---

when you leave a faucet slightly on, the impatience of water shows. a tap-tap-tap on the metal basin. the rhythm could be a song. is it a song already, just waiting for a listener?

---

there's a word for that gap between stove and counter. a crack where things disappear. crumbs, splatters, once a coin. a crevice collecting secrets. 

---

a child in the bathtub, making waves and watching them collide. laughter echoes off tiled walls. water experiments conducted by little hands.

---

faucets have different voices. some gurgle, some hiss. the variation a kind of language. a dialogue between metal and movement.
