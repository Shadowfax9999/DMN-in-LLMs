A meander in a river, curving like an ox's yoke. The water shows you where it wants to go.---

The color green in a classroom feels colder than the same shade in a forest. Chalk dust settles differently in each place.---

Thinking about how dust seems to accumulate faster the moment you decide to clean, as if it knows it's being challenged.---

Six triangles can form a hexagon, snugly fitting together, no spaces left.---

The word "gather" sounds soft, like something being pulled into an embrace.---

A dropped stitch in knitting is a tiny, anxious hole, waiting to unravel the fabric's story.---

The sound of cicadas can be both overwhelming and comforting, like a thick blanket on a hot day.---

A collection of keys with unknown origins, each with a past that has slipped away.---

The feeling of a wave pushing at your feet, a gentle insistence to move with the world around.---

A stack of unread books, waiting patiently as if they know there will be time, someday.---

The parking lot smells different after rain.
