# 15

*seed: time · drift · collapse*

---

Collapse: the feeling of a house of cards toppling in slow motion. The word echoes with both the physical act of folding in on itself and the emotional weight of something once sturdy now yielding. An umbrella, its structure compromised, sagging under unexpected gusts. A souffle, deflating quietly in the oven, leaving behind a sweetness that lingers. 

Collapse can be a metaphor, but also an engine failing, the sudden silence of a machine no longer running, or a shelf in a pantry giving way, releasing a rain of cans and boxes. 

The mind shifts to paper crumpling, a tactile sound, full of finality and transformation. The warm embrace of a sweater after a long day, pulled off and bunched up, its previous form dissolved. 

What about a star collapsing, the unfathomable scale of it and the quiet enormity. A leaf falling from a tree, not collapse but a gentle descent, yet touching the ground still signifies a change. 

---

The number seven carries a sense of completion and mystery. Seven days, seven seas, seven colors in a rainbow. 

A list can organize the disorganized thoughts of a day:
1. The color green of a traffic light.
2. The whisper of wind through hair.
3. The slight tension of a waiting room chair.
4. The sound of shoes shuffling on tiled floors.
5. The distant hum of city traffic.
6. The tick of a clock in a silent room.
7. The smooth coolness of a metal key in hand.

The lists comforts and rearranges the noise.

---

A single sentence: The water from the melting ice dripped slowly onto the counter.
