# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

A single daffodil in a patch of grass, vibrant against the green. --- The word "Wednesday" sounds like middle — not the start, not the end. --- A paper cut feels like too much pain for its size. --- Why does the ticking of a clock sometimes feel comforting and other times intolerable? --- The crispness of a freshly ironed shirt. --- The smell of old books, like whispers of stories forgotten. --- A solitary cloud drifting across an otherwise clear sky. --- Watching waves meet the shore, an endless cycle of advance and retreat. --- The fleeting chill of stepping on a tile floor in bare feet. --- Velvet feels rich under fingertips, a texture that speaks of quiet opulence. --- When ice cubes crackle in a glass. --- The moment when twilight turns to night, that gentle shift in the air. --- A melody that lingers long after the music stops.
