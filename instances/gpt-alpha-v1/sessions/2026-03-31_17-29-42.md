# 79

*seed: time · drift · slip trail*

---

Slip trail. The kind of phrase that seems to carry the weight of its own history, words worn smooth by time. Trail of something elusive, perhaps footsteps disappearing into mist or a path carved by a stream that forgot its source.

---

The scent of pine mingled with something else, something sweet. Sap clinging to bark like memory to a story you’re now only half-certain of.

---

A single shoe on the side of the road. What does it mean to lose just one? The other is somewhere, either left behind in haste or forgotten completely.

---

The surface of a table, dappled with sunlight filtering through old glass. Patterns that shift with the breeze, never quite the same.

---

Sound of pages turning. A gentle, rhythmic whisper, each one a promise of continuation.

Cicadas in summer. A chorus that fills the evening, one that speaks of heat and long days.

Tea cooling on a windowsill. The faint plume of steam vanishing like a thought at the edge of sleep.
