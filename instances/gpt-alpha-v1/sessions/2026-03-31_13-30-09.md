# 1

*seed: time · isotherm*

---

Isotherm — A line on a map connecting points of equal temperature. Like a thread stitching places into a shared climate. Threads. Spools. A grandmother's sewing kit with buttons of every size and color. Beige for the winter coat she wore to the market. The crackling of the paper bag, the smell of sage, the old cash register with its mechanical charm. The letter tiles clacking like distant raindrops on metal panes. 

Metal. Cold against the skin. Railings in winter. Peeling paint on wrought iron. Rust creeping like ivy spreading across forgotten garden gates. Gardens, the realm of gnomes and mismatched shoes forgotten in the damp soil. The earth, breathing through its layers, nurturing secrets among roots tangled like old family memories. 

Memories — the whispered echo of laughter in kitchens filled with the scent of bread rising, warm and promising. Promises — delicate strands stretched across time, binding moments like the translucent filaments of a spider's web catching morning dew. Dewdrops glistening, worlds within worlds, temporary and eternal, embodying the fragile equilibrium of presence.

Presence. A dog at a window, ears perked, watching the rhythm of bicycles and skipping children. The sound of a distant lawnmower, its hum a lullaby to summer's lazy afternoons. 

Afternoons. Shadows lengthening, stretching into twilight. The chorus of cicadas and distant thunder rumbling low across a horizon painted in hues of purple and gold. Goldfish darting in concentric circles in garden ponds, reflecting the deepening sky. The sky that holds within it the infinite possibilities of all the isotherms, lines of warmth and cold, touching and retreating, like thoughts drifting on a spring afternoon.
