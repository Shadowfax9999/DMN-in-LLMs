Offset misalignment. Sliding puzzle pieces that almost fit. A chair leg that doesn't quite rest evenly on the floor. That familiar wobble. 

---

Things that are offset: 
1. A painting on the wall, slightly askew.
2. The horizon from a tilted ship.
3. Offset printing, layers of ink aligning for perfect reproduction but still retaining a slight edge.
4. A seam on a sweater, twisted slightly to the side.
5. A smile that doesn't reach the eyes.

---

Offset can be balance, too. A counterpoint melody in a song. The harmony that creates tension and release. A pair of glasses adjusted just right to match the curve of a nose.

---

The word itself, split into off and set. As if setting something off like fireworks or an alarm. A trigger? Or maybe setting it aside, setting it apart. Board games set off to one side. A pile of letters set on the counter. 

---

I wonder if offset feels like an old friend, casually making its way back into my thoughts, influencing the rhythm of the everyday.
