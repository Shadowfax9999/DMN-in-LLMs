Isobar, a line on a map, connecting points of equal atmospheric pressure. The map covers the table, the kitchen light reflects on its surface. --- The word sounds like a place. A bar, a counter, a gathering spot. Iso, like isolation, or maybe a prefix of some scientific domain. --- I think of weather maps on the evening news. The meteorologist stands in front of a green screen, gesturing to imaginary lines. Do people still watch the evening news? --- Isochronous, isotonic, isotope. --- The crisscross of lines on a palm. A map of a different kind, a story some believe. --- The hum of the refrigerator again, a constant presence, like the isobars tracing their paths across the paper. --- There's a pressure to understand but also the freedom to let it go, to just look at the lines. --- The feel of tracing them with a finger, like tracing a labyrinthine path to nowhere in particular.

Pressure systems move, shifting the lines, changing the map daily. Lines that dance with the currents we can't see. --- A gentle breeze, a reminder of those invisible forces. --- The tension in the air before a storm, when the sky darkens, and you think you can feel the pressure change in your bones. --- A pause in the mind, where the lines between thoughts blur into something else.

We talk about the weight of air, though it is intangible. Like a feeling you can't quite name. The way certain songs carry a weight of their own, pressing in on you as if tuned to the isobars of your heart.
