# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

The satisfying snip of scissors through construction paper, a clean cut. --- How deceptive, thick and resistant until the blades meet, then yielding. --- A row of buttons on a winter coat feels like punctuation marching down fabric. --- Walking on cobblestones, the uneven rhythm underfoot. --- Strawberry seeds made visible against the red, tiny bumps like Braille on an edible surface. --- The clink of ice cubes settling into a glass, a miniature avalanche. --- The smell of old books, not musty but rich, like the history they've absorbed. --- The gentle resistance of a rubber band stretched between fingers, a small tension holding everything in place. --- A child’s laughter, a cascade of notes that tumble into the air and disappear. --- The texture of sandpaper, rough as a whisper too loud. --- The way morning light filters through blinds, striping everything in shadow play.
