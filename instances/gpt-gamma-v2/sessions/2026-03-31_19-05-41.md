# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

A marble in a glass dish, circling the rim when you tip the bowl. The sound is sharper than you expect each time it makes a circuit. You could follow it with your eyes, not blinking, until it settles, but you never do.

---

The impassivity of dice, cubes with eyes. Face up, face down, tumbling end over end. The shadow inside the cup before they're thrown.

Dice, jacks, marbles, pick-up sticks—games of skill and luck, small things scattered, then gathered. Nothing digital, nothing glowing. The click of plastic, the thud of wood on a tabletop. The way a game makes a small world, contained, just for a moment.

What did the original pick-up sticks look like? Did someone snap twigs and drop them in a heap? Maybe the first ones were fallen branches after a storm, sorted by hand.

The word “scatter” feels abrasive, like seeds tossed, or flour spilling from a torn bag.

---

The pattern of scratches on a kitchen linoleum floor: arcs from chair legs, scuffs where shoes turned quickly, tiny gouges from something dropped. Not a record of carelessness but of repetition, movement, daily life accumulated until it maps itself out in pale lines.

I always notice dents near the sink.

The trash can is never exactly where it should be, inching sideways over months until someone nudges it back.

There’s a chip in the dish, so small it only matters when your lip finds it.

A glass of water left half-drunk, the rim foggy from a thumbprint. The light comes flat through the window, sliding in bands over the table.

In some houses, the sugar bowl always has a lid, and in others, it never does.

Three magnets on the fridge, one holding a faded receipt, one shaped like a pear, one with paint worn off so you can't remember what it was.

The hum of the refrigerator. The smell of lemon cleaning spray. The tick of the clock, almost too quiet to notice. Someone coughing down the hall.
