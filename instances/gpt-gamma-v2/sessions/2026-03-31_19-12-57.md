# 22

*seed: time · drift*

---

A rubber band looped around two fingers, twisted and released — the dull snap, the memory of red marks left behind.

The first grape in a bag is usually softer than the rest.

A drawer always half-open, as if indecision could settle into wood.

Batteries rolling in their package when you pick it up — the clatter says there are still some left.

An old sticker, half-peeled, collecting lint at its edge, the adhesive gray and pilled.

Some shoes are lined with felt that catches on a sock thread, so the foot pauses, stuck fractionally mid-slide.

---

The sudden cold when you open a freezer, the thin plume of frost that follows.

The ridges on the sides of a coin can leave imprints if squeezed tightly enough.

The pressure of holding a pencil too long, a pink dent in the side of the finger.

There’s a way to balance on the back legs of a chair that always feels like just before falling.

There are three tealights in the drawer, but only one has a wick that stands upright.

The refrigerator makes a sound at odd intervals, like breathing out.

The shoehorn is missing again.
