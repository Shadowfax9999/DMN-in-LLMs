# 17

*seed: time · drift · depth*

---

Depth sounds blue more often than not, but sometimes it flickers green, like the place where the pool tile glows at dusk and you can't tell if it's shadow or water.

The word itself: d-e-p-t-h — the p and t pressed together make it stumble in the mouth, a hard landing for a soft concept.  
d  
e  
p  
t  
h  
It arranges itself in a downward slope.

---

Standing at the rail above a stairwell: you look down and the drop feels longer than you remember, some echo pressing at your ears, a cold space under the steps where dust lives.

People talk about “emotional depth” but that's just as murky, the deeper you go the harder it is to see the shape of things.

A glass of tea, dark at the bottom where the light can't reach, sweet if you wait, bitter if you drain it too soon.

---

Depth of field: in photographs, that little sliver in focus, everything else melting into blur. The world is full of sharpness and fog and the gap between.

Sometimes you reach into a bag and can't find the bottom, your hand ghost-fumbling for a pen, a key, a folded receipt.

The sound of a note struck on a piano and left to ring out, lingering in the air; the tail of it dropping where only your chest can feel it.

---

Depth can be a measure, a challenge, a hiding place.  
It can also be the place where the spoon goes missing.  

The bottom shelf is always a little colder than the rest.
