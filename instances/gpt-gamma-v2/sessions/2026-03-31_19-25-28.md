# 28

*seed: time · drift · peeling an orange in one piece*

---

Peeling an orange in one piece—something about the coil, the spiral, how it falls away in a single long ribbon, heavier at the ends. Sometimes it breaks and sometimes it doesn’t—your thumb makes its own slow path, aiming for the seam, muscle memory or just guessing where the pith will be weakest. The oil on your skin afterward clings for hours, sharper than the fruit itself.

Thumb under white pith, nails catching on a pocket of juice that wasn’t meant to burst yet.

Pith under sunlight looks almost translucent, threads that catch the yellow from the afternoon outside. I used to try for no tears, but it’s not satisfying unless something gives a little.

A bowl at the center of a table, peels stacked, curling into each other, the smell spreading out further than the fruit. It’s impossible to put the peel back into itself the way it came off, you can only half-reconstruct the sphere, loose, never whole.

---

Door hinges that stick on humid days  
A scrap of paper used as a bookmark, curling at one corner  
A clock ticking slightly out of rhythm with your pulse  
The slant of sunlight on a tiled floor, making the grout appear darker  
The way plastic bags balloon out on the sidewalk when it’s windy  
A light switch that clicks twice, too quick  
Faint sound of water rushing through pipes behind a wall  
The sticker residue left after peeling something off glass

---

When I was younger I thought oranges were more complicated than apples because you needed patience to get to the inside. An apple is immediate, no layers to cross. The hesitation before breaking the skin. Sometimes the first taste is a little bitter from the pith.
