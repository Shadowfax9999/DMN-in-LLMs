# 24

*seed: time · drift · the handwriting of someone you used to know*

---

Noticing how the loop in a lowercase "g" sometimes closes so tight it’s like a knot, and other times it’s a wide open curl. The way someone dots their “i” — a smack, a feather, a careless flick. Letters leaning forward, letters standing very still. Some people’s handwriting looks like it’s bracing for a sudden wind.

The page underneath, faintly ridged where the pen pressed too hard. Running a finger along the back and feeling the ghost of the sentence.

There’s always a word you can’t decipher, and the rest of the sentence tilts around its missing tooth.

---

The word “fallow.” I remember reading it in a book and thinking it felt yellow, like old paper, or wheat left uncut.

Words like “creak” and “cricket,” both thin and jointed, with the “k” at the end folding in on itself.

Some words feel like they only appear at a certain time of year: “thaw,” “bud,” “sheaf.”

You can almost taste the afternoon when someone writes “October” at the top of a letter.

---

Someone once gave me a list of their favorite words. Most of them had soft middles and hard endings. “Plinth,” “mercurial,” “sough.” The list was folded in quarters and smelled of rosemary.

Lists inside lists: words you mean to use, words you avoid, words that feel too heavy for what you’re trying to say.

The ink in a ballpoint skips sometimes, leaves a gap in the middle of a word, and then you have to guess what almost was.
