# 21

*seed: time · drift · developing fluid*

---

Photographs that are still soft, shapes swimming under the surface, waiting for their edges. The chemical scent is sour and sticky and metallic, or maybe it’s a memory of that: the darkroom, the bulb, the plastic tongs clacking together at the edge of the tray.

Red light that isn’t quite dark but isn’t day, either. The sense that everything is waiting, the faces half-revealed in grain. The pleasure in watching something become itself.

A word can also float like this, not yet fixed, present as a shimmer before it resolves.

---

Chalk dust collecting on the lip of the blackboard, sometimes tracing a finger’s path. The sound of erasers clapped together outside, a low thudding echo through the hallway.

Sweater cuffs damp from leaning against a sink. A single ant finding its way towards the sugar bowl.

When you walk in socks across a rug, sometimes the static gathers, waiting for the next metal touch to let go.
