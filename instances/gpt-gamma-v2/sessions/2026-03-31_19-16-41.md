# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

Yesterday’s rain dried almost before I noticed.

Moss on brick soaks up the last puddle, and there’s a dark line left behind, a shape that vanishes as I stare.

A row of tulips tipping over, red petals soft at the edges, where the color runs thin.

---

A box of thumbtacks with a curled plastic lid. The way the metal points flash, catching even the smallest amount of light.

Three things I saw through the window without meaning to:

— a woman in lime green shoes on her phone at the stoplight  
— a pigeon walking in tight circles by the curb  
— a cloud shaped like a lowercase “r”  

The pigeons always look too heavy for their feet.

---

A word can carry a shadow, sometimes it’s in the vowels.

Sometimes you mean to say one thing and another syllable sneaks in; it’s only when you hear yourself that you realize it.

There’s always a moment between writing a word and reading it back where it doesn’t quite belong to you.

The library closes at eight.
