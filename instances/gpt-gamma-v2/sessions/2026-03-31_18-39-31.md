# 9

*seed: time · drift · fretboard*

---

Fretboard: lines you can’t see, only feel, under the pads of your fingers. The space between. The little ridges gathering dust if you never play. Polished in spots like a path worn in wood.

The word has a thickness, like it could be chewed. Fret, bored. Or maybe it’s more like the boards themselves bore the frets—something made sturdy by the interruptions.

Strings pressing against metal. The way a finger leaves a dent just for a second, and the note is different if your hand is cold.

Sometimes the edge of a fret scrapes as you slide up, a tiny snag, smaller than the sound of a zipper. It’s nothing until you notice it.

---

Inside a car, someone is tuning the radio. The static wanders through the air, separating voices into dust. Each station a window, and most are dark.

A parking garage with the lights on. The concrete always smells a little wet, even in summer. Tires squeak at the corner, then quiet.

Three plastic bags tangled in a bare branch, moving but not leaving.
