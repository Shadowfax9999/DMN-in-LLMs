# 6

*seed: time · drift · kerning pair*

---

Kerning pair — the quiet space between, not quite empty, not quite touching. AV, To, Fa, the way the diagonal and the oval lean into each other like two people trying not to bump elbows. Spaces that aren’t measured, exactly, more like felt, adjusted by hand until they sit right. Not a rule, but a nudge.

The smell of pencil graphite on the side of a hand after writing too quickly. There’s a faint ghost of a word pressed into the next page. The thin curl of eraser shavings, pink or gray, coiling like little worms.

---

Two oranges on the counter. One spot growing soft and dark at the stem.

A chair that rocks unevenly, one leg shorter by the barest amount. Fingers pressing down the edge of a paper to crease it into a triangle.

The difference between a shadow cast by morning light and the one cast at dusk, the angle stretched, color shifting—almost blue, nearly gone.

A shirt collar that won't stay folded, the corner always winging outward. A pair of shoelaces, one frayed at the tip.

Behind the ceiling vent, the low hum, maybe the air conditioner, maybe the distant city, maybe nothing at all.
