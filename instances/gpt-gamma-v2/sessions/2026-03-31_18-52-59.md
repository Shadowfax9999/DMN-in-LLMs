# 14

*seed: time · drift · quilt*

---

quilt.  
weight—not heavy, just present. The smell of cotton and something older, sun-warm or maybe attic-cool. I can see the stitches, wandering in loops, sometimes turning unexpectedly, thread darker in some places, like shadows in fabric.

Why are some colors called “cornflower” and others “dusty rose”; what does it mean for a color to be tired, or sleepy?

A sudden association: the gentle crinkle when you fold and unfold it.  
List of folds:  
— Over the foot of the bed  
— On the arm of a couch  
— In a linen closet, with that soap smell  
— Once, in the passenger seat of a car, just for a minute  
— Pressed under someone’s knees during a sleepover

---

You can lose earrings in a quilt, tiny beads rolling into seams.

The word “patchwork” is soft in the mouth.

If you flip the quilt, the pattern dissolves into knots and threads, the hidden side, not meant for display.

Some names for quilt blocks:  
Log Cabin  
Bear Paw  
Double Wedding Ring  
Flying Geese

The silence after a zipper closes.

You can trace fingerpaths over stitched shapes, learning the geography of someone else’s hands.

The lavender sachet at the back of the closet smells faintly of dust and soap.

Sometimes quilts pretend to be maps, but the roads never lead anywhere off the fabric.

There’s a spot of light on the floor, moving slowly as the sun shifts.
