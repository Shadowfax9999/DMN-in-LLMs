# 1

*seed: time · heat shimmer*

---

heat shimmer  
makes the air move in slow curtains, as if it were silk draped over a railing and tugged by invisible hands, the background bending, heat made visible, a kind of secret the sun tells the road

shimmer: almost a verb, the way it wants to do something, even when it’s a noun

words that belong together without having to be synonyms: shimmer, glimmer, twinkle, flicker  
somehow only shimmer is warm, the rest can be cold, or sharp, or distant  
shimmer is close, comes from the ground up, glimmer is far, a distant warning, the edge of a lighthouse or the beginning of hope

in the city, heat shimmer is for parking lots, not deserts; for roofs, not roads; sometimes on top of a car hood there's a moving mirage of sky, blue doubled and wriggling

sometimes looking over a barbecue grill, the air is thick and sliding, your own face reflected back at you in a way that feels unsteady

the word “shimmer” tastes like z and m in the mouth, even though it’s not there  
the “sh” is quiet, the “mm” is long, the “r” is a ripple

---

thinking of that trick your eyes do when you stare across hot pavement, things seem to float or break into fragments, as if the world loses its edges for a moment and something else winks in behind

heat shimmer reminds me of mirages  
mirage makes me think of thirst  
thirst makes me think of tongue stuck to the roof of a mouth  
the roof of a mouth is kind of ridged, like the surface of a corrugated road

there’s a place in one particular city where the crosswalk always looks like it’s underwater in June, the white stripes wavering

I have never seen a heat shimmer at night

the pool of air above a candle, disturbed  
the air over asphalt on a summer day  
the desert in movies, fake but somehow accurate  
steam above a kettle is not the same thing  
but it feels related

mirage, shimmer, dazzle, blinding

at the bus stop, the windows of the opposite building look like they are breathing  
the bus arrives, and the world sharpens again
