# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

A silverfish appears in the crease of a book left open, its movement a quick sag in the spine, almost soundless.

The corner of the rug folds under itself, and the lines no longer match with the tiles.

Sometimes, when the bus slows to a stop, the reflection in the window overlays the road with the shape of your own arms.

The sound of a zipper half-open, teeth glinting in a shallow curve — the tiny spaces between each catch.

A box of thumbtacks spilled, each one upright or fallen on its side, shining and irregular, all the points facing at odd angles.

---

A word you have to say slowly: persimmon.

A yellow painted curb that flakes into powder if you scratch it.

The insides of a plastic cup, thin ridges circling down and then abruptly stopping.

Soft click of a button that doesn’t do anything anymore.
