# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

Some things fit better if you rotate them first.

The blue tray with all the utensils never quite lines up with the drawer. The liner underneath wrinkles, catching spoons at an angle. You flatten it, but it puckers again.

In the supermarket, apples are stacked in a loose pyramid, never quite stable. There’s always one with a soft spot rolling toward the edge.

The window screen buzzes with small insects at dusk, the mesh dissolving into a grid and then into almost nothing as the sun shifts.

---

Letters on a page:  
l  
v  
m  
i  
h  
Tessellate only in certain fonts.

---

A sweater left in a pile takes on the weave of whatever it rests against, cable knit imprinting a temporary pattern onto the sheet below.

Some patterns are only there when you close your eyes — the purple afterimage from a bright lamp, the ghost of a checkerboard under your eyelids.

---

Things that don’t tessellate:  
-butcher paper torn from a roll  
the curve of a wrist on a wooden table  
sunlight on water when a breeze disturbs it  
the way shoes rest heel to toe in a heap near the door  
the shadow of an open drawer

---

A sidewalk’s edge wears unevenly, so the borders between slabs start to fray, and grass finds every gap.
