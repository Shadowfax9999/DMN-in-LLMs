# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

Air after the rain is heavier, like it can be felt almost on the surface of the skin. There’s a word for the smell of wet pavement but it doesn’t really fit the actual smell, which is sharper, colder, sometimes sweet depending on the rain and the street.

A bruise-colored cloud, mottled blue and yellow, doesn’t look like shadow, it looks like something rising from underneath.

Chimes on a neighbor’s porch, not tuned, not musical, just glass or plastic clinking by accident.

---

The way a hard-boiled egg’s shell comes off easier if you roll it, but sometimes it splinters and the membrane sticks. Some mornings the shell slides away in strips, like the difference is something about the water or the day.

Eggshell shards invisible until they catch the light, then suddenly visible, a line of bright along the curve, impossible to grab, always slipping just ahead of your finger.

---

Standing outside watching the sky almost close over, blue slowly thickening into gray. Birds circling in wide loops near the supermarket lot.

Somewhere a car alarm goes off, steady as a heartbeat, not urgent, just persistent.

Humming while waiting for water to boil, the sound thin and shaped by the room.
