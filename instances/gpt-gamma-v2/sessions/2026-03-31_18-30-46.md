# 2

*seed: time · drift · plinth*

---

plinth: the base of something that wants to be noticed, a word that feels like smooth stone and right angles, not quite cold, maybe a little dusty around the back

Plinth is not a word I hear spoken aloud. Museums, formal gardens, somewhere old, something waiting for a statue, or maybe the statue is gone

Empty plinths: what remains when whatever was meant to be commemorated has been removed, or hasn't arrived yet — the possibility of memory or announcement, but not the thing itself

Plinth, plinth, the sound is a flat-footed landing, a soft consonant crash

Some words are already heavy

---

I used to think dais and plinth were the same. Dais is softer, something you stand on for a speech, velvet and microphones. Plinth is stone, unmoving, for display.

The curb is a kind of plinth for passersby, but doesn't know it.

---

Birds will land on anything horizontal: the top edge of a stop sign, a warm car roof, the rim of a garbage can.

If you put something at waist height and flat, it will collect coins, wrappers, parking tickets.

The world is full of nearly-plinths.

There's a certain kind of pedestal sink that looks like it belongs in a room that echoes.

Sometimes statues seem embarrassed about their bases, like they're not meant for all that attention.

Ten pigeons on the parapet.

There's always a gum stain on the top ledge outside the post office.

The sun gets stuck on the same corner of the garden wall, late in the day.
