# 12

*seed: time · drift · charley horse*

---

Charley horse, stringy and awkward, not a horse at all but something that grabs and holds tight in the middle of moving. Sometimes in the night, your calf sets up its own small rebellion, folding itself into a knot that only patience or stretching can release. You lie there, immobilized, as if the muscle has remembered something you forgot.

Pain that is shaped like punctuation, sharp and instant, then lingering like a question mark.

There’s that way you walk afterward, tentative, uneven, not trusting your own step.

The word itself feels old-fashioned, like an uncle with stories about baseball games before there were lights. Some phrases arrive with a trunkful of history, even if you never unpack it.

Baseball cap on a hot afternoon, legs sore, dirt in your shoe.

Morning: remembering the twinge, half-expecting it to return. Checking the muscle with your hand, pressing into it, as if touch could make a promise.

Eggshell on the counter, faint crack down the middle, yolk waiting for light.

Apricot again, soft fuzz on skin, impossible to eat without sticky fingers.

A song plays somewhere, muffled, just above the drone of the refrigerator. Not a melody you know, exactly — more like a memory shaped into sound.

Curtains breathe in and out with the evening breeze.

The grass outside: taller than yesterday, not as tall as tomorrow.
