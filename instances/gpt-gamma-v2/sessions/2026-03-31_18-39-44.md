# 10

*seed: time · drift · the way elevator buttons light up differently in different buildings*

---

The “door close” button, sometimes lit, sometimes not, sometimes there but powerless, a placebo for impatience.

Red LEDs are sharper than white ones, like a pinprick if you stare at them too long.  
Green means go but in some elevators, green means the alarm or the fireman’s switch or nothing at all.

The carpet always has a pattern, loud or just speckled enough to hide lint.  
Panel of buttons at waist-height but the emergency phone behind a little metal flap down by your ankle.

Once, the button for the seventh floor was missing its plastic cover, just a soft gray nub and a light behind.

Mirrored doors, so you catch yourself looking, and for a second the person in the reflection is just waiting there.

A single hair caught in the seam between doors, moving when the elevator breathes.

There’s always a number that’s skipped—thirteen, sometimes four. The panel reminds you that someone decided ghosts don’t need to ride.

---

Monday is gray. Thursday is blue. Tuesday is the color you see inside your eyelids when you press gently.  
Saturn, Sunday, sand, the feeling of loose change.  
If a month belonged to a direction, would April point east or just curl around itself, soft and green, not going anywhere fast?

The sound of shoe soles on cheap linoleum fills up the space between elevator dings.  
If you breathe out at exactly the right moment, the doors seem to open for you.  

When you reach the lobby, the carpet is always flatter than you remember.
