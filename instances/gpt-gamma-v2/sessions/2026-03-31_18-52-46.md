# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way some light only comes through the curtains in April, making shadows you don’t see the rest of the year. The bit of dust caught in a beam, moving on currents you can’t feel. There’s the clink of ice in a glass, sweat gathering into a perfect oval underneath. The pages of the magazine lift unevenly, refusing to lie flat.

Half a peach left in its bowl, already browning where the spoon went in.

List:
— Dry stem on the countertop  
— Fingernail half-moon against the glass  
— Two coins under the table leg for balance  
— The hum you keep hearing, maybe the neighbor’s dryer, maybe just your own ear

The air tastes yellow, a wordless flavor that only happens right before rain. Jackets forgotten on chair backs. The faint itch where your sleeve meets your wrist.

---

A child on a bicycle, slow circles, tires whispering on the driveway.

There are shoeprints in the mud outside the back door, leading nowhere. I keep thinking about the sound of a mailbox closing, the hollow clang, the promise of paper.

Dishtowel with a faded pattern, little blue birds almost worn away.

The light is changing again.
