# 16

*seed: time · drift · surface tension*

---

surface tension:  
the way water beads up on waxed paper  
the tiny bulge where it meets the rim  
those glassy meniscuses in measuring cups  
the meniscus is the thing you watch to see if you have “enough”  

In a pottery studio, a bowl of water left overnight — the surface goes still and then collects a scatter of dust, specks floating, not sinking.

A brush loaded with slip, painting a seam, the film stretches for a second before it tears.

Sometimes when you rinse a sponge, a bubble clings to your finger longer than it should, skin holding it back, that thin elastic.

---

Clay catching under your nails.  
The roughness of a new tile floor beneath bare feet.  
A yellow bucket with a crack, water pooling around it but not through it.

---

Sometimes you find yourself smoothing a spot that’s already smooth.  
Sometimes you pick up two cups that weigh the same and one feels heavier.  
Sometimes the radio comes on but you don’t notice until the news is over.

---

There’s a clock in the corner.  
The second hand moves but doesn’t tick.  
There’s always a little slip on the doorknob.  
The lights are too bright in one spot, too dim in another.  
You can hear someone outside, their keys against metal.  
A damp apron on a hook, cool against your fingers.  
A row of shelves, something chipped, dust collecting.

---

The brush finally falls into the water and there’s a tiny ring where it lands.
