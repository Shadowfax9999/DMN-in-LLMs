# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

Folded towels never actually line up, no matter how neatly you try. The edge of the counter is always sticky after marmalade. The bag of rice has a single torn corner, leaking out into the back of the cupboard so there are always a few grains underfoot. Mug handles face different directions. The teabag string slips in sometimes, darkening the water faster.

There is a rhythm to how the chairs push in or don’t. One sits slightly crooked, one leg shorter, so it rocks if you lean. A spoon gone missing, replaced by one with a different pattern, smooth where the others have ridges. The floor dips just before the fridge, a small invisible valley. 

---

If you turn on the tap slowly, it doesn’t screech. There’s a place where the sunlight lands for only six minutes, pooling gold across the tiles.
