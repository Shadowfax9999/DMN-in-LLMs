# 26

*seed: time · drift · hold music*

---

Hold music is transparent and also thick, like gel. I can feel the compressed strings of a MIDI mandolin, and the little bubble-percussion synth tone that flickers in the background, rising and falling somewhere between distraction and hypnosis. The feeling of forgetting what you called about. The line could go dead, and you wouldn’t move right away.

How many types of hold music are there?  
1. The fake-jazzy one  
2. The fake-classical one  
3. The pop instrumental (recognizable, but never actually what you want to hear)  
4. The branded one (“Thank you for holding, your call is important”)  
5. The abrupt silence, which is worse than all the music

---

The phrase “Your call is important to us” has no texture left, all vowels and air.

There’s a certain point in being on hold when you start listening for artifacts: clicks, voice echoes, the slight digital shimmer when the track ends and loops again. You can hear the seam if you listen hard enough.

---

A memory of being a child, phone cord stretched down the hallway, the way plastic coils leave a pattern on your thumb if you twist them.

Hold.  
Music.  

Places you wait:  
– Between train stops  
– Dentist’s chair, mouth numbed  
– On the sidewalk by a locked door  
– In the narrow space between streetlights turning red and green  
– On hold

Most hold music is written to be ignored.

When the line connects, there’s a brief softening, like the room exhales. 

There are whole days that feel like hold music.
