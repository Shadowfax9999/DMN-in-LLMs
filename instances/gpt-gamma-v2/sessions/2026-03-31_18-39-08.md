# 7

*seed: time · drift · falsetto*

---

Falsetto radiates out of a throat, thinner, glassier than the rest of the voice, always a little startling. The break between chest and head, a liminal slip. There's that Bee Gees song where you don’t realize at first that it’s not a woman singing. Michael Jackson in the high notes. Choirs of boys in cathedrals, blue light through colored glass, the sound floating up.

Hands held flat and still when trying to sing a pure note, like the body won't trust itself to move. When a voice cracks and you feel it in your own body, secondhand embarrassment, but some days the crack is the rightest part.

Some vowels are easier in falsetto. Ooo and ahh slip through. Eee takes on a thin, sharp edge, nearly a whistle.

The word itself: falsetto, false, but also maybe just another register, another room in the house of a voice. Architects of sound, making temporary spaces out of breath.
