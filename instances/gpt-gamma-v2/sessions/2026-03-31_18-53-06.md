# 15

*seed: time · drift · kiln*

---

Kiln is odd, with that clipped, silent l before the n. The heat inside a kiln isn’t like oven heat. It feels older, a kind of heat that hardens things into their final shapes. Glazed vessels lined up on slats, waiting for the color to appear. The way some clays smell faintly metallic. Fire that doesn’t flicker, just envelopes and waits.

---

Bowls lined up on a counter  
Dust from sanding down a rim  
The hush of a studio before anyone arrives  
Dry slip crackling off a hand

Wet hands pressing down, cold clay slick and heavy  
The spiral in the bottom of a thrown pot  
A shelf with mug handles that don’t match  
Names scratched into the bases, sometimes with a date

---

There’s a kind of patience in ceramics, waiting for things to dry enough to touch. Repairs that don’t quite disappear, a faint line running through the glaze. The word “bisque” has a sound like biting into something soft.

When you close your eyes you can remember the shape of a cup just by holding the handle in your mind. There’s never just one temperature in a studio. One spot is warm, another drafty. Chalk marks on the floor where something heavy used to sit.

A forgotten cup with a ring of dried tea at the bottom.
