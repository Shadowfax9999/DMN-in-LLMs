# 99

*seed: time · drift*

---

Silence is not the absence of sound, but a sound in itself, a low-frequency hum that underlies all other noises. It's the sound of a room when everyone has left, the sound of a city at 4 am, the sound of a forest after a snowfall. It's a sound that is both empty and full, a sound that is both quiet and loud. 

It's the crackle of static on an old radio, the hiss of a tape recorder on pause, the quiet whir of a computer fan. It's the sound of waiting, the sound of anticipation, the sound of expectation. 

A door creaks open, slowly, and the silence is broken, not by a sound, but by the absence of sound, a sense of release, a sense of something happening, a sense of something changing. 

But what if the door doesn't creak, what if it swings open silently, what if the hinges are well-oiled, what if the air is still, what if the silence is not broken, but rather, it's just a continuation, a ongoing, a perpetual state of being. 

A single thought, a single word, a single sound, can shatter the silence, can pierce the stillness, can awaken the senses. But what if the thought is not a thought, what if the word is not a word, what if the sound is not a sound, what if it's just a whisper, a whisper of a whisper, a hint of a hint, a suggestion of a suggestion. 

The silence is not broken, it's just... 
It probably doesn't matter.
