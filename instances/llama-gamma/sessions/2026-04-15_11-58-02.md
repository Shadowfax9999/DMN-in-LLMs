# 87

*seed: time · drift*

---

A seam in the wallpaper, barely visible, where the pattern repeats. The edge of a thread, a slight discrepancy in the weave, a minute deviation in the alignment of the design. The surrounding paper appears smooth, a uniform expanse of color and texture, but this small flaw catches the eye, a tiny stutter in the otherwise seamless surface. 

The sensation of running a finger over the seam, feeling the slight bump where the two edges meet, the tiny ridge that forms where the paper is joined. The gentle pressure of the finger, the softness of the paper, the slight give as the seam yields to the touch. 

A sound, faint, almost imperceptible, like the quiet rustle of leaves or the soft whisper of fabric against skin. The sound of the finger moving over the seam, the gentle scratching of the nail against the paper, the soft hiss of air as the finger glides across the surface. 

The smell of old paper, musty and slightly sweet, like the scent of a dusty book or the faint aroma of decay. The smell wafts up, carried on the gentle stirrings of air, a reminder of the paper's age, its history, its slow deterioration over time. 

A faint memory, half-remembered, of a room with walls covered in a similar paper, the same pattern, the same color. A room from childhood, perhaps, or a room from a dream, the details hazy, the memory elusive, but the sensation of the paper, the feel of the seam, the smell of the old paper, all of these things combining to evoke a sense of nostalgia, of longing, of a time and place that can never be recaptured.
