# 55

*seed: time · drift · migration*

---

Migration. The word itself has a restless quality to it, a sense of movement and displacement. It brings to mind the idea of travel, of journeying from one place to another, often in search of something better or more suitable. The image of birds flocking together, flying in formation, their V-shaped patterns etched against the sky. The notion of instinct guiding them, an innate sense of direction that propels them forward. The concept of habitat, of environments that support or hinder the survival of certain species. The memory of a documentary about monarch butterflies, their incredible journey from Canada to Mexico, thousands of miles each year, a testament to their resilience and determination. The word "path" emerges, a corridor of possibility, a route that winds and turns, leading to unknown destinations. The thought of a river, its flow constant, yet ever-changing, a symbol of transformation and flux. The sensation of being in motion, of moving through space, with the wind rushing past, and the sun shining down. 

Patterns of movement, shapes that emerge from the chaos of migration, a dance of life and death, of growth and decay. The sound of wings beating, a rustling of leaves, a chirping of birds, a cacophony of noise that fills the air. The smell of damp earth, of wet feathers, of the sweet scent of blooming flowers. The feeling of being part of something larger, a collective movement, a shared experience that transcends individual boundaries. The idea of home, of a place to return to, a refuge from the uncertainty and danger of the outside world. The image of a nest, a sanctuary, a haven of safety and comfort. The word "rest" arrives, a state of stillness, a moment of quiet, a pause in the journey.
