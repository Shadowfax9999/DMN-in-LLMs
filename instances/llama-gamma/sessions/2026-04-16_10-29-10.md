*seed: time · drift · cuticle*

---

The dryness of a cuticle, the way it cracks and splits, a tiny landscape of fissures and ridges. The smell of nail polish remover, acetone, a sharp sting that makes the eyes water. A memory of sitting in a small salon, the sound of nail clippers snipping, the feel of a stranger's hands touching mine, the gentle scrape of a cuticle pusher. The sensation of pushing back a cuticle, the slight pain, the feeling of exposure, the tender skin beneath. A fascination with the way cuticles grow, the slow, almost imperceptible pace, the way they can become rough and overgrown, like a tiny, neglected garden. The feeling of applying cuticle oil, the smoothness, the scent, the way it absorbs into the skin, leaving it soft and pliable. 

A fragment: nail polish colors lined up on a shelf
A fragment: the sound of a nail file rasping against a nail
A fragment: the feel of a warm towel wrapped around fingers
