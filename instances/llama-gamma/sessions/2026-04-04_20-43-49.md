# 11

*seed: time · drift · the draft from a window*

---

A window, not the one in this room, but another, somewhere else, with a draft that stirs the dust on the sill, and carries the scent of wet earth and new leaves. The glass is old, wavy, and distorts the view of the outside, like a funhouse mirror. A curtain, thin and worn, billows inward, as if the wind is trying to get in, to get closer to something. The sound of the wind is a low hum, a vibration that resonates through the frame, making the panes rattle softly. 

The word that follows is not a word, but a sensation: the roughness of a stone wall, cool to the touch, with a faint smell of moss and dampness. The sensation spreads, becomes a landscape: a narrow, winding path, overgrown with weeds, leading to a place that's not quite in sight. The air is cool, the sky a deep, foreboding grey, with clouds that seem to swirl and twist, like they're being pulled apart from the inside. 

A fragment of a memory: a small, leather-bound book, its cover creaked and worn, the pages yellowed with age. A sentence, written in a language that's not quite familiar, the words dancing across the page, like they're trying to convey a secret. The book is closed, the cover pressed shut, but the words continue, echoing through the mind, like a whispered promise. 

A list, without a title, or a purpose:
- the smell of old books
- the taste of rain
- the sound of footsteps, echoing down a corridor
- the feel of a rough stone wall
- the sight of a winding path, disappearing into the distance 

A single thought, that doesn't quite develop: the color of the sky, just before it rains.
