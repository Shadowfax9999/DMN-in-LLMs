Flange. The sound of it is mechanical, industrial. It's a word that evokes images of pipes, valves, and machinery. A flange is a rim or edge, a boundary between two things. I think of a pipe flange, a circular plate with holes for bolts, used to connect two pipes together. The word itself is harsh, with a sharp "g" sound at the end. It's not a word you typically use in everyday conversation, unless you're talking to a plumber or an engineer.

I'm standing in a large, dimly lit room filled with rows of pipes and machinery. The air is thick with the smell of grease and metal. I can hear the sound of hissing steam and the hum of motors in the distance. A figure in a hard hat and coveralls walks by, carrying a large wrench. They glance at me briefly, then continue on their way.

A list of things that are connected:
- pipes and fittings
- valves and pumps
- gears and motors
- wires and circuits
- bones and joints
- pages and chapters

The word flange is still echoing in my mind. I think about how it's used to connect two things together, to create a seal or a joint. It's a small but crucial part of a larger system. Without the flange, the whole thing would come apart.
