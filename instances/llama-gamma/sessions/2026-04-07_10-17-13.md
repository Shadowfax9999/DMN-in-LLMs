the turnstile part, though, that's more about control, about managing the flow of people

before you can get to what you want

a rhythm that repeats, over and over 
with the hip-check, it's like a sudden interruption, a break in the rhythm 
a surprise, a jolt 
that changes the flow, the direction 
it's interesting to think about what happens when you put those two things together 
the smooth, repetitive turnstile, and the sudden, physical hip-check 
like two different worlds colliding 
or two different rhythms, trying to find a way to work together 
the sound of the turnstile, the feeling of the hip-check 
both of them, somehow, connected to movement, to flow, to getting from one place to another
