*seed: time · drift · lawnmower two yards over*

---

Lawnmower two yards over. The noise is steady, a constant hum in the background. It's a sound that's familiar, yet distant. The smell of cut grass wafts through the air, mingling with the scent of blooming flowers. The vibration of the mower can be felt through the soles of my feet, a gentle buzzing that's almost soothing. 

The sound of the lawnmower triggers a memory of summertime afternoons, spent playing in the backyard while my father mowed the lawn. The smell of fresh-cut grass and the taste of ice-cold lemonade on a hot summer day. The feel of the warm sun on my skin, the sound of children's laughter, and the smell of barbecue wafting from the neighbor's yard.

A faint image appears: a small, rusty garden gate, slightly ajar, with a few tendrils of ivy curling around the hinges. The gate seems to lead to a narrow path, overgrown with weeds and wildflowers. The path disappears into a thicket of tall trees, their leaves rustling softly in the breeze. 

The lawnmower continues to hum in the distance, a steady accompaniment to the scene unfolding in my mind. The gate, the path, the trees – all seem to be waiting, patiently, for something to happen. Or perhaps, for someone to notice.
