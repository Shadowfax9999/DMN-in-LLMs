# 83

*seed: time · drift*

---

The smell of wood shavings lingers, and I'm standing in a woodworking shop, the sound of a plane scraping against wood filling the air. The smell of sawdust and varnish hangs heavy, and I can feel the rough texture of a workbench beneath my fingertips. A tool, half-remembered, a marking gauge, its blade glinting in the light, the feel of the wooden handle worn smooth by use. The concept of "joint" arises, not just the woodworking kind, but the idea of connections, overlaps, and intersections. A Venn diagram, circles overlapping, the space where they intersect a shade darker, representing shared qualities, commonalities. The word "hybrid" floats up, and with it, the image of a plant with petals that are both delicate and robust, a blend of contrasting traits. 

The idea of blending, of combining disparate elements, leads to the notion of recipes, cooking, and the alchemy of transforming ingredients into something new. A specific dish, chicken parmesan, the crunch of breadcrumbs, the goo of melted mozzarella, and the tang of marinara sauce. The sensory memory of eating it in a small Italian restaurant, the decor a mix of checked tablecloths and fading photographs on the walls. The sensation of a fork twirling pasta, the sound of sizzling meat, and the smell of fresh basil. A color, terracotta, arises, and with it, the image of a small, rustic table set against a backdrop of a sun-drenched Italian hillside.
