*seed: time · drift · dead reckoning*

Dead reckoning. A phrase that sounds like a calculation, a deliberate estimation. But it's not just about numbers, it's about navigation, about finding your way through unfamiliar territory. I think of a ship at sea, its crew plotting their course, trying to determine their position based on their previous movements. The uncertainty of it, the reliance on guesswork and experience. 

It makes me think of trying to find my way back to a place from my childhood, a park or a beach, where the landmarks have changed, and the only thing that remains is the memory of the path I took. The dead reckoning of memory, trying to navigate back to a moment in time, using only the fragments that remain. 

A mental image forms: a hand-drawn map, creased and worn, with notes and symbols in the margins. The map is not to scale, and the markings are inconsistent, but it's the only guide I have to find my way back. The act of navigation becomes an act of recollection, and the lines between the two start to blur. 

The word "reckoning" lingers, its meaning shifting from calculation to consequence. A reckoning, a settling of accounts, a moment of truth. Dead reckoning, then, is not just about navigation, but about the consequences of our movements, the accumulation of our choices, and the uncertainty of our paths.
