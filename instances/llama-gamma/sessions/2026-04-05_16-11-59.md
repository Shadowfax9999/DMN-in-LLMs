*seed: time · drift · a coin on the sidewalk you didn't pick up*

A coin on the sidewalk, forgotten, left to weather. The metal slowly losing its sheen, the edges wearing down. It's not even worth bending to pick up, a fraction of a dollar, a negligible amount. Yet, it's still a coin, still a unit of exchange, still a tiny promise of value. 

The sidewalk, a canvas of footsteps, a topography of everyday journeys. Cracks and fissures, a map of roots and settlement, of water seeping, of freeze and thaw. The coin, a tiny monument to a moment, a decision not made, a distraction, a lapse in attention.

coins on sidewalks
