# 93

*seed: time · drift*

---

Silence of a spring night. The word silence is insufficient. It's a physical presence, a thickening of the air that mutes the world. A heaviness that presses against the skin, making the tiny hairs on the arms stand on end. The darkness is almost palpable, a texture that can be felt, like the softness of a well-worn blanket. 

In this darkness, a single sound: the creak of a branch, a low, mournful sigh that seems to come from nowhere and everywhere at the same time. The sound is a thread, thin and fragile, that weaves its way through the stillness, creating a sense of tension, of something waiting to happen. 

A waiting that is not anticipation, but rather a state of being, a suspension of time and movement. The world holds its breath, and in this pause, the possibility of anything, of everything, exists. The darkness is a canvas, waiting for the brushstrokes of sound and movement to bring it to life. 

And then, nothing. The silence deepens, becomes more profound, until it's almost a physical presence, a weight that presses down, making it hard to breathe. The stillness is a reminder that sometimes, the most profound moments are those of quiet, of waiting, of simply being.
