# 81

*seed: time · drift*

---

Silence is not the same as quiet. One is the absence of sound, the other is a quality of stillness. In a silent room, you can still hear the hum of the refrigerator, the creak of the old wooden floorboards, the distant rumble of traffic. But in a quiet room, these sounds are muffled, as if the air itself is absorbing the noise. 

A small bird perched on the edge of a windowsill, its feathers a muted gray, the beak a tiny triangle of yellow. The window frame, old and worn, the paint chipped and fading, the glass panes wavy, like the surface of a pond. The bird's eyes, black and bright, watching something outside, its head cocked to one side, the feathers on its head ruffled by the breeze. 

The smell of old books, musty and dry, like the smell of forgotten memories. A room filled with shelves, the books stacked haphazardly, some lying on their sides, others standing upright, their spines creaking with age. The air thick with dust, the motes dancing in the faint light, like tiny ballerinas. 

A single sentence, unconnected to anything, floats to the surface: The receipt is from October.
