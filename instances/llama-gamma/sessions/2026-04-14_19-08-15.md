Spigot. The sound of it, a harsh "ig" and a soft "ot", like a sudden burst of water, and then a gentle trickle. A faucet, a tap, a valve, all these words swarm around it, but spigot is the one that sticks, with its awkward, ungainly syllables, like a rusty handle that needs to be turned, needs to be coaxed, to release the flow. And the flow itself, a gush, a spurt, a stream, it's hard to predict, hard to control, like a wild thing, untamed, un contained. The word spigot conjures up a sense of makeshift, of temporary, of jury-rigged, like a pipe held together with duct tape and prayers, a fragile, rickety thing that might collapse at any moment, leaving a mess, a flood, a catastrophe. But for now, it holds, the water flows, and the sound of it, that gentle gurgle, that soft whoosh, is like music, a soothing melody that fills the air, and calms the mind.
