A desert, perhaps, where the only sound is the wind rustling through dry underbrush. No trees, no birds, no signs of life. Just the vast expanse of nothingness. 

A fragment of a memory: a childhood birthday party, a piñata that refused to break, the disappointing realization that it was empty, contained nothing. The feeling of emptiness, a hollow sensation in the chest. Zilch, a word that echoes through the mind, a reminder that sometimes, despite our efforts, we find nothing.
