# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

The smell of cut grass and fresh asphalt, a mix of earthy and sharp, like the edge of a just-opened book. A breeze carries the scent of blooming flowers, but it's faint, like a memory that's hard to place. The sound of children's laughter is still there, but it's distant now, muffled by the hum of a lawnmower or a car driving by. The metal rod, the seesaw, the rusty chain – all gone, replaced by the rough texture of tree bark, the coolness of shade on a warm afternoon. A bird perches on a branch, its feathers a deep, iridescent blue, like the sheen on a just-polished shoe. The branch sways gently, a slow dance, and the bird's song is a series of sharp, staccato notes, like the sound of a typewriter keys clacking out a message.
