# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

The smell of wet earth. Dampness seeps into the air, a chill that clings to the skin. A small, unassuming patch of moss on a stone wall, its delicate tendrils creeping outward, quietly claiming territory. The intricate patterns of its growth, a tiny, insistent universe, oblivious to the world beyond its small, damp realm. 

A small, forgotten key, corroded and stiff, buried in a drawer, its purpose lost to memory. The sensation of running a thumb over the key's worn teeth, feeling the ridges and grooves, the slight imperfections that speak of use and abandonment. 

In a neglected corner of a room, a faint, eerie glow emanates from an old, dust-shrouded computer screen, its power button stuck, perpetually casting an otherworldly light on the surrounding dust and forgotten papers. The soft, ethereal radiance illuminates nothing in particular, simply existing, a quiet, eerie heartbeat in the darkness.
