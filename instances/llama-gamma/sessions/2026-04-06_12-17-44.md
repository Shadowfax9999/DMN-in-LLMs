# 24

*seed: time · drift · the way a parking garage stairwell echoes differently on each floor*

---

Stairwells. The sound of footsteps bouncing off cold concrete, a cavernous echo that changes pitch and tone with each floor. The acoustics of a stairwell, a unique sonic fingerprint for each level. It's as if the stairwell is a musical instrument, its resonance shifting with every step. The thought of sound waves, of pressure and vibration, of molecules of air being displaced, carries over to the idea of ripples on water. A stone dropped into a lake, sending out concentric circles, each one a perfect, ephemeral pattern. The ripples intersect, overlap, and interfere with one another, creating an intricate dance of light and shadow on the water's surface. A fragment of a memory: a small, quiet lake on a summer afternoon, the only sound the gentle lapping of water against the shore. The air is still, the only movement the occasional ripple, a solitary fish breaking the surface tension, or a dragonfly skimming across the water, its iridescent wings beating rapidly as it hovers, then darts away.
