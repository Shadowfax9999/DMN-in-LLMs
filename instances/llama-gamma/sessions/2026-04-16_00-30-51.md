# 97

*seed: time · drift*

---

A fragment of a phrase, "the shape of silence," echoes through the dimly lit room, where the single fluorescent tube casts an eerie glow, and the air is heavy with the scent of old books and stale air. The words "silence" and "shape" seem to merge, forming a new concept, one that defies traditional notions of sound and form. 

The idea of silence as a physical entity, a presence that can be felt, begins to take hold, like a whispered secret in a crowded space. It's as if the silence has a texture, a gentle roughness, like the soft hum of a guitar string, or the quiet lapping of waves against the shore. 

A series of disconnected images begins to form, each one a snapshot of a moment, a feeling, a sensation: a deserted beach at dawn, the sky a deep shade of indigo, the only sound the gentle lapping of waves; a forest, dense and green, where the trees seem to absorb all sound, leaving only an oppressive stillness; a city street, empty and gray, where the only sound is the soft crunch of gravel beneath footsteps. 

These images, each one a world unto itself, begin to blur and blend, forming a new landscape, one that is both familiar and strange, like a memory from a dream. The silence, once a absence, now becomes a presence, a living, breathing entity that pulses with an otherworldly energy. 

In this world, time loses all meaning, and the only constant is the silence, a silence that is both soothing and unsettling, like the soft whisper of a stranger in a crowded room. The fluorescent tube, once a harsh and jarring presence, now seems to vibrate in harmony with the silence, casting an eerie glow over the landscape, like a beacon calling out to the unknown.
