# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

What is the sound between two songs on an album, the brief hush where your ear adjusts and almost invents a waiting melody? The humming before a streetlight flicks on, too quiet to name. Places where music used to be: an empty band shell on a Tuesday night, the wood of the stage dulled by dust, a paper cup wedged into the steps. Phone booths still standing but with no phone inside, just the metal bracket and an echo. The line left on your wrist from a cheap hair elastic, skin marked and then forgetting. Street corners where the wind always turns a little sharper, even without buildings to funnel it. Those coins at the bottom of the fountain pressed flat and green, legends faded, wishes stacked. Some doors stick and you can tell which ones by the paint scraped off at hip height, a pale patch like a thumbprint. Frost on a windshield in late spring, pattern traveling along the glass like handwriting in a language no one uses. The smell of rain on concrete before it hits the grass.
