# 7

*seed: time · drift · gyroscope*

---

Gyroscope: spinning, but not wandering. Staying upright when it ought to tip. That humming sound when you wind a toy top and let it loose on the tile. 

The slow drift as it starts to lose its rhythm, tracing circles that grow wider, wobble, then collapse. 

Sometimes the label on a jar is slightly off-center and you keep rotating it to try to find the right place for the front. --- 

Olives taste different when they’re pitted versus unpitted. The tiny hollow left behind, sometimes a piece of pit still lodged and gritty between your teeth. 

Kitchen towel hanging by one corner, always damp at the bottom edge. If you touch it in the morning it’s cold, but in the afternoon it feels softer. 

The hum from the fridge changes pitch in the middle of the night.

A jar lid that won’t come off, rubber grip pulled from a drawer. The shape of imprint it leaves in your palm, faint crosshatch.

Magnet letters sliding down the fridge door, always gathering in a pile at the bottom, scratched and chipped where the paint has worn off.

There’s a drawer in every house that holds at least one thing that doesn’t belong anywhere else.

The clock on the wall is never exactly right.
