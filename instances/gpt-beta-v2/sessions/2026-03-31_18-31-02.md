# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

A lamp switched on in daylight always feels reluctant, the orange glow dimmer against a window’s blue.  

Paper napkins folded in triangles, the crease never perfectly straight, a soft unevenness at the tip.

The word “elastic” snaps back in my mind to gym class, armbands, ponytails, tension carried in circles.

The taste of water after something sweet is almost metallic, as if the tongue is adjusting, stripping the memory away.

A low wall painted once and then repainted, the different layers revealed where shoes scuff at the bottom, cream over beige over gray.

If you press your thumb into a cold pane, the fog of your skin remains just a moment after it lifts.

----

I used to think parsnips were ghost carrots.  

There is a particular silence when two people are both about to speak and then wait, each yielding.

The dust in sunlight looks like it’s moving with intention but it isn’t.  

You can tell the difference between the first drop of rain and the second by the way they land on your wrist.
