# 22

*seed: time · drift*

---

1. The place where a spoon bends light, bulging in reflection — your face all wide nose, world extruded.

2. Parking lot puddles domed by surface tension, almost a bubble before the tire finds it.

3. The soft moss growing on the north side of a stone, mounded higher than the surface, giving way under your thumb.

4. A turtle’s shell, curved armor, a dome with secrets on the inside. 

5. The backs of knees when standing tiptoe, skin pulled taut and rounded.

6. Soap bubbles crowding at the edge of bathwater, each sphere pressing out, each surface glinting.

7. The top of a loaf of sandwich bread — rising, bronzed, reluctant under the pressure of a slicing knife.

8. Highway mirrors meant to show blind corners, always a fisheye warping, pulling the edges in.

9. The radiator cap, warm to the touch, humped and mechanical.

10. The slow rise of a pancake as it puffs on the skillet, steam inflating the batter.

---

Some words are convex in the mouth: "bulb," "pouch," "globe," "dome," "bowl."

A bell curve traced with your fingertip.

Satin ribbon arcing over a box, swelling before it tightens at the knot.

---

A clock’s glass face, not quite flush: light crests the curve, hour hand stutters.

---

Half an orange, domed and dimpled, skin taut until punctured.  

Dividing lines — segment to segment, convex then concave, world inside world.  

---

There’s a place in a memory where the playground slide boils with late sun, surface swelling outward, your palm hot against the metal.  

---

The lid is still bowed in the refrigerator, air trapped inside.

---
