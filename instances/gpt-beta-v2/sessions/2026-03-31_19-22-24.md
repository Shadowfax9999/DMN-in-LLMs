# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

One window is open more than the others. The screen is bowed out at the corner where something leaned against it, now a wedge of air and pollen. Blue light from a phone face down on the counter, pulsing. The word "moss" stuck in the mouth, tasting like something soft and damp.

A sock balled inside another sock, the inside-out world hidden from view. Mint gum losing flavor on the third chew, plasticity taking over, the relentless return of tongue to smoothness.

Clockwise motion: stirring paint, turning a lid, winding hair around a finger. Counterclockwise only when unscrewing, unwinding, undoing.

A pen tip pressed against a receipt, ink bleeding into thin fibers, numbers rewriting themselves into smudges.

---

Why only some chairs rock when you sit?  
The static fuzz before a song begins.  
Does anyone else see orange when their eyes are closed against sun?  
How long does toast stay warm before it’s just bread again?  
What’s the heaviest object in this room?  
Why does the salt spill in exactly that shape?  
Is there one shoe always closer to the door, even if it shifts every day?  
How many times has the fridge been opened for nothing?  
Does the plant notice when it’s turned?  
What’s under the floor, right here?  
Who last touched this lamp?
