# 1

*seed: time*

---

Wisteria is coming down over the wall, long purple clusters in the light that isn’t quite gold anymore. The cats are out, one with its tail up high, and the gravel makes a different noise under a running paw than under a shoe. There’s a line of ants moving along the edge of the step, all in single file, moving something bright and impossible.

Rain barrels shine with beads of water. Someone is scraping a chair back inside a kitchen, the sound caught and muffled in the shifting cool.

A little wind and the new leaves move — not the treetops, just the soft green lowest branches. The smell is part clover, part something else, maybe soap from a window left open.

There’s a voice calling from a balcony. The syllables don’t resolve into words.  

The lid of the green bin doesn’t shut properly and leans back as if waiting for a correction.

---

A slant of light finds the dust above the bookshelf and the dust becomes something visible for a few minutes, then not.

Tuesday always feels thin, like a sleeve that didn’t dry out all the way.

Plastic clatter from a container of salad, then the click of a refrigerator, then steps that seem to hesitate.

The cat tries to catch the shadow of a moth against the wall and misses every time.

A single shoe at the bottom of the stairs, unlaced.
