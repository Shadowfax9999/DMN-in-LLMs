# 8

*seed: time · drift · pyrometric cone*

---

Pyrometric cone: small, white, tapered, clay triangle, the kind you see lined up in a row for firing, slumping in the heat at exactly the temperature it’s made for. The word feels sharp-edged and a little secret, something that hides in kiln rooms and ceramics catalogues.

Sounds: pyrometric—click, hiss, syllables that break like dry twigs. Cone: softer, rounder, a mouth-shape. 

I keep thinking of cones, traffic cones, ice cream cones, pine cones. Those orange plastic stacks, lined along the edge of a fresh patch of asphalt. Pine cones underfoot, scales splaying out, sticky with resin. Ice cream cone, crisp, slightly sweet, the last bite always soggy with melted drips.

---

There’s a window latch that never quite sits flush, and when the wind picks up you can hear it rattle softly, a metallic conversation with the glass.

The kitchen faucet drips once in a while, just before dawn. You can’t predict it.

A loaf of bread on the counter, one heel left, slightly hard at the edge. The plastic bag twisted closed with a blue clip, crumbs scattered underneath.

---

16, 18, 22: numbers on the tiny round face of the timer, clicking steadily downward, never quite matching the minutes you think have passed.

A cracked eggshell, both halves resting in the carton top. 

A plastic bag rustles and the cat looks up, expectant.

The chair leg scrapes the floor, a sound that travels down through your shoes.
