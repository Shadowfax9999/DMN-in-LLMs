# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

A line of ants on the edge of a sidewalk, perfectly straight except where the concrete cracks. The way their movement pulses, quick then paused, like a signal traveling. Dandelions tight against the curb, yellow and low, so bright they seem almost fluorescent in twilight. Someone’s shoelace left in the gutter, looped but not tied, the aglet bent where it’s been stepped on. Streetlights not fully on, just humming with anticipation, blue-white halos barely forming. The scent of hot asphalt cooling, a thickness in the air that holds the memory of daylight. A mailbox with a hinge that squeals even when empty, paint peeling in wide strips that curl like sunburnt skin. There are cigarette butts pressed into the cracks, the paper split revealing faded yellow filters. Grates sunk deep in the curb, bars collecting debris, the hollow below echoing when you walk by. The breeze picks up and dust skitters across the road, swirling around parked tires, caught and released.
