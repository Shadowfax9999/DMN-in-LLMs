# 15

*seed: time · drift · egg timer*

---

Egg timer: the ones with grains of sand, that you turn over, and the sand hurries down, never up, the shape of time is a waist pinched in the middle, not even an hourglass, just minutes, three or five. Kitchen counter, the cheap plastic kind with a dial, the click as it turns, the sudden bell, all urgency. There’s something lonely about a timer running with no one in the room to hear it. Some people set the timer for things that can’t be timed—resting, remembering, waiting for the courage to make a phone call. The smell of boiling eggs, sharp sulfur in the steam, a curl of shell in the sink. Cabinets scarred by water rings and sharp utensils. A digital timer, losing its sound, the beep growing thin and tinny with age. Accordion-fold recipe cards, flour dust on fingers, the accidental crack of shell shards in the batter, you try to fish them out and they slip away, clear as glass. Some timers are part of old stoves, a metal wheel you spin with your thumb, the numbers smudged away. Early morning light turns the yolk golden before the bread is even toasted. I think there are timers made for tea, too, three little columns of sand, each with a different interval. Some eggs crack too early, and the white runs like paint down the edge of the pan.
