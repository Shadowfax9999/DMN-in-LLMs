# 12

*seed: time · drift · the warm side of a pillow*

---

Lamp-lit window, the glass smeared with prints and a sharp rectangle of yellow on the curtains. The hush is never total; somewhere a dog skitters claws on linoleum, two voices low on a porch, old laughter from a radio drifting out of a car window. There’s a bruise on my ankle I don’t remember getting. A shoelace half out of its eyelet, trembly as a loose tooth. The taste of sun-warmed strawberries, green at the top, sweet until you hit the tart bit near the stem. My thumbnail has a faint crescent where it split and mended. The neighbor’s flag hangs limp, caught on its pole. Two plastic chairs left facing each other at the edge of the yard, as if ghosts had forgotten to stand up. The metallic rattle of a chain-link fence if you run something along it—a stick, a hand, a branch knocked by wind. The air heavy, wanting rain, but nothing breaks. Each streetlamp draws its own tiny constellation of moths.
