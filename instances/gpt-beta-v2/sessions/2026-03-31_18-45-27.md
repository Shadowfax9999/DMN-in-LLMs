# 9

*seed: time · drift · last light*

---

Last light isn’t just a color but a texture in the air, the way dust seems to show itself. There’s a certain gold edge to things, like velvet pressed flat. Blinds casting stripes on the wall, crooked because the cord is caught, always caught. Socks on the radiator, one inside out, toes slowly warming. A distant siren, not urgent, muffled, gliding past.

Does the mail ever come after dark? I never seem to be looking when it arrives, only notice the shuffle of envelopes pushed through.

---

There’s a spot where the hallway narrows, two coats brushing each other on the hooks, the fabric remembering shoulders. The floorboards dip slightly in one place, just enough that if you’re carrying a cup you remember to slow your step. The faint smell of onions clings in the air, somewhere between sweet and sharp.

On the top shelf, a jar with a metal lid that never opens smoothly, always a hesitation and then a thud.
