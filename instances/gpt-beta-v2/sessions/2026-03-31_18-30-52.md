# 3

*seed: time · drift · wrong-name response*

---

Someone calls you “Ellen” and you’re not Ellen and you answer anyway, only realizing it as your own voice says “yeah?” and you feel the outline of a different person briefly, like you’ve borrowed their shadow.

Faded stickers on a grocery store scale, someone’s initials scratched in with a key, prices in marker that never fade no matter how much the plastic is wiped.

The long beep of a microwave, echoing more in an empty house than a crowded one.

---

Clouds stacked up flat along the horizon, like someone pressed pause before they could billow upward.

There’s a particular squeak when rubber soles drag lightly on polished tile, not the squeal of sneakers in a gym, but a shushing, backing-up sound in a hallway.

Dust in the corners gathers tight, as if pulled by something, even when there’s no airflow you can feel directly.

---

If you wear someone else’s jacket, the sleeves hang differently, the smell in the lining is sharp, unfamiliar, your own arms seem like they belong to a taller, quieter person.

The first two notes of a phone alert: when you recognize it isn’t yours and your body sits up anyway.
