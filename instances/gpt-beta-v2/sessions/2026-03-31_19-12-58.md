# 20

*seed: time · drift · the sound of a basketball on asphalt vs concrete*

---

The basketball is louder on concrete, sharper, an echo that snaps back up to your ear. On asphalt it’s duller, thuddier, a kind of fuzzy rebound. You know what’s under your feet by the sound, even before you look down. The lines are different, too—parking lot white, sometimes yellow, sprayed over cracks that branch in slow motion. Asphalt gives a little. You can almost feel the ball absorb and return the energy in a way concrete refuses, always taking a tiny fraction away. Dribbling in the evening, the sound changes as the light fades, maybe not really, but the mind hears it differently. There’s the accidental music of a game: bounce, squeak, slap, the soft argument of voices. On grass, the ball goes mute, stunned, a failed experiment. I think the rim is crooked. The net is frayed and the chain links catch on themselves, so a made shot sometimes hangs a moment before dropping through. Shirts and skins is a phrase entirely disconnected from the actual shirts or skin—just code for teams, for division. The ball rolls under a car and you have to crawl for it, knees on warm blacktop, hands gritty, not caring until later when you feel it still.
