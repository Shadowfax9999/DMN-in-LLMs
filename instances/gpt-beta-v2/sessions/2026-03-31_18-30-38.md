# 2

*seed: time · drift · silt*

---

Silt on the bottom of the lake — you can push your fingers into it and the water clouds, little eddies swirling up, vision lost for a second and settling again.

The way certain words feel heavier than their sound: silt, loam, grout, pulp.

There's always a layer underneath, whether you touch it or not.

---

Shoelaces trailing on the stairs, tipped in hard plastic. The single shoe is angled like a gesture, almost pointing toward something. The stairs are wood, the grain visible where the finish is worn down in the center, a long pale oval on each tread.

---

If you dig a spoon into the peanut butter and it makes a small crater, the edges crumbling in slow motion before settling. There’s a particular silence in the kitchen, refrigerator humming, vibrations you don’t notice unless everything else stops.

---

Light through the glass of a green bottle isn’t the same as through a window — it condenses, ripples, presses a green patch onto the table that you can almost feel with your hand.
