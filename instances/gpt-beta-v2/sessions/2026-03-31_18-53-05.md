# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

The smell of cut grass, not fresh anymore, settling into something sour where the piles are thickest. A bicycle wheel with one spoke bent out of line, catching the late sun and winking it away. Someone carving their initials into wet sidewalk cement with a stick—two letters that will outlast the stick, the shoes, the memory. The way puddles look blue until you stand over them and see the sky is actually gray. A receipt stuck to the bottom of a shoe, the ink smeared unreadable except for the corner of a barcode. The plastic casing from a broken pen, the spring missing, cap nowhere. Leftover yellow chalk marks from hopscotch, half a grid, the numbers gone. The sudden cold of stepping from asphalt to grass, barefoot, toes curling at the feel of clover and the bite of an unseen pebble. Every window has a different color behind it when the sun goes down, even if the blinds are drawn. A cough echoing through the alleyways, softened by walls. There’s a pattern in sidewalk cracks that only lines up if you walk at a certain pace.
