# 6

*seed: time · drift · the inside of a glove*

---

The inside of a glove is always warmer than you expect, not quite damp but remembering the hand that was there. Cotton pills, small ridges along the seams. Sometimes a stray thread, curled, white. Leather gloves smell different than knit ones. The shape holds; fingers keep their memory, slightly bent, cramped at the tips.

---

Something slightly sticky about old rubber bands, left in a drawer among twist ties and loose screws. The way they snap when overstretched, a sudden dusty scent of latex. The color fades: pink turning yellow, tan turning brittle.

---

Stacks of magazines on the bottom shelf. Covers curling upward. Ads for things that don’t exist anymore: shampoo in bottles shaped like teardrops, cereal boxes showing a child with giant glasses. The pages heavier than they look.

The feeling you get when you try on a shirt that used to fit.

---

Sound of plastic lids nesting inside each other, a clatter that’s duller than metal but sharper than wood. Sometimes one gets stuck and makes a pop when you pull it loose. The inside has a ring of condensation.

---

Grasshoppers sometimes get inside through the screen, and for a while you don’t see them, then suddenly there’s a faint clicking from behind the curtain.

---

There’s a particular light that comes just before the porch bulb clicks on — not dusk, not evening, just a smudged in-between. The plastic chair is still cold.
