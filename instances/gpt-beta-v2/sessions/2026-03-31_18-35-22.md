# 5

*seed: time · drift · blue hour*

---

Blue hour is not really blue except in photographs — in reality it’s grayish, a kind of hush that isn’t a color but a drifting weight. Something in the air flattens sound: after dinner, plates scraped, vertebrae shifting against fabric, light pools but never deepens.

The porch steps are cold at this time, and the air is gentle against the skin, almost apologetic. Someone practicing trumpet inside, notes fat and uncertain, the window open just enough for breath to slip out.

Plastic chairs: one has a crack in the seat, a place where shorts catch. The grass underneath is patchy, tougher stalks like small flags. I keep thinking there should be more moths.

There’s that scent — leftover sun on cement, a sweetness that’s nearly burnt. The last bird makes a noise like a squeaky hinge. I wonder if the mail has come.

Sometimes, on evenings like these, the sky looks heavy but nothing happens.
