# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are boxes that are hard to open, the lid creaking and then popping up too fast, like a jack-in-the-box without the clown. The corners of cardboard, softened by handling. A piece of tape holding one edge, peeling and then snapping with a dry sound.

A pattern on the linoleum, hexagons tessellating, each tile a separate plateau edged in gray. The grout collects dust, darker in the creases, like contour lines on a map pressed flat.

A window left open all day, the screen slightly bulging where the cat leans her head, nose pressed into the wire mesh, eyes squeezed to slits. The smell of cut grass pushing in, sharp and green.

---

Questions:

How is it possible for a chair to become uncomfortable only after you notice it?

Why do spoons look so different in water?

What is the sound when the lid of a jar comes unstuck and the seal breaks?

Is there always a pause between noticing something strange and saying it aloud, or does it happen at the same time?

What color is the back of an eyelid when the sun is out?
