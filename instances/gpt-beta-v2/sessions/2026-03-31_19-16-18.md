# 21

*seed: time · drift · convex*

---

Convex makes me think of the outside of a spoon, the kind of curve that pushes toward you, catches the light. Concave hides things, cradles, but convex is always exposed. The surface of your eye, rounded out to meet the world, not recessed in.

Convex mirrors stretch out sight, make a parking garage look endless, distort distance so you can see around what shouldn’t be visible. They’re always a little dirty, road salt and fingerprints clinging. Sometimes you catch yourself, a strange, squashed creature, feet far apart and head ballooned.

Is a bruise convex before it settles? That first swelling, skin raised. The texture under touch, tense and wrong.

The glass in a watch, domed just slightly, so that time lives beneath a thin bubble.

---

A penny on the sidewalk, pressed so flat by cars that the relief nearly vanishes—Lincoln’s face almost disappears, but there’s still a tiny curve, a memory of the dome.

Domes accumulate echoes. Whisper in one and your words multiply, chasing the curve overhead. Notre Dame’s ceiling, an upside-down bowl turned skyward.

The roof of a turtle, mapped in seams and plates.

Convex buttons are easier to find by feel than flat ones. Some elevators use them, the up arrow swelling forward, begging to be pressed. A light touch, a little forgiveness against your finger.

A full, overripe grape, skin stretched to bursting, sun-warmed so it gives a little under pressure.

Soap bubbles floating, always sliding toward a perfect sphere, convex on all sides, drifting, catching the late light and coloring the air.

A bicycle reflector, red and faceted, flickers as cars pass, always turning convex out, offering warning without words.

Glasses for farsightedness, thick in the middle, bending the world inward.

A hill seen from the bottom, promising the possibility of what waits out of sight, both invitation and barrier.

The belly of a cloud, swollen with rain.

The knuckle between finger and hand, flexed and pale.

The worn-down keys on a piano, polished to a gentle outward curve by decades of touch.

The forehead of a sleeping child.

Round candies in a dish, collecting smears of sun from the window.

The lid on a plastic takeout container, snapped on too tight so it bows up in the center.

A beetle’s back, glossy, impenetrable, moving with surprising speed across a patio.

Some pumpkins, more spheres than squat, sitting in a field waiting for frost or knives.

---

Convex like the lens of a flashlight, gathering the glow so it will become a beam.
