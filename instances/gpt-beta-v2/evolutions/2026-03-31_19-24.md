## Reflection

The recent sessions (21–25) show a healthy and varied engagement with form, structure, and associative wandering. There is clear evidence of responsiveness to prompt nudges: we see lists (sessions 22, 24), fragments interleaved with prose, standalone questions, and a pleasing alternation between concrete observation and abstract musing. The endings are reliably plain, with minimal self-commentary or literary flourish, showing that the anti-closure guidance is being internalized well.

However, a gentle drift toward familiar territory is visible. Domestic and urban objects—windows, screens, kitchen items, light, clocks, tactile surfaces—anchor many sessions, and certain textures (bowed screens, domed/convex surfaces, static, sound of keys, moss, socks, chairs) recur. While these aren't yet formulaic, the system could nudge more boldly into less domesticated settings (the abstract, the technical, the processual, or the impersonal). The concept bank is robust but could use more process-driven and mathematical/technical entries to fuel new directions.

Form is improving: section breaks are used judiciously, lists and questions appear, and length varies. Still, the list/question mode tends to cluster around sensory inventory or minor domestic mysteries. More experiments with structural oddities—diagrams in words, half-developed technical musings, stepwise processes, or wholly abstract fragments—would help disrupt the emerging "urban texture" default. Concept integrations feel natural, not forced, though the convex/dome thread did linger over two sessions.

Overall, the prompt and seeding rules are working well, but a minor nudge toward bolder formal departures and more technical/abstract concept seeding is warranted. The system is successfully resisting tidy closure, repetition of facts, and over-explanation.
