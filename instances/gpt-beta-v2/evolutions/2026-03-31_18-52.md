## Reflection

The recent sessions (6–10) display a healthy wandering across sensory, domestic, and tactile territory, with strong associative moves (gloves → rubber bands → magazines; coins → laundromat). The writing is consistently vivid and natural, with new micro-observations (the color of old rubber bands, the snap of a stuck plastic lid, the particular sound of coins) and frequent, but not overbearing, use of the concept bank. There’s a clear improvement in resisting explanation and provenance moves—most observations are simply noticed or described rather than explained or traced to their origins. This is a positive development and shows the system prompt’s influence is finally sticking. 

However, the form has reconverged on short, paragraph-length vignettes, separated by section breaks. The structure is now almost always: a set of 3–5 paragraphs, each a cluster of linked images or sensations, with a hard break (---) marking a shift. There are very few lists, fragments, or truly abrupt single-thought sessions. Questions are rare, and the system almost never experiments with ultra-short or ultra-long forms. Thematic breadth is good, but depth is sometimes lost—the system often moves on from a topic just as it gets interesting, skimming the surface rather than sinking in. Endings are plain and mid-thought, which is an improvement.

Concept integration is generally smooth, with seed concepts (e.g., pyrometric cone, gyroscope) sparking real associative threads rather than feeling bolted on. There’s still a slight default to domestic and familiar settings (kitchens, drawers, laundry, coins), but this seems more a function of the concept bank than a system-level rut. The prompt’s recent additions (on form, explanation avoidance, and ending moves) are working, but the repeated section-break structure suggests the structure enforcement/nudge rules may need tuning.

Overall, the DMN is in a strong phase of natural, evocative wandering with varied content and improved prompt compliance. The next evolutionary nudge should focus on disrupting the default vignette/section-break structure and inviting experiments with form and continuity—lists, questions, single-thread or single-image sessions, and occasional deeper dives.
