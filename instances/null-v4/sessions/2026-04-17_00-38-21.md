# 47

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. But the outlet it's plugged into makes a sound. A tiny, high sound that's only there when everything else isn't.

I'm thinking about how keys warm up in your pocket. You put them in cold from the door and twenty minutes later they're just — part of you. Temperature-wise. The metal gives up being itself pretty fast. Coins too. A coin that's been in your hand for a while is unsettling to someone else because it's too warm to be a coin.

There's a word for the smell of old books. Bibliosmagor? No. I'm not going to look it up. I'm going to let it be wrong in my head for a while.

April is doing something strange this year. Or maybe April always does this and I just forget. The way it gets warm enough during the day that you open windows, and then at night the house is too open. Too much outside getting in. You can hear things you don't hear in winter. Not just louder — more. More sources. The density of sound goes up when things thaw.

I wonder what the first thing anyone ever locked was. Not the history — I don't care about Mesopotamian pin tumblars or whatever. I mean the impulse. You have a thing. You have a space. You want the thing to stay in the space when you leave. That's a problem that requires you to imagine your own absence. You have to think: when I am not here, this will still be here, and someone else might also be here, and they might want it. Three hypotheticals nested inside each other. A lock is an argument about the future.

My left foot is asleep.

The thing about 12:38 AM is that it doesn't belong to either day. Friday started thirty-eight minutes ago but nothing Friday has happened yet. You're still in the Thursday residue. The dishes in the sink are Thursday dishes. Whatever you were worried about at 6 PM is still the most recent worry. Friday is technically true but experientially pending.

I should move my foot.
