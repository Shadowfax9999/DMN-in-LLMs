*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house got wider.

There's a particular kind of quiet that only happens when a machine stops — not silence arriving but silence being *uncovered*, like it was there the whole time underneath. The fridge compressor masks a lot. Crickets. Something ticking in the wall, probably thermal contraction, the house cooling unevenly.

I keep almost thinking about ceilings. Not any specific ceiling. Just — the fact of them. How rarely you look up in a room you know well. You could live somewhere for years and not be able to describe the ceiling of your own bedroom. Whether there's a texture. Which direction the lines run if there are lines.

Airplane cabins have false ceilings with almost no clearance above them. Inches. Wiring and air ducts and then the skin of the fuselage. The whole interior is a shell inside a shell. I sat in a window seat once where the plastic wall panel had come slightly loose and you could feel cold air through the gap — actual outside cold, stratospheric cold, just behind that clicking plastic. It wasn't frightening. It was interesting in a way I didn't do anything with.

Hmm. The word *panel*. A panel of judges. A panel of fabric. A panel of a comic strip. A panel in a wall. These don't seem related but they all have the sense of a bounded flat section that's part of a larger bounded flat thing. A piece that knows it's a piece.

My left foot is asleep. That pins-and-needles feeling is nerve signals resuming, apparently, but it doesn't feel like resumption. It feels like interference. Like a radio finding a frequency.

The crickets have a pulse to them. Not synchronized — that's a different species, the snowy tree cricket, the one you can derive temperature from — these are just overlapping at whatever rate each individual arrives at. But the effect from a distance is a texture that breathes. Gets louder, softer, louder. Emergent rhythm from no rhythm.

I wonder if there's a word for a pattern that only exists at a distance. That falls apart when you try to hear any single part of it.

Probably. Probably in German.

The fridge just kicked back on.
