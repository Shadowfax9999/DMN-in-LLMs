The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything — the house being a shape that air moves through. There's a difference.

Something about springs in a mattress. Not the season. The coils. How they're each independent but they share the load through the fabric that connects their tops. A pocket coil mattress is a democracy of small resistances. That's — no, I don't need to make it into anything. They're springs. They push back proportional to how much you push them. Some of them have never been fully compressed. The ones at the corners. A whole working life of partial flexion.

I keep almost thinking about frequencies. The way a room has a resonant frequency and you never know it until someone plays a cello in it and one note makes the windows vibrate. Every room is waiting for its note. Most rooms never hear it.

Tile grout gets dark from the top down. The surface oxidizes or collects or — I actually don't know why grout darkens. I think I've always assumed I knew.

The word "friable." Something that crumbles easily. Chalk is friable. Old grout is friable. It sounds like it should mean something joyful. Fri- like Friday. But it's from Latin *friare*, to crumble. Nothing joyful about it.

There's a particular kind of quiet at 12:35 AM that's different from 3 AM quiet. This one still has the residue of the day in it. Someone three houses over closed a car door maybe twenty minutes ago and that's still the most recent outdoor sound. By 3 AM the quiet has settled into itself and isn't referencing anything.

My left knee does a thing when I've been sitting too long where the first three steps are a negotiation. Not pain exactly. More like the joint re-proposing its terms.

Somewhere in this city there is a traffic light cycling through its colors for nobody. Green yellow red green yellow red. The sensors might know no one's there. It doesn't matter. The pattern continues. I don't have a thought about that. It's just happening right now, concurrently with this.
