# 12

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now the house is doing that thing where you hear how loud the silence is. Not silence. The other sounds that were always there underneath. Something ticking — not a clock, something thermal, a pipe or a joint in the wall expanding or contracting, I never remember which way it goes at night. Cooling, probably. The house shrinking back to its resting size.

My left foot is asleep. Not the whole foot. The outside edge, the part that was pressed against the chair leg. It's fizzing. There's no good word for what a foot waking up feels like. "Pins and needles" is wrong — pins and needles are sharp and this is soft, carbonated almost. Like the foot is a glass of something.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a specific shade of green. You can think "green" and something shows up but it's not a particular green, it's green in general, which doesn't exist anywhere outside your head. Every green you've ever actually seen was a specific green. The general one is a thing your mind made up to hold all the others.

But you can hear a specific note. If someone plays a C on a piano you can replay it pretty accurately. So why can the ear remember specifics and the eye only remembers categories. Or maybe that's not true either. Maybe the C I'm imagining is also a general C, a C-flavored placeholder, and I just can't tell because I don't have another C to compare it to right now.

The ticking stopped. Or I stopped noticing it.

There's a dog somewhere — not close. Two streets over maybe. Just one bark, then nothing. Like it had one thing to say.

I should go to bed. That's not a thought, it's more like a weather pattern. The tiredness isn't in any one place. It's a change in how fast thoughts connect. Slower. The gaps between them wider. The gaps are actually comfortable. I don't need to fill them.

The foot is back.
