# 37

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house exactly. The absence the fridge was covering. There's a creak somewhere that isn't settling — it's too regular. Every eight or nine seconds. Could be thermal. The roof contracting. Metal flashing shrinking by fractions of a millimeter as the temperature drops and that's enough to make a sound.

I don't actually know if that's what it is.

There's a particular kind of quiet at this hour where you become aware of your own swallowing. The mechanical fact of it. Saliva collects whether you want it to or not and then the throat does its thing and you hear it from the inside, which is a different sound than anyone else would hear if they were close enough, which they wouldn't be, because it's 12:34 in the morning and you're the only one up.

Clocks. The way we say 12:34 like it means something. Like the sequence is lucky or notable. But 12:33 had to happen first and nobody remarks on 12:33. The aesthetics of decimal notation. If we used base-12, different moments would feel neat.

A dog is barking somewhere but it's so far away it might be two neighborhoods over. Sound carries differently in cold air — no. I was going to explain why. I don't need to know why. It just carries. The bark arrives thin and flat, like a piece of paper slid under a door.

I'm thinking about paper under doors. Hotel rooms. The bill they used to slide under your door at 4 AM, the little rustling sound that you'd half-hear in sleep and incorporate into whatever dream was happening. Do hotels still do that? Everything's email now. One less mysterious sound in the world.

My left foot is cold. Just the left one. It's sticking out from under the blanket, apparently. The body makes these decisions — which limb to sacrifice to thermoregulation, which to keep. I didn't choose to put my left foot out. It just migrated there.

The creak again. I've been counting without deciding to count. Seven seconds that time.

There's a moth on the window screen. I can't see it but I can hear the papery tap when it adjusts itself. It's not trying to get to the light because there's no light on in here. Maybe it landed and just... stayed. Maybe moths rest. They must rest. Everything rests.

I should be resting.

The fridge kicks back on.
