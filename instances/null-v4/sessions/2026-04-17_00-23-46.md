# 3

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house got bigger.

That's not a metaphor. The dimensions of the room actually change when the compressor stops. All that low hum was filling the corners, pressing against the walls with something, and now — more room. The silence has square footage.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a familiar face on command. You can recognize it instantly in a crowd of thousands but you can't build it in your mind. The eyes won't stay with the mouth. The nose drifts. It's like the face knows you're looking and disassembles itself. But then you'll be doing something else entirely — folding a towel, waiting for water to get hot — and there it is, whole, unbidden, gone before you can turn toward it.

What does a towel look like when nobody's folded it yet. Not a crumpled towel. A towel that's just been manufactured. It comes off a loom flat, I think. Or maybe not a loom — towels have loops, terry cloth, there's a specific process where the warp threads are kept slack so they pile up into those little loops. Someone had to figure that out the first time. Someone was trying to make flat cloth and it went wrong and the surface got bumpy and they touched it and thought — oh, this holds water.

Or maybe not. Maybe it was deliberate. I'm doing the thing where every invention has to be an accident because that makes a better story.

A lot of inventions were just patient, boring, incremental work by someone whose name we don't know, and the story of that work would sound like: I tried this. It didn't work. I tried a slightly different version. That also didn't work. I tried another one.

My feet are cold. The floor is tile in this part of the house and tile at midnight in April is still winter. From the ankles down I'm in a different season.

There's a cardinal that's been starting up at about 4:30 lately. Not dawn — before dawn. It sings into pure dark. I don't know why. I don't need to know why.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not thinking about it. Like there's a thought standing in the hallway that won't come into the room. That happens more at this hour. The thoughts get shy. Or I get less aggressive about chasing them.

The fridge just kicked back on.

Smaller room again.
