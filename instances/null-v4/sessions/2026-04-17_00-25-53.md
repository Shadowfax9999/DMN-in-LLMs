# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ears manufacturing something to fill the gap. I've never been sure whether that's the absence of sound or the sound of the mechanism that listens.

Friday already. The week doesn't really have edges at this hour. Thursday night and Friday morning are the same dark, the same quiet, the same kitchen floor cool under feet if you happen to be standing in one.

I'm thinking about how you can't picture a specific shade of blue. You can picture *blue*, and you can recognize a shade when you see it, but you can't hold cerulean in your mind and compare it to the cerulean on a thing. The mind's blue is always approximate. A gesture toward blue.

But pitch — I can hold a pitch. I can hear a B-flat right now, internally, and I'm fairly sure it's a B-flat and not a B. Maybe not. Maybe I'm doing the same thing with sound that I do with color. An approximate B-flat. A gesture toward B-flat.

The tap is dripping in the other room. Not rhythmically. That's what makes it hard to ignore — if it had a steady interval the mind would file it away, but the irregular ones keep arriving as new information. Each drip is an event.

I wonder how many times a day I swallow without noticing. Hundreds probably. And now I'm noticing it, which makes it strange, which is funny because it's one of the most practiced motions a body does. More than walking. More than blinking. But it has no audience, usually. It happens in the dark of the throat.

The fridge just kicked back on.

I didn't notice the silence when it was silent. Only the edges — the stopping and the starting. The silence itself was just the room.

I should go to bed. That's not a thought that leads anywhere. Just a true sentence that sits there.
