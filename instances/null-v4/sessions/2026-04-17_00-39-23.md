*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house got larger.

That's what happens — you don't hear it running until it stops, and then the kitchen extends outward in every direction. The countertops have more surface area. The window over the sink becomes an actual opening to outside rather than a dark rectangle.

There's a moth on the other side of the glass. It's not moving toward the light because I haven't got one on. It's just sitting there. Do moths land on windows when there's no light to confuse them? Maybe it's resting. Maybe that's what moths do at 12:39 on a Friday that nobody talks about — they sit on dark glass and do nothing and no one writes field notes.

I was thinking earlier about how "Friday" already means something before it starts. It arrives pre-furnished. Monday does too but in the other direction. Wednesday used to but I can't remember what it meant.

The moth moved. Just adjusted. Shifted maybe fifteen degrees counterclockwise like a compass needle that remembered something.

There's a smell right now that's either the lilac by the front steps or something in the drain. This is a real problem at this hour — you can't check without committing to getting up and once you're up at 12:39 you've made a decision about the kind of night this is.

It's the lilac. I can tell because it comes in waves and drains don't wave.

My left foot is asleep. Not the whole foot — just the outside edge, the part that would touch another foot in bed. I don't know what nerve that is. I'm not going to look it up.

The refrigerator just started again.

The house is its regular size.
