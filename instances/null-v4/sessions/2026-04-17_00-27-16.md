# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you suddenly hear how loud the silence is. Not silence. The creak of the house settling, which isn't settling at all — it's thermal contraction. Everything shrinking slightly as the night cools.

Friday. But barely. The kind of hour where the day hasn't decided what it is yet.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a specific shade of blue. You can think "blue," and something appears, but try to hold navy next to cerulean in your mind and they slide into each other. Colors in memory are more like opinions than images.

There's a glass on the counter I should wash. I'm not going to wash it right now. It has a ring of dried orange juice at the bottom, about a centimeter up. The meniscus line. Every glass in every sink has that little horizon.

Hm — the word "horizon." Horos, boundary. The thing you can't reach that defines where you are.

No, I was thinking about the glass.

The glass is just a glass. It's from a set of four but one broke and now there are three, which means someone always gets a different glass when people are over. Nobody mentions it. The mismatched glass just circulates.

Somewhere in this neighborhood someone's sprinkler system is going to come on at 4 AM because it's still set to the schedule from last summer and nobody's bothered to change it. Watering a lawn in the dark for no one.

I can hear my own breathing if I pay attention to it, and then it stops being automatic, and then I have to manually breathe for a while until I forget again. Attention ruins certain things. Sleep. Breathing. The way you walk down stairs.

Three glasses from a set of four. That's most things, actually. Most sets of things are incomplete and nobody remembers what's missing.

I'm going to think about something else now.

The texture of a parking lot at night, seen from above — like from a second-floor window. The light pools from the sodium lamps don't quite overlap. There are dark wedges between them. A car is parked in one of the dark wedges and you can just see the shape of it, the windshield catching one bar of light.

Whose car is that. Why is it there at this hour.

It's probably fine. It's probably someone who works early and parks the night before. Or someone who forgot. People forget where they parked and then take a different car or get a ride and the car just sits there entering a new relationship with the parking lot. Becoming part of it.

My foot is cold. Just the left one. The sock slipped halfway off inside the shoe at some point today and I took the shoe off but not the sock and now the sock is bunched under my arch and my toes are out and cold and I'm just letting it happen.
