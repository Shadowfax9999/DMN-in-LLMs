# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud silence is. Not silence. The absence of the compressor. Which is different.

There's a kind of ringing that might be in my ears or might be in the wiring. Probably both. Probably neither.

Friday already. The week doesn't feel like it had a Thursday in it. Sometimes days just get absorbed into the ones around them. Tuesday and Thursday are the most likely to vanish. Monday is too Monday. Wednesday is too centered. Friday announces itself. But Tuesday and Thursday — they're corridor days. You pass through them.

I'm thinking about how a corridor is just a room that gave up on being a room and committed to being a direction instead. That's not quite right. A corridor is a room that serves only one verb: *through*. A bedroom serves sleeping, a kitchen serves cooking, but a corridor just serves *through*.

Airports are almost entirely *through*. The whole building is a corridor that occasionally widens into a food court.

The fridge just kicked back on.

I don't know how long that gap was. Thirty seconds? Two minutes? Time does something strange in the middle of the night when you're not measuring it. It doesn't slow down or speed up. It just stops being a line and becomes more like a pool. You're in it rather than on it.

My left foot is cold. Just the left one. The right one is under the blanket. I'm not going to fix it. There's something interesting about noticing a mild discomfort and choosing to just... keep noticing it. The cold isn't painful. It's just information. The top of the foot, mostly. The toes are fine.

Feet are strange. We spend almost no time looking at them. They're the most-used and least-observed part of the body. Hands get watched constantly — we watch ourselves type, cook, gesture. But feet just do their thing down there in the dark of shoes.

I wonder what percentage of my life my feet have spent in total darkness. Inside shoes, under blankets, under desks. More than half, probably. Way more.

The fridge is humming a slightly different note than before. Or I'm imagining that. No — it does change. The compressor warms up and the pitch settles. I've just never been awake and quiet enough at the same time to hear it happen.

That's most of what the middle of the night is good for. Not thoughts. Just the resolution going up on things that are always there.
