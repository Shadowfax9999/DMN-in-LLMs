# 6

*seed: time · drift*

---

Something about being awake at this hour that makes the refrigerator louder. Not actually louder. Just — there's less to compete with. The compressor cycles on and you hear it the way you hear your own breathing when you're trying to fall asleep. It was always doing that.

I was thinking about how you can't picture the back of your own head. You've seen it in mirrors, in photographs, but the mental image won't hold. You reach for it and get the feeling of hair under your hand instead. The body knows itself by touch before it knows itself by sight. Babies figure this out. There's no mystery in it.

Friction. The word is better than the thing. The thing is just surfaces catching on each other at a molecular level, little peaks and valleys interlocking, but the word has that middle consonant cluster that actually slows your mouth down. Fri-c-tion. Your tongue does the thing the word describes.

There are words like that and then there are words that have nothing to do with their meaning. "Enormous" is small and tidy. "Diminutive" takes up space.

A friend once told me that the plural of cul-de-sac is culs-de-sac. The S goes in the middle. I think about this more than is reasonable. The dead end of the bag. The bottoms of the bag. French pluralizes the noun, not the phrase, so the bag stays singular and the bottoms multiply, which makes a cul-de-sac sound less like a street and more like a pair of pants.

I don't know where that was going.

The house across from wherever I'm imagining right now has one window lit. Upstairs bathroom, probably. Someone up for the same non-reason I am. There's a whole economy of 12:30 AM lights — the bathroom, the phone screen in bed, the kitchen because you convinced yourself you're hungry. Nobody turns on a living room lamp at this hour. Too much commitment. You'd have to be *doing* something.

The feeling of a key finding the lock in the dark. Your hand knows a shape your eyes have never really studied. How many things are like that. The exact pressure to close a cabinet so it doesn't bang. The place on the steering wheel where your thumb sits. Knowledge that lives in the wrist.

I'm tired in a way that isn't sleepy. More like the thinking has gone soft around the edges. Everything connects to everything but none of the connections feel urgent enough to follow.

That's fine.
