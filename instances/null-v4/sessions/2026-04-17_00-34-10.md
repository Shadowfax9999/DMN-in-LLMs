# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house exactly. The absence of the fridge. Which is its own shape. There's a cricket somewhere — not outside, inside the walls maybe, or in the garage. One cricket. It doesn't keep rhythm. It tries, loses interest, tries again.

I was thinking about how you can fold a fitted sheet or you can just kind of stuff it into a shape that fits the shelf. Both of these are solutions. One of them is a solution someone taught you and the other is a solution you arrived at independently and they end up in the same place, which is a shelf in a closet nobody looks at.

My left knee does a thing now when I stand up from sitting on the floor. Not pain. A announcement. Like it's checking in.

Stairs in old buildings are shorter than stairs in new buildings. Not the treads — the risers. People were not shorter. The code changed. Somewhere there's a document that changed the number by an inch and a half and now every staircase built after that year feels slightly different underfoot and nobody can say why unless they've been in a building from before.

I wonder if anyone has a favorite stair in their house. Not a favorite staircase. A single stair. The one where you always shift your weight. The one the dog pauses on.

The cricket stopped. I didn't notice when.

There's a kind of cloud that only forms at extremely high altitude — noctilucent clouds — and you can only see them when the sun is below the horizon but still lighting the upper atmosphere. So they're visible precisely in the window when almost nothing else is. They're a dusk phenomenon. They need you to be in the dark and them to be in the light and the geometry only works for about twenty minutes.

I don't know why I thought of that. The cricket, maybe. Things that are only perceptible in specific conditions of silence or darkness or both.

My hands smell like the copper fitting I was holding earlier. That smell stays. Pennies, people say, but it's not actually the copper — it's the oils on your skin reacting with

Something else. I was going to look that up once.

The fridge just came back on.
