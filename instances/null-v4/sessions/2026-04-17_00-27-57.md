# 16

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house exactly. The absence of the refrigerator. There's a shape left behind — a hum-shaped hole. It'll fill in. Give it thirty seconds and I won't remember what I'm listening to. That's how ambient sound works, the brain just... reassigns it to background. Stops reporting.

But right now, in the gap —

A car on a wet road somewhere. Not close. Two streets over maybe. You can tell wet road from dry road by ear. Wet has that long vowel to it. Dry is consonants.

I was thinking earlier about how I don't know what my tongue is doing most of the time. It's just in there. Touching my palate right now — I only know that because I checked. It was already touching before I checked. It's always doing something.

Teeth are stranger. You have these bones sticking out of your face. Exposed skeleton. The only part of your skeleton you can touch without cutting anything open. I think about that sometimes and then I stop thinking about it because it's not a comfortable thought.

Friday feels different from Thursday at the same hour. Shouldn't. Same darkness. Same 12:27. But there's a looseness to it. Nobody has to be anywhere tomorrow — not nobody, but the general pressure drops. You can feel a whole city's shoulders come down a quarter inch.

The car's gone.

I keep almost thinking about forks. Not in any productive way. Just — the word "fork" was in my head for a second. Fork in a road. Fork in a river. A fork is a place where one thing becomes two things. The utensil is named after the shape, not the other way around. Or — actually I don't know that. I don't know which came first. I'm not going to look it up.

There's a cricket somewhere that's slightly out of phase with the others. You'd think they'd sync up. Most oscillating things do, given enough time. Metronomes on a shared surface will sync. But this one cricket is just — committed to its own tempo. Off by maybe a sixteenth of a beat.

The fridge just came back on.
