# 45

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the other things. A clock somewhere — not in this room. The tick travels through the wall differently than through the air, arrives slightly wrong, like a word in translation.

My left hand is cooler than my right. I don't know why that would be. Maybe it was closer to the window. Maybe it wasn't doing anything.

There's a kind of leaf — I can't remember which — that curls into a tube when it dries. Not crumples. Curls. Deliberately, like it's making something. It isn't making anything. The cells on one side shrink faster than the cells on the other side and the geometry does the rest.

I keep thinking about hinges. Not door hinges. The hinge in a thought — the place where it folds. You're thinking about one thing and then you're thinking about another thing and there was a moment between them but you can't find it afterward. The leaf curled and I was thinking about hinges. What was the hinge. Probably "curls." Probably just the shape.

Someone is driving somewhere right now with the windows down because the night is finally warm enough. Not going anywhere particular. Just the driving. The sound of a car with its windows open at night is different from every other car sound — it's partially inside-out, the road noise and the radio both leaking in the wrong directions.

Friday at 00:37 is nobody's time. It's not late enough to be dramatic and not early enough to be responsible. It belongs to the people who looked up from something and didn't start the next thing.

My foot is asleep. I'm going to move it and not think about why the pins-and-needles feeling happens.

Pins and needles. Someone decided that was what it felt like and everyone agreed. It doesn't feel like pins and needles. It feels like static on a television that doesn't exist anymore. So that comparison is also dead now.

The clock is still going through the wall.
