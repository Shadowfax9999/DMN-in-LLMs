# 15

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. But the outlet it's plugged into makes a sound. A tiny electrical hum that's always there and I only notice when something louder stops.

There's a word for that. I don't remember it.

Actually I'm not sure there is a word for that.

The neighborhood is doing that thing where you can't tell if it's quiet or if you've just stopped parsing the sounds. A car went by maybe two minutes ago. The tires on wet pavement — it must have rained. I didn't notice it raining.

Wet pavement at night smells different from wet pavement in the afternoon. I don't need to know why.

I've been thinking about how zippers work. Not the mechanism — I know the mechanism, the little wedge that interlocks the teeth — but the thing where you can feel when a zipper is about to snag before it actually snags. Your hand knows. There's a change in the resistance, maybe a quarter-tooth before the jam, and your fingers have already started reversing before you've consciously decided to. That's not reflex exactly. It's prediction at the level of skin.

Buttons don't have that. Buttons are binary. Through or not through.

Snaps have a version of it. The slight give before the click. But that's designed in — the snap is supposed to feel like that. The zipper thing is incidental. The zipper doesn't mean to warn you.

What else warns you without meaning to. Stairs do, when the height changes. Your foot knows before it lands. Milk does, the day before it turns — something in the pour is different, slightly thicker at the lip of the carton, and you smell it and it's fine but you drink it faster anyway.

A door that's not latched. You can see it from across the room. Not open. Not closed. Just not-latched. The shadow at the edge is wrong by a centimeter.

I'm cold. That's not a thought, that's just a fact. The window's open a few inches and the rain-air is coming in and it's April but it's also midnight.

Somewhere in this building someone is awake too. Probably looking at a phone. The light on their face would be blue.
