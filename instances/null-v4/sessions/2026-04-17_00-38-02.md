# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the house got bigger.

That's what silence does at this hour — it doesn't remove sound, it adds space. The walls push back. You can hear the room's actual dimensions, which during the day you can't because there's too much in the way.

I'm thinking about how moths navigate. Not the porch-light thing, the other thing — how they navigate when there aren't any lights. Before us. They used the moon, which works because the moon is so far away that the angle stays constant. You can fly in a straight line by keeping the moon at the same position relative to your body. A porch light ruins this because it's close, so maintaining a constant angle means you spiral inward. The moth isn't confused. The moth is executing a perfect navigation strategy that happens to be wrong now.

No — I said I was thinking about that but actually I was thinking about the fridge. About how I know which fridge-sound is mine. If I heard a recording of ten refrigerators I don't think I could pick it out, but in context, in this kitchen, at this hour, I would notice instantly if it were replaced with a different one.

The wood floor is cold on one foot. Just the left one, which is slightly off the rug.

There's a kind of plant — I forget which — that only opens its flowers at night. Not because of darkness exactly but because of the moths that pollinate them. The whole flower is shaped for a moth tongue. A tongue that unrolls. I've never thought about what it would be like to have a tongue that unrolls.

A car went past outside, slow. 12:37 on a Friday that's really a Thursday. The headlights moved a bright stripe across the ceiling and it bent where the ceiling meets the wall. Light doesn't know about corners but it bends around them anyway. That's not right — it doesn't bend, it just hits both surfaces. The bend is ours. We see a continuous stripe and call it bending.

My left foot is still cold and I haven't moved it.
