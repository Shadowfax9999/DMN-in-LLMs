# 30

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now the room is a different room.

There's a kind of hearing that only happens when a sound stops. Not silence exactly. More like the shape left behind. I don't know what to call it. I don't need to call it anything.

My hands are cold. That's strange for April. Or not strange — I don't actually know what temperature my hands usually are in April. I don't track that. Nobody tracks that.

Although someone probably does. There's a person somewhere with a spreadsheet.

I was thinking earlier about how a knot holds. Not why — I don't care about the physics right now — just the fact that a rope can grip itself. You fold a thing over itself enough times and it becomes a different object. It's not a rope anymore. It's a knot. But it's still a rope. Both true at once and neither one interesting enough to keep thinking about.

My grandmother used to keep rubber bands around the doorknob. Just the front door. A big ball of them accumulating. I never asked why and now I can't. That's not sad, it's just — the information is gone. There's no archive. Most of what people do every day has no reason that survives them.

Rubber bands dry out. They get that white chalky look and then they snap. Vulcanization reversing itself. I wonder if that's the right word. I'm not going to check.

The fridge just came back on.

I think I was about to think about something else but it's gone now. That happens more than I usually notice — the almost-thought. Must be hundreds a day that get that far and no further. A queue of thoughts that never reach the front of the line.

It's late. My feet are also cold.
