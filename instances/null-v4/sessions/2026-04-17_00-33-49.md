# 34

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops. Then the room gets wider somehow.

There's a kind of math to how many things you can hear in total silence. First the fridge absence. Then the blood in your ears. Then something ticking — not a clock, something thermal, a pipe or a baseboard contracting. The house cooling down into itself.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a specific number of things. Like — seven. If someone says "picture seven dots" you either count them out in a line, which is really just one line, or you clump them and trust yourself. Three and four. You're not seeing seven. You're seeing a relationship between smaller numbers you actually believe.

There's probably a cap. Five, maybe six, where you stop perceiving quantity and start estimating it. Somewhere in there you cross from knowing to guessing and you can't feel the border.

Friday at 12:33 AM is an odd hour to be a specific day. It's not really Friday yet. It's the tail end of Thursday wearing Friday's name. The week doesn't feel like it's turned over. Weekdays are more like tides than walls — they bleed forward and back depending on when you slept.

A friend once said she couldn't fall asleep without a fan running because the silence was "too specific." I didn't understand that then. Silence as something pointed. But the fridge thing — the sudden shape of a room when its background drops out — that's close. Silence isn't empty. It's the mold left behind by whatever sound was just in it.

No. That's too neat.

It's just quiet and I noticed.

The pipe ticked again. Three in a row, then nothing. Metal does that — gives up its heat in little clicks, like it's letting go one finger at a time. I don't know if that's actually what's happening. It's just a sound in a wall.

I keep thinking about the number thing. About how a handful of rice is not a number. You'd never say "I'm holding four hundred grains of rice," you'd say "some rice." At a certain point quantity becomes texture. A field of grass isn't a number of blades. A beach isn't a number of grains. The many becomes a material.

That has to work the other way too. One grain of sand isn't really sand. It's a tiny rock. Sand is what happens when you stop being able to distinguish individuals. It's a crowd phenomenon.

I wonder what the threshold is for rain. One drop isn't rain. Two drops isn't rain. At some point between the second drop and the thousandth, water becomes weather.

It's late. The pipe is quiet now.
