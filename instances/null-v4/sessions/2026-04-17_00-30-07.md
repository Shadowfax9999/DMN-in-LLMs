# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house exactly. The distances inside it.

There's a difference between a room that's quiet and a room where something just stopped making noise. The second one has a shape to it. The quiet pours into the space the compressor was filling and it doesn't fit right, it's the wrong shape, there's a moment where you can almost hear the mismatch before everything settles.

I've been thinking about how water finds the lowest point. Not in the interesting geological way — in the annoying way. The way it gets under the lip of a shower door. The way a spill on a counter will find the one seam in the laminate. Water is stupid and perfect. It doesn't solve problems, it just goes down, and going down turns out to be enough to get into everything.

Keys are like that too, actually. Not water-like. But — a key doesn't know anything about the lock. It's just shaped. The knowledge is in the shape. I don't think that's a metaphor for anything. It's just something keys do.

My left foot is asleep. The tingling is so specific. It's in the ball of the foot and the fourth toe and nowhere else, which means I'm sitting on some particular nerve, one nerve, and that nerve maps to those exact locations and no others. The body is a wiring diagram that you only notice when something shorts.

Friday feels different from Thursday at this hour. I don't know why. It shouldn't. The dark is the same dark. But there's something about knowing that tomorrow is unstructured. The night gets wider.

I can hear a truck on the highway. Not the road out front — the highway, which is maybe two miles from here. At this hour there's nothing between here and there to absorb it. During the day you'd never hear it. All those daytime sounds aren't louder than a truck. They're just closer.

I'm hungry but not for anything specific. That's a particular kind of hungry. The kind where you open the fridge and nothing in there is the thing, and you know nothing in there is the thing before you open it, and you open it anyway, and the light comes on.
