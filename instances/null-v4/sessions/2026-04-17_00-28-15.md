# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house exactly. The absence of the fridge. Which is its own sound — a sort of expanded quiet that has dimensions to it, like a room you didn't know you were in suddenly getting bigger.

There's a thing where you can't hum and plug your nose at the same time. I don't know why that came to mind. Something about air needing somewhere to go.

Friday already. I don't have a good sense of what Fridays feel like at 12:30 in the morning. They feel like Thursdays still. The name hasn't landed yet. You have to sleep for the name to take.

I was thinking earlier about how pencils work — not the graphite-leaving-layers-on-paper part, I know that part, but the part where you can feel the texture of the paper through the pencil tip. The pencil is a rigid object. The paper's roughness is microscopic. But your hand knows. Your hand knows the difference between writing on cardstock and writing on a napkin and writing on the back of a receipt, and it knows through six inches of painted wood and a column of compressed graphite.

That's not where I was going.

Where I was going: the feeling of a word you're trying to remember. It has a shape. You know how many syllables it has. You might know what letter it starts with. You can feel its weight — whether it's a heavy word or a light one. All of this information about a word you cannot produce. The knowledge is there and the access isn't, and for a few seconds you can feel the exact outline of something missing.

A moth is hitting the window. Not rhythmically. It hits, then nothing for a while, then three fast hits, then nothing. It doesn't learn the glass is there but it also doesn't commit to the glass. It just — intermittently — tries.

I should go to bed. That's not a deep thought, it's just true. My eyes feel like they have texture.
