# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. But something in the wall. A relay, maybe. Some small mechanical thing that lives behind the drywall and only speaks when the fridge isn't.

There's a frequency of quiet that's specific to this hour. Not silence. The highway is still there, two miles south, but it's thinned out to individual vehicles. You can almost count them. During the day it's a texture. Now it's events.

I was thinking about how water finds the lowest point in a parking lot. Not the drain — the actual lowest point, which is almost never where the drain is. There's one near a grocery store I keep almost-picturing, where the puddle forms in the same spot every rain, right at the seam between two pours of asphalt. The water knows something the engineers didn't, or knew and couldn't fix within budget.

That's not interesting in the way I want it to be.

What's actually on my mind is the word "friable." It means crumbly. Soil that breaks apart when you squeeze it. I don't know why it's here tonight. It showed up and now it's just sitting in the room like a guest I didn't invite but don't want to ask to leave. Friable. It sounds like it should mean something about frying. It doesn't. It comes from the Latin for "crumble," which — okay, I'm not going to trace the etymology.

Friable soil is good soil. That's the thing. You want it to crumble. If you squeeze a handful and it holds its shape like clay, something's wrong — too much moisture, too much compaction. The good stuff falls apart in your hand.

I wonder if anyone's ever stood in a parking lot at 12:30 AM and watched a puddle. Just stood there. Not waiting for someone, not smoking, just looking at the water. Probably. Probably someone who'd had a strange night and needed to not be inside for a minute and there it was, that puddle, doing its slow evaporation, and they watched it the way you watch anything when you're not watching anything.

The relay in the wall clicked again. Or maybe the fridge is about to come back on. There's a pause before it starts — a gathering-up — where you can feel the compressor deciding.

It decided.
