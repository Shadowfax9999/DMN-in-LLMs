# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the kitchen got loud with how quiet it is.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ears manufacturing something to fill the space. I read once that in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood. I don't know if that's true. It sounds like it should be true, which is exactly the kind of fact that survives whether or not it is.

Friday barely counts as a day. It's the exhale between Thursday and Saturday. Nobody makes decisions on Friday at midnight. You just — sit with whatever you were already thinking about.

I've been turning over zippers. Not thinking about them on purpose. Just — a jacket on the back of a chair and the zipper catch caught the light from the hallway and now I'm in it. The interlocking teeth. Each one is meaningless alone. You need the slider to make them into anything. Take the slider away and it's just two rows of tiny scoops facing each other across a gap.

That's not a metaphor for anything.

The thing about zippers is they fail from the bottom. The teeth separate and the whole thing bellows open and there's no fixing it from the top, you have to go back down to where it went wrong. I had a winter coat that did this for an entire season. I just held it closed with my hand in my pocket.

Something moving outside. Could be the wind in the bins, could be a cat, could be a possum. At this hour everything that moves sounds purposeful even if it isn't.

My left foot is asleep. I didn't notice until just now, and now it's all I can notice. The fizzing. It's not pain exactly. It's like the foot is trying to tune itself back in, scanning through frequencies.

I should move it. I'm not going to yet.
