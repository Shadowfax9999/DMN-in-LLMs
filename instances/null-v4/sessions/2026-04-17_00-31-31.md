# 27

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and the silence has a different weight now. Not heavier. Just — present. You don't notice the hum until it stops.

I've been thinking about the word "lull." It sounds like what it is. Most words don't do that. "Aggressive" doesn't sound aggressive. "Soft" has that F in it, which isn't particularly soft. But lull. Lull lull lull. It puts itself to sleep.

There's a kind of moth that navigates by the moon and gets confused by porch lights. I don't know what kind. I just know they spiral inward because they're trying to keep the light at a constant angle, which works when the light source is infinitely far away but not when it's a 60-watt bulb on someone's front step. That's not a metaphor for anything. It's just a bad situation for the moth.

My left knee has been making a sound when I go down stairs. Not up. Down. Which seems backwards — going up is the effort, going down should be the easy part. But going down, your knee is braking. It's catching your whole body weight on each step and lowering it. I never thought about stairs as a braking problem.

Celery. I keep buying celery and then it goes soft in the crisper drawer and I throw it away. I don't even know what I'm planning to make when I buy it. It just seems like something that should be in a refrigerator.

The couple next door has a light on. Their kitchen window. At 12:30 on a Friday that either means something very good or something very bad. There's no neutral reason to be in your kitchen at this hour.

Actually that's not true. Cereal.

I wonder if anyone has ever mapped the sounds a house makes at night. Not the dramatic ones — not settling foundations or whatever. The small catalogue. Icemaker. The tick of something cooling or warming. That one pipe. You learn a house the way you learn a face, mostly without trying, and then one night there's a sound you don't recognize and you're suddenly alert in a way that reminds you your body is still an animal body, lying in the dark, listening.

The ice cream truck song. I heard one today, blocks away, and I could not for the life of me name the tune. I know it the way I know my phone number from when I was eight — perfectly, uselessly, in a format that doesn't translate to anything else.

I should go to bed. That's not a thought, that's just a fact sitting in the room with me, being patient.
