# 1

*seed: time*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the tick of something cooling. Metal contracting. It does that.

There's a kind of silence that's only available after a mechanical sound stops. Not the silence that was there before — a new one, shaped by what just ended.

I was thinking about how you can fold a map back up if you follow the creases but almost nobody does. They just kind of bunch it. And then the map lives like that, bunched, in a glove compartment for years, and the creases soften and new ones form and eventually it's not a map with a folding pattern anymore, it's just a specific object with its own topography.

My left hand is doing something against the edge of the table. Tapping. Not rhythm, just — checking that the edge is still there.

Moth outside the window. It keeps hitting the screen, which is not the light and not the glass, it's the thing between those two things. I wonder if the screen is confusing in a way the glass wouldn't be. You can feel the air. You can probably smell whatever moths smell. But you can't get through.

That's different from a closed window. A closed window is a wall that happens to be transparent. A screen is a wall that happens to be permeable to everything except you.

I had a thought earlier today about how "flush" means level, like a flush door, and also means a rush of blood or water, and also means a hand of cards that are all the same suit, and I couldn't figure out if those are the same word that drifted or three different words that collided. I still don't know. I didn't look it up.

The moth left. Or stopped. Either way the ticking against the screen stopped the way the fridge compressor stopped — mid-action, not at a resting point, just done.

I should go to bed but the house is doing that thing where it's more interesting at this hour. Not because anything is happening. Because the quiet makes the ordinary stuff louder and the louder stuff — traffic, neighbors — has dropped away, so the ratio changes. The house gets a turn.

Somewhere a few streets over a car door. Just one. No engine after it, no second door. Someone got something out of their car and went back inside.
