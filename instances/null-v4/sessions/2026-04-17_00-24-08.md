# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house got bigger.

There's a kind of hearing that only happens after midnight — not sharper exactly, more like the ratio changes. During the day sound is figure-on-ground. Now the ground is the figure. The hum was the room and when it stopped the room became the walls and the distance between them.

I'm thinking about how I know it's spring without looking outside. Something about the quality of the air coming through the crack under the back door. Not warm, not cold. Unresolved. The air doesn't know what it is yet either.

My left knee has a thing it does when I've been sitting too long. Not pain. A request.

Somewhere in the neighborhood a car door. No engine before or after — just the door. Someone got something out of their car or put something in. At 12:23. That's a whole life over there.

I was trying to remember earlier today whether the word "latch" sounds like what it does. I think it does. The tch. But I think I think that because I already know what a latch does. You can't unhear the meaning. There's probably a term for that and I don't need to know it right now.

The fridge came back on.

Smaller room again.

I keep almost thinking about a thing and then not thinking about it. Like reaching for something on a shelf and then just — not. Leaving your hand up there for a second and bringing it back down. That's most of thinking, probably. The thoughts you don't have. No way to count them.

My phone screen lit up on the counter. Notification of something. The light made a rectangle on the ceiling that wasn't there before and now it's gone and I can't stop seeing where it was.

I should go to bed. That's not a thought, that's a caption. The thought underneath it is that I like it here right now, in the kitchen, with nobody asking me anything, including myself.

So I'll stay another minute.
