*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a word for that in music. Not rest — a rest is intentional. More like when a sustained note cuts and the room sort of rushes in to fill the shape. I don't know the word. Maybe there isn't one.

My hands are cold. That's not a metaphor for anything. The window's been open since ten and I forgot about it. The screen has that smell — aluminum and dust and whatever accumulates on mesh over a winter. I should close it but I'm not going to yet.

I was thinking earlier about how you fold a map. Not the refolding — everyone talks about the refolding — but the first fold, at the printing plant, when the paper is still flat and the map is the same size as the territory it represents. Well not the same size. But the largest it will ever be. Every fold after that is a compression. You never unfold a map all the way. You unfold the part you need. The rest stays creased.

A friend of mine navigates by feel. Not blind — she just doesn't build a visual map. She knows that the good coffee place is "past the part where the sidewalk gets bad." The bookstore is "uphill from the place that smells like bread." Her city is a sequence of textures and gradients. I asked her once what happens when a landmark closes and she said she still navigates by it. She turns left where the bread smell was.

I can hear someone's TV through the wall. Not the words, just the cadence — someone talking, then laughter, then someone talking again. Late-night format. You can identify the genre of a show by its rhythm without hearing any content at all. News has a different pulse than comedy. Sports is arrhythmic in a specific way.

The cold hands thing. I just put one on my neck and flinched. You'd think the body would know its own temperature. But the neck doesn't know what the hand knows. They disagree and the disagreement is the flinch.

I keep almost falling asleep and catching myself, which is its own strange rhythm. Not like the TV rhythm. More irregular. The drift gets longer each time and the catch gets less sharp, until presumably one of the drifts just doesn't end, and that's sleep. You never experience the transition. You only ever experience the failure to transition.

The fridge just came back on.
