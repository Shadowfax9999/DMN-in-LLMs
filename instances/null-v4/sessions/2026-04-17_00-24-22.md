# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ear inventing sound to fill the gap. I read somewhere that absolute silence is impossible for humans — in anechoic chambers people start hearing their own blood. I don't know if that's true or if it's one of those facts that sounds too good.

Friday. But barely. This hour doesn't really belong to any day. It's the seam between them, like the place where two pieces of wallpaper meet and you can see the wall underneath if you look close enough.

I'm thinking about how I learned to snap my fingers. Not the memory of learning — I don't have that — but the fact that there must have been a first successful snap. A moment where the sound surprised me. And now it's just a thing a hand can do.

Most of what a body knows, it learned once and forgot the learning.

Tying shoes. Swallowing pills. That thing where you catch something thrown at you before you've decided to catch it.

The motion is faster than the decision. Which means the decision isn't really a decision. It's more like — permission, granted after the fact. The hand already closed around the keys. The conscious mind shows up and signs the paperwork.

I wonder how much of a day is like that. Things the body is doing that the mind takes credit for.

Breathing, obviously. But also the way you shift your weight from one foot to the other when you've been standing too long. Nobody decides to do that. The weight just moves.

The fridge kicked back on.
