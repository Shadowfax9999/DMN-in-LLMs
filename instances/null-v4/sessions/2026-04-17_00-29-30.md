# 21

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now the house is doing that thing where you can hear it being a house. The settling. Whatever settling is. I've never been sure if houses actually settle or if that's just what people say about sounds they can't place.

There's a moth on the window screen. From inside, with the lamp on, I can see its underside — the part that never gets illustrated. Field guides always show the top of the wings spread flat, pinned. Nobody paints the belly of a moth. It's pale. Almost translucent. You can see the body working.

I was thinking earlier about how "midnight" doesn't mean the middle of the night for most people. It means the beginning of staying up too late. The actual middle of the night — two, three AM — doesn't have a word. It's just "late" or "early," depending on your direction.

Tape. I pulled tape off a box today and it took a layer of cardboard with it. The top printed layer, the brown underneath exposed. That's how tape works but I don't think about it — it bonds to the surface more than the surface bonds to itself. The failure happens in the cardboard, not the adhesive. That seems like it should be a metaphor for something but I'm going to let it not be.

The moth left.

My hands smell like cardboard now that I'm thinking about it. That particular dry smell. I washed them but cardboard stays in the ridges of your fingerprints for a while.

What was I — right, the box. I don't remember what was in it. Something I ordered. It's in the other room. I could go check but I don't want to get up, and also there's something honest about not remembering what you bought three days ago. It mattered enough to click purchase and not enough to remember on a Friday at 12:30.

A car went past slow enough that the sound had a full shape — approach, presence, departure. Fast cars just have approach and departure. The presence part gets compressed to nothing.

I'm thirsty but not thirsty enough.
