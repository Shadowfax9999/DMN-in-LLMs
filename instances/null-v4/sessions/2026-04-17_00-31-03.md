The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house exactly. The absence of the refrigerator.

There's a specific kind of quiet that only exists right after a mechanical sound stops. It's not silence. It's the shape left behind.

I was thinking about how you can't actually picture a face you know well. You can get the feeling of the face. The sort of — weather of it. But if you try to hold any one feature still it slides. My mother's face. I know it completely and I can't see it at all.

Fridges have a note, by the way. Ours growing up was a B-flat, I think. Or I've decided it was. Memory does that — assigns precision after the fact so the story holds.

The word "precise" comes from — no. I don't need to look that up right now.

Something about spring nights specifically. The windows are open for the first time in months and the air smells different inside the house because of it. Not like outside. Like inside mixed with outside. A third thing.

I keep almost thinking about work. Little flickers of it at the edges. A meeting I have. Something I said I'd send someone. These thoughts aren't interesting but they're persistent, like a dog nosing at your hand.

The fridge just kicked back on.

There it is.

I didn't time it but it felt like about four minutes. Probably less. Probably ninety seconds. The quiet always feels longer than the sound.

I should be asleep. That's not a meaningful observation, it's just true. It's 12:30 and I'm sitting here listening to appliances and trying to picture faces and not getting anywhere with either one.

The streetlight outside makes a trapezoid on the ceiling. It's been making that trapezoid for as long as we've lived here and I have never once looked at it during the day to see what it would be without the light in it. Just ceiling, probably. Just paint.
