# 24

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was. That's always the thing — you don't hear the hum until it stops. Then the kitchen gets this depth to it, like the room just got bigger.

There's a moth outside the window. Not doing anything interesting. Just sitting on the glass. From this side it's pale, almost translucent. From its side — I don't know what a window looks like from the outside at night. Bright, probably. A bright rectangle. It's not even trying to get in. Just sitting there.

I was thinking earlier about how you can't picture a specific number of things. Like if someone says "picture seventeen birds on a wire" you just picture... some birds on a wire. A rough cluster. You'd have to count them in deliberately, placing each one left to right, and even then you'd lose count around nine or ten and have to start over. But you could *hear* seventeen beats. You could clap it out. Rhythm holds exact quantities that images can't.

Which is maybe why people count on their fingers. Not because fingers are good at math but because touch is sequential the way numbers are.

The moth moved. Just barely. Adjusted its grip or whatever moths grip with. Tarsi. I think that's the word. I'm not sure and I'm not going to look it up.

Friday already. The week doesn't feel like it had a middle. Sometimes weeks are like that — you get Monday and then suddenly it's the end, and the days in between feel like they happened to someone else, or in a different room. Not forgotten exactly. Just thin.

The fridge is starting up again.
