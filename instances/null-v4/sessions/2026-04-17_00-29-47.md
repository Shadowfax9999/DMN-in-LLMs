*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house is doing that thing where you can suddenly hear how loud it was by how quiet it is now.

There's a kind of knowing that only works in the body. Like how you can reach behind you and find a light switch without looking. Not every time. But most times. The hand just goes to where the switch is. And if someone moved it two inches to the left you'd stand there in the dark pawing at the wall feeling betrayed by something you didn't know you trusted.

I'm thinking about trust that doesn't announce itself.

The way you step onto a floor. You don't check. You don't test it with one foot like ice. You just walk. Thousands of times a day, total commitment to the idea that the ground will hold, and you never once notice yourself deciding.

What would it be like to notice. To actually feel the decision each time. That might be what anxiety is.

Something else — the phrase "dead of night." Not the death of night. The dead of it. Like night is a body and this hour is the center of its mass. The heaviest part. The part that would sink first.

2:00, 3:00 AM. The hours that don't really belong to either day. Wednesday has already ended and Thursday hasn't committed to starting. You're in the gap between two names.

A dog is barking somewhere but it's so far away it sounds more like a memory of a dog than an actual one.

I was going to think about something else. What was it. It had to do with switches, or floors, or — no. It's gone. That happens. A thought was here and then it went somewhere I can't follow. Not forgotten exactly. More like it walked out of the room while I was looking at something else, and now there's just the feeling that the room had more in it a second ago.

That's fine. The fridge just kicked back on.
