# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house doing anything. The house being a volume of air with a particular shape.

There's a difference between a room being quiet and a room becoming quiet. The second one has a direction to it. You're moving into something.

I keep almost thinking about how wood expands at night when the temperature drops — no. I don't actually know if that's true. I think wood expands when it's *warm*. The creaking at night might be contraction. Might be the joists pulling a fraction of a millimeter away from each other. Every night the house gets microscopically looser, every morning it tightens back up.

Or maybe I made that up entirely.

A sock on the bathroom floor. I noticed it this morning and I'm noticing it again now, which means I walked past it at least six times today without picking it up. At some point a thing on the floor becomes part of the floor. There's a threshold — two days? three? — where it stops registering as out of place. Guests would see it instantly. You'd see it in someone else's house. But your own sock on your own tile has a kind of diplomatic immunity.

The word "clutter" is interesting only in that I've never heard anyone use it about their own things in real time. It's always retrospective. "I let it get cluttered." Past tense. While it's happening it's just stuff where you put it.

My left knee has that thing where it's not hurting but I'm aware of it. A joint making itself known. Like it's raising a hand in a meeting but doesn't actually have anything to say yet.

I wonder what the oldest sock in my house is. Not the oldest sock I own — socks cycle out — but is there one that's been here longer than I think? Wedged behind the dryer, maybe. Moved in with me. Could predate some friendships.

I should sleep. That's not a thought, that's a caption. The thought underneath it is just the awareness of being vertical at the wrong hour and the mild defiance of staying that way.

The fridge kicks back on.
