# 42

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops. Then the kitchen gets wider somehow.

There's a thing about how you can't remember what your tongue is doing in your mouth until someone mentions it. Now you're aware of it. Sorry. It's just sitting there, touching your teeth, being this huge muscle you never think about.

Friday at 00:36 is not the same as Thursday at 00:36. Thursday late-night feels stolen. Friday late-night feels given.

I keep thinking about zippers. Not how they work — I know how they work, the little scoops, the wedge — but the sound. That specific sound has no analog. Nothing else in nature or manufacture sounds like a zipper. A zipper sounds exactly like a zipper and nothing else.

Velcro too, actually. But velcro you can sort of get to from other things. Pulling tape. Peeling bark. A zipper is just itself.

There are probably hundreds of sounds like that. Sounds that arrived with their object and will leave when the object does. The clipboard spring. The click of a retractable pen. What was the last totally new sound a human invention made? Maybe the particular whine of an electric car reversing — that was designed by someone, a speaker playing a composed sound, which means it's not really the object's sound at all. It's a sound wearing the object's clothes.

My left foot is cold. Just the left one. The right one is fine. I'm not going to do anything about it.

There's a kind of fly that overwinters as an adult and you find them in April on the inside of windows, slow and buzzing in a lower register than summer flies. They buzz like they're not fully committed to it. Like they're remembering how.

The window is open about two inches. The screen is in. You can hear something out there but you can't tell if it's wind or distant water or traffic on a road you can't see. At this hour those three things converge into one sound.

I was going to think about something else. What was it. It's gone.

That happens less during the day. Losing a thought. During the day thoughts have context — you were reading something, or someone said something, and you can retrace. At night your thoughts don't have sources. They just appear and if you don't catch them they weren't anywhere.

My foot's still cold.
