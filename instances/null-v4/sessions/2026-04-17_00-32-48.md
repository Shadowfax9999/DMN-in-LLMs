# 31

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the house. Not the house exactly — the absence the fridge was covering. There's a creak in something. Not settling. More like breathing. Wood does that when the temperature drops — contracts at slightly different rates depending on grain direction. But I don't want to explain it. It's just a sound the house makes when it thinks no one's listening.

I've been turning over the word "latch" for no reason. Latch. It sounds like what it does. That little metal catch on a garden gate — the kind you can open from the other side if you reach over and feel for it. There's a specific knowledge in your fingers when they find it without seeing it. Your hand knows the shape before your brain names it.

What else do hands know. Which pocket has the keys. How tight is too tight on a jar lid. The exact pressure to crack an egg one-handed, which I can't actually do, but I've watched people who can and their hands look bored doing it. Competence looks bored. That's — no, that's not always true. Sometimes competence looks like concentration. A cellist doesn't look bored.

A cellist looks like they're listening to something inside the note. Which maybe they are. The resonance of the string transfers through the bridge into the body of the instrument and you can feel it in your sternum if you're close enough. I sat near a cellist once in a space that was too small for the sound and it felt like the music was happening inside my ribs.

Too small for the sound. Some rooms are. Bathroom acoustics. Singing in the shower isn't about privacy, it's about the reverb making you sound better than you are. The tiles reflect almost everything. You become a better singer just by standing in a particular box.

I wonder what I'd be thinking about if I weren't thinking about this. That's an unanswerable question but I like the shape of it. There's no control group for a train of thought.

The creak again. Same spot. Could be a joist over the basement where the span is longest. I'm not going to go check.
