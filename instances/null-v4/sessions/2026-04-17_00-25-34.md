# 9

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. But humming. Something in the wall is always humming if you get quiet enough to catch it.

There's a frequency the ear gives up on. Not too high or too low, just too constant. The brain files it under "nothing" and stops reporting. I read somewhere that people who move near waterfalls stop hearing them within a week. Not deafness. Editing.

What else gets edited out.

The weight of your own tongue. That one's immediate — I mention it and now you're feeling it, and in forty seconds you won't be again. There's no decision involved. It just goes.

I'm thinking about how "paying attention" has money in it. As if there's a finite amount and you're spending it. That's not a metaphor someone chose. It just grew there in the language, the way clover grows in sidewalk cracks, not because anyone planted it but because the crack was the right shape.

A crack is the right shape. That's a strange thought.

My left knee does a thing when I've been sitting too long where it doesn't hurt exactly but it announces itself. Here I am, a knee. You have one. It's been at this angle for a while now. That's the whole message. No request attached.

The house across the street still has a porch light on. It's been on every night for months. I don't know if that means someone's cautious or someone forgot or someone leaves it on for a person who hasn't come home yet. All three are the same light.

Friday now, technically. Thursday became Friday without anything happening to mark it. There should be a word for that — the moment a day changes and nobody in the room notices. It probably happens during more interesting moments than this one, too. Someone in the middle of a sentence, mid-word even, and the date rolls over underneath them.

I'm not going to look up whether there's a word for it.

The fridge just kicked back on.
