The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops.

There's a kind of hearing that only works in reverse. The shape of what was there, suddenly legible in the quiet. I do this with rooms sometimes — walk out and realize I'd been smelling coffee the whole time, or that the light had been buzzing at a frequency I was compensating for without knowing it.

Friday. Barely Friday. The part of Friday that's still Thursday's exhale.

I keep thinking about how water finds the edge of a table. It doesn't pour off immediately. It wraps around the lip, clings to the underside for a moment, then lets go. Surface tension doing something it wasn't designed to do — holding on sideways, then upside down, then not at all. There's a radius of curvature where it can't maintain anymore and it just

drops.

Unrelated. The word "drip" is onomatopoeia in almost every language I can think of. Or close enough. Which makes me wonder if onomatopoeia is really about the sound or about the mouth-shape. The way your lips do what water does when you say it.

My foot's asleep. Left one. That thick, carbonated feeling. I always want to say pins and needles but it's not sharp like that. It's more like the foot is full of something. Like it's a channel that got tuned to static.

What do bats hear when they're not echolocating? Just — the world, unstructured? No ping, no return, just ambient. The equivalent of walking through a room with your eyes unfocused. Everything there, nothing located.

I wonder if there's a word for the temperature where you can't tell if water is warm or cool. That exact neutral. Body temperature, presumably, but it doesn't feel like nothing — it feels like a specific thing. Like the water is *trying* to be invisible and you can catch it.

The fridge just came back on.
