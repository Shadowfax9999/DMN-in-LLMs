*seed: time · drift*

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear the house.

Not the house doing anything. The house being a volume. Air sitting in rooms. That particular quality of a space at night where you become aware that the ceiling is up there, doing its job, and has been this whole time.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a specific number of things. Like, seven. You can picture a few things or a lot of things but seven requires counting and counting isn't picturing. Somewhere around four it stops being a shape and starts being a quantity. There's probably a name for that threshold. I don't need to know it right now.

The fridge is back on.

There's a thing where certain kinds of knowledge feel different to have. Knowing how to ride a bike is not the same shape as knowing the capital of Peru. One of them lives in your legs. The other one is just — available. Lima. It's just there, like a book on a shelf. The bike thing isn't on a shelf. I don't know where it is. Somewhere in the part of me that isn't verbal.

My left foot is cold and my right foot is fine. Same socks. Same floor. I don't know what to do with that.

I keep almost thinking about music — not a specific song, but the way a melody can get partway out before you realize you don't remember what comes next. The fragment just hangs there. Three or four notes with nothing after them. Your mind offers silence and the silence has a specific shape, like a socket for a piece that's missing.

No — that's too neat. What actually happens is you hum the part you know and then sort of mumble-hum and then stop. And you go on making coffee or whatever you were doing.

Friday. The word Friday doesn't sound like anything related to what it is. Most day-names don't. They're just sounds now.

My foot is still cold.
