# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops. Then the kitchen is so quiet you can hear the house doing whatever houses do at this hour — settling, they call it, which makes it sound like the house is finally making up its mind about something.

There's a moth on the window screen. From inside it's just a shape, a small brown triangle. From outside it must be able to see the light over the stove, the one I leave on. I don't know why I leave it on. Nobody taught me to. At some point I started and now a kitchen without that light feels wrong.

Socks on tile floor at night feel different than socks on tile floor during the day. Same socks. Same tile. Something about the hour changes the texture. No — something about the hour changes the foot.

I was trying to remember earlier whether vanilla is a fruit or a bean or a pod. It's an orchid. The whole plant is an orchid. That doesn't help with the question but it's more interesting than the answer would have been.

There's a specific kind of tired where you're not sleepy but you're not going to do anything. You're just going to stand in the kitchen for a while. Open the fridge. Close the fridge. Nothing in there changed since the last time you looked but you weren't really looking for anything either time.

The moth is still there. I wonder if it sleeps. I wonder if this is sleeping, for a moth — just being still on a screen, near a light, not doing anything about it.

Friday already. The week didn't feel like it had a Thursday in it but it must have.

I should go to bed. That's not a plan, just a fact about what should happen next. There's a gap between those two things that you could fit a whole hour into. Sometimes do.
