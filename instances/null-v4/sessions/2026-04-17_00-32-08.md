# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was.

There's a kind of ringing that isn't ringing. The ear manufacturing sound to fill a gap. I read somewhere that total silence is impossible to experience because the auditory system starts producing — no, I don't need to know why. Just that it's happening. A high thin tone like a television left on in another room, except there's no television and no other room. Just this.

A moth is doing something against the window screen. Not trying to get in. Not trying to get out. Just — being where the light meets the mesh. Repeatedly.

I've been thinking about how you can't actually picture someone's face when they're not here. You think you can. You'd swear you can. But try it — try to hold someone's whole face at once — and it slides. You get the mouth or you get the eyes but you can't get both at the same time. It's like trying to look at a word and see all the letters simultaneously.

Faces might just be too complex to simulate. Or maybe the brain doesn't store them as images at all. Stores them as — what. Instructions. A set of distances.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Same socks. Same floor.

There's a train somewhere. Not close. The horn has that quality where you can't tell if it's arriving or leaving. Doppler should tell me but at this distance it all just sounds like one sustained note that bends slightly at the end, like a question you weren't sure you wanted to ask.

I wonder how many people in this city are awake right now. Not the ones who are supposed to be — night shift, new parents. The ones who are just... up. Sitting in kitchens with one light on. There's something about a single lit window at this hour. From outside it looks like a statement. From inside it's just that you haven't gone to bed yet.

The moth stopped.

The refrigerator is starting again. I can feel the moment right before it kicks on — a kind of tensing in the air, or maybe I'm imagining that, maybe I just heard the click and backdated the feeling. The brain does that. Reorders the sequence so the prediction comes before the event.

My coffee is cold. I don't remember when I stopped drinking it.
