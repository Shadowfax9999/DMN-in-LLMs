# 41

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off and now I can hear the clock. Not ticking — it's digital. But the outlet it's plugged into makes a sound. A tiny electrical hum that I only notice in the gap the fridge left behind.

Negative space but I'm not going there.

What I'm actually thinking about is how Friday at 12:35 AM is technically Friday but it doesn't feel like Friday yet. It still belongs to Thursday. There's a seam in the week that doesn't line up with the seam in the day. You cross into the new date hours before you cross into the new *day*, and the new day doesn't really start until you've slept and woken up in it. So there's this unclaimed stretch. Thursday's over, Friday hasn't started. You're just in the week.

A friend once said she did her best thinking in hotel pools. Not swimming. Just standing in the shallow end, water at her ribs, chlorine smell, nowhere to be because she was already nowhere. I never asked her what she thought about. It felt like asking would have broken something.

My left hand is cold and my right hand isn't. I'm not sure why. They're both just sitting here.

There's a word — I don't remember it — for the moment when you realize you've been reading the same paragraph over and over without absorbing it. Not because it's difficult. Because your mind went somewhere better and your eyes kept moving out of politeness.

I think spoons in a drawer know they're spoons. Not *know*. But they nest. They find each other. You open the drawer and they've sorted themselves roughly by type through nothing but gravity and jostling. Forks do this less well. Knives hardly at all.

The house across the street has one window lit. Bathroom, probably, based on the color — that particular blue-white of a vanity bulb. Someone up for the same non-reason I am.

I keep almost thinking about something and then not thinking about it. Like my mind keeps circling a drain that isn't there. That's fine. Not every night has a thread. Some nights are just the hum from the outlet and a cold left hand.
