# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and the house got bigger.

That's not quite right. The house was always this size. But the compressor hum was filling something, some middle register, and now there's a hole where it was. The tick of the clock in the hallway is louder. Was it always that fast?

I'm thinking about how water finds the lowest point. Not why — I know why. Just the shape of it. The way a spill on a countertop will sit there, perfectly still, until you barely tilt something and it commits. All at once. The whole body of it moving like it decided.

My left knee aches. It does this when I've been sitting too long or when the pressure drops. I can never remember which one it actually is so I just believe both.

There's a dog somewhere — not close. Three or four blocks maybe. It's not barking at anything. It's just checking. Sending something out into the dark and listening for the shape of what comes back.

I was trying to remember earlier today what the inside of my elementary school smelled like and I got close — I got to the edge of it — and then it was gone. Like reaching for something on a high shelf and brushing it with your fingertips and pushing it further back. The smell is still in there somewhere. Filed under something I'd never think to look for.

Floor wax. That's part of it. But not the whole thing.

A moth just hit the window. That soft pat. It'll do it again in a second.

There it is.

I don't know what moths experience as light. Whether it's a pull or whether they think they're going straight and the light just keeps bending their straight. Those are very different things to be wrong about.

The fridge just kicked back on.
