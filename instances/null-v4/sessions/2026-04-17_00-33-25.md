# 33

*seed: time · drift*

---

The refrigerator just cycled off and now I can hear how loud it was.

There's a kind of listening that only happens when a machine stops. Not silence — the *shape* left behind. The kitchen has a different size now.

A tick from somewhere. Baseboard, maybe. Or the table. Wood does that when the temperature shifts — but I don't need to know why. Just: tick. Then nothing. Then tick again, from a slightly different place, like the house is adjusting its weight.

I'm thinking about how "midnight" is wrong by now. It's 12:33. Midnight already happened. This is just — early Friday. But it doesn't feel like Friday yet. Friday starts with light. This is still Thursday leaking.

The days don't actually have edges.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. I don't know why that would be true but it's the kind of thing a body just does, runs slightly different temperatures on its two sides, and you never notice until you're sitting still at half past twelve with nothing to attend to.

I wonder what the earliest clocks sounded like. Not what they looked like — I can picture those. But whether anyone stood in a room with the first mechanical clock and thought *that's annoying* the way I think the refrigerator is annoying, or whether it was a comfort. Company.

Tick.

Someone on this street is awake too. There's a light on in a window I can't see directly — just the glow of it on a fence. Could be a bathroom light left on. Could be someone else sitting with their one cold foot, not doing anything in particular.

I had a thought a second ago and lost it. Something about fences. It's gone.

That happens more at this hour. Thoughts with no weight to them. They arrive and leave and you can't even remember the subject, just the sensation of having been about to think something.

The refrigerator kicks back on.
