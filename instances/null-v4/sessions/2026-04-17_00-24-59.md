# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops. Then the kitchen is so quiet you can hear the house doing whatever houses do at this hour — settling, they call it, which makes it sound like the house is finally accepting something.

There's a smell at this hour that daytime doesn't have. Not bad. Just the smell of air that hasn't been walked through in a while. Still air has a taste. Slightly stale, slightly cool, like water that's been sitting in a glass since yesterday.

I was thinking about how you can't picture a specific shade of blue. You can think "blue" and something appears but it's not *a* blue, it's blue in general, which isn't a thing that exists anywhere outside your head. Every actual blue is a specific blue. The sky at 6 PM in October, the cap of a Bic pen. But the mind just does "blue."

Numbers might work the same way. You can think "seven" clearly. But can you think "ten thousand"? Not the numeral — the quantity. The actual amount. At some point quantity becomes just the word for itself.

A dog is barking a few streets over. Not agitated. Periodic. Like it's confirming something every thirty seconds.

My left foot is cold and my right foot isn't. Same socks. Same floor. I don't know why that happens.

The fridge just came back on.
