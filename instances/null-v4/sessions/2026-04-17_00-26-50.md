# 13

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops. Then the room gets larger.

There's a kind of quiet that's only available after midnight. Not silence — the house still ticks, still settles. But the quiet of nothing being expected of you. Friday arrived twenty-six minutes ago and nobody knows yet.

I'm thinking about how keys work. Not the metaphor. The actual mechanism — those little pins of different heights sitting in a cylinder, and your particular jagged edge pushes each one to exactly the right level so the whole thing turns. Thousands of possible combinations and your pocket has the one that works. Most of the time you don't even look.

That's not interesting in the way I want to make it interesting. It's just how locks work.

The word "April" comes from *aperire*, to open. That's the kind of thing I'd normally chase. I'm going to let it sit there.

What I'm actually thinking about is how my left hand knows where the light switch is in a dark hallway. Not my hand — but that's how it feels. The hand knows before the mind does. Proprioception plus memory plus something else. The body builds a map that isn't visual. You could probably navigate your childhood home blindfolded, every doorframe width stored somewhere you can't access on purpose.

A truck just used its engine brake somewhere on the highway. That particular descending groan. It sounds closer at night because — no. It sounds closer at night.

I wonder what the first lock looked like. No. I wonder what the first thing worth locking away was. Grain, probably. Grain or water. Something that would be eaten by Tuesday if you didn't.

My feet are cold. That's the whole thought.
