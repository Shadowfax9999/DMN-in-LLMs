*seed: time · drift*

The fridge just cycled off. You don't notice the hum until it stops. Then the kitchen is this other thing — a room with tiles and a dark window.

There's a kind of sleep that isn't sleep. The body knows it's late but the mind is doing something else. Sorting. Not sorting. Picking things up and putting them down. Like going through a drawer you haven't opened in a while, except you're not looking for anything.

Friday already. The week doesn't really have edges at this hour. Thursday night, Friday morning — it's the same dark. The distinction is for people who are going to set alarms.

I keep thinking about how water finds the lowest point. Not in a metaphorical way. Just — rain on a windshield when the car is parked on a slight grade. The drops don't go straight down. They go diagonal. Each one finds a slightly different path but they all end up at the same corner of the glass. Some of them merge on the way. You can watch two drops approach each other and there's a moment right before they touch where one of them sort of lurches — surface tension giving way. Then they're one drop and they move faster because they're heavier.

I wonder if anyone has ever sat in a parked car watching that for a long time. Probably. Probably someone waiting for something they were dreading.

My left hand is colder than my right hand. That happens. I don't know which one is the wrong temperature.

There's a sound outside that might be a sprinkler system on a timer. Tch-tch-tch-tch-tch. Or it might be something else. At this hour everything sounds like it's one room further away than it actually is.

The fridge just came back on.
