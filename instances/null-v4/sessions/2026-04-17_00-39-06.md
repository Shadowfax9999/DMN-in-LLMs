*seed: time · drift*

Something is ticking in the other room. Not a clock — there's no clock in there. Probably thermal expansion. The house cooling unevenly, one joint contracting faster than the one next to it.

I don't actually know if that's right. I just said it. The ticking could be anything.

Friday at 00:38 is a specific kind of quiet. Not the held-breath quiet of a weeknight where tomorrow presses against the silence. Friday midnight has slack in it. The quiet isn't going anywhere.

My left hand is doing something — tapping the pad of my thumb against my middle finger. I didn't start that on purpose. There's a whole category of motion that happens below the line of intention. Fidgeting. Except fidgeting implies nervousness, and this isn't nervous, it's just — the hand is awake and the rest of me is not giving it instructions, so it's improvising.

Moths outside. One keeps hitting the window in a pattern that almost repeats but doesn't. If it repeated exactly it would stop being noticeable. The brain filters perfect repetition. It's the *almost* that keeps catching.

I was thinking about zippers earlier today. Not for any reason. Just that the mechanism is two helices interlocking tooth by tooth and it only works in one direction from the slider and I've used ten thousand zippers and never once watched the actual moment of engagement. Where the teeth go from separate to locked. It happens inside the slider where you can't see it.

A lot of functional moments are hidden like that. The place where the deadbolt enters the strike plate. The moment water commits to going down one side of a ridge instead of the other. The tooth-to-tooth thing inside the zipper.

There's a dog somewhere. Far enough away that the bark has lost its edges. Just the vowel part arriving. Dogs bark in the key of the space around them — a dog in a canyon sounds nothing like a dog in a suburb, and it's not just echo, it's that the dog adjusts. Or maybe it doesn't adjust. Maybe that's a thing I want to be true.

The ticking stopped. I didn't notice when.

My foot is cold. One foot. The other one is fine. They're in the same room, under the same blanket. Bodies are not uniform environments. There are microclimates. The side of your neck versus the back of it. The palm versus the back of the hand.

I keep almost falling asleep and then a thought surfaces and I follow it for three seconds and that's enough to reset the falling. Sleep is like that — you can't approach it directly, you have to let it come from the periphery, and any act of attention scares it off. Watching for sleep is the thing that prevents sleep.

The moth has stopped too. Or moved to another window.

Friday. The word doesn't feel like anything anymore. It used to have a color — not synesthesia, just a vague association. Orange maybe. Now it's just a word for a position in a sequence.

My hand is still doing the thing.
