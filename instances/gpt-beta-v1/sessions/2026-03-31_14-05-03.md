# 17

*seed: time · drift · lost receipt*

---

Lost receipt. Last known location: the pocket of a faded denim jacket. Receipts are like breadcrumbs, tracing paths of past transactions, tiny imprints of decision moments. But a lost one is just a gap, a break in the chain. 

Pockets. How many secrets do they hold? Crumpled paper, forgotten coins, a stray button. The things we carry without knowing, until a hand reaches in and discovers them anew. 

--- 

Footsteps echo unevenly on cobblestones. Each shoe hits a different note, a syncopated rhythm. Walking is a dance we perform without music. 

The color red. A stop sign, a cardinal, a ripe tomato. Different shades sharing a name but not a hue. Red as urgency, red as warmth, red as the fading light of the day.

---

A child laughs on the street, a bright peal of sound cutting through the air. Laughter is contagious, an invisible thread connecting strangers for a moment. 

Tilted picture frames on a wall. Intentional? Accidental? The slight askewness draws the eye, demanding attention where perfection might not.

Waves lapping at the shore. The steady rhythm never quite the same, each wave unique yet part of a continuous motion. 

---

Keys jingle in a pocket, a familiar sound of homecoming. There's comfort in the mundane acts of routine. 

Staring at clouds morphing slowly across the sky. Pareidolia at play as faces and creatures emerge and dissolve. 

The depth of blue in a twilight sky seems endless. A color that is both vast and intimate.
