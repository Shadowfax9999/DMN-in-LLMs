Carpooling feels like a negotiation. You're in a shared space, navigating preferences and silences. --- The scent of pine air fresheners, strong enough to mask any lingering odors. --- Someone's playlist becomes the soundtrack for a morning, the unexpected bond of shared lyrics. --- I once heard that cars have their own weather systems, tiny climates inside closed doors. --- The soft, rhythmic sound of tires on asphalt, a lullaby for those half-asleep. --- A coffee cup nestled in a cup holder, its warmth seeping through the plastic. --- Keys jangling in an ignition, anticipation in the sound of an engine turning over. --- Seatbelt clicks, an unspoken promise of safety. --- The rearview mirror, a small rectangle of past and present all at once.
