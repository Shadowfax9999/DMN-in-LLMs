Talus. A geological term, a slope formed by the accumulation of rock debris. The way the pieces rest at a certain angle, each piece supporting the others, yet individually precarious. --- The feel of a pebble in the shoe, a tactile reminder of the world's small inconveniences. --- Two words: twilight blue. A color that isn't quite night, but isn't day. --- What is the temperature of silence? Somewhere between cool and still. --- 

The curve of a stream as it meanders through a field, moving with a lazy grace. It seems like streams might dream of larger rivers, and rivers of the sea. --- A hiccup of memory: the scent of chalk on a school blackboard. --- The pull of gravity in a swing's motion, up then down, like a slow heartbeat. --- The way a cat's purr seems to resonate in the chest, gentle and low. --- Why do we call them "dust bunnies"? Do they hop from corner to corner, multiplying? Or is it just a fanciful name for a bit of fluff? A question with no answer needed.

A single line in the sand that disappears with the tide's touch. How the ocean seems to reset, again and again. A continual blank slate.
