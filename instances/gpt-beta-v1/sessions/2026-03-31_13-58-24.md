# 15

*seed: time · drift · median strip*

---

Median strip.  

Median strip. Median — why does it sound like a middling resolve, a compromise, or a buffer? Retaining order, dividing the chaos of opposing sides that rush, hurry, speed by. Inanimate arbiter of right and left, guiding lanes like an invisible conductor.  

Grass sometimes, or concrete, a few wildflowers daring to grow amidst leaf litter and gravel. Shimmer of heat waves above it.  

It separates, but does it also connect? A pause in the flow, perhaps, like a breath held between two words spoken.  

---

The hum of tires over asphalt. A sudden change in texture as a vehicle crosses a bridge. The way wind chimes sound in the distance on a windy day.  

---

Blinds casting striped shadows across a room. The reflection of sunlight on water, dazzling and elusive. The coolness of a marble surface on a summer day.  

Median strip again — a curious thought. What’s planted there sometimes: small trees, bushes, even benches in some places. But mostly empty. Plant life often caught in the periphery, seen but not quite noticed.  

---

A key turning in a lock. The faint rustling of leaves in the breeze. A melody that plays in your head unbidden, looping until it fades away.  

---

Median strip once more — dividing but also present, a constant even when eyes move elsewhere.
