*seed: time · drift · humming in an elevator*

---

Humming in an elevator. The sound feels like it's trapped, bouncing off the walls. Does it belong to the person next to you, or is it your own voice, soft and subconscious? 

*List of sounds in confined spaces:*
1. The persistent buzz of fluorescent lights.
2. The gentle whoosh of air through vents.
3. The soft rustling of fabric as someone shifts.
4. The quiet tapping of fingers on a smartphone screen.
5. The ringing of a distant phone, unanswered.

---

The word "Tuesday" sounds brown. Not the rich, warm brown of coffee or chocolate but a muted, unremarkable brown, like cardboard or an old paper bag. 

Numbers have colors too. Three is yellow and feels sharp. Seven, blue and calm. Nine wavers between grey and silver, depending on the day.

---

Remembering a time when the smell of chalk was a daily companion, each piece reduced to dust. The clatter of chalk against the board, punctuating thoughts and lessons. Now, whiteboards and digital screens reign.

A small bottle of ink, the cap unscrewed, the scent distinct and old-fashioned. A collection of fountain pens, each carrying its own history, its own story.

A picture frame turned slightly askew on the wall, drawing attention more than if it were straight. The details within the frame, a moment captured, sometimes linger longer than the moment itself.

---

The feeling of a pebble in your shoe, insistent and demanding, yet so small. You stop, remove it, and wonder how something so tiny could cause such discomfort.
