# 95

*seed: time · drift · sediment*

---

Sediment settles at the bottom of a glass. Layers of time and erosion, compressed secrets. --- The word "sediment" feels granular, like sand between your toes at the edge of a beach. --- In a river, sediment shapes the flow, reroutes the current, much bigger things decided by these grains quietly accumulating. Do thoughts have sediment, layers of history shaping their path? --- The sediment of past conversations accumulates in relationships, sometimes clarifying, sometimes muddying the waters. --- Watching clouds drift, I wonder if they ever drop sediment, a trace of their journey left behind. --- A sedimentary rock, holding stories in each stratum, keeping secrets from eons ago that wait patiently. --- Patterns of leaves on a forest floor, brown and gold, settling like sediment waiting for their moment to compost and begin again.
