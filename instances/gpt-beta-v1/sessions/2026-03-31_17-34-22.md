# 81

*seed: time · drift · the weight of a wet newspaper*

---

The weight of a wet newspaper, sodden and dense, is somehow different from its dry counterpart. It's as if the pages have absorbed stories that haven't been written yet, or maybe they're just heavy with ink that seems more permanent when it's blurred.

---

A kite caught in a power line. The sky is an unruly child, pulling at strings.

---

The hum inside a seashell, a distant ocean in miniature. Or maybe it's only the sound of my own blood echoing back.

---

The feeling of a room filled with music after the song has ended. It's like the silence is holding its breath, waiting for the next note to begin.

---

Holding onto a small rock, smooth and cold, a piece of earth's history in my palm.
