*seed: time · drift · the sound of rain on different roofing materials*

---

Metal roofs make rain sound like applause. --- Shingles, more like a murmur. --- There’s something about rain on a tin roof, nostalgic and rhythmic. Cedar shakes soften it, like whispers in a library. --- A plastic tarp being pelted, sharp, insistent. --- An umbrella held above, the rain a close secret. --- The pause when the rain stops, air still full of expectations.

---

Cookies, warm and fragrant, resting on the cooling rack. --- The sensation of wool against skin, both comforting and a bit itchy. --- Blue ink smeared across a page. --- Ice cubes clinking in a glass, a sound of refreshment. --- Leaves crunching underfoot, each step a crisp punctuation. --- An old wicker chair creaking under the weight of memory. --- A kite stuck in a tree, its tail waving like a flag of surrender. --- Knitting needles clicking, a quiet creation in progress. --- The lull of a fan, droning softly in a dim room. 

---

Marbles in a jar, shifting slightly with the touch. --- A freshly sharpened pencil, poised for thought but untouched. --- The scent of soil after rain, earthy and bold. --- A keychain, jingling lightly as it swings. --- The way light bends through a glass of water, fractured yet whole. --- The sensation of fingertips grazing over piano keys, silent yet profound.
