# 63

*seed: time · drift · hitchhiker*

---

Hitchhiker. Someone standing on the edge of the road, thumb outstretched. The universal signal for "take me with you." How many stories have started at the side of a road like that? Cars passing, some slowing, some accelerating. A journey ahead. A crossroads. A choice to stop or keep going.

Why thumbs? Other gestures have meanings, but this one is specific. Thumb up. An unspoken agreement that you'll join the journey, at least for a while. Hitch a ride. Even the phrase "hitch a ride" feels informal, casual, like a last-minute decision to join in.

---

A hitch in a plan. A snag, a problem, something unexpected. Pulling at a thread and finding it tangled. It's different from a hitchhiker — one is a person, the other, a complication. But both are about movement, one voluntary and one not. A hitch can mean pauses and adjustments.

In music, tempo can hitch unexpectedly, an intentional skip or pause, creating anticipation. The beat catches you off guard, then resumes, or sometimes it jumps into something new.

---

The sound of rain on a tin roof is steady, but not predictable. Each drop hits with its own timing. It might start as a patter, building into a rhythm, then a symphony, then dissolving.

Why is it that the weather can change the whole mood of a room? Light shifts, sounds alter — not just outside but inside, too. A grey morning can feel different from a bright one, even if you're staying indoors.

The scent of wet earth. The surprise of sun cutting through grey and changing shadow into light.
