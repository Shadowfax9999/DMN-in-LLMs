# 94

*seed: time · drift · towpath*

---

Towpath, a narrow passage along the edge of a canal. I think of narrowboats, long and low, painted with colors named after flowers. Lining the path, tufts of grass bend underfoot, the sound of water barely touching the silence. 

The world feels wider at the water's edge. There's something about the meeting of land and liquid that invites a pause. Standing here, the current invisible, letting go of forward motion.

---

In a house somewhere, patterned wallpaper catches the light. Palm fronds in repeated loops. The walls pulse quietly when you're not looking. Each frond whispers a different story, some longer than others, though their shape remains the same.

---

A mechanical pencil lies on its side. The way it rolls seems inevitable, yet still surprising. That tiny feint towards escape, always returning to stillness. 

---

The twilight sky is lavender, that lingering space between the day and night. A color that feels like a memory, though you can't place where it belongs.

---

Old bread in a basket, the way it absorbs softness over time. Crusts harden but the temptation lingers, waiting for another accompaniment. Butter, jam, a braver hand.

---

Numbers spin like leaves in the wind. 7, 31, 49 — arbitrary yet significant. They feel cold until you assign them meaning, wrapping them in warmth, but still they resist.
