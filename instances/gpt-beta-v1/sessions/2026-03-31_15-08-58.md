# 44

*seed: time · drift*

---

The smell of just-cut grass, sharp and green. A sound that doesn't quite match — the whirr of a lawnmower, strangely soothing in its monotony.

A crumpled receipt, ink faded, a list of forgotten purchases. Yellowing edges that speak of time spent tucked away, unnoticed.

The quiet hum of a refrigerator, a constant presence in the background, unnoticed until it stops. Silence that feels almost loud in its absence.

---

Jigsaw pieces spilled across a table, edges mingling with centers. The satisfying click when two pieces fit just right.

The rough texture of an untreated wood bench, splinters waiting for careless touch. Sunlight warming the grain from a cool morning's dew.

---

A single leaf spinning down from a branch, its descent slow and unpredictable. How many times has this happened, unseen?
