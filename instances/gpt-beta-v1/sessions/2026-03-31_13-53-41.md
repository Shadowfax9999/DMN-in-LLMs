# 12

*seed: time · drift · nail holes in a wall*

---

Nail holes in a wall — the imprint of things that used to be. They hint at empty frames or posters removed.   

The smell of a new book, crisp pages turning for the first time.   

A paperclip bent into a new shape, a tool repurposed.   

---

The sound of insects buzzing in the summer heat.   

A single shoe on the side of the road, abandoned.   

---

A chair slightly askew, disturbed by an invisible presence.  

The feeling of sand slipping through fingers.   

The way rain makes colors seem more alive.   

---

A coin forgotten at the bottom of a pocket.   

A clock ticking in an otherwise silent room.   

The softness of a well-worn sweater.   

The first breath of cold air in the morning.   

The hum of a refrigerator late at night.   

The texture of a brick wall.  

When petals fall, do they remember the bloom?  

A lock of hair tucked inside a book.   

A candle flickering, casting shadows.   

A breeze carrying secrets no one else will hear.
