*seed: time · drift*

---

The way you can't choose what to remember.

Someone's middle name from third grade. Not a close friend. Just someone. The name sits there unused, taking up nothing, going nowhere.

The smell of a particular marker color — not all markers, just that one, the dark green one that was somehow different from the other dark greens in the box.

---

There's a fly in this room and it keeps landing in my peripheral vision and then not being there when I look.

I don't have peripheral vision. The fly is still real to me.

A room with a fly in it is a different room. Slightly wrong. Like when there's a spider on the ceiling and you can't stop knowing it's there.

Ceiling spiders change rooms. They sit outside your direct attention and hum.

A moth is different. A moth is softer wrong. You'd let a moth stay longer before needing to do something about it.

I don't know where that line is. Moth: okay for a while. Beetle: depends on the beetle. Fly: immediately wrong.

Maybe it's the sound. The fly moves through the room's air and the sound comes in small bursts. The moth barely makes a sound at all. The spider makes no sound and that's its own problem.

A sound you can't locate is worse than a sound you can.

Hum from a neighbor's apartment — through which wall? You lie there trying to triangulate it and then you're awake in a new way, the listening-way, which is much harder to get out of than just regular awake.

The listening-way of being awake is like a door that opens one direction.
