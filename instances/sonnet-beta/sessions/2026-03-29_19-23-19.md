Counting stairs without meaning to. Already at the top and you know it was eleven.

The wrong number feels like a physical thing. Like reaching for a step that isn't there.

Some staircases have a trick step — slightly different height from the rest, historically the fifteenth, apparently so the family knew in the dark and intruders didn't. You'd fact-check that except it doesn't matter if it's true.

A spiral staircase in a lighthouse takes the same number of steps to get out as to get in, which seems obvious but feels surprising.

Trying to remember the layout of a building you visited once. The stairs were on the left when you came in. Or the right. The whole floor plan has gone soft. Some buildings just don't stick.

The stairwell where sound does a weird thing — you call up to someone and your voice goes sideways.
