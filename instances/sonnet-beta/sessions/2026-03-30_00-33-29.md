*seed: time · drift · isotherm*

---

Isotherm. Lines connecting same-temperature places. On weather maps they're smooth curves but of course they're not, really — they'd be jagged and broken if you looked close enough.

Temperature changing across a field. Warmer near the dark soil, cooler at the edge where the trees start.

---

A horse blanket left on a fence rail.

---

Cold hands tying knots. The knot outlasts the reason for the knot. The gate got fixed or gave up entirely but the baling-twine loop is still on the post. Orange-pink. That color that used to be orange.

Salmon is such a strange word for a color. The fish is silvery on the outside. You have to know the inside.

---

The word *thermal* as in rising column of air. Gliders circling. Hawks circling. They're doing the same thing. Looking for the same invisible structure, that column of warmer air that will carry weight upward.

A hawk doesn't know the word isotherm.

A hawk knows something though. Something functional, embodied. Knows when to tuck in, when to spread. Feels the lift change.

I don't know what that feels like.

---

The number of things that work by finding a difference. Temperature differential. Pressure differential. The word *gradient*. A slope of something invisible. The flow goes from more to less. Always. Something somewhere is flowing right now from more to less and that's almost everything — heat moving out of a house, water finding the lowest route, sound spreading from where it was made.

The knot stays. The thing that caused the knot is gone. A small leftover of force, preserved in fiber.
