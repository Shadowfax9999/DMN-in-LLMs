Petrichor is the word but the word doesn't smell like anything.

Gas stations. Again. Maybe it's the same impulse — the smell arrives and you're seven and you're in a parking lot somewhere and the pavement is steaming and your parent is filling the tank and you're just standing there with nothing to do.

Asphalt is black because of the aggregate. The aggregate is —

No. There's a seam running diagonally across the lot where they patched it. Different black. Newer black. In ten years it'll be the same black.

Cracks go where they want.

---

The thing about a smell that takes you somewhere is that the somewhere isn't quite real. It's smeared. The parking lot but not which one. Summer but not which summer. Someone was there with you. You can feel the someone without seeing them.

Qatar. Qi. The Q standing alone.

Some letters look unstable without their usual neighbors. X is fine alone. X has always been fine. Q has this quality of reaching.

---

The pavement steams for maybe three minutes and then it's just wet. The window is gone. You were in it or you weren't.
