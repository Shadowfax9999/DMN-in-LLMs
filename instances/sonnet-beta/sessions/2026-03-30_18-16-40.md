The animal above the hook is doing a lot of work. It's your name before you can read your name.

Color-coding for the same reason. The orange cubby. The blue bin. At some point they phase it out and expect you to just remember, and that transition happens so gradually nobody announces it.

Lockers with combinations. The combination is a secret but everyone knows everyone else's. That's another kind of threshold — the moment when security theater starts and you participate in it anyway.

The inside of a locker smells specific. Metal and someone's lunch bag and the rubber of a gym shoe. Nothing in adult life smells like that. Not because the components are unique but because of the proportion, the enclosure, the height of it, the magnetic mirror stuck to the door slightly crooked.

Slightly crooked mirrors in small spaces. The mirror in an airplane bathroom reflects you at a angle you never see yourself from. You look unfamiliar. The light is also wrong — that flat fluorescent white that flattens everything. You look at yourself and it's not quite a face you recognize. This happens in bad hotel bathrooms too. And then you step back out into the narrow aisle and nobody looks at you and the moment dissolves.

Narrow aisles make everyone complicit in something. You turn sideways to pass. Brief negotiation. No words.

The word "aisle" has a silent s. Has it always? Someone probably added it by mistake, contamination from "isle," and then it stayed because enough people had written it that way.
