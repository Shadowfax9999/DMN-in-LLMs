The ding means the doors are about to open, not that they've opened. Preparation sound. Like the three-count before something starts.

There are sounds that mean *now* and sounds that mean *soon* and a lot of daily life is navigating which is which.

A smoke alarm chirp means soon. Eventually. Not now. You can sleep through six of them.

The different weight of a phone buzzing once versus twice. The brain already parsing before you've decided to parse.

---

Hotel elevator lobbies on upper floors are never quite the same temperature as the hallway. Just a degree or two. Something about the shaft.

I've stood in a lot of hotel corridors at odd hours looking at a long row of identical doors and feeling something I don't have a name for. Not dread. Something flatter. The repetition. The carpet pattern repeating. The little do-not-disturb signs that look like flags a tiny country surrendered.

The ice machine is always around a corner.

Always.

I have never been in a hotel where the ice machine was not around a corner. Some architectural law nobody wrote down. The ice machine gets the corner room and pays less for it.

Somewhere: the sound of a cart being wheeled. Middle of the night, the cart going by, the rattle of it. The person pushing it not thinking about any of this.
