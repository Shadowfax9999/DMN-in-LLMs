# 73

*seed: time · drift · a flag at exactly half mast*

---

The cable makes a sound against the pole when wind hits it. Metal on metal, irregular. You could stand there a long time.

A flag at half mast means someone checked the news this morning and then went outside with a purpose. There's a pulley up there. Most pulleys you never think about and then one day you're really looking at one.

Somewhere a warehouse full of folding tables. Thousands of them. All the same. Waiting for a church to call, a school, a family that's having too many people over. They get unfolded and refolded so many times the hinges go loose. You put a glass on one and it tilts just slightly. Nobody says anything.

The number of things that are slightly tilted that nobody mentions.

A car that pulls left. A door that needs lifting to latch. The way a chair at a certain table is shorter than the others and you always end up in it.

I'm thinking about spackle. A wall that was fixed but you can still tell. In certain light, raking across it. Someone patched something there — a picture hook, a doorknob that caught the drywall too many times. The texture doesn't quite match. It's fine. The room is fine. You notice it every time you're in that room and say nothing.

Barry is a real person somewhere not thinking about his name at all.
