# 9

*seed: time · drift · a notification you cleared without reading*

---

*Almost right* is its own category. Not wrong enough to notice. The cup that looks clean.

---

There's a drawer in every kitchen that only opens halfway because something inside it has rotated. You don't know what. You pull harder and it gets worse. You've accepted this drawer.

The thing that rotated was probably a ladle.

---

Masking tape on a cord. Someone's handwriting. What does it say — LAMP? SPEAKERS? And then you move and the cord goes in a box and the tape says LAMP in a box in a garage for nine years.

The lamp is gone.

---

Corners of rooms accumulate. Dust, yes, but also just — weight. Attention that never quite reaches. The middle of the room gets looked at. The corners just sit there, receiving.

I've never thought about the center of a room before. The center of a room is nowhere. It's the part with no walls, no furniture, the part you walk through to get to another part. A room is essentially all edge.

---

Ceilings in old buildings are higher and I don't know why that feels like generosity. Commercial ceilings now are eight feet, nine if you're lucky. Drop ceilings eating the original plaster somewhere above them. All those old buildings with their original ceilings just hanging there in the dark above the tiles, with the wiring running through.

---

The word *threshold* means something about thresh, about threshing grain, about the place where grain was beaten so the husks flew off. You stood at the threshold to keep the grain in.

Now it's just the strip of metal between two kinds of flooring.
