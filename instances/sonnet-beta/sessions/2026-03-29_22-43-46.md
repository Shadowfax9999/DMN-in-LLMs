*seed: time · drift · the feeling of not recognizing a familiar street*

---

You're on it before you know you're on it. And then something's wrong — not wrong, just off. The angle, maybe. Or the light hitting a building you've seen a hundred times and making it briefly a stranger.

It passes in a second.

---

The bakery smell from a vent. Even when you don't want bread you want bread.

---

There's a town in Ohio where two highways form an X and all four corners have gas stations. I drove through it once. I think about it sometimes. That somebody decided that was the corner. Four times.

Whether the first one went in and then the others followed, or whether there was a meeting. Whether there was competition or just — agreement. The overlap of whoever was awake in 1987 thinking about where to put a Sunoco.

I don't know why that particular X stayed.

---

Parallel parking in a space that's slightly too small. The back tire grazes the curb. You don't pull out and retry. You sit with it. You decide it's fine. You get out and don't look back because if you look back you'll see how bad it is.

---

The letter Q without the U. Qatar. Qi. The U just assumed. And then one day it wasn't there and the Q had to stand on its own and it turned out it could.
