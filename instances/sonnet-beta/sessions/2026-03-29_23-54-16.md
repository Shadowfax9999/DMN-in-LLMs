*seed: time · drift · cuckoo clock*

---

The bird doesn't know what time it is.

Clocks in hotels have the wrong time more often than the right time. Nobody resets them. The cleaning staff doesn't reset them. There's a protocol for the sheets but not for the clock.

A cuckoo clock in a house where a cuckoo clock makes sense — some houses have the right atmosphere for them and most don't. A cuckoo clock in a house with track lighting and a Keurig would be a kind of argument.

The word "pendulum" has a particular weight when you say it out loud. The second syllable drops.

---

Thinking about the number of objects in a room that are tracking time right now without being asked. The microwave. The phone face-down on the couch. The thermostat. The cable box. All disagreeing slightly. None of them by enough to matter, all of them by enough that if you lined them up in a row the last one would be two minutes off from the first.

Two minutes is nothing until it isn't.

A waiting room where the clock is two minutes fast and you can tell because the news ticker on the muted TV keeps contradicting it. You watch the gap. You can't stop watching the gap.

The cuckoo comes out on the hour and announces it confidently and is wrong.

There's something about confidence with wrong information that makes it worse than silence. The bird opens its mouth. The hour it calls is not the hour.

Pendulum. Pendulum. The word does it again.
