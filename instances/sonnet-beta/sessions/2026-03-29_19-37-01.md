# 19

*seed: time · drift · a stain on a coffee mug that won't come out*

---

The stain is technically clean. It's been through the dishwasher maybe a hundred times. Whatever was in it is gone. The stain is just the ghost of the tannins having rearranged something in the ceramic, and there's nothing left to wash.

Still feels dirty.

---

Inside a mug is a weird place. You can't really see it straight on. You're always looking at it from slightly above and at an angle. The bottom of a mug is a surface almost nobody sees clearly.

Things with insides that don't match their outsides:

- old thermoses, the inside gone dark
- the underside of a couch cushion
- the back of a painting
- a jacket pocket that's somehow full of grit

---

Grit accumulates. That's not a thought, just a fact. There's always grit at the bottom of a jacket pocket and it's always slightly damp-feeling even when dry.

The word *grit* also means determination and also means a kind of coarse food and also has that sound in it — that hard g, the short i, the t stopping it dead. Sounds like what it is.

*Gravel* is softer sounding than gravel should be.

---

Stones on a driveway after rain. The way they look briefly like they have depth — color — and then dry back to gray.
