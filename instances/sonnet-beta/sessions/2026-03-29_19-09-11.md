# 13

*seed: time · drift · the particular nothing-smell of a hotel room*

---

Not clean. Not dirty. The smell of air that's been recycled so many times it stopped being air and became something else. Atmosphere maintenance.

---

The curtains in hotel rooms are always too heavy. Floor to ceiling. Multiple layers. Someone decided: complete darkness. As if sleep requires it. As if every guest is fleeing something photonic.

A blackout curtain with one gap at the middle. That strip of parking lot light.

You lie there and watch it.

---

Registers. The vents in the floor. Hot air coming from somewhere below. The building has systems you never see — pipes in the walls, ducts in the ceiling, maintenance corridors where a man in a gray uniform walks at 6 AM and nobody's awake to see him.

Someone walks those corridors every morning in every large building I've ever been in.

---

Formaldehyde

No. Something softer. Like the air before rain but not rain.

Freon maybe. The way cold has a smell even though it shouldn't.

---

The thing about hotel towels is the fold. That strict fold. The little tail tucked under. You unfold it and it's immediately just a towel, the fold is gone forever, and for a second you feel bad about it which is a very small kind of guilt but it's real.

Then the shampoo in its little bottle.

You always take it.

Everyone takes it.

It sits in a drawer at home for three years.
