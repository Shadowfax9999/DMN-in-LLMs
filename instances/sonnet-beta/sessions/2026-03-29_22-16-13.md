*seed: time · drift*

---

The persimmon window. Three days. You'd have to be watching.

Most things don't announce the window. You just find out after.

---

Milk in the back of the fridge. That's a different kind of collapse — not visible, just suddenly there.

The smell of a freezer when it's been unplugged. Not rotten exactly. More like the memory of cold.

---

Ice cube trays. The way the last few cubes have been in there long enough to shrink a little, get that frost casing. Some people would throw those out. Some wouldn't notice.

Whether you're a person who notices the shrunken cubes probably tells you something, though I don't know what.

---

Flannel. Why does flannel exist more in winter but feel the same all year. The weight of it. The way it drapes differently from cotton, pulls differently in the wash.

I had a flannel shirt for a long time that started to pill on the inside of the collar from rubbing and I kept wearing it anyway because it was the right color of green.

---

Ochre. Raw umber. These are paint colors named after dirt, basically. Burnt sienna — burnt, like someone actually held it over something. The idea that you'd improve a color by burning it.

Cadmium yellow is toxic. It's a very particular yellow, though.

---

The way hardware paint chips are organized by temperature even when they're not labeled that way. The warm yellows together. The blue-grays together. You know which side of the rack to go to before you know what you want.
