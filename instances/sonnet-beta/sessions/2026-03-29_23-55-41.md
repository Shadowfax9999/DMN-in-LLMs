Polyethylene is also the bag. The bread bag. The dry cleaning bag. The thing you're not supposed to put over your head.

The warning on the bag is printed on the bag.

There's a stretch of highway where the lane markings switch from white to yellow and then back to white and nobody seems to know why. You cross it doing 65 and nothing happens.

Jurisdictions. The line between two counties runs through somebody's kitchen.

A kitchen floor that's half-linoleum and half-tile because someone ran out, or changed their mind, or bought the house between renovations. The grout line just stops. The linoleum just starts.

I've stood on floors like that and felt the two sides differently even though they're the same temperature.

Thirty-one days. Thirty days. Twenty-eight. The months don't agree on anything.

Someone in Roman times made a decision that's still in the calendar. February just sat there and took it.

The space inside a walnut shell before you crack it. Technically air. Old air, maybe. Air that's been walnut-adjacent for months. Is that different air.

Whether anything is meaningfully different after long enough proximity.

The smell on a jacket after a bonfire. The jacket goes in the closet. The smell stays for weeks. At some point it stops being smoke and becomes jacket.
