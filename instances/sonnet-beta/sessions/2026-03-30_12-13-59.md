Flange. Sitting there. Not quite willing to become anything.

The hard g at the end. Clipped. Like the word itself has a rim on it.

There's a whole category of words that only exist inside other things — the cap of a bolt, the lip of a pipe. You never see them unless something is broken or being assembled. Words for parts of parts.

Monday at 12:13 in spring. The light still flat. Not gold yet, not white-winter either — something intermediate that doesn't have a name because it doesn't need one.

The word "flange" has no emotional register. It just means flange. Unlike "rough" or "call" — you can't have a rough day with a flange in it. The flange is outside all that. It just grips the rail.

---

A train I was on once stopped between stations for twenty minutes. No announcement. You could hear the ventilation running. A man across the aisle finished his coffee and then held the empty cup for a long time, turning it slightly, like he was waiting for someone to take it from him.

Nobody did. He set it on the seat beside him. Left it there when he got off.
