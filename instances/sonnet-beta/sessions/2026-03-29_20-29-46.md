The rough ashlar and the perfect ashlar. Rough before, smooth after. But what if the roughness was the point.

Grout lines that aren't straight. Whether that bothers you or not is probably information about you.

---

Somewhere there's a list of words that sound like what they are and a list of words that sound like the opposite and the second list is longer. Flaccid. Hulk. Mellifluous — that one earns it. Moist is on everyone's list. Lush doesn't bother anyone.

---

The feeling of walking into a room where two people just stopped talking. Not suspicious. Just the ordinary trailing edge of a conversation that was nearly over anyway. The sentence that was going to be last is now trapped.

A hinge that doesn't quite seat right, so the door swings open a few inches on its own.

I keep thinking about maps where the edges are marked with coastlines that get vaguer. Not *here be dragons*. Just — the surveyor ran out of certainty and drew what seemed likely. Probable shore.

Spoons nest but forks don't, really. The tines catch.
