# 12

*seed: time · drift · doldrums*

---

Doldrums. The belt around the equator where the wind just stops. Ships stuck for weeks. The actual physical place.

But also just: Tuesday at 2pm.

The word has more geography than the feeling does.

---

The grain direction thing is still bothering me. Not the word — just the fact that there's a right way to lay it, and you can feel when it's wrong, and most people feel it without knowing they feel it. You walk into a room and something is slightly off and you leave and never know why.

Rooms that feel wrong.

A hotel room once where the bed faced the door and I kept waking up. Just that. Faced the door.

The blue window is still lit across the street. Still want it to be an aquarium. A big one, the kind with the bubbler going all night, the filter humming. Fish don't sleep the way things sleep. They just slow down.

The doldrums again. A sailor in 1840 writing a letter that won't get sent for months, if ever. Running out of things to observe. Starting to describe the color of the water very precisely. The way close observation becomes its own activity when there's nothing else to do.

Maybe that's what this is.

The carpet going up the walls six inches. I keep seeing it. The metal strip. The decision to stop exactly there.
