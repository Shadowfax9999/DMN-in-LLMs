*seed: time · drift · vanishing point*

---

Vanishing point. The place where parallel lines agree to meet even though they don't.

Railroad tracks doing it. But also: a hallway, a very long one, the end going slightly darker than the beginning. You can tell yourself the walls stay the same distance apart.

---

There's a specific moment when you're driving and the road tilts over a hill and the road disappears. Just gone. You're driving into nothing for a second. Then the nothing becomes more road.

I've done that and not thought about it. Many times. Just drove into the vanishing point and came out the other side.

---

Peripheral vision has a vanishing point too, roughly. The edge where you stop seeing anything. You can't look at it directly. It moves when you try.

The edge of hearing is like that. A sound getting farther away. At some point you can't tell if you're still hearing it or imagining you're still hearing it.

---

Receding.

That's the word for the tracks, for the road, for the sound. Things recede. The point they recede to is the vanishing point. But the word "recede" also: hairlines. Floodwater. Gums.

The gum line receding from the tooth. That's a kind of vanishing point. The tooth getting longer, technically. Getting revealed.

---

A thing can look like it's growing when it's just the surround shrinking.
