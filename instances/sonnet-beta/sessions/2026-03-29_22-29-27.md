*seed: time · drift · the sound of someone deciding not to say something*

---

The pause that's full versus the pause that's empty. They feel different in the room.

Rooms hold pauses differently. A bathroom holds them tight — everything reflects. A living room with carpet absorbs them. The pause someone makes before answering a question in a kitchen feels different than the same pause in a hallway.

Carpets in hotels are always patterned. Dense, slightly nauseating patterns. Probably so the stains don't show. But also — you're not supposed to look at them. Your eyes are supposed to slide off.

---

Verdant. Who uses verdant. It's one of those words that exists almost entirely in descriptions of Ireland. "Verdant hills." Like the word only gets unlocked in certain latitudes.

Viridian is different. Viridian sounds like a person's name. A character in something.

---

When you're learning to drive and you first merge onto a highway there's this moment where the speed feels completely wrong and then it becomes fine and then you can't remember what wrong felt like. That transition happens in a specific few seconds and you can't go back.

The seam between not-knowing-how and knowing-how. You can't stand in it.

Like riding a bike — the instant before it clicks is gone the instant it clicks.

Locks. The way a combination lock has that little give at the right number, slightly, before you finish. The click isn't just sound, it's resistance changing.

Flannel pills on the collar from rubbing. Something in that — friction as documentation. The shirt keeps a record of the neck that wore it.

Not the neck. The habit. The same angle of turn, over and over, for however many years.
