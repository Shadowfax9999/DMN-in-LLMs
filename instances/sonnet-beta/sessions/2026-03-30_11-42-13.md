# 57

*seed: time · drift · the before-rain smell vs during-rain smell*

---

Before is sharper. During is fuller. They're not versions of the same thing.

---

A billiard ball rolling to a stop takes a long time. Longer than you expect. You keep thinking it's about to stop and it keeps going, slower and slower, and the slowing down has a sound that changes.

Asphalt holds heat and releases it at night. Sidewalks don't do this the same way. There's a temperature difference at the edge where they meet — you can feel it through shoes if you're paying attention, which you're not, which means you've felt it without knowing and filed it as nothing.

Parking decks smell different on each level.

The word *damp* is doing a lot of work. It covers too much range — a damp towel, damp air, damp earth. These have almost nothing in common except the word.

---

Fitting a sheet over the last corner of a mattress. The small violence of it. The corner springs back once, twice. You have to commit.

Things that resist being finished:

- last corner of a fitted sheet
- the last inch of caulk in the tube
- backing out of a parking space with poor sight lines
- the last line of an email

The last line of an email is always bad. You write *Thanks* and hate it. You write *Best* and that's worse. You delete it and there's no ending at all and that feels wrong too. The email just stops. Sometimes you send it that way.

---

Wet concrete has a color that dry concrete doesn't have a name for. It's the same color but darker and more present. It asserts itself.

The smell of rain on concrete specifically — not grass, not soil, not the air before. Concrete. A city smell. Flat and mineral and kind of blunt about it.
