# 52

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth from friction that you only notice after.

A blister you don't feel forming.

---

Somewhere there's a spreadsheet someone built in 2003 that is still running. The formulas reference cells that reference other cells. Nobody knows what it calculates anymore. It just has to stay open.

The coffee ring on the desk that came with the apartment. Rings inside rings. The desk was someone's before it was the apartment's.

Tile grout that's been painted over. You can see the brush marks in it if the light is right. Somebody stood there with a small brush and painted each line.

A drain with a square grate instead of round. The square ones always seem slightly wrong. Like they were put in by someone who was working from a description.

---

The specific weight of a cordless drill when the battery is full versus almost dead. The balance shifts. You can tell before you pull the trigger.

A rubber band that's been on a newspaper long enough to fuse to the plastic sleeve. You try to pull it off and it tears in half.

Monday morning in a building that's mostly empty. The HVAC running for no one. The refrigerator in the break room humming at a slightly different pitch than usual because nobody's opened it.
