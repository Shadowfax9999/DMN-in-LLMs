# 55

*seed: time · drift · the particular blue of a pilot light*

---

Not quite blue. More like the idea of blue before blue commits to anything.

Gas stoves in apartments where the pilot light is always on. The smell is there before you notice the smell. You're already in it.

Basement laundry rooms at 2am. The particular acoustic quality of concrete block. Every sound lands and stays there.

A vending machine hum in an otherwise silent hallway. The way it cycles — not steady, almost steady. You wait for the next shift in pitch without meaning to.

Fluorescent lights in a stairwell. You've been in that stairwell. The steps are a different material than you expected.

---

There's a blue in old Bunsen burner flame too. Science class. Someone always turned the collar wrong and got the yellow smoky version. The yellow was more dramatic but the blue was hotter.

Hotter meaning more energy. That's not what I want to think about.

The color exists at a temperature. That's the actual situation.

---

Copper burns green. Someone's campfire with a piece of wire in it. You didn't put it there. You were just watching the green.

Purple is lithium, I think. Or potassium. One of those elements that seems like it shouldn't be an element.

The periodic table poster in every classroom. Some of the squares yellowed differently than others.
