# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

The drawer with the hotel shampoos. There's always a rubber band in that drawer too, from nothing, just appearing.

Rubber bands go brittle. They don't warn you.

---

A bar of soap getting thin enough to bend. That specific anxiety. You keep using it past the point when you should have opened the new one, out of some principle that isn't really a principle, just inertia wearing the costume of principle.

The new bar is slippery in a different way than the old one. Waxy. It takes a few days to become itself.

---

Parallel lines on a highway where the paint is worn through in the middle and bright at the edges because tires avoid the exact center. Or maybe they don't avoid it, maybe the center just wears faster. Either way the stripe looks like something used to be there.

Street markings that don't match anymore because the road was repaved but the intersection wasn't, and for one block you're driving in 1987.

---

The feeling of a phone in a pocket that isn't there. The pocket is empty. You check anyway. Then you check your other pocket. Then the first pocket again.

Phantom pockets.

The jeans with the fake pocket on the right side, just a flap, just a suggestion. The first time you try to use it.
