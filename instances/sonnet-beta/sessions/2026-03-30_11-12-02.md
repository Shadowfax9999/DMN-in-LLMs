# 53

*seed: time · drift · karst*

---

Karst. The word feels geological and also like a name. Like someone's last name on a mailbox in a small town.

Underground rivers that have no surface. You wouldn't know they were there. The ground above them looks fine.

---

A room where the ceiling is lower on one side than the other and nobody's ever explained why. You just duck slightly when you cross to that side. After a while you don't notice you're doing it.

The specific anxiety of a fitted sheet that's one size too small for the mattress. Getting three corners on and losing the fourth.

Limestone dissolves. That's what karst is. Slowly, over a long time, the rock just goes. And then there are caves, sinkholes, springs that appear in fields with no obvious source. The water comes up because it had nowhere else to go.

A building on a street that's been converted from something else. You can't always tell what it was. The ceiling height is wrong for what it is now. The windows are in the wrong places. Something about the loading-dock configuration.

Rooms that used to be something else still feel like what they were.

---

Full battery versus almost dead. I was thinking about this before. The weight.

A rechargeable flashlight that starts to flicker and you shake it instinctively, like the old kind. The shake doesn't do anything. You knew that.
