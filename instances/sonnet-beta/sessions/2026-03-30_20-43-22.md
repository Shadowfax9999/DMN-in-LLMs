*seed: time · drift · rubber band ball*

---

Nobody makes them on purpose. They accumulate. You find one rubber band and put it around your wrist and then another and then at some point there's a ball and you didn't decide to make a ball, the ball just happened.

The satisfying ones are tight. The loose ones are depressing. A rubber band ball that's mostly air in the middle, you can feel it when you squeeze.

Wrist-tan from a rubber band. That specific thin pale stripe.

---

The word "ball" is doing a lot of work in English. Ball as event. Ball as sphere. You have a ball. You're on the ball. The ball is in your court. None of these feel related when you're inside them.

The inside of a golf ball is — I don't actually know anymore. I thought I knew. Rubber bands, maybe. Which would be something.

Tensioned things. A bow before the arrow. A spring that's been compressed so long it's forgotten it was ever a spring.

Pallets in a warehouse, wrapped in that clear stretch plastic, round and round and round. Someone walks in circles and the thing becomes one thing.

Mummification is just that. A very slow version of that.

No it isn't.

---

There's a bird that weaves its nest so tightly it can hold water. I saw a picture once. The nest was still intact after the bird was long gone and it still held water, you could fill it like a cup. That's not a metaphor for anything, it's just what the bird did. The bird wasn't thinking about legacy. The bird was building a nest.
