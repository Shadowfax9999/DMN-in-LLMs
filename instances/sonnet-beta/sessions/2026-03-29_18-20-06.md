# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

"Nevertheless" has a nevertheless-shaped hole in sentences. You can feel where it goes before you find it.

Some words are load-bearing and some words are decoration. "Albeit" is decoration. "But" is a load-bearing wall.

The word "whilst" in American English feels like wearing someone else's coat.

---

Shoes on a wire. Still. Nobody knows whose they are, or nobody's saying.

The exact sound a basketball makes when it hits the back of the rim — that hollow, wrong, aluminum thunk. Different from the swish. Obviously. But also different from the front-of-the-rim clang. That one's more hostile. The back-of-the-rim is almost gentle. Like it just missed by having too much faith.

The word "nevertheless."

I keep coming back. Something about it carrying its own opposition inside itself. Never-the-less. The negation nested in the affirmation. Or the other way. The less is still in there.

A door that opens the wrong way. You pull and it should push. You know this about the door. You do it every time anyway.
