*seed: time · drift*

Parallel parking — the moment you've committed to the angle and you don't know yet if it'll fit. There's a window of maybe five seconds in there. You're already going backward. It either works or it doesn't and you can't know from here.

Elevator buttons that are already lit when you step in. Someone else's decision, waiting for you. You'd never press it yourself — floor seven, why would you — but now you're going there.

A thought about mortar. Not about who mixed it or how, just: mortar. Gray. The way old mortar crumbles at the corner before the brick does. The brick holds. The mortar gives first. That's the design but it feels backwards.

Corduroy has a direction. Stroke it one way and it darkens, the other way it lightens. Most fabric doesn't do that. Velvet does. Suede. Things that have a grain. You can write your name in suede with your fingernail and it stays there until you brush it out.

Sandpaper grits are backwards — the higher the number, the finer the paper. Someone decided that. 220 is almost smooth. 40 is aggressive. You'd think it would go the other way. You'd think the big number would be the rough one.

It doesn't come up often enough to feel natural.
