# 21

*seed: time · drift · on-ramp*

---

On-ramp. The acceleration lane. You're already going fast before you're officially on.

There's a word for that in music — when you come in before the beat. Anacrusis. Pick-up note. The held breath before the phrase starts.

A lot of things have on-ramps that aren't called that. Sleep has one. The part where you're still aware of your own breathing. Conversations have them — the first thirty seconds where you're both still deciding what kind of conversation this is.

The ramp itself is curved so you can see the traffic before you merge. That's the whole engineering logic: make the curve reveal the gap. You're reading the stream and fitting yourself into it simultaneously.

I keep thinking about the word "merge." It's a polite word for what is actually a negotiation at speed. Nobody stops. Everyone adjusts slightly.

The smell coming through a window from outside when it's warm enough to have the window open but not so warm you need it open. That specific mix. Something cut earlier in the day, dried a little, cooled down.

I don't know what kind of bird makes that sound — the one that goes up twice and then stops. Not a question sound. More like it's marking something.

My knee has been clicking when I go up stairs for about two weeks now. Left one. It doesn't hurt.

The word "cartilage" sounds exactly like what it is. I don't know why.
