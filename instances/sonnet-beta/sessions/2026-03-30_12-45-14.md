# 67

*seed: time · drift*

---

Intermittent fasting. Intermittent windshield wipers. The setting between off and constant — the one that senses the rain and decides for you.

Something about that feels like being managed.

---

A school photograph where everyone is slightly too formal. The shirt collar that was only worn that one day. The background that means nothing — a mottled blue-gray that belongs to no room, no sky.

All the children pressed against the same infinity.

---

The word "puce." What color is puce. Brownish-purple or greenish-yellow depending on who you ask. The word itself looks like it should be a sound a drain makes.

---

Two people on a bench, both on their phones, not talking. This reads as sad from a distance. Up close it might be completely fine. They might be texting each other.

---

There's a kind of cold that's just inside the ankles. Not the whole foot. Just there, just that one hinge of the body deciding to register temperature independently.

---

Driving through a town you went to once for a specific reason. The reason is gone. The town is still there, doing its thing, completely unaware it was ever a destination.
