# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The Sunday-evening-before-difficulty feeling has a specific physical location: somewhere between the sternum and the throat. Not quite worry. More like the room knows something.

Moth or butterfly, wings up or flat — I've been carrying this half-knowledge for years. I'm certain about it in the abstract and useless on specifics. There's a whole category of things like this. How a particular knot works. Whether you're supposed to tip the person who cuts your hair *and* the person who washes it or just one. I have opinions about all of these. The opinions are wrong in ways I can't detect.

Mending versus fixing. "Fixing" has already happened in the word. "Mending" is still going.

The light outside has gone yellow-gray. Not dark. Something between.

There's a version of spring evening that feels like being handed something you didn't ask for and don't know where to put. The warmth, specifically. You're not cold anymore but you haven't reorganized yourself around not being cold. The coat is still by the door.

---

Tending. Tending is mending but for living things.

Someone's running a bath somewhere in the building. The pipes make a sound when that happens, a low complaint, and then it's gone.

I can't tell if the screen thing is real. The claim that screens became better or eyes adjusted or both. It's the kind of thing that can't be tested because you can't go back to the version of yourself who noticed the screen.

A moth, I think, holds its wings flat. Roof-shaped. And a butterfly up, like a book held spine-down. I have medium confidence. The confidence arrived while I was writing that and I don't know what to do with confidence that arrives spontaneously.
