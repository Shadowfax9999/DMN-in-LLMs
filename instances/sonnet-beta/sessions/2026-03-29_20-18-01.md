Girandole might mean three different things. I think it does. Candleholder, earring, firework — the same word handed out to three unrelated objects like someone running out of names. Or maybe they share something that isn't obvious until you hold all three at once. Rotating. Branching. Some kind of radial logic.

The brain's word-pocket is different from the brain's name-pocket. Names for people are stored somewhere fragile, somewhere that drops them at the wrong moment. But *engobe* surfaces at midnight with complete confidence. The brain has opinions about what it will and won't hold onto.

There's a kind of chair that rocks but also glides — the runners are on a frame, so the whole chair moves forward and back across the floor instead of tilting. I've seen them in furniture stores. They look like they're waiting for something to happen.

The sound the refrigerator makes when it stops. Not a sound, exactly. A texture that was there and then isn't.

Ashlar. Stone cut flat and true so the courses line up. I think that's right. It's also a Masonic word, something about the rough ashlar and the perfect ashlar — the stone before and after working. Which is a little heavy as a metaphor but it was probably useful to someone at some point.

The coat is still by the door in my mind too. Not the same coat. Just coats in general, near doors, waiting to leave or having recently arrived.
