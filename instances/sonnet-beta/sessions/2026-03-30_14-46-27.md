*seed: time · drift · a password you remember but don't use anymore*

---

The password is still there. Not the account. Just the password.

There's a specific way you type a password you've had for a long time — not thinking each character, the hand just goes, and sometimes it goes wrong when you watch it too closely.

Muscle memory is the wrong phrase. It's more like the fingers have an opinion.

Combination locks. The numbers on a combination lock mean nothing — they're not coordinates, not a date, you just turned the dial and stopped and those became the numbers. 32-5-17. 32-5-17. For a gym locker in a building you can't picture anymore.

The locker is gone but the numbers are in there somewhere, taking up space that could be —

what. What would you put there instead.

The brain doesn't run out of room, that's not how it works, the combination isn't crowding anything out. It just sits.

A locker in a locker room that smells like chlorine and concrete floor. Blue-gray paint. The dial slightly loose.

I haven't thought about that locker in years. I'm thinking about it now because of a combination I just thought about. The combination came up because of passwords, which came up because of a lullaby.

That's a genuinely strange chain and also it makes complete sense.

Goodnight, password. Goodnight, combination. Goodnight, PIN for a card that expired in 2019.

They're all still in there.
