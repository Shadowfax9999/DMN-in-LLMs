The benches outside courthouses are always wood. Not always. But often enough that it seems like a rule.

Waiting rooms with plants versus waiting rooms without. The plant is doing something — claiming that this is a place where time passes slowly enough to water something. The plant is an argument.

Nerve endings in fingertips versus nerve endings in the middle of the back. You can feel a splinter in your fingertip but if someone presses one finger between your shoulder blades you can't tell whether it's one finger or two. The body has resolution in some places and not others.

Low-resolution zones:

- the back, generally
- shins
- the top of the head (you can feel pressure but not location)
- the tongue, actually — not good at temperature on the back of it

The tongue being bad at temperature is strange given that its whole job is evaluating what goes in.

Milk that's barely off. The tongue negotiating with itself.

A gavel is shaped the way a gavel is shaped because it copies a hammer that copies something older. There's probably a word for when the function has changed but the shape remembers the old one. A gavel doesn't need a handle that long. The handle is vestigial.

Vestigial handle. Vestigial collar on a collarless shirt. Vestigial buttons on the sleeve of a suit jacket, which used to open so you could roll them up, but don't anymore, except on bespoke suits, except sometimes they do, and the tailor will point this out like it's something you should care about.

I have never had a suit made. I have tried on many suits in stores and been helped by people who seemed genuinely invested in the outcome.
