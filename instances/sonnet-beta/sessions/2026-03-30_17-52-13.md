Backwards scales. The Richter scale. Stellar magnitude. The brightest stars have the lowest numbers, sometimes negative. Sirius is -1.46. That should feel wrong but it doesn't anymore, once you've known it long enough.

Wine vintages work forwards. Older is higher. That one makes sense.

The floor of a lake is called the bed. The floor of the ocean too. Everything horizontal that holds something gets called a bed eventually — a river bed, a flower bed, a truck bed. Like lying down is the default state of surfaces.

Asphalt has that same thing as suede, actually. Fresh asphalt in the dark, under streetlights — you can see the direction it was laid. A grain. Then it rains and you can't anymore.

There's a smell that parking garages have on level two that they don't have on level one. Something about the airflow not reaching. Dim concrete smell. Tire rubber that's been sitting.

Coins in a vending machine making different sounds depending on which slot they fall through. The quarter makes a different landing than the dime even before the machine decides what it is. The machine still checks. It doesn't trust the sound.
