# 8

*seed: time · drift · harmonic (guitar)*

---

Touch the string at the 12th fret, barely, and pluck — and it's not the same note at all. Bell-like. Clean in a way the fretted note isn't.

The 7th fret one is slightly eerie. Higher than you expect.

---

There's a shape numbers make sometimes. 3, 7, 12, 19. Harmonic positions. They don't feel evenly spaced even though something about them is.

Actually I don't know if 19 is right.

---

The word *harmonic* also belongs to: overtones, physics, color theory for some reason, the way you describe something going well with something else. *They were in harmony.* Meaning no friction. But harmonics on guitar aren't frictionless — they depend on exactly where you *don't* press, on restraint.

A bell after it's been struck keeps ringing in decreasing rings. You can see it on a spectrogram, probably. All those layers quieting at different speeds.

---

Someone told me once that whales hear in frequencies so low the sound takes an hour to travel between them. I don't know if that's true. It's the kind of fact that might have been assembled out of two real facts and one guess somewhere along the way and then passed around.

Telephone, but for facts.

---

The game telephone, where the message degrades. It always ends funny. The degradation is the point — everyone laughs at the last version and the first version and how far they are from each other. But nobody ever laughs at the third or fourth version, which is where the real drift starts. That middle version where it's almost right.

*The captain wore his purple coat* becomes *the captain warmed his purple throat*.

Something like that. Not quite wrong yet.
