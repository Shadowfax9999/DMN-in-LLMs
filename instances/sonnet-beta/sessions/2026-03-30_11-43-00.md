# 58

*seed: time · drift · spore*

---

Spore. It's a good word for something so small. Has a kind of drama to it.

You don't see spores land. You just eventually see what they became.

---

There's a color that exists only on the underside of things. The underside of a mushroom cap. The underside of a leaf when it's about to rain. The inside of a coat you've had for a long time.

Not the color itself. Just the fact that it's facing down.

---

I keep thinking about whether maps have a front and a back. They do — there's printing on one side and not the other, usually — but it doesn't feel like they should. A map should be the same on both sides. It wants to be a sphere and it can't be.

---

Things that work by accumulation:
- mold
- trust
- a complaint that's been held for a long time
- silt
- the smell of a car

The car smell one is real. It takes months. Then one day it's just there.

---

Rhizome. That's the word that's nearby. Underground stems sending up shoots at intervals. So the plant you're looking at isn't where the plant starts.

A lot of things are like that. That's almost certainly a metaphor arriving. Skip it.

---

The word "propagate" is trying to be about ideas, about light, about plants, about radio waves, all at the same time. It's overworked.

---

There's a fern in a doctor's office somewhere that nobody has watered since before I can calculate. It's plastic and it's fine and it will be fine.
