# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

The batteries in the drawer, the ones you're not sure about. Some of them have dates stamped on them that are already past. The date is just a guess anyway.

Tape measure at the bottom of the same drawer probably. The hook at the end is loose. It rattles when you let it retract. Mine has a smear of something yellow on the two-inch mark and I have no idea when that happened.

Knowing a measurement and then walking to the other room and losing it. The number just gone. You have to walk back. This happens every time and you never learn from it.

Carpet tacks. The little row of them along the edge of the stairs where the carpet used to be. Some of them bent, some still standing. Nobody pulls them out. They just stay there.

I'm thinking about how a set of stairs sounds different when you know the house and when you don't. The specific sequence of which ones creak. When you're new somewhere you just hit them randomly. After a while you step around without thinking.

Muscle memory that's actually spatial memory. Your feet remember a floor plan.

The floor of the laundromat has grout lines that are darker in some places, lighter in others, no pattern to it. The machines shake at different intervals. You sit and try not to watch the drum going around and you watch the drum going around.

I don't know what happens to lost socks. Not philosophically — literally, mechanically. Where they go. The drum of the dryer. The seam where the door closes.
