# 93

*seed: time · drift · the weight of an envelope you haven't opened*

---

The way you can tell before you open it. Something about the weight means it's not good news, or something about the crispness means it's official, or the handwriting means it's from someone who only writes letters when something has happened.

But sometimes you're wrong. Sometimes the weight is just a coupon booklet.

The assumption built into weight. The body guessing ahead.

A dog's nose doing this constantly — not guessing, knowing. Walking into a room and the whole history of the room just sitting there in the air.

What it would be like to remember every smell you've ever encountered. I don't think that's how it works even for dogs. Probably more like — pattern recognition than archive. This smells like that. Not: I remember that.

The difference between recognizing and remembering.

A song you know but can't name. You're inside it completely, every note landing right, and the title is gone. That's recognition without retrieval. The experience is intact. Only the label is missing.

Jeopardy makes this seem like the only kind of knowing that counts.

There's a cabinet in the back of a restaurant kitchen that nobody opens because nobody needs to, and inside it are things from the previous owners — a single chopstick, a takeout menu from a place that's also closed now, a rubber band that's gone stiff. Not haunted. Just still.

The rubber band thing — when they go stiff and then you stretch one and it snaps. The snap sounds louder than it should.
