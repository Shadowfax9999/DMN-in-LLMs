# 16

*seed: time · drift · fermata*

---

Fermata. The bird above the note that means: hold this longer than it wants to be held.

Or shorter. You can fermata a rest.

---

The tuba player in a concert has to breathe somewhere and everyone else keeps going. The breath is inaudible but the slight sway is not. Watching an orchestra from the balcony you see all the swaying at different times, like a field.

Gut strings versus steel. The gut ones stretch when it gets humid.

---

Seventeen steps down to the BART platform on Market Street and then the air changes, goes stale and warm, and there's a low hum that's probably electrical and probably constant but you only notice it when the train hasn't come for a while. The hum underneath. Most rooms have one if you wait.

A pause that's longer than the music planned.

Someone in the row ahead of you at a concert who doesn't know when to clap. The silence after a movement, the tension of it, everyone holding, and then the one person. And then everyone, slightly grateful.

The silence after isn't silence. It's all the same breath.

---

Spruce versus maple. The top versus the back. One resonates and one reflects and between them the sound happens — not in either piece of wood but in the air caught between them, in the box that is mostly empty.
