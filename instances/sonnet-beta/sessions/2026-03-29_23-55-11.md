*seed: time · drift · taxonomy*

---

Taxonomy. The impulse to sort before you understand what you're sorting.

Bins at a recycling center. You stand there holding something that is partly cardboard and partly plastic and partly whatever the shiny layer is, and none of the bins are for partly.

---

The shape of a key in your pocket versus the key on the hook. Same key. Different object, somehow.

Whether that's taxonomy's problem or yours.

---

Phylum. The word feels like something you'd cough up. Class. Order. Family. Genus. Species. A whole sentence that means: here is where this thing lives in relation to everything else.

The sentence has to end somewhere.

---

A filing cabinet with one drawer that's just called OTHER. Every office has one.

---

Colors are like this too — somewhere between blue and green there's a place that English just walks past. Other languages stop there. Put up a sign. Call it something.

The color still exists whether anyone stops.

---

I think the shiny layer in cardboard packaging is polyethylene.
