# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

A gross of pencils. That's a real thing people order. You'd have to have a specific kind of job to need 144 pencils at once.

Pencil lead isn't lead. Everyone knows this but the name didn't care.

---

The shape of a pencil sharpener hole. That particular cone. It has a name in geometry but I don't know what it is — not a cone exactly, more like the inside of a cone. A socket. A recess.

The smell.

---

Twelve is from the Old English for "two left over." So eleven and twelve are both remainders. After ten, you have one left, two left, then the teens begin. The whole number system is just ten fingers running out of room and having to improvise.

Roman numerals didn't have a zero. Just kept stacking. MMMCCCXXXIII looks like a fence falling over.

---

Tally marks are base five. Four verticals and a diagonal. The diagonal is the satisfying part. Prisoners scratching them on walls in every movie. You never see anyone scratch the first four and then not make the fifth. The fifth is the point.
