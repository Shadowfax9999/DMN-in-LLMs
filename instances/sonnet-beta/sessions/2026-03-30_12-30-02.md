# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

Bone or plastic. The cheap ones are plastic. The expensive ones are bone — actual bone, from cattle probably, cut into tiny rectangles and shaped.

A nut is also bone or plastic. Also a tiny rectangle. The nut sits at the other end, near the headstock. Two pieces of bone holding six strings. The strings do all the work but the bone keeps them in their lanes.

---

Lane as a word for a road but also a word for a designated strip on a road. Lane as a name. Lane as something you stay in.

A swim lane is literal and then borrowed into office language and then literal again in a pool.

---

The grout between tiles in a public bathroom. White when it was put in, brown after ten years, and you can't tell if it was ever really clean or just new. The grout in very old buildings is sometimes softer, lime-based, and if you pressed on it with a key it would crumble a little.

The tiles in the subway are glazed so hard they survive everything.

---

There's a word — spall — for when concrete flakes off in chunks. The surface just lets go. Usually because water got in and froze.

A sidewalk corner somewhere, lifted by a root, the edge of it already starting to spall where it broke.

---

Broke and broken are the same word in different tenses but they feel like different words. Broke sits there flat. Broken has that long o in the middle, takes up more room.
