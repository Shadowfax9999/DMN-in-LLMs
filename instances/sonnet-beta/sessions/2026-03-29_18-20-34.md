# 3

*seed: time · drift · quilt*

---

Quilt. The weight of it is different from a blanket's weight. A blanket is one thing. A quilt is a count.

Names of quilt patterns:

- Flying Geese
- Bear's Paw
- Tumbling Blocks
- Grandmother's Flower Garden
- Log Cabin
- Wedding Ring

Someone looked at triangles and saw geese. That's the whole thing right there.

"Batting." The inside of a quilt. Also what a cleanup hitter does. Also eyelashes. The word is spread thin across too many things and somehow still works in all of them.

Sleeping under pieces of something that used to be something else. A shirt, a curtain, a dress someone wore to a thing. The quilt is warmer than the sum of the fabric.

---

The word "nevertheless" from before is still in my head. It won't leave. Never-the-less. The "less" is in there surrendering to itself.

Words that contain their own opposition:

- Nevertheless
- Inflammable
- Cleave (to hold together / to split apart — the only word that means its own opposite because it was two different words that got tired and collapsed into each other)

Cleave is actually two words wearing the same coat. Which is different from "whilst" wearing someone else's coat.

The coat thing keeps coming back.

A quilt is a coat that gave up on being a coat.
