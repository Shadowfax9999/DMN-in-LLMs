# 20

*seed: time · drift · flinch*

---

Flinch is a whole-body word for something that happens in less than a second.

The shoulder comes up. The chin goes down. It's a shape the body makes before you decide anything.

---

Something about the word *involuntary* has always seemed unfair, like the body is being accused.

The reflex arc goes through the spine, not the brain. There's a version of you that's already moved before you knew to move.

Splinter on a stair rail. The way your hand reads it before your eyes do.

Braille is fingers reading. Splinter is fingers getting interrupted.

---

Reading with the hand —
the edge of a countertop that's slightly sticky
a hem that's starting to fray
a key that's cut differently from the others, the shoulder of it thicker

The ring of keys that's heavier on one side. How you always know which end the car is on without looking.

---

My grandmother's key ring had a round red tag on it and I don't know why that image is there but it is. Specific red. The kind of red that comes from being handled a long time.

Fading is a kind of recording.

The flinch again. Where did it come from. It doesn't have an etymology I'm reaching for — it just appeared, the way a word sometimes surfaces from nowhere with a specific pressure behind it, like something half-remembered that turns out not to be a memory at all.

Maybe it was the stones drying. The way they flinch back to gray.
