The radius and ulna crossing. The grip feeling its own interior. That double-ness.

There's a kind of touching where you're not sure which hand is the subject.

---

Calluses are just pressure recorded. The palm of someone who rows, or climbs, or plays guitar — that's a map of where the force went. The fingertip calluses on the left hand only. A right-handed guitarist's hands are asymmetrical in a very specific way.

Skin thickening at exactly the place where the string bites down.

The word "bite" for string pressure. That's the right word though.

---

Springs expanding in a mattress. The way a hotel mattress feels slightly wrong — not bad, just not yours. The springs are the same springs. The foam is the same foam probably. But the particular pattern of give, the specific small geography of where it dips and resists — none of that is calibrated to you yet.

The body has a map of the bed. When the map is wrong there's just a low-level wrongness that isn't quite discomfort.

---

Fog on a bathroom mirror clears from the edges inward. I think. Or is it the opposite. I can't actually reconstruct it. I've seen it a hundred times and I cannot summon the direction.

Some things you've seen so many times you've stopped recording them.

---

A coat on a hook. The way a heavy coat just hangs there with a kind of — authority. Taking up more space than it does when you're wearing it. The arms go slack in a way they never do on a person.
