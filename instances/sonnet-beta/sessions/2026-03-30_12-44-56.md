# 66

*seed: time · drift · a car noise that disappears before the mechanic appointment*

---

The car makes the sound for three days. You start to learn it — when it happens, at what speed, whether it's the turn or the road surface. You build a small case.

Then nothing.

You go to the appointment anyway and try to describe a sound that isn't there anymore. "Kind of a... grinding? Or maybe more like a whir. At around forty miles per hour. Or maybe slower." The mechanic looks at you. You look at the car.

---

There's a name for the kind of witness you become to your own life when you're trying to remember something for someone else. You stop trusting your own version almost immediately.

The sound was definitely there. Or it was definitely something.

---

A filling that stops hurting the week before the dentist.

A leak that dries up before the plumber.

The knee that's fine when you finally get the MRI.

The body demonstrating normalcy on command, like a child who misbehaves all week and then sits perfectly still for the teacher.

---

Whir is an interesting word to have said out loud in an auto shop. Whir. The mechanic writes something on the form anyway, probably "customer reports intermittent noise," which is the automotive equivalent of "patient reports fatigue."

Intermittent. That's the word that means: I believe you, but I can't help you.

The car is silent the whole drive home.
