Not wrong, just — you pull and nothing happens, and then you push, and it opens, and every time it's a small humiliation.

There's a particular cabinet in every kitchen that does this. Usually near the corner. Usually the one with the good scissors in it.

---

The scissors question: why does everyone have good scissors and bad scissors. The bad scissors are in a drawer somewhere and they work fine but you'd never choose them. What makes them bad. They're probably identical to the good scissors by any measurable standard.

Actually they're not. The good scissors are slightly heavier. The blades meet differently. You can feel it when you close them.

A handle that's slightly too cold. The way metal doorknobs in winter register before you've fully committed to touching them. Anticipating the cold is different from the cold.

The gap between anticipating and arriving is where most of the feeling lives.
