Standing. The right to bring a claim. You have to have been injured first, or they won't hear you.

Which means you can watch something go wrong and have no standing. Just watching.

---

The word *standing* also means you're upright. As opposed to sitting, lying, crouching. And it means ongoing — a standing invitation. A standing army. Something that persists without being renewed.

Standing water.

---

The number on a hotel room door is at eye level for someone, and too high for someone else, and there's no way around it.

---

A cat's weight while sleeping. The whole cat committed to one spot.

---

Injury in fact. That's the legal phrase — you need an injury in fact, not a hypothetical one. The word "fact" doing a lot of work there. As though some injuries aren't.

---

There are arguments that never get heard because the person making them can't prove they were harmed yet. The harm is coming. Everyone can see it. Still no standing.

The room where the argument would happen stays empty.
