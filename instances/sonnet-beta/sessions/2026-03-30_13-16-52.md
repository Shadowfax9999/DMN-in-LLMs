*seed: time · drift*

---

A filing cabinet that gets moved from office to office because nobody knows what's in it and nobody wants to be the person who opens it.

Packing tape yellowing at the edges. The cardboard underneath is still fine.

---

A bolt that's been painted over so many times the head is almost smooth. The slot for the screwdriver is just a suggestion now. Someone will strip it eventually and then it'll be that person's problem forever.

The weight of a door that's been hung slightly wrong. You don't notice until you let go and it drifts.

Drafts in buildings. Not from windows — from the building breathing. Pressure differentials nobody planned.

---

A hotel carpet pattern that's specifically designed so stains don't show. That's a real discipline. Someone with a degree spent time on that. The pattern looks chaotic but it's controlled chaos, which is different from actual chaos, which a carpet cannot be.

Actually I don't know what actual carpet chaos would look like.

Random fiber direction. No pattern. Just texture.

Velvet does that — runs differently in different directions so it looks lighter from one angle, darker from another. A hand passing over it leaves a trail.

Corduroy has ridges. The word "wale" for the ridges. A wide-wale corduroy is a different thing from a narrow-wale corduroy in a way you can feel more than see.

The jacket my father had in the eighties. I'm not doing that.

Fabric with too much body. Stiff at the collar. Some shirts just stand up slightly when you take them off.
