# 94

*seed: time · drift*

---

Rubber bands go stiff and then they don't exist anymore — there's no in-between where they're just a little broken. Either they work or they snap.

Cap guns were like that. The whole thing was the sound being louder than it should be for what it was — a small paper circle, basically nothing.

Paper can be surprisingly loud. A book dropped flat. The specific crack of a newspaper being shaken into shape, which nobody does anymore because newspapers are mostly gone and the ones that aren't are smaller, thinner, less satisfying to shake.

Things that got thinner:
- newspapers
- phone books (then disappeared entirely)
- wrists, apparently, after a certain age
- the cardboard on the back of legal pads

Legal pads are yellow for a reason someone decided once and now it's just what they are.

Yellow as a default. Yellow taxis, yellow pencils, yellow legal pads, yellow caution tape, yellow school buses. Yellow got picked for visibility and now it means something else — it means official, provisional, temporary, about to be signed.

The inside of a school bus smells the same everywhere. Every school bus, same smell. Green vinyl, diesel faint underneath, something sweet and synthetic. A smell that doesn't remind you of anything except itself.
