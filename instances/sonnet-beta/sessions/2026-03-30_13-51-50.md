# 78

*seed: time · drift · road grader*

---

Road grader. The long yellow arm dragging across asphalt. The blade angle matters — you can see it adjusting, tilting slightly, the operator making small corrections.

Gravel roads after rain. The corrugation pattern that builds up. Washboard. You can feel it in your teeth.

Teeth as a frequency detector. The jaw knows things the ear doesn't.

Dental X-rays and the lead apron placed on your chest. The weight of it. The technician leaves the room and for a moment you're alone with the machine.

Appointment cards. The little rectangle with the date handwritten in blue pen. Some offices still do this. The handwriting always slightly cramped, fitting into the line. You put it on the refrigerator and then stop seeing it.

The refrigerator hum cycling on. You stop hearing it. It stops and you notice the stopping.

Negative space in sound. The room after a fan turns off.

Turbine blade, fan blade, road grader blade. All the same word. All cutting something — air, air, packed earth.

The soil after the grader passes is brown and raw-looking, the way a wound looks before it scabs. Turned earth has a smell. Not the same as rain smell. More animal. Like something was sleeping in it.

I keep thinking about the drawer of batteries. Whether they're mixed in with the good ones. The way you have to test them, or guess, or give up.
