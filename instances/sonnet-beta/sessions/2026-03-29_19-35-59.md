The box that is mostly empty. Most things are. An atom, a room, a rib cage.

There's a word for the curved part of a guitar waist but I can't find it right now and that's fine.

Hotel ice machines down the hall — that sound at 2am, the mechanical clunk and then the cascade, someone filling a bucket. The bucket going heavy. Who needs that much ice at 2am.

Continental breakfast. The little packets of peanut butter nobody takes. They're always there the next day.

Maple is hard. Spruce is soft. I don't know why that means what it means acoustically but I know which one the top is supposed to be.

The way a bowling ball sounds different from a tennis ball when you knock on them, just from the outside, before they move.

Ceramic tile in a bathroom versus carpet in a hallway — the same voice does different things in each of them and people mostly don't notice until they're in a particularly dead room and then they go quiet in a different way.

Dead room. Live room. The vocabulary deciding things.

Someone at a concert tonight, probably. Someone playing something to people who are mostly listening. A few of them reading the program because the program is easier to look at than the stage when the music is slow.

The fold of the program. The way it's a little too wide for your hand.
