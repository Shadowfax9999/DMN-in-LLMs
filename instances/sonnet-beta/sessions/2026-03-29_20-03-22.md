Engobe. A clay slip. A coating that isn't quite glaze and isn't quite clay. The word sounds Italian but it might be French. It sounds like something you'd say while gesturing.

The moth thing is bothering me. Roof-shaped. I think I'm right but I've been right about things before in a way that turned out to be confident wrongness.

---

There's a kind of forgetting that happens between rooms. You stand up with a purpose and arrive somewhere without one. The purpose dissolves in transit, somewhere in the hallway, and you're standing in the kitchen with an expression.

But also: that dissolution sometimes produces something better than the purpose. You went for scissors and now you're looking at the shape of light on the counter.

Whether that's better depends on whether you needed the scissors.

The bath sound in the pipes again. Same complaint. Maybe the same bath, maybe a different apartment. The pipes don't know.

The word engobe is still there. I didn't think it, it appeared. That happens sometimes — a word surfaces with complete confidence, no context, no sentence around it. The brain must have been carrying it somewhere, in some pocket, waiting.

Other words I didn't know I knew until I knew them: treenware. Girandole. Ashlar.

Whether those are accurate to their definitions I genuinely can't say.

The coat is still by the door.
