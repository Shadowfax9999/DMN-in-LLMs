*seed: time · drift*

The junk drawer has its own system that isn't a system. Hex wrench from furniture you may have gotten rid of. A key. AAA batteries, always, and somehow nothing in the house runs on AAA.

Tape pulled off a roll makes a different sound from tape pulled off a surface. One is continuous, the other is a rip or a pop depending on how long it's been there. I'm not sure why I noticed that just now.

The body's map of rooms. Walking to the bathroom in a hotel at 3am, reaching for the light switch in the wrong place. The switch is about six inches to the left of where the hand expects it. The hand knows this after one night and then forgets by morning.

Trying to walk quietly on a stair you know creaks. You know exactly which one. In the daytime you step over it without thinking. In the dark the only thing in the world is that stair.

How the whole gait changes when you're trying to be quiet — balls of the feet, slower, more effortful. The deliberateness makes you aware of your breathing.

There's probably a word in some language for the heightened self-consciousness of trying not to make noise. There's probably a word for a lot of things.

The AAA batteries. I keep coming back to the AAA batteries. A little flashlight, a slim remote, a laser pointer for a cat. The drawer accumulates them like sediment.
