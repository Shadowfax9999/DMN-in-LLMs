Off-peak. The word implies a peak somewhere. A peak you're not at.

Trains at 2pm on a Tuesday. The car where the seats all face the same direction and nobody's sitting in the backward ones.

---

Pricing that changes based on when you want a thing. The thing is identical. The hotel room is the same room.

Shoulder season. That's a good one. The season that exists in the gap between the seasons people want.

Dead mall hours: 10am on a weekday. The janitor and the woman doing laps and the one store that's always open, the one selling phone cases.

The fluorescent hum gets louder when nobody's there to cover it.

---

I think there are times of day that feel parenthetical. Not leading anywhere. 2pm. 11am on a Saturday when you haven't decided what the day is yet.

Off-peak is cheaper because you're the only one who wanted it then.

Or you're the kind of person who can move through time differently. 

The people who eat dinner at 4:30. The people who go to the beach in October. Some of them are just old and some of them know something.

---

A parking lot with four cars in it has a different texture than the same lot with forty. The sound bounces differently. Your footsteps are an event.

You can hear yourself lock the car.
