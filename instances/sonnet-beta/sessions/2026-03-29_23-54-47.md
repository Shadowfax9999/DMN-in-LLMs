Stammer. The sound that's trying to happen and is also in the way of itself.

Not the same as silence. Silence is the space before. A stammer is already in motion — the word has launched and then the word arrives again, and again, and the mouth knows it's repeating but can't stop repeating, which seems like it might be the worst part.

A record skip sounds like a stammer. The needle caught in the groove, the same eighth-note of music returning until someone crosses the room.

A river does something like this around certain rocks. The water pushes and pushes into the same narrow place and eddies back on itself. You could watch it for a long time. No one's frustrated. The water doesn't know.

There's probably a word for the moment it releases. In a stammer, in a river, in a drain that suddenly lets go. The sound of it clearing.

The cuckoo was wrong but at least it committed. A stammer is the opposite — the throat keeps checking, keeps offering a draft, keeps pulling the draft back. Confidence in the wrong direction. Or not confidence at all. The wanting to speak is obvious. That's what makes the room careful.
