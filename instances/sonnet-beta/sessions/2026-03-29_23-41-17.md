# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

The fresh yellow on a speed bump after years of faded yellow. How wrong it looks. Like a tooth.

Cardboard that's gone soft from humidity but hasn't gotten wet. A box of crackers. The corners start to round.

Somewhere there's a power line making a 60hz hum and nobody within a mile of it is thinking about it right now.

A screen door closing. The spring, the slap, then the quieter second slap that's just the door settling.

Whether you can still count the steps between the kitchen and the front door of a place you don't live in anymore. Whether you counted them while you lived there or just walked them until the number was in your legs somehow.

The fresh yellow is still bothering me. Paint that exact shade — that municipal yellow, the yellow of caution, of sawhorse barriers — it doesn't age gracefully. It peels in long strips or fades to almost white. Nothing in between. And when it's new it looks like it's accusing the parking lot of something.
