# 98

*seed: time · drift*

---

The baking dish that's been soaking for three days in the sink. You keep meaning to scrub it. You walk past it. It's just there.

Wool suits in summer. The way someone keeps wearing them because they've always worn them.

There's a parking garage stairwell near a mall I used to go to where the paint on the steps was a very specific hospital green and someone had written SECOND FLOOR on the wall in a font that was trying too hard. I don't know why that stairwell is still in me.

The specific weight of a cordless drill when the battery is almost dead.

Ceiling fans in winter running the wrong direction, which is something you're supposed to know about but don't actually do.

Whether the people in the apartment above you are fighting or just moving furniture. The way you listen for a second and then decide it's furniture.

Avocados that are all wrong at the grocery store — too hard, too soft, nothing in between — and the way you stand there pressing them anyway like something will change if you just press more of them.

The accordion folder that gets bought for organization and lives in a drawer empty. Every drawer has one.
