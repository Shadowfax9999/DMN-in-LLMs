Monsoon. The word has weight at the front of it. Mon-. Like a warning.

A season that has a name instead of just weather.

---

The smell before rain is different from the smell during rain. The during-rain smell is the one everyone knows. The before-rain smell is harder to describe and more useful.

A dog that knows.

---

Certain words in English just borrowed a whole climate. Monsoon. Typhoon. Simoom. Words that arrived with sailors who'd been somewhere the weather had names.

The English word for the regular rain that comes in August: nothing. Just "it rains in August."

---

The gutters fill faster than they can drain. The water finds a seam. It has been finding that seam for fifteen years and the seam is a little more of a seam every time.

---

Flooding that happens slowly enough that you keep thinking it'll stop.

Moving things to higher shelves. Then higher. Then you're standing in six inches and holding a box of photographs and you're not sure where you thought you were going with it.

---

Two-thirds of the world's rice depends on monsoon timing. I read that somewhere. The somewhere of it is completely gone.

---

The word "deluge." How it sounds like it should be French, and it is, and the French got it from Latin, and at some point there was a person who needed a word for more water than there should be and made one.

The thing they were describing is still the thing.
