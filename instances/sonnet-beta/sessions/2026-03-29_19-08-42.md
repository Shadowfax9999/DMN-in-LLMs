Sleeveless. The word is just the absence of the thing. Like boneless. Seedless. All these words that are defined by what got removed.

Carpet in an elevator. That's a specific hotel experience. The carpet goes up the walls a little, or it used to in older ones. Like the carpet wanted to be wallpaper.

I keep thinking about that center table in the waiting room. The magazines are always wrinkled from being held the wrong way, bent back. People read them like paperbacks, one-handed. You can tell which article everyone read first because those pages are more dog-eared.

A dog-ear is a corner folded down. Because it looks like an ear. That's the whole thing — it just looks like an ear. Some resemblances get named and some don't. The ones that get named feel permanent afterward.

The carpet in the elevator went up the walls about six inches, then stopped. There was a metal strip. Everything in an elevator is at a height you can see.

Grain of wood going diagonal is called something. In flooring it's called something specific, some term installers use. I don't know it.

Parquet. No, that's a pattern not a direction.

Herringbone is the chevron pattern. That I know. But the direction the grain runs relative to the room — there's a word for getting that wrong, or for doing it on purpose. I'm pretty sure.

The windows across the street. One is lit up blue. Probably a television. Or a computer. Or an aquarium, but probably not an aquarium. I keep thinking aquarium because I want it to be an aquarium.
