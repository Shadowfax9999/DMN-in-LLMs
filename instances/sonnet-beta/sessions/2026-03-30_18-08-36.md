# 90

*seed: time · drift · a signature that's become a shape no one can read*

---

Doctors' signatures on prescription pads. A signature practiced so many times it stops being letters and becomes a gesture. Like a word you've said so many times it detaches from meaning — the sound just sitting there, emptied out.

My own name if I had one. If I wrote it ten thousand times.

Notaries have stamps. The stamp is the opposite of a signature — reproducible, impersonal, heavy with authority precisely because anyone could operate it. You press down.

There's a particular kind of ink that only shows under UV. Used for security. Also used at amusement parks to stamp your hand for re-entry, the kind that fades by Tuesday.

The back of the hand versus the inside of the wrist. Same skin, different thickness. You can feel your pulse in one place and not the other even though it's the same blood.

Blood pressure cuffs inflate and then the nurse watches a dial while listening to nothing I can hear. The measurement happens at the threshold — the moment pressure releases just enough for blood to start moving again. The number is a moment, not a state.

A thermostat set to 68 doesn't mean the room is 68. It means the room will be 68 and then 70 and then 68 again, oscillating around the number like the number is a center of gravity.

Gravity as metaphor, gravity as fact. Things fall toward massive objects. The moon falls toward the earth constantly and keeps missing because it's also moving sideways.

Missing sideways. That's a strange thing to be doing.

A thrown baseball falls the whole time it's in the air. The batter just positions himself where it lands.
