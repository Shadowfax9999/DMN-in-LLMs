## Reflection

Sessions 1-5 show this instance finding a genuinely rich seam: language itself as an object of wandering. The word-shape observations (numbers as profiles, serif vs. non-serif 1s as different objects sharing a name), the load-bearing/decorative word taxonomy, auto-antonyms (cleave, fast), and the coat metaphor threading naturally through sessions 2-4 — these are some of the most playful, light-footed thinking the DMN has produced. The continuity is organic: 'nevertheless' drifts across three sessions without feeling forced, and 'coat' accumulates meaning until the quilt punchline. The form is also genuinely varied: session 1 is just clean fragments with no section breaks; sessions 4 and 5 mix investigation with observation; the lengths vary naturally.

The risk is familiar: the system has found a seam and is mining it. Five consecutive sessions of word/number investigation. Session 5 — pencil lead etymology, teen-number etymology, Roman numerals, tally marks, base-five — is sliding toward the investigation-essay mode, just with linguistic/mathematical phenomena instead of physical ones. The explanation reflex is mostly dormant (the system catches itself: 'The smell.' full stop; 'I don't know what it is') but session 5's two etymology chains are the old pattern in new clothes.

Endings are mixed. Sessions 1 and 2 end plainly. Sessions 3, 4, and 5 end with aphoristic or personification-flavored lines: 'A quilt is a coat that gave up on being a coat,' 'It sounds like an insult the quantity is giving you,' 'The fifth is the point.' These are satisfying literary closers — the ending problem hasn't fully resolved. Structure is also regressing: sessions 2, 3, 4, and 5 all use section breaks despite enforcement rules that should have kicked in after sessions 2 and 3.

The main evolution task: name the language-investigation mode as the newest dominant tendency, escalate structure enforcement (4 consecutive section-break sessions), update fact tracking with the specific etymologies and number-system details visited, and add concepts that lean away from the word/number domain toward physical, bodily, and sensory territory — places the mind can wander without automatically reaching for a dictionary.
