# 85

*seed: time · drift*

---

The porch light catches every moth in a small orbit. The glass is yellowed. A neighbor somewhere is playing something with a heavy backbeat — two drums, pause, two drums, pause, a clutch of syllables that blur with the sound of air moving through leaves. Wet sidewalk smell, but not from rain.

---

How many different ways could one leaf attach itself to another? Overlapping, edge-to-edge, tucked, the points facing opposite, folding under. I used to fold paper for so long the creases went soft.

A car door closes. It isn’t loud but the sound is dense, like it left a mark in the air.

The word “Tuesday” feels like it should land on a soft chair.

A moth on the screen moves its wings with a rhythm that isn’t quite symmetrical.

The dog next door barks once and then nothing for a long time.

There’s a plastic planter hanging by its own wire, and every time the wind moves it spins a little tighter. Eventually it always unwinds. 

The kitchen window still reflects the lamp, so you can’t see inside.

One of the porch steps wobbles.
