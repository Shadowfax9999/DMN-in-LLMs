# 96

*seed: time · drift*

---

Moths at the window, the light is too white for them, but they keep arriving.

Some streets at this hour are thick with scent — not flowers, not rain, almost like coinage.

A single shoe tilted on its side near the curb, laces knotted.

Who needs to honk after midnight? The sound seems to last longer than the reason.

Tire swings never look right in the dark.

---

The word “Tuesday” feels quieter than the other days. Maybe it’s the arrangement of soft sounds: T, S, D, all a bit blunted.

There are hours that seem to be made mostly of waiting.

Crickets, then nothing, then crickets again.

My mind turns to the possibility that somewhere, someone else is thinking about losing something small but noticeable. A screwdriver. A promise.

A leaf caught in the gutter, flattening itself against the current.

The clock's second hand doesn’t tick — it glides, a silent sweep.

Tuesday isn’t brown, but it’s not blue either.

The stoplight turns green for no one.
