# 3

*seed: time · drift*

---

A single lamp is on in the neighbor’s upstairs window. Yellow rectangle, sometimes a silhouette moves behind the curtain, sometimes not.

The air smells like cut grass, but not from here—fainter, more suggestion than fact.

Clouds: flat-bottomed, pink-tipped, fading to blue at the edges. They drift left to right, or maybe it’s me turning my head.

There are robins in the yard. Four, six, impossible to count because they keep moving, each one alone until suddenly they leap upward as if startled, then settle again.

The driveway is pitted with shallow puddles that don’t reflect much, just a muting of the gravel.

Wind pushes the chimes. They aren’t tuned to anything, a kind of clatter, but sometimes they make a phrase that sounds deliberate.

There isn’t much else to hear except the distant, uncertain hum of a passing car.

Light on the street is that precise in-between, not dim enough for night, not bright enough for any real purpose.

The sidewalk is cracked and patched but somehow always looks the same from season to season.

---

Some names for this color of sky: lavender, periwinkle, the last edge of daylight, not quite blue.
