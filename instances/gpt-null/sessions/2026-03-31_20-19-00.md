# 26

*seed: time · drift*

---

A thin branch, cut and laid on the sidewalk, bark half-stripped so white wood flashes underneath. If you stand over it, you can see faint lines spiraling where the cambium was peeled.

Half a yellow rubber band in the gutter. The other half missing. How do rubber bands die? Usually the sun turns them brittle and they break, but sometimes they just go missing. There's no one watching when it snaps.

—  

The warmth from the bricks on the west-facing wall lingers later than you think, the day held in them a little longer.  
Dandelions at the edge of the curb look like they belong to a different season from the rest of the grass.

The purple shadows down the side street are almost blue at this hour, not quite matching the color in any crayon box.  

A dog barks once, far away. Not again.

The wind carries something that smells like hot pavement cooling.
