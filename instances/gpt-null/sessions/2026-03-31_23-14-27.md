# 86

*seed: time · drift*

---

A cricket is inside the house, or maybe it’s the hallway. The chirp becomes a pattern, or it always was, and just now I’m matching its length with my breath. Odd, this comfort. Knowing which drawer the tape is in, the smell of the brown paper bag cupboard.

There’s a black thread stuck to my sleeve. Not a hair. Thicker, looped tight at one end. I hold it in my palm for a second before letting it fall.

The refrigerator’s hum shifts every minute, a slow cycle between almost silent and almost a whine. I forget it’s part of the room until it isn’t. The overhead light flicks once, just once.

Down the hallway — the plink of water in the bathroom sink. Not a drip, but a quick, brittle note.

Glass with a moth print. Who chose a moth for a drinking glass. The wings are gray and a little smug.

---

A switch labeled “FAN” that doesn’t turn anything on.

I have not opened the window yet.

The street outside is quiet except for the engine idling three houses down. It goes on for a long time and then stops.
