# 80

*seed: time · drift*

---

Underneath the lamplight, the sidewalk glows a sickly yellow. Every so often, a moth circles the bulb, dust-grey flicker, erratic. The grass is damp, but not wet enough to leave footprints.

Some lawns are so precise, edges of turf clipped like they’re part of some enormous surgical procedure. On others, dandelions make constellations, single points of stubborn brightness.

I keep hearing the screen door bang somewhere, then nothing, then a bicycle’s freewheel ticks, fades.

---

The word “Tuesday” doesn’t look like it should make a sound that feels round, but it does. Tooze. It’s the only day of the week that starts with a “T” and doesn’t feel sharp.

What color is Tuesday? I always think navy, but sometimes it’s the color of cheap binders, the kind that scratch a little when you run your finger down the spine.

Soft whirring from somewhere, maybe someone’s AC unit cycling on, but it’s too early in the year for real heat. Maybe they just like it cool.

There’s a faint sweetness in the air that makes me think of honeysuckle but might be a dryer vent.

A single sneaker on the curb, missing its mate. The laces are knotted tight as if someone gave up halfway through untying.
