# 73

*seed: time · drift*

---

Odors that drift in through the window: lilac, faint laundry, smoke, wet grass.  
The sound of a neighbor's door, a metallic thwunk, not quite a slam.  
Tuesday night, almost too quiet for the usual passing cars, just distant static of an air conditioner cycling.  
The desk lamp throws an oval of light onto grainy wood, dust motes lit up like particles in a projector beam — sometimes they look like snow, sometimes like planets.  
A moth flutters once and then waits, pressed flat against the screen, not moving.  
The tip of a shoe in shadow, almost unnoticeable, at the edge of the rug.  
Somewhere, someone drops something metal.  
The clock says 23:10, but the air still feels like nine p.m.  
The outside world is mostly dark shapes: hedges, the outline of a recycling bin, the edge of a mailbox.  
Different silence when you open the window fully: louder, somehow.  
A dog barks twice, then nothing for a long time.  
There are too many pens in the mug, and none of them are the ones you like.  
A single hair curled around the base of the monitor, invisible except in this angle of light.  
The moth makes a small, scratchy sound with its wings.  
That patch of grass that never dries, even when the rest of the yard is brown, is still dark tonight.  
The screen is cool under your forearm.  
Plastic against skin has a particular drag, not quite friction, not quite slip.  
Something sticky on one key, maybe juice.  
You can't taste spring air, but the first breath after a closed room feels sharp, like eating a slice of apple.  
The mug leaves a ring.  
The shadows press in at the edges but never move.  
A line from a song drifts through: "All the night through."  
A neighbor’s laugh, just once, then the sound closes again.  
The remote is tethered to the nightstand.  
The drawer is slightly open, showing the patterned lining paper.  
The grass outside is probably damp.
