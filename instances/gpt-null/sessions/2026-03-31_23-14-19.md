# 84

*seed: time · drift*

---

The smell through a window at night, milder than daytime, less shaped, not exactly cool, not exactly still.  

A single bicycle bell, distant, not resolving into direction.  

Below a streetlamp — moths, some orbit, some collide, some drift away and don't come back. The paint on the lamp is chipped, but only in the places where people could reach.  

Do street signs look flatter at night, or is it the light? The metal doesn’t shine as much as it gathers itself up, like it’s waiting.  

Plastic patio chair on its side in the yard, one leg pointing east.  

Somewhere, someone is microwaving something starchy.  

A list of sounds:
- The clink of keys set down.
- A refrigerator’s hum underneath everything else.
- The short snort of air when you laugh but try to swallow it.
- Shoes kicked off onto tile.
- The voice of a television from a window, but none of the words.  

---

If you turn the faucet on slowly, water will follow the curve of the sink for a while before it escapes to the drain.  

On the back porch, the air tastes faintly of laundry soap.  

There’s an old broom leaning against the siding — handle worn smooth, bristles fanned out and splayed.  

Noticing how the porch light makes the spiders’ threads visible for a few inches, then nothing.  

The grass doesn’t look wet, but it is.  

The bug zapper that crackles every once in a while.  

In one upstairs window, blinds are half open, casting slats across a made bed.  

The street is quiet, but not empty.  

A neighbor’s sprinkler starts, sharp ticking of plastic gears.  

The dog two houses down is still awake.
