# 27

*seed: time · drift*

---

The hose is coiled but not tidy, half-looped around itself, water pooling at the lowest point. From the kitchen window, the glass distorts the color of the yard — greener in the corners, a pale stripe where the light falls directly, as though that patch of grass were painted on later.

Low voices from the next apartment leak through the wall, but only in vowels, no words, a warm swelling and receding.

A moth tries the screen over and over, its wings brushing the metal with a dry, papery sound. For a second it rests before starting again.

---

The smell of new mulch, sharper and almost sweet, like pencil shavings left in the sun. 
A dog collar jingling in the hallway, then silence. 
Unwashed cups on the counter, one stacked inside the other, rim to rim.

---

All the shoes by the door are pointed in the same direction.
