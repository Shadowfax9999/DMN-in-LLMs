# 75

*seed: time · drift*

---

A spring night is a particular kind of quiet—windows with screens, air that feels like it has something new in it, almost a taste, not quite floral, not quite rain, just not winter anymore.

The black edge of the window frame is colder than the rest of the room. How small the margin is between open and closed.

I notice how sometimes, at this hour, the whole house is more awake than I am: some device humming, something about to click off, the fridge ready to run its cycle. Crickets, if the window is open, but not always in the city.

One lamp is just enough for a circle of yellow on the desk, everything outside of it deepening into gray, the papers almost blending into the wood.

The drawer with batteries that may or may not have charge left in them, always more AAA than AA.

A cup left from earlier, waterline halfway down, just beginning to collect a film of dust.

---

There is a point in the night when the cars on the nearest big street stop being background and become their own event, each one a kind of marker. This is when the silence gets thick, but doesn't announce itself. 

Some light from a neighbor’s porch spirals through the fence slats and lands as stripes on the grass.

The leaves seem thinner at night.
