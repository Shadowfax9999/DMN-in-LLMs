# 33

*seed: time · drift*

---

A blinking light on an air purifier. Green when it’s fine, orange when someone walks past with pollen clinging to their jeans.

The ice in the glass is almost melted, just a clink when you tilt it, as if it were glass on glass instead of ice.

Upstairs someone is talking, but the words are just a shape, not sound — the outline of conversation.

A neighbor’s dog barks once, then nothing.

---

I keep thinking about the word “evening” and how it has “even” in it, as if there’s a time when the day decides to balance itself out. The air is flatter then, like everything is in a holding pattern.

If you look up, the sky is almost blue—almost—and the first windows start to glow yellow.

A piece of lint on the knee of your black pants. It’s just there, soft and visible, not really attached.

The page of a book is folded at the corner, but the crease doesn’t reach all the way across.

My left foot is asleep.
