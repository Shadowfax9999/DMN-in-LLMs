# 15

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sound of a plane overhead — you can’t see it, but there’s a faint scrape in the sky like something being dragged.

There’s a plastic bag caught in the branches, not moving, just fixed up there, pale in the dusk.

Pavement cracks filled with moss in a flat green line. Small stones pressed into the tar, sometimes in patterns where someone must have kicked them.

A girl on a scooter flashes by, the wheels on the concrete make a scraping, rubbery noise that fades for a second and then comes back as she circles.

Tire swing, not moving, rope frayed at the top, old duct tape around the knot.

A shoe on its side by the curb, toe pointing toward the street. Not new, not terribly old, laces missing.

The streetlights haven’t turned on yet. The light is getting stranger, sharper at the edges.

---

The word "evening" used to mean the coming together, the leveling out — the evening of things. 

It doesn’t feel like smoothing. More like pulling apart.

I keep thinking the word "Tuesday" isn’t settled, like it’s leaning forward, getting ready for something that doesn’t happen.

You don’t see as many robins in the actual spring as you do in the pictures of spring.

The smell from the neighbor’s grill is drifting through the closed window.
