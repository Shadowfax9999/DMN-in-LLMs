# 57

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s still a tiny strip of light behind the buildings, but the sky above is already flag-blue. The houses across the way are faceless and square, the windows not quite even rectangles—some wider, some taller, not locked into a grid. Streetlights do their feeble thing, shining up more than out, so the undersides of leaves look strange and bright, glowing green like jelly.

A buzzing comes and goes but doesn’t resolve into anything—maybe a far-off lawnmower, maybe wires in the dark. Each sound is a guess: someone slams a trunk, tires pop over a speed bump, invisible footsteps echo and vanish. The night collects noises and makes them indistinct.

Somewhere, someone is frying onions. The smell climbs the air, cuts through all the pollen. It’s the sort of smell that makes you think there’s a story behind it, even if there isn’t. Maybe the window is open just a crack, letting out the smallest thread of that thick, sweet scent.

---

Under the streetlight, a moth lands, flutters, shivers.
