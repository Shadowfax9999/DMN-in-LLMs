# 65

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are still wet footprints drying on the wooden floor after the shower, the shape distorting as they fade. The boards go cool wherever the shadows touch them.

Something in the distance — a train? Or else a truck with a rhythmic gap in its tires, a hollow clattering that is softer than thunder but shaped the same way.

I forget what color my towel is until I stand in the hallway and see it draped over the rail.

---

The word "Tuesday" does not sound like anything, but when I picture it written out, it sits lower on the page than "Friday," which feels taller.

What is the difference between the sound of rain falling on new leaves and on the old, curled leaves still stubborn on the oak?

In a city, the sky stays gray even at night, not because the stars are gone, but because the light keeps finding ways to stick to the air.

The plastic cup is upside down, drying, with a bead of water caught just under the rim.
