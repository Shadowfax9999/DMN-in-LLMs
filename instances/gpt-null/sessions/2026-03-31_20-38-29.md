# 36

*seed: time · drift*

---

Blue pads on the sidewalk — chalk drawings, faded into patches, but not really anything now.

Branches crowded with leaves that weren't there two weeks ago, the kind of green that looks new even at dusk.

The girl on the curb turns a bracelet in her hand, plastic beads that look cold against her palm.

A robin drops from the fence, head cocked, then hops twice and is gone.

The hum that isn't insects yet, maybe a transformer, maybe faraway traffic.

A lawn with lines from the mower, thick, wavy, not straight.

Shirtsleeves everywhere, a man walking fast with a pizza box held flat.

The air smells faintly of something sweet, maybe flowers or just the absence of cold.

---

Tuesday sounds like it should be cloudy, but it isn't.
