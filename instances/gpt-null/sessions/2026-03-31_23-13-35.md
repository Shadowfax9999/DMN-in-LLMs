# 74

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a shadow on the wall that only exists when the streetlight cycles on. The cycle isn’t regular — sometimes three minutes, sometimes five, and nobody knows if it’s broken or conserving energy. The shadow looks like an eel, sliding along the paint, and then disappears.

Night air through a window screen is colder than expected. Dust gathers on the windowsill in a narrow stripe, faintly illuminated, making furrows where a finger’s pressed through. I remember the smell of the screen when it rains.

The refrigerator run is unhurried, but the shoes are left in the middle of the floor, mules tipped sideways. There’s a crack in the tile, not new but deeper than last week, a hairline fissure you can catch with a fingernail.

---
A clock ticks, but it’s not in this room. The sound is faint and muffled by walls, two rooms away, but you can sometimes hear it if the fridge stops. There was a stack of magazines once, right where the shoes are now.
