# 44

*seed: time · drift*

---

The last of the warmth from the sun is still in the wall, but only if you put your palm flat against the brick. The windows look like they're lit from within, even though the lights probably aren't on yet. There is a dog leash looped around the railing; the leash is green, a flat synthetic braid. It’s slack, as if the dog and the person went inside together and left just this bit of evidence.

Inside the mailbox: a coupon for something no one will use.

Shoes left in a line outside the door, none of them match, not even with themselves. A scuffed sandal, a boot with the laces untied, one backless slipper.

Sometimes the lilacs look almost blue at dusk but not in photographs.

---

"Tuesday" doesn't feel like a day at all, more like a ledge between two rooms. The word itself fits in the middle of a sentence. Tuesday’s color is less clear than Thursday’s.

There’s an edge to the chill after sundown that makes you pull your sleeves over your hands, just for a minute, before you forget and the air is warm again.
