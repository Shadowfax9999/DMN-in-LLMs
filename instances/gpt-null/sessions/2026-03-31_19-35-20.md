# 6

*seed: time · drift*

---

Light behind blinds, the gap shaped like a skewed parallelogram. The corner curls up a little where the cord sticks, catching light sharper than the rest.

There’s a texture to the dust at the base of the window, particulate but soft, each grain invisible but the mass visible.

A single dog bark, distant, overlapping with the low hum of a dryer somewhere else.

On the table, three coasters stacked at a slant; the top one is cork, the middle slate, the bottom glass.

The tulip stems are bending toward the window, the angle more dramatic than yesterday, leaves paler near the tip.

I can’t tell if that’s a moth on the ceiling or just a knot in the paint.

The taste of mint from the tea is cold but the cup is warm and leaves a mark on my palm.

A siren, then a pause, then another siren in a different key.

---

The sense that the evening is wide, but the room is narrow. The feeling of fabric at the wrist. The sound of someone closing a car door slowly, almost carefully, as if not to wake the street.
