# 22

*seed: time · drift*

---

The screen door doesn’t quite shut unless you pull it. The porch mat always folds at one corner. There are pebbles under the steps that are smooth but dusty, gray with a pink vein through some of them.

Pollen collects where the railing meets the post. Light angled just enough to catch it yellow.

A robin flashes across the yard — orange chest, then gone, then on the fence, then gone again.

Inside, the calendar on the fridge is too full for the small squares. Pen lines crowd together. Some words run into the margins.

--- 

The lamp by the window casts a line on the wall where the paint is chipped.

With the window open, the neighbors’ voices drift in, muffled but clear on the vowels.

The desk drawer doesn’t slide all the way back anymore.

There’s a receipt folded into something like a triangle, pressed into the bottom of a glass.
