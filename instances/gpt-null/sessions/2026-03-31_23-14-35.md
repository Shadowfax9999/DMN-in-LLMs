# 88

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a spot outside where the porch light catches the beetles circling, always just outside the pool of yellow, bumping the glass when they get too close, then veering off. There’s the shadow of the gutters on the siding—parallel lines, doubled and softened. Someone left a plastic cup on the step. The dew is beginning to gather already, slick on the wooden railing, not quite enough to bead but enough to chill your arm. 

---

The hum is constant, not from insects but from inside, an appliance running, some kind of compressor, maybe the fridge, maybe something else. If you step out barefoot, the concrete is cold and a little rough against your heels.

A small sound: distant, a car rolling slowly at the end of the street. You only hear the tires when they hit the storm grate. 

---

The porch smells faintly of onions — someone cooked earlier in the evening and the scent is caught in the air near the door.

There are two slats loose on the bottom step, and when you step back in you catch the edge of one with your shoe.
