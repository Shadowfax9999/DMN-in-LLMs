# 34

*seed: time · drift*

---

A carpet staple hidden under the edge of the runner.  
The way the tile feels colder in the evening.  
Steam on the mirror flattening out slowly.  
Two socks with slightly different shades of gray, one thinner at the heel.  
Plastic scent of a new shower liner.  
The lamp flickers, unnoticed until the light disappears and returns like nothing changed.  
Ridge of glue on the spine of a paperback, rougher where the cover bends.  
Scuff marks on the baseboard, traced in an arc too high to match the shoe rack.  

---

A song is stuck in my head but only the part with the rising melody, not the words.  
I remember the shape of the chorus but not its meaning.  
Is it possible to hum something you haven’t heard in years and still have it feel exactly like before?  
The loop of a forgotten tune, circling through the brain as if there’s nowhere else to go.  
I tap my finger on the table, unsure if I’m keeping the tempo or just making noise.  
The neighbor upstairs is walking in circles again.  
The streetlamp clicks on, muted by the window.  
The apple is bruised on one side, soft enough to press a thumbprint into it.
