# 45

*seed: time · drift*

---

A dog barks, then falls quiet. The echo of tires on wet pavement fades slower than the actual car. The window is cracked an inch and the air is cooler near that line. Streetlights hum, but the sound is lower than the refrigerator’s whir, which comes and goes as if remembering itself.

In the hallway, the light from the bathroom slips out under the door and makes the floor look warmer than it is. The blue rug curls up at the corner. A thump overhead—maybe a chair leg, or someone dropping shoes. Old pipes tick.

---

The word "Tuesday" has a heaviness, heavier than "Monday" but not as settled as "Wednesday." Sometimes days feel like colors, sometimes like weights in the hand. There are days that taste metallic, but Tuesday does not. Tuesday is lint on black pants.

---

The smell of cut grass mixed with the first cigarette in months. Birds argue in the hedge and someone across the parking lot is playing a scale on a trumpet. The sound wobbles halfway through and restarts, each attempt rounding off the same wrong note.

The sidewalk is patchy, narrow, half-replaced. Weed cracks. Chevy keys jingle and then are hushed.

There are receipts shoved into the pocket of the coat you don't wear until spring. Some of them have faded into unreadable pink ghosts.
