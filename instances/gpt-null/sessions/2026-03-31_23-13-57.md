# 79

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sound of a train horn but muffled from very far away, almost like someone blowing across the mouth of a bottle. Streetlights with enormous halos tonight, fog leaning against the glass. A neighbor’s laughter splits for a moment, bounces high and then is gone — do they know how thin the air is under a streetlamp?

The tip of one sandal sticks out from under the porch, pale and waiting. There is always some single object left behind by daylight.

Peonies are closed tight, fists of green, but tomorrow they might split. That point just before bloom is heavier than opening.

A moth presses softly, softly, pressing its dust onto the screen.

In the alley someone’s window is blue, television flicker, no voices audible.  

A car’s tires on wet gravel, a different pitch, more of a hush than a crunch. The smell rises up: earth and oil mingled.

---  

There is a word for the precise color of sky before dawn but I never remember it.  

The dog next door circles once, twice, three times before settling against the cool tile.  

Inside, the clock’s minute hand shifts — it is quieter than breath.
