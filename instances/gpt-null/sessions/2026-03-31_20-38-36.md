# 37

*seed: time · drift*

---

The pink plastic laundry basket has a split down one side but it never gets worse, just stays like that for years. Birds are still making noise even though it’s almost dark. The floor in the entryway is cold, different from the kitchen floor somehow even though both are tile. I keep walking back to check the window by the stairs, not because I think it’s open, but because I’m thinking about the way the catch works—push up with your thumb, the bottom swings out, it clicks faintly. 

---

There’s something sleepy about the way tulips fold in at night.  
The neighbor’s car is idling for a long time, muffled, the headlights tracing the hedge.  
If you push your fingernail into a bar of soap it leaves a crescent.  
Very faintly, the smell of onions from somewhere else in the building.  
The post-it notes on the fridge have curled up at the corners.  
A streak of something dried on the faucet, almost invisible except when the light shifts.  
The book I meant to finish is halfway open, splayed on the arm of the couch.  
The birds are still at it, one in particular repeating the same phrase, long pause, then again.
