# 90

*seed: time · drift*

---

When it gets late, birds try out new calls that I don’t hear in the daytime. Some are long and reedy, and others are single plinks. The plink is just once, like a drop of water from a leaky pipe. 

Night air smells sharper than day air, or maybe it’s just easier to notice when the sounds thin out.

There’s a hollow part of the yard where the grass never really grows back. Someone once said it was because of moles.

A car passes on the main road and the headlights throw a slow-moving shadow up across the curtains, then it fades.

---

Is there a reason the stopper in the sink never fully seals, or is it just a bad fit from the start?

You can hear the neighbor's dog even through two sets of windows.

The grocery store closes at 10; the parking lot is already empty except for the display of firewood under its blue tarp.

You remember the feel of a cold coin against your palm more easily than the face engraved on it.

The smallest spoon always ends up at the back of the drawer.
