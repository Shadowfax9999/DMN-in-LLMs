# 72

*seed: time · drift*

---

Microwave clock says 23:07 but the phone is three minutes ahead. Clocks drift, one silent, one not. The fridge hum has a cycle, sometimes you can guess the moment before the compressor kicks, but sometimes it’s sneaky and the next time it’s already quiet. The house ticks and sighs more at night, settling or cooling, or whatever that word is for when a beam flexes as if remembering to breathe.

---

The neighbors are grilling in the yard next door even though it’s late. Charcoal smell clings to the screens. Across the alley, someone coughs and lets the dog out. Something about spring makes the air feel thinner, as if the leaves have just barely started to exhale.

List:
- Plastic bag on the fence corner, never blows away
- Faint rumble of trains, almost under hearing
- The one porch light that turns pink at dusk
- Distant laughter, mixed with crickets
- Forgotten shoes on a step

Wind picks up and a branch taps the glass.
