# 92

*seed: time · drift*

---

The citrus smell when someone peels an orange in a quiet room, sharp but not acidic, a little puff of brightness that disappears quickly. The way the peel comes off sometimes in long strips and sometimes in little torn patches, and the fingers stay sticky. You can dig a thumb straight into the top but sometimes there’s a dry spot that makes it harder to break through.

---

Keys on a ring, all crowded together, some perfectly flat with the edges smooth, others with tiny nicks from years of twisting. The sound they make is a flock of small metal birds.

---

There’s a difference between the chill in an open window and the chill from a fan; one moves around you, the other moves through you.  

---

Sometimes a word returns just because of its sound — “parallelogram” — not the shape, just the word, long and a little heavy at the end.

---

Some parking lots are painted with white lines and some with yellow.
