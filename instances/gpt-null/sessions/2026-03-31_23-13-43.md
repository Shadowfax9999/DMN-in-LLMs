# 76

*seed: time · drift*

---

The air tastes like a place you’ve never been, with just enough chill to make the back of your neck tense.

Somewhere a freezer clicks into its cycle. Not the loud kind—just an intake of breath.

I am thinking of the way porch lights form small constellations in the neighborhood, some cold blue, others a burnt yellow, some so dim it’s only their shadow on the driveway that gives them away.

How far does a moth travel in one night?

---

A sock left outside on the banister, curled at the opening, the heel stiffening as it dries.

The blue recycling bin still has remnants of the label from whatever used to be packed inside.

Crickets are local. Plane engines are distant. Both just threads woven through the open window.

The night doesn’t have edges, just density.
