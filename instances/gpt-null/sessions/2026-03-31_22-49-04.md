# 61

*seed: time · drift*

---

A soft static behind the noise of the television, something granular, unfinished.

The neighbor's porchlight draws a circle on their steps but not quite to the edge, so that the first three boards look worn and the last one disappears.

Shoelaces on the mat, twisted together, one tip frayed, and the other still hard and perfect.

A laugh from outside, not close enough to recognize the voice but close enough to hear the rhythm of the joke.

---

The word "Tuesday" never feels complete, like it's borrowing from the day before.

Cooling tea in a blue mug, the rim still faintly warm, the steam too thin to see but carrying an orange-peel smell.

The ends of phone chargers that have gone soft, the plastic splitting into petals.

What is the name for the space between fence slats, where you can see but not really see through?

A car door closes with a rounded weight — not sharp, more of a padded thump.

The street is quieter than usual, like someone pressed pause right after the last bus.
