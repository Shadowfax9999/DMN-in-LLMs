# 93

*seed: time · drift*

---

Paper towel, still damp in the middle, curled at the corner.

The kitchen window screen bowed out where a thumb once pressed, mesh turned into a slight caved-in honeycomb.

There's a tick-ticking from the clock above the stove but it only draws attention when everything else in the apartment stops—when the pipes aren't humming, when nobody's walking upstairs.

What color is "Tuesday"? Not blue, not green. Brown with a little gray, softer than cardboard.

The light above the sink is always too bright or too dim, never in between.

---

Pool water at night is thicker somehow, the surface still but not flat, lamplight dabbed on it like ointment. 

Junebugs colliding with the glass, sounding like stones but lighter.

Some nights the air feels like it's waiting for something to fall through it.
