# 54

*seed: time · drift*

---

The wind seemed too soft for all the noise it made.

The headlights of a car came down the street very slowly, then stopped in the exact center of the cul-de-sac, idling.

There are ants on the kitchen counter. They have found the sticky patch where the honey bottle dripped. The line of them is thinner at night.

The glass is still cool from the water that was in it. I turn it upside down on the rack and there’s a scattering of little droplets, some on the rim, some falling to the metal below. There’s a sound they make, that tapping, if you listen.

Why are streetlights orange and not white.

---

A click from the neighbor’s yard, maybe the metal gate? I can't tell what direction it's coming from, only that it’s not the same as footsteps.

Blinds half-lowered in the window across the street flicker with the light from the television.

Jasmine in the dark, thick enough to catch in the throat.

The water in the birdbath doesn't reflect the moon, it's just a flat blank.

The mailbox is full of catalogues that are already curled at the edges.

Frogs start up somewhere in the gutters.

Shoes left by the back door, one upright, the other tipped over and empty.
