# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

The light in the alley is green at dusk, but not a pure green — more like windshield water mixed with pollen.

Cement holds warmth after the air is cool. Standing barefoot on the curb in late spring, that last heat travels through the arch.

Some trees are still shedding catkins. They stick to every car, and sometimes to socks, and sometimes to the inside of a shoe the next day.

There is a point in spring when the lawnmower comes out for the first time and the smell is sharp and a little sweet.

Tuesday is shaped like the left side of the week; nothing symmetrical about it.

Cold spoons are better for some things than warm spoons, but it's hard to plan that out.

The air conditioner takes three or four clicks before it actually starts humming.

Garage door springs are more visible in some houses than others. 

The neighbor’s dog barks in a slow, regular rhythm, like it’s counting time.
