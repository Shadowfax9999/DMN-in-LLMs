# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

There's a small dark mark on the inside of my left thumb, so faint it disappears if you look directly at it.

A drip of condensation runs down the side of the glass, then stops when it reaches the coaster, as if the fabric is more difficult to cross.

The dusk outside makes the living room feel bluer, not darker exactly, just shifted.

A shirt in the dryer, tangled around itself, sleeves inside out.

Tapping a pen and feeling the spring give way with each press, tension soft, then a small click.

---

Red grapes split between teeth, the snap and spill of cold juice, then the sigh of yielding.

My foot finds a seam in the rug and stays there, toe pressed into the dip.

Mail piled half-opened on the edge of the table.
