# 60

*seed: time · drift*

---

The screen door is propped open with a sneaker, foam sole pressed flat against the metal. There is a flickering behind the porch light, lacewings or moths or dust. A distant dog barks, the sound coming through layers of warm air and leaves just starting to open. The stillness carries a suggestion of pollen, a thickness on the tongue that feels yellow even when it can’t be seen.

Somewhere down the block, a car alarm whoops and then relents, its tiny drama shrugged off by the night.

The seat of the plastic chair is curved in a way that fits only some people. Someone left a mug near the railing, ringed with gray at the bottom, handle turned toward the street.

There are more windows open in spring than any other time, just enough air moving to keep voices from sticking.

A bee is wedged between the glass and the screen, moving in slow diagonals.
