# 20

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sky in spring can be blue and cold or gold and warm, but at this hour the edges go pink and then drain away again. The way light reflects on windows changes, the glass getting almost opaque at certain angles.

A car horn somewhere that is too long, meaning not the usual beep, more like a signal or a protest, or else someone sitting with their hand pressed down, staring ahead.

Shoes left by a door, but one of them is tipped over and the toe points toward the wall.

Upstairs, the sound of someone rolling something, maybe a suitcase or a chair on wheels, the rhythm a little uneven — heavy, then soft, then heavy again.

Mint grows in cracks where the soil stays damp. If you brush it with a foot it smells sharp and wet at the same time.

There’s a pile of mail on the table, one of the envelopes torn at the corner but not all the way open.

It takes longer to get dark than you expect, but once it does, all the color drops out of the air at once.

A refrigerator hum that only gets noticed when the rest of the house is quiet.

The hallway feels colder because the windows are closed.
