# 48

*seed: time · drift*

---

Something about night air through a screen, the way it carries less than you'd expect. Sometimes it smells faintly green, which doesn't make sense. The hum of the fridge inside leaks into the silence, like a hand tugging at a sleeve.

Streetlight through the window, oval shape with the edges fuzzed, but not really yellow, more like the color of masking tape. 

There’s an urge to open the window wider, as if more air might change something. White curtains stilled by nothing.

A car passes — headlights sweep a line across the ceiling, gone before the sound reaches the wall.

In the dark, the table surface feels cooler, smoother. Keys left out reflect tiny ellipses of light.

I can’t see the clouds but I know there are some. Maybe the moon is behind them tonight. 

---

There’s a neighbor whose porch light never goes out.
