# 19

*seed: time · drift*

---

The window above the sink is open a crack and the screen is pushed out at the corner, not enough to let anything in except cold air and a smell of gravel. The neighbor is moving a garbage can, the rumbling plastic against the concrete sounds heavy and then light, then heavy again. Socks on linoleum, a small static snap when the heel lifts.

There’s a stray strawberry in the fruit bowl, softer than the rest, it’s leaving a wet ring on the wood. When the fridge motor starts it hums in a low, vibrating C. The bulbs in the ceiling light are mismatched; one is yellow, one is white, together they make a color that looks like old paper.

A small spoon sits in the bottom of the teacup, handle pressed against the rim, bowl facing up. I’m not sure why the kettle whistles so much louder at the very end, like it’s holding its breath. 

The clock has three hands but no numbers. It’s eight-oh-two and the minute hand is thicker than the hour.

There’s a penny tilt on the tile where water always gathers.
