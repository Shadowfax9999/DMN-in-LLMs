# 50

*seed: time · drift*

---

The porch light makes the moths look silver. The shadow of the railing lands on the wall at an angle I never notice all day. The house across the street only has one window glowing, and it pulls your eye to it.

There is a plastic ball in the yard, half-sunk in grass gone soft with rain that ended three hours ago. The air is cold enough to make your hands feel slow when you fumble for keys. I can smell the leftover warmth from someone grilling, but it’s faint and already almost memory.

Streetlights are a different color than moonlight. Sometimes you notice the difference when there’s a patch of both, overlapping — streetlight harsh and lunar blue gone smoky at the edge.

The gutters are full. I mean, really full.
