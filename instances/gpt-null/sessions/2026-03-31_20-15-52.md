# 24

*seed: time · drift*

---

Some letters feel more open than others. A "C" has a mouth; a "Q" has a tail; a "G" seems like it wants to eat something soft.

Sidewalk chalk crumbles in the hand, leaves dust on the fingers. Sidewalks are sometimes too rough for it, and then the chalk skips, squeaks, and the line breaks apart. Every color leaves a slightly different residue.

Three windows across, one window glows blue and the others are dark. The blue is cold, like aquarium light, not television, something else.

Standing in sock feet on a wooden floor, the planks are smooth but there are little dips where the grain runs softer.

---

If you press your thumb into the spot just above the wrist bone, there's a slight hollow, and it almost remembers pressure the next time.

Tulips in a vase never quite open all the way.
