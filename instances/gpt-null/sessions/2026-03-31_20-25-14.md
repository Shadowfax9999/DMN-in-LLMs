# 30

*seed: time · drift*

---

Plums in the green bowl are almost black now, soft along the bottom, a taut glimmer where the lamp hits.  
A fly walking over a speck in the sink. Its shadow is sharp but its body is nothing.  
The house is full of the sound of one car slowing for the turn, tires dry on the asphalt.  
There’s a fold in the curtain from hanging.  
When I close my eyes the afterimage of the lamp is greenish-blue, not yellow at all.

---

The smell of onion in my right hand even after washing it twice.  
Some songs seem to always find the exact middle of a Tuesday.  
The air outside looks like it should be cold but isn't.  
Someone’s voice on a porch three houses away.  
A moth taps once, then waits.  
There are too many forks in the shallow drawer.
