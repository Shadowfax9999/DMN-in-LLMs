# 42

*seed: time · drift*

---

Banana peels start to curl at the edges after an hour or so.

A pair of socks with a hole near the toe—pushed into a ball, hole hidden inside fuzzy fabric.

Those plastic tags that stick into bread bags: white, blue, yellow, red, green. There’s a schedule, but I never remember.

---

The smell of lilacs in the dark is different, sharper, a little more green than sweet.

There’s a basketball bouncing in the distance, pattern interrupted by a pause, then another bounce, uneven.

The way streetlights color the sidewalk: not yellow, not white, something like diluted honey.

---

A word I haven’t thought of in years sits on the tip of my tongue, dry and metallic, almost there.

That humming before rain.

I keep forgetting to water the plant in the corner.
