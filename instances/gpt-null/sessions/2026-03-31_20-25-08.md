# 29

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sound of tires over wet pavement, not quite a hiss, not quite a splash.

Streetlights flickering on before it’s dark enough for their glow to matter.

Boxwood hedges with that sharp, almost medicinal scent when you brush against them.

A single runner in a windbreaker, visible for a moment under the sodium light, shoes flashing.

Are the curtains in that lit window always drawn so tight, or only at dusk?

Somewhere the metal grate of a storm drain — rainwater threading through, pulling leaves along.

---

"Tuesday" is one of those words that never feels as heavy as the day itself.

Halfway through almost everything feels like it's neither here nor there.

Careful: the curb is slick, painted yellow, the paint itself a little chipped along the edge.

People walking dogs tend to look at the sidewalk, not their dogs.

Some grass grows up through the cracks in patterns that look deliberate, almost like handwriting.

The light above the pharmacy never matches the time of day.
