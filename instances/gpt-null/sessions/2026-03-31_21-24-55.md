# 56

*seed: time · drift*

---

A jar of pens, all capped, except one, which has its nib pressed against the side of the glass, ink gathering in a slow, blurry spot. 

Night air coming through the window isn’t quite cold or warm, just the gentle hush of a room that has stopped holding heat. 

There is a hum from something plugged in — could be the refrigerator, could be the neighbor’s fan, could be a memory of hum layered under the actual sound. 

Dishes in the rack: an arc of metal handles, spoon bowls holding tiny puddles, a mug turned sideways because the handle wouldn’t fit through the wires. 

A pair of sneakers with laces undone, splayed open like peeled fruit. 

Somewhere a screen is flickering, its light bouncing into corners, making long, slow-moving shadows across the carpet. 

---

Tuesday is a word that feels heavier than Monday but less decisive than Wednesday. 

Spring night: all the branches lean away from the streetlight, casting tattered nets of shadow, but the shadows don’t move unless you do.
