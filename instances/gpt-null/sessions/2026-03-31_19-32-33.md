# 4

*seed: time · drift*

---

Blue-ish light leaking from somewhere west, the way it gets trapped between buildings, reflected off window glass. The tree in front still has last year’s seed pods all tangled at the ends, little dark knots against the sky. Somewhere a child shouting words I can’t make out, the sound bounces once and dissolves. Bicycle tire skidding on a driveway, that little gasp of rubber, then nothing. Inside, the hum of the refrigerator with a rhythm I can only hear when I’m not listening for it.

A stray thought about the inside of a pocket: lint, a receipt corner, a forgotten battery. Fabric worn thin in the curve where keys always press. Is there a particular smell to the depth of a pocket, or just the borrowed scent of washed cotton?

---

Metal chair legs on concrete, a scuff.

The color chartreuse.

What keeps the smell of lilac from drifting indoors?

A moth tapping at a lamp, wings fanning and closing.

There’s a sound to the way hair falls down a back, though maybe it’s imagined.

Mail trucks move slower in the evening.
