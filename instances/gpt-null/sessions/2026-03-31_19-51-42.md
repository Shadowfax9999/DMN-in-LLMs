# 14

*seed: time · drift*

---

The birds are louder just before dusk, as if there’s a deadline for singing.

Light on windows makes every room look like a photograph of itself.

When a streetlamp turns on while it’s still blue outside, the light looks green.

There’s a house three doors down with tulips bent sideways, not broken.

Shoes by the door, all pointed different directions, like a stalled parade.

A neighbor’s barbecue smell drifts past, smoke thinner than a thought.

Some patches of grass are already longer than the rest.

Walking past hedges, the static from leaves brushing together.

Mailboxes at odd tilts, some leaning forward, some back, none straight.

The back gate is never quite latched.

The concrete is warm to the hand even after dark.

---

Inside, the sound of water running in a sink makes the whole kitchen echo.
