# 51

*seed: time · drift*

---

The way streetlights color tree leaves at night, everything underneath flattened into two dimensions, the yellow shadow of each branch trembling just a little because the air never really stops moving.

---

That thin pale section of the sky just before it goes dark, the way it sometimes makes the silhouettes of chimneys look sharp and important for a second.

The sound a car makes when it slows down at a curb — more tire than engine.

Someone talking on the phone, window open, voice carrying in fragments: “— no, but I said — wait, hold on — I’ll check!”

You can smell tulips if you rub the petals between your fingers, otherwise you can’t.

I used to think moths were soft, but their wings feel papery when you touch them, powder coming off in streaks.

A barking dog, one voice, then another answering from farther away.

A green blinking light above the intersection where the road curves.

---

There’s a receipt in my pocket, folded into a square.
