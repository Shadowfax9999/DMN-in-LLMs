# 43

*seed: time · drift*

---

There is a specific blue that starts to show up on cars more in spring, like a shimmer instead of a flat coat, almost leaning lavender but not quite. 

Sparrows sound mechanical when they're clustered over a single tree — all the peepings arrange themselves into a synthetic chorus, metallic and glittering in the gaps between leaves.

The cheap folding chair leaves a pattern on the back of a leg — five slats, not evenly spaced.

A dog on a leash keeps pausing to stare at the same bush each day, nose wrinkling, as if something invisible in the air just staggered past.

The word "Tuesday" feels like a rung in the middle of a week-ladder you can't see the top or bottom of.

A smell of laundry vent mixed with the sharper note of cut grass and some half-warm concrete.

The loose zipper pull on a backpack keeps catching in the same place, right where the curve starts near the edge.

---

There are types of wind that only happen when the sun has just set. Warmer at ankle level, colder at the neck. 

The sound that a heavy plastic bin makes when you let the lid fall shut: not a thud, not a snap, an uncertain hollow clap.
