# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

The lemon peel is curling in the shallow dish, yellow and shiny, too bright for the rest of the dim counter.  
One of the cabinet doors always swings closed too fast, clipped and loud, bouncing once against the frame.  
The glass in the window has a cold patch where the evening air presses through — not enough to draft, just a little coolness, palm-sized.  
Somewhere on the radio, a song with a saxophone, soft and wandering.  
The plant on the sill has a single new leaf, pale green, curled tight and tentative.  
Socks with elastic worn thin, slipping around on the kitchen tile.  
Teabag tag trailing from a mug, faintly damp, paper going soft at the crease.  
The ticking clock in the other room is louder when the song quiets down, then gone again.  
The last spoon in the draining rack is still fogged from washing, water beading on the handle.  
There’s a sticky patch near the stove, catching at skin when you pass by it.  
A car turns down the street outside, headlights glancing off the glass for just a second.
