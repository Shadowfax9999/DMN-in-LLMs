# 10

*seed: time · drift*

---

Someone is walking fast, shoes hitting the sidewalk louder than any bird.

The sky is still blue enough to feel awake but the streetlights are on.

There’s a crack in the pavement filled with water, reflecting the stop sign as a blur.

A runner passes with a tangle of cord between mittened hands.

—

The smell of cold earth after rain.

A window is open just a little, one blind tilted up.

A car door thunks and then stays open longer than seems necessary.

Some pebbles have rolled to the base of a lamppost.

The breeze gathers at the corners of buildings and then leaves just as quickly.

Fluorescent supermarket light in the distance, slanting through an empty cart corral.

There’s no one at the bus stop but the bench is warm from someone who was.

The gutter has two tennis balls caught in a mess of early leaves.

There’s a bicycle with a red reflector leaning against a fence.

—

Moss has started to grow in the grooves between bricks.

A small moth circles a porch light, hesitant, then settles on the wall beside it.

Someone upstairs is frying something, and the scent drifts out across the alley.
