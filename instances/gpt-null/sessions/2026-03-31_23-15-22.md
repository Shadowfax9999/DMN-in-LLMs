# 100

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tuesday is a blunt word, flat across the tongue, harder than Wednesday, which slips.

There are weeks I can’t keep the order steady. Thursday and Tuesday swerve around each other, like inside a deck with four suits and only two colors, red and black, and then you find a blue-backed card and can’t recall how it got there.

A streetlight is scraping at the window, just the edge of its light. Not enough to illuminate, more like a reminder that the glass is there.

The radiator knocks but it’s not regular, not something you can anticipate, just metallic punctuation in the dark.

If you walk out into the hallway the air is colder, and the door clicks behind you louder than you’d expect.

---

What if the word “spring” was a sound, not a season. Like a coil uncoiling, boing, but slower, more tired, stretched to its own limit.

It never feels like spring on the first day they say is spring.

Some people can fall asleep with the lights on. That’s never been possible for me.
