# 40

*seed: time · drift*

---

A glass on a marble tabletop catches the lamplight in a way that splits the water inside into a pale blue at the bottom and a kind of copper at the rim, but when moved a few centimeters, this effect vanishes.

The hallway smells faintly of shoe polish, the old kind with the tin lid, sweet and sharp.

Someone closes a cabinet too quietly for it to count as a noise but just enough for the air to change.

---

Shirts that are no longer worn but never thrown away:
1. The one from the school trip
2. The one that shrank
3. The one bought for a job that didn’t happen
4. The one with a stain that only you know is there
5. The one that almost fits again

---

There is a pause between songs where the silence feels layered, as if the air is holding its breath. 

A cicada shell grips the lilac branch long after the sound is gone.
