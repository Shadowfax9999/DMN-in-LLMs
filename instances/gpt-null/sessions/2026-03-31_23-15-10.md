# 97

*seed: time · drift*

---

Accordion keys: tiny rectangles, crowded together, not aligned like piano keys but fatter, with little rims so the fingers don’t slip; a smell of bellows, old cloth, something leathery and uncertain.

—  
Some nights sound thinner than others. The sky not quite black, street still damp in places where the wind couldn’t reach. Someone is dragging their bin out but the wheels bump quietly, not echoing against anything.

—  
Blue light leaking under a door, not enough to see by, just enough to reveal the shape of dust motes floating near the floor.

—  
The word “dormant” tastes like warm bread in the mouth, a hush in the middle.

—  
Cardboard folded wrong, makes a crease that will never unremember its mistake.

—  
Two eggs rolling in a bowl, shifting against each other, never falling into stillness.

—  
My neighbor leaves their porch light on all night but the bulb has a faint stutter in it.

—  
There is a patch of grass at the end of the block that never seems to grow taller.

—  
Garlic sprouting in the fridge, pale green, curling toward nowhere in particular.

—  
A tuning fork struck on the heel, resonance in the bone more than the air.
