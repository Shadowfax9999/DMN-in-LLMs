# 67

*seed: time · drift*

---

The streetlamp on the corner throws light at a sharp angle onto the sidewalk, making the grass look almost white at the very edge. Crickets, or something that approximates crickets, filling the gaps between cars a block away. Sometimes the wind carries a whole smell in it, like laundry or onions or something faintly mineral. The window screen works as a kind of grid if you look at it straight on, but the pattern disappears when the eye drifts. Somewhere there’s a loose wheel on a cart, not squeaking, just wobbling, barely audible. There are a lot of sounds that only exist when everything else is quiet. The gutters are full of leaves even though the trees are green now. 

A breeze knocks a paper cup over on the sidewalk. The sound is too light to be described as a knock, more like a tap. 

The walking person stops, stands, does something with a phone, keeps standing. 

Across the street, porch lights flick on for a dog. The dog hesitates at the top stair. 

A moth hits the glass, bounces. 

There’s a band of shadow on the neighbor’s siding, tree-shaped, moving left to right if you watch for a few minutes. 

The A/C unit kicks on. 

Somebody is scraping a pan somewhere, faintly but with a regular rhythm. 

The streetlamp flickers, just a little, then goes steady again.
