# 49

*seed: time · drift*

---

Orange streetlights facing in every direction, each one humming, or seeming to. I can’t remember when the hum stopped being a sound and turned into a feeling in the air, all the way up the block, each post its little sun. I think of the glass ring around the bulb, the way bugs cluster on some nights and not on others—tonight they aren’t.

The air feels uncommitted—between cool and warm, faint with cut grass, the sky not entirely black. The neighbors’ porch is lit blue, the decorative kind, like an aquarium glow. Someone is walking their dog, leash slack, shoes scuffing the pavement. The plastic bag tied at the handle, swinging.

There’s a cardboard box by the curb. Wet enough to collapse, but not yet fallen. Socks, maybe, or a blender in pieces. Sometimes the boxes last all week.

There’s a little pulse of warm air every time a car goes by, even three houses away. The leaves move and then fall back to their positions, hands resettling on the armrests.
