# 52

*seed: time · drift*

---

The overexposed look of a porch light seen through rain, halos pooling on the pavement, almost as if you could cup your hands around the glow and spill it like water.

Damp air feels wide, like it stretches the space between houses.

A bicycle wheel leaned upside-down, spinning idly, spokes making a hum that's almost a whine.

You can smell soil that’s just been turned, sharp and sweet, the way old leaves give up to new roots.

The word "Tuesday" feels green only sometimes.

---

Light from a passing car moves along the hallway wall, slow enough to see the shapes rise and sink again.

The sound the refrigerator makes at night is lower, more like breathing.

Some moths fall still against window screens, just holding there, legs tucked, waiting for something not quite named.

In the next room, shoes, side by side, one resting slightly inside the other.
