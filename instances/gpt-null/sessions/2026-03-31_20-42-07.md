# 38

*seed: time · drift*

---

Eyelashes catch pollen; nobody mentions that, but sometimes you can feel the tiny grit at the end of the day.  
If you run your tongue along your teeth, you can find places you missed with the brush.  
The air outside is smooth, almost oily, not cold but with a thickness—not quite humidity, maybe something to do with the season.  
Somewhere a screen door thuds and there's a little echo, like someone snapping a finger in a half-empty gymnasium.  
Sometimes the word "evening" feels longer than the actual hours between sunset and sleep.  
Tiny green shoots pushing up through a sidewalk crack, too soft to make the concrete move, but the shape is changing anyway.  
A sock with a hole at the toe always finds a way to twist around so the hole is on top.  
The white car in the lot is parked at an angle again.  
There's still a faint smell of cut grass even though nobody mowed today.  
The plastic lid on the cup has a rim of condensation.  
Music from a neighbor’s window, not loud, but you can tell it’s in a major key, some song nobody quite knows by name.  
Chirping and then silence between, as if the birds are passing something back and forth.  
A dog collar jingles, fades, stops.  
Some cars just sound heavier driving by, like they're carrying something large.  
The porch light hasn’t been changed in years, but it glows the same color every time.  
A page of a book folded down at the corner, forgotten.  
The left shoe is always tighter than the right.  
A streetlight flickers but doesn’t go out.  
The bobbin case is shaped so the thread slides around it.
