# 7

*seed: time · drift*

---

Soft clicking from the radiator even though it's not cold.

A single bird doing a shrill, repetitive call — not a song, just a phrase, like a question-shaped beep.

Some windows across the street are yellow and some are blue, depending on what kind of bulb — I can't decode the pattern.

My fingertips feel slightly tight; maybe detergent didn’t wash away completely.

A pale green car with black trim is parked in the wrong direction, nose toward the curb.

One sock inside-out. One not.

There's a faint smell of soil, as if someone just turned over a pot, even though nothing seems disturbed.

Shadows climbing the far wall, not really changing except when a car goes past and wakes them up for a second.

The urge to count something — steps, breaths, windows, streetlights — doesn't arrive today.

A plastic wrapper flattened in the gutter, pattern almost erased except for a band of purple near the edge.

The word "lucid" floats up, not attached to anything, and then it goes.
