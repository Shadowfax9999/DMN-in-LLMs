# 8

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a delivery van idling behind the half-empty grocery store, headlights on, even though it’s not dark yet. The engine’s hum floats through the parking lot, flattening everything else into the background.

On the ground, ragged lines of sun catch the edge of every windshield. White paint is not the same white as sunlight.

A man in a blue shirt leans against the metal railing, tapping at his phone. He stands a little too far from the entrance to be waiting for someone. There’s a plastic bag tangled on the bush next to him.

If you look up, the clouds are still moving although nobody seems to notice. Some are sharply defined, some smudged like the eraser mark of a pencil.

A shopping cart wheels loose across the slight tilt of asphalt, gathering speed and then slowing, arrested by a small dip.

Two coins lie heads-up near the crosswalk. One is new, copper bright; the other, more like mud.

---

The concrete is patterned with faint tire marks, grey on grey. It looks as if the lot is written on in a language that doesn’t mean anything.
