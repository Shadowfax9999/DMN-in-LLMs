# 28

*seed: time · drift*

---

The streetlight hums a pale yellow.  
A man walking two brown dogs, both tails zigzagging, leash slack.  
Light catching in the space between branches, a pattern on concrete that wants to be lace but is too patchy.  
Wind bends the topmost leaves and the sound is rough, not soft, like corduroy rubbed backwards.  
The neighbor’s recycling bin has a blue lid, warped at the corner, held with gray tape.  
Sweet scent, false orange, somewhere close — a bush, invisible from here.  
A car door shuts somewhere behind me, more of a thump than a slam.  
Shadows stretch too far sideways, making the curb look longer than it is.  
A porchlight flicks on as if remembering something.
