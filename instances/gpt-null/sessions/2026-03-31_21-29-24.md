# 58

*seed: time · drift*

---

Wind in the screens, silver motion, a sound that doesn’t match the temperature. Sometimes spring is colder at night than the mind wants. I think of condensation on bottles I left out, the drops starting to catch reflections from underneath the porch light.

Old porch furniture creaks if you shift your weight, a woven pattern pressed into the back of a thigh, but only for a few minutes, fading as the skin resets. Plastic, metal, the vague smell of something left damp too long, almost sour, almost sweet.

The neighbor’s kitchen window is on, rectangle framed by darkness, and I see someone with their head bent over a counter, maybe reading the back of a box. The lit interior looks sealed, insulated against the outside chill even with the window slightly open.

Somewhere in the block, a dog barks twice, pauses, and then a third, more questioning bark.

I can hear the low drone of a distant car, tires on still-wet pavement, a loping sound that only becomes distinct as it draws closer and then is gone.

---

Daylight savings always makes this hour feel stolen. The clock says it’s late but my body won’t believe it. I check the hour again and it hasn’t moved.
