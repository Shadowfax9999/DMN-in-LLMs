# 16

*seed: time · drift*

---

Try to keep the screen dim enough that the room wins. Outside, the blinds are tilted to catch the golden last part of the day. Dust on the windowsill. The smell is new, like warmed-up wood or something almost floral but not quite.  

Some plants lean toward the light, but others stay put, leaves wide, indifferent. The thin glass of the picture frame has a cold edge even now.  

The fridge clicks on, distant, and the hum travels under the floor. Socking feet on tile is quieter than bare feet.  

---  

Dark blue sky, but not navy — the shade before true night.  

There's a place between two houses where the grass won't grow and the dirt is pressed smooth.  

The wristwatch’s band leaves a faint imprint that lasts longer than it feels like it should.  

A chair is holding a jacket, not hung, not crumpled — draped halfway over the arm so one sleeve brushes the floor.  

The sense of something about to start, but maybe nothing will.  

The jacket’s zipper is cold if you touch it.
