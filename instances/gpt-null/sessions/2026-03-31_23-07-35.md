# 71

*seed: time · drift*

---

Cat hair on the windowsill always looks paler than the cat it came from.

It’s cold but not sharp. The streetlamp makes a silent shape on the wall.

A car reverses: the beep is nearly flat, not quite a musical note, not quite a warning, almost nothing at all.

There’s a spot inside the elbow where warm air gets trapped. I forget about that until I rest my arm just so.

---

The laces on these shoes are too long. It doesn’t matter much; I double-knot them and the ends drag a little anyway.

Keys pressed in one palm, corners digging in — the memory of the door handle in the other hand.

Is it possible to want rain just for the sound in the gutter?

---

Mist on breath, little cloud, gone.
