# 21

*seed: time · drift*

---

My shoulders always feel uneven when I stop moving and notice how they land against the back of a chair. One presses in, the other seems farther away, as if the bones are listening to a different rhythm.

Dust patterns on the window, visible only at sunset. Horizontal bars, a streak low down, the memory of a fingertip pressed months ago.

A shout from somewhere outside, but it doesn’t sound urgent. It hangs in the air and then dissolves, taken up by the white noise of cars two streets over.

The smell of tomato vines when the leaves are crushed between your fingers. Not the fruit, but the green part, acidic and a little bit bitter.

---

The word "Tuesday" is sharp at the beginning, softer at the end. Sometimes I hear it and think of the color blue, even though the day itself feels unfinished.

Plastic containers nesting inside one another in the cupboard. There’s always an extra lid that doesn’t fit.
