# 17

*seed: time · drift*

---

The patch of grass under the tree is uneven, almost bumpy, and the ants are making highways in the dirt. There’s a single dandelion sticking up, but the stem is bent, so it looks like it gave up halfway.

A dog barking, but the voice is distant, muffled by leaves and fences and maybe the shape of the block itself. The house across the street has lights on in two rooms only: the one above the garage and the kitchen. The windows are open and you can hear the faint clink of silverware.

I think the streetlights come on just before the sun disappears, not after.

There’s a bicycle wheel propped against someone’s recycling bin.

The breeze smells like cut grass and propane, maybe someone grilling or just the memory of it lingering from the weekend.

---

The taste of apple skin: slightly bitter, tight against your teeth, green more than red. The first bite is always coldest, right through the front.

If there’s a bruise on the apple, it never tastes like the rest of it — sweeter, darker, almost as if it’s been another fruit for a moment.
