Some plants don't know it's spring yet. Dusk doesn’t really end; the blue just pools in the corners. On the sidewalk, a squashed snail glistens and dries, and I think about how little it would take to miss it entirely.

---

The window's open and the sound is not one sound but a stack: cars, a faint television, something metallic clicking again and again, air moving somewhere not close. 

If I get up to close the window, the screen will rattle in its frame. There's dust along the inner sill—too fine for a cloth, best gathered with a fingertip. 

My legs stick slightly to the chair. 

There is a yellowish glow in the alleyway. 

The fan on the ceiling starts slow and then gains speed, and nothing in the room changes visibly, but I can feel it in my ears.
