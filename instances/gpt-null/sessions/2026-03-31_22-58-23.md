# 66

*seed: time · drift*

---

A cherry leaf stuck to the windshield, flat against the glass, held there by the wet. The wipers push once, slow and hesitant, and the leaf slides half an inch but stays on.

A pale blue wrapper curled up next to the curb. Whatever was inside is gone, but the curl remains, a ribbon shape.

Streetlights make everything look cleaner but also far away. Shadows are thinner on wet pavement, stretched like fabric pulled tight.

The sound of distant tires on gravel is soft, but the mind pulls it closer — crunch, pause, crunch, as if the car is endlessly arriving.

Sometimes the night feels like it has more air in it, as though every breath opens something wider.

The orange plastic fence around the new construction site sags a little in the middle.
