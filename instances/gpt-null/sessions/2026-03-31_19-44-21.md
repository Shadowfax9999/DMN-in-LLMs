# 12

*seed: time · drift*

---

The blue of dusk in the window won’t match the blue of the tiles on the kitchen wall, even though both seem like attempts at the same thing.

The refrigerator clicks on: a different pitch in a humid spring. Its sound is fatter, more insistent, like it wants to be comfort rather than backdrop.

A memory of a specific smell: cut grass and unspooling hose, water turning the dirt under the spigot dark.

An ant walking along the rim of a mug. Pause, feelers up, continuing, then around and over into nothing.

There’s a stickiness to the air close to the window. You don’t notice it until you move your hand through it.

A page corner curled under a pot, too close to damp. The sound when it tears is uneven, not sharp. 

Some words make you hungry even if you’ve just eaten.

---

The sky goes grey and then suddenly it’s navy.
