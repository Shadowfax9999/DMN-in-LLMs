# 47

*seed: time · drift*

---

Porch light and moths. The glass is warm above the bulb, thin layer of dust. Every once in a while a moth gets caught inside the fixture and you wonder how it managed to slip through the tiny seam. The buzzing isn't constant, just an occasional rattle against the glass. Sometimes, from inside, it's hard to tell if the movement is from wings or just some shift in the night breeze.

—

A cup left outside, rim filled with pollen in a yellow ring. Pollen collects more neatly in places where water was — a memory of an earlier rain, or just dew. The cup is blue plastic and the yellow ring stands out even in the dark.

—

The neighbor’s car door, opening and closing, opening and closing, a kind of rhythm you stop noticing after a while unless it breaks the pattern.
