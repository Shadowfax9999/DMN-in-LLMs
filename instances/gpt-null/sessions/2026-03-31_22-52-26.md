# 63

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s lemon balm crushed along the garden path, the scent rising if you step just so. Some leaves are almost sticky, still holding that afternoon warmth. There are moths bumping the window glass, soft little collisions. Porch light casts a pale triangle over the flagstones, speckled with droppings, bits of crumbled shell, a feather from somewhere. Someone's watering can has a long copper spout, green and bent at the lip. Crickets sound less rhythmic than they did last week. A spiderweb is stretched between the handles of a wheelbarrow.

---

I once thought shadows moved faster than me, but really they just give an impression of hurry.

A staircase that doubles back on itself always makes me feel taller while I’m walking on it.

There's a stack of board games in the hall closet and the top box is crushed in the corner.

The air smells of cut grass and old rain.
