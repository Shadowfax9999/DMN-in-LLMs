# 9

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word “evening” has such an old, soft shape, like satin after so many washings you can run it through your fingertips and it won’t catch.  

Shadows on the sidewalk aren’t sharp; they feather out at the edges, and the puddle left from last week’s rain is rimmed in pollen, yellow tracing the oval.  

It is still light, but not in the middle of things — not noon, not “day,” but not quite the hush that gets called “night.” There’s birdsound, not really insistent, not background either.

I keep forgetting what month it is when the windows are open.  

---

Tuesday is a square word, rectangular, not like Saturday, which is round and blueish. Ordinal but not urgent.

The lids on plastic containers are always either too tight or stretched out, never the original fit they once had.  

The pile in the hallway that’s meant to go somewhere grows taller with the daylight, like it feeds on sun.  

There are always three colors of dish soap, but each time it becomes less clear which was the original.  

Cotton T-shirt, slightly stiff on the shoulders from hanging to dry. There’s a place where the seam was stitched twice, the line doubling back for no visible reason.  

The apricot tree across the street is blooming white, not apricot.  

The door stops just short of clicking shut.
