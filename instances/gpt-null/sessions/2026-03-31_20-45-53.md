# 39

*seed: time · drift*

---

There is a swallow in the dusk, almost too high to follow. Sometimes it seems to vanish entirely, then turns and catches more of the last light for a moment.  

How many colors does the sky have at the edges? There’s the kind of blue that only happens behind the branches, a cooler one, and then a streak that is not exactly orange, not exactly gold.  

If you stare at the tip of a branch, the wind looks like it belongs to nothing.

Someone is walking and whistling, but the song keeps getting swallowed by a passing car.  

What does spring smell like if it rained the day before? Not the grass, but the sidewalks, the curb, and something that came up out of the ground.  

The streetlight has thin halos tonight, almost like rings seen through water.  

Inside the house the floor is warmer than the air outside.  

There’s a pair of shoes near the door. The laces are loose.  

The lemon in the bowl is softer than it should be.  

Sometimes you can hear the refrigerator hum, sometimes you can’t.  

The wall clock is much quieter when the window is closed.  

There’s a lamp that turns on with a small flick of the wrist, but it doesn’t always catch on the first try.  

---  

A moth tapping at glass.  

A crumpled receipt in a coat pocket.  

A spoon cooling in a mug that’s almost empty.  

A sentence that won’t start.
