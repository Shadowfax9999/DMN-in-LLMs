# 98

*seed: time · drift*

---

Some nights have a blankness, not empty but unpatterned, like the road after midnight where the lights just quietly subdivide the dark. Things drift past the windows and you can’t tell which ones will matter in the morning. Cardboard boxes stacked under a streetlamp — the tape on them glimmers, and for a second it looks wet. There’s a motorcycle parked close to the curb, helmet hanging off the handlebars, visor half-raised. I’ve never ridden one.

Shoes in a line by the door, all of them pointed toward the same invisible exit. The soles show thin streaks of dried mud down the middle, like the river delta on a map. You can get a lot of dust under your fingernails just walking home.

There’s a plastic bag hanging from the back of a kitchen chair. It’s translucent and the handles are stretched thin, turning nearly white under the ceiling light. There’s a sound that only happens when you shift in a chair late at night, the wooden legs grumbling against the floor, softer than footsteps but still something that fills the kitchen.

---

Red traffic lights from the other block blink onto the ceiling in a perfect little rectangle if you leave the shades halfway up. The cat finds that spot and sits in the pool of red, tail curled tight, the rest of the room dark except for that slice. The clock on the stove is stuck at 2:17; nobody remembers to reset it. Some clocks never matter until the power goes out.

I think the neighbors moved out last week.
