# 64

*seed: time · drift*

---

The sound in a spring night window is not quite crickets yet—not that thick, not that endless—just one kind of insect, or maybe two, slow patterns, a little stutter, then blankness. They stop when the train passes. The train is so distant it feels more like a memory than like an event, but the glass shakes just a little, and if you watch the light on the far wall there’s a ripple, a fat slow flicker, and then nothing again.

Somewhere a bird tries out two syllables, repeats them twice, and gives up.

What is the shape of the dark outside, if you could hold it? Would it be soft, or would you feel the branches threading through?

I can never really tell if that’s rain or just air moving through leaves.

There’s a way the night makes rooms seem unfinished, like the corners are waiting for something.

Sometimes the floor creaks with no footstep.

---

Objects in the night:  
a glass of water on the sill;  
the key in its dish;  
a single sock, folded;  
the line of the shadow from the lamp;  
the shoes turned outwards;  
the scent of onions from the kitchen hours ago;  
a plant whose leaves close when touched;  
the faint square where mail was placed and then moved.

When the refrigerator motor cuts off, there is a kind of relief.
