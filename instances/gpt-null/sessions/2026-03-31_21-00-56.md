# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

The streetlamp has a halo from the pollen, moths zigzagging through the light.  
Some windows are open, some not, but it’s warm enough for all.  
A faint, rising and falling siren, farther than I can guess, fades into the crickets.

There’s a pile of shoes by the door — sneakers, sandals, one boot collapsed sideways.  
Inside, a fan clicks as it turns, then is silent, then clicks again;  
I expect it to wobble, but it doesn’t.

The table’s surface is oily under the fingertips — not greasy, just that wood-polish feeling.

---

Garbage trucks come later in the week now, but the bins are out early just in case.  
Somewhere a door slams, two short bursts, and then a laugh.  
A passing train makes the glass rattle very slightly, a sound almost hidden by the fan’s steady hum.  
I can’t remember if I left the keys in the bowl or the jacket pocket.
