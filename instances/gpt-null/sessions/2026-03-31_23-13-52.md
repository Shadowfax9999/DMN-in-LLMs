# 78

*seed: time · drift*

---

The glass of water is warm, not cold. Hands don't expect it. 

A jacket hangs crooked on the chair. There's always one sleeve longer than the other. Or maybe just the way the chair is shaped.

The sound from the street is cars on wet pavement, even though it hasn't really rained. Sometimes it still sounds wet, like the tires remember rain.

A drawer that sticks halfway, then gives. The wood on wood, a hushed stutter.

---

Upstairs, a hallway: half-dark, the way a bulb glows through closed doors but never into the deeper corners.

Sometimes shoes pick up grit from outside and it finds its way into the carpet.

There's a box with receipts folded in impossible ways, some of them with blue ink that's already fading.

Mug, coaster, ring of moisture not matching the mug size.

Pillows look like different colors in the night.

---

Tuesday doesn't look like the color brown. It looks like slightly faded blue.
