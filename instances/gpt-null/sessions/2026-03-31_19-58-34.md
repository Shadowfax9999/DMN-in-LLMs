# 18

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word "Tuesday" does feel like it should be blue, but the blue of a mailbox, not the blue of the sky, more metal than air. Names of days stack up quietly, Tuesday never quite at the top or bottom, just there. Springs evenings are long in the northern hemisphere, the light lingering in a way that feels like it’s pausing in the doorway, not sure whether to leave or come in.

Metal cools slower than stone. The stones along a path remember the sun for hours. There’s a kind of spring air that smells like mail—paper, ink, maybe faint glue. The door’s slot rattles at odd times in the evening, your body tensing before you remember it’s just the mailman closing up. Mail usually comes earlier, but sometimes there are deliveries at night. Catalogs aren’t as thick as they used to be.

---

There’s a sound that happens when someone sits down on a couch that’s not their own, a held breath, fabric brushing against itself. Plastic-wrapped magazines on a coffee table. The ticking of a kitchen timer in another room. A pencil knocking to the floor, the tip breaking so cleanly you can see the fresh wood spiral inside.

---

A single porch light turns on before the sun is fully gone. The bugs are drawn to it, one after another, as if the dark behind them is too empty to tolerate.
