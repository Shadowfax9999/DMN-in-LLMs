# 81

*seed: time · drift*

---

There are moths clustering on the porch light, their wings overlapping and separating in intervals I don't track.

A car passes and the headlights make the curtains thin for a moment.

Somewhere above the roof, the sound of geese in a V; they move too quickly for the eye but the voices linger.

I can hear the click of a plastic lighter, twice, then the sound of someone exhaling.

There’s a patch of grass where the clover has outgrown the blades, little white heads scattered low.

The window screen vibrates if I touch it, faintest metallic hum.

The porch steps feel cooler than the floor inside.

My foot finds a soft spot in the wood; the paint comes off as a chalky stripe on my heel.

---

Rain gutters holding old leaves, and the smell when you reach up to clear them out.
