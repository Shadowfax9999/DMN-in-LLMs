# 77

*seed: time · drift*

---

Stillness in the neighborhood, except for the low hum of a distant air conditioner. The neighbors’ porch light flickers and goes steady again—sometimes that makes me think of a lighthouse, but there’s nothing here to warn or save.

The smell of laundry from the vent drifts over, warm, almost sweet in the damp air. Somewhere a car door thumps shut, not hard, not urgent.

I can’t decide if the streetlamp out front is more yellow or more white. Its glow makes the leaves look like coins or cut paper. 

There’s a plastic cup in the gutter, lying on its side, catching a little moonlight inside the rim.

The house behind ours sometimes plays a radio after midnight, quietly enough that it’s more like hearing a shape than a song.

The driveway gravel never sounds the same from inside as it does when you’re walking on it.

The window screen mesh warps the view, stretching the streetlights and pinching the headlights of passing cars.

The time on the phone display doesn’t feel like the real time, just a number ticking over.

Still a breeze.
