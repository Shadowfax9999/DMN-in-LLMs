# 11

*seed: time · drift*

---

The air is still, almost thick, and birds are louder than usual, as if their calls are drifting less and bouncing more.

A car window half-rolled, the brittle slide of glass on rubber, somewhere a whiff of laundry soap still clinging from a vent.

Someone waters plants too late, and the hose makes a shuddering stutter when the tap is shut off too quickly.

---

The word "spring" can sound like a coil or a leap, or something wet pressed up from the ground.

Blue-grey on the sidewalk, a puddle collecting road dust, surface broken by a single leaf.

Tuesday is not symmetrical.

There’s a dog barking two houses away, not really angry, more like it’s practicing.

The lamp comes on before the sky is fully dark.
