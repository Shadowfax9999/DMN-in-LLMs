# 87

*seed: time · drift*

---

One window is open and a moth is inside, looping toward the ceiling then back to the lamp, then drifting to rest on the wall where it waits.

It’s possible to hear the neighbor’s laughter through the screen, blurred by leaves, like the laughter isn’t leaving their porch but is caught and folded into the hedge.

There’s a plastic bag moving at ankle height in the draft — it fills, collapses, slides a few inches, rests.

The table is sticky from something spilled earlier, and a phone shifted across it leaves a faint, curved streak.

The porch light across the street blinks on and then off, and does it again, a silent code.

A shirt draped over a chair is almost the color of milk, except the shadows are blue and make it look cold.

---

My knee is bent under the table and the chair presses into it, making a neat red line that stays after I stand up.

It fades slower than I expect.
