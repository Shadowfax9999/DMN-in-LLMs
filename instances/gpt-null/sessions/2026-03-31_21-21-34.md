# 55

*seed: time · drift*

---

The word “Tuesday” seems denser than “Monday.” A little more blue, less gray.

A wet mark on the concrete dries from the edge inward, sometimes leaving a ring.

Old radiators clicking somewhere below, the heat as a backdrop, not really warming.

There’s a softness to air after a sudden rain. Something left suspended, a kind of hush.

Shoes by the door, always in pairs or alone, never quite matching the lives that pass by.

The streetlamp sends out a cone, but the light at the edge is always blurry, as if it’s tired.

A page dog-eared and then straightened again keeps the memory of the fold.

---

The quiet tick of the microwave. The interval when nothing is moving—food waiting to turn, heat gathering.

A plant on the sill growing toward the window, each leaf a little twist of wanting.

Banana peels never quite fit in the trash can as neatly as they did in the bunch.

Some nights the breeze brings the smell of laundry, even if it’s not yours.

An ambulance passes on the avenue, siren falling and rising, Doppler stretched.

There’s always a sticky spot somewhere on the kitchen counter, even after it’s wiped down.
