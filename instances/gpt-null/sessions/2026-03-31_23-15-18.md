# 99

*seed: time · drift*

---

Tuesday never feels like the right day for anything that begins.

Pavement holds warmth long after midnight, and sometimes you can feel it through your shoes.

There are houses with windows still lit and blinds not fully closed, rectangles of yellow that seem to hang in the air above shrubs.

I keep hearing a train but there are no tracks nearby. Maybe it’s just the distant highway, a sound that seems to roll and break in waves that aren’t water.

There’s a shape to the smell — sharp, wet, a little bit like cut grass, but deeper in the lungs it’s almost metallic.

---

There’s a pile of laundry on the chair, shirts and towels and something blue I don’t remember wearing.

Tuesday is always the heaviest traffic at the intersection by the pharmacy.

The air tastes colder when you breathe out facing north.
