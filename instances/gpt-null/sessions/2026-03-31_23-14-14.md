# 83

*seed: time · drift*

---

A spring night with windows open is almost quiet, as if the sound comes in waves — alternating between distant cars and a thin scream of an insect, which sounds like a wire being scraped.

There's a gap in the screen where someone's finger once poked through, the mesh warped and sharp at the edges, not enough to let in a bird but maybe a moth.

If I look at the sky from this angle, the light from the streetlamp makes the clouds seem lower, taut and slightly yellowed like stretched canvas.

The smell is half earth, half something anonymous — a kind of wetness, not quite rain, maybe dew or the aftertaste of cut grass.

A pair of running shoes is left on the porch, the laces splayed wide, tongues pulled forward as if in anticipation.

Inside, the hum of the refrigerator is steadier than everything else.

---

Once in a while a gust comes through and shifts something small: a piece of paper on the table, a thin curtain.

The sound of keys being dropped on the counter and a cupboard closing.

Peeling a clementine and the rind is almost too thin, not enough to keep the segments compact.
