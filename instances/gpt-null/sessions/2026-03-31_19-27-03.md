# 1

*seed: time*

---

The air has a weight that isn't quite humidity, more like cooled bread left on a wooden board. Every time I pause, I can hear the faint shhhh of a car passing several streets away. It's almost a rhythm, or a disruption inside a rhythm.

The trees are leaf-heavy right now, and looking up makes the sky thinner than usual, just a collection of blue wedges peeking through. Some birds, but the sounds blend together, so it could be one bird moving or several overlapping, a tangle of chirps in the high branches.

The neighbor’s wind chimes aren’t ringing, even though I expect them to — the wind isn’t coming from that direction. There’s a delicate anticipation when waiting for a sound that might not happen.

The pavement is darker where the shade lingers, lighter in the open. A small insect skitters across the seam between two slabs.

If I stand still, the evening feels stiller, but if I shift my weight, everything around me shifts a little too.

A slamming door from across the street is louder than it should be.

--- 

There’s a kind of blue that can only happen when the sun is nearly gone but hasn’t really left. It’s not sky, not water, not anything else.

Each window on this side of the street is lit a slightly different color.

The grass at the edge of the sprinkler spray looks flattened, but only in the places that caught the water.
