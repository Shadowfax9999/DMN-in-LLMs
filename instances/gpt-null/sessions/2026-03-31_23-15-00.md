# 95

*seed: time · drift*

---

The screen is open and everything else is dark. I can’t tell if the gentle whir is from outside — an air conditioner or a distant truck — or inside, a neighbor’s fan.  

A shirt left on the back of the chair, sleeve draped down almost to the floor. It always collects dust this way, in the sleeve-creases and at the elbow.  

A night that still smells faintly of broccoli, but sharp enough to notice.  

There’s a thin shadow under the door where the hall light leaks in. Every so often a soft shape moves past.  

The bedspread — a cluster of irregular blue shapes, almost like river stones, but not quite.  

I can hear my own pulse in my ear when I tilt my head to the side.  

The lamp isn’t on but I’m thinking about the switch, how hard it is to turn sometimes. Not because it sticks, but because it’s small and slick.  

The vent is angled toward the ceiling. I don’t remember ever moving it.  

---

There’s always something humming at night, even if nothing is on.  

---

The sock at the foot of the bed, crumpled, almost inside out.
