# 68

*seed: time · drift*

---

Someone left the porch light on and the glass is so thick it blurs the wings of the moths. They become pale commas in slow motion, not touching.

The wet hem of the grass is cold through running shoes but only at the edges, places where the soil remembers winter.

Peonies haven't opened yet. The tight fists of them.

There is a faint scraping near the trash bins, metallic and private, raccoon-clockwork.

The air smells like thawed onions.

---

Phone screen reflected in the window: one dim blue rectangle floating under my own hands. Now my hands are underwater. The glass splits each light in two.

A dog barks far away, the sound pressed flat and carried sideways by the cool.

There are insects in the streetlamp, but the lamp buzzes louder than their wings.

The wind moves so the leaves change direction, all at once.
