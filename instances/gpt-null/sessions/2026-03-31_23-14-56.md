# 94

*seed: time · drift*

---

There’s a sound outside that doesn’t repeat. It’s not the kind you listen for. Some kind of shuffling mixed with leaves, but not quite leaves, a different scritch.

The window is open just enough for the cooler air to find the gap. It slides in uneven, sometimes a little gust, sometimes just a suggestion. The curtain tugs back and then drops.

I never realized how different window screens all look until I tried to see through them at night, with the inside lights on. Daylight, and the mesh vanishes. At night, it becomes a grid. The world is divided, everything out there slightly farther away.

There’s a sweetness to the air, not floral, more like the echo of grass. When I was small, I used to think that dew made the grass smell sweeter. I don’t know if that ever made sense.

One streetlamp flickers. Not quite off, not on. It’s as if it’s practicing blinking.

A car door, far off, closes so softly I almost miss it.

I can’t see the moon but I know it’s somewhere out of frame.
