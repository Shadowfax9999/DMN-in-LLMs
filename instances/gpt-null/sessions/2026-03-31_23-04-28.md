# 69

*seed: time · drift*

---

A spring night and the trees make a cottony hush above the sidewalk, like a muted ceiling, not exactly blocking the light from the streetlamp but tinting it. On the leaves, the glow goes a pale green and sits there as if hovering, not quite on the leaf surface but not detached from it either. The sky is pale gray instead of dark blue. An occasional car, but distant, easy to miss. 

The smell is part floral, part dust, part something sharp that doesn't really belong to anything in sight — maybe something carried over from earlier, a collision of dinners and cut grass and rain that hasn't happened yet. 

Shoes on the pavement: scuff, scuff, then a pause, as if someone is uncertain whether to go back for a missing object. A plastic bag caught in a hedge looks gray, transparent, then silver as it flickers in the wind. 

There's a slow undulation to the lights inside the houses, as if the curtains have weight and momentum, swaying a fraction from a draft nobody can feel. 

Gutters with grit from winter, small pale stones and a quarter smashed almost flat. 

--- 

The word "Tuesday" rhymes a little bit with "bluejay," but only if you say it wrong. If you write it in lowercase, it looks less official, almost apologetic, tuesday. 

When a day feels like it doesn't fit, is it the day that's the wrong shape, or the feeling? 

A mailbox that leans forward by two degrees, just enough to look as if it might tip in a stiff wind. The red flag is up but there's no mail inside. 

A street number missing its middle digit. 

Honeysuckle that hasn't opened yet.
