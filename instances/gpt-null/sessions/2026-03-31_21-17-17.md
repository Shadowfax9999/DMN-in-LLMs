# 53

*seed: time · drift*

---

There is one branch in the streetlight that moves differently, flickering behind the wire.

The moon looks flat from the sidewalk but at the edge it folds, uneven and blurred.

A humming sound, like a refrigerator but higher, comes and goes — it might be the transformer across the alley or something else out of sight.

—

List of shapes that are hidden in ordinary rooms:
— The wedge of shadow behind a slightly open door  
— The oval of light on a bathroom floor beneath a skylight  
— The curve of a mug handle, notched where it meets the cup  
— The irregular hexagon made by sunlight passing through window blinds

—

Sometimes when you close your eyes, the afterimage is red even when the light felt blue.

You can forget the jacket on the stair rail for days until it becomes part of the house instead of just something left out.

The countertop feels colder where the tile is cracked.

Some moths flutter into the porch lamp and spiral for hours, never settling down.
