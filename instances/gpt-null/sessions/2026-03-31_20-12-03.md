# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The last light is turning everything blue at the edges. If you’re near a window, it always feels a little colder against the glass, even if your skin stays warm. 

Noise outside: a thunk, like a bird against something, or maybe a neighbor shutting their trunk. For a minute it’s hard to tell the difference between a real interruption and just a shift in air pressure.

The clock in the next room is still audible if you stop listening for other things. It's uneven, like it sometimes skips. 

Mail left in the box overnight feels damp, even if the air is dry.

A bowl with just a little bit of water in it looks like more than it is.

---

There’s a smell on some spring nights right after dark, like cut vines or cold sap. Not as sweet as grass, not as sharp as rain.

Traffic slows down in long gaps, then a cluster comes—four cars, then nothing.

It’s hard to tell if the cat is awake from this angle.

The flag on the building across the street barely moves, but the streetlight makes it visible in flashes.

The wood of the windowsill is lighter under the latch.
