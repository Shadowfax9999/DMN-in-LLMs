# 62

*seed: time · drift*

---

A smell comes through the window, sweet, a little like honeysuckle but sharper, maybe the tree with white blossoms whose name is always almost remembered.  
The hum in the walls isn't the refrigerator, it's deeper, background, probably not even mechanical, maybe just the air itself, or the ear itself.  
There's that blue of a streetlight brushing the ceiling where the paint is thinner.  
---  
Which windows are open in the building, what other bedrooms are still lit.  
Names for colors in the dark: navy, coal, velvet, slate, nightshade, bruise, whale.  
You can't really map the walls in the dark, only by memory or by the odd triangle of light around the door.  
The phone screen still glows a rectangle through your closed eyelids.  
The clock's colon is steady, not blinking.  
Some nights you can hear footsteps overhead, and sometimes you imagine there isn't any upstairs, just the same room stacked again and again.
