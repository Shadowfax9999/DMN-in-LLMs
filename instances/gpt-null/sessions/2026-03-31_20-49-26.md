# 41

*seed: time · drift*

---

A piece of sidewalk has a shallow handprint and three fingertip dents, as if someone changed their mind halfway through. The concrete is rough there, like the imprint never fully healed over.

A dog’s ear flicks, not quite a shake, more a nervous reset.

A car reverses, its lights throwing two bright ovals onto the wall, which shrink and widen as the wheels turn.

There’s a smell that comes right before rain but the air is dry, and the leaves are still.

The word “evening” makes me think of navy blue, then of old city streetlights with nets of moths around them.

---

Orange reflections on the glass, fluttering as someone walks past with a phone held low.

The syllables in “Tuesday” feel rounder than “Monday,” softer at the edges.

Somewhere a metal latch claps shut and there’s a pause before footsteps start again.

The tip of my tongue fits into the gap behind my front teeth when I’m thinking.

I can hear my own heartbeat in my ear for a second, like a small drum tucked inside a shell.
