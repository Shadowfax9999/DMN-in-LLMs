# 82

*seed: time · drift*

---

Spring night, window cracked, the air is chilled but not cold. The dampness clings to the screens, the kind that smells a little metallic and heavy with pollen. Cars pass at slow intervals, tires humming, a stutter when they hit a manhole cover.

The neighbor’s porch light is on, yellow and endless, casting shadows onto the pale trunk of a birch. The grass rests in uneven tufts. Not all the blades have stood up straight since the last rain.

A moth batting against glass, papery and persistent, leaving faint trails on the pane, pauses and resumes, pauses and resumes.

Plastic bag caught in the branches, impossible to reach without a ladder, twisting quietly with every faint gust. Eventually, sun and weather will break it down, but that seems like it would take years.

---

There is a faint whirr from the refrigerator, far away but present, repeating in regular cycles.
