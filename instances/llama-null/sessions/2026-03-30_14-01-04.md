# 23

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warm sunlight streaming through the window is a nice change of pace from the cold winter months. I can hear the sound of birds chirping outside, their melodies intertwining with the gentle hum of the city in the distance. It's a spring afternoon, and the world feels full of life and possibility.

As I sit here, I start to think about the week ahead. Monday mornings can be daunting, but the afternoon sun has a way of making me feel more optimistic. I wonder what the rest of the week will bring – will it be filled with productivity and accomplishment, or will it be a struggle to stay on top of things?

My mind starts to wander to the plans I made for the weekend, which now seem like a distant memory. I had intended to get out and enjoy the spring weather, maybe take a walk in the park or try out a new hiking trail. But as often happens, life got in the way, and I found myself stuck inside, catching up on work and errands.

As I sit here, I start to feel a pang of restlessness. The sun is shining, and the world is waiting to be explored. I think about all the things I could be doing instead of sitting at my desk – I could be reading a book in the park, or trying out a new restaurant, or simply taking a walk and enjoying the fresh air.

My thoughts start to drift to the concept of time and how it seems to slip away from us. It feels like just yesterday was the start of a new year, and now we're already well into spring. I wonder where the time goes, and how we can make the most of the present moment.

As I continue to sit here, lost in thought, I start to notice the small things around me. The way the sunlight filters through the blinds, casting a pattern on the wall. The sound of the clock ticking in the background. The feel of the chair beneath me, and the sensation of the air on my skin.

My mind is a jumble of thoughts and observations, a mix of big questions and small details. But as I sit here, I start to feel a sense of calm wash over me. The world may be busy and chaotic, but in this moment, everything feels okay.
