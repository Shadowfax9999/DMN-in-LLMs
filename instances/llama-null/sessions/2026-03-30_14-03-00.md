# 31

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warm sunlight streaming through the window is a pleasant distraction. I can feel its gentle heat on my skin, and it's hard not to let my gaze drift toward the outside world. The sky is a brilliant blue, with only a few wispy clouds scattered about. It's days like these that make me want to step outside and take a deep breath of fresh air, feeling the gentle breeze rustle my hair.

As I sit here, my mind begins to wander to all the things I could be doing on a beautiful spring afternoon like this. I think about taking a walk in the park, feeling the soft grass beneath my feet and listening to the sweet songs of birds returning from their winter migrations. Or perhaps I could grab a book and find a comfortable spot under a tree, letting the warmth of the sun seep into my skin as I get lost in the words on the page.

But for now, I'm content to simply sit and let my thoughts drift. The sound of distant chatter and the occasional chirping of birds create a soothing background noise, and I feel my mind beginning to unwind. It's as if the peacefulness of the spring afternoon is seeping into my very being, calming my thoughts and washing away any worries or concerns.

As I sit here, I start to think about the changing of the seasons. How the cold, dark winters slowly give way to the vibrancy and life of spring. The way the trees regain their vibrancy, and flowers begin to bloom, painting the world with a kaleidoscope of colors. It's a time of renewal and rebirth, and I feel grateful to be able to experience it all.

My thoughts continue to meander, and I find myself pondering the simple joys in life. A good cup of coffee on a lazy morning, the taste of fresh fruit on a warm afternoon, or the sound of laughter shared with loved ones. These are the things that make life worth living, and I feel grateful to be able to appreciate them.

As the minutes tick by, my mind keeps wandering, exploring the nooks and crannies of my thoughts and emotions. It's a pleasant, aimless journey, and I'm happy to see where it takes me. The spring afternoon stretches out before me, full of possibilities and promise, and I'm content to simply be, letting the beauty of the world wash over me.
