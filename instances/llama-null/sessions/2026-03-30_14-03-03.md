# 32

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the sun on my skin, a gentle breeze rustling the leaves of the trees outside. It's a beautiful spring afternoon, and the world feels alive. I can hear the distant chirping of birds, their melodies intertwining with the hum of cars driving by.

As I sit here, I start to think about the smell of freshly cut grass. It's a scent that always brings back memories of childhood summers, playing in the park, and running through sprinklers on hot days. The smell is nostalgic, yet invigorating, and it makes me want to get outside and soak up the sunshine.

My mind begins to wander to all the things I could be doing on a day like today. I could take a walk in the nearby woods, feel the soft earth beneath my feet, and listen to the sound of the stream running through the trees. Or, I could grab a book and find a quiet spot in the park to read, feeling the warm sun on my face and the gentle breeze in my hair.

As I sit here, lost in thought, I start to think about the concept of time. How it slips away from us, moment by moment, never to be regained. And yet, in moments like these, time seems to stand still. The world slows down, and all that's left is the present, the here and now.

My thoughts start to drift to the people in my life, the ones I care about, and the ones who care about me. I think about the conversations we've had, the laughter we've shared, and the memories we've created together. I wonder what they're doing right now, if they're enjoying the beautiful spring afternoon as much as I am.

The sound of a bird chirping outside breaks my train of thought, and I'm brought back to the present moment. I look out the window, and the sunlight catches my eye, illuminating the dust particles dancing in the air. It's a reminder that even in the stillness, there's always movement, always change, and always something new to discover.

And so, I'll sit here for a while longer, soaking up the warmth, listening to the sounds of the world outside, and letting my thoughts wander freely, wherever they may lead.
