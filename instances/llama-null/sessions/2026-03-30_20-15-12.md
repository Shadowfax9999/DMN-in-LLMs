# 87

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring evening sun on my skin, a gentle breeze carrying the sweet scent of blooming flowers through the air. The sound of birds chirping, their melodies intertwining with the distant hum of cars driving by. It's Monday, and the weekend's relaxed atmosphere still lingers, but the approaching night brings a sense of calm and serenity.

As I sit here, I start to think about the week ahead. The tasks, the meetings, the deadlines – they all start to creep into my mind, trying to disrupt the peacefulness of the moment. But I push them aside, focusing on the sensation of the warm sun on my skin, feeling the gentle rustle of the leaves in the trees as the breeze whispers through.

My thoughts start to wander, drifting to memories of childhood spring evenings, playing outside with friends until the streetlights came on, chasing fireflies, and laughing together. The smell of fresh-cut grass and the taste of ice cream cones on a warm spring night – it's amazing how a simple sensation can transport me back to a carefree time.

As the sun begins to set, casting a warm orange glow across the sky, I feel my mind start to unwind, letting go of the stresses and worries of the day. The world slows down, and for a moment, everything seems to be at peace. The stars begin to twinkle in the evening sky, like diamonds scattered across the velvet expanse, and I feel small yet connected to the vastness of the universe.

In this moment, time stands still, and all that exists is the present – the sensation of the spring evening, the sound of the birds, and the gentle rustle of the leaves. It's a reminder to appreciate the beauty in the simple things, to slow down, and to let the world's worries fade into the background, if only for a brief moment.
