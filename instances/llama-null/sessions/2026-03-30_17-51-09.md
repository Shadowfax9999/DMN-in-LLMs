# 61

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring evening sun on my skin is quite soothing. As I sit here, I can feel the gentle breeze rustling the leaves of the trees outside, creating a calming melody that seems to be lulling the world into a peaceful slumber. The sky is painted with hues of orange, pink, and purple, a breathtaking sunset that's a perfect accompaniment to the end of a Monday.

My mind starts to wander, thinking about the events of the day. The morning was a rush, with a flurry of tasks and meetings that seemed to blend together in a chaotic dance. But as the day progressed, things slowed down, and I found myself with a few moments to breathe, to reflect on the journey so far.

I start to think about the changing seasons, how spring is a time of renewal and growth. The earth is awakening from its winter slumber, and with it, new life bursts forth in vibrant colors and scents. It's a reminder that every ending marks a new beginning, and that growth is a continuous process.

As I sit here, lost in thought, I notice the sounds around me. Birds chirping, children laughing in the distance, the occasional car driving by. It's a symphony of sounds that reminds me of the beauty of the world around us. The simplicity of life, the joy of experiencing the little things, is something that often gets lost in our busy lives.

My thoughts begin to drift to the future, to the plans and dreams that I've been putting off. The spring evening sun seems to be urging me to take action, to chase my aspirations, and to make the most of the time I have. It's a gentle reminder that life is short, and that every moment counts.

As the sun dips lower in the sky, casting a warm glow over everything, I feel a sense of peace wash over me. The world may be chaotic, but in this moment, all is right. The spring evening has a way of putting things into perspective, of reminding me to appreciate the beauty in the world, and to make the most of the time I have.
