# 67

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring evening sun on my skin is a nice sensation. I can almost smell the blooming flowers and fresh cut grass. It's as if the world is coming alive after a long, cold winter. The sound of birds chirping and children playing in the distance adds to the serene atmosphere.

As I breathe in the cool breeze, I start to think about the upcoming week. Monday evenings are always a bit of a struggle, trying to shake off the weekend blues and get back into the routine of things. But there's something about the spring season that makes it feel more manageable, more hopeful.

My mind starts to wander to all the things I want to do as the weather gets warmer. I think about taking long walks in the park, having picnics with friends, and trying out new outdoor activities like hiking or biking. The possibilities seem endless, and I feel a sense of excitement and anticipation.

As I continue to daydream, I start to think about the simple pleasures in life. A good cup of coffee on a quiet morning, a beautiful sunset, or a great book that transports me to another world. These small joys can make even the most mundane days feel special.

My thoughts begin to drift to the people in my life, and I wonder what they're up to at this moment. Are they enjoying the spring evening as much as I am? Are they looking forward to the week ahead, or are they dreading it? I think about sending a text or making a phone call to catch up with someone, just to share in the beauty of the moment.

As the sun begins to set, casting a golden glow over everything, I feel a sense of peace wash over me. It's as if the world is reminding me to slow down, appreciate the little things, and enjoy the journey. And in this moment, I feel grateful for the beauty of the spring evening, and the freedom to let my thoughts wander wherever they may go.
