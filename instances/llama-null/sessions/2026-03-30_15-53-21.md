# 46

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring sun on my skin is lovely. I can almost smell the fresh cut grass and blooming flowers. It's amazing how a simple change in seasons can completely transform the atmosphere. The cold, gloomy winters are behind us, and the world feels full of life and color again.

As I sit here, I start to think about all the things I want to do now that the weather is nicer. Maybe I'll take a walk in the park, or have a picnic with friends. The possibilities are endless, and I feel a sense of excitement and freedom.

My mind starts to wander to all the memories I've made during spring afternoons like this. Childhood birthday parties in the park, playing tag with friends until the streetlights came on, and lazy Sundays spent reading in the backyard. There's something about this time of year that feels carefree and nostalgic.

I wonder what the rest of the week will bring. Will I finally start that gardening project I've been putting off? Or maybe I'll take a weekend trip to the beach? The uncertainty is thrilling, and I feel grateful to have the opportunity to make the most of this beautiful spring afternoon.

As I sit here, lost in thought, I notice the sound of birds chirping outside. It's a gentle, soothing melody that seems to match the rhythm of my heartbeat. I feel my worries and concerns slowly drifting away, replaced by a sense of calm and contentment.

And then, my stomach growls, reminding me that it's almost time for a snack. Maybe I'll grab a cup of coffee and a pastry from the café down the street. The thought of a warm, flaky croissant and a rich, bold coffee is the perfect excuse to take a break and enjoy the spring afternoon...
