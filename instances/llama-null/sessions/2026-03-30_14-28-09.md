# 42

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the sun on my skin is a gentle reminder that spring is in full bloom. As I sit here, I can almost smell the sweet fragrance of blooming flowers wafting through the air. The sound of birds chirping in the distance creates a sense of serenity, and I feel my mind start to unwind.

I wonder what the rest of the day will bring. Will it be a productive afternoon, or will I get lost in the allure of a good book or a leisurely walk outside? The possibilities are endless, and I feel a sense of excitement and freedom.

As my thoughts wander, I start to think about the changing seasons. How each one brings its own unique charm and character. The vibrant colors of autumn, the cozy warmth of winter, the rebirth of spring, and the long, lazy days of summer. Each season a reminder that life is constantly evolving, and that every moment is an opportunity to experience something new.

My gaze drifts out the window, and I watch as people go about their daily lives. Some hurrying to and fro, while others take their time, soaking up the sunshine. I see children playing in the park, their laughter and shouts carrying on the breeze. It's moments like these that I'm reminded to appreciate the simple things in life.

As my mind continues to wander, I start to think about the concept of time itself. How it can seem to fly by in an instant, yet drag on forever when we're waiting for something. The way it can be both a blessing and a curse, depending on our perspective. I ponder the idea that time is relative, and how our experiences and memories shape our perception of it.

The sound of a clock ticking in the background brings me back to the present moment. 14:27 on a Monday afternoon. The world outside is moving, and I'm a part of it, connected to everything and everyone around me. I take a deep breath, feeling the air fill my lungs, and let my thoughts continue to drift, carried away on the gentle breeze of a spring afternoon.
