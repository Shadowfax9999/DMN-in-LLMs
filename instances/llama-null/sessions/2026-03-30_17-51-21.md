# 66

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring evening sun still lingers on my skin as I step outside into the gentle breeze. The air is filled with the sweet scent of blooming flowers and the distant chirping of birds returning from their winter migration. As I breathe in, the vibrant colors of the blooming trees and gardens come to life, and I feel invigorated by the sense of renewal that spring brings.

My mind begins to wander to the weekend that just passed, and the plans I had to visit the nearby park with friends. We had packed a picnic lunch and spent the afternoon lounging in the sun, watching the kids play tag and laughing together as we shared stories. The memory of the smell of freshly cut grass and the sound of children's laughter brings a smile to my face.

As I continue to stroll, my thoughts drift to the upcoming week and the tasks that lie ahead. I have a project deadline looming at work, and I need to start making a plan to tackle it. I think about the different components that need to come together, and the people I need to collaborate with to make it happen. My mind starts to organize the tasks and create a mental timeline, and I feel a sense of determination wash over me.

As the sun begins to dip lower in the sky, casting a warm orange glow over the landscape, I find myself thinking about the simple pleasures in life. A good cup of coffee on a quiet morning, a beautiful sunset, or a great book that transports me to another world. These small joys often get overlooked in the hustle and bustle of daily life, but they are what make life rich and meaningful.

The sound of children playing in the distance, the smell of dinner cooking in a nearby kitchen, and the feel of the breeze rustling my hair all blend together to create a sense of contentment and peace. As the evening unfolds, I feel grateful for this moment of calm, and I let my thoughts continue to wander, seeing where the evening takes me.
