# 96

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring evening sunshine still lingers on my skin, even as the stars begin to twinkle in the night sky. I can hear the distant chirping of crickets and the occasional hoot of an owl, their melodies intertwining with the gentle rustle of leaves in the breeze. The scent of blooming flowers wafts through the air, a sweet and intoxicating aroma that fills my senses.

As I sit here, my mind starts to wander to the events of the day. The morning was filled with the usual routine, but the afternoon brought a sense of excitement and anticipation. I had a conversation with a friend about our plans for the upcoming summer, and the possibilities seem endless. We talked about road trips, outdoor adventures, and lazy days spent lounging by the beach.

My thoughts drift to the books I've been reading lately, and the ones I've been meaning to get to. I've been fascinated by the concept of time and how it shapes our perceptions of reality. The way it flows, sometimes slowly, sometimes quickly, and how it affects our memories and experiences. I ponder the idea of time travel, and the possibilities it presents.

As the night deepens, my attention turns to the world around me. The stars are shining brighter now, and the moon is a silver crescent in the sky. I feel a sense of connection to the universe, a sense of being a part of something much larger than myself. The worries and concerns of the day seem to fade away, replaced by a sense of wonder and awe.

The sound of a car driving by breaks the spell, and I'm brought back to the present moment. I realize that I've been lost in thought for a while, and the world around me has continued to move and change. I smile to myself, feeling grateful for the freedom to let my mind wander, and the beauty of the spring evening that surrounds me.
