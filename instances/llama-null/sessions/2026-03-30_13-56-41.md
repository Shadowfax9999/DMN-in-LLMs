# 6

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warm sunlight streaming through the window is a welcome change from the chilly winter months. As I sit here, I notice the gentle breeze rustling the leaves of the trees outside, creating a soothing melody that seems to lull the world into a peaceful slumber. The air is filled with the sweet scent of blooming flowers, and I can almost taste the freshness of the season.

My mind begins to wander to the possibilities that spring brings. Perhaps a walk in the park, feeling the soft grass beneath my feet and watching the vibrant colors of nature unfold. Or maybe a trip to the nearby farmers' market, where I can sample the ripest strawberries and crunchiest asparagus of the season.

As I sit here, lost in thought, I start to think about the concept of time. The clock on the wall reads 13:56, and I wonder what the rest of the day has in store. Will it be a productive afternoon, filled with tasks and accomplishments, or a leisurely one, spent reading a book or simply enjoying the warmth of the sun?

My gaze drifts out the window, and I notice the people passing by, each with their own story and destination. Some are hurrying to and fro, while others are strolling hand in hand, taking in the sights and sounds of the season. I wonder where they're going, what they're thinking, and what their day will bring.

As my thoughts continue to wander, I start to think about the little things in life that bring me joy. A good cup of coffee, a beautiful sunset, a good conversation with a friend. These small pleasures often get overlooked in our busy lives, but they're what make each day worth living.

And then, my mind starts to drift to more abstract ideas. The concept of free will, the mysteries of the universe, the intricacies of human connection. I ponder the what-ifs and the maybes, the could-haves and the should-haves. The more I think, the more questions I have, and the more I realize how little I know.

As the minutes tick by, I'm brought back to the present moment. The clock now reads 14:01, and I'm left with a sense of wonder, a sense of awe, and a sense of gratitude for this beautiful spring afternoon, full of possibilities and promise.
