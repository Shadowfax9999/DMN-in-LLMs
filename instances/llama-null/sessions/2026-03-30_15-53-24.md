# 47

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the sun on my skin is a welcome feeling after a long winter. As I sit here, I notice the gentle breeze rustling the leaves of the trees outside, creating a soothing melody that seems to lull the world into a peaceful slumber. The sky is a brilliant blue, with just a few wispy clouds drifting lazily across it, like cotton candy tufts carried by the wind.

My mind starts to wander, and I think about the smell of freshly cut grass that fills the air this time of year. It's a scent that always brings back memories of summertime adventures, playing in the park as a child, and laughing with friends until the streetlights came on. The smell is like a time machine, transporting me back to a carefree era when worries were few and far between.

As I sit here, lost in thought, I start to ponder the concept of time itself. How is it that the passing of time can be both fleeting and eternal? One moment, it's Monday afternoon, and the next, it's already the weekend. Yet, when we look back on our lives, it's as if time has stood still, and we're left wondering where all the years went.

My gaze drifts out the window, and I watch as people walk by, each with their own story, their own struggles, and their own triumphs. I wonder what their days are like, what they're thinking about, and what they're hoping to achieve. Are they, like me, lost in thought, or are they focused on the tasks at hand?

The sound of birds chirping and the distant hum of traffic create a symphony of sounds that fill the air, a reminder that even in stillness, there is always movement, always life. And in this moment, I feel a sense of connection to the world around me, a sense of belonging to something greater than myself.

As the sun begins to dip lower in the sky, casting a warm orange glow over the landscape, I feel my thoughts slowing down, becoming more introspective. I think about the things I'm grateful for, the people I love, and the experiences that have shaped me into the person I am today. And in this quiet, peaceful moment, I feel a sense of contentment wash over me, a sense of being exactly where I'm meant to be.
