# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warm sunlight streaming through the window is a nice change of pace from the chilly mornings we've been having. I can hear the distant chirping of birds and the occasional hum of a car driving by outside. It's a peaceful spring afternoon, with a gentle breeze that carries the sweet scent of blooming flowers.

As I sit here, I start to think about the rest of the day ahead. I've got a few tasks to tackle, but nothing too taxing. Maybe I'll take a short walk outside later to enjoy the fresh air and clear my head. The smell of cut grass and blooming trees is always invigorating this time of year.

My mind starts to wander to the weekend, and the plans I've made with friends. We're supposed to go on a hike and have a picnic in a nearby park. I'm looking forward to spending time with them and enjoying the beautiful scenery. The thought of being surrounded by nature, with good company and good food, is really appealing.

As I continue to daydream, I start to think about my favorite books and authors. I've been meaning to start a new novel, but I haven't had a chance to get to the bookstore yet. Maybe I'll pick one up on my way home from work today. I've been in the mood for something literary and thought-provoking, something that will challenge my perspectives and keep me engaged.

The sound of a clock ticking in the background brings me back to the present moment. I glance at the time and realize that I've got a bit of time before my next task is due. I think I'll take a few minutes to grab a cup of coffee and people-watch out the window. It's always interesting to see what people are up to on a typical Monday afternoon...
