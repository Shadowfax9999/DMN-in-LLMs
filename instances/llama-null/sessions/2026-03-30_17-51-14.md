# 63

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring evening sun on my skin, a gentle breeze carrying the sweet scent of blooming flowers. As I sit here, I start to feel my mind unwind, letting go of the stresses of the day. The sound of birds chirping in the distance creates a soothing melody, a symphony of nature that calms the mind.

I think about the weekend that just passed, and the memories that were created. A hike in the nearby woods, the thrill of discovering hidden streams and secret meadows. The taste of a freshly brewed cup of coffee on a lazy Sunday morning, savoring the quiet moments before the chaos of the week begins.

My thoughts start to wander to the books I've been meaning to read, the ones that have been sitting on my shelf for months, waiting to be devoured. I imagine getting lost in the pages of a fantasy novel, exploring new worlds and meeting new characters. The thrill of the unknown, the adventure that awaits, it's exhilarating.

As I continue to sit here, I start to notice the little things around me. The way the light filters through the leaves of the trees, casting dappled shadows on the ground. The sound of children playing in the distance, their laughter and shouts carrying on the wind. It's moments like these that I feel grateful for, the simple joys in life that often go unnoticed.

My mind starts to drift to the future, to the plans and dreams that I've been putting off. The trips I want to take, the places I want to see, the experiences I want to have. I think about the people I want to meet, the connections I want to make, the memories I want to create. The possibilities seem endless, and it's both exciting and terrifying at the same time.

As the sun begins to set, casting a golden glow over the landscape, I feel a sense of peace wash over me. The world slows down, and for a moment, everything feels right with the world. It's a feeling that's hard to put into words, but it's one that I've come to cherish, a sense of contentment and wonder that comes from simply being present in the moment.
