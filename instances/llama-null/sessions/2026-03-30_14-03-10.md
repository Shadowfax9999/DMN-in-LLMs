# 35

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warm sunlight streaming through the window is a welcome respite from the chill of morning. As I sit here, I can feel the gentle breeze rustling the leaves of the trees outside, carrying the sweet scent of blooming flowers into the room. The sound of birds chirping and the distant hum of a lawnmower create a soothing background melody, a symphony of springtime sounds that seem to lull the world into a state of relaxation.

My gaze wanders to the clock on the wall, and I notice the time: 14:03. The afternoon is still young, full of possibilities and promise. I think about all the things I could do with this spare time – take a walk, read a book, or simply sit and watch the world go by. The freedom to choose is exhilarating, and I feel a sense of excitement and anticipation building up inside me.

As my mind begins to wander, I start to think about the changing seasons. Spring is a time of renewal and growth, a season of transformation and rebirth. The snow is melting, the days are growing longer, and the world is slowly coming back to life. It's a time of hope and new beginnings, a reminder that every ending marks a new start.

My thoughts drift to the memories of springs past, of laughter and adventures, of warm sunshine and cool rain. I remember the smell of freshly cut grass, the taste of juicy strawberries, and the sound of children playing outside. The nostalgia washes over me, and I feel a sense of longing for those carefree days of childhood.

But as I sit here, lost in thought, I'm brought back to the present by the sound of a car driving by outside. The world is still moving, still spinning, and I'm a part of it. I take a deep breath, feeling the air fill my lungs, and let my gaze fall upon the world outside. The sun is shining, the flowers are blooming, and the possibilities are endless. What will the rest of this spring afternoon bring?
