# 25

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the sun on my skin is quite pleasant. I can almost smell the blooming flowers and fresh cut grass. It's amazing how a simple change in seasons can lift one's mood. The sound of birds chirping and leaves rustling in the gentle breeze is like music to my ears.

As I sit here, I start to think about all the things I could be doing on a beautiful spring afternoon like this. I could take a walk in the park, have a picnic, or simply sit outside and read a book. The possibilities are endless.

My mind begins to wander to a memory from my childhood. I must have been around 8 or 9 years old. My family and I went on a picnic in a nearby park. We brought all sorts of food, including sandwiches, fruit, and cookies. I remember running around, playing tag with my siblings, and laughing until my sides hurt.

As I continue to reminisce, I start to think about the concept of time. How is it that something as simple as a season can evoke such strong memories and emotions? It's as if the passing of time is what gives our experiences meaning and depth.

I start to ponder the idea of nostalgia. Is it a bittersweet feeling, a longing for something that's lost, or is it a celebration of the good times we've had? Perhaps it's a little bit of both. Nostalgia allows us to appreciate the present moment while also cherishing the memories of the past.

My train of thought is interrupted by the sound of a bird singing outside my window. I get up to take a look, and I'm greeted by a beautiful robin perched on a branch. Its brown back and red breast are a stunning sight. I watch as it sings its heart out, oblivious to my presence.

As I stand there, taking in the beauty of nature, I start to think about the interconnectedness of all things. The bird's song is a reminder that we're not alone in this world. There's a whole ecosystem out there, full of creatures big and small, each playing their own unique role.

My mind continues to wander, exploring the intricacies of the natural world and our place within it. The warmth of the sun, the sound of the bird's song, and the memories of the past all blend together, creating a sense of wonder and awe that's hard to put into words.
