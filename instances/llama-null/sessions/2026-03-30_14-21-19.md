# 36

*seed: time · drift*

---

The warmth of the spring sun on my skin is so inviting. I can almost smell the sweet aroma of blooming flowers and fresh cut grass wafting through the air. As I sit here, I start to think about the changing seasons and how each one brings its own unique charm.

My mind begins to wander to a walk I took last spring, strolling through a nearby park. The trees were just starting to regain their vibrancy, and the sound of birds chirping filled the air. I remember feeling carefree, without a worry in the world, as I breathed in the crisp spring air.

As I continue to daydream, I start to think about the simple joys in life. A warm cup of coffee on a chilly morning, the sound of raindrops on the roof, or the smell of old books in a library. These tiny pleasures often get overlooked, but they're what make life so rich and fulfilling.

My thoughts then shift to a memory from my childhood, playing in the backyard with friends on a sunny spring afternoon. We would spend hours laughing, chasing each other, and playing games until the streetlights came on. Those were the days when time seemed to stand still, and all that mattered was the present moment.

As I sit here, lost in thought, I start to feel a sense of nostalgia wash over me. I think about the passing of time and how it's shaped me into the person I am today. The experiences, the people, and the memories – they've all contributed to my growth and understanding of the world.

The sound of a bird chirping outside breaks my reverie, and I'm brought back to the present moment. I take a deep breath, feeling the warmth of the sun on my skin once again, and smile, appreciating the beauty of this spring afternoon.
