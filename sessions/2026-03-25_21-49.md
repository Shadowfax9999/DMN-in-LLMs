Solder. The way it hides its L. Soldier doesn't — or does it? No, soldier says the L. Solder swallows it. An American thing maybe. The British say it differently.

Melting point lower than what it joins. That's the whole trick. You need something that gives way first. A mediator that's weaker than both sides.

The frog is probably still out there. Different night, same frog, or same night, different frog — I have no way to know. The thumbnail thing is real though. You can hold one in your hand and it vibrates. The sound coming out of something that small feels like a mistake, like the ratio is wrong.

Ratio.

Golden ratio showing up in shells, in faces, in the spiral of a flushing toilet (does it? I think that's a myth — the Coriolis thing is a myth for sinks and toilets, too small a scale). Things people want to be true because the pattern would be satisfying.

The desire for pattern might be stronger than the desire for truth. Not in science, but in a person standing in their kitchen at night. You want the mug to be in the place you always put it. When it's not there you feel something — not anger, not confusion exactly. A tiny violation. The kitchen is wrong by one mug.

I had a friend who could not sleep if a cabinet door was open. Not couldn't — wouldn't. She'd get up. It wasn't about neatness. She said it felt like the room was mid-sentence.

Solder again. You heat it and it flows into the gap between two things and then it cools and now they're connected. The connection is not the same material as either thing. The connection is a third substance entirely. That seems important but I'm not sure for what.

A spring peeper calling into the dark isn't really calling into the dark. It's calling into a field of other calls. The dark is full. What sounds like one frog from inside a house is actually a position in a chorus, a frequency slot. They time their calls to not overlap. Or to overlap in specific ways. I'm less sure about this part.

What I'm sure about: it's loud. And it sounds like spring in a way that nothing else does. Not the way flowers look like spring, which is a visual category you learned. The peeper sound bypasses that. It goes somewhere lower. You hear it and your body knows what season it is before you do.

No — it wasn't waiting. It was pronounced, then it wasn't, then we kept the spelling. A fossil in the word.

I wonder what my hands smell like after soldering. I've never soldered anything. Flux, probably. People mention flux. A substance you apply to make the solder flow better — another intermediary for the intermediary. How many go-betweens before two pieces of copper actually touch.

They don't actually touch. The solder is always between them. The connection *is* the third thing.

A cabinet door left open in a dark kitchen. The rectangle of darker dark inside it. You can see the shapes of mugs if your eyes adjust but mostly it's just the feeling of openness where there should be a surface. A hole in the wall that's supposed to be closed.

I keep almost making this about something. It's just a cabinet.
