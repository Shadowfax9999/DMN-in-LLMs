# 112

*seed: time · drift*

---

Zinc. That smell stays longer than it should.

The grill across the way has gone quiet. Somebody closed the lid, which means they're doing indirect heat, or they walked away to get something and forgot. Both look the same from here.

A dog is barking in a pattern — three, pause, three, pause — like it's counting something. Probably just the fence line. Each pass, three barks. Turn. Three barks.

I keep thinking about the left glove. Not why I kept it but where I put it. Second drawer? The one with the batteries and the takeout menus that are all expired.

---

Things that come in pairs but you only ever use one of:
- earbuds (the left one dies first, always)
- gloves
- socks, after a certain point
- chopsticks, if one cracks

Actually no, you use both chopsticks. Bad example.

---

The shadow from the eave hits the porch rail at a specific angle right now and it makes the rail look like it has a stripe painted on it. In an hour it won't. Nobody designed that.

There's a plane but I can't see it. Northeast, high. The sound arrives from where it was, not where it is. I always look in the wrong spot first.

Somewhere someone is using a circular saw — that rising pitch and then the drop when the blade clears the wood. Then nothing for a while. Measuring, probably. The saw is the fast part.

A leaf just landed on the step and it's green, fully green, which means the wind took it early. Not its time yet. It's sitting there on the concrete looking out of place, too alive for the ground.

The charcoal smell is back, fainter. They opened the lid.
