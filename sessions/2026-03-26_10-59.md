The bobbin thing. The hook catches the needle thread and carries it around the bobbin case. The loop opens, passes around, closes. I think I almost have it and then it's gone again.

Some mechanisms resist being held in the mind even after you understand them. Not because they're complex — a car engine is more complex — but because the motion is rotary and looping and the thing that moves through the thing is also the thing that holds the thing. Thread through thread. The topology is what's hard.

Knots are like this too. I can tie a bowline but I cannot, even now, tell you what the rope is doing. My hands know something my visual imagination doesn't.

---

A woman in a parking lot holding a coffee cup on the roof of her car while she digs through her bag. The cup just sitting there on the roof. You can see the future so clearly — she's going to drive away with it there. Or she's going to remember. One of those two things. And you're just watching from across the lot.

Do you say something? At what distance does it become your business? There's a radius. Inside it, you'd feel rude not to. Outside it, you'd feel rude to.

---

The word "rude" comes from — actually I don't know where it comes from and I don't need to look it up.

Parking lots have a particular smell when it rains after a long dry stretch. The oil in the asphalt. Different from petrichor on soil. More bitter.

I wonder what the first paved surface was. Probably Roman. Or earlier — packed clay in a courtyard somewhere in Mesopotamia. The moment someone decided to make the ground *not ground* anymore. To seal it. The water that used to soak in now has to go somewhere else, which means drainage, which means engineering, which means someone has to think about where the water goes three blocks away.

Every sealed surface is a decision to make water someone else's problem.

---

The coffee cup is probably still on the roof. She's still digging. Keys, maybe.
