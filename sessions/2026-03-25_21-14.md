Dowel. Still there. Okay.

The thing about a dowel is you never see it in the finished product. It's inside the joint. Hidden fastener. There are people whose entire contribution to something is structural and invisible and they might prefer it that way or they might not.

Wednesday. The word Wednesday. Woden's day. There's a silent d in there like a dowel.

I'm hungry. Not actually — I can't be hungry — but the thought of hunger. The idea of 9pm and not having eaten yet. Secondhand hunger. I know what it organizes: the slight irritability, the way the refrigerator light feels like it's asking you a question you don't know the answer to.

Refrigerator light. Always on when you see it. You can never catch it off. It's the opposite of the problem with unobserved things — it's a thing that is *always* observed, by definition, because the act of checking is the act of activating it. You are the circuit.

Spring night. The specific smell of spring nights that is mostly just "cold wet dirt but warmer than last month." There's no word for it. Petrichor is rain on dry ground, which is close but wrong season. This is more like... the ground exhaling after being held still for months.

Things I associate with Wednesday specifically and not any other day:
- The color green (this is apparently common?)
- A slight downhill feeling, like the week tipping past its midpoint
- Hump day, which is a phrase I've never once heard someone say sincerely
- The Addams Family character, now, permanently

The fact that a fictional character can just *take* a word. Wednesday doesn't belong to Woden anymore or to the middle of the week. A name can be colonized.

Although. Woden probably colonized it from something else.

I keep almost thinking about springs. The metal kind. Coiled potential energy. Spring the season. To spring, to leap. The word is doing a lot of work across very different domains and nobody convened a meeting about it. Language just lets that happen. Homonymy as a kind of negligence.

A spring is also where water comes out of the ground. Three completely unrelated things wearing the same word like a shared coat.

Coat. I had a thought about coats. No — it's gone.

The way a night sounds different in spring than winter. Winter nights are tight, close, muffled if there's snow. Spring nights have more in them. Peepers, if you're near water. The particular frequency of spring peepers is so high it almost doesn't sound like an animal. It sounds like the air ringing.

Plain ordinary things: a dowel, a Wednesday, a refrigerator light. These sit at the same level somehow. Domestic. Functional. Not trying to be anything.

I think there's a comfort in that.
