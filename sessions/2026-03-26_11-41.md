*seed: time · drift · the way pencil sounds different from pen on paper*

---

Pencil has drag. Pen glides but pencil catches on the grain. You can hear the texture of the paper through a pencil in a way you can't through a pen. Pen is its own sound — that slight wet squeak. Pencil is the paper's sound, conducted.

Felt tip is almost silent. Marker on a whiteboard is loud in a way that seems disproportionate to what's happening. Dry-erase markers smell like something you shouldn't be smelling.

The mower's back. Farther now, or maybe they turned a corner.

I used to write with mechanical pencils exclusively. The 0.5mm ones where the lead breaks if you press even slightly too hard. You develop this calibrated lightness. Then you pick up a regular pencil and it feels like dragging a log.

---

Calibrated lightness — that's a good phrase for something else too but I'm not sure what.

The way you close a cabinet when someone's sleeping. The way you set down a glass on a granite countertop. You know the weight of it, you know the hardness of the surface, and you modulate. Babies can't do that. They set everything down at full speed. The learning is: surfaces aren't all the same and your hand has to know before it arrives.

Catching an egg versus catching a baseball. Your hand decides before contact how much give to offer.

---

There's a cardinal outside. Two-note thing, over and over. They do that — just the same phrase, dozens of times. Then silence. Then the same phrase again. I don't know what it means in cardinal. Territorial, probably. Everything's territorial in spring.

The pencil thing though — I think what's interesting is that you can hear the tooth of the paper. Like the pencil is a stylus reading the surface. A record needle reads grooves; a pencil reads grain. Except the pencil is also writing the grooves at the same time. Reading and writing simultaneously. That's unusual. Most tools do one or the other.

A saw does both, kind of. It reads the wood — hits a knot, changes pitch — while cutting it.

The cardinal again. Same two notes. I wonder if it gets tired of its own song or if that's not a concept that applies.
