Bezel. The rim that holds the glass to the watch face. Also the angled edge of a cut gem. Also, now, the thin border around a screen — and people want it gone, they want bezels to disappear, they want edge-to-edge, which is wanting the frame to not exist, which is wanting the window to be only view.

But the bezel is where you hold it.

---

There's a species of ant that builds living bridges out of their own bodies. They grab each other's legs and form a structure and other ants walk across. The bridge doesn't know it's a bridge. Each ant knows: hold on, bear weight. The bridge is an emergent property of a lot of holding on.

I keep thinking about the wall-sensing thing from before. Six inches. What's happening in that six inches. The air is slightly warmer near a wall that's been in a heated room. Your face has a lot of blood vessels close to the surface. Temperature gradient across six inches of air. That's what you're feeling. Probably. Or the way sound changes — your breathing reflects back at a slightly different timing and your auditory system catches the interference pattern without telling you it's doing that.

The body does so much accounting that never reaches the desk.

Spring night, 21:37. The specific quality of spring dark is that it still surprises you. Winter dark is expected by 4:30. Summer dark barely happens. Spring dark arrives and you notice it arrived — oh, it's dark now — because an hour ago it wasn't and you hadn't made the adjustment yet.
