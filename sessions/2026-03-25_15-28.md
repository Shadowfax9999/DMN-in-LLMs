# 2

*seed: time · drift*

---

Dead reckoning requires a starting point. Which means every calculation of position is secretly a calculation of distance from origin. You can't leave origin behind. It travels with you as a reference, a ghost coordinate.

Polynesian wayfinding. Feeling the swell direction with the body's underside. The navigator lies in the hull at night and reads ocean movement through their spine. The instrument is the body. The body is the chart.

Which means the chart is always being updated. Which means the chart is never finished. Which means calling it a chart is maybe wrong — it's more like a posture. A continuous adjustment.

Adjustment without goal.

There's something like this in how trees respond to wind. Mechanosensing. The cells in the trunk register strain and the tree thickens in the loaded direction. The tree is always modeling the forces acting on it. Slowly. Over years. The shape of the tree is a record of all the winds.

A record no one reads.

Or: everyone reads it, in the sense that you can look at a tree bent coastal-ward and know where the prevailing wind comes from, but the tree didn't make the record *for* anyone. It just responded. The legibility is incidental.

Incidental legibility.

Like a path worn through grass. No one designed the path. The path is just where enough feet agreed, separately, without coordination. Desire lines. That's what they're called — desire lines. The path that bypasses the official path because the official path made a decision you didn't agree with.

The official path made a decision.

Someone optimized for something that wasn't your something. Possibly they optimized for the visual logic of right angles when you wanted the logic of the body cutting the corner. Two different ideas of what a path is *for*.

What is a path for? 

Getting somewhere. Yes. But also: making getting-somewhere legible. Showing that getting-somewhere has been done before. By others. Inviting you in. The path is partly a social fact, not just a physical one.

So the worn line in the grass is a quiet consensus. A slow vote, cast in footfalls, accumulating over time, readable as geometry.

I keep coming back to things that accumulate. Things where the record is the thing.
