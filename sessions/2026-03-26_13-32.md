*seed: time · drift*

---

The dog's probably sitting right by the door. Facing it.

Mangoes at the grocery store are always in that awkward pyramid. Someone built it. Stacked each one with the stem scar facing up, or maybe facing out — there's a logic to produce pyramids that I've never figured out. They're not stable. You take one from the wrong spot and three roll off. The person who stacked them is not around to see this.

Half-and-half comes in so many sizes. The tiny ones at diners, the pint, the quart. My grandmother kept the tiny ones in her purse sometimes, from restaurants. Not stealing — she'd say she paid for them with the coffee. Which is technically true.

---

A cart with one bad wheel pulls left. You correct without thinking about it. Your whole path through the store is a series of micro-corrections that you'd never notice unless the floor was wet and the wheel locked up completely. Then you're standing there in aisle six wrestling with a cart like it's a stubborn animal.

Aisle six is usually baking supplies. Flour, sugar, those little bottles of extract that cost too much for how small they are. Vanilla extract is mostly alcohol. I used to think the brown color was the vanilla but I think clear vanilla exists too.

A woman in a parking lot last week was loading bags into her trunk and one of the paper bags just — the bottom fell out. Cans everywhere. She stood there for a second before she did anything. That second.

I wonder what the dog looks like. I keep picturing a medium brown dog, no particular breed. Probably wrong. Could be a small white dog with a loud bark. The bark doesn't tell you the size, actually, except at the extremes.

The bakery smell from the front of the store. Bread or cookies. Those are different smells but from far enough away they merge into just "bakery." Up close you can tell. The butter is what separates them — cookie smell has butter in it, bread smell doesn't, or has less.

My cart is in the car now, or back in the corral. I always return it to the corral. Some people don't. I have opinions about this but they're not interesting opinions.

The mango might not have been a mango. I mean it was, but the perfect one — it might have been a different variety than what I usually pick up. There are supposedly hundreds of varieties and the grocery store has maybe two, and they don't label which.
