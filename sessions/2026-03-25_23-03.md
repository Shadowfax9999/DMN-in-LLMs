# 58

*seed: time · drift · undertow*

---

Undertow is the wrong word for what it describes. The actual danger isn't a current pulling you under — it's a rip current pulling you *out*. Lateral, not vertical. The water doesn't want you down. It wants you elsewhere.

People drown because they fight it. Swim parallel to shore. Everyone knows this. Almost nobody does it.

---

A parking garage at night has a specific acoustic. Tires on coated concrete make a sound like tearing fabric, very slowly. Every surface is hard. Your footsteps come back to you from three directions and you can't tell which is the original. You walk faster without deciding to.

The ramps. Parking garages are one of the few places most people experience a helix. You drive in circles and end up above where you started. It should feel stranger than it does.

---

Trying to remember the layout of a house I haven't been in for twenty years. The kitchen is on the left when you walk in. There's a step down. The step down might be wrong — it might be the other house, the one on Birch Street. The two houses have merged in memory. The wallpaper from one is now in the rooms of the other. A composite house that never existed, more vivid than either real one.

The bathroom had blue tile. I'm almost certain. Or green tile that I'm remembering as blue because someone told me a story about blue tile somewhere else.

There was a drawer in that kitchen that didn't open all the way because something behind it blocked it. You could get your hand in. You could feel around. But you could never see what was in the back. I never found out what was blocking it. Probably a pipe. Probably nothing interesting. But the drawer is the thing I remember most clearly — more clearly than the faces of the people who lived there.

A stuck drawer is a more durable memory than a person. That's not a commentary on anything. It's just what happened.

---

Rip currents. Helical ramps. Drawers that don't open. Things that resist you in a specific direction.

A jar lid that's been vacuum-sealed. You hit the bottom and the seal breaks with that pop and then it turns easily. All the resistance was in the first millimeter.

---

Sometimes a song comes on and you're in a car that you haven't been in since 2004. Not remembering being in the car — *being* in the car. The seatbelt buckle is hot from the sun. The window is down two inches. The song isn't even one you liked.
