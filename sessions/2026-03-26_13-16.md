*seed: time · drift*

Figured out how to hold it. That's most of learning, really.

The gutter across the street has grass growing out of it. Not weeds exactly — actual grass, like a little strip of lawn up there in the aluminum channel. Somebody's lawn clippings got up there, or seeds blew. Either way it's thriving.

A blue truck just went by too fast.

I used to think all pickup trucks were the same truck. Like there was one archetype and everything else was a variation. Now I notice the differences immediately — the squared-off ones from the late eighties, the rounded ones from the mid-nineties that were trying to be aerodynamic, the new ones that are so tall the bed is at chest height. You'd need a step stool to load Quikrete into one of those.

The dog stopped. When did the dog stop.

There's a sprinkler going somewhere I can't see — just the sound of it, that tik-tik-tik-tik-tik and then the long hiss of the sweep, and then tik-tik-tik-tik-tik again. Sprinklers used to be the whole sound of summer afternoons. That and screen doors.

---

Three things I can't picture no matter how hard I try:

- my own face from the side
- the back of a shelf while the shelf is against the wall
- what a room looks like when I leave it

---

The grass in the gutter is going to die when it gets hot. It doesn't have enough soil up there. Maybe a quarter inch of silt and decomposed leaf matter. It doesn't know that though. It's just growing because the conditions were close enough.

I had a coworker once who called every small project a "quick Quikrete job" whether or not concrete was involved. Replacing a light switch was a quick Quikrete job. Nobody ever asked him what he meant by it.

The sprinkler's still going.
