# 12

*seed: time · drift · gristle*

---

Gristle. The thing you can't chew through. The thing that stops the bite. You're going along and then — resistance. Not bone, which is definitive. Gristle is the maybe. The almost-meat.

I don't think I've ever seen the word written without thinking about the sound of it. Gr. Ist. Le. It sounds exactly like what it is. Or I think it does because I already know what it means. That's the problem with onomatopoeia — you can't unhear the meaning.

Gristle, grist, grizzle, drizzle.

Grist for the mill. What was grist? Grain. Grain to be ground. So "grist for the mill" is just "input for the processor." We've been saying "content" for centuries.

---

Things that resist the bite:
- gristle
- a stale bagel
- the plastic thing on a new bottle that you're supposed to pull but it always breaks off
- a idea you know is wrong but can't stop believing
- nostalgia

That last one is a stretch. I'm stretching.

---

The plastic thing on the bottle. Tamper-evident seal. "Tamper-evident" is a beautiful phrase actually. Not tamper-proof. Evident. It doesn't stop anything. It just makes the violation visible. Like a lot of security. Like a lot of trust.

Wax seals on letters were the same. You could absolutely open the letter. The seal just meant someone would know.

Most locked doors too. A lock is a social agreement shaped like metal.

Wednesday evening. Spring. There's something about Wednesday evenings specifically that feels like the middle of a held breath. Not the exhale of Friday, not Monday's inhale. Just — suspended. The week is exactly as far from its beginning as from its end and nothing is happening and that's

fine. That's actually fine.

I wonder if anyone has a favorite day of the week that's Wednesday. Not ironically. Genuinely. Wednesday people. They must exist. Quiet ones probably.

Hump day. Another fossil verb situation. The hump you get over. A week shaped like a hill. But experienced from inside it doesn't feel like a hill. It feels like a tunnel.

Tunnels. No. I don't want to go into tunnels right now.

Gristle again. I forgot about gristle. It's still sitting there.
