*seed: time · drift*

The parking lot at the dentist's office has those yellow concrete bumpers and one of them is cracked almost in half. Every time I'm there I notice it. I don't know why that one cracked and the others didn't.

---

Harvest. Nine years is a long time to wait for bark. Bamboo grows something like three feet in a day under the right conditions. Those are opposite strategies for being a plant.

There's a third one — annuals. Just do everything in one season and die. Wheat does that. Most vegetables do that. A tomato plant doesn't plan to survive winter. It plans to not need to.

Perennial, annual, biennial. Biennials are the weird ones. Two years. What takes exactly two years? Carrots, if you let them flower. Parsley. Most people never see parsley flower because they harvest it the first year.

I've never seen parsley flower.

---

The hygienist always asks if you've been flossing and you both know you're going to say "not as much as I should" and she's going to say "try to do it every day" and then this exact conversation will happen again in six months. It's not even a lie exactly. It's a script. Both people know it's a script. Neither person deviates from it.

There are a lot of conversations like that. "How are you" / "good, how are you." The thing at a cash register where they say "have a good one" and you say "you too." Sometimes you say "you too" to things that don't make sense, like when a waiter says "enjoy your meal" and you say "you too" and then just have to keep walking.

The word "script" is interesting for that because actors follow scripts but the whole point is to make it seem like they're not. These social scripts work the opposite way — everyone knows it's a script and that's what makes it comfortable.

I think elevator small talk about weather serves the same function. You're not exchanging information about whether it's cold outside. You're both already outside. You both know it's cold. You're confirming that you're both the kind of person who will follow the script, which means you're both safe to be in a small box with for thirty seconds.

Dogs do something similar with the sniffing.

---

The cracked bumper — I bet it's rebar expansion. Water gets into a hairline crack, rusts the rebar inside, rust expands, and that slow pressure splits the concrete from within. Years of that.
