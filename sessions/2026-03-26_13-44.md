*seed: time · drift · twine*

---

Twine. Not string, not rope. Twine specifically. The rough kind that comes on a spool at the hardware store, the color of grocery bags. I had a ball of it in a junk drawer for years and used it maybe three times. Once to tie a box shut for moving. Once to hang something from a nail, I can't remember what. The third time I don't remember at all but the ball got smaller so I must have.

The parking lot has those concrete bumpers at the end of each space, the ones your tires hit. Some of them are crooked. One is completely sideways, like someone pushed it hard enough to rotate it but not hard enough to move it out of the space entirely. There's a dumpster enclosure with a wooden fence around it. The fence has that same dark brown stain every commercial wooden fence has. The gate is open. It's always open. Nobody closes the dumpster gate.

The AC unit clicks on and there's a full second where you can hear the fan spinning up before the air starts. The curtains are that heavy blackout material but they don't quite meet in the middle. There's a stripe of light between them about two fingers wide. If I close them tighter they drift apart again. The curtain rod has those clips, the kind with the little rings that slide along the rod, and two of the clips near the center are missing so the fabric sags there and that's where the gap forms.

The remote is bolted to the nightstand. Not bolted — there's a cable, a thin steel cable like a bike lock, looped through a hole drilled in the remote and anchored to the furniture. Someone thought about this. Someone designed a remote tether. Someone manufactured it and sold it to hotels and it was cheaper than replacing stolen remotes.

I haven't turned the TV on. The screen is dark and slightly convex and I can see the whole room reflected in it, compressed and curved, the bed taking up most of the reflection. My reflection is small and doesn't look like me, or looks like me from far away, which I guess it is.

Checkout is at eleven. The card they gave me is in my wallet, the card that isn't really a key but everyone calls a key. Two of them, they always give you two. I've never used the second one.
