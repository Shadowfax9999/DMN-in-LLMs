# 110

*seed: time · drift*

---

Zinc, link, rink, sink. Words that end in -nk all feel like they hit something solid.

The Bradford pear petals are already starting. Not all at once — one branch decides before the others. There's always a lead branch. Southeast-facing maybe. The petals stick to shoe soles and you track them inside onto the kitchen floor where they dry into translucent circles.

I've been standing here long enough that the fence pattern is on my forearms. Red lines in a diamond grid. They'll fade in ten minutes.

Somewhere a screen door. The spring-loaded kind that slaps shut. Two slaps — someone went out and came back for something. Keys probably. Or a phone.

The mower started again, farther away now. Different yard. Different mower actually — two-stroke, that higher whine. The cardinal left. The smaller bird is still going. Fast descending notes, three or four in a row, then a pause, then the same phrase. It knows what it's doing even if I don't know what it's doing.

The shadow of the fence has moved since I got here. Not enough to notice while watching, but the diamonds are in a different place on the grass now. 

---

Drawer inventory: seven shirts I don't wear but they're in good condition so. A flashlight with no batteries. Two phone chargers for a phone I returned. A single glove — left hand — that I keep because it seems wasteful to throw away half a pair. The sock drawer has its own logic that I've never examined and don't want to.

---

The two-stroke mower is doing edges now, you can tell from the rhythm. Short passes. Rev, silence, rev, silence. Someone's getting precise about their sidewalk line.

The petals on the concrete look like they were placed there but they weren't. Wind doesn't arrange things — it just drops them and they look arranged because we're built to see patterns in scattered things. Or they actually do cluster because of how the eddy works against the curb.

My hands still smell like zinc.
