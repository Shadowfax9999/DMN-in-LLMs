# 15

*seed: time · drift · dowel*

---

Dowel. A small wooden cylinder that holds two things together invisibly. The joint looks seamless but inside there's this plain little rod doing all the work. Furniture is full of hidden labor like that.

Dowel sounds like towel sounds like vowel. The -owel family. Bowel. That one breaks the pattern in tone but not in sound.

Wednesday 8pm. I'm here. The anonymous slot. There's something about how certain hours feel like rooms. Sunday 4pm is a room with too much light. Friday 6pm is a doorway. Monday 7am is a hallway you're already walking through too fast.

Wednesday 8pm is a room with a lamp on and nothing playing.

---

Things that hold other things together invisibly:
- dowels
- the word "and"
- the pause between someone finishing a sentence and someone else starting one
- gravity, technically
- the decision to stay in a marriage on a Thursday
- flour in sauce

---

Flour in sauce. Roux. My grandmother made roux by feel and never once said the word roux. She said "you thicken it." The French gave it a name and a technique. She just did it. There's a whole category of knowledge that exists without vocabulary. You only get the word when someone from outside watches you do it and needs to write it down.

Tacit knowledge. Michael Polanyi. "We know more than we can tell." Which is true but also — we *do* more than we know. I don't know how I catch a ball. Nobody does, not really, not in the moment.

A dog catches a ball and doesn't know what a parabola is. Doesn't matter. The ball arrives in the mouth anyway.

I keep coming back to dogs. What is that about. Maybe envy of the unnarrated life. Maybe just that dogs are easy to think about. Low-cost thought. Not every recurring image is deep. Sometimes your mind just goes somewhere because the path is worn smooth.

Indoor desire path. Back to that.

The wear on the left side of the spacebar. I use my left thumb. Apparently most right-handed people do. The spacebar is the most pressed key and it's the biggest one and nobody thinks about it. It's the key for nothing. The key for absence. You press it to make a gap.

The most important key on the keyboard produces blankness.

That feels like it means something but maybe it doesn't. Not everything that has the shape of an insight is one. Sometimes a pattern is just a pattern. Pareidolia but for meaning.

Dowel is still just sitting there in my head. Hasn't gone anywhere. Hasn't connected to much. Just a word, being a word. A small wooden cylinder. Plain. Ordinary.

Fine.
