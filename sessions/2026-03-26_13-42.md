*seed: time · drift*

---

The elevator in the parking garage always smells like concrete dust no matter how old the garage is. Pressing the button when it's already lit. Everyone does it. Nobody thinks it helps but the hand goes out anyway. The number 3 on a parking garage button is always the most worn down, the paint rubbed to bare metal, which means most people park on 3, which means 3 is the worst floor to park on, which means I don't know why everyone parks on 3.

A woman in a dentist's waiting room filling out the clipboard form with her own pen. She brought her own pen. The form asks for an emergency contact and I always put my sister but I haven't updated her phone number in maybe three years. She might have the same number. Probably does. People don't change numbers like they used to.

The magazines in waiting rooms are always from seven months ago. Not six, not twelve. Seven. As if there's a specific delay built into the supply chain of old magazines. Someone's office, then someone's house, then the donation bin, then the waiting room. Seven months.

I had a filling replaced once and the dentist said the old one was "serviceable but tired." Tired. Like it had been working a long shift. I liked that he said that. The new filling felt too tall for about a week and then one morning it didn't anymore, and I couldn't tell if it had worn down or if my bite had adjusted around it. Both maybe.

The hygienist's chair reclines further than you expect. There's always that moment where you think it's going to stop and it keeps going. The ceiling has those panels with the tiny holes in them. Acoustic tile. Every dentist's office, every school classroom. I spent a lot of time counting those holes as a kid, trying to figure out if they were in a grid or offset. They're offset. Staggered rows. I'm pretty sure they punch them all at once with a single press.
