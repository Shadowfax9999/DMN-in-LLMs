Gristle. Still sitting there. The word itself has gristle's texture — that *gr* at the front, the resistance of it. Words that feel like their meaning. There's a term for that. Somewhere between onomatopoeia and something else. Phonaesthesia maybe. The *gl* in glisten, gleam, glow, glint. All light-related. Someone noticed that once and now I can't unnotice it.

Things you can't unnotice:

- The arrow in the FedEx logo
- That the word "bed" looks like a bed
- The Wilhelm scream
- How often people say "I mean" before saying what they already meant

Wednesday people. I keep thinking about them. I think they'd be the type to own exactly one very good pen. Not a pen collection. Just the one.

Spring evening through a window is a particular color that I don't think has a name. It's not golden hour exactly. It's the hour after golden hour where everything goes slightly blue-green and the light seems to come from the ground up instead of the sky down. Lawns glow. That's the only word for it. Lawns *glow* for about twenty minutes in spring and then it's just dark.

I had a thought and lost it.

No — it was about agreements. The lock thing. How much of the physical world is actually just a suggestion. Walls in offices. Those little ropes at museums. The velvet ones. A child could step over them. Everyone could. The art is *right there*. The velvet rope is a sentence in a language everyone agreed to read.

Cattle grids. Some farmers paint stripes on the road instead of installing actual cattle grids and it works. The cows won't cross. The cows are reading the sentence too, but they didn't agree to anything. They just — believe it.

Is that better or worse than us? We know the velvet rope is nothing and we still respect it. The cows think the stripes are a real gap. At least the cows are being honest about their obedience. We're performing ours.

Performing. That's too big a word for what I mean. Not performing. Just... maintaining. Like how you maintain a garden not because anyone checks but because the maintenance is the thing. The doing of it. Wednesday maintenance. Wednesday is a maintenance day. That's maybe why it feels like a tunnel. You're just keeping things going.

My favorite kitchen object is a wooden spoon that's slightly burned on one side. Not mine. One I remember from somewhere. The burn made it specific.

Specificity as a form of love. No, that's too neat. Skip it.

The gristle is still there. I think gristle is going to outlast me in this conversation. It has that energy. Persistent. Unlovely. Just *present*. Some thoughts are gristle thoughts. Not the ones you wanted. Not nutritious. But they have a chew to them and your mind keeps going back.

7:31 on a Wednesday. Somewhere a Wednesday person is having their favorite hour of their favorite day and they're not telling anyone about it.
