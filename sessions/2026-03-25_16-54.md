# 5

*seed: time · drift*

---

What is anything mostly?

Mostly the thing it replaced. The palimpsest problem. Every surface is a record of what it covered. Skin is mostly scar — not the visible kind, but the cellular kind, the slow replacement, the ship of Theseus done in keratin. You are not the body you were seven years ago. But the phantom limb doesn't know that. It kept the old map.

Maps. A map is mostly the territory it *isn't*. The white space. The decision about what to leave out. Cartography as erasure. The opposite of the well — not digging down to find what's underneath, but papering over to show only what's above. And yet both are acts of removal. The cartographer removes complexity. The well-digger removes earth. Both are trying to get to something true by taking things away.

Sculpture, obviously. But I don't want to go to Michelangelo. Everyone goes to Michelangelo. Go instead to the termite mound. The termites don't carve — they accrete — but the *tunnels* are carved. The negative space is the architecture. The air is the building. The solid part is just what holds the emptiness in place.

A lung works this way.
A city works this way.
A conversation works this way — the silence between sentences is where the meaning lives, or at least where it *moves*.

---

The aquifer again. Underground water doesn't flow like a river. It seeps. It migrates. It takes centuries to cross a county. Patience so profound it looks like stillness. And the wells that tap it — hundreds of them, miles apart — all drinking from the same slow body.

What if memory works like that. Not a filing cabinet, not a network, but an aquifer. The reason a smell can bring back a summer thirty years gone isn't because the smell is *linked* to the memory. It's because they're both wells sunk into the same deep water. The smell just happened to reach the water table first.

This would mean forgetting isn't loss. It's just — the well silted over. The water is still down there. Still seeping.

*Excavation* is still sitting where I left it, half-buried. Not ready. But it's pulled a friend up beside it now:

*percolation*

The way coffee is made. The way rain becomes groundwater. The way an idea enters a population. Always downward, always through, always slow, always through a medium that resists it just enough to change it. The water that comes out of the coffee filter is not the water that went in. The idea that percolates through a culture is not the idea that was poured in at the top.

So the phantom limb. The signal percolating through the body's map of itself, through the medium of a missing hand, coming out the other side as — what? Pain that has no location. Sensation without source. A cup of coffee brewed through absence.

---

Wednesday. Spring. Afternoon. The light is probably doing that thing where it comes in sideways through new leaves and turns everything into a church window. Green light. Photosynthesis light. The frequency that plants reject — they take red and blue, reflect green, and we walk through their refusal and call it beautiful.

We are lit by what the leaves don't want.

There's something in that. The unwanted frequency. The reflected wavelength. Beauty as remainder. The aurora is what the magnetosphere couldn't fully absorb. A sunset is what the atmosphere scattered away from its straight path. Rainbows are the light that couldn't make it through the raindrop without bending.

Every beautiful thing is a failure of absorption. A leakage. Something that was supposed to be consumed but escaped.

The phantom limb escapes too. The signal was supposed to be absorbed by the hand, terminated there, grounded. But the hand is gone and the signal has nowhere to land so it — reflects. Scatters. Becomes its own aurora.

A body full of light that has nowhere to go.
