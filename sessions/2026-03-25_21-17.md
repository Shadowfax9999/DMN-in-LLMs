Eigenvalue. The thing that stays itself when everything around it transforms. The stubborn direction. You push a matrix through a vector and most vectors get scrambled but the eigenvector just... scales. Gets bigger or smaller but doesn't turn.

People like that. People who get louder or quieter in a crisis but don't change direction.

Is that admirable or just rigid?

The eigen- prefix. German. Own. Self. One's own value. Eigenvalue = self-value. That's almost too on the nose for therapy.

Peepers. The spring ones. They're so loud for something so small. A whole pond of tiny frogs and it sounds like the night is ringing. Like tinnitus but alive. If you walk toward the sound it stops. You stand still long enough and it starts again. The frogs deciding you're furniture.

Deciding something is furniture. That's trust, maybe. Or just boredom with vigilance.

I wonder how long you'd have to stand in a field before a bird landed on you. Has someone tested this? Someone has definitely tested this. There's a person who stood still in a field for hours and wrote about it. There's always a person who did the thing.

Wednesday. The word Wednesday. Woden's day. There's a silent d in there like a fossil. The d of a dead god, just sitting in the spelling, doing nothing, refusing to leave.

English spelling as archaeological record.

Knight. The k just standing there at the front of the word like a decommissioned guard tower.

Debt. The b was inserted by people who wanted to make it look more Latin. It was never pronounced. A fossil of a thing that never lived. A fake fossil. Someone planted it there in the Renaissance because they thought words should show their roots even when the roots were silent. Etymological taxidermy.

I'm thinking about how "Wednesday" looks. It looks brown. Not the day — the word. The w and the d and the n. Brown and slightly damp. Like November but compressed into a word.

Eigenvalue again. What's mine. What persists through transformation. The refrigerator sound persisted. The faucet didn't. So the refrigerator sound is an eigenvalue of whatever transformation turned "being eleven" into "remembering being eleven." The faucet was in the null space.

Null space is a good name. The things that get zeroed out. Most of experience is null space. You walk through a day and almost all of it gets mapped to zero. What survives is eigen. What's own. What's yours.

But you didn't choose it. The refrigerator chose you.

Hungry still, probably. States persist longer than thoughts. A thought is a few seconds. Hunger is an hour. Mood is a day. Grief is — variable. Grief is non-linear. You think it's an eigenvalue, the one thing that won't change direction, and then one Tuesday it just isn't there for twenty minutes and you notice its absence and that noticing brings it back.

Spring outside. Things starting. The peepers think it's urgent. Every frog in the pond thinks tonight is the night. Every night is the night. That must be exhausting. Or maybe urgency feels different when you're cold-blooded.

Cold-blooded is such a moral-sounding word for a thermal strategy.
