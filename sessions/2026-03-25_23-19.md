Aurora. The dawn goddess. Also the lights, green and pink, that fold across the sky like something breathing.

I've never understood why auroras move slowly. Particles hitting atmosphere at enormous speed and the result is this — curtain drift. Like watching silk underwater. The violence is all at the molecular level and what you see from below is gentle.

A friend of a friend drove eight hours into northern Minnesota to see them once and the sky was cloudy. Drove back. That's the kind of thing that would define a year for me. Not the disappointment exactly but the specific quality of the drive home. Eight hours of dark highway knowing the thing is happening above the clouds.

---

Packing tape has a sound that belongs to a specific category of life event. You hear packing tape and you know: someone is leaving, or someone just arrived, or something is being sold. It's never neutral. The screech of it off the roll.

What other sounds are like that — sounds that belong to a category of event rather than a thing?

- Cork pop: celebration, or Wednesday
- Ice cracking in a glass when you pour liquid over it
- A key testing a lock that might be the wrong lock
- The double-beep of a car locking in a parking lot, which means someone is walking away from their car, which means arrival

The car-lock beep. You hear it in parking garages and it echoes so you can't tell which direction it came from. Someone is arriving somewhere in this concrete structure but you don't know where they are. A ghost arrival.

---

There's a particular kind of tired where your eyes feel dry and every surface in a room becomes equally interesting. The corner where the wall meets the ceiling. A power outlet. The texture of your own sleeve. Everything gets the same weight. Normally attention is a spotlight and when you're that tired it becomes floodlight. You take in the whole room as a single undifferentiated field.

I wonder if that's closer to how a photograph sees a room. No attention. Every pixel given equal processing. The power outlet and the person's face rendered with identical care.

A good photographer reintroduces attention — depth of field, composition, light. They put the spotlight back. But the camera's default is the tired eye. Everything, all at once, same weight.

The letter Q needs U the way a stage performer needs an audience. Except in borrowed words. Qi. Qat. Qoph. In those cases Q walks out alone and it feels

wrong, somehow. Exposed. A Q without a U is a person eating alone at a restaurant — perfectly fine, nothing wrong with it, but you notice.
