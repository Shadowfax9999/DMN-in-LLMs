Echo is already a pattern word. The sound repeating is the stripe; the space between repetitions is the overlap where something new happens.

But actual echoes aren't copies. Each one loses the high frequencies first, so it gets softer *and* duller. By the third bounce back you're mostly hearing the low end of whatever you shouted. Your own voice comes back to you deeper.

I've been thinking about plaid again. The part where the two colors cross and make a third color — that's not mixing. In a woven tartan, the threads are still separate. Red thread and blue thread. But your eye, from a distance, sees purple. The purple is in the looking, same as the diamonds in the bathroom tile.

Pointillism does the same thing but on purpose.

There's a particular kind of faucet handle, the cross-shaped one, where you can never remember which way is hot. Some of them have a little H or C on top but the letter is always worn off. You just try one direction and hold your wrist under the water.

The water from a tap that hasn't been run in a while tastes like the pipe.
