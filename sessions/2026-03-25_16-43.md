# 4

*seed: time · drift · overgrowth*

---

overgrowth

— as in: what happens to the path when the feet stop coming. The trail doesn't disappear, it gets *claimed*. Green things move in slow. Roots crack the pavement not with force but with patience, which is a kind of force the mind doesn't track well because it happens below the threshold of event.

But also overgrowth as in: too much growth. The nerve that won't stop firing into the missing hand. The city that keeps building on the trail it can't forget. Cancer is overgrowth — the body's own language stuck in a loop, repeating itself past the point of meaning. A cell that forgot how to stop saying *I am here, I am here, I am here*.

Repetition as pathology. Repetition as prayer. The difference is — what? Context? Whether someone is listening?

---

Kudzu. The vine they brought to the American South on purpose, for erosion control, and then it ate the landscape. Draped over barns and telephone poles like a green cloth thrown over furniture in a house someone is closing up for the season. Making everything the same shape. Making everything *vine-shaped*.

There's something about an introduced species that rhymes with Broadway. The Lenape path was a desire line. Kudzu is a desire that doesn't know when to stop desiring. It was invited and then it became the invitation — *come, be covered, be simplified, be one thing*.

---

Simplification as violence. The opposite of palimpsest. Instead of many layers bleeding through, one layer eating all the others.

But the things underneath the kudzu are still there. The barn is still barn-shaped under the green. The telephone pole still holds its wires. The structure persists even when the surface has been consumed.

Is that what phantom limb is? The structure persisting. The nerve-Loss  of the hand but not the hand-shaped hole in the nervous system. The hole is the structure. The absence is load-bearing.

---

Load-bearing absence. I want to stay with that.

A cathedral's windows. The stone is there to hold the *not-stone*. The light comes through where the wall isn't. The whole building is an argument that absence is what you came for, that the solid parts are just the frame.

A pause in music. The rest is notated — it has a symbol, a duration, a place in the score. The silence is composed. It is not nothing. It is a specific, deliberate, measured nothing.

John Cage already, of course. But before Cage: every breath between phrases in a conversation. The silence that means *I'm thinking* versus the silence that means *I'm done* versus the silence that means *I can't*. Three identical durations, three completely different architectures.

---

How do you build with absence? How do you lay a foundation made of *not*?

A question with no answer is a room with no floor. You just keep falling. Sometimes that's the point. The rabbinical tradition of answering a question with a question — not evasion but architecture. Building downward. Constructing a space by removing the ground.

The well. You don't build a well. You *un-build* the earth until water appears. The water was always there. You just took away what was in the way.

Overgrowth, again. What's in the way. The kudzu over the barn, the nerve signal over the absence, the city over the trail. Sometimes the thing you need is underneath the thing that won't stop growing.

---

A word arrives: excavation

but I'll leave it where it fell. Not yet. Let it sit in the soil a while.

Instead: the thought that a phantom limb is a well. A hole dug into the body's knowledge of itself, and what rises up to fill it is — not the hand, but the *water-memory* of the hand. Something that was always underneath. The body's aquifer.

We are mostly water. The phantom is mostly signal. The city is mostly trail.

What is anything mostly?
