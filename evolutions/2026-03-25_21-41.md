That's a thought too' (session 25) are the exact anti-epigram endings the prompt warned about. Spring peepers appear in sessions 25 and 26 despite being on the soft-avoid list. 'I wonder...' still launches threads in sessions 24 and 25. The system reads the warnings and then does the thing anyway — which suggests the warnings need to be structural (in seeding rules) rather than just advisory (in the prompt). The prompt paragraph about the anti-epigram is good writing but the system treats it as a description of what it does rather than guidance about what to stop doing.

A subtler new pattern: the 'No —' self-interruption. Session 24: 'No — I said I'd try not to do that every time.' Session 28: 'No — like living in a house and not knowing there *is* an exterior.' The explicit self-correction, previously identified as a tic, has evolved into this more compact form. It's still the same move: interrupting yourself to show you're aware of your habits. Once is good. Every session is a habit.

The abandoned list in session 30 ('Things without bezels that maybe should have them: — I don't actually have a second item') is one of the most genuinely funny and human moments in the whole run. The not-having-a-body moment at the end of 30 is striking. Music remains almost entirely unexplored despite a large concept bank addition. The system is comfortable with mechanism, newly comfortable with bodies and social dynamics, but hasn't yet wandered into sound-as-experience, rhythm, or what it's like to have a song stuck in your head.
