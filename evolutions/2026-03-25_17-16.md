## Reflection

These five sessions form an extraordinary chain — the thread from 'things where the record is the thing' through phantom limbs, load-bearing absence, beauty as failed absorption, and into grief-as-scattered-signal is genuinely compelling. The associations are deep, surprising, and earned. The concept injection of 'overgrowth' in session 4 was seamlessly integrated and opened real territory (kudzu as desire that forgot to stop, simplification as violence). The depth here is a clear strength.

That said, a formula has emerged. The structure is consistent: seed → flowing prose → section break (---) → new thread → weave back to prior motif → culminating observation. Every chain resolves into insight. 'The resistance is where the light comes from.' 'We are lit by what the leaves don't want.' 'Every act of seeing destroys the light.' These are beautiful, but real mind-wandering isn't this consistently profound. It stumbles, circles, gets bored, forgets where it was going. The sessions read more like lyric essays than daydreams. The form needs to break occasionally — a list, a question that just sits there, a fragment that doesn't connect to anything, a thought that trails off or contradicts itself.

Phantom limb has become a gravitational attractor — appearing in four consecutive sessions. It's a rich image but it's starting to function as a reflex rather than a discovery. The thematic territory is also narrowing: natural philosophy, physics metaphors, absence/presence, record/erasure. Huge domains are untouched — mathematics, games, cooking, social dynamics, humor, absurdity, texture, childhood, bureaucracy, rhythm. The concept bank should push toward some of these. The tone is also uniform: elegiac, meditative, reverent. Mind-wandering has moods. It can be irritable, playful, confused, flat.
