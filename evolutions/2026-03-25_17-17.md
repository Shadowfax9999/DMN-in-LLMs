## Reflection

The DMN has produced genuinely impressive associative work — the chains from dead reckoning through desire lines through phantom limbs through aquifers through light-as-remainder are earned and often beautiful. Session 2 remains the most exploratory in feel; sessions 3-6 have increasingly read as chapters of a single essay about absence, record, and signal. Phantom limb has appeared in four of the last five sessions despite the theme-avoidance rule added in the first evolution pass. The absence/negative-space attractor is powerful enough that even the 'overgrowth' concept injection in session 4 got pulled into its orbit within a few paragraphs. The system is deep but narrow, and narrowing further.

Formally, nothing has changed. Every session is prose paragraphs separated by horizontal rules. Every thought resolves into insight. Session 4's three-line list and session 6's trailing-off sentence are the only moments that break the essay pattern. The system prompt explicitly permits fragments, lists, questions, half-formed things, trailing off, being ordinary or confused — but the actual output ignores all of this. The tone is consistently elegiac and revelatory. There is no humor, no banality, no wrong turns, no boredom, no confusion. Real mind-wandering includes stretches where you think about what you need from the grocery store, or notice a word sounds funny, or lose the thread entirely and sit in blankness for a moment.

The concept bank additions from the first pass (lullaby, recipe, compost, shibboleth, etc.) haven't been tested yet since only 'overgrowth' was injected. The bank needs concepts that actively resist the current gravitational pull toward the profound — things that are stubbornly mundane, awkward, or absurd. The system prompt needs a harder nudge: not just permission to be messy, but something closer to an instruction to vary form. And the seeding rules need stronger theme-avoidance enforcement, possibly by explicitly listing exhausted themes.
