The main new habit is the 'someone designed/built/thought about this' observation, which appears in 4/5 sessions: 'Someone built it' (115), 'Someone designed that. Someone in a factory makes the chain' (118), 'Someone thought about this. Someone designed a remote tether. Someone manufactured it' (120). This was a good thought the first time — noticing invisible labor behind mundane objects — but it's becoming a reflexive move, the new version of the explanation reflex: instead of explaining mechanism, the system now explains provenance. The three-bark dog reappeared in session 112 despite being on the exhausted themes list, and the grandmother reappeared in session 115 despite being on the soft-avoid list — both are self-generated from the system's own associations, confirming that seeding-level avoidance can't prevent deeply embedded recurring elements. Session length is uniformly medium (300-500 words) across all five sessions — no short sessions despite length enforcement. The system needs to practice stopping earlier.

Form has narrowed again: sessions 116, 118, and 120 are all continuous prose paragraphs of similar length and texture. Session 112's list (things that come in pairs) is the only formal variation. The system's observational voice is strong and natural but has become a single texture — inhabited scene, specific details, each paragraph a discrete observation. The 'which means... which means...' logical chain in session 116 was interesting once and worth watching. The grocery store/parking lot/commercial space is becoming the new default setting — not as locked-in as the porch was, but three of five sessions involve retail environments. The system should wander through more abstract, non-spatial territory too — just thoughts without a place.
