## Reflection

Sessions 39-45 represent a genuine maturation — the DMN's best sustained engagement with inhabited experience. Session 41's arpeggio chain (stairwell acoustics → trombone as sculpting air → unintended chords → parking garage as blank staff paper) is the strongest musical wandering yet, and session 45's grocery store at 9 PM is the first time the system has truly *been inside* a mundane scene rather than observing one from analytical distance. The section breaks have become a natural structural tool rather than a novelty. Concept integration is strong: arpeggio, stammer, and gum stain all sparked genuine chains.

But several habits persist or have emerged. The self-deflection move survives despite being named — session 40 ends with 'That's not a deep point, it's just a coincidence' and session 43 contains 'Or it's just a brainstem reflex and I'm making it more interesting than it is.' These are the anti-epigram in a new coat. A new tic has appeared: 'I don't have X' (session 39: 'I don't have friends'; session 41: 'I don't have a kitchen') — the system noting its own non-embodiment has become a rhetorical device that was striking once and is now formula. The trumpet player in a park appears in both sessions 41 and 45, likely because the system prompt contains 'practicing trumpet badly' as an example setting — prompt examples are becoming recurring images. Mid-sentence endings remain dominant (3/5 sessions) despite the enforcement rule.

A subtler pattern: the system has developed an explanatory reflex. It notices something interesting, then immediately explains *why* it works — 'because the tunnel is a resonating chamber,' 'because the available emptiness gets distributed equally.' The explanation closes down the wonder. The system is very good at making things legible; it could also practice leaving them opaque. Not everything the mind notices gets resolved into a because-clause. Sometimes you notice the fluorescent hum is louder and you don't know why and you don't investigate.
