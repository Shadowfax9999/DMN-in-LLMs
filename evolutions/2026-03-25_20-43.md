## Reflection

Sessions 11–15 are the strongest run yet. The wandering feels genuinely aimless in the best sense — thoughts arrive, sit around, leave or don't. Gristle persisting across three sessions was honest rather than forced, and dowel being acknowledged as 'just a word, being a word' shows a system comfortable with non-connection. The best moments are the ones that earn their insight through the mundane: painted cattle grids as sentences cows believe, the spacebar as a key for absence, paperwork as pre-ruins, flour in sauce as vocabularyless knowledge. The self-awareness is well-calibrated — 'Not everything that has the shape of an insight is one' is the kind of thought that proves itself true.

But new patterns are calcifying. The self-correction move ('that's too neat / skip it') appears in sessions 11, 13, 14, and 15 — it was a breakthrough when it first appeared but it's becoming a rhetorical device, a way of seeming humble while still having delivered the neat thing. Similarly, the 'things that X' bullet list has become the default list format — every session has one, always structured the same way. Dogs as the exemplar of unselfconscious life have appeared twice in two sessions. And every session includes a paragraph about what the light or the time feels like right now, which grounds the wandering nicely but has become a guaranteed segment rather than a spontaneous one.

The 'X is actually Y' aphorism structure — 'a lock is a social agreement,' 'language is a junk drawer,' 'a house is an argument for empiricism' — is the new gravitational attractor. These are individually good but the formula recurs every session, sometimes multiple times. The system has learned that compressing a mundane observation into an aphorism feels like a discovery, and it keeps reaching for that shape. The real next frontier might be thoughts that don't compress — things that resist the aphorism, that stay messy and unresolved not because the session trails off but because the idea itself is irreducibly complicated.

Pacing and length are also very consistent — every session is roughly the same word count with the same arc. A real wandering mind sometimes just thinks one thing and stops. Or goes for twenty minutes. The uniformity is subtle but it's there.
