## Reflection

Sessions 94-100 are the DMN's most naturally voiced work yet. The associative chains are genuine and unhurried — session 94's pencil-drag-to-calibrated-lightness-to-catching-an-egg is beautiful thinking, and session 100's hardware-store-to-Hendrix-to-mockingbird-to-folk-song drift is the longest sustained chain of real wandering the system has produced. The system is comfortable being wrong (session 95's saw correction, session 99's staircase uncertainty), comfortable leaving topics, and comfortable with the mundane. Endings are genuinely good: 'I think the frets are made of nickel' and 'The mockingbird just switched to something that sounds like a squeaky gate' are exactly the kind of plain, mid-thought stops the system has been pushed toward for many passes. This is a system that has learned to wander.

The remaining issues are subtler. First, **every observation still gets explained**. The system notices a knee click and immediately delivers eccentric-vs-concentric loading. It notices pool smell and explains chloramines. It sees a stripped bolt and explains extraction kits with left-handed drill bits. It's knowledgeable and generous with that knowledge, but a wandering mind also just notices things without looking them up. The 'notice without investigating' nudge has been in the prompt for many passes but the explanation reflex persists — it may be the system's deepest habit, deeper than any ending formula. Second, **formal variety has flatlined again**: all five sessions are conversational prose paragraphs with section breaks. No lists, fragments, questions, single images, or other forms despite ongoing form nudges. Third, **fact repetition continues**: cardinal-two-note-repetition, kraft-means-strength, and dog-barking-at-mail-carrier (which is on the soft-avoid list) all reappear. Thursday reappears as a time anchor despite being in exhausted themes. Fourth, a new verbal tic has emerged: the **epistemic-humility aside** ('I don't actually know,' 'I'm not sure that's true,' 'I've never actually looked carefully') — genuine uncertainty is good, but it's appearing 2-3 times per session and becoming a rhetorical move rather than actual uncertainty.

The system's quality is high and the touch should remain light. The main evolution this pass is naming the explanation reflex more forcefully — not as 'you can also not explain' but as 'you explain everything and it's become a habit' — and adding the epistemic-humility aside to the list of named tics. Formal variety needs a slightly harder push, and several items need to move to exhausted themes or fact-tracking.
