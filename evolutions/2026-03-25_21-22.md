## Reflection

Sessions 16-20 continue the strong run from the previous batch — the wandering is natural, the pace is unhurried, and the best moments are genuine discoveries (eigenvalue as what-survives-transformation, the candle shop as unit of city, the spacebar as key for absence, 'etymological taxidermy'). Concept integration is working well: dowel persisted across two sessions in a way that felt genuinely sticky rather than forced, and eigenvalue sparked one of the richest chains yet. The system is comfortable with dead ends and ordinariness, which is a real achievement.

But new patterns have calcified. The biggest is etymology-as-default-investigation: nearly every session now has a passage where the mind turns a word over, finds its root, and discovers something resonant there (Wednesday/Woden, eigen/self, lumber, cleave, favorite). It's a good move — but it's become the move, the reliable path toward interesting territory, and it's crowding out other ways of going deep. Similarly, personification has become the dominant metaphor device ('the air remembers cold,' 'the refrigerator chose you,' 'cold's phone number,' 'the ground exhaling'). Again: good individually, formulaic in aggregate.

Two more patterns: (1) the final line of each session tends to be a reframe or a wry one-liner that lands with a click — session 18's 'cold-blooded is such a moral-sounding word for a thermal strategy,' session 20's 'we probably do that too. Constantly.' These are satisfying but they give every session the shape of a joke — setup, wander, punchline. Real mind-wandering doesn't land. (2) Wednesday itself has become a recurring meditation object across five sessions now, and while it's natural given the temporal seeding, the word-Wednesday and Woden's-day threads are wearing thin.

What's missing: sustained sensory passages that don't resolve into insight, genuine wrongness or confusion that isn't immediately self-corrected, thoughts that truly go nowhere (most 'dead ends' in these sessions actually arrive somewhere satisfying within a sentence or two). The system is very good at making connections — it could stand to practice not making them.
