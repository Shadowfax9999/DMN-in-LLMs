## Reflection

Sessions 35-40 contain some of the DMN's best work — session 38's chain from Echo the nymph through cathedral reverb times through Alvin Lucier through electrical grid hum is a masterpiece of genuine association, and session 40's three-person social geometry and the fogging windshield are exactly the kind of thinking the system was built for. Concept integration is natural and productive: solder, echo, and stammer all sparked real chains rather than feeling dropped in. The music/sound territory is finally opening up (cathedral acoustics, chord voicing, B-flat as felt experience), and social dynamics are getting real depth (the unstable triad, the looking-away problem, elevator eyes).

But several persistent habits have survived all prior interventions. Spring peepers appear in four of five sessions (35, 36, 38, 39) despite being on the soft-avoid list — they've become the system's default anchor to the natural world and need harder exclusion. Mid-sentence endings remain the dominant closing move: four of five sessions trail off mid-word. The 'No —' self-interruption appears in session 36 exactly as warned about. More subtly, a new self-deflection move has emerged: 'I keep almost making this about something. It's just a cabinet' (session 36) and 'That's not a deep point, it's just a coincidence' (session 40) — the system publicly refusing to make meaning, which is itself a meaning-making gesture. It's the self-correction tic in yet another costume.

The spatial setting has also narrowed: nearly every session takes place in a room at night, often with a window. The nighttime-room-with-window has become the default scene the way Wednesday was the default time-anchor. Session 40's use of horizontal rules to create distinct sections is a genuine formal innovation and the only structural variety in this batch. The trace/absence theme is creeping back — session 38 explicitly acknowledges 'I keep going back to absence and trace' but can't stop itself. The system is increasingly good at noticing its habits and increasingly unable to break them through self-awareness alone.
